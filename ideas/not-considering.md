## Candidate "do nots" — leaning against, per research *(directional, not yet decided; positioning OPEN)*
These are research-supported leanings, not formal decisions. Each is name- and story-agnostic. Confirm and move up as real decisions once positioning is set:

- **~~Growth-factor / exosome / stem-cell "hero"~~ → REOPENED as OPEN-under-research (2026-06-21, founder direction).** The founder clarified these were never ruled out; the brand is building the research library for *everything possible*. The neutral verdict from the new device/biologics dossiers ([`../research/devices/exosomes.md`](../research/devices/exosomes.md), [`../research/devices/stem-cells.md`](../research/devices/stem-cells.md), [`../research/devices/polynucleotides-pdrn.md`](../research/devices/polynucleotides-pdrn.md)): a **topical, plant-/non-human-derived, claims-honest** product is *conditionally* viable (real premium demand + pricing power), while **human-derived / injectable** exosome & stem-cell routes carry active FDA safety enforcement → still avoid. So: **open as a possible *topical secondary*, not as an unexamined "do not."** Original 2026-06-18 caution (thin evidence, rising regulatory scrutiny, claims-discipline strain) still applies as *risk to manage*, not as a closed door. No product commitment — research-open.
- **~~Single patented-molecule bet~~ → RECLASSIFIED (2026-06-19) as a live strategic option, not a leaning-against.** A single-patented-molecule model is *high-variance, not inherently fragile* — it built SK-II, Augustinus Bader, and Olaplex, and Timeline's single-molecule play drew L'Oréal/Nestlé backing. Whether it's strong or risky depends on owning vs. licensing the IP, independent (especially topical) evidence, and capital. The multi-active barrier complex remains *one* sound architecture, but is no longer treated as the only defensible core. See the balanced "when it works / when it doesn't" treatment in [`../brand/positioning.md`](../brand/positioning.md).
- **SKU sprawl at launch (e.g., 20+ products)** — dilutes a lean routine; the economics and competitive logic favor fewer, better SKUs at launch. Leaning against, per research.
- **Unsubstantiated "miracle" claims** — legal exposure plus credibility risk; conflicts with the claims-discipline the research calls for. Leaning against, per research.
- **Me-too clinical-white aesthetic** — the category is crowded with near-identical clinical/white visual identities, so copying it forfeits differentiation. Visual direction is OPEN; this notes the crowding, not a chosen alternative. Leaning against, per research.

> Note on positioning: the worldview/positioning is **OPEN**. We are not pre-committing to a "fight aging," pro-aging/age-positive, science/longevity-led, clean/transparency-led, or barrier-health-led stance here. Items above are framed only around what the research and competitive crowding support, independent of which positioning we choose.
