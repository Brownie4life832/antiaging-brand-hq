# Device / Procedure: PRP / PRF — Platelet-Rich Plasma / Platelet-Rich Fibrin ("Vampire Facial")

**Researched:** 2026-06-21 · **Status:** ☑ context-only (📚 education/context-only — see §10)
**Category:** autologous biologic / device-driven procedure (clinic/provider-only; **no at-home equivalent**)
**Regulatory class (US):** **Autologous biologic regulated under PHS Act §361 (HCT/P) — not FDA-approved for aesthetic use; aesthetic application is off-label.** The *centrifuge/preparation devices* are separately FDA-cleared (510(k)), generally for **orthopedic/blood-prep** indications, not facial rejuvenation. "Vampire Facial®" is a **trademark** (Charles Runels). *Flag clearly.*

> Scope note: Exhaustive, citation-backed evidence review for the brand's research base. Evidence is
> separated strictly by tier. PRP/PRF is **autologous** — the patient's own blood, centrifuged to
> concentrate platelets (PRP) or to form a fibrin matrix (PRF), then **microneedled into** ("vampire
> facial") or **injected into** the skin to deliver growth factors. It is a **provider procedure, not a
> product a topical brand can sell.** The headline safety lesson — the **2018–2023 New Mexico HIV
> cluster** — is the single most important fact in this file. Cross-references the brand's
> [`../ingredients/growth-factors.md`](../ingredients/growth-factors.md) dossier (topical GFs are a

## 1. Identity

- **What it is:** A small volume of the patient's **own blood** is drawn and spun in a centrifuge to separate and concentrate **platelets** (and, in PRF, a fibrin scaffold). The concentrate is then **microneedled into the face** (the "vampire facial") or **injected intradermally** (the "vampire facelift") to deliver a bolus of autologous **growth factors** to the skin. The pitch: platelets release PDGF, TGF-β, VEGF, EGF, IGF → fibroblast activation → collagen/ECM and "regeneration."
- **Modality family:** **Biologic (autologous growth-factor delivery)** + a **mechanical delivery vector** (microneedling channels or needle injection). Note the overlap: the *vehicle* is microneedling (see [`microneedling.md`](microneedling.md)); the *payload* is autologous blood concentrate.
- **PRP vs PRF — the key fork:**
  - **PRP (1st-gen):** blood drawn **with anticoagulant**, higher-speed spin → liquid platelet concentrate. Platelets activated (CaCl₂/thrombin) release ~95% of growth factors in a **rapid burst**.
  - **PRF (2nd-gen):** drawn **without anticoagulant**, gentler/slower spin → a **fibrin matrix** trapping platelets, leukocytes, and cells that releases growth factors **slowly over days**. Variants: solid PRF, **i-PRF** (injectable fluid PRF), PRFM. PRF's "sustained release" is the marketing rationale for it being "better than PRP" — *this is largely a mechanistic/biological claim, not a proven clinical-superiority claim* (§4, §9).
- **"Vampire Facial®" / "Vampire Facelift®":** trademarked by **Dr Charles Runels** (Alabama, ~2010). The trademark covers the *branded protocol/name*, not a distinct cleared device. "Facial" = PRP + microneedling; "facelift" = PRP injection (often + HA filler).
- **At-home equivalent:** **none, and none possible.** PRP/PRF requires venipuncture, a centrifuge, and sterile handling of blood — inherently a clinical procedure. Any consumer "PRP-in-a-jar" or topical claiming to be PRP is **not** PRP.

## 2. Mechanism of action

**Plausible mechanism.** Concentrated platelets, once activated, degranulate and release a cocktail of growth factors — **PDGF** (fibroblast proliferation, angiogenesis), **TGF-β** (matrix synthesis, but also a *pro-fibrotic/pro-scarring* signal in some contexts), **VEGF** (angiogenesis), **EGF**, **bFGF**, **IGF-1**. In skin, these are proposed to drive fibroblast activation, neocollagenesis, and microvascular support. When delivered **via microneedling**, the micro-channels add their own independent wound-healing stimulus (so the "vampire facial" is *two* mechanisms stacked, which confounds attribution — is the benefit the PRP, the needling, or both?). **PRF** adds a fibrin scaffold that (in vitro) prolongs growth-factor release.

**Proven in-skin effect.** Growth-factor release from activated platelets is **real and well-characterized biochemistry**. The leap that is *not* cleanly proven is whether a **single bolus of autologous growth factors produces durable, clinically meaningful dermal remodeling in aging facial skin** beyond what the microneedling vehicle (or a saline injection's own trauma) delivers. The most rigorous blinded RCT (Alam 2018, §4) found **no objective benefit over saline** — a direct hit to the "proven remodeling" story.

**Mechanistic caveat (autologous ≠ standardized):** because the "drug" is made fresh from each patient's blood, **platelet concentration, leukocyte content, activation method, and growth-factor yield vary enormously** between patients, devices, and protocols. There is **no standardized "dose."** This is the central reason the clinical evidence is so heterogeneous (§4).

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Targeted? | Strength / where the evidence comes from |
|---|---|---|
| Collagen / ECM loss | ⚠️ Claimed primary | **Weak–moderate.** Mechanism (GF-driven fibroblast activation) is plausible; some histology/density signals; but the best blinded RCT found no objective wrinkle/texture benefit over saline. Much "evidence" confounds PRP with microneedling. |
| Photoaging (rhytides/texture) | ⚠️ Mixed | **Weak–moderate.** Several open-label/split-face studies show modest improvement; **patient-perceived** texture gains real, **blinded-rater** gains often not significant (Alam 2018). |
| Pigmentation / tone | ⚠️ Weak | A minority of studies report luminosity/tone improvement; low-quality, often combination. |
| Hydration / water-loss | ⚠️ Weak/indirect | Some elasticity/hydration endpoints (Hersant); strongest when combined with **hyaluronic acid**, which confounds. |
| Cellular senescence | ❌ [unverified for this endpoint] | No direct human senolytic evidence for facial PRP. |
| Inflammaging | ⚠️ | Procedure is acutely pro-inflammatory (that's part of the mechanism); leukocyte-rich PRP may be *more* inflammatory. Not an anti-inflammaging tool. |
| Barrier decline | ❌ | Not a mechanism; microneedling vehicle transiently *breaches* the barrier. |
| Oxidative stress | ❌ | No evidence. |
| Glycation / AGEs | ❌ | No mechanism. |
| Mitochondrial decline | ❌ | No evidence. |
| Microbiome | ❌ (risk-side) | Injecting/needling blood into skin is an **infection/bloodborne-pathogen** vector if non-sterile — the opposite of a benefit (see §6, the HIV cluster). |

**Net:** Marketed as a **collagen/ECM + photoaging** regenerative treatment. The honest read: a **plausible but inconsistently-proven** texture/appearance effect, heavily confounded by the microneedling vehicle and by non-standardized preparation. Strongest *real* evidence in this modality is actually for **hair (androgenetic alopecia)**, not facial skin aging — kept here only for contrast.

## 4. Evidence review by tier  *(the core of this dossier)*

> **Critical framing:** there is **no at-home tier** to grade separately (PRP is inherently clinical). The split that matters here is **(a) facial skin rejuvenation — the brand-relevant endpoint, MIXED/WEAK** vs **(b) androgenetic alopecia — stronger, but off-topic.** And throughout: **protocol heterogeneity** (no standard PRP) is the dominant limitation.

### Tier 1 — Mechanistic / in-vitro / ex-vivo
- Platelet activation → **growth-factor release (PDGF, TGF-β, VEGF, EGF, IGF, bFGF)** is well-established biochemistry. In vitro, PRP/PRF lysates stimulate fibroblast proliferation and collagen gene expression. **PRF's fibrin matrix** demonstrably **slows GF release** vs PRP's burst (in-vitro release kinetics). *Limitation:* in-vitro fibroblast stimulation ≠ clinical wrinkle reduction; tells us the payload is bioactive, not that the facial outcome is real or durable.

### Tier 2 — Animal
- Scattered rodent/porcine wound-healing and hair-cycle models support angiogenesis and follicular stimulation. *Limitation:* not facial-aging models; small, heterogeneous; **[not the basis of any aesthetic claim — treat as unverified for skin rejuvenation].**

### Tier 3 — Human, lower-grade (open-label / uncontrolled / split-face uncontrolled / case series)
- **Hersant et al., 2021, *Aesthetic Surgery Journal*** (n=93, randomized into 3 arms: **PRP alone vs HA alone vs PRP+HA "Cellular Matrix"**, 3 sessions). PRP+HA gave the **largest** improvement in overall facial appearance (P<0.0001) and elasticity; **FACE-Q rose ~9–11% for PRP-alone** at 1–6 mo vs ~17–24% for the combination. *Limitations:* the strongest arm is the **combination** — PRP-alone effect is modest; HA is a known volumizer, so it **confounds** the PRP contribution; not blinded-rater objective imaging.
- **Tsai et al.** (split-face/self-controlled) — reported significant **periocular wrinkle** improvement 3→6 mo, ~20% modified Fitzpatrick wrinkle-score gain at 3 mo. *Limitations:* small, self-controlled, subjective scales.
- **Large appraisal denominator:** a 2021 systematic review (*CCID*, Hesseler-type appraisal) catalogued **36 studies / 3,172 patients** but found **80% used PRP in *combination*** (microneedling, CO₂ laser, HA, fat grafting, subcision) and only **25% as monotherapy** — i.e., most "PRP works" data **cannot isolate PRP**. Of 36 studies, only **20 reported the PRP volume used**, **7 reported platelet counts**, and **1 reported leukocyte count** — quantifying the standardization problem.

### Tier 4 — Human, controlled (RCT / vehicle- or sham-controlled / split-face randomized)  *(the tier that supports real claims)*
- **🔑 Alam et al., 2018, *JAMA Dermatology*** — the most rigorous facial-rejuvenation RCT. **Split-face, randomized, double-blind (participants + 2 masked dermatologist raters)**; PRP to one cheek vs **normal saline** to the other; single session, 3 mL intradermal; n=19 completers (27 enrolled). **Blinded raters found NO statistically significant difference** between PRP and saline on any photoaging subscore (fine lines, mottled pigmentation, roughness, sallowness) at 6 months. **Participants** rated the PRP side better for **texture (P=.02)** and **wrinkles (P=.03)** — a **placebo/perception gap**: subjects perceived improvement that blinded experts could not detect. *This is the single most important efficacy data point in the file: against a proper vehicle control, objective benefit vanished.* *Limitations:* small n, single session (proponents argue a course is needed), one preparation protocol.
- **Systematic reviews (facial):** multiple 2021–2025 reviews (e.g. *CCID* 2021; *Enfermería Clínica* 2025, PubMed 40118148) converge on the same verdict — PRP **may** improve wrinkles/texture/elasticity, but evidence is **low-level, heterogeneous, and lacks Level-I confirmation**; authors explicitly call for **"large-scale RCTs using a standardized PRP preparation."** Heterogeneity (I² high) and **no preparation standard** prevent meta-analysis of facial endpoints.
- **PRP vs PRF (facial/periorbital):** a 2024–25 systematic review of **PRP vs PRF for periorbital rejuvenation** found **both used, no robust evidence of one being clinically superior** for skin aging — PRF's sustained-release advantage remains **mechanistic, not clinically proven** for facial outcomes.
- **CONTRAST — Androgenetic alopecia (NOT skin aging, included for honest tiering):** **Kieling et al., 2024** (*Anais Bras. Dermatol.* / ScienceDirect) — systematic review + meta-analysis, **14 RCTs, 431 patients (13 in meta-analysis)**: pooled **+27.55 hairs/cm² (95% CI 14.04–41.06)** favoring PRP over placebo — **statistically significant**, but **I²≈96% (very high heterogeneity)**, publication bias, and **GRADE "low quality."** PRP+minoxidil also beats minoxidil alone in other meta-analyses. **So the modality's best controlled evidence is for HAIR, not facial skin** — and even that is graded low-quality due to protocol heterogeneity.

**What is proven vs borrowed.**
- **Proven (modest):** PRP releases bioactive growth factors (biochemistry, Tier 1). For **androgenetic alopecia**, multiple RCTs show a **real but heterogeneous, low-GRADE** hair-density benefit.
- **Mixed / weak (the brand-relevant claim):** **facial skin rejuvenation.** Open-label and some split-face studies are positive, but the **best blinded vehicle-controlled RCT (Alam 2018) showed no objective benefit over saline**, and the literature is dominated by **combination protocols** that can't isolate PRP from microneedling/HA/laser.
- **Borrowed / not proven:** "PRF > PRP for your face" (mechanistic only); "regenerates/rebuilds collagen" as a durable facial claim; any "skin longevity" framing. The biology is borrowed from wound-healing and from the (stronger) hair data.

## 5. Treatment parameters / protocol

- **"Dose" analogue (and its problem):** there is **no standardized dose.** Variables: blood volume drawn, spin speed/time (single vs double spin), **platelet concentration** (target often cited ~3–5× baseline, frequently unmeasured), **leukocyte content** (LR-PRP vs LP-PRP), **activation** (CaCl₂/thrombin or none), PRP vs PRF vs i-PRF, and **delivery** (microneedled vs injected vs both). This non-standardization is the root cause of the heterogeneous evidence (§4).
- **Course:** typically **3 sessions ~4–6 weeks apart**, then **maintenance every 6–12 months**. Single sessions (as in Alam 2018) are often called insufficient by proponents.
- **Operator skill:** **high and load-bearing** — and uniquely, **sterility/bloodborne-pathogen handling is part of the skill.** Venipuncture, centrifugation, and re-introduction of blood demand strict aseptic technique and single-use, labeled equipment. The New Mexico cluster (§6) is what happens when this fails.
- **Time-to-visible result:** weeks to ~3 months (collagen remodeling lag); subjective "glow" earlier (partly microneedling/edema).
- **Durability:** months; not permanent; maintenance needed.
- **Setting:** **clinic/provider-only.** No at-home protocol exists or is safe.

## 6. Safety

**🚨 THE CAUTIONARY TALE — the New Mexico HIV cluster (lead with this):**
- **CDC MMWR, 2024;73(16):372–376** (Investigation of Presumptive HIV Transmission Associated with PRP Microneedling Facials — New Mexico, **2018–2023**). At an **unlicensed Albuquerque spa ("VIP Spa")**, **five HIV infections** were linked to "vampire facials": **four female spa clients** who received PRP microneedling plus **one male sexual partner** of a client. Molecular/phylogenetic analysis showed all five HIV-1 (subtype B) sequences **"clustered together strongly in a monophyletic clade with high confidence"** — i.e., genetically linked, consistent with a **common source at the spa**.
- **The unsafe practices found (Sept 2018 inspection):** a **centrifuge, heating dry bath, and a rack of unlabeled tubes of blood on a kitchen counter**; **unlabeled blood and injectables stored in the kitchen fridge alongside food**; **unwrapped syringes** in drawers, on counters, and in regular trash; **no autoclave on the premises**; disposable tips "cleaned" with alcohol and **reused**. The likely vector: cross-contamination of one client's blood into another.
- **Scale of response:** **198 former clients/partners tested** (2018–2023); no further HIV (and no HBV/HCV) infections identified beyond the cluster. The spa **closed in fall 2018**.
- **Why it matters:** this is the **first documented HIV transmission via a cosmetic injection/microneedling service** in the US. It is a story about **non-sterile technique in an unlicensed setting**, not an intrinsic property of properly-performed autologous PRP — **but** it is the defining public-health fact of this modality and the strongest argument for "licensed provider only."

**Otherwise (properly performed, autologous):**
- **Low immunogenic/allergic risk** — it's the patient's **own** blood, so no foreign-material hypersensitivity, no graft rejection. This is PRP/PRF's genuine safety advantage over synthetic injectables.
- **Common/expected:** injection-site **pain, bruising, swelling, erythema**, pinpoint bleeding (microneedling channels), transient tenderness — usually days.
- **Real risks if poorly done:** **infection** (local; or — as above — **bloodborne-pathogen transmission** with reused/contaminated equipment), **vascular occlusion/bruising** from injection, PIH at needling sites (skin of color), and rare granulomatous/nodular reactions. Microneedling-vehicle risks apply (see [`microneedling.md`](microneedling.md), incl. granuloma risk if *other* topicals are introduced into channels).
- **Contraindications:** active infection/HSV at site, platelet/bleeding disorders, anticoagulation, active malignancy (relative — GF concern), pregnancy (precautionary [unverified]), and **any non-sterile/unlicensed setting** (the operative contraindication from the cluster).

## 7. Regulatory & claims

- **Biologic line (US):** PRP/PRF is an **autologous human cell/tissue product (HCT/P)** generally regulated under **PHS Act §361 / 21 CFR Part 1271** as **minimally manipulated, same-surgical-procedure** material — meaning the **PRP itself is largely outside premarket FDA approval**, and **all aesthetic use is "off-label."** There is **no FDA approval of PRP for facial rejuvenation.**
- **The preparation devices** (centrifuge/PRP kits) **are FDA-cleared (510(k))** — but typically for **preparing PRP to mix with bone graft / orthopedic use or general blood handling**, *not* for an aesthetic indication. Marketing them for facial rejuvenation is off-label.
- **"Vampire Facial®"** is a **trademark** (Charles Runels / Cellular Medicine Association), licensed to providers — a branding/IP fact, not a regulatory clearance.
- **What a cosmetic brand may claim:** essentially **nothing about performing or replicating PRP/PRF.** It is a **medical procedure** (venipuncture + injection/needling of blood). A topical skincare brand:
  - **must not** sell, bundle, or instruct PRP/PRF; **must not** call any product "PRP," "plasma," or "growth-factor injection";
  - **must not** imply a topical reproduces an injected/needled autologous growth-factor effect (drug/biologic-territory claim needing substantiation/clearance);
  - **may** *educate* (what PRP/PRF is, what the evidence shows, the safety story) and may position a barrier-support cream as **post-procedure aftercare** on **intact, healed** skin — never as a substitute, and never to be introduced into needling channels.
- All such language needs substantiation/legal review per [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md). **Cross-ref:** topical "growth factor" *cosmetics* are a separate question handled in [`../ingredients/growth-factors.md`](../ingredients/growth-factors.md) (verdict there: **Lean SKIP / C-grade**, industry-funded evidence) — PRP is *not* a route to those claims either.

## 8. Cost & accessibility / market

- **Cost per session:** PRP facial / "vampire facial" commonly **~$500–$1,800**, with full PRP-facial protocols quoted **~$1,500–$2,500/session**; a starter course is usually **2–3 sessions**. Injectable "vampire facelift" (PRP±HA) sits at the higher end. **Out-of-pocket, recurring.**
- **Market:** the broad **PRP market ≈ $0.73B (2025), → ~$1.3B by 2030 (~12% CAGR)** across mainstream forecasts (dermatology/aesthetics is the largest application segment, ~22% share). The American Society of Plastic Surgeons reported a **~47% jump in PRP-based cosmetic sessions in 2024** — demand is real and growing, evidence quality notwithstanding.
- **Adjacency note:** PRP/PRF is a **provider-delivered biologic procedure** — capital (centrifuge), licensure (venipuncture/medical), and **bloodborne-pathogen liability** put it **entirely outside a topical brand's lane.** The only realistic adjacency is **education + optional clinic partnership + barrier-repair aftercare positioning** — not manufacturing or bundling.

## 9. Hype vs evidence

- **Overruns the data:** "regenerates/rebuilds your skin with your own growth factors," "natural facelift," "collagen banking," "skin longevity," and **"PRF is better than PRP for your face"** (the last is mechanistic in-vitro reasoning, **not** a proven clinical-superiority finding for facial aging).
- **The placebo gap:** the celebrity-driven "vampire facial" glow is real to *patients* but, in the best blinded RCT, **invisible to masked dermatologists vs saline** — much of the perceived benefit may be the **microneedling vehicle + expectation**, not the PRP.
- **Confounding everywhere:** ~80% of the supportive literature pairs PRP with microneedling/HA/laser, so "PRP works" frequently means "PRP-plus-something works."
- **Genuinely supported:** (1) the **biochemistry** (platelets release GFs); (2) **autologous = low immunogenic risk**; (3) **androgenetic alopecia** hair-density benefit (multiple RCTs, but **low GRADE / high heterogeneity**). Facial rejuvenation is the **weakest-supported** of the common claims.
- **Underplayed:** **protocol non-standardization** (no two PRPs are the same) and the **bloodborne-infection risk in non-sterile/unlicensed settings** (the New Mexico cluster).

## 10. Brand relevance & strategic verdict

- **Interaction with our thesis:** PRP/PRF attempts the thing a topical routine structurally can't — **deliver growth factors past the barrier into the dermis** — via a medical procedure. Versus a topical barrier-repair routine it is therefore best read as a **complement, not a competitor or substitute**: an occasional clinic procedure a customer might add, with the brand's product playing **post-procedure barrier/aftercare** on healed skin and **daily maintenance** between sessions. It does **not** threaten the topical thesis; it sits in a different (biologic/procedural) intervention class — and its **own facial-aging evidence is weaker than marketing implies**, which the brand can say honestly.
- **Link to the brand's growth-factor stance (cross-ref):** This reinforces [`../ingredients/growth-factors.md`](../ingredients/growth-factors.md). If **topical** growth-factor cosmetics are only **"Lean SKIP / C-grade"** (industry-funded, small effects), then **injected/needled autologous** growth factors are **not a credibility shortcut either** — the best blinded facial RCT was **null vs saline**. The consistent, on-brand position: *growth factors are biologically interesting but clinically oversold for facial aging, whether in a jar or in a syringe.*
- **Product-adjacency call:** **📚 context/education-only.** Rationale: it's an **off-label, provider-only autologous biologic** with **non-standardized protocols**, **mixed/weak facial-rejuvenation evidence**, and a **documented HIV-transmission cluster** in an unlicensed setting. A topical brand **cannot sell, bundle, or claim** it, manufacturing/licensure is out of lane, and any "growth-factor regeneration" borrow is a regulatory and scientific non-starter. The honest, on-brand move is **trustworthy education** (what PRP/PRF really does, the placebo gap, the safety story) plus optional **aftercare/maintenance** positioning for a barrier-repair cream — explicitly *not* a substitute claim.
- **Content/SEO angle:** *"Does the 'vampire facial' (PRP/PRF) actually work — and is it safe?"* An evidence-tiered, risk-honest explainer (facial aging: mixed, null vs saline in the best blinded trial; hair: better but low-quality; safety: low immune risk **but** the New Mexico HIV cluster shows why "licensed provider only" is non-negotiable) builds exactly the science-credibility the brand trades on, and naturally bridges to "what to use to support your skin between/after procedures."
- Log the call in [`../../ideas/considering.md`](../../ideas/considering.md) (education/content asset + possible clinic-partnership exploration) — **not** as a product to build or bundle.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-21. Independent re-check of headline claims against primary sources.

### Overall evidence grade: **C** (facial skin rejuvenation — the brand-relevant endpoint)
Weak/indirect, mechanism-led, and **mixed** controlled data: the biochemistry is real, several open-label/split-face studies are positive, but the **single best blinded vehicle-controlled RCT (Alam 2018) found no objective benefit over saline**, ~80% of supportive studies confound PRP with microneedling/HA/laser, and there is **no standardized preparation protocol** to pool. Not D, because a genuine bioactive mechanism and some positive (if low-grade) controlled signals exist; not B, because the rigorous blinded facial data is null and heterogeneity blocks meta-analysis.
- **Androgenetic alopecia (off-topic, graded separately): B−.** Multiple RCTs and a 14-RCT meta-analysis show a real hair-density gain (+27.55 hairs/cm²), but **I²≈96%** and **GRADE "low"** keep it below B.
- **At-home consumer claim: N/A → F.** No at-home PRP exists or is safe; any "PRP/plasma in a jar" is unsupported.

### Citation audit (do the studies exist and say what's claimed?)
- **CDC MMWR 2024;73(16):372–376** (5 infections incl. 1 partner; kitchen-counter centrifuge/unlabeled blood; no autoclave; monophyletic HIV clade; 198 tested): **verified** via CDC MMWR page + PMC + CBS/WaPo corroboration. Numbers and unsafe-practice details quoted from the primary report.
- **Alam 2018 JAMA Dermatol** (split-face, n=19 completers, PRP vs saline, blinded raters null, patients perceived texture/wrinkle gains P=.02/.03): **verified** (JAMA + PubMed). The "null vs saline objectively, positive subjectively" discordance is accurately represented.
- **CCID 2021 appraisal** (36 studies/3,172 pts; 80% combination; volume reported in 20, platelets in 7, leukocytes in 1): **verified** (PMC). Standardization-deficit figures quoted from the source.

### Main caveats
1. **Protocol heterogeneity is the master limitation.** "PRP" is not one thing — platelet concentration, leukocytes, activation, PRP vs PRF, and delivery all vary and are often unreported. This alone justifies a conservative grade and blocks meta-analysis of facial endpoints. **(Lead caveat.)**
2. **The HIV cluster.** Real, CDC-documented, first-of-its-kind — but it reflects **non-sterile, unlicensed practice**, not intrinsic autologous PRP risk. State both halves honestly: low immune risk *when done right*, catastrophic infection risk *when done wrong*.
3. **The placebo/perception gap.** Best blinded RCT = null vs saline objectively, positive subjectively. Much "it works" may be microneedling + expectation.
4. **Confounding.** ~80% of supportive studies bundle PRP with other modalities (microneedling/HA/laser) — can't isolate PRP.
5. **Hair ≠ face.** The strongest controlled evidence is for alopecia, not facial aging; don't let it halo the skin claim. Tier 2 (animal) and exact regulatory IDs are **[unverified]** and labeled.

### Claims that should be softened
- "Regenerates/rebuilds your skin with your own growth factors" → "delivers autologous growth factors; **facial-rejuvenation evidence is mixed and was null vs saline in the best blinded trial.**"
- "Natural facelift / proven anti-aging" → "modest, inconsistent texture/appearance effects; not a facelift; durability and effect size are uncertain."
- "PRF is better than PRP for your face" → "PRF releases growth factors more slowly **in vitro**; **clinical superiority for facial aging is not proven.**"
- "Safe because it's your own blood" → "**low immunologic risk**, but **not risk-free** — bloodborne-infection transmission has occurred with non-sterile equipment (CDC, New Mexico); **licensed provider only.**"
- Any topical/product claiming to be or replicate PRP/"plasma"/"growth-factor injection" → **prohibited**; biologic/drug territory.

**Net:** PRP/PRF ("vampire facial") is a real autologous biologic with genuine growth-factor biochemistry and a real **low-immunogenicity** advantage — but for **facial skin aging** the controlled evidence is **mixed and weak (grade C)**: the best blinded RCT was **null versus saline**, the literature is confounded by microneedling/HA, and there is **no standardized protocol**. Its strongest evidence is for **hair (alopecia, B−, still low-GRADE)**, not skin. The defining safety fact is the **CDC-documented New Mexico HIV cluster** — a non-sterile, unlicensed-setting catastrophe that makes "licensed provider only" non-negotiable.
