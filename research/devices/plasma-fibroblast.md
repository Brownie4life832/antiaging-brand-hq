# Device / Procedure: Plasma Skin Treatments — "Fibroblast" Plasma Pens & Nitrogen/Helium Plasma Resurfacing

**Researched:** 2026-06-21 · **Status:** ☑ context-only / ☑ avoid (as a build/bundle)
**Category:** in-clinic device / device-driven procedure (a low-end "at-home/spa pen" tier exists and is the most dangerous part)
**Regulatory class (US):** **Two very different stories — keep them separate.**
- **Plasma "fibroblast" pens (Plamere, Subnovii, Plaxpot, generic AliExpress pens):** electrosurgical class; cleared (where cleared at all) for *removal of benign skin lesions / electrocoagulation* — **NOT** FDA-cleared for the marketed skin-tightening / "non-surgical blepharoplasty" use. Many units sold online are **uncleared** and used by **unlicensed operators**.
- **Nitrogen plasma resurfacing (Portrait PSR / Rhytec, NeoGen):** FDA-cleared device for skin resurfacing/treatment of rhytides — "first non-laser" resurfacing clearance.
- **Helium plasma + RF (Renuvion / J-Plasma, Apyx):** originally cleared only for **general-surgical** cutting/coagulation; FDA issued a **2022 safety communication** against off-label aesthetic use, then cleared two specific handpieces for narrow aesthetic indications.

> Scope note: Exhaustive, citation-backed evidence review for the brand's research base. Evidence separated strictly by
> tier (mechanistic → animal → uncontrolled human → controlled human). **This is a HIGH-CAUTION modality.** The cheap
> "fibroblast pen" end is one of the most overhyped, under-evidenced, and injury-prone things in aesthetics; the
> clinic-grade nitrogen/helium plasma end is a real, regulated resurfacing technology with thin-but-genuine data.
> Do not let the second lend credibility to the first. Claims discipline per [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md) and
> [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 1. Identity

- **What it is:** Two distinct families that both use "plasma" (ionized gas) but differ enormously in device grade, evidence, and risk:
  1. **Plasma "fibroblast" pens (a.k.a. plasma exeresis / fibroblast therapy / "plasma pen"):** a handheld probe creates a tiny electrical **arc/spark** that jumps to the skin, producing a grid of **superficial thermal carbonized dots ("sublimation")** on the *epidermis*. Marketed for skin tightening, especially upper-eyelid hooding ("non-surgical blepharoplasty"), neck, and "fibroblast lift." Heavily promoted on TikTok; frequently performed by aestheticians/lay operators.
  2. **Plasma energy / nitrogen-plasma resurfacing (Plasma Skin Regeneration, PSR):** a clinic device (Rhytec **Portrait PSR3**, now **NeoGen**) ionizes **nitrogen gas** into a plasma plume delivered in millisecond pulses — a controlled, *non-contact*, full-field ablative/sub-ablative resurfacing comparable in role to a medium-depth peel or fractional laser.
  3. **Subdermal helium plasma + RF (Renuvion / J-Plasma, Apyx Medical):** helium ionized at the tip of an RF probe, applied **under the skin** (often with liposuction) for soft-tissue contraction/"tightening."
- **Modality family:** energy (thermal plasma — arc-based contact for pens; nitrogen/helium plasma plume for resurfacing).
- **Key forms & representative brands/devices:**
  - *Pens (lay/spa tier):* Plamere, **Subnovii** (Bovie/marketed as higher-end, low-frequency), Plaxpot, plus generic uncleared pens.
  - *Nitrogen PSR (clinic):* Rhytec **Portrait PSR1/PSR2/PSR3**; successor **NeoGen** (Energist).
  - *Helium plasma + RF (clinic/surgical):* **Renuvion / J-Plasma** (Apyx Medical).
- **The specific named products called out:** Plamere, Fibroblast/"plasma pen," Portrait/Rhytec, J-Plasma/Renuvion — all covered.

## 2. Mechanism of action

**Plasma fibroblast pen — plausible mechanism:** a high-voltage arc ionizes the air gap between probe tip and skin, depositing heat at discrete points. Each dot **sublimates/carbonizes the stratum corneum and upper epidermis** (a controlled micro-burn), causing immediate tissue contraction ("shrink-wrap" of surface skin) and triggering a wound-healing cascade that — *in theory* — drives dermal fibroblast activation and neocollagenesis. **Proven in-skin effect:** the injury and immediate epidermal contraction are real and visible; the deeper *dermal* remodeling claimed (the "fibroblast" in the name) is largely **inferred from wound-healing biology, not demonstrated by controlled dermal histology** for these consumer pens. Depth is operator- and device-dependent and poorly standardized.

**Nitrogen plasma resurfacing — mechanism:** nitrogen gas is converted to plasma by an ultra-high-frequency generator and delivered in millisecond pulses *without touching the skin*. Energy heats tissue uniformly to a controlled depth, leaving the **desiccated epidermis intact as a natural biologic dressing** while the dermis below remodels — driving re-epithelialization, **reduction of solar elastosis, and a new band of dermal collagen at the dermal–epidermal junction** (this IS shown on histology). Energy tiers: PSR1 (low, 1–1.2 J, series), PSR2 (single high pass, 3–4 J), PSR3 (two high passes).

**Helium plasma + RF (Renuvion):** ionized helium delivered at an RF probe tip produces rapid, focused heating for **subdermal** soft-tissue coagulation/contraction; the helium allows tissue heating then rapid cooling. Mechanism is thermal collagen contraction, applied under the skin rather than on its surface.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Targeted? | Strength / where the evidence comes from |
|---|---|---|
| Collagen/ECM loss | ◑ Partly (clinic plasma); ✗ (pens, unproven) | Nitrogen PSR shows new DEJ collagen band & reduced solar elastosis on histology (Bogle/Kilmer/Foster). Pens: claimed, not shown. |
| Photoaging (rhytides, texture, laxity) | ◑ Partly | Best-supported endpoint for **nitrogen PSR** (RCT + open studies). Pens: low-grade case series only. |
| Pigmentation / tone | ◑ Mixed | PSR can *improve* dyschromia; but **both** modalities frequently *cause* PIH — net risk, esp. Fitzpatrick III–VI. |
| Barrier decline | ✗ (and worsens acutely) | Procedure *deliberately injures* the epidermis; barrier is transiently destroyed and must heal. Opposite of a barrier-repair thesis. |
| Hydration / water-loss | ✗ | Acute TEWL increase during healing. |
| Oxidative stress | ✗ | Not a mechanism; thermal injury is pro-inflammatory acutely. |
| Glycation / AGEs | ✗ | No relevant mechanism. |
| Cellular senescence | ✗ [unverified] | No human data showing senolytic/senescence effect. |
| Mitochondrial decline | ✗ | No relevant mechanism. |
| Inflammaging | ✗ (acutely pro-inflammatory) | Controlled inflammation is the *mechanism*; not an anti-inflammaging tool. |
| Microbiome | ✗ | Not studied; open wounds raise infection risk. |

**Net:** plausibly touches collagen/ECM and photoaging **for the clinic-grade nitrogen/helium devices**; for the consumer "fibroblast pen," the hallmark mapping is mostly **aspirational**.

## 4. Evidence review by tier *(core of this dossier)*

### Tier 1 — Mechanistic / in-vitro / ex-vivo
- Plasma-tissue interaction is well characterized physically (ionized gas → controlled thermal deposition). Histologic *ex-vivo/biopsy* work (below) sits at the human-tissue boundary. No need to over-weight in-vitro here.

### Tier 2 — Animal
- **Holcomb & Schucker, *Lasers in Surgery and Medicine*, 2019 — porcine model (n = 4 mini-pigs).** Compared **helium plasma (Bovie J-Plasma)** vs **nitrogen PSR (Portrait PSR3)**. Nitrogen produced deeper acute injury (~404 µm below basement membrane vs ~222–281 µm for helium); helium produced **greater acute tissue contraction**. At 30 days both showed epidermal hyperplasia and regenerative granulation tissue/dermal remodeling. *Limitations:* animal model, tiny n, contraction variable between animals, no aesthetic-outcome endpoint. **Establishes mechanism and depth, not clinical benefit.**

### Tier 3 — Human, lower-grade (open-label / uncontrolled / case series)
**Nitrogen plasma (clinic):**
- **Kilmer et al., 2007 (Portrait PSR3 full-face pilot, two-site, prospective, uncontrolled).** Single high-energy pass; **mean ~50% improvement by 1 month**; likened to a medium-depth chemical peel with modest tightening. *Limitations:* uncontrolled, investigator-assessed, small.
- **Potter et al. / Bogle et al. (open studies).** ~**39% reduction in fine-line depth at 6 months** (one high-energy full-face treatment); low-energy series ~**37% improvement** in facial lines at 3 months with **patient self-rated ~68%** overall improvement. Biopsies: reduced solar elastosis, new interdigitating collagen, ~72 µm mean new-collagen depth. *Limitations:* open-label, sponsor-linked era, self-report inflation.

**Plasma "fibroblast" pen / plasma exeresis (clinic-ish, but low-grade):**
- **Ferreira et al., *J Cosmet Dermatol*, 2021 — upper-eyelid plasma exeresis (observational, n = 16).** Dermatochalasis treated; satisfaction peaked at day 7 then **declined**, and the authors flagged **"overall patient satisfaction is questionable"** given low willingness to repeat. *Limitations:* tiny, observational, no control, short follow-up, subjective endpoints.
- **Prospective series of upper blepharoplasty with plasma blade (n = 25).** Reported feasibility/satisfaction. *Limitations:* uncontrolled, small, surgeon-reported.
- The bulk of "fibroblast pen" evidence is **before/after photos, vendor case collections, and social-media content** — not study-grade.

### Tier 4 — Human, controlled (RCT / sham- or split-face randomized) *(the tier that supports real claims)*
- **Theppornpitak et al., *J Cosmet Dermatol*, 2019 — nitrogen plasma for mild-to-moderate periorbital wrinkles. Prospective, randomized, controlled, evaluator-blinded, split-face (n = 18).** Treated side got 3 nitrogen-plasma sessions at 3-week intervals vs untreated contralateral control. Treated side showed **significantly better** Lemperle wrinkle score, Visioscan SEw wrinkle score, and Mexameter melanin index, plus higher satisfaction (all p ≤ 0.004). *Limitations:* **n = 18, single center, short follow-up, the "control" was untreated (no sham), one anatomic site, modest absolute effect.* This is the **single best-quality study** in the whole modality.
- **Renuvion (helium plasma + RF):** clearance was supported by manufacturer pivotal studies (the basis of the May/July 2022 510(k)s for wrinkles and neck/submental laxity), but **independent RCT evidence remains thin**; much of the aesthetic literature is retrospective case review.
- **Plasma "fibroblast" pens:** **no credible RCT** demonstrating the marketed tightening benefit. This is the central honesty point.

**What is proven vs borrowed:**
- *Proven (modest):* nitrogen plasma resurfacing improves periorbital/facial rhytides and induces real dermal collagen — supported by **one small RCT + several open/histology studies**.
- *Plausible but under-proven:* helium-plasma (Renuvion) subdermal tightening — cleared on manufacturer data, weak independent RCT base.
- *Largely borrowed/aspirational:* the consumer **"fibroblast pen"** trades on the credibility of clinic plasma devices and on generic wound-healing biology, **without** controlled evidence for its own claims. The "fibroblast/collagen" branding is essentially **borrowed legitimacy**.

## 5. Treatment parameters / protocol
- **Fibroblast pen:** grid of carbonized dots over target area; topical anesthetic pre-treat (a known hazard — see §6); 7–14 day visible crust/scab phase; results claimed to build over 8–12 weeks; often 1–3 sessions. Operator skill is **everything** and is wildly variable; no standardized energy dosimetry across cheap devices.
- **Nitrogen PSR:** non-contact plasma passes; **PSR1** low-energy (1–1.2 J) series with ~3–4 day downtime; **PSR2/PSR3** high-energy (3–4 J) single/double pass with 5–10 days peeling and erythema that can persist 6–8 weeks; results immediate and progressive over ~1 year. Clinic-only, physician/trained-provider operated.
- **Renuvion:** intra-operative/subdermal, surgeon-administered, usually with liposuction; single session.
- **Durability:** PSR improvements reported up to ≥1 year; fibroblast-pen durability poorly documented (vendors cite "2–3 years," **[unverified]** by controlled data).

## 6. Safety  *(lead concern for this modality)*
**This is one of the higher-risk aesthetic categories, driven mostly by the consumer/spa pen tier.**
- **Burns & scarring:** the procedure IS a controlled burn; over-treatment → second-degree burns and **permanent scars**. Documented: **Goulliart et al., *Cureus*, 2024** — a 44-yo woman got second-degree burns on both breasts from an esthetician using a PlasmaPen for "breast tightening" seen on social media; **6 months of treatment, permanent erythematous/dyschromic scars, no benefit.**
- **Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH) & dyspigmentation:** common, especially **Fitzpatrick III–VI**; the dotted application pattern can leave a **grid of hyper-/hypopigmented marks**. Multiple sources flag elevated PIH/scarring risk in darker skin — a major equity/safety limiter.
- **Anesthetic-related injury:** **Wong et al. (PMC7447566)** — bilateral **chemical eye injury from EMLA cream** during periocular plasma fibroblast treatment. Periocular use near the globe is inherently hazardous.
- **Infection** (open micro-wounds), prolonged erythema/edema, and **nerve damage** have been reported with aggressive/untrained use.
- **Operator/training problem:** widely performed by **non-medical operators**; **Hernandez et al., *J Cosmet Dermatol*, 2022** analyzed fibroblast-pen content on TikTok and flagged frequent lay use, unregulated devices, and unsubstantiated claims.
- **Device-quality/counterfeit:** many pens sold online are **uncleared/illegally marketed**; uncontrolled arc/energy → unpredictable injury.
- **Who should not use it / DIY:** **at-home use is strongly contraindicated.** Darker skin types, keloid-prone, periocular self-treatment, pregnancy, active infection, isotretinoin — avoid. Clinic nitrogen/helium plasma is far safer but still has real downtime and PIH risk and must be provider-administered.

## 7. Regulatory & claims
- **Fibroblast pens:** Where cleared, clearance is typically for **removal/coagulation of benign skin lesions (electrosurgery)** — **NOT** for cosmetic skin tightening or "non-surgical blepharoplasty." The FDA "has not approved the PlasmaPen for aesthetic purposes" (Goulliart 2024). Marketing it for tightening is **off-label/uncleared**, and selling pens for home/unlicensed use raises **illegal-marketing** concerns. Vendor "FDA cleared" badges usually refer to a *lesion-removal* clearance, not the advertised use.
- **Nitrogen PSR (Portrait):** FDA-cleared resurfacing device (marketed as "first non-laser" resurfacing technology). Legitimate clinic device.
- **Renuvion / J-Plasma (Apyx):** originally cleared only for **general surgical cutting/coagulation/ablation**. **FDA Safety Communication (March 2022)** warned consumers and providers **against** using it for **dermal resurfacing or skin contraction**, citing **serious and potentially life-threatening adverse events** from off-label aesthetic use. FDA then cleared **specific handpieces**: **Renuvion Dermal Handpiece (May 25, 2022)** for moderate–severe wrinkles/rhytides, **limited to Fitzpatrick I–III**; **Renuvion APR Handpiece (July 15, 2022)** for subcutaneous improvement of lax skin in the **neck/submental** area. Use outside those cleared indications is off-label.
- **What a cosmetic brand may claim (if it ever touched this):** essentially nothing favorable without crossing lines. A topical brand must not imply its product "tightens like" plasma, "stimulates fibroblasts" as a device does, or pair products with an uncleared pen. Educational/comparative content is fine **if** it states the evidence and safety limits honestly. Any device sale/bundle would pull the brand into **medical-device regulation, training liability, and adverse-event exposure.** See [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md) claims rules.

## 8. Cost & accessibility / market
- **Fibroblast pens:** cheap and accessible — that's the danger. Generic pens **$30–$300** online; spa "fibroblast" sessions roughly **$300–$1,000+** per area. Low barrier to entry fuels the untrained-operator problem.
- **Nitrogen PSR / Renuvion:** clinic procedures, roughly **$1,000–$4,000+** per treatment/area depending on device and extent; physician-supervised.
- **Market:** energy-based "non-surgical tightening" is a fast-growing aesthetics segment; the **fibroblast-pen niche is largely social-media-driven** and recurrently flagged by dermatologists/regulators rather than a credible premium category. Manufacturers: Apyx (Renuvion), Energist (NeoGen), various OEMs (pens).

## 9. Hype vs evidence
- **Biggest overclaim:** "non-surgical blepharoplasty / fibroblast lift" from a consumer pen — sold as a permanent, surgery-equivalent eyelid lift. Reality: **no RCT support**, the one observational eyelid study showed **declining/"questionable" satisfaction**, and the periocular site carries serious risk.
- **"Stimulates your fibroblasts to rebuild collagen"** — true mechanism for clinic-grade plasma resurfacing (shown on histology); for the cheap pen it is **mechanism-borrowing without device-specific proof.**
- **"FDA cleared"** badges — usually a lesion-removal clearance misrepresented as endorsement of cosmetic tightening.
- **Genuinely supported:** nitrogen plasma resurfacing produces modest, real improvement in periorbital/facial wrinkles and dermal collagen (one small RCT + open/histology data). Renuvion has narrow cleared aesthetic indications. Everything beyond that, especially in the consumer tier, outruns the data.

## 10. Brand relevance & strategic verdict
- **Interaction with our thesis:** **Substitutes for / opposes** a topical barrier-repair routine in mechanism — it works by **deliberately damaging the epidermal barrier** to force remodeling, the inverse of "protect and repair the barrier." It does not complement a gentle, barrier-first regimen; if anything, post-procedure skin *needs* exactly the kind of bland barrier-repair recovery cream the brand could make. That's a content/positioning bridge, not a product to sell.
- **Product-adjacency call:** **❌ avoid (build/bundle)** + **📚 context/education-only.** One line: the clinic-grade end is a regulated medical procedure outside a topical brand's lane, and the consumer "fibroblast pen" end is an **uncleared, injury-prone, hype-driven category we should not touch or be associated with** — but it's a high-search topic worth answering honestly.
- **Content/SEO angle:** "Are plasma pens / fibroblast treatments safe and do they work?" — the brand can be the **trustworthy, evidence-led voice** that separates clinic nitrogen-plasma resurfacing (modest real data, provider-only) from cheap at-home/spa fibroblast pens (uncleared, real burn/scar/PIH risk, FDA warnings), and positions a **post-procedure barrier-recovery routine** as the supportive role topicals actually play.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-21. Independent re-check of headline claims against primary sources.

### Overall evidence grade: **D overall** — split:
- **Plasma "fibroblast" pen (consumer/spa tightening claim): F–D.** No RCT for the marketed benefit; only small observational series (one of which reported *declining/"questionable"* satisfaction) plus before/after marketing. Evidence is essentially anecdotal.
- **Nitrogen plasma resurfacing (Portrait/NeoGen, periorbital/facial rhytides): C+ (approaching B).** One small randomized evaluator-blinded split-face RCT (n = 18) plus several open-label and histology studies showing real but modest collagen/wrinkle effects. Single-center, small, short follow-up.
- **Helium plasma + RF (Renuvion, neck/wrinkle laxity): C.** FDA-cleared for narrow indications on manufacturer data; independent controlled evidence thin.

### Citation audit (do the studies exist and say what's claimed?)
- Theppornpitak 2019 RCT — **verified** (J Cosmet Dermatol 18(1):163–168; randomized, controlled, evaluator-blinded, split-face, n=18; significant wrinkle/melanin/satisfaction improvement, p≤0.004). Real and as described.
- Holcomb & Schucker 2019 porcine helium-vs-nitrogen — **verified** (depths and contraction figures as cited).
- Goulliart 2024 *Cureus* PlasmaPen breast-burn case — **verified** (2nd-degree burns, permanent scarring, FDA-not-approved-for-aesthetics statement).
- Ferreira 2021 eyelid plasma exeresis (n=16, "satisfaction questionable") — **verified**.
- FDA Renuvion communication + May/July 2022 handpiece clearances — **verified** via FDA Roundup; the primary FDA safety-comm URL returned 404/403 on direct fetch but content is corroborated by the FDA Roundup mirror and multiple secondary sources. Recommend re-confirming the exact handpiece-clearance dates against the live FDA page before publishing any consumer-facing claim.
- Kilmer 2007 / Potter / Bogle PSR open studies — **verified via secondary aggregation** (Skin Therapy Letter); the ~50% / ~39% / ~37% figures are from those secondary summaries, not re-read from each primary paper — treat percentages as approximate. **[partially second-hand]**

### Main caveats (safety/regulatory first)
1. **Safety is the headline.** Documented burns, permanent dyschromic scarring, periocular chemical injury (from anesthetic), infection, and a high PIH rate in Fitzpatrick III–VI. The cheap-pen + untrained-operator + social-media pipeline is the core hazard.
2. **Regulatory:** consumer fibroblast pens are **not FDA-cleared for the marketed tightening use** (and home sale may be illegal-marketing); Renuvion carries an explicit FDA aesthetic-use warning with only narrow subsequent clearances; only nitrogen PSR is a straightforwardly cleared resurfacing device.
3. **Evidence asymmetry:** do not let nitrogen-PSR's modest real data launder the pen tier's near-absent data — they share a word, not a body of evidence.
4. Best clinical study (Theppornpitak) is still n=18, single-site, no sham, one anatomic area.

### Claims that should be softened
- "Plasma pens tighten skin / non-surgical eye lift" → "marketed for tightening; **no controlled evidence** supports the consumer-pen claim, and it carries real burn/scar/pigment risk."
- "Stimulates fibroblasts / rebuilds collagen" (consumer pen) → only demonstrated for **clinic-grade nitrogen plasma**, not consumer pens.
- "FDA cleared" (pens) → clarify it refers to **lesion removal/electrosurgery**, not cosmetic tightening.
- Any durability claim ("lasts 2–3 years") for pens → **[unverified]**.

**Net:** Two things wearing one name. Clinic **nitrogen/helium plasma resurfacing** is a legitimate, regulated, modestly-evidenced procedure (grade ~C) outside a topical brand's lane. The viral **"fibroblast plasma pen"** is, on the current record, an **uncleared, injury-prone, hype-driven category (grade F–D)** with FDA warnings and real scarring/PIH harm — the brand should **avoid any build/bundle** and use it purely as an **education topic**, positioning honest barrier-recovery support rather than the device. Trustworthiness of the favorable claims: low for pens, moderate-but-thin for clinic plasma.
