# Device / Procedure: Polynucleotides / PDRN injectables ("salmon DNA skin boosters")

**Researched:** 2026-06-21 · **Status:** ☑️ product-adjacency candidate (TOPICAL only) / ☑️ context-only (injectable)
**Category:** injectable (provider-administered skin booster) — plus a separate **topical cosmetic** adjacency (PDRN serums)
**Regulatory class (US):** Injectable PN/PDRN = **NOT FDA-approved/cleared** for aesthetic use (not on FDA's dermal-filler list) — *flag clearly*. Topical PDRN = **cosmetic** (no premarket approval; sold as "sodium DNA" / "hydrolyzed DNA").

> Scope note: Exhaustive, citation-backed evidence review. Evidence separated strictly by tier. The headline
> issue for THIS brand is the **injectable-vs-topical split**: the strong(er) data is for *injection*; the
> product a cosmetic brand could actually sell is a *topical serum*, where the evidence is far thinner and a
> real molecular-penetration objection applies. The two are graded separately. Claims discipline per

## 1. Identity

- **What it is:** Polynucleotides (PN) and polydeoxyribonucleotide (PDRN) are purified DNA fragments extracted from the gonadal tissue of salmon/trout (sperm → PDRN; testes → longer-chain PN). Injected intradermally as a "skin booster" to improve skin quality — elasticity, hydration, fine lines, texture — rather than to add volume like a filler. A parallel **topical** category (PDRN serums/ampoules) applies the same molecule to the skin surface in a leave-on cosmetic.
- **Modality family:** injectable (regenerative "biostimulator") · biologic-derived · + topical cosmetic active.
- **PN vs PDRN:** PN = longer chains, higher MW, more viscous/gel-like (structural/rheological support, hydration scaffold). PDRN = shorter chains (~50–1,500 kDa), lower viscosity, more "pharmacologically active" via the receptor pathway. Marketing uses the terms loosely; clinically they overlap. (Kim ST, *Pharmaceutics* 2025.)
- **Representative brands/devices:** **Rejuran** (PharmaResearch, Korea — the category-defining injectable; see teardown), **Nucleofill** (Promoitalia, Italy), **HP Cell Vitaran** (BR Pharm). Topical side: Rejuran Cosmetics, INKEY List PDRN Serum, plus a flood of K-beauty "salmon DNA" serums.
- **Specific products to cover:** Rejuran injectable + Rejuran/INKEY/K-beauty topical PDRN serums.

## 2. Mechanism of action

Two pathways, both first characterised in **wound-healing/tissue-repair** literature, then ported to aesthetics:

1. **A2A adenosine-receptor agonism (the signature mechanism).** PDRN fragments selectively activate the **adenosine A2A receptor** on fibroblasts, endothelial cells and macrophages → Gs-protein → adenylate cyclase → cAMP. Downstream: fibroblast proliferation, **VEGF upregulation → angiogenesis**, type-I collagen synthesis, and an **anti-inflammatory** shift (modulation of pro-inflammatory cytokines). (Squadrito et al., *Curr Pharm Des* 2017; Kim ST 2025.)
2. **Salvage pathway.** Enzymatic breakdown yields nucleosides/nucleotides that cells reincorporate into DNA synthesis — a "raw materials" effect supporting repair.
- **PN's extra role:** acts as a **structural/rheological/hydrating scaffold** (water-binding ECM-friendly gel) independent of receptor signalling.

**Plausible mechanism vs proven in-skin effect:** The A2A→fibroblast→collagen cascade is well-evidenced *in vitro and in wound models*. Translation to a measurable aesthetic outcome (firmer, less-wrinkled facial skin) is supported for **injection** by small human trials; for **topical** application it is largely *assumed*, because the molecule must first cross the stratum corneum (see §9).

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Targeted? | Strength / where the evidence comes from |
|---|---|---|
| Collagen/ECM loss | ✅ (injectable) | Type-I collagen ↑ in vitro + dermal-thickness ↑ in small split-face trials (injection). Topical: mechanism-only. |
| Hydration / water-loss | ✅ | PN's water-binding scaffold; hydration endpoints improved in injectable studies; a plausible topical humectant effect. |

## 4. Evidence review by tier *(core)*

### Tier 1 — Mechanistic / in-vitro / ex-vivo
- A2A-receptor agonism → fibroblast proliferation, VEGF, type-I collagen, anti-inflammatory cytokine modulation (Squadrito et al., *Curr Pharm Des* 2017). **Robust at this tier**, but largely derived from wound-healing/ulcer/burn biology, not facial photoaging.

### Tier 2 — Animal
- Wound-healing, diabetic-ulcer, tendon and osteoarthritis models underpin PDRN's regulatory heritage as a tissue-repair agent. **Supports plausibility; not a facial-aesthetic endpoint.**

### Tier 3 — Human, lower-grade (open-label / uncontrolled / split-face uncontrolled / case)
- **Kim et al. 2020** (*J Cosmet Dermatol*; PMID 31702128), split-face, **n=20**, 12 wk: improved elasticity + increased dermal thickness on the Rejuran-treated side vs untreated. *Limitation: small, single-centre Korean, short follow-up, no sham.*
- **Real-life / prospective observational cohorts** (e.g., periorbital rhytides; PMC12905022, PMC12759169): patient-reported improvement + minimal AEs. *Limitation: uncontrolled, satisfaction-weighted, open-label.*
- Topical-vs-microneedling comparison (Indonesian student journal, JIMKI 2024): microneedling-delivered PDRN > topical serum for regeneration; topical alone gave milder tone/oxidative-stress benefit. *Limitation: very low-grade venue, small.*

### Tier 4 — Human, controlled (RCT / active- or split-face-randomized) *(the tier that supports real claims)*
- **Pak et al.** — Phase III **randomized, double-blind, active-controlled**, **n=120**, crow's-feet: PN filler showed longer duration + better efficacy than HA filler; well tolerated. *Strongest single trial; active-comparator (HA), not vehicle/sham; industry context likely (Korean filler).*
- **Lee et al.** — randomized double-blind **split-face**, **n=30**, periocular: PN improved elasticity/texture more than HA; minimal AEs.
- **Jeong et al.** — split-face randomized, **n=20**, canthal lines: both PN and comparator improved wrinkles; PN faster onset, fewer AEs.
- **Systematic reviews:** Lampridou et al. 2025 (*J Cosmet Dermatol*) — **9 studies, ~219 patients, "low and moderate quality."** Lee KWA et al. 2024 (*IJMS* 25:8224) — concludes a **"scarcity of high-quality, large-scale RCTs."**

**What is proven vs borrowed (injectable):** *Proven (modestly):* injected PN/PDRN produces measurable short-term gains in elasticity, dermal thickness, hydration and periocular wrinkles in small, mostly Korean, mostly active-comparator (not sham) trials. *Borrowed:* the muscular collagen/angiogenesis/anti-inflammatory mechanism story is imported from wound-healing science. **Body-wide/long-term aesthetic durability is under-evidenced.**

**What is proven vs borrowed (topical):** Essentially **all topical efficacy is borrowed** from the injectable + mechanism story. There is no robust controlled human trial showing a leave-on PDRN serum on intact skin replicates injectable outcomes (see §9).

## 5. Treatment parameters / protocol

- **Injectable "dose":** typically **2–3 sessions, 2–4 wks apart**, then maintenance every ~3–6 months. Intradermal microinjections across the face; concentration e.g. ~20 mg/mL (Rejuran). **Provider-administered**, moderate injector skill; some downtime (wheals/bruising). Time-to-visible-result ~2–4 wks; durability months, not permanent.
- **Topical serum:** 5–15 mg/mL (some labels claim "20,000 ppm"); daily leave-on; vendors quote **8–12 wks** to any visible effect. **Penetration is the crux** — vendors cite ~3–8% passive uptake rising to ~35–60% *with microneedling/microchannel delivery* (vendor figures, [unverified] as independent measurements).

## 6. Safety

- **Injectable AEs:** transient injection-site reactions — erythema, swelling, **tiny wheals (resolve 12–24 h)**, **pinpoint bruising in ~15–25%** (higher on anticoagulants), mild pain. Serious AEs rare across the cited trials. Provider-dependent risks: nodules, infection, vascular events if mis-injected.
- **Salmon/fish allergy:** the headline patient-facing concern. High-purity processing removes most allergenic protein, so reactions are rare, **but trace protein can remain** — patients with significant fish/seafood allergy should disclose, and severe fish allergy is treated as a contraindication.
- **Skin-tone risk:** PIH possible at injection sites (needle trauma), as with any micro-injection in higher-Fitzpatrick skin.
- **Topical:** low-risk cosmetic profile (irritation/sensitivity possible); no injection risk. Microneedling add-on reintroduces infection/barrier risk.
- **Counterfeit/grey-market:** unregulated "salmon DNA" injectables and dupes are a real safety concern in the US grey zone.

## 7. Regulatory & claims

- **Injectable (US):** **not FDA-approved/cleared** for aesthetic injection; **not** on FDA's approved dermal-filler list. Clinics should not present it as FDA-approved. Approved/used as a device or drug in Korea, parts of Europe, UK, and elsewhere — a genuine **US regulatory grey zone**. *A cosmetic brand must NOT sell, bundle, or imply endorsement of unapproved injectables in the US.*
- **Topical (US):** sells as a **cosmetic** (no premarket approval), INCI usually **"sodium DNA"** or **"hydrolyzed DNA."** Permissible cosmetic claims: appearance/feel of hydration, smoothness, "looks firmer," post-procedure soothing. **Off-limits without drug/substantiation review:** "boosts collagen," "repairs," "regenerates," "replicates the injectable," any structure/function or anti-aging-as-treatment claim — these carry regulatory weight per [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md).

## 9. Hype vs evidence

- **The penetration objection (the big one for topical).** PDRN MW is ~**50–1,500 kDa**; passive transdermal penetration realistically caps near **~500 Da** (the "500-Dalton rule"). The molecule is **orders of magnitude too large** to cross intact stratum corneum in meaningful amounts. Dermatologist consensus (Dr. Rachel Ho; brand's own [`../competitors/rejuran.md`](../competitors/rejuran.md) source [7]): **topical PDRN does not replicate injectable results** and is best framed as a possible post-procedure adjunct, not a needle substitute. Marketing phrases like "injectable effect without needles" are **overclaims**.
- **Mechanism ≠ facial outcome.** The A2A/collagen story is real biology but mostly from wound healing; it is routinely presented as if it proves facial anti-aging.
- **"Salmon sperm" sensationalism** drives virality but inflates expectations.
- **Genuinely supported:** *injected* PN/PDRN modestly improves elasticity/hydration/periocular wrinkles short-term, with a clean safety profile, in small trials. That is the honest floor.

- **Interaction with our thesis (barrier-repair topical routine):**
  - *Injectable* = **substitute/adjacent channel**, not something we sell. It competes for the same "skin quality / regeneration" wallet but lives in the clinic. Our role is honest education, not provision.
  - *Topical PDRN serum* = **potential complement** to a barrier-repair routine — a hydration/soothing/"looks-firmer" step, NOT a barrier-repair hero and NOT an injectable replacement.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-21. Independent re-check of headline claims against primary sources.

### Overall evidence grade — graded separately:
- **Injectable PN/PDRN (aesthetic skin quality): B−.** Real human controlled data (Pak n=120 active-controlled; Lee n=30 and Jeong n=20 split-face) showing elasticity/wrinkle/hydration gains, but the body is **small, mostly Korean, mostly active-comparator (HA) rather than vehicle/sham, short-follow-up, and review-graded "low–moderate quality"** with explicit scarcity of large RCTs. Not an A.
- **Topical PDRN serum (anti-aging endpoint): D.** No robust controlled human trial that a leave-on serum on intact skin delivers the injectable outcome; defeated on first principles by the MW/penetration objection. Generous **C− only** if claims are limited to *hydration/soothing/appearance* (humectant-level plausibility) rather than collagen/regeneration.

### Citation audit (do the studies exist and say what's claimed?)
- Pak / Lee / Jeong trials, Kim 2020 (PMID 31702128), Jeong 2020 (PMID 31680395), Squadrito 2017 (PMID 29028075), Lee KWA 2024 *IJMS* 25:8224, Lampridou 2025 *JCD* (9 studies/~219 pts/"low–moderate"), Kim ST 2025 *Pharmaceutics* — all located and consistent with the claims as stated. ✔
- US "not FDA-approved injectable / topical = cosmetic" — corroborated by two independent sources + the brand's existing Rejuran teardown. ✔
- **Weaknesses:** several supporting numbers (penetration %, "20,000 ppm," some pricing, salmon-allergy framing) come from **vendor/clinic blogs, not peer-reviewed primary data** — flagged [unverified] in-text. Pak trial industry-funding status not independently confirmed → **treat as likely sponsored**.

### Main caveats
1. **US regulatory:** injectable PN/PDRN is unapproved in the US — do not sell, bundle, or imply endorsement.
2. **Topical penetration skepticism:** large DNA molecule vs ~500-Da barrier; "needle-free injectable effect" is not supported.
3. **Sponsorship/geography skew:** evidence concentrated in Korean centres and filler-maker-adjacent trials; active-comparator, not sham.

### Claims that should be softened
- "Boosts/stimulates collagen" (topical) → "supports the look of firmer, hydrated skin." · "Regenerates/repairs" → drop or qualify. · "Injectable results without needles" → **delete**. · "Clinically proven" (topical) → not supportable; reserve for the (modest) *injectable* literature with caveats.

**Net:** The *injectable* is a real, modestly-evidenced clinical procedure (grade B−) the brand should respect and educate around but never sell. The *topical serum* is an on-trend, low-risk cosmetic that is **credible only as a hydration/soothing active (claims-gated)** and **hype as an injectable substitute** (grade D for the regeneration claim). PDRN is a viable *secondary* active for us — never the brand's foundation, and only carried with disciplined claims. Trust the evidence to the floor, not the marketing to the ceiling.
