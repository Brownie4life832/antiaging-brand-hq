# Evidence Matrix — Devices & Procedures (master synthesis)

**Compiled:** 2026-06-21 · **Source:** the 20 per-modality dossiers in this folder (read directly from disk). · **Dossiers covered:** **20**.

> **How to read this.** One row per modality. Rows are sorted **strongest-evidence-first** for the *best credibly-supported aesthetic endpoint*. Two grade columns because at-home and in-clinic versions almost always diverge: **Clinic/best grade** = the strongest tier the modality actually earns in professional hands; **At-home grade** = what the *consumer* device/version earns (often far lower, or N/A if no real home version exists). The **Brand call** is the product-adjacency verdict for a premium *topical* skincare brand: ✅ build/sell · 🤝 partner/bundle/aftercare · 📚 educate-only · ❌ avoid. Grades use the same A–F scale as the ingredient [`_EVIDENCE-MATRIX.md`](../ingredients/_EVIDENCE-MATRIX.md): **A** strong human RCT · **B** moderate real human controlled · **C** weak/indirect/mechanism-led · **D** unproven for the cosmetic endpoint · **F** no relevant evidence (for that endpoint).

## Strategic synthesis — what the whole vertical says for the brand

**1. The single most valuable, repeatable finding: the *post-procedure barrier-recovery aftercare* wedge.**
The strongest-evidence modalities (lasers A−, deep peels B+, HIFU B−, RF, microneedling, mechanical resurfacing) all work by *deliberately injuring/breaching the skin barrier*. Controlled data show barrier-support topicals (ceramide creams, panthenol, beta-glucan) measurably reduce post-procedure erythema/TEWL and speed re-epithelialization. This is a **claims-safe, on-thesis adjacency for a barrier-repair hero** ("what your skin needs *after*") — surfaced independently in the lasers, peels, mechanical-resurfacing, and microinfusion dossiers. It complements rather than competes with the in-clinic spend, and rides the highest-intent "aftercare" search demand. *(Claims discipline: soothe / hydrate / support — never heal / repair / replace a medical step.)*

**2. At-home consumer devices almost universally collapse to D/F.** Every energy device that has a real clinic version (LED, RF, HIFU, microcurrent) earns a far lower grade at home — under-dosed, barely studied, borrowing the clinic device's reputation. The brand should **not build/own hardware** (regulatory burden + it's exactly where device-native competitors like Dr Dennis Gross are defended). The honest **"does the at-home version actually work?"** explainer is high-trust SEO the device brands won't write against themselves.

**3. The education/credibility lane is the biggest near-term prize.** A claims-disciplined, science-honest brand can own the questions consumers actually ask — *do red-light masks work, is microcurrent real, do exosome serums do anything, are beauty drips safe, will plasma pens scar me* — with tiered, cited, non-hype answers. Several modalities hand us sharp, defensible hooks: **"topical Botox" is a failed-Phase-3 myth**, **PRP was null vs saline**, **IPL "reverses aging" rests on n=5/n=15**, **skin-of-color PIH risk** across lasers/IPL/peels, and the **safety cautionary tales** (HIV-cluster vampire facials, glutathione-IV deaths, WBC frostbite, microneedling granulomas).

**4. The biologics (exosomes / stem-cell conditioned media / PDRN) are genuinely open — and the honest read is "conditional, topical-only, not now."** Per founder direction (2026-06-21) these are *not* ruled out. The neutral verdict across three dossiers: real premium demand and pricing power exist, but human evidence is thin, topical penetration is unsolved, and the injectable/human-derived side carries active FDA safety enforcement. **What would have to be true** to do one credibly: plant-/non-human-derived source, characterized & sterility-tested supply, appearance-only counsel-vetted claims, and *radical honesty about what it is* (secretome ≠ live stem cells; topical ≠ injectable). Best sequencing: **own the content first, productize later if at all.**

**5. Injectables/Rx (toxin, fillers, PDRN-injectable, PRP, IV) are 📚-only by definition** — a cosmetic brand can't sell or claim them. Their value here is (a) honest education on *what topicals can and cannot do* vs. a needle, and (b) competitive context for the 35–55 buyer who is also a tox/filler patient.
