# Device / Procedure: Dermal Fillers, Biostimulators & Injectable Skin Boosters

**Researched:** 2026-06-21 · **Status:** ☑ context-only (📚 education/context-only — see §10)
**Category:** injectable (Rx medical device / biologic-adjacent; provider-administered) — three distinct sub-types
**Regulatory class (US):** **FDA-approved (PMA) medical devices** — most fillers/biostimulators are Class III PMA devices, NOT cleared cosmetics. Provider-only; cannot be sold OTC. (Profhilo / many "skin boosters" are **not FDA-approved in the US** — European CE-marked; flag clearly.)

> Scope note: Exhaustive, citation-backed evidence review for the brand's research base. Evidence is
> separated strictly by tier, and **graded separately by sub-type** because the three diverge sharply.
> These are **injectables delivered by licensed injectors** — squarely outside what a cosmetic brand
> sells. The most brand-adjacent angle is honest education, especially the **topical-HA vs injectable-HA**
> confusion (§9–§10). Claims discipline per [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md) and
> [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 1. Identity

- **What it is:** A family of dermally/subdermally **injected** materials used to restore volume, soften folds, or improve "skin quality." All require a licensed injector and a needle/cannula. Three sub-types, kept distinct throughout this dossier:

**Sub-type A — HA (hyaluronic acid) fillers** *(volumizers; reversible)*
- **What:** Cross-linked hyaluronic acid gels that occupy space → **immediate volume**. The defining safety feature: **reversible with hyaluronidase** (an enzyme that dissolves HA), making them the most forgiving filler class.
- **Named products (FDA-approved PMA devices):** **Juvéderm** family (Allergan/AbbVie — Ultra/Ultra Plus XC, Voluma XC, Vollure XC, Volbella XC), **Restylane** family (Galderma — Restylane, Lyft, Defyne, Refyne), **Belotero** (Merz), **RHA collection** (Revance).
- **Duration:** ~6–18+ months depending on product/site (Voluma ~18–24 mo; lip fillers ~6–12 mo).

**Sub-type B — Biostimulators** *(stimulate the patient's own collagen; mostly NOT reversible)*
- **What:** Materials that provide little/no immediate "filler" volume but trigger a **foreign-body / fibroblast response → neocollagenesis over weeks-to-months.** Effect builds gradually and lasts longer.
- **Named products (FDA-approved):** **Sculptra** (poly-L-lactic acid, PLLA — Galderma; FDA 2004 for HIV lipoatrophy, 2009 for aesthetic nasolabial folds/wrinkles); **Radiesse** (calcium hydroxylapatite, CaHA — Merz; FDA 2006 for moderate-severe facial wrinkles incl. nasolabial folds; CaHA microspheres in a carboxymethylcellulose gel, so it is **part immediate volume, part biostimulator**, and is **partially reversible** — sodium thiosulfate is investigational, not hyaluronidase).
- **Duration:** ~18–36 months (PLLA up to ~25 mo per label; CaHA ~12–18 mo).

**Sub-type C — Injectable "skin boosters" / bio-remodeling** *(skin quality, not volume — the most topical-adjacent)*
- **What:** Microdroplet or bolus injection of **non- or minimally-cross-linked stabilized HA** spread through the dermis to improve **hydration, elasticity and "glow"** rather than to add contour. Conceptually the closest injectable analogue to a topical "skin-quality" claim.
- **Named products:** **Profhilo** (IBSA — stabilized hybrid high+low MW HA via NAHYCO thermal process, no BDDE cross-linker; **CE-marked, widely used in Europe/UK; NOT FDA-approved in the US** [flag]); **Restylane Skinboosters Vital / Vital Light** (Galderma — stabilized HA microdroplets); microdroplet HA / "mesotherapy HA" generally.
- **Duration:** ~6–12 months; requires a loading course (e.g. Profhilo: 2 sessions 4 weeks apart, then ~6-monthly).

- **Modality family:** **injectable** (chemical/biologic implant). None has any at-home equivalent — injection into living dermis by a licensed clinician is intrinsic.

## 2. Mechanism of action

- **HA fillers (A):** **Space-occupying + hydrophilic.** Cross-linked HA gel physically occupies tissue and binds water (HA holds up to ~1,000× its weight in water), lifting folds and restoring contour **immediately**. Secondary, lower-grade mechanism: mechanical stretch of fibroblasts may induce some neocollagenesis around the bolus. *Proven in-skin effect:* the volumizing/wrinkle-correction effect is robust and directly observed; the "stimulates your own collagen" sub-claim is weaker and partly borrowed from biostimulator marketing.
- **Biostimulators (B):** **Controlled foreign-body response → collagenesis.**
  - *PLLA (Sculptra):* PLLA microparticles are gradually phagocytosed; the subclinical inflammatory/fibroblastic response lays down **type I/III collagen** over weeks-to-months. Initial post-injection "volume" is mostly the diluent water, which resorbs in days — the *real* effect appears later as collagen.
  - *CaHA (Radiesse):* CaHA microspheres give **immediate scaffold volume** AND act via **mechanotransduction** — fibroblasts react to the microsphere matrix and lay down new collagen/elastin (vendor-cited "up to 160% increase in type I/III collagen"; vendor figure — see §9). So CaHA is a hybrid filler+biostimulator.
- **Skin boosters (C):** **Bio-remodeling.** Spreading stabilized HA through the dermis hydrates the ECM and — per the manufacturer mechanism — the hybrid HA complex signals fibroblasts/keratinocytes toward **new collagen and elastin** and improved tissue quality, rather than occupying space for contour. *Proven vs plausible:* the **hydration** effect is mechanistically direct and instrumentally measurable; the deeper **collagen/elastin "remodeling"** claim is more mechanistic/histology-led than proven by large clinical endpoints.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

Fillers/biostimulators address **structural/volume loss** (a real feature of facial aging, but partly *bone/fat* remodeling rather than skin biology); skin boosters reach further into ECM/hydration hallmarks.

| Hallmark | Targeted? | Strength / where the evidence comes from |
|---|---|---|
| Collagen/ECM loss | ✅ (B, C primary; A secondary) | Strong for **biostimulators** (histology + controlled volume data); moderate for skin boosters (histology + smaller clinical); weak/indirect for HA fillers |
| Hydration/water-loss | ✅ (C primary; A secondary) | Skin boosters: real instrumental hydration gains (RCT-level for Restylane Skinboosters); HA fillers bind water locally |
| Photoaging | ⚠️ Indirect | Some texture/fine-line improvement (skin boosters, PLLA décolletage); not a photoprotection or pigment mechanism |
| Barrier decline | ❌ No | Not a mechanism; injection transiently breaches skin |
| Pigmentation/tone | ❌ No | Not a depigmenting modality (some "glow" is hydration/light-reflectance, not pigment correction) |
| Cellular senescence | ⚠️ Weak/mechanistic | Bio-remodeling fibroblast-activation theory; not a proven senolytic effect |
| Oxidative stress | ❌ No | No evidence |
| Glycation/AGEs | ❌ No | No mechanism (the injected HA itself is not anti-glycation) |
| Mitochondrial decline | ❌ No | No evidence |
| Inflammaging | ❌ No | Acutely pro-inflammatory (that *is* the biostimulator mechanism) |
| Microbiome | ❌ No | No evidence; injection is a theoretical infection/biofilm route (§6) |

**Net:** This class is primarily about **volume/structure** (fillers, biostimulators) — the hallmark a topical routine genuinely *cannot* touch — plus **hydration + some ECM remodeling** for skin boosters. It is **not** a barrier, antioxidant, anti-glycation, or pigment tool.

## 4. Evidence review by tier  *(the core of this dossier)*

### Tier 1 — Mechanistic / in-vitro / ex-vivo / human histology
- **CaHA collagenesis (B):** Histology and reviews document CaHA microspheres driving fibroblast activity and new type I/III collagen + elastin/angiogenesis ("regenerative" framing). *Limitation:* much pro-collagen quantification (e.g. "160%") is **vendor/narrative-review sourced**, not pooled RCT data.
- **PLLA collagenesis (B):** Established that PLLA induces a fibroblastic/foreign-body response producing collagen; basis of the gradual, durable effect. *Limitation:* mechanistic/histologic, not the clinical endpoint itself.
- **Stabilized-HA bio-remodeling (C):** In-vitro/ex-vivo work (NAHYCO hybrid HA) shows enhanced fibroblast/keratinocyte behavior beyond passive hydration. *Limitation:* lab/histology surrogate; vendor-associated; not a clinical outcome.

### Tier 2 — Animal
- Not the basis of any aesthetic claim here; the field moved directly to human histology/clinical work. Treat as **[unverified / not claim-bearing]**.

### Tier 3 — Human, lower-grade (open-label / uncontrolled / case series / consensus)
- **Skin boosters (C) — Profhilo:** Evidence base is **real but thin and largely small/open-label/industry-linked.** Independent clinic summaries explicitly call it "promising but still modest in size," with "small or industry-funded" studies and few large RCTs. A systematic review pooling studies reports the **majority of treated patients improved in elasticity, laxity, hydration and overall skin quality** — but pooled from heterogeneous, mostly low-grade studies.
- **Restylane Skinboosters (C) — consensus:** A published **consensus meeting** statement supports skin-quality use — expert opinion, lowest evidence tier.
- **CaHA (B) — hyperdilute "biostimulation":** The popular hyperdilute CaHA protocol for neck/décolletage/hands rests largely on **case-series / starting-point protocol papers**, not RCTs.
- **PLLA late events:** Case reports of **late-onset granulomas** (one >70 months post-injection) anchor the long-tail nodule risk (§6).

### Tier 4 — Human, controlled (RCT / split-face / within-subject randomized)  *(the tier that supports real claims)*
- **HA fillers (A) — strong, approval-grade.** Multiple multicenter, randomized, double-blind, within-subject (split-face) RCTs underpin US FDA PMA approvals for nasolabial folds:
  - **Juvéderm Vollure XC:** n=123, randomized split-face vs an approved HA control; coprimary endpoints at month 6; **benefit lasting ≥6 months in ~93%** (label-supporting durability). *Limitation:* active-comparator (non-inferiority) design, industry-sponsored.
  - Earlier Juvéderm Ultra/Ultra Plus and Restylane pivotal split-face RCTs vs bovine/human collagen showed **HA superiority and longer duration** → basis of 2006 approvals.
- **Biostimulators (B) — strong/moderate, approval-grade.**
  - **PLLA (Sculptra):** Pivotal multicenter double-blind RCT vs CosmoPlast collagen for nasolabial folds → **significantly greater and longer-lasting improvement**; a manufacturer release cites **~13-month** durability; FDA aesthetic approval 2009 (HIV lipoatrophy 2004). A **2024 RCT (n=260)** reported **67.6% wrinkle-severity improvement at 52 weeks** with >90% satisfaction, and a 2025 midface RCT reported strong 12-month volume retention. *Limitation:* sponsored; comparator-relative; nodule rate non-trivial (§6).
  - **CaHA (Radiesse):** Pivotal split-face RCT, **n=117**, CaHA vs human collagen for nasolabial folds → **79% of subjects superior on the CaHA side through 6 months**; FDA approval 2006. **Long-term safety: 102 patients followed 3 years — no late/delayed nodules, granulomas, or infections.** *Limitation:* sponsored; comparator-relative.
- **Skin boosters (C) — real but thinner / mostly European / often sponsored.**
  - **Profhilo:** A **randomized controlled triple-blind trial** of Profhilo for skin texture/perioral wrinkles exists (2026, *Aesthetic Plastic Surgery*) — the strongest design in the sub-type, but **small** (perioral split-face studies in this literature run ~12–25 subjects). [full text gated — design verified, magnitudes not independently re-extracted]
  - **Restylane Skinboosters Vital:** Prospective **randomized multicenter** trial (~50 women, 2 vs 3 sessions): **skin hydration significantly increased vs baseline at multiple timepoints; elasticity improved only occasionally/inconsistently;** 75–84% rated superior facial appearance at month 3. *Limitation:* small, no true placebo arm (compares session counts), sponsored.

**What is proven vs borrowed:**
- **Proven (A, strong human RCT):** HA fillers correct folds/restore volume for months, reversibly (hyaluronidase). This is robust and approval-grade.
- **Proven (B, strong-moderate human RCT + histology):** PLLA and CaHA produce durable (≈1–3 yr) fold/volume improvement via collagen stimulation; approval-grade for nasolabial folds.
- **Thinner / partly borrowed (C):** Skin-quality/"glow" and ECM-remodeling claims for Profhilo/skin boosters rest on **smaller, often European, frequently sponsored** studies; **hydration** is the best-supported endpoint, **collagen remodeling** the more borrowed one.
- **Borrowed across the board:** Any framing that injectable HA "is the same as" or validates **topical HA** (§9) — mechanistically false.

## 5. Treatment parameters / protocol

- **"Dose" analogue:** product type, **volume injected (mL)**, depth/plane (dermal vs subdermal vs supraperiosteal), and dilution (esp. PLLA reconstitution volume; hyperdilute CaHA).
- **Course & cadence:**
  - *HA fillers:* often single session per area; **top-up at 6–18 months** as it resorbs.
  - *PLLA:* typically **3 sessions ~4–6 weeks apart**; result builds over **3–6 months**; durability up to ~2 years.
  - *CaHA:* 1–2 sessions; durability ~12–18 months.
  - *Skin boosters (Profhilo):* **2 loading sessions 4 weeks apart**, maintenance ~6-monthly.
- **Operator skill:** **high and load-bearing** — outcome and the serious vascular risk (§6) are directly operator-dependent (anatomy, plane, aspiration, slow low-pressure injection, cannula vs needle).
- **Time-to-visible result:** HA fillers **immediate**; biostimulators/skin boosters **weeks-to-months**.
- **Durability:** HA ~6–18 mo (reversible early with hyaluronidase); PLLA/CaHA ~1–3 yr; skin boosters ~6–12 mo.
- **Setting:** **clinic-only, licensed injector.** No at-home version exists or should exist.

## 6. Safety

- **Common/expected (all):** injection-site bruising, swelling, redness, tenderness, lumpiness for days; usually self-limited.
- **Nodules / granulomas:**
  - *HA fillers:* **delayed inflammatory nodules ~0.5%** of treatments (e.g. ~0.5–1% with Juvéderm Voluma over 2 years), appearing weeks-to->1 year later; mechanisms include **bacterial biofilm** and immune response to degrading HA fragments. Advantage: HA nodules are **dissolvable** (hyaluronidase ± steroids/antibiotics).
  - *PLLA (biostimulator):* **subcutaneous papules/nodules historically 6–44%** in early trials (driven by under-dilution/superficial placement); modern higher-dilution + immediate-reconstitution + deeper placement cut this sharply (some FDA-summary rates ~1–2%). **Late-onset granulomas reported 6–60+ months** out (a case >70 months). **Not reversible** with an enzyme — harder to manage.
  - *CaHA:* nodules mostly from **superficial placement**; pivotal 3-year follow-up found no late nodules/granulomas, but real-world foreign-body granuloma case reports exist.
  - *Skin boosters:* generally milder (small HA volumes), but injection-site papules/bruising occur.
- **Vascular occlusion — the rare but catastrophic risk (all fillers, esp. HA):** Inadvertent intra-arterial injection → **embolus → skin necrosis, and via retrograde flow into the ophthalmic/retinal circulation, irreversible blindness, and rarely cerebral infarction/stroke.** Highest-risk danger zones: **glabella, nose, nasolabial/forehead.** Retinal infarction can occur within **~12–15 minutes**; resulting **blindness is usually permanent and not reliably reversible.** Estimated incidence of filler-induced visual loss has been suggested around **~1 in 4,618 to 1 in 9,012** cases (likely underreported; methodology debated). A large retrospective series (290,307 HA injections) found 10 vascular-obstruction events. **Cannulas reduce but do not eliminate** the risk (one series: 25/28 severe HA emboli used a cannula). For HA, **flooding the area with hyaluronidase** is the emergency antidote — a key reason HA's reversibility matters; **biostimulators (PLLA/CaHA) are NOT dissolvable**, so an arterial event with those is harder to rescue.
- **Infection / biofilm:** any injection seeds a low risk of infection or **biofilm** formation (a driver of delayed nodules and treatment-resistant inflammation).
- **Contraindications/cautions:** active skin infection at site, known hypersensitivity, (relative) keloid/granulomatous-disease history, pregnancy/lactation (precautionary), recent dental work or concurrent illness (cited triggers for delayed HA reactions). Biostimulators are **avoided in/around the lips and very thin/dynamic areas** (higher nodule risk).
- **At-home misuse:** **DIY filler kits sold online are dangerous and illegal/unapproved** — they carry the full vascular/blindness/necrosis risk with none of the anatomical training or hyaluronidase rescue. The brand should explicitly warn against these.

## 7. Regulatory & claims

- **Device/drug/cosmetic line:** US dermal fillers and biostimulators (Juvéderm, Restylane, Sculptra, Radiesse) are **FDA-approved Class III medical devices (PMA)** — a **higher** bar than the 510(k) clearance used for energy devices, and **far** above cosmetic regulation. **Profhilo and many "skin boosters" are NOT FDA-approved in the US** (CE-marked / used abroad) — say so plainly; do not imply US availability/approval.
- **What a cosmetic brand may claim:** essentially **nothing about delivering or equating to these injectables.** A topical brand may **educate** (what each sub-type is, the evidence, the risks) and may position a topical as **adjunct skincare** (e.g. barrier support before/after a clinic visit) — but **must not**:
  - imply a topical (HA serum, "collagen" cream) **replicates a filler's volumizing or a biostimulator's collagen-induction** — this is the single most important false-equivalence to avoid (§9);
  - use "filler in a bottle," "needle-free filler," "injectable-level results," or "boosts/restores collagen" as efficacy claims without substantiation (these veer into drug/device territory per [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md));
  - suggest any at-home injectable.
  - All such language needs substantiation/legal review. Safe register: descriptive education + honestly-scoped topical-hydration/comfort claims only.

## 9. Hype vs evidence

- **The central consumer confusion — "topical HA = injectable HA":** Marketing of HA serums frequently implies they do what HA *fillers* do. **They cannot.** Topical HA sits **on/in the upper epidermis**, drawing/holding water at the surface for **temporary plumping and hydration** — a real but shallow, transient effect. Injectable HA is a **cross-linked gel implanted in the deep dermis/subdermis** that physically **restores volume and lifts folds for months**. **No topical can replicate injectable volumizing** — the skin barrier blocks meaningful penetration of high-MW HA, and even if it didn't, surface hydration ≠ deep structural fill. This is the brand's single strongest honest education angle (§10).
- **"Collagen-boosting" creams vs biostimulators:** Biostimulators (PLLA/CaHA) drive measurable neocollagenesis via a controlled wound/foreign-body response in the dermis. **Topical "collagen creams" do not** — collagen molecules are too large to penetrate, and most topical "collagen support" is indirect (e.g. retinoids/vitamin C signalling). Don't let topical copy borrow biostimulator credibility.
- **Skin boosters overreach:** "Profhilo regenerates your skin" outruns its **small, often sponsored, mostly-European** evidence base; the well-supported claim is **improved hydration** (and modest elasticity/texture), not dramatic regeneration. Flag US **non-approval**.
- **Vendor mechanism figures** (e.g. CaHA "+160% collagen") are **manufacturer/narrative-review sourced** — cite as such, not as RCT outcomes.
- **Genuinely supported:** HA fillers reversibly restore volume/correct folds (strong RCT); PLLA/CaHA give durable collagen-mediated volume (strong-moderate RCT); skin boosters improve hydration (moderate RCT). All are **clinic injectables**, none are topicals.

## 10. Brand relevance & strategic verdict

- **Interaction with our thesis:** These injectables do what a topical barrier-repair routine **structurally cannot** — restore lost **volume** (fillers/biostimulators) and, for skin boosters, drive deeper **dermal hydration/ECM** changes. So vs a topical routine they are a **complement, not a competitor or substitute** for the volume/structure job, and only a **partial overlap** with skin boosters on the *hydration* axis (where a topical is the cheaper, lower-risk, maintenance layer). They sit "upstream" in a different, medical intervention class and do **not** threaten the topical thesis.
- **Product-adjacency call:** **📚 context/education-only.** Rationale: these are **FDA-approved (or abroad, CE-marked) injectable medical devices delivered by licensed injectors**, with a rare-but-catastrophic vascular/blindness risk and biostimulator nodule risk. A cosmetic brand cannot sell, bundle, or credibly equate a topical to them, and manufacturing is far out of lane. The honest, on-brand move is **trustworthy education** plus optional **pre/post-procedure barrier-support positioning** — explicitly *not* a substitute claim.
- **Content/SEO angle (high-value):** **"Topical hyaluronic acid vs injectable HA filler — what can a serum actually do?"** A clear, evidence-honest explainer that (a) validates topical HA for surface hydration/temporary plumping, (b) states plainly that **no cream replaces a filler's volumizing**, and (c) tiers the evidence (fillers/biostimulators: strong; skin boosters: real-but-thin, often European/sponsored, US-unapproved) is exactly the science-credibility play the brand trades on — and it **defuses a confusion the rest of the category exploits**. Secondary angle: "Filler vs biostimulator vs skin booster — which does what?" and "Is a topical 'collagen cream' the same as Sculptra? (No — here's why.)"
- Log the call in [`../../ideas/considering.md`](../../ideas/considering.md) (education/content asset; possible clinic-partnership exploration) — **not** as a product to build.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-21. Independent re-check of headline claims against primary sources.

### Overall evidence grades (by sub-type)
- **HA fillers (A): A−** — strong, **FDA-PMA approval-grade** RCT evidence (split-face multicenter trials) for fold correction/volume, with reversibility a genuine, well-characterized safety advantage. Not a clean "A" only because durability/skin-quality sub-claims and "stimulates your own collagen" are weaker/borrowed, and most data is industry-sponsored.
- **Biostimulators (B): B+ / A−** — PLLA and CaHA both have **pivotal RCTs and FDA approval** for nasolabial folds plus histologic collagen evidence and multi-year durability; docked for sponsored/comparator-relative designs and a real (dose/technique-dependent) nodule liability.
- **Skin boosters (C): C+ / B−** — evidence is **real but thinner, smaller, often European, frequently sponsored**; **hydration** is the best-supported endpoint (RCT-level for Restylane Skinboosters), **collagen "remodeling"/regeneration** is more mechanistic/histology-led; Profhilo is **not FDA-approved in the US**.
- **At-home / topical-equivalent claim: F** — no topical replicates injectable volumizing; any "needle-free filler" claim is unsupported.

### Citation audit (do the studies exist and say what's claimed?)
- HA-filler split-face pivotal RCTs (Vollure n=123; ≥6-mo benefit ~93%): **verified** (PMC6221389 + FDA PMA labeling). Active-comparator/non-inferiority design noted.
- PLLA pivotal vs CosmoPlast collagen + ~13-mo durability: **verified** (Sanofi release + FDA SSED P030050). 2024 RCT n=260 / 67.6% at 52 wk and 2025 midface RCT: **verified via search snippets** (full texts not all re-extracted) — flagged as sponsored.
- CaHA pivotal n=117 split-face (79% superior through 6 mo) + 3-yr no-late-nodule follow-up: **verified** (PubMed 20442101). CaHA "+160% collagen": **vendor/narrative-review sourced** — labeled as such, not as trial data.
- Profhilo triple-blind RCT (2026): **verified to exist** (Springer); **full text gated** — effect magnitudes NOT independently re-extracted; sub-type characterized as small/sponsored-leaning per independent summary (source 3).
- Restylane Skinboosters RCT (hydration ↑ significant, elasticity inconsistent; 75–84% superior at M3): **verified** (NCT02403986 protocol + search). Compares session counts, **no true placebo** — noted.
- Filler-blindness incidence **~1/4,618–1/9,012** and "retinal infarction in ~12–15 min": from consensus literature (source 20) **via search snippet** — direct WebFetch of PMC7427155 was **CAPTCHA-blocked**; figure is explicitly flagged as **underreported / methodology-debated**, not a hard rate. 290,307-case series (10 occlusions) and ~0.5% delayed-nodule rate: **verified** (PubMed 40358958; PMC9153319).
- PLLA nodule range **6–44%** (early) → ~1–2% (modern dilution) and late granulomas to >70 mo: **verified** (search + PMC4809477 + FDA SSED).

### Main caveats
- **Sponsorship is pervasive.** Most pivotal and skin-booster data is manufacturer-funded; independent head-to-head, standardized-protocol trials are scarcer (especially for skin boosters).
- **Profhilo/skin-booster tier is the weakest** and partly **non-US-regulated** — do not overstate; full-text magnitudes for the 2026 RCT were not re-extracted (gated).
- **Vascular/blindness incidence is an estimate**, likely underreported, with debated methodology — present as "rare but catastrophic," not a precise rate.
- **Cost and market figures are approximate ranges**, from clinic/market sources, not re-verified to a single authority — labeled [approximate].
- Vendor mechanism numbers (CaHA collagen %) are **not** RCT outcomes.

### Claims that should be softened
- "Topical HA works like filler / needle-free filler" → **false**; "topical HA hydrates and temporarily plumps the surface; it cannot replace injectable volumizing."
- "Profhilo regenerates / remodels skin" → "improves **hydration** (and modestly texture/elasticity); deeper remodeling claims rest on small, often sponsored, European, US-unapproved data."
- "Biostimulators safely build collagen with no downside" → real collagen benefit **but** non-reversible nodule/granuloma risk (PLLA 6–44% historically; late granulomas to >5 yr).
- Any topical "boosts/restores collagen" borrowing biostimulator credibility → drug/device-territory; prohibited without substantiation.

**Net:** This is a legitimately evidence-backed **medical injectable** class — **strong** (HA fillers, A−) and **strong-moderate** (biostimulators, B+/A−) RCT/approval evidence for volume/fold correction, and **real-but-thinner** (skin boosters, C+/B−, hydration-led, US-unapproved) evidence for skin quality — but it is **clinic-only, injector-delivered, recurring, and carries a rare catastrophic vascular/blindness risk plus biostimulator nodule risk.** For the brand it is firmly **📚 context/education-only**: the highest-value use is an honest **topical-HA-vs-injectable-HA** explainer that validates what a serum *can* do (surface hydration/temporary plump) while stating plainly that **no topical replicates injectable volumizing** — a confusion the rest of the category quietly exploits, and one the brand can win trust by clarifying.
