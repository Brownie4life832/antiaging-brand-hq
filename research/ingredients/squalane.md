# Ingredient: Squalane (+ Squalene) (INCI: Squalane; Squalene)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ shortlisted (supporting role) — see Verdict
**Class:** Barrier lipids & skin-identical actives
**Researcher note:** This dossier separates **squalane** (the saturated, stable, cosmetic-grade emollient) from **squalene** (the unsaturated, oxidation-prone molecule found in human sebum). They are constantly conflated in marketing. The distinction is the single most important fact about this ingredient.

---

## 1. Identity

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Common name** | Squalane (cosmetic emollient); Squalene (the precursor / sebum lipid) |
| **INCI** | `Squalane` (the saturated form used in skincare); `Squalene` (the unsaturated form) |
| **Chemistry — squalene** | A C30 triterpene, 2,6,10,15,19,23-hexamethyltetracosa-2,6,10,14,18,22-hexaene. **Six C=C double bonds.** Intermediate in the cholesterol biosynthesis pathway. |
| **Chemistry — squalane** | 2,6,10,15,19,23-hexamethyltetracosane. The **fully hydrogenated (fully saturated)** version of squalene — zero double bonds. |
| **Why hydrogenate?** | The six double bonds in squalene make it highly prone to autoxidation (it goes rancid and forms irritating, comedogenic peroxides). Hydrogenation to squalane removes every double bond, producing a stable, shelf-stable, odorless, colorless oil. **Squalane is what belongs in a product; squalene generally does not.** |
| **Key forms / derivatives** | (1) **Sugarcane (bio-fermented) squalane** — INCI Squalane, from *Saccharum officinarum*; (2) **Olive squalane** — from *Olea europaea* deodorizer distillate; (3) **Shark-liver squalane** — historical, ethically/legally problematic, avoid; (4) **Phytosqualane** (plant marketing term); (5) Oxidized forms — **squalene monohydroperoxide (SQOOH)** and squalene cyclic peroxides are degradation products, NOT ingredients (they are pro-inflammatory). |
| **Role in human skin** | Squalene is a native component of sebum/skin surface lipids — roughly **~10–13% of skin surface lipids** are squalene. The skin barely makes squalane endogenously; squalane is largely a manufactured (hydrogenated) emollient. |

---

## 2. Class & mechanism

Squalane is an **emollient / occlusive lipid** that is "skin-identical" in spirit (the skin makes the closely related squalene) but is structurally inert and stable.

**How it works on skin:**

- **Emolliency & barrier integration.** As a lightweight branched-chain hydrocarbon with low surface tension, squalane spreads into the gaps between corneocytes and integrates with the stratum-corneum lipid matrix (ceramides, cholesterol, fatty acids). It smooths the surface, softens, and improves perceived suppleness without a heavy occlusive feel.
- **Occlusion / reduced water loss.** It forms a light, breathable film that reduces transepidermal water loss (TEWL), helping the barrier hold water. This is the mechanism behind its hydration benefit — it is occlusive/emollient, not a humectant (it does not draw water in).
- **Solvent / penetration carrier.** Its moderate polarity and low surface tension make it an excellent vehicle for lipophilic actives (retinol, tocopherol, ceramides, fullerene-C60, polyphenols). Much of the formal "squalane" literature is really about squalane *as a carrier*.
- **Antioxidant behavior (mostly squalene, not squalane).** *Squalene* is an efficient quencher of singlet oxygen and sacrificially protects other skin-surface lipids from photo-oxidation. **Important caveat:** this antioxidant role belongs to *squalene* (the double-bonds are what react). Squalane, being saturated, is far less chemically reactive — its "antioxidant" benefit is mostly indirect (it doesn't itself oxidize, and it can carry/protect other antioxidants), not a strong direct radical-scavenging effect.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Relevance | Strength (for topical squalane) |
|---|---|---|
| **Barrier decline** | Core. Emollient that integrates with the SC lipid matrix and lowers TEWL. | Moderate–strong (mechanistic + emollient-class evidence) |
| **Hydration / water-loss (TEWL)** | Core. Reduces TEWL, improves softness/suppleness. | Moderate (emollient effect well-accepted; squalane-specific RCTs thin) |
| **Oxidative stress / photoaging** | Squalene quenches singlet oxygen on the skin surface; squalane spares this by not consuming itself. UVA-fibroblast protection shown in vitro. | Weak–moderate, and mostly squalene / in-vitro |
| **Inflammaging** | Indirect: by *preventing* squalene→peroxide conversion (peroxides are pro-inflammatory via NF-κB/IL-6), stable lipids reduce a known irritant load. | Weak (inferential) |
| **Collagen / ECM loss** | Only via **oral** squalene (procollagen ↑) and in-vitro/explant work — NOT demonstrated for topical squalane. | Weak; format-borrowed |
| **Pigmentation / tone, microbiome, senescence, mitochondrial, glycation/AGEs** | No meaningful direct evidence. | None / not an indication |

**Bottom line on hallmarks:** squalane is a **barrier/hydration** ingredient. Anti-aging hallmark claims (collagen, photoaging) are largely borrowed from *squalene* in *other formats* (oral, in-vitro) and should not be put on a topical squalane product.

## 4. Evidence review by tier *(the core of this dossier)*

> **Read this first:** The biggest honesty problem with squalane is that in most "clinical studies," **squalane is the vehicle or the placebo**, not the tested active (e.g., fullerene-C60 *dissolved in* squalane; bakuchiol-*plus*-squalane; squalane-*based* delivery of ceramide/vitamin E). Genuinely **squalane-alone topical RCTs in humans with hard endpoints are essentially absent.** Most "anti-aging" data are for **squalene** and come from **oral** or **in-vitro/animal** work. Below, tiers are kept strictly separate.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro (cell / lab)

- **Wolosik et al., 2025, *Antioxidants*** — Plant-derived **squalane** protected primary human dermal fibroblasts against UVA-induced apoptosis. Squalane (0.005–0.015%) dose-dependently countered UVA-induced ROS, restored DNA synthesis to ~80% of control, reduced thiol depletion, suppressed p53/caspase-3/-9/PARP and activated p-Akt/mTOR survival signaling. *Limitations:* cultured cells, not skin; micro-concentrations; mechanism-only. **Supplier-tie caveat:** the test squalane was supplied by **Aprinnova, LLC** (the sugarcane bio-squalane producer); the authors declare no conflict of interest and the work was funded by Medical University of Białystok grants, but the material provenance is a commercial squalane maker and should be read with that in mind. PMC12291772.
- **Ottaviani, Picardo et al., 2006, *J Invest Dermatol*** — **Peroxidated squalene** (the oxidized degradation product, not squalane) induced inflammatory mediators in HaCaT keratinocytes (NF-κB activation, IL-6 ↑), implicating squalene peroxides in acne. *Relevance:* this is the case *for* using the stable squalane and *against* leaving raw squalene exposed to air. PMID via ScienceDirect S0022202X15326890.
- **Photo-oxidation / lipidomics work (Ottaviani/Picardo; De Luca; Pham 2015, *Int J Cosmet Sci*; squalene cyclic peroxide identification 2021, *Antioxidants* PMC8614752)** — establish that *squalene* on the skin surface oxidizes under UV/pollution into SQOOH and cyclic peroxides that are irritating/pro-inflammatory and serve as pollution/photo-oxidative-stress markers. Again: argues for the saturated squalane.
- **Cho et al. / review-cited keratinocyte work** — topical squalene reduced superoxide anion (O2⁻) production in cultured keratinocytes; squalene suppressed TNF-α/NF-κB and raised IL-10/IL-13/IL-4 in cell models (summarized in the tocotrienol+squalene review, PMC11442061). In-vitro/animal level.

### Tier 2 — Animal

- **Desai et al. (cited in PMC6253993)** — 5% **squalene** reduced chemically-induced tumor incidence by ~26.7% in mice. *Oral/animal; not a skin claim.*
- **Erythema model (Wistar rats, cited in PMC11442061)** — topical squalene markedly reduced erythema score, correlated with reduced O2⁻. *Animal.*
- **Wound-healing emulgel (agar-based, SQ-loaded; cited in PMC11442061)** — improved granulation, epithelialization, reduced wound diameter, scarless growth; squalene+vitamin C raised epidermal thickness and collagen III on skin **explants**. *Animal/explant.*
- **Okuda et al. (cited in PMC6253993)** — squalene reversed elevated TEWL in detergent-treated skin (p<0.05). *Model; supports the barrier/TEWL mechanism.*

### Tier 3 — Human, ORAL

- **Cho S, Choi C-W, Lee DH, Won C-H, Kim SM, Lee S, Lee M-J, Chung JH. 2009, *Clin Exp Dermatol* 34(4):500–508** (Seoul National Univ / Boramae). **40 women >50 yr**, oral squalene 13.5 g/day (low) or 27 g/day (high) ×90 days. **Results:** procollagen type I mRNA ↑ significantly (low-dose group); UV-induced keratinocyte apoptosis and thymine-dimer staining (DNA-damage marker) substantially ↓ in both groups. **Adverse:** transient loose stool in 35% (low) / 55% (high). PMID 19522983. *This is the strongest "anti-aging" signal squalene has — but it is **oral at gram doses**, not topical, and doses cause GI side effects. It cannot be claimed for a topical squalane cream.*
- **Cardiovascular (oral): systematic review, Ramirez-Torres / Sabeena Farvin et al. context, 2020, *Nutrients* 12(2):414 (PMC7071298)** — squalene's effect on plasma lipids in humans is **inconsistent**. Chan et al. (1996) RCT: 860 mg/day ×20 wk lowered total/LDL cholesterol in hypercholesterolemics; other trials showed no consistent effect. **Bani et al. / Strandberg-type single-dose work (PMID 10998465)** — a single oral dose *increased* cholesterol synthesis markers. *Net: cardiovascular evidence is mixed and irrelevant to a skincare claim, but useful for honesty.*

### Tier 4 — Human, TOPICAL

This is the tier that matters for the brand, and it is **thin and confounded**:

- **Fullerene-C60 dissolved in squalane — Kato et al., 2010, *J Nanosci Nanotechnol* 10(10):6769–6774 (PMID 21137794).** 23 Japanese women, randomized matched-pair double-blind **split-face** ×8 wk; fullerene/squalane cream vs **squalane-only "placebo."** Wrinkled-area % significantly reduced on the active (fullerene) side at 8 wk (not at 4 wk). *Critically: squalane was the PLACEBO/vehicle. The study shows squalane is well-tolerated and a good vehicle — it does NOT credit squalane with wrinkle reduction.*
- **Bakuchiol + squalane serum, 2024 (Cosmoderma; ResearchGate 379445339).** 47 women 18–50, once-daily ×4 wk: elasticity ↑ ~11.9% at day 28, TEWL ↓. *Confounded — bakuchiol is the established active; squalane's independent contribution is indeterminable. The authors themselves note this.*
- **Squalane as a penetration/stability carrier** — ex-vivo and emulsion studies (e.g., squalane-based emulsion improving polyphenol penetration, *Colloids Surf B* S0927776522004623; squalane O/W nanoemulsion for vitamin E, *ACS Omega* 2025; reports of ~43% better Ceramide NP delivery vs conventional oil phase). *These validate squalane as a delivery vehicle, not as a standalone active.*
- **CIR-cited formulation tolerance data** — squalene/squalane in finished formulations showed no significant human irritation or sensitization (see Safety).

**Explicit format-attribution summary:**

| Claimed benefit | Proven for TOPICAL squalane? | Where the evidence actually comes from |
|---|---|---|
| Softens, smooths, reduces TEWL, lightweight emollient | **Yes (emollient-class + mechanistic)** — but few squalane-only RCTs | Mechanism + emollient class + carrier studies |
| Good, non-greasy vehicle / boosts other actives' penetration | **Yes** | Ex-vivo & emulsion delivery studies; fullerene/bakuchiol vehicles |
| Non-comedogenic, well-tolerated | **Yes** | CIR + comedogenicity data |
| Antioxidant / photoprotective | **No (borrowed)** | This is *squalene*, in-vitro/skin-surface lipid chemistry |
| Boosts collagen / procollagen, reduces UV DNA damage | **No (borrowed)** | *Squalene*, **oral** 13.5–27 g/day (Cho 2009) |
| Wound healing / anti-inflammatory | **No (borrowed)** | *Squalene*, animal/explant/cell |

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- Squalane is a low-molecular-weight, branched saturated hydrocarbon with high lipid affinity for the stratum corneum. It absorbs quickly (commonly described as full absorption in well under ~30 min) and leaves little oily residue — strong sensory advantage.
- It is largely **substantive to the upper stratum corneum / skin surface** rather than deeply penetrating to the dermis (consistent with an emollient/occlusive, not a transdermal active). Its main "penetration" role is as a **carrier** that improves delivery of co-formulated lipophilic actives (ceramides, retinol, tocopherol, polyphenols).
- It does not need to reach living cells to work — barrier/emollient benefits are surface/SC-level.

## 6. Formulation

| Parameter | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Use level** | Versatile: ~1–5% as a co-emollient/sensory modifier; up to ~10–100% as a standalone facial oil (it is sold as a 100% oil). No defined "active dose" because the benefit scales with emolliency, not a receptor effect. |
| **pH** | pH-independent — chemically inert, stable across the full cosmetic pH range. Compatible with low-pH actives (vitamin C, AHAs) and high-pH systems alike. |
| **Stability** | **Excellent** — saturated, so resistant to oxidation; long shelf life; no special antioxidant package required (a major advantage over plant oils and over raw squalene). Heat-stable for emulsification. |
| **Pairings** | Plays well with almost everything: ceramides/cholesterol (barrier trio synergy), niacinamide, hyaluronic acid (pairs an occlusive with a humectant), retinol (buffers/solubilizes, may reduce irritation), vitamin C and E (carrier + stability), peptides. Excellent retinol/oil-phase vehicle. |
| **Conflicts** | Essentially none chemically. **Caution:** do not formulate with *squalene* (unsaturated) expecting squalane stability — squalene needs robust antioxidant protection or it forms irritant peroxides. **Malassezia note:** squalane is a saturated hydrocarbon, generally considered safe for fungal-acne–prone (Malassezia-folliculitis) users (unlike fatty-acid/ester oils), though formula-level testing is wise. |
| **Sensory** | Light, silky, fast-absorbing, "dry" finish, odorless, colorless. One of the best-feeling natural-derived emollients — a key reason it is so widely used. |

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation / sensitization:** Very low. The **CIR Expert Panel** assessed Squalane and Squalene as **safe as used** (Final Report 1982; re-review confirmed, *Int J Toxicol* / cir-safety.org). Squalene is a normal skin constituent; limited contact-sensitization testing showed it is not a significant allergen or irritant, and despite decades of heavy use there is a notable **lack of sensitization case reports.**
- **Comedogenicity:** **Squalane is non-comedogenic / very low** — saturated, so it cannot form the comedogenic squalene peroxides. **Squalene** (unsaturated), once oxidized to SQOOH, is **comedogenic and pro-inflammatory** (Condrò, Sciortino, Perugini 2023, *Pharmaceuticals* 16(12):1704: acne-prone skin had ~5× more squalene and peroxide-squalene present *only* in acne subjects). This is the core practical reason to use **squalane, not squalene.**
- **Sun sensitivity:** None — squalane does not cause photosensitivity. (It is not a sunscreen; do not imply photoprotection.)
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** No known concerns for topical squalane; it is inert, barrier-level, skin-mimetic. (Standard caveat: not medical advice.)
- **Sourcing / sustainability:**
  - **Shark-liver squalane** — historically the richest source; **ethically unacceptable and reputationally toxic** (deep-sea shark depletion). Avoid entirely; many regions/buyers reject it.
  - **Sugarcane (bio-fermented) squalane** (e.g., Amyris/Neossance) — microbially produced farnesene → squalane; **molecularly identical, high purity, predictable cost, vegan, shark-free.** Amyris has stated it supplies a large share of the squalane market and frames it as saving millions of sharks/yr. **Preferred for a premium, claims-clean brand.**
  - **Olive squalane** — from olive-oil refining by-product; plant-based and skin-aligned, but yields/quality fluctuate year to year and purity can vary.

## 8. Regulatory & claims

**Cosmetic vs. drug line (per CLAUDE.md guardrails):**

- ✅ **Safe cosmetic language (defensible):** "lightweight, skin-mimetic emollient," "helps soften and smooth," "supports a healthy moisture barrier," "helps reduce moisture loss (TEWL)," "non-greasy hydration," "fast-absorbing," "non-comedogenic," "vegan / shark-free (sugarcane-derived)," "helps deliver/carry other actives." All of these track to emollient-class function and tolerance data.
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation or avoid:** "boosts collagen," "increases procollagen," "reduces UV DNA damage," "antioxidant protection," "anti-wrinkle," "photoprotective," "anti-inflammatory," "heals." These either (a) are *squalene* benefits, (b) are *oral* benefits (Cho 2009 at gram doses with GI side effects), or (c) are in-vitro/animal — **none are substantiated for a topical squalane cosmetic.** Claiming them risks drug-territory and unsubstantiated-claim exposure.
- 🚫 **Do not claim** any disease treatment (acne, eczema, dermatitis "treatment/prevention"). Emollients *supporting* the barrier is fine; *treating a condition* is a drug claim.

**TL;DR for copy:** sell squalane on **feel, barrier support, hydration, tolerance, and clean sourcing.** Do **not** borrow squalene's oral collagen/UV story.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier:** **Low–moderate.** Squalane is a commodity-scale emollient. Sugarcane bio-squalane sits at a modest premium over basic plant oils but is inexpensive relative to "hero" actives (peptides, growth factors, encapsulated retinoids). It is cheap enough to use generously.
- **Suppliers:** Amyris/Neossance (sugarcane, the dominant bio source), Sophim (olive & sugarcane), Henry Lamotte Oils, and numerous distributors. Cosmetic-grade, high-purity, COA-backed material is readily available.
- **Supply note:** Bio-fermented squalane has a **more predictable cost/quality profile** than olive (no harvest-year volatility) and avoids shark-supply legal/ethical risk — strategically the right pick for a brand that buys commodity bases and invests in brand (matches the repo's product logic).

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

| Marketing claim seen in the wild | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Squalane is a powerful antioxidant" | The antioxidant chemistry belongs to **squalene** (the double bonds). Squalane is *saturated* — minimal direct radical scavenging. Overreach. |
| "Boosts collagen / anti-aging" | Only **oral squalene** at 13.5–27 g/day raised procollagen (Cho 2009), with GI side effects. Not shown for topical squalane. Unsubstantiated. |
| "Mimics your skin's natural oils / skin-identical" | Half-true. Skin makes **squalene**; squalane is the hydrogenated, more-stable cousin. Fair to say "inspired by / similar to a skin-native lipid," not "identical to your sebum." |
| "Photoprotective / sun protection" | No. Don't imply SPF or UV protection. |
| "Clinically proven to reduce wrinkles" | The split-face wrinkle data used squalane as the **placebo**. Active was fullerene-C60 / bakuchiol. Misattribution. |
| "Hydrates deeply / 40% more moisture" | It's an occlusive emollient (reduces water *loss*); it is not a humectant and doesn't add water. Marketing percentages are usually formula- or vendor-specific, not squalane-specific. |
| "Non-comedogenic, great for acne-prone" | True for **squalane**. (And avoiding oxidized squalene is genuinely relevant to acne.) Legitimate. |

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ SUPPORTING (workhorse emollient / vehicle) — not a hero active.**

One line: **Squalane is a best-in-class, stable, non-comedogenic, sustainably sourceable (sugarcane) emollient and active-carrier that belongs in the barrier-repair cream's lipid phase — but its anti-aging story is borrowed from squalene/oral data, so position it on feel, barrier support, and clean sourcing, never on collagen or antioxidant claims.**

- **Fit with the barrier-repair thesis:** Strong. It is exactly the kind of stable, skin-aligned lipid that complements the ceramide/cholesterol/fatty-acid barrier trio, and its elegant sensory + carrier function make a premium cream feel premium while improving delivery of true hero actives (retinoids, etc.).
- **Longevity-relevance note:** Real but modest and indirect. Its durable longevity angle is **barrier/inflammaging**: by using the *saturated* squalane (and by helping prevent the *unsaturated* squalene in skin from becoming pro-inflammatory peroxides under UV/pollution), it supports a calm, intact barrier — a legitimate skin-longevity lever — rather than directly hitting collagen/senescence/mitochondrial hallmarks.
- **Action:** Log in `ideas/considering.md` as a supporting/base ingredient; specify **sugarcane-derived (shark-free, vegan)** sourcing in any formulation; add "squalene vs. squalane / SQOOH" entries to `brand/glossary.md` so copy never conflates them.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

*Verified 2026-06-19 against primary sources (PubMed/PMC, CIR). This is a skeptical second pass; the dossier was already unusually disciplined, so most findings are citation-metadata fixes rather than overclaiming.*

### Overall evidence grade

**For TOPICAL squalane as an anti-aging active: D (essentially unproven topically / borrowed-evidence territory).**
**For TOPICAL squalane as an emollient / barrier-support / carrier ingredient: B (moderate — emollient-class + mechanistic + carrier evidence, but squalane-alone human RCTs with hard endpoints are effectively absent).**

The split grade matters and the dossier already makes it: squalane is a real, defensible *emollient* (B) but an unproven *anti-ager* (D). There is **no** A-grade (squalane-alone human-topical RCT with hard anti-aging endpoints) evidence anywhere in the file, and the verification found none externally either.

### What checked out (HUMAN/TOPICAL vs borrowed — audit)

- **Cho 2009 (procollagen / UV DNA damage), ref 3 — VERIFIED, and correctly labeled as ORAL.** Real (PMID 19522983, *Clin Exp Dermatol* 2009). It is **oral squalene at 13.5–27 g/day**, 40 women >50 (37 completed), procollagen-I mRNA ↑ in the low-dose group, UV thymine-dimers/apoptosis ↓, loose stool in 35%/55%. The dossier's own authors flagged this as oral and unusable for a topical claim — correct. *This is the single biggest "anti-aging" number in the file and it is NOT human-topical.*
- **Fullerene-C60 split-face, ref 13 — VERIFIED, and correctly framed as squalane = placebo.** Real (PMID 21137794). The substantive claim (squalane was the vehicle/placebo, active was fullerene-C60, 23 women, split-face, 8 wk, wrinkle benefit on the *active* side) is accurate. **Citation metadata was wrong** and has been corrected in-file: it is **Kato et al., 2010, *J Nanosci Nanotechnol* 10(10):6769–6774**, not "2011, *J Cosmet Dermatol*."
- **Wolosik 2025 (UVA-fibroblast protection), ref 2 — VERIFIED, but IN-VITRO and supplier-supplied material.** Real (PMC12291772). Genuinely about *squalane* (not squalene), which is a point in the dossier's favor — but it is **cultured human dermal fibroblasts, not skin**, at **micro-concentrations (0.005–0.015%)**, mechanism-only. Newly flagged in-file: the test squalane was **supplied by Aprinnova, LLC** (a commercial sugarcane-squalane producer). Academic grant funding, declared no COI — but treat as a mild material-provenance tie, not independent confirmation.
- **Condro 2023 (acne squalene peroxidation), ref 5 — VERIFIED.** Real (PMC10748031). Acne-prone skin ~4.7× more squalene (dossier's "~5×" is fair rounding); peroxidated squalene found **only** in acne subjects. **Small pilot: 10 acne + 5 controls.** No external funding, no COI — clean. Supports the squalane-not-squalene thesis.
- **CIR "safe as used", ref 17 — VERIFIED.** 1982 Final Report concluding Squalane and Squalene safe as used; reaffirmed (panel declined to re-open) in 2001. Dossier's "safe as used (1982; re-review confirmed)" is accurate.

### Main caveats (the honest read)

1. **Zero human-topical squalane RCTs with hard endpoints.** Every "clinical" datapoint either uses squalane as the **placebo/vehicle** (fullerene; bakuchiol) or is **oral squalene** (Cho) or **in-vitro/animal** (Wolosik, Condro, the peroxide chemistry). The dossier states this plainly in the Tier-4 intro and the format-attribution table — that honesty is the dossier's strongest feature and should be preserved.
2. **The strongest "anti-aging" signal is oral, gram-dose, with GI side effects** — and belongs to squalene, not squalane. Not transferable to a cream.
3. **The in-vitro squalane study uses supplier-provided material** at concentrations 100–1000× below in-use levels; useful for mechanism, not for efficacy claims.
4. **Small samples throughout** the human work (Condro n=15 total; fullerene n=23; bakuchiol n=47 and confounded; Cho n=40 and oral). None powered for, or designed to test, topical squalane alone.
5. **"Skin-identical" is half-true** — skin makes *squalene*; squalane is the hydrogenated cousin. The dossier already corrects this in §10.
