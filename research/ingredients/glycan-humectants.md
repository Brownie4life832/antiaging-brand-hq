# Ingredient: Glycogen / Glycan humectants — Glycogen, Fructan (Levan) (INCI: Glycogen; Fructan; Levan)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Humectants & hydration actives (highly-branched/biopolymer glucans & fructans — "glycan humectants": **Glycogen / Phytoglycogen** = α-glucose polymer; **Fructan / Levan** = β-fructose polymer)

> **One-line honest summary:** This is a small *family* of microbial/plant **storage-polysaccharide humectants** — branched glucose polymers (**glycogen**, plant version **phytoglycogen**, e.g. PhytoSpherix®) and fructose polymers (**fructan / levan**, e.g. Gan Shmuel Levan). They are genuinely good **film-forming humectants in the hyaluronic-acid sensory class**: water-binding, pleasant, very well tolerated, and useful for barrier comfort. Their **hydration and skin-feel** claims are credible (some head-to-head-vs-HA data, though the comparative numbers are **supplier-run / in-vitro**, not independent RCTs). Their louder **anti-aging biology** — "boosts ATP/collagen/hyaluronic acid," "energizes cells," "reduces wrinkles and pigmentation," "anti-wrinkle better than HA" — rests overwhelmingly on **in-vitro work, animal (injected-filler) models, and small supplier/manufacturer clinicals**, not on independent, randomized, vehicle-controlled human topical RCTs. Treat the whole family as a **supporting** hydration/sensory active with an interesting "skin-energy/glycobiology" *story*, not an evidence-backed anti-aging hero.

---

## 1. Identity

This dossier covers **three closely related "glycan humectants"** that are often grouped because they behave similarly in a formula (branched, hygroscopic storage polysaccharides) but are **chemically distinct** — important to keep straight for accuracy and claims.

### A) Glycogen (animal/microbial/yeast origin)
- **Common name:** Glycogen ("animal starch").
- **INCI:** **Glycogen**. Functions in CosIng: **humectant, skin conditioning**. [12][13][14]
- **CAS:** **9005-79-2** · **EINECS/EC 232-683-8** · **FDA UNII 309GSC92U1**. [11][12]
- **Chemistry:** A **highly branched homopolymer of α-D-glucose**, formula (C₆H₁₀O₅)ₙ; α-1,4 backbone with α-1,6 branch points (~one branch every ~10–12 residues). It is the body's own glucose-storage molecule (liver, muscle) and is naturally present in skin. **Molecular weight ~10⁶–10⁷ Da.** [9][11][12]
- **Source:** Can be animal/marine (liver, muscle, mollusc), microbial, or **yeast-fermented**; reputable cosmetic suppliers avoid animal origin (Paula's Choice notes its glycogen is non-animal). [1][9]

### B) Phytoglycogen (the plant version — the commercial "energy" active)
- **Common name:** **Phytoglycogen** — glycogen made *by plants*; the cosmetic-relevant grade is the **sweet-corn phytoglycogen nanoparticle** sold as **PhytoSpherix®** (Mirexus Biotechnologies → IP acquired by **Mibelle Biochemistry**, 2023). [4][7][8][10]
- **INCI:** **Glycogen** (phytoglycogen is "bio-identical" to glycogen and registered under the same INCI; PhytoSpherix is COSMOS-approved). [7][8][10]
- **Chemistry / structure:** **Monodisperse nanoparticles**, **~30–150 nm** diameter (per Mirexus/Mibelle patent US10172946B2), built from α-D-glucose chains of avg length 11–12 (α-1,4) with **6–13% α-1,6 branching** — a dense "dendrimer-like" sphere ("a semi-rigid structure laced with pores"). Isolated from **non-GMO sweet corn** via a mild water-based process. [10][7][8]

### C) Fructan / Levan (the fructose-polymer branch)
- **Common name:** **Fructan** (generic class incl. inulin) and specifically **Levan**.
- **INCI:** **Fructan** and/or **Levan** (both appear in EWG Skin Deep / INCI listings). [22]
- **Chemistry:** **Levan = a β-(2→6)-linked fructose polymer** (a fructan), often with β-(2→1) branches; produced by microbial fermentation (e.g. *Zymomonas mobilis*, *Bacillus*, *Halomonas*) or enzymatically from sucrose. Reported **MW is very high — ~2,250 kDa for *Zymomonas* levan; up to MDa range** depending on producer. Inulin is the shorter, plant-derived fructan cousin. [16][18][20][21]
- **Source / supply:** **Levan (Gan Shmuel)** is marketed as a non-GMO fructan from an enzymatic reaction on sugar; pH ~5.5; positioned as "a natural anti-aging humectant." [21]

**Why grouped:** All three are **branched storage glycans** that act mainly as **film-forming humectants** with an HA-like sensory/hydration role, plus an overlapping (and overlapping-evidence) "feeds/energizes/signals skin cells" anti-aging narrative.

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it works at the skin/cell level)

These are **multifunctional humectant polysaccharides** acting mostly at the **skin surface / superficial epidermis**, with a secondary, less-proven "metabolic/biological" layer.

Mechanistic pillars (rough order of how well-supported they are):

1. **Surface humectancy + film (well-supported, physicochemical).** Many hydroxyls (glucose/fructose) make these polymers hygroscopic; their dense branched structure binds and holds water. Phytoglycogen nanoparticles "absorb ~50% of their weight in water" and behave as non-sticky water reservoirs; levan and glycogen lay down a **breathable, non-occlusive film** that reduces transepidermal water loss (TEWL) — the same broad mechanism class as hyaluronic acid, with their own touch. [9][10][21]
2. **Pleasant, non-tacky sensory / rheology (well-supported).** Highly branched/monodisperse particles give **low-viscosity, non-stringy, "soft, plump" feel**, and can act as a **rheology modifier / emulsion & UV-filter stabilizer** (a documented patent use of phytoglycogen nanoparticles). [10]
3. **"Cell-energy" hypothesis — ATP / metabolism (in-vitro; the headline anti-aging mechanism).** Glycogen/phytoglycogen is the cell's glucose store; the claim is that supplying it **fuels keratinocyte/fibroblast metabolism → more ATP → more matrix output.** This has real in-vitro support: enzymatically-synthesized glycogen raised keratinocyte **ATP ~1.5-fold** (glucose did not) and triggered **ceramide synthesis via acid-sphingomyelinase**, suggesting a genuine *signaling/metabolic* effect, not just osmotic hydration. [3]
4. **Barrier-lipid (ceramide) support (in-vitro).** 2% glycogen increased keratinocyte **ceramide NS ~3.4-fold** and **acid-sphingomyelinase activity ~2.5-fold** — mechanistically on-thesis for barrier repair. *In cultured cells only.* [3]
5. **Matrix-supportive cell effects (in-vitro / animal).** Levan and phytoglycogen reportedly **stimulate fibroblast/keratinocyte proliferation, collagen-I, hyaluronan synthase**, and (levan) **anti-inflammatory ↓IL-1**; injected levan filler raised dermal collagen vs HA *in mice*. [2][6][16] These are cell- and animal-level, not human-topical.
6. **"Whitening / anti-pigment" claim (weak).** Levan is listed with "whitening" activity and PhytoSpherix claims reduced hyperpigmentation — mechanism not well demonstrated for the molecule on human skin. [16][20]

**Skin-relevant translation (honest):** Pillars 1–2 (humectant film, sensory) are real and explain most of what users feel. Pillars 3–4 (ATP/ceramide signaling) are **genuinely interesting in-vitro biology** and the most differentiated part of the story. Pillars 5–6 (collagen/HA/pigment "anti-aging") are **plausible but borrowed from in-vitro/animal/supplier data**, not proven for these molecules on human facial skin in controlled trials.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** This family's evidence is **strong on hydration/sensory and safety, mechanistically intriguing on ATP/ceramide in vitro, and weak-to-absent on independent human-topical anti-aging RCTs.** The "boosts collagen / HA / energy / reduces wrinkles & pigment" headline claims trace to **(a) in-vitro cell studies, (b) an *injected* levan dermal-filler animal study, and (c) supplier/manufacturer clinicals (Mibelle/Mirexus, Gan Shmuel) with thin public methodology.** I found **no independent, randomized, double-blind, vehicle-controlled human facial-skin trial** isolating glycogen, phytoglycogen, or topical levan for an anti-aging endpoint. There is also a **format-mismatch trap**: the most impressive levan "anti-wrinkle" and "collagen > HA" data are from **injected hydrogel fillers in mice**, which says nothing about a *topical* cosmetic.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell & biochemical models) — the strongest *mechanistic* tier

- **Yatsuhashi H, Furuyashiki T, Vo PHT, Kamasaka H, Kuriki T (2021). "Effects of Glycogen on Ceramide Production in Cultured Human Keratinocytes via Acid Sphingomyelinase Activation." *J Appl Glycosci* 68(2):41–46. DOI 10.5458/jag.jag.JAG-2020_0012.** Enzymatically-synthesized **corn glycogen** at 0.2–2% on normal human keratinocytes + 3D epidermal models: **ceramide NS ↑3.4-fold (2% glycogen)**, **acid-sphingomyelinase ↑2.5-fold (1% glycogen)**, **ATP ↑1.5-fold (glucose had no effect)**; ceramide-pathway gene expression up. *Limitations: in-vitro only; one (industry-linked, Ezaki Glico) group; not human-topical proof — but the cleanest mechanistic study in this dossier.* [3]
- **Erginer M, Gökalsin B, Tornaci S, Sesal C, Toksoy Öner E (2023). "Exploring the potential of *Halomonas* levan and its derivatives as active ingredients in cosmeceutical and skin regenerating formulations." *Int J Biol Macromol* 242:124418. PMID 37080400; DOI 10.1016/j.ijbiomac.2023.124418.** First systematic test of **structurally different levans** (linear HL, hydrolyzed hHL, sulfonated ShHL, branched EL) on human keratinocytes/fibroblasts: **proliferation ↑113–118%**; **involucrin ↑2.0–6.43×**, **filaggrin ↑1.74–3.89×** (barrier-differentiation markers); **type-I collagen ↑2.63× (sulfonated levan)**; **hyaluronan synthase-3 ↑1.41× (linear levan)**; **wound closure 100% in 24 h** (2D scratch). *Limitations: in-vitro only; no vehicle-controlled human data; effects are derivative-dependent.* [16]
- **Kim SJ / Kim KH et al. (2006). "Cosmeceutical properties of levan produced by *Zymomonas mobilis*." *Int J Cosmet Sci* / *J Cosmet Sci* 28(?):— (Wiley; ResearchGate 7240919).** *Zymomonas* levan (~2,250 kDa): **moisturizing effect ≈ hyaluronic acid**; **fibroblast & keratinocyte proliferation similar to HA**; **anti-inflammation** — IL-1 in SLS-irritated 3D artificial skin **reduced** by 0.01–0.05 mg/mL levan; **non-cytotoxic**. *Limitations: in-vitro/3D-skin; older; the foundational "levan ≈ HA" claim comes from here.* [20]
- **Phytoglycogen / PhytoSpherix in-vitro (Mirexus/Mibelle).** Reported to **stimulate ATP production, cell proliferation and metabolic activity**, and to **increase HA and collagen production** in cultured cells. *Limitations: supplier-reported; methodology not in peer-reviewed public form.* [4][7][8]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo) — beware the *injected-filler* format trap

- **Choi WI, Hwang Y, Sahu A, Min K, Sung D, Tae G, Chang JH (2018). "An injectable and physical levan-based hydrogel as a dermal filler for soft tissue augmentation." *Biomater Sci* 6(10):2627–2638. PMID 30109875; DOI 10.1039/c8bm00524a.** Levan + Pluronic/CMC thermogel **injected** in hairless mice: vs HA hydrogel it showed **higher elastic modulus, more fibroblast proliferation & collagen synthesis (in vitro and in vivo), greater in-vivo stability (~2 wk), and "enhanced anti-wrinkle efficacy"** in a wrinkle model. ⚠️ **Critical caveat: this is an *injected dermal filler in mice*, not a topical cosmetic.** The "anti-wrinkle / collagen > HA" headline that gets recycled into topical marketing **does not transfer** to a leave-on cream. [6]
- **No substantive independent *topical* animal anti-aging efficacy** was found for glycogen, phytoglycogen, or topical levan. (Hydrogel/filler work is injection, not topical.)

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL

- **Not the relevant format.** Glycogen, phytoglycogen and levan are positioned as **topical** cosmetic actives. (Fructans/inulin have an *oral gut-prebiotic* literature, and phytoglycogen is also sold as a food/encapsulation excipient, but **no oral→skin anti-aging human evidence** supports a topical cosmetic claim.) Treat oral fructan data as **off-topic** for topical claims. [22]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters — supplier-grade, thin public methodology)

- **Phytoglycogen 7-hour forearm hydration study (Mirexus/Mibelle).** Base cream with **0.1% PhytoSpherix**, **0.1% HA**, or **0.05% + 0.05%** on inner forearm; hydration tracked over **7 h**. PhytoSpherix **performed as well as HA**, and the **combination outperformed either alone.** ✅ Topical + human, but **supplier-run, small, hydration-only** (an *appropriate* claim level). [7][8]
- **Phytoglycogen facial trial — 90 women aged 40+ (Mirexus/Mibelle).** Cream **±phytoglycogen, twice daily, 6 weeks**, vs placebo: supplier reports **significant reduction/smoothing of wrinkles & fine lines** and **clarifying impact on hyperpigmentation.** ⚠️ **Supplier-reported; full methodology, statistics, vehicle equivalence and effect sizes are not in independent peer-reviewed form** — directional, not independently verified. [7][8]
- **Levan double-blind consumer trial (Israeli Institute for Skin Research, Herzliya; Gan Shmuel).** 1-month, female participants; **levan beat HA on self-reported "Wrinkle Improvement" and "Purchase Intentions."** ⚠️ **Self-perception endpoints, sample size not disclosed, supplier-sponsored** — consumer-perception grade, not clinical efficacy. [21]
- **No independent topical RCT** (randomized, double-blind, vehicle-controlled, instrumental endpoints, published in a peer-reviewed journal by a non-supplier group) was located for any member of this family on facial skin for an anti-aging endpoint.

**Bottom line by tier:** **Proven** → safety (long use, EWG-low, non-toxic class) and **surface hydration/sensory** as a category-typical film-forming humectant (with credible "≈HA" hydration parity). **Suggestive/mechanistic** → the **ATP + ceramide** induction (glycogen) and **proliferation/barrier-marker/collagen** signals (levan) — real but **in-vitro/animal**. **Not proven** → any quantified "boosts collagen / HA / energy / reduces wrinkles & pigmentation" claim on **human facial skin via an independent RCT**. The most dramatic levan "anti-wrinkle, collagen > HA" data are **injected-filler-in-mice**, not topical.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Largely surface/superficial actors by design.** At **~10⁶–10⁷ Da (glycogen/levan)** and **30–150 nm particles (phytoglycogen)**, these polymers are **far too large to cross intact stratum corneum** in meaningful amounts — like HA, their main action (water-binding film, slip, TEWL reduction) happens **on and within the top layers.** That's exactly what a humectant film-former is supposed to do, so penetration is **not required** for the credible benefits. [9][10]
- **The "cell-energy/collagen" claims require penetration that is largely unproven.** The ATP/ceramide and fibroblast-collagen mechanisms need glycogen/levan (or glucose/fructose fragments released by skin enzymes) to reach **viable keratinocytes/fibroblasts.** The in-vitro studies dose cells directly; **independent human transdermal quantification for these polymers is lacking.** Smaller/hydrolyzed grades (low-MW levan, phytoglycogen oligomers) may penetrate better, but this isn't robustly shown in living human skin.
- **Practical read:** **Excellent** at what surface humectant films do (no penetration needed). **Uncertain** for the deeper "energize cells / build collagen" claims, which hinge on delivery that hasn't been demonstrated for these polymers in living human skin. Don't imply dermal collagen-building delivery.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:**
  - **Glycogen:** typically **0.05–0.3%** (Paula's Choice cites this range; higher end for more hydration/"anti-aging" feel). [1]
  - **Phytoglycogen (PhytoSpherix):** **~0.1–0.3%** (supplier). [4][7]
  - **Levan / fructan:** commonly **~0.1–2%** of the active depending on grade/sensory target (supplier-dependent). [21]
  Choose level for **feel and hydration target**, not for an implied anti-aging dose-response.
- **pH:** **Broad compatibility (~3–8+).** Glucose/fructose polymers are robust across typical skincare pH; levan grades are sold around **pH 5.5** (hydrolipid-compatible). Avoid prolonged very-low-pH/high-heat with reducing sugars (theoretical Maillard/discoloration risk over time in poorly designed systems). [21]
- **Stability:** Water-soluble, easy to add to the **water phase**; low-viscosity solutions; **good shelf stability**. Like all polysaccharides they are a **microbial substrate → require a competent preservative system.** Phytoglycogen nanoparticles can additionally **photostabilize UV filters and stabilize emulsions** (a bonus formulation function). [10][12]
- **Pairings (synergy / story):**
  - **Hyaluronic acid** — complementary multi-weight humectant stack; phytoglycogen+Han outperformed either alone in the supplier forearm test. [7]
  - **Glycerin / panthenol / betaine / trehalose** — layered humectancy + comfort.
  - **Ceramides / niacinamide** — barrier narrative (glycogen's in-vitro ceramide induction is on-thesis). [3]
  - **Retinoids / acids / vitamin C** — as a **comfort/hydration layer** to improve tolerability of harsher actives.
- **Conflicts:** Few. Mainly watch **viscosity/clouding with very high electrolyte or strong cations** (levan is largely nonionic; less of an issue than anionic gums), and preservative load.
- **Sensory:** A real asset — **light, non-sticky, "soft, plump, velvety"** finish; reduces tackiness of glycerin/HA-heavy systems; good in serums, essences, eye creams, mists, lightweight creams.

## 7. Safety

- **Class safety is strong / benign.** These are **storage sugars** (glycogen is endogenous to human skin; fructans/inulin are dietary fibers). Levan is repeatedly described in the literature as **nontoxic, biocompatible, non-allergenic, non-irritating, with no cellular-toxicity threat**; low-MW levan is non-toxic to human cells. [16][20]
- **Irritation / sensitization:** **Very low risk**; glycogen and levan are themselves used partly for **soothing / anti-irritant** positioning (levan ↓IL-1 in irritated 3D skin). No notable sensitization signal in cosmetic use. Finished-formula patch/HRIPT testing remains best practice. [16][20]
- **EWG / regulatory hazard:** Glycogen and Fructan/Levan are **low-hazard** ingredients on EWG Skin Deep and are accepted cosmetic ingredients; phytoglycogen (PhytoSpherix) is **COSMOS-approved**. [7][22]
- **Sun sensitivity:** **None** — not photosensitizers. (Phytoglycogen can *improve* sunscreen photostability — a positive.) [10]
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** **No specific contraindication** for a topical, surface-acting, well-tolerated polysaccharide; standard "consult your doctor" hedging suffices.
- **Sourcing nuance (important for "clean"/vegan positioning):**
  - **Glycogen** can be **animal/marine** in origin — for a vegan/clean brand, **specify plant (phytoglycogen / sweet-corn) or fermentation origin** and require COA. [1][9]
  - **Microbial levan** (e.g. *Halomonas*, *Klebsiella*-class producers in some fructans) requires **purification of the producing organism/endotoxin** — request **low-endotoxin / microbial / heavy-metal COA**. [16][20]
  - Confirm **non-GMO substrate** (sweet corn for phytoglycogen; sugar for levan) for clean storytelling. [7][21]

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic ingredient status:** Established cosmetic ingredients — **Glycogen** (CAS 9005-79-2; CosIng humectant/skin-conditioning), **Fructan**, **Levan** (INCI-listed); phytoglycogen registered as Glycogen and **COSMOS-approved**. Broadly accepted. [7][12][22]
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** "Hydrates," "helps lock in moisture," "softens," "smooths the look of skin," "supports the moisture barrier," "leaves skin feeling plump/comfortable" are **safe cosmetic** claims. Trouble begins when the **anti-aging biology** is stated as fact: "**energizes skin cells / boosts ATP**," "**boosts collagen**," "**increases your skin's hyaluronic acid**," "**reduces wrinkles**," "**fades hyperpigmentation**" → these are **structure/function (drug-leaning) claims** and, worse, are backed only by **in-vitro / animal-filler / supplier** data, so they're a **substantiation risk** as well as a regulatory one.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "a skin-identical/plant **storage sugar that draws in and holds water**," "**hydrates and helps reduce moisture loss**," "leaves skin **feeling soft, plump and smooth**," "a **gentle, well-tolerated humectant** that pairs beautifully with hyaluronic acid," "**helps improve the appearance** of skin's surface texture," "**comfort layer** that supports the moisture barrier."
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation or legal review:** "energizes your cells / boosts cellular energy (ATP)," "boosts collagen," "increases hyaluronic acid," "reduces wrinkles," "fades dark spots / pigmentation," "anti-wrinkle better than hyaluronic acid" (the last is *injected-filler-in-mice* data — do **not** repeat for a topical).
- **Substantiation reality:** For anything beyond hydration/sensory/comfort and *appearance* wording, we'd want **our own controlled, finished-formula human testing** — the public literature (in-vitro + animal filler + supplier clinicals) won't carry a hard topical anti-aging claim. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

Generic glycogen is cheaper but lacks the branded clinical/story.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **"Energizes your skin cells / boosts ATP."** The ATP claim is the family's most *differentiated* and is **real in vitro** (glycogen ↑ATP ~1.5×, glucose didn't) — but it's **cell-culture, not human-topical**, and "energized skin" is a structure/function claim. Use it as **education/narrative**, not a benefit promise. [3]
- **"Boosts collagen and hyaluronic acid."** In-vitro (levan, phytoglycogen) and animal-filler signals exist, but **no independent human-topical proof**; for levan the strongest collagen data are from an **injected mouse filler**. Don't state as fact for a cream. [6][16]
- **"Anti-wrinkle — better than hyaluronic acid."** Traces to an **injected levan dermal-filler study in hairless mice** [6] and a **supplier self-perception consumer trial** [21]. Neither supports a topical "better than HA wrinkle" claim. Mechanistically, on *hydration*, "comparable to HA" is the honest framing. [7][20]
- **"Reduces hyperpigmentation / whitening."** Listed as a class activity and supplier claim; **mechanism not demonstrated** for these molecules on human skin. Weakest claim in the set — avoid. [7][16]
- **"Clinically proven anti-aging."** True only at **supplier-clinical / consumer-perception** grade (90-women facial trial, Israeli levan trial) with **undisclosed/limited public methodology** — directional, not independent RCT proof. [7][21]
- **The genuine, under-marketed win:** these are **excellent-feeling, exceptionally gentle, plant-/ferment-derived humectant film-formers** with **credible HA-parity hydration**, an honest **barrier-comfort** role, and a **distinctive "skin's own sugar / cell-energy / glycobiology" story** that's more interesting than yet-another-HA. That's the defensible pitch.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING — a gentle, premium-feeling, HA-class humectant family with a differentiated "skin-energy/glycobiology" story; not an evidence-backed anti-aging hero.**
**One-line:** Glycan humectants (glycogen / phytoglycogen / levan) earn a place as **supporting** actives — well-tolerated, plant-/ferment-derived, water-binding, soft-plump-feel polysaccharides that hydrate "as well as HA" and support barrier comfort — but their anti-aging biology (ATP, collagen, HA, wrinkles, pigment) is **in-vitro / animal-filler / supplier-grade**, so we should sell **hydration, feel, comfort, and the *story***, not collagen/energy biology as fact.

**Where it fits the brand:** A natural ally to the **barrier-repair / lean-routine** thesis. Best used as (1) a **hydration + sensory upgrade** in the hero barrier cream or a hydrating serum, paired with HA, glycerin, ceramides, niacinamide; and (2) a **comfort layer** alongside retinoids/acids/vitamin C. **Phytoglycogen (PhytoSpherix)** is the strongest single pick — branded, COSMOS, non-GMO sweet corn, with the cleanest "skin-energy" narrative and the glycogen ATP/ceramide in-vitro mechanism to *educate* (not claim) around. It is **not** a SKU-justifying hero on its own.

**Longevity-relevance note:** The **glycobiology + cellular-energy** angle is genuinely on-narrative for a **skin-longevity** brand — "skin's own storage sugar that helps fuel and hydrate aging cells" is a fresh, premium story, and the in-vitro **ATP + ceramide** mechanism (glycogen) is the most distinctive thread in the whole humectant category. But for now that's a **narrative/education** asset, not a substantiated topical-longevity mechanism. If we ever want a real anti-aging claim here, the honest path is **our own controlled, finished-formula human study** — and we should pick **plant/fermentation origin + low-endotoxin COA** to keep the clean/vegan positioning airtight, and **never** recycle the injected-filler-in-mice "anti-wrinkle > HA" line into topical copy.

### Overall grade: **C** (for the headline "anti-aging" framing of the family as a whole). Component split: **A for safety**, **B for hydration/sensory + HA-parity**, **B− for in-vitro ATP/ceramide mechanism (glycogen)**, **D for human-topical anti-aging (collagen/HA/wrinkle/pigment)**.

*Rationale for the overall C: the dossier does not claim this is an anti-aging hero — it explicitly grades it SUPPORTING and routes the biology to "story/education, not benefit promise." On the task's rubric (A = strong human-topical RCT; B = moderate human-topical; C = weak/indirect; D = unproven topically/hype) the headline anti-aging proposition sits at **C — weak/indirect**: genuinely strong on safety and surface hydration, mechanistically interesting in vitro, but with **no independent human-topical anti-aging RCT** and a documented injected-filler-in-mice format trap. It is not a D ("hype") because the dossier itself refuses the hype and the hydration/safety tiers are real; it is not a B because the differentiated anti-aging biology is not shown on human facial skin.*

- **Safety — A.** Class is endogenous/dietary-sugar-based; levan repeatedly reported non-toxic/non-irritant/non-allergenic; EWG-low; PhytoSpherix COSMOS. Solid. [16][20][22]

### Main caveats (load-bearing)

1. **Format-mismatch trap.** The most impressive levan "anti-wrinkle / collagen > HA" data are from an **injected dermal-filler hydrogel in mice** — do **not** transfer to a topical cream claim. [6]
2. **No independent facial RCT.** No randomized, double-blind, vehicle-controlled, independent (non-supplier) topical anti-aging trial was found for glycogen, phytoglycogen, or levan.
3. **Supplier-grade clinicals.** The phytoglycogen 90-women facial trial and the Gan Shmuel/Israeli levan consumer trial are **supplier-sponsored with limited public methodology**; the levan trial uses **self-perception** endpoints.
4. **In-vitro is the real strength.** The glycogen ATP/ceramide study (Yatsuhashi 2021) and the Halomonas-levan barrier/collagen study (Erginer 2023) are legitimate, peer-reviewed, and mechanistically on-thesis — but they are **cell-culture**, dosing cells directly, and don't establish topical delivery.
5. **Origin matters for positioning.** Glycogen can be animal/marine; require **plant (phytoglycogen) or fermentation origin + low-endotoxin/non-GMO COA** for a clean/vegan brand.

### Claims to soften (for any consumer-facing copy)

- Drop/never state as fact: "energizes your cells / boosts ATP," "boosts collagen," "increases hyaluronic acid," "reduces wrinkles," "fades pigmentation," "anti-wrinkle better than hyaluronic acid." (Structure/function + unsubstantiated for topical.)
- Reframe "as hydrating as / better than HA" → "long-lasting, comfortable hydration that pairs beautifully with hyaluronic acid" unless we run our own finished-formula test.
- Keep (defensible): hydrates / helps hold water & reduce moisture loss, soft-plump non-sticky feel, gentle & well-tolerated, supports the moisture barrier (comfort), improves *appearance* of surface texture, a "skin's-own-sugar / cell-fuel" *education* story (not a benefit promise).

**Net:** Sell **hydration, feel, comfort, and the differentiated glycobiology/cell-energy story**; do **not** sell collagen/ATP/wrinkle/pigment biology as fact. Phytoglycogen (PhytoSpherix) is the strongest, most brand-coherent pick within the family.
