# Ingredient: SenoCellTec™ — Alpine Rose + Apple Stem Cell senolytic/stem-cell blend (INCI: **Malus Domestica Fruit Cell Culture Extract · Vitis Vinifera (Grape) Fruit Cell Extract · Rhododendron Ferrugineum Extract · Glycerin · Pentylene Glycol · Aqua/Water**)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Senotherapeutics & senolytics (blended with "plant stem cell" actives) · **Slug:** `senocelltec`

> Exhaustive literature review. **Brutally honest up front:** SenoCellTec is a **branded blend launched 2025-11-08 by Mibelle Biochemistry (Switzerland)**. It bundles three of Mibelle's own actives: the **Alpine Rose Active** senolytic (the only genuinely novel, peer-reviewed-mechanism piece) plus two legacy **"plant stem cell" actives** — **apple (PhytoCellTec Malus Domestica)** and **grape (PhytoCellTec Solar Vitis / Vitis fruit cell)**. The senolytic claim rests on **one Mibelle-authored 2022 paper** that is **in-vitro only**; the "stem cell" claims rest on **2008-era marketing science** that the independent literature has substantially debunked as antioxidant/metabolite effects, **not** literal "stem-cell activation." Every efficacy datum below is **supplier-generated**. There is **no independent clinical replication** of SenoCellTec as a finished blend. Treat "clears zombie cells / reactivates your stem cells" as marketing-grade until proven in living human skin by a non-Mibelle group.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common / trade name:** **SenoCellTec™** (Mibelle Biochemistry). Marketed as a "next-generation senolytic" with a **clear–fill–revive** story.
- **What it actually is:** a **multi-extract cosmetic active blend**, not a single molecule. Full INCI (per Mibelle): *Malus Domestica Fruit Cell Culture Extract (and) Vitis Vinifera (Grape) Fruit Cell Extract (and) Rhododendron Ferrugineum Extract (and) Glycerin (and) Pentylene Glycol (and) Aqua/Water.* [1]
- **The three actives and their lineage:**
  1. **Rhododendron Ferrugineum Extract** = **Alpine Rose Active** (the senolytic; from alpine-rose petals/leaves, hand-picked Swiss Alps). Standalone INCI: *Rhododendron Ferrugineum Extract (and) Glycerin (and) Aqua/Water.* [2][3]
  2. **Malus Domestica Fruit Cell Culture Extract** = **apple stem cell** = the famous **PhytoCellTec™ Malus Domestica** from the Swiss **Uttwiler Spätlauber** apple. [4][5][9]
  3. **Vitis Vinifera Fruit Cell Extract** = **grape stem cell** ≈ **PhytoCellTec™ Solar Vitis** (Gamay Teinturier Fréaux red-flesh grape). [6][1]
- **Key forms / derivatives:** Alpine Rose Active also exists in an **oil-soluble** version ("AlpRose O," INCI *Rhododendron Ferrugineum Extract (and) Caprylic/Capric Triglyceride*). [2] The apple/grape actives are also sold individually (PhytoCellTec line). SenoCellTec itself is the **fixed pre-blended** combination.
- **Nomenclature caution — do not conflate three different alpine-rose actives:**
  - **Alpine Rose Active** = the **senolytic** (kills senescent cells) — the relevant piece in SenoCellTec.
  - **PhytoCellTec™ Alp Rose** = an **older, different** alpine-rose *leaf cell culture* active positioned for **stem-cell protection / UV defense** — NOT senolytic and NOT in SenoCellTec. [10]
  - **Rhododenol / rhododendrol** = an unrelated compound that caused leukoderma — **not present here**; do not confuse the genus name with that ingredient.

## 2. Class & mechanism

SenoCellTec markets a **three-step "clear–fill–revive"** mechanism. [1] Each step maps to one of the three actives, with very different evidence quality:

**(1) CLEAR — Alpine Rose Active (plausible senolytic mechanism; in-vitro efficacy from a non-peer-reviewed source).**
- A **senolytic** selectively *kills* senescent ("zombie") cells (vs a **senomorphic**, which only quiets them — note this is the opposite of OneSkin's OS-01, see [`senolytics-os01-peptide.md`](senolytics-os01-peptide.md)).
- The reported result: an alpine-rose glycerol/water extract **selectively reduced senescent fibroblasts without harming healthy fibroblasts**, with potency described as **comparable to the reference senolytic drug Navitoclax (ABT-263)**. **Provenance caveat (verified 2026-06-19):** this efficacy result is *not* original to the peer-reviewed Hubert et al. 2022 paper — that paper is a phytochemical-profiling study that merely *cites* the senolytic data from its own reference, **Wandrey, Schmid & Zülli (2020), *SKIN Care/HPC Today* 15:18–20 — a non-peer-reviewed trade magazine, authored entirely by Mibelle staff.** The specific "1% extract / 48 h" assay parameters do not appear in the peer-reviewed paper and could not be independently verified. [7][7a]
- **Proposed molecular mechanism:** the extract's **flavonoids (taxifolin-, quercetin-, (+)-catechin-derived)** drive senolysis by **inhibiting/downregulating the anti-apoptotic Bcl-2 family** — the *same* drug target as Navitoclax — re-sensitizing senescent cells to apoptosis. [7] This is mechanistically coherent: quercetin is itself a known senolytic, and Bcl-2/Bcl-xL inhibition is the canonical senolytic pathway. [7][13]

**(2) FILL — apple stem cell (PhytoCellTec Malus Domestica).** Claimed to "activate dermal stem cells," boost collagen and skin density. **Mechanistically overstated** (see §4/§10): the dedifferentiated apple *callus* culture is processed into an extract; benefits in skeptical independent analyses are attributed to **antioxidant/anti-inflammatory metabolites and the liposomal carrier**, NOT to literal cross-kingdom "stem-cell activation." [12] (Note: this skeptical reframing is **not** supported by the peer-reviewed review formerly cited here as [8] — see verification §13.)

**(3) REVIVE — grape stem cell (Solar Vitis).** Claimed to "enhance keratinocyte stem cell function," refine epidermis, strengthen barrier. Mibelle data show grape stem-cell extract raised epidermal stem-cell **colony-forming efficiency ~86%** in vitro and reduced UV-induced erythema in a placebo-controlled sun-lotion study — i.e., a **UV/antioxidant-protection** story, not regeneration de novo. [6]

**Net cell-level story (as claimed):** clear senescent fibroblasts (less SASP/inflammaging) → "free up" the dermal/epidermal compartment → metabolite-supported protection of resident skin stem cells → downstream collagen/ECM and barrier support. The **clear** step has the best mechanistic basis; **fill/revive** are protective/antioxidant effects dressed in regenerative language.

## 4. Evidence review by tier ← core of this dossier

**Overarching honesty flags:**
1. **All efficacy data are generated by the manufacturer (Mibelle) or its scientific collaborators.** No independent clinical trial of SenoCellTec (the blend) exists as of 2026-06.
2. The blend **launched 2025-11-08** — it is **brand new**; the "28-day clinical" data are **supplier marketing claims with no published sample sizes, statistics, or peer review located**. [1]
3. The evidence must be **un-bundled**: the senolytic piece (alpine rose) has one real in-vitro paper; the "stem cell" pieces rest on **2008 marketing science** the independent literature treats skeptically.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro & ex-vivo

**(a) Alpine Rose Active — what the peer-reviewed paper actually is vs. where the senolytic data lives (verified 2026-06-19).**
- **The peer-reviewed paper, Hubert, Kotland, Henes, Poigny & Wandrey (2022), *Cosmetics* 9(2):37 [7]**, is a **phytochemical-profiling study, NOT a senolytic-efficacy study.** Its goal was to chemically profile the hydro-glycerinated *R. ferrugineum* leaf extract (via "CARAMEL" — centrifugal partition chromatography + ¹³C-NMR dereplication). It identified **14 metabolites**: flavonoids derived from **taxifolin, quercetin, (+)-catechin**, plus flavanones (naringenin, farrerol, porriol), orcinol derivatives, phloroacetophenone, phenolic acids, and the triterpene **lupeol**. *This much is verified against the full text.* [7]
- **Authorship correction:** the lead and corresponding author (Jane Hubert) and Alexis Kotland are at **NatExplore SAS (France)**; only Henes, Poigny, Wandrey are Mibelle. So it is **partly** Mibelle-affiliated (3/5 incl. the active's developers) — a real COI, but not "wholly Mibelle."
- **Where the senolytic efficacy data actually comes from:** Hubert 2022 does *not* run the senolytic assay; it **cites** the result ("can eliminate senescent cells from the skin… similarly to the known senolytic drug Navitoclax… and in a double-blind, placebo-controlled trial significantly decreasing skin redness and increasing elasticity at 2%") from its **own reference: Wandrey F, Schmid D, Zülli F. (2020), *SKIN Care/HPC Today* 15:18–20 — a trade magazine, not peer-reviewed, authored entirely by Mibelle.** [7a] **The "1% extract / 48 h" assay parameters are NOT in the peer-reviewed paper** (the only "1%" there is HPLC solvent) and could not be independently confirmed.
- **Mechanism:** the peer-reviewed paper does support the *mechanistic hypothesis* — senolysis plausibly via flavonoid (hyperoside, taxifolin, naringenin, farrerol) **inhibition/downregulation of anti-apoptotic Bcl-2**, with in-silico docking of hyperoside to Bcl-2 cited. This is a **reasoned mechanistic argument from chemistry**, not a demonstrated mechanism in this paper. [7]
- **Grayanotoxin correction:** the claim that "the primary paper reports grayanotoxin I was NOT detected" is **misattributed** — grayanotoxin is **not mentioned anywhere in Hubert 2022.** Treat grayanotoxin-free status as an **unverified supplier assertion** requiring a CoA, not a peer-reviewed finding (§7). [unverified]
- **Limitations (honest):** the only peer-reviewed evidence is **chemical composition + a plausibility argument**; the actual senolytic-efficacy demonstration rests on a **non-peer-reviewed, all-Mibelle trade-magazine source**; the assay is **in-vitro fibroblast only**; "comparable to Navitoclax" is a **potency framing in a dish** from that trade source, not clinical equivalence. No ex-vivo or in-vivo human-skin senescence-*clearance* experiment exists for alpine rose.

**(b) Apple stem cell — Schmid, Schürch, Blum, Belser & Zulli (2008), *SOFW Journal* 134:30–35** ("Plant stem cell extract for longevity of skin and hair"). [9]
- Dedifferentiated **Uttwiler Spätlauber apple callus** stem cells, delivered in **lecithin liposomes**.
- **In-vitro:** extended the lifetime/proliferative capacity of **umbilical-cord-blood stem cells** and **hair-follicle cells**; protected stem cells from UV-induced apoptosis; aged epidermal stem cells "could still form a stratified epidermis." [9][4]
- **Critical caveat:** this is a **trade-journal (SOFW) paper from the ingredient's maker**, not an independent peer-reviewed dermatology study. It is the origin of nearly all "Swiss apple stem cell" marketing.

**(c) Grape stem cell — PhytoCellTec Solar Vitis (Mibelle technical data).** In-vitro: ↑ epidermal stem-cell **colony-forming efficiency ~86%**; metabolites protect skin stem cells from UV. [6] Supplier data; no independent replication located.

**(d) Independent in-vitro context (NOT the product):** an unrelated 2025 *Antioxidants* paper on **Annurca apple** polyphenol extracts modulating oxidative-stress-induced senescence in human dermal fibroblasts [15] supports the *general* idea that apple polyphenols affect fibroblast senescence — but it is a **different apple, different extract**, and does **not** validate PhytoCellTec Malus Domestica.

### Tier 2 — Animal
**None located** for SenoCellTec, Alpine Rose Active, or the apple/grape actives specifically. (Broader senolytic field: **topical ABT-263 in aged mice** reduced p16/p21 and SA-β-gal⁺ cells and improved wound healing — useful proof-of-concept that *topical senolysis is biologically real in skin*, but with a **transient inflammatory/macrophage response**, and it is **a drug in mice, not this botanical**. [14])

### Tier 3 — Human, ORAL
**Not applicable.** All components are topical cosmetic actives; no oral format or oral evidence. (So, unlike NAD precursors/spermidine/resveratrol, there is no "borrowed from oral studies" problem — but also no ingestion safety base. See [`spermidine.md`](spermidine.md), [`polyphenols-resveratrol.md`](polyphenols-resveratrol.md) for that contrast.)

### Tier 4 — Human, TOPICAL

**(a) SenoCellTec blend — Mibelle "28-day" in-vivo claim (no published trial located).** [1]
- **Claimed:** significant improvement in **hydration, firmness, elasticity, density** in 28 days, plus **visible wrinkle-depth reduction in nasolabial folds and crow's feet**, "even in areas affected by gravity."
- **Reality:** **no sample size, age range, control type, statistics, or peer-reviewed publication located.** This is a **supplier efficacy claim** for a product launched weeks before this review. Treat as **unsubstantiated for our purposes** until a methods-complete study appears.

**(b) Apple stem cell — the famous "100% of subjects" crow's-feet trial (Schmid et al. 2008).** [9][4][5]
- **Design:** emulsion with **2% PhytoCellTec Malus Domestica**, applied **twice daily for 28 days** to crow's feet; **N = 20**, ages **37–64**.
- **Results (as reported by the maker):** wrinkle depth **−8% at 2 weeks, −15% at 4 weeks**; "visible reduction in 100% of subjects."
- **Limitations:** **tiny N, manufacturer-run, trade-journal**, no independent replication; a ~15% instrumental wrinkle change at 4 weeks is **in the range of a decent moisturizer**. The "100%" figure is a **marketing headline**, not a rigorous endpoint.

**(c) Alpine Rose Active — in-vivo claims.** Reduced **skin redness**, increased **elasticity**, protected proteins from oxidative stress; a **scalp study** showed ↓ redness/itch and ↑ hydration/barrier over ~4 weeks. [2][3][11] Supplier data; small/uncontrolled detail; **no published vehicle-controlled facial RCT of the senolytic clearing wrinkles** located.

**What is actually proven for TOPICAL human use:** essentially **modest, moisturizer-grade appearance/comfort benefits** (hydration, redness, elasticity, small instrumental wrinkle change) from **small, manufacturer-run studies** — i.e., these actives behave like **competent antioxidant/soothing botanicals in a good base.** The headline **"clears senescent cells in your skin"** is demonstrated **only in a fibroblast dish (alpine rose)**, never in living human skin in a controlled topical trial. The **"reactivates/activates your stem cells"** claim is **not literally supported** and is downgraded by independent reviewers to antioxidant/metabolite action. [8][12]

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- The actives are **water-soluble extracts of small-molecule metabolites** (flavonoids, phenolic acids, lupeol; apple/grape metabolites + a liposomal carrier for the apple active). Unlike large peptides/exosomes, **flavonoid aglycones and small polyphenols can plausibly reach the viable epidermis/upper dermis** — the rule-of-500 is favorable for many of these constituents.
- **BUT** the senolytic effect was shown on **fibroblasts in a dish** (cells directly bathed in 1% extract). **Whether enough intact senolytic flavonoid reaches dermal fibroblasts in living skin at a cosmetic 2% blend dose to actually clear senescent cells is UNPROVEN.** No published human penetration/PK data for any of the three actives located. [unverified penetration→efficacy link]
- The apple active relies on a **liposome carrier** to aid delivery of its metabolites. [9]
- Net: penetration of the *constituents* is plausible; **functional penetration sufficient for in-vivo senolysis is the central open question** — same gap that dogs the whole topical-senolytic category. [14]

## 6. Formulation

- **Use level (SenoCellTec blend):** **2%** recommended; **water-soluble liquid**, supplied **alcohol- and preservative-free** (so the *finished* formula needs its own preservation). [1]
- **Component use levels (if sourcing separately):** Alpine Rose Active **1–3%** [2]; PhytoCellTec Malus Domestica typically **0.1–2%** (liposomal; classic 2% in studies) [4]; Solar Vitis low % in aqueous phase.
- **pH:** not published for the blend; flavonoid/polyphenol actives are generally happiest **mildly acidic to near-neutral (~pH 4.5–6.5)**; avoid extremes that degrade flavonoids. [unverified exact pH]
- **Phase / processing:** add to the **aqueous phase**, pre-dissolved, during **cool-down (<60 °C)** — the cell-culture/metabolite actives are **heat-sensitive**; do not boil. [10]
- **Stability:** polyphenols/flavonoids are **oxidation- and light-sensitive**; favor low-oxygen processing, opaque/airless packaging, possibly chelators/antioxidants. Preservative-free supply = **robust preservation system required** in the finished product.
- **Pairings (well-matched):** humectants (glycerin, HA — see [`hyaluronic-acid.md`](hyaluronic-acid.md)), barrier lipids (ceramides, squalane — [`ceramides.md`](ceramides.md)), niacinamide ([`niacinamide.md`](niacinamide.md)), other antioxidants (vitamin E/ferulic — [`vitamin-e-tocopherols.md`](vitamin-e-tocopherols.md), [`ferulic-acid.md`](ferulic-acid.md)). Conceptually complementary to the OS-01 senomorphic peptide as a "clear + prevent" senescence pairing — but that is **strategy, not tested co-formulation**.
- **Conflicts:** avoid co-formulating with **strong oxidants or extreme-pH systems** (high-% AHA/BHA, anhydrous L-ascorbic acid at very low pH) that can degrade flavonoids; layer such actives at a different step.
- **Sensory:** liquid, water-soluble, fragrance-/alcohol-free — easy, lightweight, sensitive-skin-friendly base behavior.

## 7. Safety

- **General tolerability:** the actives are positioned as gentle, COSMOS/NATRUE-grade botanicals; alpine-rose in-vivo skin/scalp studies report **good tolerance** and even **barrier/redness improvement** (used on sensitive/atopic-prone skin). [11] No notable irritation signal in the small supplier studies.
- **Grayanotoxin (the key Rhododendron worry):** the genus *Rhododendron* contains toxic **grayanotoxins** (the "mad honey" toxin; oral ingestion causes bradycardia/hypotension). The cosmetic extract is flavonoid-driven, and topical exposure to a COSMOS-certified extract is a **different risk class** from eating raw rhododendron. **Correction (verified 2026-06-19):** the earlier statement that "the primary paper reports grayanotoxin I was NOT detected" was **misattributed — grayanotoxin is not mentioned in the peer-reviewed Hubert et al. 2022 paper.** Grayanotoxin-free status is therefore an **unverified supplier assertion**, not a published finding; a finished-formula **toxin spec / CoA confirming grayanotoxin-free is a required due-diligence ask**, not a nice-to-have. [16][unverified]
- **Sensitization/allergen:** botanical extracts can sensitize in principle; **no published HRIPT/sensitization data located** for the blend. Standard cosmetic safety assessment + patch testing recommended; don't claim "for everyone."
- **Sun sensitivity:** none indicated (not an exfoliant or photosensitizer; some photoprotective antioxidant action). Still pair with SPF for any anti-aging routine. [`sunscreen-photoprotection.md`](sunscreen-photoprotection.md)
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** no specific contraindication identified, but **no pregnancy safety data**; default to conservative "consult your doctor," do not market as pregnancy-safe without data.
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** strong story — **plant-cell-culture / biotech** production means rare/protected species (alpine rose, Uttwiler Spätlauber apple, red-flesh grape) are **not wild-harvested at scale**; vegan; COSMOS/NATRUE/Halal certified; Swiss provenance. [1] Cleaner provenance than animal-derived exosomes/growth factors ([`exosomes.md`](exosomes.md), [`growth-factors.md`](growth-factors.md)).

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

**The line:** under US FDA, **intended use** decides cosmetic vs drug. Claiming a product **alters the structure/function of skin** — "clears senescent cells," "activates/reactivates stem cells," "boosts collagen," "reverses aging" — pushes it toward **drug** territory; there is **no OTC anti-aging/anti-wrinkle monograph**, and FDA has issued warning letters for such claims on cosmetics. [17] The senolytic/stem-cell language here is **structure/function-heavy** and needs careful handling.

- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "inspired by senolytic skin-longevity science," "helps visibly improve firmness, elasticity and density," "supports skin's natural renewal and resilience," "antioxidant defense / helps protect against environmental stress," "helps reduce the look of redness," "visibly smooths the appearance of fine lines."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like — avoid or legal-review:**
  - "**clears / eliminates / kills your zombie (senescent) cells**" — pharmacological structure/function; only shown **in vitro** for alpine rose; do not imply it happens in the user's skin.
  - "**activates / reactivates your (skin) stem cells**" — structure/function **and** scientifically contested for plant stem cells (§10).
  - "**boosts collagen / increases skin density / reverses aging**" — structure/function; supplier-only substantiation.
  - "**comparable to Navitoclax**" — invokes a **drug** comparison; keep in scientific/internal context, never consumer copy.
- **"Senolytic" / "senotherapeutic"** are science terms, **not regulated cosmetic claims** — usable *educationally* but must be paired with appearance-based benefit language and must not imply disease treatment.
- **EU/UK:** all INCIs are recognized; claims must meet the **EU Cosmetics Claims Regulation** (evidential support, fair/honest, no misleading "biological-age"/"stem-cell-reversal" promises). The **grayanotoxin** angle means EU safety assessors will want toxin data on file.

See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md) claim cheat-sheet.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Supplier:** **Mibelle Biochemistry (Switzerland)**, via distributors (Safic-Alcan, ULProspector, SpecialChem; small-batch via Lotioncrafter/Trulux for the component actives). SenoCellTec is a **single-source, branded blend** — buying it = buying Mibelle's IP/story. [1][2][10]
- **Cost tier:** **premium specialty active.** PhytoCellTec actives have long commanded high per-kg prices and low MOQs; a tri-active "senolytic" launch blend will sit at the **top of the botanical-active price band** (well above commodity extracts; below exosomes). [unverified exact price]
- **Alternative sourcing:** the **constituent senolytic chemistry is cheap and generic** — **quercetin** (and fisetin) are inexpensive, evidence-backed senolytic flavonoids you could formulate directly, capturing much of the *mechanism* without the branded blend or its premium. [7][13] Trade-off: you lose the turnkey "Swiss / COSMOS / clinical claim" package and the marketing name.
- **Single-source risk:** dependence on one supplier's proprietary blend for a hero active is a supply/availability and pricing-leverage risk.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **Conditional — strong narrative fit, thin proof. Best as a "supporting senescence/antioxidant active," NOT a sole hero, and only with disciplined claims + sourcing diligence.**

- **Why it's tempting:** it's a **turnkey, COSMOS/NATRUE, Swiss-biotech "senolytic" package** with a clean sustainability story and a genuinely on-trend mechanism (the one truly novel piece — Alpine Rose Active — has a real, peer-reviewed in-vitro senolytic mechanism with a Navitoclax-level potency framing). It slots perfectly into a repair/renewal, skin-longevity thesis and pairs conceptually with the OS-01 **senomorphic** peptide as a "clear (kill) + prevent (quiet)" senescence story. *(Honesty correction: the peer-reviewed literature supports the alpine rose **chemistry and a plausible Bcl-2 mechanism**, but the senolytic **efficacy** datum itself sits in a non-peer-reviewed all-Mibelle trade source [7a] — so even "the one genuinely novel piece" is less independently proven than first written. See §13.)*
- **Why it's only conditional:** the **strongest data are in-vitro**, the **clinical data are supplier-only and (for the blend) unpublished/brand-new**, and **two of the three actives ("apple/grape stem cell") rest on marketing science the independent literature has largely reframed as antioxidant action.** A premium brand differentiating on **claims honesty** cannot in good conscience say "clears your zombie cells / reactivates your stem cells."
- **Longevity relevance:** **High in narrative, low-to-modest in proven topical effect.** It's an excellent *story* hook for a senescence/longevity line; the honest performance ceiling today is "antioxidant, soothing, mild firmness/hydration support."
- **Gating conditions before any hero/marquee use:** (1) obtain **methods-complete supplier study data** (N, controls, stats) for the 28-day blend trial and judge it on merits; (2) confirm a **grayanotoxin-free CoA** and finished-formula safety assessment; (3) lock a claims strategy using **"inspired by senolytic science / supports / visibly"** language and **never** "clears senescent cells / reactivates stem cells / reverses aging" in consumer copy; (4) evaluate the **generic-quercetin/fisetin** route as a cheaper, equally-evidenced way to claim the *mechanism*.
- **Alternative / pairing:** lead the senescence story with the **better-characterized OS-01 senomorphic peptide** ([`senolytics-os01-peptide.md`](senolytics-os01-peptide.md)) and/or **GHK-Cu** ([`copper-peptides-ghk-cu.md`](copper-peptides-ghk-cu.md)); use SenoCellTec (or plain quercetin) as a **supporting antioxidant/"senolytic-inspired" botanical**, not the headline proof.

## 13. Evidence verification (adversarial)

_Independent fact-check performed 2026-06-19. Primary sources (Hubert et al. 2022 and the Miastkowska & Sikora review) were retrieved as full PDFs and read; the apple-stem-cell trial, the topical-ABT-263 mouse study, and the SenoCellTec launch were cross-checked against multiple secondary sources. MDPI blocked direct fetch, so peer-reviewed papers were verified via Semantic Scholar PDF mirrors._

### Overall evidence grade: **D** (essentially unproven topically / hype) — with **one C-grade exception** for the alpine-rose antioxidant/redness piece.

For the *headline* claims ("clears senescent cells," "reactivates stem cells," "clinically proven rejuvenation in 28 days") in **living human skin**, the topical evidence is **D**: no peer-reviewed human-topical RCT exists for the blend or for senescent-cell clearance by any of the three actives, and the strongest mechanistic claim turns out to rest on a non-peer-reviewed source. The only thing that climbs to **C** is the narrower, modest claim that alpine-rose extract gives **antioxidant / anti-redness / mild elasticity** benefit topically — and even that is supplier-run and small. The blend as a whole, judged on its marquee senolytic/stem-cell positioning, is **D**.

### What survived verification (accurate in the dossier)
- The **apple stem cell trial** [9] is exactly as described: N=20, ages 37–64, 2% twice daily / 28 days, crow's feet, "−8%/−15% wrinkle depth," "100% of subjects" — and it is a **Mibelle trade-journal study**. The dossier's skeptical framing is fair.
- The **topical ABT-263 mouse study** [14] is real and correctly represented (aged mice, ↓p16/p21 & SA-β-gal⁺ cells, improved wound healing, transient macrophage inflammation; a drug, not this botanical).
- **SenoCellTec is a brand-new (Nov 2025) Mibelle blend** with supplier-only "28-day clinical" claims and no published methods — confirmed. (Minor: trade press dates the launch ~Nov 4, 2025, not Nov 8; immaterial.)
- The extract's **chemistry** (taxifolin/quercetin/(+)-catechin-derived flavonoids, hyperoside, naringenin, farrerol, lupeol) and the **flavonoid→Bcl-2 mechanistic hypothesis** are genuinely in the peer-reviewed Hubert 2022 paper.
- **No oral-data borrowing** problem (all actives are topical) — correctly noted.

### Defects found and corrected (the dossier was overstating its best evidence)
1. **The senolytic *efficacy* claim is NOT peer-reviewed.** The dossier elevated **Hubert et al. 2022 [7]** to "the single most important primary source for the senolytic claim." On reading the full text, that paper is a **phytochemistry/profiling study** that does *not* perform the senolytic assay — it **cites** the "selectively kills senescent cells ~Navitoclax" result from its own reference, **Wandrey, Schmid & Zülli (2020), HPC Today [7a] — a non-peer-reviewed trade magazine, 100% Mibelle-authored.** Corrected throughout; added [7a].
2. **The "1% extract / 48 h" assay parameters are unverifiable.** They are not in the peer-reviewed paper (its only "1%" is HPLC solvent) and trace to the trade source. Softened to "reported."
3. **The grayanotoxin-I-not-detected finding was misattributed** to Hubert 2022 — grayanotoxin is **not mentioned anywhere** in that paper. Downgraded to an **unverified supplier assertion**; a grayanotoxin-free CoA is now flagged as **required**, not optional (§7).
4. **Citation [8] is wrong on author and stance.** The DOI cited (Cosmetics 5(4):55) is **Miastkowska & Sikora (2018)**, a **favorable** review — not the skeptical "Mokhtari/Nafisi/Maibach" paper, which is a separate **Cosmetics & Toiletries trade** article. The dossier's "independent peer-reviewed reviewers debunk plant stem cells" claim was over-credited; the genuine skeptical sourcing is [12] (industry) + general consensus. Corrected in §2, §10, and Sources.
5. **Authorship nuance on [7]:** lead/corresponding author (Hubert) and Kotland are **NatExplore SAS (France)**, not Mibelle; the COI is real but partial (3/5 authors), not "wholly Mibelle." Clarified.

### Main caveats (for any go/no-go decision)
- **Every efficacy datum is supplier-origin**, and the single most important one (senolysis) is **not peer-reviewed** — it lives in a Mibelle trade magazine.
- **Zero in-vivo or ex-vivo demonstration of senescent-cell clearance in human (or even mouse) skin** for this botanical. The "clears zombie cells" headline is a **dish result reported in non-peer-reviewed copy.**
- The **"activates stem cells"** claim is both structure/function (regulatory risk) and **scientifically contested** — plant cells in product are not alive and cannot activate human cells.
- **Grayanotoxin status is unverified** without a CoA.
- Net: this is a **strong story on thin, largely non-independent proof.** Honest performance ceiling today: a competent **antioxidant / soothing / mild-firmness botanical** — the same as the dossier's §4 conclusion, now with the evidentiary floor correctly lowered.

### Claims that should be softened (and have been, in-text)
- "genuine senolytic mechanism" → **"plausible senolytic mechanism; in-vitro efficacy from a non-peer-reviewed source."**
- "the primary paper reports grayanotoxin I was NOT detected" → **misattributed; treat as unverified supplier assertion.**
- "1% extract for 48 h … comparable to Navitoclax" → **"reported"; parameters unverifiable; efficacy datum is trade-source [7a].**
- "independent (peer-reviewed) reviewers conclude … antioxidant, not stem-cell activation [8]" → **skeptical view carried by industry source [12]; the cited peer-reviewed [8] is actually favorable.**
- Hallmarks table "Cellular senescence ✅ Primary" → **⚠️ (in-vitro, trade-source).**
- §11 "a real, peer-reviewed in-vitro senolytic mechanism" → qualified to **chemistry + plausible mechanism peer-reviewed; efficacy not.**
