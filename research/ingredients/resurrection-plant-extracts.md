# Ingredient: Resurrection Plant Extracts — *Haberlea rhodopensis* & *Myrothamnus flabellifolia* (INCI: Haberlea Rhodopensis Leaf Extract; Myrothamnus Flabellifolia Leaf/Stem Extract)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Humectants & hydration actives (more precisely: desiccation-tolerance / "anhydrobiosis"-inspired botanical cell-protectants — a cousin to extremolytes like ectoine/trehalose)
**Evidence verdict at a glance:** A genuinely interesting *story* active with real, on-mechanism science for **oxidative-stress defense + hydration/barrier protection**, anchored mostly in **in-vitro** work plus a **small number of supplier-run human cosmetic trials**. The single most-cited "anti-aging" human result (Haberlea, n=20) is a **supplier study** with a hydration/radiance-grade readout, not independent remodeling proof. The related single-molecule actives — **glyceryl glucoside** (Myrothamnus-derived) and **trehalose** — actually have *better* human-topical evidence than the whole-plant extracts. Honest grade: **moderate, leaning weak** for the bold anti-aging claims; **moderate** for hydration/antioxidant-defense framing. Best used as a **supporting** narrative active, not a hero.

---

## 1. Identity

**"Resurrection plants"** are ~330 desiccation-tolerant ("poikilohydric") higher-plant species that can lose >90% of their water, sit dormant for months to years, and fully revive within hours of rehydration. In cosmetics, the term covers several distinct species with *different* chemistry — they are not interchangeable, and copy that treats them as one thing is botanically wrong.

The two named in this dossier's slug, plus the close cousins that matter commercially:

- **_Haberlea rhodopensis_ Friv.** (Gesneriaceae) — "Orpheus flower," a Balkan endemic (Bulgaria/Greece, Rhodope Mountains).
  - **INCI:** *Haberlea Rhodopensis Leaf Extract* (also produced from *in-vitro* / cell culture to avoid harvesting a protected wild plant).
  - **Signature actives:** **myconoside** (a caffeoyl phenylethanoid glycoside; the dominant phenolic, reported up to ~330 mg/g dry weight / ~89% of the phenolic mixture) and **paucifloside**, plus hispidulin-derived flavone C-glycosides. (Ebrahimi et al.; Mihaylova; Staneva et al.)
  - **Marketed as:** the branded cosmetic active commonly sold under the "Resistem"-type positioning (supplier lineage Vincience → ISP → **Ashland**), and as cell-culture grades from other suppliers (e.g. INNOVA BM).
- **_Myrothamnus flabellifolia_ Welw.** (Myrothamnaceae) — "resurrection bush," a woody southern-African shrub (Namibia, South Africa, etc.); also written *M. flabellifolius*. A traditional caffeine-free medicinal/herbal "tea."
  - **INCI:** *Myrothamnus Flabellifolia Leaf/Stem Extract* (sometimes *Myrothamnus Flabellifolia Extract*).
  - **Signature actives:** **3,4,5-tri-O-galloylquinic acid** (the dominant polyphenol, reported >50–70% of dry weight in some Namibian populations), galloylated **arbutin** derivatives, quercetin/catechin flavonoids, anthocyanins, and the disaccharide **trehalose** + the glucosylglycerol **glyceryl glucoside**. (Bentley, Moore & Farrant 2019; Moore et al.)
  - **Marketed as:** RAHN's **Myramaze** (water/1,3-propanediol extract of leaves & twigs).
- **Related "resurrection-derived" single molecules (better-evidenced; see §4):**
  - **Glyceryl glucoside (glucosylglycerol)** — INCI *Glyceryl Glucoside*; naturally made by *Myrothamnus* and cyanobacteria; sold as **Glycoin natural** (CLR). This is a *defined molecule*, not a whole-plant extract, and has a real peer-reviewed human-skin paper (Schrader et al. 2012).
  - **Trehalose** — already covered in its own dossier (`trehalose.md`); the *Myrothamnus* protective mechanism leans heavily on it.
  - ***Craterostigma plantagineum*** — the lab "model" resurrection plant; not a major cosmetic INCI but appears in desiccation-tolerance science used to justify the category.
- **Physical/forms:** Typically aqueous or glycol (glycerin/propanediol) leaf extracts, standardized loosely to total phenolics or to a marker (myconoside; galloylquinic acid). Cell-culture (biotech) grades exist specifically to dodge sustainability/CITES-style concerns around wild Balkan *Haberlea*.

## 2. Class & mechanism

The category's whole pitch is **biomimicry of anhydrobiosis** ("life without water"): resurrection plants survive desiccation using the *same* toolkit that protects skin against drying and oxidative stress, so transferring their metabolites to skin should confer "resilience." The mechanism is real and reasonably well-characterized at the plant-biology level; the leap is whether it translates to a cosmetic facial benefit.

Core mechanisms (by actor):

- **Compatible-solute / water-replacement protection (trehalose, glyceryl glucoside).** During drying, resurrection plants accumulate non-reducing sugars (trehalose) and glucosylglycerol (glyceryl glucoside) that form glassy, hydrogen-bonded matrices, replacing water at membrane and protein surfaces and vitrifying the cytoplasm — preventing membrane fusion and protein denaturation. This is the *same* "preferential hydration / membrane stabilization" logic as ectoine and is the most mechanistically solid part of the story. On skin: humectancy + membrane/barrier stabilization + reduced TEWL.
- **Polyphenol antioxidant shielding (myconoside; 3,4,5-tri-O-galloylquinic acid).** Desiccation generates massive ROS; the plants survive partly by stockpiling potent radical-scavenging polyphenols. Myconoside is repeatedly described as a strong radical scavenger that inhibits lipid peroxidation and, at low concentrations, *increases membrane lipid order and strengthens adherens junctions* in cells. Galloylquinic acid protects membranes from desiccation-induced ultrastructural ROS damage. On skin: antioxidant defense and (claimed) protection of dermal matrix from UV-induced oxidation.
- **ECM / "longevity-gene" signaling (Haberlea, supplier mechanism).** The supplier narrative is that the extract stimulates fibroblast synthesis of collagens and elastin and "delays senescence / activates longevity genes." The collagen/elastin part has *in-vitro* support (Dell'Acqua & Schweikert 2012); the "longevity gene / FOXO / senescence-delay" framing is largely **marketing language not anchored to a specific published skin dataset** — treat as **[unverified]**.
- **Aquaporin-mediated hydration (glyceryl glucoside).** Glyceryl glucoside upregulates **aquaporin-3 (AQP3)** — the water/glycerol channel — in human keratinocytes, improving water transport, barrier function and hydration (Schrader et al. 2012). This is the best-defined molecular mechanism in the whole category, but it belongs to the *single molecule*, not the crude *Myrothamnus* extract.
- **Autophagy modulation (Myrothamnus + Coffea blend).** A blend modulated autophagy markers (↑LC3B, ↓mTOR/Atg7, ↑Park2) in fibroblasts — an on-trend "cellular housekeeping / longevity" mechanism, but tested as a *2-species blend*, not isolated *Myrothamnus* (Biscaro et al. 2022).

**One-line mental model:** Resurrection-plant actives are *desiccation-survival chemistry borrowed for skin* — humectant sugars + bodyguard polyphenols that help skin cells and the barrier hold water and resist oxidative stress. They are a **resilience/defense** story far more than a proven **regeneration** story.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Relevance | Evidence quality (topical human skin) |
|---|---|---|
| **Hydration / water loss (TEWL)** | Primary, on-mechanism (trehalose, glyceryl glucoside, polyphenols) | Moderate (best for glyceryl glucoside; Myrothamnus extract supplier-grade) |
| **Barrier decline** | Membrane/lipid stabilization, AQP3 upregulation, TEWL reduction | Moderate (glyceryl glucoside human data; extract = supplier/in-vitro) |
| **Oxidative stress** | Core mechanism — potent polyphenol radical scavenging | Moderate–strong *in vitro*; weak *in vivo on skin* |
| **Photoaging** | Claimed protection of dermis from UV-induced oxidation | Weak (one ex-vivo biopsy result; not a sunscreen) |
| **Collagen / ECM loss** | ↑collagen VI/XVI/elastin mRNA in stressed fibroblasts | Weak (in-vitro only; not human-skin remodeling) |
| **Inflammaging / soothing** | Claimed redness/irritation reduction (Myramaze) | Weak–moderate (supplier claims; some AD-adjacent data) |
| **Cellular senescence** | "Delays senescence / longevity genes" | **None published & verifiable** (marketing) |
| **Mitochondrial decline** | ↑mitochondrial membrane potential, ATP (in vitro) | Weak (in-vitro: Haberlea cell study; glyceryl glucoside ATP) |
| **Glycation / AGEs** | Not a meaningful anti-glycation actor | None established |
| **Pigmentation / tone** | Arbutin content (Myrothamnus); spot reduction in a blend RCT | Weak–moderate (confounded; arbutin is itself regulated) |
| **Microbiome** | Antimicrobial polyphenols/essential oil (Myrothamnus) | Weak (antimicrobial ≠ "microbiome support"; could even disrupt) |

**Bottom line on hallmarks:** This is a *hydration + antioxidant-defense + barrier-resilience* family. The "regenerative" hallmarks (senescence, collagen remodeling, glycation) are **not** proven for topical human use; the senescence/longevity-gene framing is essentially unsupported marketing.

## 4. Evidence review by tier (core of the dossier)

> **The single most important honest framing:** the resurrection-plant *category* is rich in beautiful *plant-biology* and *in-vitro* science, but the human-skin evidence is thin and almost entirely **supplier-generated**. Crucially, the best human-topical data belong to the **defined single molecules** (glyceryl glucoside, trehalose) — *not* to the crude whole-plant extracts that the marketing sells. Keep formats strictly separate.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro / ex-vivo (mechanism; the bulk of the evidence)

**Haberlea rhodopensis:**
- **Dell'Acqua G & Schweikert K, 2012**, *Int J Cosmet Sci* 34(2):132–139 — the foundational cosmetic paper (the in-vitro half). In **peroxide-stressed normal human dermal fibroblasts**, the myconoside-rich extract increased **collagen VI (+822%), collagen XVI (+928%), elastin (+144%)** mRNA (RT-qPCR), reportedly *exceeding* retinoic acid/retinol benchmarks; in **ex-vivo human skin biopsies at 3%**, it "protected against UV-induced dermis oxidation by ~100% (P<0.01)" by immunohistochemistry. *Limitations:* mRNA ≠ deposited protein; huge percentage effects off a stressed-baseline are easy to inflate; **authors are from the ingredient supplier (Induchem AG, Switzerland; © 2011 Induchem AG)** — supplier-conflicted. (Affiliation **verified 2026-06-19** via PubMed/journal record: Dell'Acqua G & Schweikert K, Induchem AG. Note: earlier drafts mis-attributed this to the Vincience/ISP→Ashland lineage; the correct supplier is **Induchem**.)
- **Georgiev V, Ebrahimi/Gafner et al.** — phytochemistry & antioxidant: myconoside, paucifloside and hispidulin C-glycosides characterized; myconoside described as "a new powerful natural antioxidant" inhibiting lipid peroxidation; at low concentrations myconoside increases membrane lipid order and adherens-junction integrity. (Cytoprotective/antioxidant phenolics papers, Gesneriaceae.)
- **Staneva et al., 2023**, *Int J Mol Sci* 24(21):15964 (PMC10647427) — Haberlea extract "tunes the cellular stress response": concentration-dependent **genoprotection** (≈40% less γ-radiation DNA damage at 10 µg/mL; max H₂O₂ protection at 50 µg/mL), **↑SOD (~38%)**, preserved glutathione, and normalization of radiation-upregulated DNA-repair genes (ATM/BRCA1/RAD51). *Major caveat:* performed in **HeLa cervical-cancer cells**, not normal skin cells — limits direct cosmetic read-across; also a *mild pro-oxidant priming* (↑lipid peroxidation) was seen. Academic-funded (Trakia University), no COI declared.
- **Vasileva et al., 2024**, *Cells* 13(13):1118 (PMC11240332) — **biosafety**: *in-vitro* culture total ethanol extract on **Lep3 human fibroblasts** showed high survival, **no significant genotoxicity** (neutral comet vs H₂O₂ control), and **↑mitochondrial membrane potential (~1.6×)** and viability at 25–100 µg/mL. Useful *safety* signal; academic (Bulgarian Science Fund); no COI.
- **2025**, *Cosmetics* 12(3):90 — Haberlea extracts **and extracellular vesicles** in human fibroblasts (recent in-vitro "cosmetic potential" paper); adds to mechanism, still cell-level.

**Myrothamnus flabellifolia:**
- **Bentley J, Moore JP, Farrant JM, 2019**, *Molecules* 24(7):1240 (PMC6479747) — metabolomics across the natural range: **41 phenolics**, dominated by **3,4,5-tri-O-galloylquinic acid** (>50–70% dw in Namibian plants), plus **arbutin** derivatives, quercetin, catechins, anthocyanins. Key point for us: **huge chemical variability by provenance** → batch standardization is a real issue, and **arbutin** content has cosmetic (and regulatory) implications. Academic (NRF/UCT); no COI.
- **Moore et al. / Cosmetic Science Technology 2014 (Bänziger, Suter, Obermayer — RAHN AG)** — the **Myramaze** technical article: 3,4,5-tri-O-galloylquinic acid protects membranes from desiccation ROS; *Myramaze* claimed to improve cell-membrane plasticity and defend skin lipids. **Supplier (RAHN) authored** — treat as marketing-grade mechanism.
- **Essential-oil / antimicrobial work** — *M. flabellifolia* oil is fungicidal vs *Candida albicans* and bacteriostatic vs *S. aureus* (0.0625–2%). Relevant to a *preservative-boosting/antimicrobial* angle (and a **caution**: broad antimicrobial activity is not the same as "microbiome-friendly").

**Glyceryl glucoside (Myrothamnus-derived single molecule):**
- *In-vitro* anchor for the human paper below: AQP3 mRNA/protein significantly increased in cultured human keratinocytes at 3% w/v; reports of ↑procollagen I in aged cells, ↑SOD1, ↑ATP synthesis (supplier/CLR data).

### Tier 2 — Animal
- **No meaningful, independent rodent *skin* photoaging/efficacy model** anchors the cosmetic claims for either species. *Myrothamnus* has some animal-nutrition/feed-additive and antimicrobial work, and traditional-medicine pharmacology (antidiabetic/antiviral) — **not skin**. This absence of animal skin efficacy data is a genuine weakness; the case jumps from cell culture straight to small human cosmetic trials.

### Tier 3 — Human, ORAL
- *Myrothamnus* is a **traditional caffeine-free herbal tea** with claimed antioxidant/antimicrobial/antidiabetic/antiviral properties (ethnopharmacology review, Mathibela/various 2020, *J Ethnopharmacol*). This is **oral/traditional**, with **scarce toxicology and no genotoxicity/in-vivo safety package** — and tells us **nothing** about topical cosmetic efficacy. Do **not** borrow tea/medicinal claims into skincare copy. No relevant oral *Haberlea* skin data.

### Tier 4a — Human, TOPICAL — whole-plant extracts (the claim that matters most — and the thinnest)
- **Dell'Acqua & Schweikert 2012 (human half)** — the famous Haberlea clinical line: **n=20 volunteers, 3% in a cream vs placebo**; **skin elasticity ~3× placebo (P<0.0002)** and **skin radiance ~4× placebo (P<0.05)** by **day 15**, sustained to day 60. *Limitations:* **very small (n=20)**, **supplier-run**, "elasticity/radiance" are hydration- and surface-optics-grade endpoints (consistent with humectant/antioxidant action, **not** demonstrated collagen remodeling), no independent replication. This is the entire human anchor for *Haberlea* and it is a small supplier study.
- **Myramaze (RAHN) human claims** — "one application invigorates skin for >48 h," "**+25% hydration** lasting >2 days," "reduces irritation/redness in dry/atopic skin." These are **supplier marketing claims**; I could not locate an independent peer-reviewed RCT for the *Myramaze* crude extract specifically. Treat magnitudes as **manufacturer-grade / [partly unverified]**.
- **Biscaro RC, Mussi L, et al., 2022**, *J Cosmet Dermatol* 21(10):4901–4912 — **randomized, single-blind, placebo-controlled**, topical **2% _Myrothamnus flabellifolia_ + _Coffea arabica_ (MflCas)**, 56 days. Results: **hands** — spot length −30.8%, contrast −42.2%, homogeneity +63.2%; **face** — spot length −21.5%, wrinkle area −8.1%, wrinkle volume −5.6%, homogeneity +59.5%; plus in-vitro autophagy modulation. *Major limitation:* it's a **2-species blend with Coffea** — the *Myrothamnus* contribution **cannot be isolated**; single-blind; effects are largely *tone/spot/homogeneity* (consistent with arbutin + antioxidants) with **small wrinkle changes**; supplier-aligned (Brazilian actives lab).
- **Scientific Reports 2026 (Sci Rep 16:12509), "Skin metabolomic response to medicinal shrub _Myrothamnus flabellifolia_…"** — **before/after, n=32**, a *Myrothamnus*-containing formulation, D0 vs D56, with clinical scoring + untargeted skin metabolomics. A genuinely *novel mechanistic-in-humans* approach (it shows the actives measurably shift skin metabolites). *Limitation:* **no placebo/vehicle control reported** in the summary (before/after design), so efficacy vs vehicle is not established; a *formulation* (not isolated extract) was tested. Strong for "the actives reach and modulate skin," weak as proof of clinical benefit.

### Tier 4b — Human, TOPICAL — the better-evidenced single molecule (glyceryl glucoside)
- **Schrader A, Siefken W, Kueper T, et al., 2012**, *Skin Pharmacol Physiol* 25(4):192–199 — **glyceryl glucoside** increased **AQP3 expression** in human keratinocytes (in vitro) and improved **barrier function and skin hydration in vivo**. This is the **closest thing to real peer-reviewed human-topical proof** in the resurrection-plant orbit — but again, it is the **defined molecule (Glycoin natural / glyceryl glucoside)**, *not* a crude *Haberlea* or *Myrothamnus* extract. Some authors are industry-affiliated. (See also `trehalose.md` for the trehalose half of the compatible-solute story.)

**What is genuinely proven for TOPICAL HUMAN SKIN:**
- ✅ **Hydration / barrier support** — real, best-evidenced for **glyceryl glucoside** (AQP3 + barrier human data) and **trehalose**; for the *crude extracts* it rests on small supplier trials.
- ⚠️ **Antioxidant "defense" / radiance / elasticity (appearance)** — plausible and on-mechanism, but the human data are **n=20 supplier (Haberlea)** and **confounded blend (Myrothamnus+Coffea)**.
- ⚠️ **Tone / spot reduction** — only in the *Myrothamnus+Coffea* blend (Coffea + arbutin confound).
- ❌ **Borrowed-not-proven:** "boosts collagen / elastin" (in-vitro mRNA only), "delays senescence / activates longevity genes" (unsupported marketing), "photoprotection" (one ex-vivo biopsy; **not a sunscreen**), "anti-inflammatory/atopic-soothing" (supplier claims), and any "longevity" framing.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Compatible solutes (trehalose, glyceryl glucoside):** small, **highly hydrophilic** — they act largely **at/within the stratum corneum** (humectancy, glassy matrix, AQP3 modulation in the epidermis). Deep dermal delivery is **not required** for the hydration/barrier benefit, and limited transdermal penetration is expected. Glyceryl glucoside's AQP3 effect is an **epidermal/keratinocyte** action, consistent with shallow delivery.
- **Polyphenols (myconoside, 3,4,5-tri-O-galloylquinic acid):** larger, glycosylated/galloylated, **poorly skin-permeant** as a class. Their antioxidant action is therefore plausibly **surface/upper-epidermal**; claims that they reach and remodel the **dermal matrix** (collagen/elastin) in vivo are **not supported by penetration data** and should be treated as **[unverified]**. The Dell'Acqua "UV-induced dermis oxidation" result was an **ex-vivo biopsy** (full-thickness application), which does not establish in-vivo dermal delivery from a finished cream.
- **No dedicated, peer-reviewed human PK/penetration study** exists for either crude extract — a real gap. The 2026 *Sci Rep* metabolomics paper is the best signal that *Myrothamnus* actives at least **measurably reach and modulate the skin metabolome**, but it doesn't quantify depth.

## 6. Formulation

- **Typical use levels:** Crude extracts are usually sold/used at **1–5%** of the (often glycol/glycerin) extract as supplied — e.g. Haberlea cosmetic trials at **3%**; Myrothamnus+Coffea blend at **2%**. Glyceryl glucoside (Glycoin natural) typically **0.5–3%** active. Actual *active* content of crude extracts is low and **poorly standardized** (see §9).
- **pH:** Polyphenol/galloyl-rich extracts are generally happiest in **mildly acidic** systems (~pH 4–6); strongly alkaline conditions risk hydrolysis/oxidation of gallate esters. Glyceryl glucoside and trehalose are broadly **pH-tolerant and very stable**.
- **Stability:** The **sugars (trehalose, glyceryl glucoside) are extremely stable**; the **polyphenols are oxidation- and light-sensitive** (typical for galloyl/caffeoyl phenolics) and can **discolor** (browning) — antioxidant packaging, chelators, and opaque/airless packaging help. Anthocyanins in *Myrothamnus* can shift color with pH.
- **Pairings (synergistic / sensible):**
  - With **other humectants/compatible solutes** (glycerin, HA, trehalose, ectoine, betaine) — layered "resilience/hydration" story.
  - With **ceramides / niacinamide / panthenol** — barrier-repair core (fits the brand thesis).
  - With **vitamin E / ferulic / other antioxidants** — coherent "environmental-defense" antioxidant network.
  - **Glyceryl glucoside** specifically pairs well as the *human-evidenced hydration engine* under a *Myrothamnus/Haberlea* "resurrection plant" banner.
- **Conflicts:** Avoid **high-pH** or **strong-oxidizer** systems for the polyphenol fraction; standard antioxidant/chelation precautions. No notable incompatibilities for the sugar actives.
- **Sensory:** Light, water-soluble; sugar actives give a pleasant non-tacky "cushioned" hydration feel. Crude extracts can carry slight color/odor; dose accordingly.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation/sensitization:** Generally well-tolerated at cosmetic levels; no notable irritancy signal in the small human cosmetic trials. *Haberlea in-vitro* biosafety (Vasileva 2024) showed no genotoxicity and good fibroblast biocompatibility at 25–100 µg/mL. The polyphenol-rich fraction *could* (as with many botanical polyphenols) be a low-frequency contact-sensitization risk; patch-test guidance is sensible. No human sensitization dataset of note exists — typical for niche botanicals.
- **Arbutin caveat (Myrothamnus):** *M. flabellifolia* contains **arbutin** (a hydroquinone glycoside). Arbutin is itself a **regulated/scrutinized** skin-lightening ingredient (hydroquinone-release concerns; EU/SCCS and other bodies have set limits/cautions). A *Myrothamnus* extract is therefore not automatically "just a gentle botanical" — its arbutin content (highly **batch-variable** per Bentley 2019) is a real **safety/regulatory** consideration, and any tone/brightening claim leans on a regulated mechanism.
- **Sun sensitivity:** None expected; polyphenols are, if anything, antioxidant. **But it is not a sunscreen** and must not be positioned as sun protection.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** No specific cosmetic-topical contraindication identified, but **toxicology data are scarce** (esp. *Myrothamnus*: the ethnopharmacology review explicitly flags gaps in genotoxicity and in-vivo safety). Standard caution; defer to clinician; avoid medicinal-strength oral framings.
- **Sourcing/sustainability (a real differentiator AND a real risk):**
  - **_Haberlea rhodopensis_** is a **rare, protected Balkan endemic** — wild harvesting is ethically/legally fraught. The credible supply route is **plant cell culture / in-vitro propagation** (several suppliers do this), which is a genuine clean-biotech sustainability story *if* sourced that way. **Verify the supplier uses cell culture, not wild-collected leaf.**
  - **_Myrothamnus flabellifolia_** is **wild-collected** in southern Africa (also harvested for tea). Wild collection raises **overharvesting + benefit-sharing (Nagoya Protocol / access-and-benefit-sharing)** concerns; RAHN markets "sustainably grown crops near Pretoria," which (if true) mitigates this. **Verify cultivation/ABS compliance.**

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **The cosmetic-vs-drug line is the key risk.** The category's most attractive narratives ("delays senescence," "activates longevity genes," "boosts collagen/elastin," "repairs," "protects DNA," "treats atopic/dry-skin irritation," "photoprotection") drift into **drug/structure-function** territory and/or are **unsubstantiated for topical human use**. Several are borrowed from in-vitro, ex-vivo, traditional-tea, or cancer-cell data.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "inspired by the desiccation-survival biology of resurrection plants," "helps skin **hold onto moisture**," "supports the look and feel of a **resilient, well-hydrated barrier**," "rich in **antioxidant** polyphenols that help defend the skin's appearance against daily environmental stress," "helps skin look **revived / replenished**," "visibly improves **radiance and suppleness**."
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation or avoid:** "boosts collagen by X% / rebuilds elastin" (in-vitro mRNA only), "delays cellular senescence / longevity genes" (**unsupported — do not use**), "protects against UV / photoaging" (drug/SPF territory; one ex-vivo result), "anti-inflammatory / soothes eczema/atopic skin" (drug-adjacent; supplier claims), "brightens/lightens" (leans on **regulated arbutin**), "anti-aging" as a structural claim.
- **Arbutin/hydroquinone watch (Myrothamnus):** any **brightening** angle implicates a **regulated** ingredient class — flag for legal/regulatory review and confirm finished-product arbutin levels are within limits for target markets.
- Maintain claims discipline per [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md): "supports / helps / visibly," not "treats / prevents / boosts X%."

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier:** **Mid to mid-high.** Branded actives (Resistem-type Haberlea, RAHN Myramaze, CLR Glycoin natural) carry premium pricing and a strong marketing dossier; commodity/cell-culture grades vary. Cost-in-formula at 1–3% is moderate; you're partly paying for the **story and supplier substantiation**, not a uniquely potent clinical effect.
- **Standardization problem (important):** crude extracts are **poorly standardized** and **highly provenance-variable** — *Myrothamnus* phenolic/arbutin content swings widely by region (Bentley 2019), and crude *Haberlea* myconoside content depends on material/process. **Insist on a standardized marker** (myconoside for Haberlea; total galloylquinic acid for Myrothamnus) and **batch CoAs**, or prefer the **defined molecule (glyceryl glucoside)** where reproducibility matters.
- **Supply considerations:**
  - *Haberlea:* prefer **cell-culture / in-vitro** grades (sustainability + protected-species compliance). Suppliers: Ashland (Resistem lineage), INNOVA BM, others.
  - *Myrothamnus:* RAHN (Myramaze) with cultivation claims; confirm **ABS/Nagoya** documentation for wild/cultivated southern-African material.
  - *Glyceryl glucoside:* CLR (Glycoin natural) — ECOCERT/biotech, defined, the most "buildable-on" option.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **"Resurrection / comes back to life" framing:** botanically true and a *fantastic* story — but it describes the **plant**, not a demonstrated effect on your skin. The leap from "plant survives total desiccation" to "your face becomes that resilient" is **narrative, not data**.
- **"Boosts collagen VI +822% / elastin +144%":** these are **in-vitro mRNA** changes in **peroxide-stressed** fibroblasts (Dell'Acqua 2012), off a damaged baseline, by the **supplier**. mRNA ≠ deposited dermal collagen; the human arm measured **elasticity/radiance (n=20)**, not collagen. Do not present the percentages as a skin-firming claim.
- **"Delays senescence / activates longevity genes":** I found **no verifiable published skin dataset** behind this — it's the most overreached line in the category. **[Unverified] — do not use.**
- **"100% protection against UV-induced dermis oxidation":** a single **ex-vivo biopsy** immunohistochemistry result, not in-vivo photoprotection, and **never** a substitute for SPF.
- **One species' data ≠ the other's:** *Haberlea* (myconoside) and *Myrothamnus* (galloylquinic acid/trehalose/arbutin) have **different chemistry**; the strong human-ish data (AQP3/barrier) actually belong to **glyceryl glucoside**, a *molecule*, not either crude extract. Marketing that pools all "resurrection plants" into one claim set is scientifically sloppy.
- **Conflict-of-interest pattern (pervasive):** the favorable cosmetic literature is **supplier-authored** — Dell'Acqua & Schweikert (**Induchem AG**) for Haberlea; Bänziger et al. (RAHN) and the Brazilian actives lab (Biscaro 2022) for Myrothamnus; CLR for glyceryl glucoside. The **independent** literature (Bentley 2019 metabolomics; Staneva 2023/Vasileva 2024 cell biology; the ethnopharmacology review) is **mechanistic/safety**, not efficacy. So there is **no independent clinical efficacy anchor**.

**Net:** Resurrection-plant extracts are among the *best-story, mid-evidence* botanicals — genuinely on-mechanism for hydration and antioxidant defense, but oversold on collagen/senescence/longevity. Sell the **resilience/hydration** truth; resist the regenerative halo.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL (not hero).** One line: *A scientifically charming, on-mechanism hydration + antioxidant-defense botanical with a uniquely ownable "desiccation-survival" story — worth using as a supporting/narrative active (ideally paired with, or delivered as, the better-evidenced glyceryl glucoside), but NOT a hero and NOT a basis for collagen/senescence/longevity/photoprotection claims.*

- **Why supporting, not hero:** the proven benefits (hydration, barrier, antioxidant *appearance*) overlap with cheaper, equally/better-evidenced actives (glycerin, HA, niacinamide, trehalose, ectoine), and the human efficacy data are **small and supplier-run**. Its distinctive value is the **story + antioxidant polyphenol profile**, not a unique clinical effect.
- **Why not skip:** the **"resurrection plant" narrative is genuinely differentiating** and maps cleanly onto a **skin-longevity / resilience** brand thesis; the chemistry is real; and a **cell-culture Haberlea** or **cultivated Myrothamnus** can carry a sustainability/biotech story. It's a strong *texture-of-brand* ingredient.
- **Conditional on:** (1) **sustainable, traceable sourcing** — cell-culture Haberlea; ABS-compliant cultivated Myrothamnus; (2) **standardization** to a marker + batch CoAs (or use glyceryl glucoside for the reproducible hydration engine); (3) **claims kept to hydration/resilience/antioxidant-appearance**, with the arbutin/brightening angle vetted by regulatory; (4) ideally **co-formulated with the human-evidenced compatible solutes** (glyceryl glucoside, trehalose) so the human-data weight sits on a defensible molecule.
- **Longevity-relevance note:** maps to the **resilience/defense + hydration** side of skin-longevity (helping cells and barrier withstand desiccation and oxidative stress), **not** the regenerative side (senescence clearance, collagen remodeling, glycation). Frame as "helping skin stay resilient and replenished," never as senescence/longevity-gene action.
- **Recommended use:** 1–3% crude extract (standardized) and/or 0.5–3% glyceryl glucoside, in a barrier/hydration formula alongside HA + ceramides/niacinamide; antioxidant-protective packaging; claims limited to hydration/resilience/radiance.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Method: each headline efficacy claim was checked against the primary source for (a) evidence type (in-vitro / ex-vivo / oral-traditional / human-topical-extract / human-topical-defined-molecule), (b) whether the cited study is real and says what's claimed, and (c) sample size / industry funding. Sources independently fetched or cross-checked: Dell'Acqua & Schweikert 2012 (PMID 22023081; in-vitro + n=20 human numbers cross-checked across Wiley/ResearchGate/secondary), Staneva 2023 (PMC10647427), Vasileva 2024 (PMC11240332), Bentley/Moore/Farrant 2019 (PMC6479747), Biscaro 2022 (PMID 35220641 / DOI 10.1111/jocd.14888), Sci Rep 2026 (16:12509), Schrader 2012 (PMID 22584263), ethnopharmacology review (PMID 31958487), RAHN Myramaze technical PDF (read directly).

### Overall evidence grade: **C / C+ (weak-to-moderate; mechanism-rich, efficacy-thin, supplier-dominated)**
- For the **bold anti-aging claims** (collagen/elastin remodeling, senescence delay, longevity genes, photoprotection): **grade D** — in-vitro mRNA, ex-vivo single results, cancer-cell biology, or pure marketing; no independent human remodeling proof.
- For **hydration + barrier + antioxidant-defense (appearance)** — what the dossier recommends selling: **grade C+ / B-**, but the **strongest human-topical data belong to the defined molecule glyceryl glucoside (Schrader 2012)**, not the crude extracts; crude-extract human data are **n=20 supplier (Haberlea)** and a **confounded blend (Myrothamnus+Coffea)**.
- The at-a-glance verdict ("moderate, leaning weak; supporting/conditional, not hero; sell resilience/hydration, resist the regenerative halo") **survives scrutiny.**

### Are the studies real, and do they say what's claimed?
All cited human/efficacy studies are **real and resolve.** Key accuracy points captured: (1) the famous Haberlea collagen/elastin figures are **in-vitro mRNA in stressed fibroblasts**, and the human arm is **n=20, elasticity/radiance** only; (2) the strong "Myrothamnus" RCT (Biscaro 2022) is a **2-species blend with Coffea**, so Myrothamnus is **not isolable**; (3) Staneva 2023's striking genoprotection is in **HeLa cancer cells**; (4) the 2026 *Sci Rep* paper is **before/after (no vehicle control)**; (5) the best AQP3/barrier human data are for **glyceryl glucoside (a molecule)**, not the extracts. The dossier states each of these.

### Main caveats / corrections folded into the body
1. **No independent clinical efficacy anchor.** Every efficacy study is supplier-authored (Induchem [Haberlea], RAHN [Myramaze], Brazilian actives lab [Biscaro], Beiersdorf/CLR [glyceryl glucoside]); independent work is mechanistic/safety only.
2. **"Delays senescence / longevity genes"** — could **not** be sourced to any published skin dataset; marked **[unverified]; do not use.**
3. **Species are not interchangeable** — Haberlea (myconoside) ≠ Myrothamnus (galloylquinic acid/trehalose/arbutin) ≠ glyceryl glucoside (defined molecule). Pooling them is a common error and is flagged.
4. **Arbutin in Myrothamnus** is a genuine **regulatory** flag (hydroquinone-glycoside; scrutinized in EU/elsewhere) and is **batch-variable** — any brightening angle needs legal review.
5. **Sourcing is both differentiator and risk:** protected Balkan *Haberlea* (use **cell culture**) and wild southern-African *Myrothamnus* (Nagoya/ABS) — verify before any "sustainable/natural" copy.
6. **Penetration unproven** for dermal-remodeling claims; polyphenols are poorly permeant; the dermis-oxidation result was **ex-vivo**.
7. **Dell'Acqua/Schweikert affiliation — now confirmed (correction).** Re-verified 2026-06-19 against the PubMed/journal record: the authors are at **Induchem AG, Switzerland** (© 2011 Induchem AG), **not** the Vincience/ISP→Ashland lineage stated in earlier drafts. Supplier conflict stands; the specific corporate parent was wrong and has been corrected throughout.

### Claims that should still be softened / never used in marketing
- ❌ "Boosts/rebuilds collagen & elastin," "delays cellular senescence," "activates longevity genes," "anti-aging" (structural), "protects against UV/photoaging," "DNA protection," "treats/soothes eczema/atopic skin."

**Net adversarial verdict:** Honest, well-tiered, and correctly separates the *story* and *in-vitro* strength from the *thin, supplier-run, sometimes-confounded* human efficacy data — and correctly notes that the best human evidence belongs to the defined single molecule, not the crude extracts.
