# Ingredient: Peptides — Signal peptides (Matrixyl / Palmitoyl Pentapeptide-4) + Copper peptide GHK-Cu (INCI: Palmitoyl Pentapeptide-4; Palmitoyl Tripeptide-1 + Palmitoyl Tetrapeptide-7 ["Matrixyl 3000"]; Copper Tripeptide-1 [GHK-Cu])

**Researched:** 2026-06-18 · **Status:** ✅ shortlisted (SUPPORTING)

## What it is
Peptides are short chains of amino acids. In skincare the most relevant class is **signal peptides**, which act as "messengers" that nudge fibroblasts to ramp up collagen and other matrix components, mimicking the fragments released during natural skin repair. The flagship is **Matrixyl / palmitoyl pentapeptide-4** (and the blend "Matrixyl 3000"). **Copper peptide GHK-Cu** is a well-studied signaling peptide with collagen-synthesis and wound-healing/anti-inflammatory roles. (Other classes: neuromodulating "Botox-like" peptides e.g. Argireline; carrier and enzyme-inhibitor peptides.)

## What it does (claimed → evidenced)
| Benefit | Evidence strength | Source(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Reduces appearance of wrinkles / improves texture (palmitoyl pentapeptide-4) | **Moderate** — split-face vehicle-controlled trials show improvement; effects modest, mostly industry-sponsored | [1](https://www.mdpi.com/2079-9284/4/2/16), [2](https://www.peptidejournal.org/research/matrixyl-clinical-studies-anti-wrinkle-evidence) |
| Signals collagen / matrix synthesis (mechanism) | **Moderate** — strong in vitro / explant data, fewer large independent RCTs | [1](https://www.mdpi.com/2079-9284/4/2/16) |
| GHK-Cu supports collagen synthesis, wound healing, antioxidant/anti-inflammatory | **Moderate** — among the better-evidenced cosmetic peptides; much data is in vitro/explant or small trials | [1](https://www.mdpi.com/2079-9284/4/2/16) |
| "Botox-like" wrinkle relaxation (neuropeptides, context only) | **Weak** — popular claim, limited robust topical evidence | [1](https://www.mdpi.com/2079-9284/4/2/16) |

## How it fits Kintsugi
**Good supporting fit, with discipline.** Peptides are the textbook "signal skin's own repair" ingredient — the language ("supports renewal," "messengers of repair") is squarely on-brand and gentle. But evidence is **moderate, not strong**, and the category is heavily hyped, so they belong as **supporting actives that round out a hero (retinoid/vitamin C)**, not as the sole proof-point. Be careful not to over-claim — that would undercut the brand's evidence-first credibility.

## Formulation notes
- **Effective concentration:** Used at low %; commercial blends (e.g., Matrixyl 3000) are dosed per supplier guidance (often 3–8% of the trade blend, which is itself dilute). GHK-Cu typically ~0.1–2%.
- **pH:** Generally mild/near-neutral; most signal peptides are reasonably stable in well-designed aqueous systems.
- **Stability:** Peptides can be sensitive to pH extremes and certain preservatives/enzymes; formulate per supplier data.
- **Pairs well with:** Niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, ceramides — gentle "repair stack." Good daytime or nighttime.
- **Conflicts:** **GHK-Cu (copper)** is classically flagged as potentially destabilized by **strong direct acids (high-strength vitamin C) and possibly strong chelators** — separate routines or steps; this conflict is debated but worth respecting. Signal peptides generally have few conflicts.
- **Sensory:** Lightweight, elegant — easy to make feel premium.

## Concerns & cautions
- **Irritation:** Low — a key selling point (gentle alternative/companion to retinoids).
- **Sun sensitivity:** None.
- **Pregnancy:** Generally considered low-risk topically; standard boilerplate.
- **Hype risk:** The biggest "concern" is **evidence overreach** — many peptide claims rest on lab/explant data or sponsored studies. Keep marketing measured.

## Claims caution
- ✅ **Safe cosmetic language:** "peptides help support skin's natural renewal," "for visibly firmer, smoother-looking skin," "helps reduce the look of fine lines."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like:** "boosts collagen by X%," "rebuilds collagen," "Botox in a bottle," "heals/repairs wounds." Avoid quantified collagen claims and any wound-healing/medical framing (especially for GHK-Cu, whose strongest data is wound-related — that's a drug context).
- See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## Cost / sourcing
- **Moderate to high**, depending on peptide and supplier (branded blends like Matrixyl/Sederma carry a premium and marketing pull). GHK-Cu moderate. Widely available; branded ingredients add a storytelling/cost layer that fits premium positioning.

## Verdict for Kintsugi
✅ **SUPPORTING** — on-message "repair signal" actives that enrich a hero formula and tell a gentle renewal story; do **not** rely on them as the primary efficacy claim given moderate evidence. Log in [`../../ideas/considering.md`](../../ideas/considering.md).
