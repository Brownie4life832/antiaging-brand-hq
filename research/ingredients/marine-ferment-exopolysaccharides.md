# Ingredient: Marine / Ferment Exopolysaccharides (Algae Extract, *Pseudoalteromonas* Ferment / Antarcticine, *Sphingomonas* Ferment Extract)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Humectants & hydration actives (marine/bacterial exopolysaccharides — EPS)

> **One-line honest summary:** This is a *family* of marine and ferment-derived sugar (and sugar-protein) polymers that are genuinely good film-forming humectants and pleasant sensory/texture agents, with real (if mostly in-vitro and supplier-generated) signals for hydration, fibroblast support and barrier comfort. The biology is plausible and the safety is excellent, but the headline anti-aging numbers ("wrinkle depth −44%," "ceramide synthesis up," "better than HA") come almost entirely from **manufacturer marketing, patents, and in-vitro/3D models** — **independent, peer-reviewed human topical RCTs on the named cosmetic ingredients essentially do not exist.** Treat the class as a credible **supporting hydration/sensory active**, not an evidence-backed anti-aging hero.

---

## 0. Scope note — this slug bundles three different things

The brief groups three INCI entries that are biochemically related (all are microbial/marine polysaccharide systems) but are **not** interchangeable. Honesty requires keeping them distinct:

1. **Algae Extract** (INCI *Algae Extract*) — an umbrella seaweed extract; chemically a grab-bag of polysaccharides (alginate, fucoidan, ulvan, laminarin, carrageenan, agar) plus minerals, proteins and pigments depending on species and process.
2. ***Pseudoalteromonas* Ferment Extract** — trade name **Antarcticine®** (Lipotec/Lubrizol). A glycoprotein/EPS from an Antarctic bacterium; the marquee "anti-aging" member.
3. ***Sphingomonas* Ferment Extract** — a ferment of *Sphingomonas*, marketed for barrier/ceramide/soothing benefits; the *Sphingomonadaceae* are also the industrial source of **sphingans** (gellan-type polysaccharides).

I also pull in the broader marine-bacterial EPS landscape (Vibrio, Alteromonas, Halomonas, Cobetia, Polaribacter) because (a) they share mechanism and (b) the only quantitative, peer-reviewed data in this space lives there.

## 1. Identity

### 1a. Algae Extract
- **Common name:** Algae extract / seaweed extract.
- **INCI:** *Algae Extract* (a deliberately broad term; the INCI name dates to 1979 and was given **"retired" status in 2015**, reflecting that it is too non-specific to characterize). [11]
- **Composition:** Highly variable. Depending on the alga (brown/*Phaeophyceae*, red/*Rhodophyta*, green/*Chlorophyta*, blue-green/cyanobacteria) and extraction, it can contain **alginate, fucoidan, laminarin** (brown); **carrageenan, agar** (red); **ulvan** (green); plus mannitol, minerals (iodine), amino acids, polyphenols and carotenoids. [4][11]
- **Functions (INCI/CosIng):** Humectant, emollient, skin-conditioning. [4][11]
- **Key caution:** Because "Algae Extract" can be "anything from La Mer's Miracle Broth to a simple brown-algae extract," the label tells you almost nothing about actual actives or potency — a COA and species/standardization spec are mandatory. [11]

### 1b. *Pseudoalteromonas* Ferment Extract (Antarcticine®)
- **Common/trade name:** Antarcticine® (Lipotec, now Lubrizol). [6][8]
- **INCI:** *Pseudoalteromonas Ferment Extract* (and) *Aqua/Water* (typical commercial form). [6][9]
- **Source organism:** *Pseudoalteromonas antarctica* (strain **NF3**), a psychrotolerant marine bacterium isolated from mud at Admiralty Bay, King George Island, Antarctica (1988 Spanish expedition). [1][6][7]
- **Active material:** A secreted **cryoprotective glycoprotein/exopolymer** ("Antarcticine-NF3"); characterized in the literature as a **hexapolymeric glycoprotein ~14% carbohydrate / ~86% protein** that self-assembles and can coat/protect liposomes. (Note: it is therefore as much a *glycoprotein* as a classical polysaccharide.) [2][7]
- **Related strains in the literature:** *Pseudoalteromonas* sp. CNCM I-4150 — an **anionic heteropolysaccharide** (Glc, Gal, GlcA, GlcNAc, GalA, Man; depolymerized MW ~8 × 10⁵ g/mol) used for moisturizing via water-retention. [3]

### 1c. *Sphingomonas* Ferment Extract
- **Common name:** *Sphingomonas* ferment extract.
- **INCI:** *Sphingomonas Ferment Extract*. CosIng function: **skin-conditioning**. [10][12]
- **Source:** Fermentation product of *Sphingomonas* (genus *Sphingomonas*, family *Sphingomonadaceae*). [5][10]
- **Notable biology:** *Sphingomonas* are unusual Gram-negatives that carry **glycosphingolipids (GSLs) in their outer membrane instead of LPS** — this is the (indirect) basis of the marketing "ceramide/sphingolipid" story. [5] The genus is also the industrial producer of **sphingans** — gellan-type linear anionic heteropolysaccharides (rhamnose, glucose, glucuronic acid, acetyl-substituted; MW ~0.24–2.2 × 10⁶ g/mol), used as thickeners/stabilizers (e.g., gellan gum, Kelcogel®). [3]

### 1d. Cousins worth knowing (peer-reviewed data lives here)
- ***Vibrio diabolicus*** EPS ("diabolican," HE800; deep-sea vent; MW ~1 × 10⁶) — HA-like (GlcA + GlcNAc/GalNAc); promotes collagen structuring & fibroblast colonization in dermal-equivalent models. [3]
- ***Alteromonas macleodii*** EPS — **Abyssine®** (soothing/anti-UVB-irritation). *Alteromonas* sp. CNCM I-4354 — wrinkle/tensing claims. [3]
- ***Halomonas*** EPS — **Hyadisine®/Hyanify®** (Lubrizol; "boost-your-own-HA"/firming). [3]
- ***Polaribacter* sp. SM1127** (Arctic kelp) — the best-quantified moisture/antioxidant EPS in the literature (see §4). [13]

## 2. Class & mechanism

Exopolysaccharides (EPS) are high-molecular-weight sugar polymers (often with protein, sulfate, acetyl, pyruvate or amino-acid decoration) that microbes secrete to retain water, adhere, and survive stress (cold, salinity, desiccation). That ecological job — **holding water and protecting cells in a hostile environment** — is exactly why they are attractive skincare humectants. [3][4][13]

Mechanistic pillars (varying by molecule; strongest where noted):

1. **Humectancy + film formation (the core, best-supported mechanism).** Long, hydrophilic, often anionic chains bind and hold water, lay down a breathable film, and slow transepidermal water loss (TEWL). For several seaweed/marine EPS this water-holding is reported **comparable to or exceeding hyaluronic acid** in benchtop assays. [3][4][13]
2. **Fibroblast / ECM support.** In cell and 3D dermal-equivalent models, certain EPS (diabolican; Cobetia; some *Pseudoalteromonas*) promote fibroblast adhesion, migration, collagen structuring and ECM synthesis. [3]
3. **Barrier / keratinocyte support.** Some EPS (*Vibrio alginolyticus* Epidermist 4.0™; Cobetia depolymerized) increase keratinocyte differentiation and cornified-envelope/barrier-protein expression in reconstructed skin. The *Sphingomonas* "ceramide/sphingolipid" story is mechanistically *adjacent* (the bacterium makes GSLs) but **not the same as proof the ferment raises human stratum-corneum ceramides**. [3][5]
4. **Antioxidant / radical scavenging.** Several marine EPS scavenge DPPH/hydroxyl/superoxide radicals in vitro (Polaribacter SM1127 is the clearest example). [13]
5. **Soothing / anti-inflammatory.** Cobetia and *V. alginolyticus* EPS reduce IL-8 in LPS-challenged skin explants; Alteromonas EPS (Abyssine) reduces UVB/chemical/mechanical irritation signals. [3]
6. **Cryo/bioprotection (the Antarcticine origin story).** As cold-adapted exopolymers, they protect cells/membranes against cold and surfactant stress — the rationale for "protects skin in harsh/cold conditions." [1][2][7][13]

**Honest translation:** Mechanism #1 (humectancy/film/TEWL) is solid and transferable to topical use. Mechanisms #2–#6 are real in cells/models and in supplier claim files, but their **human topical, anti-aging relevance is largely inferred, not demonstrated by independent clinical trials.**

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core)

> **The single most important honesty point:** For the three *named* cosmetic INCIs (Algae Extract, *Pseudoalteromonas* Ferment Extract/Antarcticine, *Sphingomonas* Ferment Extract), the deep, citation-grade evidence is **structural/biochemical and in-vitro**. The flashy human numbers (e.g., Antarcticine "−44% wrinkle depth," "+14.8% hydration," "+30% hydration in 50 subjects," *Sphingomonas* "increases ceramides") trace to **manufacturer marketing, technical files, and patents** — I could **not** locate independent, peer-reviewed, controlled human trials for them. The strongest *peer-reviewed* data in this whole class is on **cousin EPS** (Polaribacter SM1127, diabolican), and even that is in-vitro/ex-vivo, not human topical RCT. A skincare brand must treat almost every anti-aging claim here as **borrowed (from cousin molecules), in-vitro, or supplier-asserted**.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell, biochemical & 3D models) — strongest real data

- **Benhadda, Zykwinska, Colliec-Jouault et al., 2023, *Marine Drugs* 21(11):582 (DOI 10.3390/md21110582).** The key scholarly review of marine vs non-marine bacterial EPS for skincare. Catalogs structures, MW, monosaccharide profiles, mechanisms and commercial products. Explicitly notes most bioactivity evidence is **in-vitro 2D/3D and patent-derived, with no large RCTs and sample sizes generally unspecified.** Documents: *Pseudoalteromonas* CNCM I-4150 (moisturizing via water-retention); diabolican (collagen/fibroblast support in dermal equivalents); Epidermist 4.0™ (keratinocyte differentiation/barrier); Cobetia (anti-inflammatory IL-8 ↓, angiogenesis, barrier in aged 3D skin); Halomonas → Hyadisine/Hyanify (collagen synthesis, anti-cellulite). *This is the honest backbone of the dossier.* [3]
- **Sun et al., 2015, *Scientific Reports* 5:18435 (Polaribacter sp. SM1127 EPS).** The best-quantified moisture/antioxidant EPS paper. ~220 kDa, hyper-branched heteropolysaccharide (GlcNAc 28%, Man 23%, GlcA 21%, Gal 17%, Fuc 7%). **Moisture retention 75.79 ± 2.5% at 72 h — superior to HA** (moisture *absorption* was below HA). Radical scavenging at 10 mg/mL: DPPH 55.4%, •OH 52.1%, O₂•⁻ 28.2% (above HA controls). Protected human dermal fibroblasts at 4 °C (76.1% viable vs 44.2% control at 500 µg/mL). Non-irritating (PII = 0); oral LD₅₀ > 5000 mg/kg. *Limitation: a cousin Arctic strain, not the commercial INCIs; benchtop + cell assays, not human topical use.* [13]
- **Nevot et al. (Bozal/Mercadé group), 2006, *Microbial Ecology* 51:501–507 — ultrastructure of *P. antarctica* NF3 EPS.** Characterizes the secreted exopolymer (high protein content, self-assembly, liposome coating/protection). *Structural basis for Antarcticine's "bioprotective" claim; not an efficacy study.* [2]
- **Carrión et al. / Bozal group, 2015, *PLoS ONE* — outer-membrane vesicles of *P. antarctica* NF3 (PMC4379500).** Further characterization of the NF3 secretome. *Microbiology, not skin efficacy.* [7]
- **Antarctic cryoprotective EPS (p-CY02), 2024, *Marine Drugs*/PMC11180913 — *Pseudoalteromonas* sp. RosPo-2.** Optimization and cryoprotective activity of an Antarctic EPS. *Confirms the cold-protection mechanism class; strain ≠ commercial INCI.* [14]
- **Immunomodulatory EPS from Antarctic *Pseudoalteromonas* sp. LP6-12-2, 2025, *Extremophiles* (DOI 10.1007/s00792-025-01394-2).** Structural characterization + immunomodulatory activity. *Mechanistic support for "soothing"; not skin-clinical.* [15]
- **Seaweed/algae polysaccharide literature (for "Algae Extract").** Multiple reports that algal polysaccharides match/beat HA on benchtop hydration: *Laminaria japonica* polysaccharides > HA for hydration; sulfated *Ulva fasciata* polysaccharide out-absorbed/retained vs glycerol over 96 h; fucoidan/alginate/ulvan act as humectant + soft occlusive; various seaweed extracts show in-vitro antioxidant, anti-collagenase/elastase and anti-tyrosinase activity. *All benchtop/in-vitro; topical human anti-aging data are sparse.* [4]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)

- **Polaribacter SM1127 EPS — wound healing & frostbite, Sun et al., 2020, *Marine Drugs* 18(1):48 (PMC7024241).** Topical EPS promoted wound healing and prevented frostbite injury in **rat skin**. *Cousin strain; rodent; acute injury model, not chronic aging skin or human.* [16]
- **General:** No peer-reviewed animal anti-aging studies specific to the three commercial INCIs were located. Cryoprotection/wound data exist for cousin marine EPS only.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL

- **Fucoidan (brown-seaweed polysaccharide), oral RCT in atopic dermatitis (low-molecular-weight fucoidan, 12 weeks, double-blind, placebo-controlled).** Reported anti-inflammatory benefit. *Oral, disease population, single polysaccharide — not relevant to topical anti-aging claims for these INCIs, but the closest "real RCT" in the broad algal-polysaccharide space.* [17]
- No human oral data exist for *Pseudoalteromonas* or *Sphingomonas* ferment as skincare actives (nor would oral be the relevant route).

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters — and it's supplier-grade, not independent)

- **Antarcticine® supplier clinical claims (Lipotec/Lubrizol technical literature, repeated across distributor and ingredient-dictionary pages).** The widely cited results: a **5% Antarcticine cream, twice daily for 30 days, n = 10 healthy women**, reduced **wrinkle depth ~44% (max 50–60%)** by silicone-imprint analysis around the eyes; a **1% cream raised hydration ~14.8% in cold conditions**; claims of increased collagen I/IV and elastin. **Critical caveats:** (a) tiny (n = 10), women-only, manufacturer-run, **not independently replicated or, to my finding, published in a peer-reviewed journal**; (b) the "−44%" figure is a marketing staple, repeated verbatim by resellers, which is a red flag for un-vetted provenance; (c) Paula's Choice and other neutral dictionaries treat *Pseudoalteromonas* ferment as a **good water-binding/soothing agent but flag that independent corroboration is lacking.** [1][6][8][9]
- **"30% hydration increase in 50 participants over 4 weeks."** Appears in secondary ingredient-marketing pages with **no traceable primary citation**. Treat as **[unverified]** marketing until a primary source is produced. [9]
- ***Sphingomonas* ferment "increases ceramide synthesis / improves suppleness."** This is **mechanistic inference + marketing**, not a located peer-reviewed human study for the *hydration/ceramide* claims. The genus makes glycosphingolipids in its *own* membrane [5]; that does **not** establish that the topical ferment raises human stratum-corneum ceramides. **The one genuinely peer-reviewed, in-vivo human study in this corner of the space** is a 2025 *International Journal of Cosmetic Science* paper (Hilaire et al., PMC12666735) on a **characterized *Sphingomonas xenophaga* ferment extract** (isolated from La Roche-Posay thermal spring water and produced by an industrial fermentation process — i.e. it *is* a Sphingomonas ferment extract, just a specific strain). However: (a) it is **industry-funded** (La Roche-Posay/L'Oréal); (b) its endpoint is **soothing / vascular disorder / sensitive-reactive skin** via the kallikrein-kinin system — **not hydration and not ceramide synthesis**. So it lends modest, real human support to a *soothing* positioning for a Sphingomonas ferment, but it must **not** be cited as proof of the *ceramide* or *hydration* claims that the generic marketing attaches to *Sphingomonas Ferment Extract*. [10][12][18]
- **Algae Extract.** No INCI-specific topical anti-aging RCT (and given the term's "retired"/non-specific status, one would be uninterpretable). [11]

**Bottom line by tier:** Proven for the *class* → benchtop humectancy/water-retention (often ≥ HA), in-vitro antioxidant, and in-vitro/3D fibroblast & barrier support. **NOT proven by independent human topical RCTs** for any of the three named INCIs. The signature anti-aging numbers are **supplier/patent-grade or unsourced**.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **These are large polymers (10⁴–10⁶ g/mol; Antarcticine glycoprotein, sphingans, diabolican, SM1127 ~220 kDa).** They are **not meant to penetrate** — and largely cannot cross intact stratum corneum at these sizes. Their value is **surface and superficial:** forming a hydrating, water-holding, film over the skin and within the upper corneum. [3][13]
- **Implication for claims:** A "boosts collagen in the dermis" story is mechanistically awkward for an unpenetrating macromolecule — the in-vitro collagen/fibroblast effects come from **direct cell-culture dosing**, which a topical film cannot replicate in living dermis. Smaller **depolymerized** EPS fractions (suppliers deliberately depolymerize to ~2 × 10⁵ for some products) penetrate somewhat better and are where signaling claims are slightly more plausible — but still unproven transdermally. [3]
- **Practical read:** Excellent **humectant/film/sensory** performance is well-founded; **deep biological remodeling from topical application is not.** No published human transdermal penetration quantification for the named INCIs.

## 6. Formulation

- **Use level:** Commercial actives are typically supplied as dilute aqueous solutions; **use as supplier-recommended** (Antarcticine is commonly used at ~1–5% of the *commercial solution*, not 1–5% pure polymer; the "5%" in the wrinkle claim is the diluted trade material). Algae extracts and ferments usually 1–5% of the supplied liquid. Do **not** imply a validated dose-response — none is independently established.
- **pH:** Generally water-compatible and tolerant of normal skincare pH (~4.5–6.5). Anionic EPS and sphingans can be **cation-sensitive** (divalent cations like Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ can gel or precipitate them — sphingans/gellan gel *deliberately* with divalent cations). Watch electrolyte load and chelators. [3]
- **Stability:** Polymer solutions are prone to **microbial growth** (sugar-rich → robust preservation + challenge testing essential), and to viscosity drift; ferments/glycoproteins can carry slight color/odor and are heat-sensitive (avoid high-temp processing of the glycoprotein actives — add in cool-down). Verify supplier stability data.
- **Sensory / texture:** This is a genuine strength. EPS give **slip, cushion, film-forming "second-skin" feel, and instant smoothing/plumping** — they double as natural rheology modifiers/texturizers (sphingans/gellan are classic thickeners). Excellent for a premium feel without silicones. [3]
- **Pairings (conceptual):**
  - Hyaluronic acid / glycerin / panthenol — layered humectant system (different MW water-binders).
  - Niacinamide, ceramides, cholesterol — barrier-repair thesis (the *Sphingomonas* angle, framed honestly).
  - Antioxidants (vitamin E, ferulic) — complement EPS radical-scavenging.
  - Soothing actives (centella, allantoin) — reinforce the comfort/soothing positioning.
- **Conflicts:** High electrolyte concentrations and some cationic actives (e.g., cationic polymers/surfactants) can salt-out or complex anionic EPS; strong acids/oxidizers can degrade polymers. Test compatibility.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation/sensitization:** Excellent tolerability profile. EPS are large, inert, non-penetrating sugars/glycoproteins; SM1127 EPS was non-irritating (PII = 0). [13] *Pseudoalteromonas* and *Sphingomonas* ferment extracts are widely used and well tolerated. No notable sensitization signal in the literature; still, **generate our own patch/HRIPT data** for any finished formula.
- **Algae-specific cautions:** "Algae Extract" can carry **iodine** and species-specific proteins → rare allergy in iodine/seaweed-sensitive users; some red-algae thickeners (**carrageenan**) carry higher comedogenicity ratings (~3–4). Species/grade selection matters. [11][4]
- **Sun:** No photosensitivity concern; some marine EPS are mildly photo-*protective* against UVB irritation in vitro (Abyssine). Not a sunscreen. [3]
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** No specific contraindication for topical EPS; default conservative copy ("consult your doctor"). Avoid iodine-heavy algal extracts marketing toward pregnancy without checking.
- **Sourcing/biotech note:** Ferment actives are produced by controlled industrial fermentation → reproducible, vegan, and avoid wild-harvest pressure; this is a genuine sustainability/clean-beauty positive. Algae extracts vary (wild-harvest vs cultivated). [3][10]
- **Regulatory safety status:** Gellan/sphingan reviewed by **CIR as safe** for cosmetic use; algae/brown-algae-derived ingredients covered by CIR safety assessments; EWG generally rates these low-hazard (algae extract ~1). [3][11][12]

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic ingredient status:** All three are established cosmetic INCIs with skin-conditioning/humectant/emollient functions (CosIng-listed). Sphingomonas/algae are low-hazard per EWG/CIR. No drug status as humectants. [3][10][11][12]
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** Anything implying the ferment **"boosts collagen," "stimulates elastin," "regenerates skin," "repairs," "heals wounds," "increases ceramide synthesis,"** or **"reduces wrinkle depth by X%"** moves toward **structure/function (drug) claims** and would need substantiation we do not have (the supporting data are in-vitro or supplier-run). The Antarcticine wound-healing heritage is a **patent/biology story, not a cosmetic claim we can make.**
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "marine-derived humectant that helps skin hold onto moisture," "forms a breathable, hydrating veil," "helps reduce the look of dryness-related fine lines," "supports a comfortable, resilient-feeling barrier," "visibly smooths and plumps," "soothing marine active," "inspired by extremophile biology / cold-adapted marine science."
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation or legal review:** "−44% wrinkle depth," "increases collagen I/IV and elastin," "boosts ceramide synthesis," "repairs the skin barrier," "anti-aging at the cellular level," "better than hyaluronic acid" (the HA comparison is benchtop on cousin molecules — do not claim for our product without our own data).
- **Substantiation reality:** To make any performance claim beyond hydration/sensory, we'd need **our own finished-formula human testing** — the public literature will not substantiate a conventional marine-EPS anti-aging claim, and supplier "−44%" data is not ours to assert. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Forms/suppliers:**
  - Antarcticine® / *Pseudoalteromonas* Ferment — **Lubrizol/Lipotec** (premium, branded; COSMOS-approved variant exists). [6][8]
  - *Sphingomonas* Ferment Extract — multiple ferment-active suppliers (Asian and EU biotech houses). [10]
  - Algae Extract — abundant, many suppliers, huge price/quality range (commodity seaweed extract → ultra-premium "broths"). [11]
  - Cousin branded EPS: Abyssine® (Lucas Meyer/IFF), Hyadisine®/Hyanify® (Lubrizol), Epidermist 4.0™/EPS-series (Codif). [3]
- **Cost tier:** **Mid → high.** Generic algae extract is cheap; branded marine-ferment actives (Antarcticine, Codif/Lubrizol EPS) carry premium pricing and a strong "marine biotech" story that justifies it on a premium SKU.
- **Supply/standardization risk:** "Algae Extract" is the big one — **demand species, extraction method, and a quantified actives spec** or the ingredient is a black box. Ferment actives are more reproducible (controlled fermentation). [3][11]

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **The "−44% wrinkle depth" staple.** Repeated identically across resellers; traces to a **manufacturer study of n = 10**, women only, 30 days, not (to my finding) peer-reviewed or independently replicated. A single tiny vendor trial dressed as established fact. [1][6][8]
- **"Better than hyaluronic acid."** True *on a benchtop water-retention assay for a cousin Arctic EPS (SM1127)* [13] and some algal polysaccharides [4] — **not** demonstrated for the commercial INCIs in human skin, and water-retention in a dish ≠ superior clinical hydration.
- **"Boosts collagen/elastin in your skin."** In-vitro/3D, direct cell dosing — and the active is a **macromolecule that doesn't reach the dermis** topically. Mechanistically strained as a topical claim. [3]
- **"*Sphingomonas* restores your ceramides."** The bacterium makes glycosphingolipids in *its own* membrane [5]; there is **no located human study** showing the ferment raises *human* stratum-corneum ceramides. (The one peer-reviewed in-vivo human Sphingomonas-ferment study [18] is industry-funded and measures *soothing/vascular* benefit, not ceramides — so it does not rescue this specific claim.) Inference sold as fact. [10][18]
- **"Algae Extract is a powerhouse active."** "Algae Extract" is a **retired, non-specific INCI** [11] — it can be almost anything; potency claims without species/standardization are meaningless.
- **Borrowed-cousin problem.** The genuinely good peer-reviewed data (diabolican, SM1127, Cobetia) belongs to **different organisms** than what's on most labels — readers assume the data transfers; it doesn't automatically. [3][13]
- **What's actually solid:** These are **excellent humectants/film-formers with great sensory and a strong sustainability/marine-biotech narrative, and a clean safety record.** That is a real, defensible value proposition — just not the cellular-anti-aging one the marketing implies.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING — strong hydration/sensory + narrative asset; weak independent anti-aging-efficacy asset.**

**One-line:** Marine/ferment exopolysaccharides are a credible, beautifully-on-theme **hydration, comfort, and texture** active with a premium "extremophile marine biotech" story and excellent safety — but their anti-aging headlines are **in-vitro, patent-, or supplier-grade**, so use the class as a **supporting humectant/sensory layer with honest, hydration-framed copy**, not as a proof-backed anti-aging hero.

**Why supporting, not hero:** The barrier-repair / skin-longevity thesis wants *evidence-backed* heroes (retinoids, niacinamide, ceramides+cholesterol, peptides with human data). This class doesn't clear that bar on independent human evidence. But it **earns its place** as: (1) a genuinely effective humectant/film-former that improves immediate feel, plumping and TEWL; (2) a **soothing, well-tolerated** active suited to a barrier-comfort product; (3) a **premium sensory/texture** workhorse that lets us avoid silicones; and (4) a **differentiating narrative** (Antarctic/extremophile marine biotech, sustainable fermentation) that fits a science-forward premium brand — *if* copy stays in the hydration/comfort lane.

**Recommended use:** Supporting humectant + sensory modifier in the hydration system of a barrier/moisture product. If we want to feature it on-pack, lead with **Antarcticine® (branded story + the marine-biotech narrative)** and/or a **depolymerized Codif/Lubrizol EPS with the most credible technical file**, choose **fermentation-derived (not vague "algae extract")** for sustainability and reproducibility, and **commission our own finished-formula hydration/TEWL human testing** before any performance claim.

**Longevity-relevance note:** The longevity *fit* is thematic and mechanistic-plausible (cold-adapted survival polymers, barrier resilience, antioxidant scavenging) rather than evidence-proven (no senescence/autophagy/lifespan data like spermidine or NAD actives). It supports the **"resilience/protection"** facet of skin longevity well as a comfort/hydration layer — but it is not, on current evidence, a cellular-aging intervention. Honest path to any stronger claim is **our own clinical data**, ideally on a specific branded, characterized EPS rather than the umbrella "algae extract."

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

*Independent adversarial check performed 2026-06-19. Method: for each headline claim, I asked (a) is the evidence HUMAN and TOPICAL, or borrowed from in-vitro/animal/oral?; (b) are the cited studies real and do they say what's claimed?; (c) is the source industry-funded or tiny? Spot-checked the load-bearing primary sources against the literature.*

### Overall grade: **C (weak / indirect for anti-aging; solid only for the narrow humectancy/sensory claim)**

The dossier itself already argues for ~this grade, and the verification confirms it. The class is a **legitimate humectant/film-former with excellent safety and a strong narrative**, but for **anti-aging efficacy on the three named INCIs there is no independent human-topical RCT** — the headline numbers are supplier/patent/marketing-grade or borrowed from cousin strains in vitro. (If the brand's claim were narrowed strictly to *immediate hydration / sensory feel*, that sub-claim would merit ~B on benchtop physiology + supplier human-use data; the anti-aging framing as a whole does not.)

### What checked out (verified against primary sources)
- **[13] Sun 2015 (Polaribacter SM1127), *Sci Rep* 5:18435 — VERIFIED EXACTLY.** ~220 kDa; moisture retention 75.79 ± 2.5% at 72 h > HA; DPPH 55.4%, •OH 52.1%, O₂•⁻ 28.2% at 10 mg/mL; HDF viability 76.1% vs 44.2% at 4 °C / 500 µg/mL; PII = 0; oral LD₅₀ > 5000 mg/kg. Every number in the dossier matches. **But it is a cousin Arctic strain, benchtop + cell assays — NOT human, NOT topical, NOT the commercial INCIs.** The dossier labels it correctly.
- **[3] Benhadda et al. 2023, *Marine Drugs* 21(11):582 — VERIFIED.** Real review, correct authors/journal/volume, correct thesis (catalogs marine/non-marine bacterial EPS for skincare; flags evidence as largely in-vitro/patent-derived). Correctly used as the honest backbone.
- **Antarcticine "−44% wrinkle depth" — VERIFIED as described, and verified as supplier-grade.** The 5% cream / twice-daily / 30 d / n=10 women / ~44% (max 50–60%) figure is real *as a marketing claim* and is repeated verbatim across resellers and even consumer press (e.g. Vogue Scandinavia). No peer-reviewed primary source located. The dossier's red-flag treatment is correct and appropriately harsh.
- **[16] rat wound-healing/frostbite — REAL** (Marine Drugs 2020). Rodent, cousin strain, acute-injury model — correctly downgraded.

### Defects found and FIXED in this pass
- **[2] mis-cited.** Dossier had "Nichols C.M. / Bozal" (and §4 "Mejía et al.") at "*Microbial Ecology* 53:159." The real paper is **Nevot, Deroncelé, López-Iglesias, Bozal, Guinea, Mercadé (2006), *Microbial Ecology* 51:501–507** (DOI resolves correctly). Author and volume/page corrected in §4 and §12.
- **[16] wrong lead author.** Was "Liu S.-B."; actual lead author is **Mei-Ling Sun** (same group as [13]). Corrected.
- **[18] overstated dismissal — softened.** The dossier said the *S. xenophaga* study is "a different organism, different active … must NOT be cited." Verification shows it is in fact a **characterized *Sphingomonas* ferment extract** tested **in vivo in humans** — so it IS relevant human-topical data for a Sphingomonas ferment. BUT its endpoint is **soothing / vascular disorder**, it is **industry-funded (La Roche-Posay/L'Oréal)**, and it does **not** measure ceramides or hydration. Rewritten to credit it as modest human support for a *soothing* angle only, while still barring it from the ceramide/hydration claims. This *strengthens* a soothing sub-claim slightly without rescuing the anti-aging story.

### Main caveats (read before using this ingredient in copy)
1. **Borrowed-cousin problem is the dominant risk.** Almost all the quantified, peer-reviewed data (SM1127, diabolican, Cobetia) is on organisms that are **not on the label**. Do not let "marine EPS studies show…" imply the data transfers to *Pseudoalteromonas/Sphingomonas/Algae* INCIs.
2. **In-vitro ≠ topical.** The collagen/elastin/fibroblast results come from **direct cell-culture dosing** of macromolecules that **do not penetrate intact skin** (10⁴–10⁶ g/mol). The "boosts collagen in your dermis" story is mechanistically incoherent for a topical film.
3. **The flagship human numbers are tiny and conflicted.** Antarcticine's "−44%" is n=10, women-only, manufacturer-run, unpublished. The "30% hydration / 50 subjects" figure has **no traceable primary source** and remains **[unverified]**.
4. **Algae Extract is a black box.** Retired/non-specific INCI; potency claims without species + standardization are meaningless.

### Claims to soften / avoid (now reflected in §8 and §10)
- ❌ "−44% wrinkle depth," "increases collagen I/IV + elastin," "boosts ceramide synthesis," "repairs the barrier," "anti-aging at the cellular level," "better than hyaluronic acid." None are defensible from independent human-topical evidence on these INCIs.
- ✅ Defensible lane: "marine-derived humectant that helps skin hold moisture," "forms a breathable hydrating veil," "visibly smooths/plumps," "soothing marine active" (the soothing angle now has modest *industry-funded* human support for a Sphingomonas ferment via [18]), "extremophile/marine-biotech story," plus the sustainability narrative.
- **Any performance claim beyond immediate hydration/sensory requires our own finished-formula human testing.**

### Verdict
The dossier was already honest, well-tiered, and correctly skeptical — it did the hard adversarial work itself and lands on the right call (**SUPPORTING humectant/sensory/narrative asset, not an anti-aging hero**). Verification upholds that verdict and the **C grade**, with three citation/scoping fixes applied. No headline claim in the dossier was found to be fabricated or materially overstated *beyond* what it already disclosed.
