# Ingredient: Japanese Knotweed (resveratrol source) (INCI: Polygonum Cuspidatum Root Extract / Reynoutria Japonica Root Extract)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Botanicals & plant bioactives (stilbenoid polyphenol source — primary active *trans*-resveratrol; co-actives polydatin, emodin, quercetin)

> **One-line honest summary:** Japanese knotweed is the cheapest, most concentrated natural source of *trans*-resveratrol, and resveratrol has a deep, genuinely impressive mechanistic and longevity pedigree (SIRT1, Nrf2, anti-glycation, senescence). But for **human topical skin**, the honest verdict is sobering: the **single best RCT (2025) found topical-only resveratrol did NOT significantly reduce wrinkles** — the wrinkle benefit needed *oral* dosing. Resveratrol also penetrates poorly (stays in upper stratum corneum), oxidizes/isomerizes in light, and the knotweed extract co-delivers **emodin**, an anthraquinone with genotoxicity flags. Strong **antioxidant/brightening/narrative** asset; weak **proof-backed anti-wrinkle hero**. Treat as a **supporting/conditional** active, and strongly prefer a **purified, low-emodin, stabilized** grade.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Japanese knotweed (also Asian knotweed, Mexican bamboo, *Hu Zhang* in TCM). "Resveratrol source" because it is the dominant *commercial* feedstock for natural *trans*-resveratrol.
- **Botanical name:** *Reynoutria japonica* (older/synonym name *Polygonum cuspidatum*, also *Fallopia japonica*). The plant-name churn matters for INCI — both forms appear on labels.
- **INCI:** **Polygonum Cuspidatum Root Extract** (most common cosmetic listing) / **Reynoutria Japonica Root Extract**. The purified isolate is listed separately as **Resveratrol** (INCI: Resveratrol). Confirm which one a supplier means — "knotweed extract" and "resveratrol" are *not* interchangeable on a COA.
- **CAS (resveratrol):** 501-36-0. **Molecular formula:** C₁₄H₁₂O₃ · **MW ~228.24 g/mol** (small molecule — favorable for penetration on size alone, *but* hydrophobic; see §5).
- **Chemical class:** Resveratrol is a **stilbenoid** (hydroxylated stilbene; 3,5,4′-trihydroxy-*trans*-stilbene). The cosmetically/biologically active isomer is ***trans*-resveratrol**; UV light converts it to the less-active **cis** isomer (a core stability problem — see §6).
- **What's actually in the root extract (the honest composition point):** Knotweed root is a *polyphenol cocktail*, not pure resveratrol:
  - ***trans*-resveratrol** — the headline active. Standardized grades run **50%, 70%, 95%, up to ~98%** resveratrol.
  - **Polydatin (piceid)** — resveratrol-3-O-glucoside; more water-soluble, more stable, a "pro-resveratrol."
  - **Emodin** and other **anthraquinones** (physcion, chrysophanol) — these are the **safety liability** of the whole-plant extract (laxative, genotoxicity signals; §7).
  - **Quercetin, catechins, other flavonoids.**
- **Key forms / grades you will choose between:**
  - **Whole/low-standardization knotweed extract** (e.g., glycerin/water "Polygonum Cuspidatum Root Extract") — carries the full polyphenol + **emodin** load.
  - **Standardized high-% *trans*-resveratrol** (50–98%) purified from knotweed — the form most "resveratrol serums" actually use; emodin largely removed at higher purities (verify on COA).
  - **Synthetic / fermentation-derived resveratrol** (e.g., Evolva/Lallemand "Veri-te") — yeast-fermented, knotweed-free, **emodin-free**, traceable; used in the 2025 RCT.
  - **Esterified/stabilized analogs** (not "knotweed" per se but the same chemistry, better-behaved): **resveratryl triacetate (RTA)** and **resveratryl triglycolate (RTG)** — more stable, less irritating, with their own human data (§4).

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it works at the skin/cell level)

Resveratrol is a multi-target polyphenol antioxidant. Its mechanistic story is unusually rich and maps onto several aging hallmarks — which is exactly why it is *narratively* attractive and why caution is needed (rich mechanism ≠ proven topical outcome).

Mechanistic pillars (mostly established in cells/animals; route matters):
1. **Direct antioxidant / ROS scavenging + Nrf2 activation.** Resveratrol scavenges free radicals and **upregulates the Nrf2/HO-1 axis** and endogenous antioxidant enzymes (Mn-SOD), reducing oxidative stress. This is its best-supported, most route-agnostic action — and it retains antiradical activity even when it sits in the stratum corneum. [9][10]
2. **SIRT1 (sirtuin) activation.** The famous "longevity gene" link: resveratrol activates SIRT1 (and SIRT6), which regulate DNA repair, mitochondrial biogenesis, and senescence markers (p16/p21). In dermal fibroblasts, **SIRT1 knockdown raises MMP-1/MMP-3**, and resveratrol suppresses that induction — a plausible anti-collagen-breakdown mechanism. *Caveat: the "resveratrol is a direct SIRT1 activator" claim has been scientifically contested (assay-artifact debate); effects may be partly indirect/AMPK-mediated.* [4][5][13]
3. **MMP suppression / collagen preservation.** In UVB models, resveratrol suppresses **MMP-1/MMP-9** via inhibition of MAPK and Akt/mTOR pathways, limiting collagen degradation; maintains collagen IV. [9][12]
4. **Anti-inflammatory (NF-κB / cytokine suppression).** Inhibits NF-κB and lowers IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α, COX-2 — relevant to "inflammaging." [9][13]
5. **Anti-melanogenic / tyrosinase inhibition.** Inhibits tyrosinase catalytic activity, gene expression, and protein maturation — a real, repeatable mechanism behind the brightening claims (and the one with the cleanest *topical* human-adjacent data via analogs). [7][8]
6. **Anti-glycation.** Reduces AGE formation and blocks AGE-RAGE signaling (↓RAGE, ↑PPAR-γ, ↑AGE scavenger receptor), protecting collagen in glycated 3D skin models. [6]
7. **Senolytic-adjacent / SASP suppression.** Chronic low-dose resveratrol attenuates the senescence-associated secretory phenotype (↓IL-1α/β, IL-6, IL-8, TGF-β, MMP-2/3) and restores type I collagen toward presenescent levels in cultured fibroblasts. [14]

**Skin-relevant translation (honest):** items 1, 5, 6 translate reasonably well to "applied antioxidant/brightening" because they can act at/near the surface or in upper layers; items 2, 3, 4, 7 are real cell biology but require resveratrol to actually *reach viable cells at active concentrations*, which topical delivery does not reliably achieve (§5). Mechanistic breadth is genuine; topical efficacy is the bottleneck.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** Resveratrol's evidence pyramid is **broad at the bottom (in vitro/animal mechanism) and oral-heavy in the middle**, but **thin and unflattering at the top for purified topical anti-wrinkle use**. The first rigorous RCT to isolate *trans*-resveratrol as the *only* active (2025) found that **topical resveratrol alone did NOT significantly reduce wrinkles** — wrinkle reduction required the *oral* arm. The genuinely positive *topical* human data are mostly for **pigmentation/brightening**, for **multi-ingredient blends** (so you can't attribute the effect to resveratrol), or for **esterified analogs** (RTA/RTG), not plain knotweed resveratrol.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell & 3D models) — *strong and abundant*

- **Ohguchi et al. (SIRT1/MMP-1).** SIRT1 knockdown in dermal fibroblasts ↑MMP-1/MMP-3; resveratrol (SIRT1 modulator) suppresses IL-1β–driven induction. *Mechanistic basis for "preserves collagen." Cell culture only.* [4]
- **Wu/Wang/Liu et al. (resveratrol reverses SIRT1↓ and MMP-1↑ in vitro), 2015** (PubMed 26505388). Resveratrol restored SIRT1 and reduced MMP-1 expression. **Caveat (verified):** this is an **intervertebral-disc (spinal disc) cell** study, *not* dermal/skin tissue — the SIRT1→↓MMP-1 mechanism is borrowed from a non-skin system and should be read as suggestive mechanism only, not skin-specific evidence. *Cell culture.* [5]
- **Anti-glycation, 3D human skin models, 2022 — Tang et al., *Clin Cosmet Investig Dermatol*** (DOI 10.2147/CCID.S364538; PMC9126233). Resveratrol, oxyresveratrol, piceatannol, and **triacetyl-resveratrol** reduced AGE formation, ↑collagen, restored epidermal stratification in glycated dermal-fibroblast and 3D models. *In vitro 3D; derivatives tested alongside parent.* [6]
- **SASP suppression / senescence, 2013 — Menicacci/Pitozzi et al., *J Gerontol A Biol Sci Med Sci*** (academic.oup.com/biomedgerontology/article/68/4/371). Chronic 5 µM resveratrol in near-senescent MRC5 fibroblasts ↓IL-1α/β, IL-6, IL-8, TGF-β, MMP-2/3, ↓NF-κB, and restored type I collagen + cell adhesion toward presenescent levels. *Cell culture; chronic low dose.* [14]
- **Tyrosinase / melanogenesis (multiple).** Resveratrol inhibits tyrosinase catalytic activity, gene expression, and protein maturation; reported in vitro to be a **more potent tyrosinase inhibitor than hydroquinone, arbutin, and vitamin C**. *In vitro potency ≠ in vivo clinical equivalence — see hype §10.* [7][8]
- **Photoaging mechanism — Cui et al., 2022, *Oxidative Medicine and Cellular Longevity*** (DOI 10.1155/2022/6037303; PMC8752231). In HaCaT cells (and ICR mice), resveratrol acted via anti-MMP, anti-inflammatory (↓IL-6/TNF-α/COX-2), antioxidant, antiapoptotic pathways and ↑VEGF-B. [9]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo) — *consistent photoprotection signal*

- **Cui et al., 2022 (ICR mouse arm).** Resveratrol pretreatment protected against UVB photoaging (anti-MMP, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, suppressed MAPK). [9]
- **Kim et al. (mouse UVB).** *trans*-resveratrol protected against UVB-induced epidermal thickening and wrinkle formation; induced Nrf2-dependent antioxidant enzymes; inhibited MMPs. Oral gavage ~2 mg/kg 3×/week improved skin structure/hydration. *Mouse; partly oral route.* [12]
- **Skin metabolism caveat — Murakami et al., 2014, *Biol Pharm Bull*** (PMC4266648). Skin-absorbed resveratrol in mouse skin is **metabolized to its glucuronide** in situ — i.e., even what penetrates may be partly conjugated/inactivated locally. *Directly relevant skeptic's point for topical claims.* [17]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL (where much human data live — not skin-specific, and bioavailability is poor)

- **Walle et al., 2004, *Drug Metab Dispos*** (PubMed 15333514). Landmark PK: oral resveratrol is **well absorbed (~70%)** but has **very low bioavailability** — only trace unchanged resveratrol in plasma (<5 ng/mL) due to **rapid sulfate/glucuronide conjugation**. *Foundational reason oral resveratrol underdelivers; route-agnostic caution.* [15]
- **Resveratrol bioavailability meta-analysis, 2024, *Phytotherapy Research*** (DOI 10.1002/ptr.8379). Confirms low, variable systemic exposure across clinical trials; metabolite-dominated. [18]
- **2025 RCT oral + topical (the pivotal study) — Rao, Briskey, Roche et al., *Frontiers in Aging*** (DOI 10.3389/fragi.2025.1727244; PMC12757695). See Tier 4 — it had an **oral arm** that *did* drive the wrinkle benefit (A/A group: wrinkles −11.9% vs placebo −6.3%, p<0.05; serum *trans*-resveratrol conjugates rose). [1]
- *Broader oral human literature (cardiometabolic, glycemic, etc.) is mixed and outside skin scope; the takeaway for us is the bioavailability ceiling, not a skin claim.*

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters for a skincare brand)

> Read this tier carefully — it is where the honest case is made or lost.

- **★ Rao, Briskey, Roche, Tremblay, Da Silva Pinto, Tompkins (2025). "Trans-resveratrol reduces visible signs of skin ageing in healthy adult females over 40: an 8-week randomized placebo-controlled trial." *Frontiers in Aging* (DOI 10.3389/fragi.2025.1727244; PMC12757695).** The most important and most honest single source. Double-blind, **2×2 factorial RCT, n=134 enrolled / 122 completed**, women ≥40, 8 weeks. Four arms: placebo/placebo, **oral 150 mg/day + placebo cream**, **placebo capsule + topical 1.5% cream**, and **oral + topical**.
  - **Topical-only (P/A) vs placebo:** **NO significant wrinkle reduction** (primary endpoint failed for topical alone, and no difference on forehead/glabellar/crow's-feet/nasolabial secondary wrinkle measures). **Pigmentation** decreased significantly more than placebo (p<0.001). **Sebum (U-zone)** increased significantly (P/A +88.7% vs P/P +34.7%). **No** significant change in moisture, elasticity, pore size, or "skin age."
  - **Combined oral+topical (A/A) vs placebo:** wrinkles **−11.9% vs −6.3% (p<0.05)**; pigmentation ↓ (p<0.001); sebum ↑.
  - **Limitations (author-acknowledged):** **collagen not assessed**; no senescence biomarkers; no androgen profiling (despite the odd sebum rise); 8 weeks is short; seasonal confounding; **industry-funded** (Evolva AG; two authors employed by Lallemand, which acquired Evolva). Women-only.
  - **Bottom line:** This is the cleanest topical evidence we have and it says **plain topical resveratrol's strongest skin effect is on pigmentation/tone, not wrinkles**, and the much-hyped wrinkle benefit needed the *oral* route. [1]
- **Farris et al., 2014, *J Drugs Dermatol*** 13(12):1467–72 (PubMed 25607790). Open-label, 12 weeks: a nighttime serum with **1% resveratrol + 0.5% baicalin + 1% vitamin E** improved fine lines/wrinkles, firmness, elasticity, laxity, hyperpigmentation, radiance, roughness; periorbital **dermal thickness +18.9%** by ultrasound; gene-expression changes (HO-1, VEGFA, COL3A1). **Crucial caveat:** this is a **3-ingredient blend** with **no placebo control** — you cannot attribute the benefit to resveratrol, and baicalin + vitamin E are themselves active antioxidants. [16]
- **Ryu et al., 2015, *Arch Dermatol Res*** 307(3):239–247 (DOI 10.1007/s00403-015-1556-0). **Resveratryl triacetate (RTA)** — an esterified, stabilized resveratrol prodrug — studied in humans at **0.4%** for efficacy and **0.1%/0.5%** for irritation. **Verified scope (corrected):** this study measured **skin-whitening/depigmentation only** — ↑ITA° in an artificial-tanning model (test +17.06% vs control +13.81%, p<0.05) and ↓hyperpigmented-spot intensity (test −2.67% vs control −1.46%, p<0.05) — **plus an irritation/safety endpoint: 0.5% plain resveratrol caused weak irritation whereas RTA did not.** It did **NOT** measure wrinkles, wrinkle area/volume, sagging, or elasticity. *(An earlier version of this dossier attributed anti-wrinkle/elasticity numbers to this paper; those were unsupported and have been removed.)* *Active is a derivative, not knotweed resveratrol; vendor-adjacent (Amorepacific "Resveratrate" lineage).* [3]
- **Park/Boo et al., 2018, *Arch Dermatol Res*** — **resveratryl triglycolate (RTG)** human depigmenting study (PubMed 29663438). Hybrid of resveratrol + glycolic acid showed human skin-depigmenting effects. *Derivative.* [8]
- **Post-laser adjuvant RCT, 2024 — Shi et al., *JEADV*** (DOI 10.1111/jdv.20070). Split-face RCT: a regimen including a **resveratrol B E serum** (again a **multi-ingredient** SkinCeuticals-type product) supported recovery after ablative fractional CO₂ laser. *Cannot isolate resveratrol; blend + procedure context.* [19]
- **Acne / wound pilots** (per 2025 review [10]): a topical resveratrol gel reduced microcomedone area ~66.7% and GAGS ~53.75% (small pilot, sample size unspecified); RES-impregnated matrices (Dermalix®) improved diabetic foot ulcer closure. *Pilot/disease contexts, small, not anti-aging.* [10]

**Bottom line by tier:** Mechanism (in vitro/animal) is **strong and broad**. Human **oral** data exist but bioavailability is poor and skin-specific oral data are limited. Human **topical** data for *plain* resveratrol show its reliable wins are **pigmentation/antioxidant**, **not wrinkles** (2025 RCT); the only impressive anti-wrinkle *topical* results come from **uncontrolled multi-ingredient blends** (Farris) — and even the **esterified analogs (RTA/RTG)** have *verified* human data only for **depigmentation + better tolerability**, not for wrinkles (the RTA anti-wrinkle figures previously cited here were unsupported). No high-quality RCT validates a plain *Polygonum cuspidatum* (knotweed) extract as a topical anti-wrinkle hero.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Size says yes, chemistry says no.** Resveratrol is small (~228 g/mol) — good on size — but **poorly water-soluble and moderately lipophilic in an awkward way**: low aqueous solubility limits how much loads into the stratum corneum, and once applied it **largely stays in the upper stratum corneum layers**, with limited transdermal permeation to viable epidermis/dermis. [11][20]
- **It works partly *because* it stays put.** Reassuringly, resveratrol **retains antiradical/antioxidant activity within the stratum corneum** — so a surface/upper-layer antioxidant-shield benefit is real even without deep penetration. This supports "environmental defense / antioxidant" positioning more than "rebuilds dermal collagen." [11]
- **Local metabolism.** What does penetrate can be **glucuronidated in the skin itself** (shown in mouse skin), partially inactivating it in situ. [17]
- **Delivery systems are the active research frontier** precisely because plain delivery is weak: nanoemulsions (chitosan-coated, reported ~96% release over 36 h), solid lipid nanoparticles (↑cutaneous retention), niosomes/ethosomes, liposomes, cyclodextrin complexes, lipid-core nanocapsules (also ↑photostability), and microneedles. These improve penetration *and* protect from light degradation. [10][11][20]
- **Practical read:** For a conventional emulsion/serum, expect a **topside antioxidant/brightening** benefit and be skeptical of deep-dermal "collagen-rebuilding" claims. A delivery system (encapsulation) or an **esterified analog (RTA/RTG)** is the credible route to better performance and stability.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** Common cosmetic use **0.5–1%**; the 2025 RCT cream used **1.5%**; RTA analog studies used **0.4–0.8%**. There is **no validated topical dose-response for wrinkles** (the one clean RCT failed that endpoint topically). Brightening/antioxidant positioning is the better-supported use of ~0.5–1%. Don't imply a proven wrinkle dose.
- **pH:** Resveratrol is **most stable in acidic-to-neutral conditions and least stable in basic media** — keep formulas **acidic/neutral (~pH 5–6.5)**. Avoid high-pH systems. [11]
- **Stability (the big one):**
  - **Light:** *trans* → *cis* **photoisomerization within 1–2 hours of light exposure**, converting it to the less-active isomer. **Opaque/airless packaging is essentially mandatory.** [11]
  - **Oxidation:** polyphenol — oxidizes and can **discolor** (yellow/brown) over time; pair with chelators and consider antioxidant co-stabilizers. Oxidized resveratrol is also implicated in **sensitization** (§7).
  - **Mitigations:** encapsulation (nanocapsules/liposomes ↑photostability), low pH, opaque airless pack, antioxidant synergists, or switch to the more stable **polydatin** or **esterified analog (RTA/RTG)**.
- **Solubility/incorporation:** hydrophobicity makes it **hard to load** — often pre-dissolved in glycols/solvents or delivered as the glycoside (polydatin) or encapsulated.
- **Pairings (conceptual):**
  - **Vitamin E + ferulic acid / vitamin C** — classic antioxidant network; resveratrol regenerates/cooperates with these (the SkinCeuticals "Resveratrol B E" logic). [16][19]
  - **Baicalin** — co-antioxidant used in the Farris blend. [16]
  - **Niacinamide** — barrier/tone, well-tolerated co-active.
  - **Other longevity-narrative actives** (spermidine, NAD precursors) — story synergy; no proven topical combo data.
- **Conflicts:** high-pH bases (destabilizing); strong oxidizers; anything that pushes browning. Co-formulating raw acids is fine (acidic pH is actually *good* for stability), but watch overall irritation if also using retinoids/AHAs.
- **Sensory:** purified resveratrol is a pale powder; whole knotweed extract can carry **color and earthy odor**, and discoloration risk over shelf life is real.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation:** Generally well tolerated at **0.5–1%**. Notable nuance: **0.5% plain resveratrol caused weak skin irritation** in the RTA comparison study, whereas the **RTA ester did not** — i.e., the parent can be mildly irritating where the derivative isn't. The 2025 RCT reported only mild adverse events across all arms. [1][3]
- **Sensitization / allergy:** **Documented allergic contact dermatitis to resveratrol in a cosmetic cream** exists; resveratrol appears on lists of antioxidant/herbal sensitizers, with **oxidation-related allergenicity and cross-reactivity** reported. Oxidized product is more likely to sensitize — another reason stability/packaging matters. **HRIPT on the finished formula is advisable.** [21]
- **Emodin / anthraquinone liability (knotweed-specific — important):** Whole/low-purity *Polygonum cuspidatum* extract co-delivers **emodin** and related anthraquinones. Emodin is a **laxative** and shows **genotoxicity/mutagenicity signals** in bacterial and mammalian-cell assays (and hepatotoxicity at high doses in animals); EFSA treats hydroxyanthracene derivatives as **genotoxic/carcinogenic concerns** absent specific contrary data. This is primarily an **oral/food** concern, and topical exposure is far lower, but it argues strongly for a **purified, low-emodin (or emodin-free synthetic/fermentation) grade** and for requiring **emodin content on the COA**. [22][23]
- **Sun sensitivity:** No photosensitization flag; mechanistically resveratrol is **photoprotective** (antioxidant, anti-MMP under UV). It is **not** a sunscreen — pair with SPF messaging, don't replace it. [9][12]
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** No specific topical pregnancy safety data. Resveratrol has **phytoestrogenic / SERM-like activity** in some models, so default to **conservative copy ("consult your doctor")**; the emodin (laxative anthraquinone) issue is another reason to keep whole-extract exposure low.
- **Sourcing/allergen:** Knotweed-derived material is generally low-allergen, but disclose botanical source; fermentation/synthetic grade avoids plant-contaminant variability entirely.
- **Net:** Low-to-moderate concern at cosmetic levels **with a purified grade and good packaging**; the realistic risks are **oxidation-driven sensitization** and **emodin carryover** in cheap whole-extract material.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic ingredient status:** **Polygonum Cuspidatum Root Extract** and **Resveratrol** are established cosmetic INCIs (antioxidant / skin-conditioning functions). No specific cosmetic concentration restriction widely flagged, but emodin/anthraquinone content can attract scrutiny — keep it low and documented. [24]
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** The temptation is to import resveratrol's **longevity / SIRT1 / "cellular renewal" / "boosts collagen"** narrative into topical claims. "Activates your skin's longevity genes," "rebuilds collagen," "anti-aging at the cellular level," and any wrinkle-*reduction* performance claim would be **structure/function (drug-like)** and, for wrinkles specifically, **contradicted by the best topical RCT**. High legal/substantiation risk.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "antioxidant-rich botanical," "helps defend against environmental/free-radical stress," "helps visibly even skin tone / improve the look of dullness and dark spots" (best-supported topical effect), "from one of nature's richest sources of resveratrol," "inspired by longevity science."
- ⚠️ **Risky / substantiation-or-legal-review:** "reduces wrinkles," "boosts collagen by X%," "activates SIRT1/longevity genes in your skin," "reverses aging," "as effective as hydroquinone." The in-vitro "stronger than hydroquinone" line is **lab potency, not clinical equivalence** — do not use comparatively.
- **Substantiation reality:** To claim wrinkle/elasticity performance we'd need **our own finished-formula human testing** (ideally with a delivery system or analog), because the public literature **does not** support a plain topical-resveratrol anti-wrinkle claim. Tone/antioxidant claims are more defensible. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Forms/suppliers:** (1) **Whole/standardized knotweed extract** and **50–98% *trans*-resveratrol** from botanical-extract suppliers (large Chinese supply base; knotweed is the global natural-resveratrol feedstock). (2) **Fermentation/synthetic resveratrol** (e.g., Evolva/Lallemand "Veri-te" / Veriol; DSM and others) — knotweed-free, emodin-free, traceable. (3) **Esterified analogs RTA/RTG** (Amorepacific lineage).
- **Cost tier:** **Low–mid** for knotweed-derived resveratrol (it's the cheap natural source — a key reason knotweed dominates). **Higher** for fermentation-derived, encapsulated, or analog grades — but these buy you **emodin removal, stability, and a cleaner claims/safety story**, which for a premium brand is likely worth it.
- **Supply/standardization risk:** Whole-extract **resveratrol % and emodin content vary widely**; cheap grades may be under-spec on resveratrol and over-spec on anthraquinones. **Require a COA quantifying *trans*-resveratrol AND emodin**; specify the *trans* isomer (not total stilbene) and consider fermentation grade to sidestep botanical variability and the "invasive species / sustainability" framing entirely.
- **Sustainability angle:** Knotweed is a notorious **invasive weed** — there's a marketable "we use an invasive plant" story, but supply is actually industrially cultivated/harvested in Asia; don't overclaim a foraged-invasive narrative without verifying the actual supply chain.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **The longevity halo.** Resveratrol's fame comes from **yeast/worm/fly/mouse lifespan and SIRT1** work (Sinclair et al.) and **red-wine/French-paradox** lore. That is *oral/systemic/model-organism* science being lent to a topical serum. Even the **SIRT1-as-direct-target** claim is scientifically **contested** (assay-artifact controversy).
- **"Topical resveratrol reduces wrinkles."** The **best RCT (2025) says topical-only did NOT** significantly reduce wrinkles — the benefit needed the **oral** route. Topical resveratrol's reliable win was **pigmentation**. [1]
- **The blend trick.** The most-cited "resveratrol works topically" studies (Farris 2014; post-laser RCT) are **multi-ingredient** (baicalin, vitamin E, ferulic, etc.) and often **uncontrolled** — you can't credit resveratrol. [16][19]
- **The analog switch.** The clean positive *topical human* data for **RTA/RTG esters** are for **whitening/depigmentation and tolerability**, not wrinkles — and they're vendor-driven; they are still not plain knotweed resveratrol. (Marketing that implies RTA proves an anti-wrinkle effect is overreach — the human RTA paper did not measure wrinkles.) [3][8]
- **"Stronger tyrosinase inhibitor than hydroquinone."** True **in a test tube**; not demonstrated as clinically superior depigmenting in humans. Don't translate in-vitro IC₅₀ into a comparative consumer claim. [7]
- **The senescence paradox.** Low-dose resveratrol suppresses SASP [14], but **high-dose resveratrol can INDUCE premature senescence and DNA damage** (down-regulating SIRT1/SIRT2) in some cell studies — a reminder that "more is better" is wrong and dose matters.
- **Penetration silence.** Most marketing ignores that resveratrol **mostly sits in the upper stratum corneum** and can be **locally metabolized** — i.e., it's a surface antioxidant more than a dermal remodeler. [11][17]
- **Emodin omission.** Marketing never mentions the **anthraquinone (emodin) genotoxicity** baggage of whole knotweed extract. [22][23]

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL — excellent antioxidant + brightening + narrative asset; NOT a proof-backed topical anti-wrinkle hero.**

**One-line:** Japanese knotweed gives us the cheapest, richest natural *trans*-resveratrol and a phenomenal longevity/antioxidant story, but the honest topical evidence supports **antioxidant defense and tone/brightening**, not wrinkle reduction — so use it as a **science-forward supporting active** with tone/antioxidant-framed copy, and only if we use a **purified, low-emodin, stabilized (encapsulated or analog) grade**.

**Conditions to make it a credible inclusion:**
1. **Use a purified or fermentation/synthetic grade** with **COA-quantified *trans*-resveratrol and emodin** (avoid cheap whole-extract emodin carryover).
2. **Stabilize it:** acidic/neutral pH, opaque airless packaging, and ideally **encapsulation** (or an ester analog like RTA) to beat photoisomerization/oxidation.
3. **Claim it honestly:** lead with **antioxidant/environmental-defense and tone/brightening**; avoid topical wrinkle-reduction and "boosts collagen / activates longevity genes" language unless we run **our own finished-formula human testing**.

**Longevity-relevance note:** For a skin-*longevity* brand, resveratrol is one of the **most recognizable "longevity molecules"** alive in consumer culture — superb for **education and differentiation** alongside spermidine and NAD precursors. The gap, as with spermidine, is the **route/format translation** (oral/animal longevity science → topical human skin) plus **penetration, stability, and the emodin liability** of the cheap source. It earns a place on the bench as a **supporting/narrative antioxidant**, not a hero we hang anti-wrinkle proof on.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19 (independent re-check of the load-bearing citations against primary sources).

### Overall grade: **C → C+ (weak/indirect for the topical anti-aging claim; honest about it)**

The honest *spine* of this dossier is **correct and well-sourced**: the headline verdict — "rich oral/animal/in-vitro mechanism, but plain **topical** resveratrol's only reliable human win is **pigmentation/antioxidant**, NOT wrinkles" — is accurately grounded in the one rigorous RCT and is appropriately skeptical. It grades C rather than D because the skeptical conclusion is right and the disqualifying negatives (topical wrinkle failure, blend confounding, penetration/stability, emodin) are real and verified. It does **not** rate B because there is **no** strong human-topical RCT supporting an anti-aging (wrinkle/collagen) benefit for the actual ingredient; the supportive topical data are pigmentation-only, blend-confounded, or from analogs.

**Evidence-type reality check (route/species):**
- **Anti-aging / anti-wrinkle / collagen / SIRT1 / MMP / senescence claims:** evidence is **in-vitro + animal + oral**, NOT human-topical. The single human-topical RCT (Tier 4) **failed** the wrinkle endpoint for topical-only. So every "longevity/collagen/SIRT1" claim is **borrowed**, not demonstrated on applied human skin. The dossier already says this; the grading just makes it explicit.
- **Pigmentation/brightening (topical):** this is the **best-supported topical use** — real human signal from the 2025 RCT (plain resveratrol topical, p<0.001) and from RTA/RTG analog human studies. Defensible at roughly **B− for tone/brightening only**.
- **Antioxidant/environmental-defense (topical, upper-SC):** mechanistically sound and route-appropriate (it stays in the stratum corneum and retains antiradical activity) — defensible as a *function* claim, not a clinical outcome.

**Citations checked against primary sources:**
- **[1] Rao/Briskey 2025 RCT (Frontiers in Aging, PMC12757695) — VERIFIED, accurately represented.** Topical-only (P/A) wrinkles −7.8% vs placebo −6.3%, **not significant**; oral-only (A/P) −9.6%, **not significant**; only **combined oral+topical (A/A) −11.9% vs −6.3%, p<0.05**. Pigmentation ↓ for topical arms (p<0.001); U-zone sebum **+88.7%**; no change in moisture/elasticity/pore/skin-age; **collagen not assessed**; funded by **Evolva AG**, authors employed by **Lallemand** (acquired Evolva). Dose = 75 mg capsule **twice daily (150 mg/day)** + 1.5% cream — consistent with the dossier. This citation is the dossier's strongest asset and is sound.
- **[3] Ryu 2015 RTA — WAS MISREPRESENTED; now corrected.** The real paper ("A study of the human skin-whitening effects of resveratryl triacetate," DOI 10.1007/s00403-015-1556-0) measured **skin-whitening/depigmentation only** (ITA° in a tanning model; hyperpigmented-spot intensity) **plus an irritation endpoint** (0.5% resveratrol weakly irritating; RTA not). It did **NOT** measure wrinkles, wrinkle area/volume, sagging, or elasticity. The dossier had (a) an invented "...and anti-aging effects" title, (b) **fabricated/unsupported anti-wrinkle & elasticity numbers** ("↓wrinkle area ~5%, volume ~8–10%, ↓sagging, ↑elasticity ~3–4%"), and (c) a **wrong PMID (25690405 = an unrelated neuropathy paper)**. All three fixed; the genuine whitening + tolerability findings retained.
- **[5] Wu/Wang/Liu 2015 (PMID 26505388) — REAL but MIS-CONTEXTUALIZED; now flagged.** It is an **intervertebral-disc (spinal disc) cell** study, not a skin/dermal-fibroblast study. The SIRT1→↓MMP-1 finding is genuine but borrowed from non-skin tissue; annotated as suggestive-only.
- **[15] Walle 2004 oral PK — VERIFIED.** ~70% absorbed, <5 ng/mL unchanged resveratrol, rapid sulfate/glucuronide conjugation. Accurate.
- **[16] Farris 2014 blend — VERIFIED.** 1% resveratrol + 0.5% baicalin + 1% vitamin E; +18.9% periorbital dermal thickness; the dossier's "blend / uncontrolled, can't attribute to resveratrol" caveat is the correct read.
- **[4] Ohguchi and [12] Kim** were **already self-flagged "partially unverified / cited via review"** in the dossier — honest, and unchanged here.

**Industry-funding / size flags:** The pivotal RCT [1] is **industry-funded (Evolva/Lallemand)** and n=122 completers across 4 arms (~30/arm) — small per-arm. The analog studies [3][8] are **vendor-driven** (Amorepacific lineage) and small (n≈21–33). The strongest *positive* topical anti-aging-adjacent data therefore come from interested parties; the dossier already discloses this for [1], and it is now disclosed for the analogs too.

**Main caveats:**
1. The anti-aging case rests on **non-topical or non-skin evidence**; the only clean human-topical anti-aging test **failed** for the actual ingredient.
2. The cleanest *topical* wins are **pigmentation/tone**, and even those carry **industry/vendor funding** and small samples.
3. Whole-knotweed **emodin/anthraquinone** genotoxicity liability is real (EFSA, mammalian-cell genotox) — purified/fermentation grade strongly preferred.

**Claims softened in this revision:** removed fabricated RTA anti-wrinkle/elasticity figures; reframed the "analog switch" so RTA/RTG are credited only with **whitening + tolerability** human data (not wrinkles); flagged the [5] SIRT1/MMP-1 finding as non-skin; corrected [3]'s title and PMID. No change was needed to the dossier's core thesis, which survives adversarial review intact.

**Net:** Trustworthy verdict and trustworthy negatives; one materially overstated supporting citation (RTA anti-wrinkle data) has been corrected. Treat resveratrol/knotweed as a **C-grade ingredient for topical anti-aging** (unproven on applied human skin) but a defensible **B−** for **tone/brightening** and a sound **antioxidant-narrative** asset — exactly as the verdict already positions it, now with the citations cleaned up.
