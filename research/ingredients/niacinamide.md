# Ingredient: Niacinamide (Vitamin B3) (INCI: Niacinamide)

**Researched:** 2026-06-18 · **Status:** ✅ shortlisted (strong SUPPORTING / near-hero)

## What it is
Niacinamide is the amide form of vitamin B3 — a water-soluble, exceptionally well-tolerated multitasker. It supports the skin barrier (drives ceramide/lipid production), helps even tone, regulates oil, and provides antioxidant support. It is one of the few cosmetic actives with broad clinical backing *and* a near-universal tolerability profile.

## What it does (claimed → evidenced)
| Benefit | Evidence strength | Source(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Reduces appearance of hyperpigmented spots / evens tone | **Strong** — multiple controlled split-face studies (5%) | [1](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1467-2494.2004.00228.x), [2](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8389214/) |
| Supports skin barrier / increases ceramide & lipid synthesis, lowers TEWL | **Strong** — mechanistic + clinical | [2](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8389214/) |
| Improves appearance of fine lines/wrinkles | **Moderate** — significant vs vehicle in 12-wk studies, modest effect | [1](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1467-2494.2004.00228.x) |
| Reduces sallowness (yellowing) & red blotchiness | **Moderate–strong** | [1](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1467-2494.2004.00228.x), [3](https://www.jaad.org/article/S0190-9622(04)02915-9/fulltext) |
| Antioxidant / supports skin resilience | **Moderate** | [2](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8389214/) |

## How it fits the brand
**Excellent fit.** Niacinamide is the connective tissue of the whole "barrier repair / renew" story — it literally helps the skin rebuild its own lipid barrier. It is gentle, plays well with almost everything, and lets us layer benefits (tone, barrier, resilience) without irritation. Ideal as the **workhorse SUPPORTING active across multiple SKUs**, and arguably a hero for a "barrier renewal" product.

## Formulation notes
- **Effective concentration:** ~2–5% is the well-supported sweet spot. **Above ~5–10% adds little and raises irritation/flushing risk** for sensitive skin; "10%+ is better" is largely marketing.
- **pH:** Stable and effective across a broad range (~5–7); not pH-sensitive like vitamin C/AHAs.
- **Stability:** Very stable, water-soluble, easy to formulate.
- **Pairs well with:** Essentially everything — retinoids (buffers irritation), peptides, hyaluronic acid, ceramides, vitamin C (the "they cancel out / cause flushing" myth is overstated in modern formulas).
- **Conflicts:** None significant. (Trace nicotinic-acid impurity in low-quality raw material can cause flushing — use cosmetic-grade purified material.)
- **Sensory:** Lightweight, plays nicely in serums, lotions, creams.

## Concerns & cautions
- **Irritation:** Rare; occasional transient flushing at high % or with impure raw material.
- **Sun sensitivity:** None.
- **Pregnancy:** Considered low-risk topically; standard "consult your doctor" boilerplate.
- **Allergens/sourcing:** Low allergen risk; widely available.

## Claims caution
- ✅ **Safe cosmetic language:** "supports the skin's moisture barrier," "helps visibly even skin tone," "for a smoother, more refined look," "improves the appearance of enlarged-looking pores," "comforts and strengthens-looking skin."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like:** "increases ceramide synthesis" (structure/function — keep to "supports the barrier"), "treats hyperpigmentation/melasma/acne," "repairs DNA." Keep barrier language to *appearance/support*.
