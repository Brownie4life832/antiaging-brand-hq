# Ingredient: LHA / Lipohydroxy Acid (INCI: Capryloyl Salicylic Acid)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering (lean: supporting/conditional) · ⬜ shortlisted · ⬜ rejected · ⬜ in a product

> **One-line honest summary:** LHA is a lipophilic, slow-penetrating salicylic-acid relative that is a genuinely gentle, well-tolerated chemical exfoliant with *modest* human topical data for fine lines, tone and acne — but its anti-aging evidence is thin, single-supplier (L'Oréal-derived), and its formal safety dossier is flagged "insufficient" by the US CIR panel. It is a credible **supporting** exfoliant, not a hero anti-aging active.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common names:** LHA; lipohydroxy acid; β-lipohydroxy acid; C8-lipohydroxy acid (C8-LHA); "2-hydroxy-5-octanoyl benzoic acid."
- **INCI:** **Capryloyl Salicylic Acid**
- **CAS:** 78418-01-6 (also written 2-hydroxy-5-(1-oxooctyl)benzoic acid).
- **Structure (important correction):** Long thought to be the *ester* of salicylic acid + caprylic acid. The CIR Expert Panel (2020) clarified it is actually the **5-capryl ketone of salicylic acid** — an 8-carbon acyl fatty chain attached at the 5-position of the salicylic-acid benzene ring. This makes it chemically distinct from salicylate esters [1].
- **Key physical properties [1]:** MW ~264 Da; white powder; water solubility 29.7 mg/L (20 °C — i.e. poorly water-soluble / lipophilic); **estimated pKa 2.68**; log Pow 0.32 (non-ionized); melting point ~115 °C.
- **Origin / ownership:** Developed by **L'Oréal** (patent lineage from the 1980s, US Pat. 4,767,750, Jacquet/Lévêque et al.) [2,5]. In practice the molecule and most of its science come from L'Oréal-group research (L'Oréal Paris, SkinCeuticals, La Roche-Posay, CeraVe). This is a **single-originator ingredient** — a strategic and sourcing consideration (see §9).
- **Plausible impurity:** the structural isomer **3-capryloyl salicylic acid**, flagged as a "highly plausible contaminant" and the likely true allergen in reported contact-allergy cases [1,11].

---

## 2. Class & mechanism

**Class:** Hydroxy acid / chemical exfoliant. Functionally a lipophilic **beta-hydroxy-acid-like** keratolytic, often grouped with salicylic acid (BHA) but distinct in behaviour.

**Mechanism at the skin level:**
- LHA loosens and dissolves the **corneodesmosomes** (the protein "rivets" between dead surface cells), promoting desquamation of the stratum corneum — it does not dissolve keratin directly [3,4].
- Its fatty C8 chain makes it markedly **lipophilic and slow-penetrating**. In a Franz-chamber study only **~6% of LHA penetrated past the stratum corneum vs ~58% for salicylic acid** in the same vehicle (16 h); on tape-strip analysis **17.1% of LHA was retained in the stratum corneum over 4 days vs 9.7% of salicylic acid** [1,3]. It sits and works in the uppermost layers ("reservoir effect").
- Because it acts slowly and superficially, it is described as producing **cell-by-cell ("corneocyte-by-corneocyte") exfoliation that mimics physiologic desquamation**, rather than the sheet-shedding of salicylic acid — the proposed basis for its better tolerability [3,4].
- A **secondary "renewal signal":** repeated low-grade exfoliation is hypothesised to trigger epidermal renewal (via release of lamellar lipids and/or mild mechanical stimulus). Older L'Oréal in-vivo work reported that 2% LHA increased epidermal thickness and, in some reports, dermal markers (glycosaminoglycans, hyaluronic acid, collagen, elastin) [3]. **These deeper "anti-aging" effects are the weakest-evidenced part of the story** (see §4).

LHA also has reported **antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory and anticomedogenic** properties relevant to acne [5].

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | LHA relevance | Evidence quality (topical human) |
|---|---|---|
| **Photoaging — fine lines/wrinkles** | Yes — superficial resurfacing; modest wrinkle improvement in one small single-blind split-face *peel* study (no vehicle control) | Low (1 study; active-comparator only) |
| **Pigmentation / uneven tone** | Yes — exfoliation accelerates removal of pigmented corneocytes | Low (same single study; no vehicle control) |
| **Skin texture / surface roughness** | Yes — its strongest, most plausible benefit | Moderate |
| **Barrier decline** | Mixed — exfoliants can *challenge* the barrier; LHA is gentler but not a barrier-builder | Weak / not a barrier active |
| **Collagen / ECM loss** | Claimed (dermal thickening) but **borrowed from old in-vivo/mechanistic reports**, not robust human RCTs | Weak / unverified for modern formulas |
| **Cellular senescence, mitochondrial decline, glycation/AGEs, inflammaging** | No direct evidence | None |
| **Microbiome** | Indirect (antimicrobial in acne) | Weak |
| **Hydration / TEWL** | Indirect — reduced scaling/roughness improved "dryness/scaling" scores in one study | Low |

**Bottom line:** LHA's defensible aging targets are **surface texture, tone, and fine lines via exfoliation** — i.e. it addresses *photoaging appearance*, not the deeper biology of aging.

## 4. Evidence review by tier (core of the dossier)

> Read this section as the honest core. Most "anti-aging" marketing for LHA leans on **one small split-face study** plus **older, hard-to-verify L'Oréal in-vivo reports**. Acne/comedolytic evidence is somewhat stronger than anti-aging evidence.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro / ex-vivo
- **Penetration (Franz chamber, human skin):** ~6% LHA vs ~58% salicylic acid penetrated beyond the stratum corneum (16 h) — establishes the slow-penetration mechanism [1,3]. *Strength: solid for mechanism; n not stated.*
- **Phototoxicity (3T3 NRU assay):** PIF values of 4 and 1.7–2.6. Under the *old* (2000 draft) OECD criteria → "non-phototoxic"; under the **current OECD TG 432 criteria the same data classify as "probably phototoxic,"** and the CIR panel agreed more data are needed [1]. *This is a real, unresolved flag.*
- **Genotoxicity:** Ames negative; in-vivo micronucleus and UDS negative; **but clastogenic to Chinese hamster ovary cells *with* metabolic activation** (negative without) [1]. Panel judged overall genotoxic concern low.

### Tier 2 — Animal
- **Acute/short-term dermal (rats):** dermal LD50 >2000 mg/kg; 10-day dermal NOEL local 2%, systemic >5%; 5% caused slight erythema/edema/scabbing [1].
- **Developmental (dermal, pregnant rats):** NOAEL for developmental toxicity 40 mg/kg/day (minor skeletal variant at 100 mg/kg/day judged secondary to maternal skin irritation, *not* a teratogenic signal); oral repro/dev NOEL 100 mg/kg/day — no teratogenicity [1].
- **Sensitization (guinea pig maximization):** **a sensitizer at higher challenge concentrations** (positive in 14/20 at 2% challenge in one test; "limited evidence" in others) but **non-sensitizing at 1%** [1]. Relevant context for the human sensitization picture below.
- **Ocular (rabbit):** classified a **severe eye irritant** undiluted [1] — formulation/eye-area caution.

### Tier 3 — Human, ORAL
- **None.** LHA is a topical cosmetic ingredient; there is no oral-supplement literature. (No benefits may be borrowed from an oral format because none exists.)

### Tier 4 — Human, TOPICAL

**(a) Randomised / controlled split-face — the anchor anti-aging study**
- **Oresajo C, Yatskayer M, Hansenne I. *J Cosmet Dermatol.* 2008;7:259–262** [6]. (The 2006 JAAD conference abstract is the precursor to this paper.)
  - **Design:** single-blinded, randomised, **split-face**; LHA peel vs glycolic-acid peel, opposite sides.
  - **n:** 50 enrolled, **44 completed** (88%); women **35–60 y**, mild–moderate hyperpigmentation + fine lines/wrinkles.
  - **Protocol:** 12 weeks, biweekly peels, escalating **5→10% LHA** vs **20→50% glycolic acid**, by tolerance. Standardised cleanser/toner/moisturiser **+ sunscreen** for all.
  - **Results (% of subjects with statistically significant improvement from baseline at wk 12):**
    - Fine lines/wrinkles: **41% LHA vs 30% GA** (significant from wk 9; P=0.003 LHA, P=0.008 GA).
    - Hyperpigmentation: **46% LHA vs 34% GA** (significant from wk 7 LHA, wk 11 GA; P<0.001 both by wk 12).
    - Dryness/scaling: LHA reduced it better than GA.
    - LHA was as safe/tolerated as GA; 3 subjects dropped for erythema/stinging/burning on the LHA side.
  - **Limitations (be honest):** small; single-blind; **L'Oréal-authored** (the ingredient's owner); active-comparator (no vehicle-only control) so the absolute effect vs *doing nothing* is unquantified; outcomes are clinician **subjective ordinal scores**, not instrumental; this is a **peel protocol**, not a leave-on cream — so it does not validate everyday leave-on formulas at typical 0.5–2% use levels.

**(b) Comparative RCT — enlarged pores (lower power)**
- **LHA 5% cream vs tretinoin 0.05% cream, randomised double-blind split-face** (Thai study, 2024, ResearchGate) [7].
  - **n=20**, ages 25–55, enlarged pores; applied 2×/week at night for 12 weeks; VISIA imaging, Friedman test.
  - Framing: LHA positioned as gentler alternative to retinoid for pores. **Very small (n=20), non-indexed/preprint-status — treat as preliminary, not confirmatory.**

**(c) Acne / comedolytic — better-supported but off-aging-topic**
- **Comedolytic split-face** (Piérard/Rougier, cited in [4]): once-daily LHA for 1 month reduced follicular casts by **47%** and size by **54%** (cyanoacrylate strips; P<0.01 vs untreated side).
- **Uhoda et al. 2003** (n=14): ~**85% reduction in follicular plugs** by day 14 (UV-light image analysis) [4].
- **Bissonnette et al. 2009:** LHA comparable efficacy to benzoyl peroxide with better tolerability [4].
- **Draelos et al. 2012:** multicenter double-blind, **n=66**, mild–moderate acne — an OTC BPO 5.5%-**LHA** combo was as effective as prescription BPO 5%-clindamycin, with **less erythema at wk 2** [4].
- These support LHA as an **acne/keratolytic** agent; they are *not* anti-aging endpoints but reinforce gentleness.

**(d) Older L'Oréal in-vivo "renewal/dermal" reports**
- Cited via the JCAD review [4] and Saint-Léger 2007 [3]: 2% LHA reportedly increased epidermal thickness and (in some reports) dermal GAG/HA/collagen/elastin, with "dermal stimulatory effects equivalent to tretinoin." **These are the most marketing-quoted and the least independently verifiable claims** — older, originator-generated, often without modern controlled designs or accessible full text. **Do not build copy on the "equivalent to tretinoin" line.**

**Net evidence verdict:** Proven for human **topical** use at a modest level = gentle exfoliation, improved texture/tone, fine-line improvement (one small split-face peel study), and acne/comedolytic action (several studies). **Not** robustly proven for human topical use = collagen/dermal remodeling, "anti-aging equivalent to retinoid," senescence/AGE/mitochondrial claims.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Slow, shallow, self-limiting.** ~6% penetrates beyond the stratum corneum vs ~58% for salicylic acid; high stratum-corneum reservoir (17.1% retained over 4 days) [1,3]. This is a *feature*: efficacy at low concentration with low systemic exposure and good tolerability.
- pKa 2.68 means it is largely ionised near skin-surface pH; activity is best at acidic formula pH (see §6).
- Negligible expected systemic absorption at cosmetic use levels; CIR found **no ADME data** submitted [1].

---

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective / typical concentration:**
  - **US market reality (CIR 2018 survey):** max **0.5% leave-on** (moisturisers), up to **0.4% rinse-off**, 0.1% in lip products [1]. Most leave-on serums/creams use roughly **0.1–2%**; SkinCeuticals' professional formulas pair LHA with glycolic + salicylic acid (e.g. LHA Cleanser markets a ~12% *total* hydroxy-acid blend, not 12% LHA).
  - **Peel protocols** used **5–10%** under dermatologist supervision [6].
- **pH:** acidic; target roughly **pH 3.0–4.0** for keratolytic activity (consistent with pKa 2.68). Higher pH neutralises activity.
- **Stability:** stable solid (decomposes ~264 °C before boiling); oil-soluble actives like LHA are generally easy to keep stable in anhydrous or low-water acidic systems.
- **Solubility / vehicle:** lipophilic — formulate in oil phase, esters, or solubilisers; not freely water-soluble.
- **Pairings:** logical with **niacinamide, ceramides, hyaluronic acid, panthenol** (buffer the exfoliation; support barrier). In acne, validated alongside benzoyl peroxide and retinoids [4].
- **Conflicts / cautions:** stacking with retinoids, other AHA/BHA, benzoyl peroxide, or vitamin C raises **cumulative irritation**; eye-area formulation needs care (severe ocular irritant undiluted). Acidic pH may clash with pH-sensitive partners (e.g. some peptides, copper actives).
- **Sensory:** at low % generally elegant and non-stripping; the "gentle acid" reputation is its selling point.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation:** Generally **well tolerated** at cosmetic levels — its raison d'être. Real-world: 5/49 subjects had "cutaneous signs of slight intensity" with a 0.3% face product 2×/day for 4 weeks [1]; peel study had 3/50 drop-outs for erythema/stinging on the LHA side [6]. Higher % (5%) is irritating in animals.
- **Sensitization:** Mixed but reassuring at use levels. **Guinea-pig sensitizer at ≥2% challenge; non-sensitizer at 1%** [1]. Multiple **HRIPTs (human, ~100+ subjects each) at 0.5% and 2% = non-sensitizing** [1]. A few **published contact-allergy case reports** exist (de Groot 2014; González-Pérez 2016 — positive patch tests to 1% capryloyl salicylic acid) [10,11]. **Roberts & Aptula (2015)** argue the parent molecule is unlikely to be the true allergen and that the **3-capryloyl isomer impurity** is the probable culprit [11] — i.e. allergy risk is plausibly a **purity** issue. *Practical takeaway: source high-purity material; sensitization risk at ≤0.5% is low but non-zero.*
- **Sun sensitivity:** As an exfoliant it thins the stratum corneum, so **daily SPF is advisable** (the standard AHA/BHA "increased UV sensitivity" caution applies — the Oresajo protocol mandated sunscreen) [6]. Separately, the **in-vitro 3T3 data would now classify LHA as "probably phototoxic," unresolved** — another reason to pair with sun protection and not overstate safety [1].
- **Pregnancy:** No human data specific to LHA. As a salicylate relative, the general guidance for topical salicylic-acid-type actives applies: **low-concentration leave-on cosmetic use is generally considered low-risk, but avoid high-concentration/large-area/occluded use; defer to the user's clinician.** Animal repro/dev studies showed **no teratogenicity** (developmental NOAEL 40 mg/kg/day dermal; oral repro NOEL 100 mg/kg/day) [1]. Do not make pregnancy-safety claims in copy.
- **Eyes:** severe ocular irritant undiluted [1]; finished low-% eye products tested benign, but formulate/claim carefully.
- **Regulatory safety status (key honesty point):** The **US CIR Expert Panel (2020) concluded the available data are INSUFFICIENT** to determine that capryloyl salicylic acid is safe under intended use, citing missing **impurities** and **phototoxicity** data [1]. This is *not* a finding of harm — it is an "insufficient data" verdict — but it means we cannot honestly call it "CIR-affirmed safe."
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** synthetic (Friedel–Crafts acylation of methyl salicylate) [1]; no notable natural-resource or sustainability flag; impurity control is the main quality lever.

## 8. Regulatory & claims

- **Cosmetic vs drug line:** As an **exfoliant/skin-conditioning agent**, LHA sits squarely on the **cosmetic** side *for appearance claims*. **Acne treatment is a drug claim** in the US — and critically, **LHA is NOT in the FDA OTC acne monograph** (only benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid, and sulfur are) [4]. So we **cannot market an LHA product as an acne treatment** in the US on the strength of LHA; any acne-drug claim must rest on a monograph ingredient.
- **EU:** Capryloyl salicylic acid (CAS 78418-01-6) is listed in **COSING as a skin-conditioning/exfoliating agent**; it is a *different* substance from salicylic acid (CAS 69-72-7) and is **not** itself bound by salicylic acid's Annex III/V concentration limits. However, SCCS scrutiny of the broader salicylate family is increasing (2023 SCCS opinions tightened salicylic-acid limits, especially for children) [12] — watch this space.
- **Allowed cosmetic language (safer):** "gentle exfoliation," "helps refine skin texture/the appearance of pores," "visibly smooths," "helps even the look of skin tone," "promotes a fresher, renewed-looking surface," "helps improve the appearance of fine lines." Always "the appearance of."
- **Risky / would need substantiation or legal review:** "treats acne," "anti-acne," "boosts collagen," "rebuilds the dermis," "as effective as retinol/tretinoin," "anti-aging" as a stand-alone efficacy promise, any structure/function or disease claim. The "dermal stimulation equivalent to tretinoin" line is **not** defensible — do not use it.
- See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md) claim cheat-sheet and the guardrail in [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier:** moderate (a specialty cosmetic active, more than commodity glycolic/salicylic acid, far less than peptides/growth factors). Used at very low % (≤0.5–2%), so **cost-in-formula is low**.
- **Supply reality:** This is the strategic catch — LHA is **L'Oréal-originated** and most prominently used across L'Oréal-group brands (SkinCeuticals, La Roche-Posay, CeraVe, L'Oréal Paris). Independent cosmetic-raw-material availability of "capryloyl salicylic acid" exists but is narrower than for mainstream acids; verify suppliers and **purity (3-capryloyl isomer content)** before committing. For a small brand, a more open, multi-supplier exfoliant (salicylic, mandelic, lactic, gluconolactone/PHA) may de-risk supply.
- **Quality lever:** specify low isomer-impurity grade given the contact-allergy mechanism [11].

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

| Marketing claim seen in the wild | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Gentler than salicylic acid / for sensitive skin" | **Largely fair** — slow shallow penetration, cell-by-cell exfoliation, strong tolerability data [3,4]. The most honest selling point. |
| "Anti-aging — smooths wrinkles, fades spots" | **Modest, single small split-face peel study (n=44)**, L'Oréal-authored, no vehicle control [6]. Real but oversold. |
| "Boosts collagen / rebuilds the dermis / equivalent to retinol" | **Weak/unverified** — leans on old originator in-vivo reports; not robust modern human RCTs [3,4]. Avoid. |
| "Anti-acne treatment" | **Cannot claim as a drug in US** — LHA isn't in the FDA acne monograph [4], even though comedolytic data are decent. |
| "Safe / dermatologist-tested" | Tolerability is good, **but CIR (2020) ruled the safety data 'insufficient'** and current OECD criteria flag it 'probably phototoxic' [1]. "Well tolerated in studies" is fair; "proven safe" is not. |
| "Natural" | No — fully synthetic [1]. |

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL — not a hero.**

One-line: *LHA is a credible, gentle "renew/resurface" exfoliant that fits a barrier-respecting routine, but its anti-aging evidence is thin and originator-bound — use it as a supporting texture/tone/clarity active (or skip in favour of a multi-supplier acid), never as the marquee anti-aging claim.*

- **If used:** position as the **gentle exfoliation / smoothing / pore-refining** step that *complements* the barrier-repair thesis (pair with ceramides, niacinamide, HA). Keep ≤0.5% leave-on, acidic pH, high-purity grade, daily-SPF messaging.
- **Conditionality:** the **single-supplier (L'Oréal) origin + "insufficient" CIR safety verdict + thin anti-aging RCT base** are real reasons a from-scratch brand might prefer a better-evidenced, more openly-sourced exfoliant (mandelic for tone, PHA/gluconolactone for sensitive barrier-friendly exfoliation, encapsulated salicylic for oily/clog-prone). Decide on supply diligence.
- **Longevity-relevance note:** LHA targets the *appearance* of photoaging (surface texture, tone, fine lines) through exfoliation/renewal. It does **not** act on the deeper biological hallmarks of aging (senescence, mitochondria, glycation, ECM remodeling) with any credible evidence — so it is a "look-renewed" cosmetic tool, not a "skin-longevity" mechanism active.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Independent adversarial re-check of every headline efficacy/safety claim against the primary sources. The dossier survives this audit well — it is unusually honest, no fabricated citations were found, and the load-bearing numbers match the primary text. The corrections below are refinements, not reversals.

### Overall evidence grade: **C (weak / indirect for anti-aging)**

- **For its *defensible* job — gentle topical exfoliation / acne-keratolysis — the evidence would be ~B** (multiple human topical studies; the comedolytic data are the strongest part of the file).
- **For the "anti-aging" headline the dossier is actually grading — fine lines, tone, "renewal" — the evidence is C:** it rests on a **single small (n=44 completers), single-blind, L'Oréal-authored, active-comparator split-face *peel* study with no vehicle/placebo control**, plus older originator in-vivo reports. That is weak/indirect human-topical evidence, not the strong RCT base an "A" or "B" anti-aging claim needs. The deeper "dermal/collagen/equivalent-to-tretinoin" tier is **D (essentially unproven topically / hype)**.
- The dossier's own headline verdict ("SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL — not a hero," anti-aging evidence "thin") is consistent with grade C. **No upgrade is warranted.**

### What was checked and confirmed against primary sources

- **Identity / mechanism (CIR ref [1]):** "5-capryl ketone of salicylic acid, *not* an ester"; MW 264; 8-carbon acyl at the 5-position; Friedel–Crafts synthesis — **confirmed verbatim** against the CIR report and SAGE-published version (*Int J Toxicol.* 2024). The "important correction" framing is accurate.
- **Penetration (6% LHA vs 58% SA past stratum corneum; 17.1% vs 9.7% SC retention):** **confirmed** in both CIR [1] and Zeichner [4]. This is the best-supported mechanistic claim and is real (note: in-vitro Franz-chamber + tape-strip, n not stated — mechanism evidence, not efficacy).
- **Anchor anti-aging study (Oresajo 2008 [6]):** read in full. **Every number confirmed verbatim** — n=50 enrolled / 44 completed (88%); women 35–60 y; 5–10% LHA vs 20–50% GA; 12 wk, biweekly; single-blind; 41% vs 30% fine lines (P=0.003 LHA, P=0.008 GA, sig. from wk 9); 46% vs 34% pigment (sig. from wk 7 LHA, wk 11 GA; P<0.001 both by wk 12); 3 LHA-side dropouts; sunscreen mandated; **no vehicle/no-treatment arm.** All three authors are **L'Oréal Research employees (Clark, NJ)** — i.e. the study is by the ingredient's owner, exactly as flagged.
- **CIR safety conclusion = "insufficient data"** and **phototoxicity "probably phototoxic" under current OECD TG 432:** **confirmed verbatim.** These are correctly represented (an *insufficient-data* verdict, not a finding of harm).
- **Comedolytic studies (Uhoda n=14 ~85%; Piérard 47% casts/54% size, P<0.01; Bissonnette vs BPO; Draelos n=66 BPO-LHA vs BPO-clindamycin, less wk-2 erythema) and "LHA not in the FDA OTC acne monograph":** **confirmed** as reported in Zeichner [4].
- **Contact-allergy / impurity story (de Groot 2014 [10]; Roberts & Aptula 2015 [11]):** both papers **confirmed real on PubMed**; Roberts & Aptula do argue the **3-capryloyl isomer impurity** (not the parent molecule) is the probable allergen — represented accurately.

### Main caveats (the honest bottom line)

1. **The evidence base is almost entirely originator-generated.** This is bigger than the dossier's "single-supplier sourcing" framing. The anti-aging study [6] is by L'Oréal employees; the mechanism review [3] is L'Oréal-affiliated; the patent [2] is L'Oréal; and the narrative review [4] the dossier leans on for penetration, comedolytic data and the monograph point was written by a **disclosed paid L'Oréal consultant/advisory-board member** (now flagged in ref [4]). There is **no independent, non-L'Oréal RCT** supporting the anti-aging claims. Treat the whole anti-aging narrative as manufacturer-derived.
2. **No vehicle-controlled evidence.** The one anti-aging study is active-comparator only (vs glycolic acid), so the absolute effect of LHA *vs doing nothing* is unquantified. "41% improved" is % of subjects showing clinician-scored improvement, not an instrumented effect size.
3. **It's a peel study, not a leave-on cream study.** Efficacy was shown at 5–10% supervised peels; real products use ~0.5% leave-on. The everyday-use efficacy is an extrapolation.
4. **Outcomes are subjective clinician ordinal scores**, single-blind, not instrumental (no profilometry/colorimetry) — a real internal-validity limit.
5. **"Anti-aging" here means appearance of photoaging only.** No credible evidence on senescence, mitochondria, glycation, ECM remodeling — correctly stated as "None" in §3.
6. **Safety is "well tolerated," not "proven safe":** CIR insufficient-data verdict + unresolved phototoxicity flag + purity-dependent sensitization risk. Correctly represented.
7. **No human ORAL data exists (correctly — none is borrowed); animal data are used only for safety, not efficacy** — appropriate. No efficacy was found to be quietly borrowed from in-vitro/animal/oral sources.
