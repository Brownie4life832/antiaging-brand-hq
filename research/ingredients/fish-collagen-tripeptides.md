# Ingredient: Fish Collagen Tripeptides (oral-adjacent) (INCI: Hydrolyzed Collagen / Hydrolyzed Fish Collagen / Pisces Collagen)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Anti-glycation & related (collagen-derived signaling peptides; "oral-adjacent" — most evidence is for ingestion, not topical)

> **One-line honest summary:** Fish collagen tripeptides (CTPs) — chiefly **Gly-Pro-Hyp (GPH)** and its breakdown products **Pro-Hyp (PH)** and **Hyp-Gly** — are the real, absorbable "active fraction" of hydrolyzed marine collagen, and there is a body of **oral** human RCT evidence (~1 g/day, 12 wk) reporting improved skin hydration, elasticity and wrinkle scores — though, as below, that evidence is **bias-fragile and largely industry-authored**. The catch for a skincare brand is threefold: (1) almost all the good evidence is **oral**, not topical; (2) the **best meta-analysis to date (Myung & Park 2025) found the skin benefit largely *vanishes* once you restrict to high-quality, non-industry-funded trials** — and nearly every positive CTP trial is run by the ingredient maker (Amicogen, Newtree); (3) the **one** real human topical CTP study is a tiny (n=22), single-arm, manufacturer-funded 4-week pilot that itself measured only **~6.7% skin permeation** of the GPH tripeptide. The anti-glycation hook is legitimate at the mechanism level (CTPs scavenge radicals, inhibit AGE formation, and that one pilot showed a small drop in skin AGE autofluorescence), but it is thinly evidenced topically. **Treat fish CTP as a "supporting / story" hydration-and-comfort active with a soft anti-glycation narrative — not an evidence-backed topical hero — and never silently import the oral-supplement data into topical claims.**

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Fish (marine) collagen tripeptide(s); "collagen tripeptide" (CTP); "low-molecular-weight collagen peptide" (LMWCP); marine collagen peptides (MCP). Branded oral/ingredient forms include **Collagen-Tripep20™ / Tripep20** (Amicogen), **EverCTP™** (Newtree), and various "marine collagen" actives.
- **What it physically is:** Type I collagen extracted from **fish skin or scales** (commonly Nile tilapia *Oreochromis niloticus*, pangasius *Pangasius hypophthalmus*, cod, pollock, snapper), then cut down by **enzymatic hydrolysis** — frequently a dedicated **collagenase** step — into very short peptides. A true "tripeptide" grade is enriched for **3-amino-acid fragments with the repeating collagen motif Gly-X-Y**, the hero being **Gly-Pro-Hyp (glycyl-prolyl-hydroxyproline, "GPH")**. Commodity "hydrolyzed collagen / collagen peptides" (1,000–10,000 Da) are mostly larger; "tripeptide" grades advertise a high % of <500 Da material. [1][3][6]
- **INCI:** **Hydrolyzed Collagen** (the umbrella INCI; CosIng skin-conditioning/humectant). Marine-specific labels also use **Hydrolyzed Fish Collagen**, **Pisces Collagen**, **Soluble Collagen**, or species-named marketing terms. There is **no separate INCI for "tripeptide"** — on a label, a tripeptide grade and a generic hydrolysate look identical ("Hydrolyzed Collagen"), so any "tripeptide" distinction is a *supplier spec*, not a regulatory one. [confirm exact CosIng entry before EU labeling — function listed as skin conditioning/humectant]
- **Key molecular players (the part that actually matters):**
  - **Gly-Pro-Hyp (GPH)** — MW ≈ **285 Da** — the signature collagen tripeptide; small enough to be *theoretically* below the 500-Da skin-penetration ceiling (see §5).
  - **Pro-Hyp (PH)** — dipeptide, MW ≈ **228 Da** — the dominant Hyp-containing peptide that actually circulates in blood after ingestion; the best-studied *bioactive*. [9][10][11]
  - **Hyp-Gly** — dipeptide, MW ≈ **188 Da** — chemotactic on fibroblasts. [10][12]
  - **Gly-3Hyp-4Hyp** — a 3-hydroxyproline-containing tripeptide identified in **human blood** that is *extraordinarily* protease-resistant (stays at peak ~4 h) and chemotactic on skin fibroblasts. [13]
- **Forms used:** oral powders/liquids/capsules (the main format); cosmetic water-phase additive (serum/ampoule/cream); rarely as an encapsulated/"penetration-enhanced" topical.

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it could work)

Collagen tripeptide is **not a structural ingredient** — applied or eaten, it does **not** "become" your collagen. Its plausible action is as a **low-MW signaling fragment** that fibroblasts read as a "remodeling cue." Three mechanistic layers:

1. **Fibroblast signaling (the headline mechanism — but established mostly via the *oral/blood* route).** After ingestion, GPH is absorbed *intact* across the gut via the **PEPT1** peptide transporter, appears in plasma within ~30–60 min, and is partly hydrolyzed to **Pro-Hyp / Hyp-Gly**, which circulate and reach skin. [3][11] In cell culture these fragments are **chemotactic** (draw fibroblasts in) and **growth-initiating**: Pro-Hyp (200 nmol/mL) drives dose-dependent fibroblast proliferation and migration; Pro-Hyp selectively activates **p75NTR-positive** wound-associated fibroblasts via peptide-transporter uptake → integrin-β1 signaling. [9][12] Pro-Hyp also raised fibroblast proliferation ~1.5× and **hyaluronic-acid synthesis ~3.8×** (200 nmol/mL) in human dermal fibroblasts. [8] Vendor/mechanistic write-ups extend this to TGF-β/Smad and MAPK/ERK activation, ↑type-I collagen and elastin, and ↓MMP-1 — but those specific pathway claims are **less independently nailed down** and partly vendor-sourced. [6]
2. **Anti-glycation / antioxidant (the reason this sits in the "anti-glycation & related" class).** Fish collagen *hydrolysates* show **free-radical scavenging** (DPPH/ABTS; small fractions strongest — e.g. ~87% DPPH at 1 mg/mL for the smallest fraction) and **direct inhibition of glycation / AGE formation** in vitro (mackerel-scad hydrolysate gave 50% glycation inhibition at ~68 ppm vs ~239 ppm for intact collagen). Specific fish peptides (e.g. Asp-Asp-Asp) bind **RAGE** (the AGE receptor) and tyrosinase. [14][15] The logic: by mopping up reactive carbonyls/radicals and engaging RAGE, CTP could *blunt* the glycation cascade that stiffens and yellows aging collagen. **This is mechanistically real but largely in-vitro/biochemical — it is not the same as proven anti-glycation benefit in human skin.**
3. **Humectancy / film (the boring-but-real topical layer).** Like any hydrolyzed protein, CTP is **hydrophilic and water-binding**; on skin it behaves as a lightweight humectant/film-former that can modestly improve immediate hydration and smoothness regardless of any "signaling." This is the part most defensible for a *topical* claim.

**Honest translation:** the signaling biology is genuinely interesting, but it was worked out for **molecules delivered through the bloodstream after eating collagen**. Whether a topically applied tripeptide reaches living fibroblasts in active form and at meaningful dose is the open question (see §5).

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** Fish collagen tripeptide's strongest, most rigorous human literature is **oral supplementation**, and even *that* literature is heavily clouded by **industry funding** — the headline positive CTP trials are run by the ingredient makers (Amicogen, Newtree), and the most careful recent meta-analysis found the skin benefit **disappears in high-quality, non-funded studies**. For **topical** use there is **one** small, single-arm, manufacturer-funded human pilot. A "topical fish collagen tripeptide serum" therefore borrows almost all of its glamour from the *oral* and *in-vitro* worlds.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell / biochemical) — strong on *mechanism*, blind to *delivery*

- **Shigemura et al., 2009, *J. Agric. Food Chem.* 57(2):444–449 (doi:10.1021/jf802785h).** Pro-Hyp, the food-derived collagen dipeptide that appears in human blood, **increased migration of mouse-skin fibroblasts onto plates (200 nmol/mL, 72 h)** and **dose-dependently enhanced their growth on collagen gel (0–1000 nmol/mL).** Foundational evidence that circulating CTP fragments act on fibroblasts. *Limitation: mouse cells, in vitro, the dipeptide not the tripeptide, blood-route context.* [9]
- **Ohara/Shigemura et al. (cited), Pro-Hyp ↑ HA synthesis.** Pro-Hyp (200 nmol/mL) raised human dermal fibroblast proliferation ~1.5× and **hyaluronic-acid synthesis ~3.8×**, with a related glycopeptide (Pro-Hyp-CONH-GlcN) upregulating hyaluronan synthase-2/-3. *In vitro, dipeptide/glycopeptide.* [8]
- **Sato, Asai & Jimi, 2020, *Front. Cell Dev. Biol.* 8:548975 (doi:10.3389/fcell.2020.548975).** Pro-Hyp (200 µM) **selectively triggered proliferation of p75NTR-positive (wound-associated) fibroblasts** via peptide-transporter uptake → integrin-β1; FITC-Pro-Hyp accumulated only inside p75NTR+ cells. Frames Pro-Hyp as a "growth-initiating factor." *Limitation: in vitro, wound (not aging) context, dipeptide.* [12]
- **Sontakke, Jung, Piao & Chung, 2016, *J. Agric. Food Chem.* 64(38):7127–7133 (doi:10.1021/acs.jafc.6b02955).** Fish-scale collagen tripeptide (CTP): **GPH and Pro-Hyp were stable in GI fluid and rat plasma for 2 h**, GPH crossed an intestinal-cell monolayer, and CTP gave higher GPH/PH plasma levels than high-MW collagen — the absorption rationale for tripeptide grades. *Limitation: rat + cell model (relevant to the **oral** route).* [3]
- **Tilapia-skin collagen peptides, zebrafish + in-vitro anti-aging mechanism, 2026, *Food Bioscience/ScienceDirect* (S2212429226001355).** Fish CTPs mitigated aging via **antioxidant, antiglycation and tyrosinase-inhibition** mechanisms; peptides (e.g. Asp-Asp-Asp) bound RAGE and tyrosinase. *Model organism / docking — mechanistic, not human skin.* [14]
- **Antiglycation / antioxidant hydrolysate work** (mackerel scad *Decapterus macarellus*; redlip croaker; carp byproduct). Fish-skin collagen hydrolysates inhibit glycation (50% at ~68 ppm) and scavenge DPPH/ABTS (smallest fraction strongest, ~87% at 1 mg/mL). *Biochemical, not skin.* [15]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)

- **Biological safety of tilapia collagen — Yamamoto et al., 2014, *BioMed Research International* 2014:630757 (doi:10.1155/2014/630757).** ISO-standard biocompatibility battery: **no cytotoxicity** (V79 colony formation 93.9% vs 96.8% control), **no skin sensitization** (guinea-pig maximization, 0/10 reactions), **no intracutaneous irritation** (rabbit, difference score −0.24), **no genotoxicity**, non-pyrogenic, non-hemolytic, no acute systemic toxicity. Concludes tilapia atelocollagen is safe as a biomaterial. *This is a **safety** study (great for §7), not an efficacy/aging study.* [16]
- *General note:* numerous rodent wound/photoaging models with fish collagen hydrolysate exist but are wound/UV-damage contexts and feed-route; they reinforce the fibroblast/wound-healing mechanism rather than intact-skin aging.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL (the deepest tier — and the source of the marketing) 

The center of gravity. Repeating motif: ~**1 g/day fish CTP, 12 weeks**, modest but statistically significant gains — and **manufacturer authorship**.

- **Kim, Chung, Choi, Sakai & Lee, 2018, *Nutrients* 10(7):826 (doi:10.3390/nu10070826).** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; **n=64**; **fish** low-MW collagen peptide (LMWCP; tripeptide content >15%, **3% GPH**), **1,000 mg/day, 12 wk.** Skin **hydration** ↑ vs placebo at 6 & 12 wk; **3 wrinkle parameters + visual score** improved at 12 wk; **2/3 elasticity parameters** improved. No adverse events. *Limitations: n=64, 12 wk, cosmetic-grade endpoints; manufacturer (Newtree) involvement; effect sizes modest.* [1]
- **Lee, Yoon, Kim, Kim, Kang & Shin, 2024, *Nutr. Res. Pract.* 18(3):357–371 (doi:10.4162/nrp.2024.18.3.357).** RDBPC; enrolled **85 / completed 78**; **freshwater pangasius** liquid collagen **Tripep20™** (20% tripeptides, 3.2% GPH), **1,000 mg/day, 12 wk.** **Hydration ↑** from wk 6 (P<0.001); **roughness Ra/Rq/Rmax ↓** at 12 wk; **elasticity R2/R5/R7 ↑** from wk 6; **crow's-feet score ↓** at 12 wk (P=0.004 vs placebo). No AEs. *Limitations: **all key authors employed by Amicogen Inc.**, company funded & designed; cosmetic endpoints; liquid format.* [2]
- **Tak et al. (Amicogen-linked), 2021, *Front. Med.* 7:608903 (doi:10.3389/fmed.2020.608903).** RDBPC; **84 enrolled / ~74 completed**; **Nile tilapia** CTP (avg MW **~500 Da**, 20% CTP, 3.2% GPH), **1,000 mg/day, 12 wk**, women 40–60. Primary win: **TEWL reduced** more in CTP group at 12 wk (P<0.05, robust to climate adjustment); general hydration improved but **not** significantly between groups; elasticity/wrinkle changes minimal. *Limitations: single-center, women-only, modest effects, capsule format; **3 authors employed by Amicogen.*** [5]
- **Choi, Ko, Lee & Lee, 2014, *J. Cosmet. Laser Ther.* 16(3):132–137 (doi:10.3109/14764172.2013.854119; PMID 24131075).** Randomized controlled; **n=32**, four arms (no supp / CP 3 g / CP 3 g + vit C 500 mg / vit C), 12 wk. **Hydration** and **elasticity** improved significantly vs control in CP arms; low-dose vit C added no benefit. *Limitations: very small per arm, generic "collagen peptide" (not specified fish-tripeptide), short.* [17]
- **Meta-analysis — Myung & Park, 2025, *Am. J. Med.* (doi:10.1016/j.amjmed.2025.04.003; S0002-9343(25)00283-9).** Systematic review + meta-analysis of **23 RCTs, ~1,474 participants**, GRADE-graded. Pooled across **all** studies: collagen supplements improved hydration, elasticity and wrinkles. **But in the subgroup of high-quality studies and studies *not* funded by industry, there was *no significant effect* on any skin-aging outcome** (only low-quality studies showed elasticity gains). Authors' conclusion: current evidence does **not** robustly support a benefit once bias is controlled. Industry groups (BioCell, CSA, GROW) rebutted (heterogeneous pooling, alleged funding-classification errors, other meta-analyses positive at 2.5–10 g/day) — note these rebuttals are themselves conflicted. *This is the most important single citation for honest framing.* [18]
- **Bioavailability anchors (human):**
  - **Virgilio, Schön, Mödinger et al., 2024, *Front. Nutr.* 11:1416643 (doi:10.3389/fnut.2024.1416643).** RDB crossover, **n=6**, single 10 g doses. Free Hyp ΔCmax 6–10× baseline at ~100–130 min; **Pro-Hyp most abundant circulating peptide (~3.8 µg/mL, porcine)**; absorption of **fish vs porcine vs bovine "substantially comparable."** [11]
  - **Gly-3Hyp-4Hyp tripeptide — Taga et al., 2022, *npj Sci. Food* 6:27 (doi:10.1038/s41538-022-00144-4; PMC9166765).** Identified a **3-hydroxyproline-containing tripeptide in human blood** post-ingestion that is **extraordinarily protease-resistant** (held peak ~4 h) and **chemotactic on skin fibroblasts** — strongest evidence that *some* collagen tripeptides survive intact and stay bioactive in circulation. [13]
  - **Pharmacokinetics — rat (2023/2024, PMID 38051020).** Absolute oral bioavailability **Gly-Pro-Hyp ~4.4%**, **Pro-Hyp ~19.3%** — i.e. GPH is detectable fast but only a small fraction reaches circulation; PH is the more bioavailable workhorse. [19]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters for us — and it is one tiny pilot)

- **Lee, Lee, Jung, Suk, Lee, Kim & Lee, 2022, *Int. J. Mol. Sci.* 23(3):1101 (doi:10.3390/ijms23031101).** *"Effect of a topical collagen tripeptide on antiaging and inhibition of glycation of the skin: A pilot study."* **Single-arm, no placebo**, **n=22** Asian women (30–54), **hydrolyzed fish-skin extract (EverCTP™, >25% tripeptide, 4% GPH)** applied **twice daily as an ampoule, 4 weeks.** Reported (all P<0.001 vs baseline): periorbital roughness Ra **20.77→19.24 µm**; glabellar Ra **21.40→20.21 µm**; skin density **55.66→59.67**; elasticity R2 **0.81→0.83**; **AGE accumulation (autofluorescence) 2.26→2.16 AU**. **Permeation of the GPH tripeptide measured at ~6.74%.** No AEs; 95.5% satisfaction. *Limitations (severe): single-arm/uncontrolled (no placebo, no vehicle control — regression-to-mean and humectant effects uncontrolled), tiny n, only 4 weeks, manufacturer (Newtree)-funded, changes are small in absolute terms, "anti-glycation" inferred from a ~4% drop in autofluorescence.* **This is the *entire* human topical evidence base — and it is the kind of study you generate hypotheses with, not the kind you build a hero claim on.** [7]

**Bottom line by tier:** *Proven-ish* for fish CTP → **oral** improvement in skin **hydration/elasticity/wrinkles** at ~1 g/day (but bias-fragile per the 2025 meta-analysis). *Mechanistically real* → fibroblast chemotaxis/proliferation + HA synthesis (in vitro, mostly dipeptide), and anti-glycation/antioxidant activity (biochemical). *For human **topical** anti-aging*: **one small uncontrolled manufacturer-funded 4-week pilot.** Anything beyond "helps hydrate and visibly smooth" is borrowed from the oral/in-vitro literature.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration (make-or-break)

- **The 500-Dalton ceiling (Bos & Meinardi, 2000).** Intact stratum corneum passively admits mainly molecules **<~500 Da**. Generic hydrolyzed collagen (1,000–10,000 Da) is **above** the ceiling and stays on the surface; native collagen (~300 kDa) certainly does. [20]
- **Where tripeptides are *different*:** GPH (~285 Da), Pro-Hyp (~228 Da), Hyp-Gly (~188 Da) are **below** 500 Da, so they are the *only* part of "collagen" with a credible passive-penetration story. That is the genuine scientific reason a "tripeptide" grade is interesting topically.
- **But the one direct measurement is humbling:** the topical pilot measured **~6.7% permeation** of the GPH tripeptide. [7] So even the small fraction crosses only modestly — and a commercial "tripeptide" grade is still a *mixture*, much of it >500 Da and going nowhere.
- **Hydrophilicity works against it:** these peptides are very water-loving; the stratum corneum is lipid-rich, so even sub-500-Da hydrophilic peptides penetrate worse than their size alone predicts. Enhancers (penetration boosters, microneedling, post-procedure/compromised skin) increase delivery but change the safety/claims profile.
- **Stability of action:** even if a little penetrates, skin peptidases can clip it. The most protease-resistant species (e.g. Gly-3Hyp-4Hyp) are the exception, not the rule, and that resistance was characterized in *blood*, not skin. [13]
- **Practical read:** on intact facial skin, expect topical fish CTP to act mostly as a **hydrating, smoothing, film-forming humectant** with a *small* fraction of genuine peptide delivery and an *uncertain* dermal-signaling contribution. The honest active rationale is strongest **orally**; topically it is "humectant-plus-maybe." Do not equate "applied tripeptide" with "active tripeptide at the fibroblast."

## 6. Formulation

- **Concentration:** No validated *topical* efficacy %. The topical pilot used a finished ampoule (>25% tripeptide-grade material in the raw; in-formula % unstated). [7] Hydrolyzed collagen is typically used **1–5%** in the water phase for sensory/humectancy; treat anything higher as marketing rather than dose-response.
- **pH:** Stable and skin-friendly across **~4.5–6.5**. Short peptides are robust to mild pH; avoid extremes that could deamidate/hydrolyze further.
- **Stability:** Hydrolyzed protein is a **microbial nutrient** — demands robust preservation and challenge testing; can yellow/develop fishy odor over time (sourcing & deodorization matter). Generally heat/shelf-stable as a small peptide (more so than whole proteins or growth factors), which is an advantage over biologics like colostrum/growth factors.
- **Pairings (conceptual):**
  - **Humectants/occlusives (glycerin, HA, panthenol, ceramides, squalane):** reinforce the hydration/TEWL/smoothness benefit that the data actually support and the brand's barrier-repair thesis.
  - **Niacinamide:** complementary barrier/tone, well-tolerated, evidence-backed.
  - **Anti-glycation co-actives (carnosine, certain phenolics):** narrative-coherent with the class; lets the "anti-glycation" story rest on more than CTP alone (see related dossiers: `b-vitamin-anti-glycation.md`, `anti-glycation-phenolic-acids.md`, `branded-anti-glycation-actives.md`).
- **Conflicts:** none chemically serious; very high electrolyte/cationic loads can salt out some HA-peptide systems — formulate and stability-test normally.
- **Sensory:** pleasant, water-light, slightly tacky at higher % (protein film); can leave a smoothing "primer" feel — a selling point if dosed sensibly.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation/sensitization:** Low. Tilapia collagen passed ISO sensitization (0/10 guinea pigs) and intracutaneous irritation tests with no reactions. [16] Hydrolyzed collagen has a long, benign cosmetic safety record.
- **Allergen (the real flag):** **Fish allergy.** Hydrolyzed *fish* collagen has caused **ingestion-triggered anaphylaxis**, and case literature implicates **epicutaneous (skin) sensitization** — i.e. repeated topical exposure to fish collagen may *sensitize* a person who then reacts. The culprit appears to be **fish collagen itself, not parvalbumin** (the classic fish allergen), so "parvalbumin-free / not the usual fish allergen" is **not** a safety guarantee. [21] **Implication: must carry a fish-allergen declaration; consider it unsuitable for a "hypoallergenic" positioning; pescatarian/vegan-incompatible.**
- **Sun sensitivity:** None known; no phototoxicity expected.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** No specific topical concern for a hydrolyzed peptide; standard "consult a professional" caution; not a retinoid-type risk.
- **Heavy metals (sourcing-dependent):** Fish can bioaccumulate mercury/lead/arsenic. Collagen is extracted from **skin/scales** (low-fat, and mercury is largely fat/organ-bound), so contamination risk is lower than for whole-fish products — **but not zero**, especially from polluted waters. **Require CoA heavy-metal testing per batch.** [22]
- **Sourcing ethics:** wild vs farmed, antibiotics/hormones in some aquaculture, traceability/MSC certification. [22]

## 8. Regulatory & claims (cosmetic vs drug — per CLAUDE.md)

- **Topical = cosmetic** as long as claims stay on **appearance/feel**. Safe register: *"helps hydrate," "supports a smoother, more supple look," "visibly improves the appearance of fine lines," "supports skin comfort."*
- **Lines that cross into drug/over-claim territory (flag for substantiation/legal):** *"boosts/rebuilds collagen," "increases skin collagen by X%," "reverses glycation," "removes AGEs," "anti-glycation treatment," "replaces lost collagen."* Structure/function claims like "boosts collagen" carry regulatory weight (US FDA, EU). The anti-glycation angle is *especially* risky: the only topical evidence is a ~4% autofluorescence drop in an uncontrolled n=22 pilot — **do not make a quantified or therapeutic anti-glycation claim.** [7]
- **Do NOT import oral data into topical copy.** The hydration/elasticity/wrinkle RCTs are *ingestion* studies; using them to substantiate a *cream* claim is a substantiation failure (and the underlying oral evidence is itself contested per Myung & Park 2025). [18]
- **"Tripeptide" wording:** legitimate as an ingredient-tech descriptor, but on-pack INCI is just "Hydrolyzed Collagen" — keep "tripeptide" claims tied to a supplier spec you can document.
- **Allergen labeling:** fish-derived → declare per market food/cosmetic allergen rules. [21]

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Raw material:** widely available commodity. **Tilapia / pangasius (farm-raised, SE Asia)** is the cheap, abundant base; **cod/pollock/snapper (wild, cold-water)** command a **~2–5× premium** and a cleaner-sourcing story. [22]
- **Cost tier:** **Low–moderate** as a generic hydrolyzed-collagen humectant; **moderate** for a genuine **collagenase-processed, high-%-tripeptide / branded** grade (Tripep20, EverCTP). Branded grades let you cite "the study material," at a markup — but note those studies are the *manufacturers'* own.
- **Supply considerations:** require batch CoAs for **MW distribution / tripeptide %**, **heavy metals**, **microbial**, and **species/traceability**; specify deodorized grade to avoid fishy notes.

## 10. Hype vs evidence

- **Hype:** "Collagen tripeptides rebuild your skin's collagen / topical collagen replaces what you've lost / clinically proven anti-glycation." 
- **Evidence:**
  - Topical collagen of any size does **not** replace dermal collagen; tripeptides at best *signal* and *hydrate*, with **~6.7% measured penetration** of even the small GPH fraction. [7][20]
  - The **best human data are oral**, and the **most rigorous meta-analysis (2025) found the skin benefit collapses in high-quality, non-industry-funded trials** — and essentially every positive fish-CTP trial is **maker-funded** (Amicogen/Newtree). [18][1][2][5]
  - "Anti-glycation" is mechanistically plausible (in-vitro radical/AGE inhibition, RAGE binding) but topically rests on **one uncontrolled pilot** showing a small autofluorescence drop. [7][14][15]
  - The genuinely solid science is **mechanistic and oral-pharmacokinetic**: Pro-Hyp/Hyp-Gly/GPH are real, absorbable, fibroblast-active fragments. [9][11][12][13]
- **Net:** a respectable *ingredient story* with thin *topical proof*; the honest sell is **hydration + smoothing + a soft, non-quantified longevity/anti-glycation narrative**, not collagen "rebuilding."

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ Conditional / Supporting — not a hero.**

- **Why not hero:** the topical evidence is a single small uncontrolled manufacturer-funded pilot; penetration is ~6.7%; and the broader (oral) evidence is bias-fragile (Myung & Park 2025). A premium, science-credible brand cannot honestly headline "collagen tripeptide" as a proven topical anti-ager. Our settled product logic puts the **barrier-repair cream** and evidence-backed actives (ceramides, niacinamide, retinoids, SPF) at the center — CTP is not in that tier.
- **Where it earns a place:** as a **supporting hydration/sensory active** that also carries a **coherent "anti-glycation & related" narrative** alongside stronger anti-glycation co-actives (carnosine, phenolics, B-vitamins). It is cheap-ish, safe (allergen aside), stable, pleasant, and lets the brand talk *honestly* about glycation/longevity science without over-claiming.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Method: independent fetch/search of the primary sources behind every headline claim, checking (a) human vs in-vitro/animal and topical vs oral route, (b) whether each cited study is real and says what the dossier claims, (c) study size and industry funding.

### Overall grade: **D** (for the *topical* anti-aging proposition — the only one that matters for a cream)

The dossier's own framing is honest and the grade reflects the evidence it accurately reports, not a flaw in the write-up:
- **Topical human anti-aging evidence = D (unproven topically / hype-adjacent).** The *entire* human topical base is **one** study: Lee et al. 2022, *Int J Mol Sci* [7] — single-arm, no placebo/vehicle control, n=22, 4 weeks, NEWTREE-funded, with absolute changes that are tiny (e.g. roughness 20.77→19.24 µm; AGE autofluorescence 2.26→2.16 AU) and only ~6.74% GPH permeation. This is hypothesis-generating, not claim-supporting.
- **Oral human evidence = C (real but bias-fragile and off-route).** Multiple RCTs at ~1 g/day × 12 wk report modest gains, but (i) it is the *ingestion* route, irrelevant to a topical claim, and (ii) the most rigorous meta-analysis (Myung & Park 2025 [18]) finds the benefit **vanishes** once restricted to high-quality / non-industry-funded trials — and the positive fish-CTP trials are authored/funded by the ingredient makers (Amicogen, Newtree). If one were grading an *oral/ingestible* product, it would land at **C/B-minus**; topically it does not transfer.
- **Mechanism = real but in-vitro/blood-route.** Fibroblast chemotaxis/proliferation and HA synthesis are genuine but were shown for the *dipeptide* Pro-Hyp delivered via blood, in cultured (often mouse) cells — not topically delivered tripeptide at a living human fibroblast.

### Citation accuracy (spot-checks against primary sources)
All load-bearing citations verified as **real and accurately characterized** — no fabricated or misquoted studies found:
- **[7] Lee 2022 (topical pilot)** — confirmed: single-arm, n=22, 4 wk, NEWTREE-funded; roughness/density/elasticity/AGE figures and 6.74% GPH permeation all match exactly. (PMC8835374)
- **[18] Myung & Park 2025 meta-analysis** — confirmed: 23 RCTs, 1,474 participants; benefit absent in high-quality/non-industry-funded subgroup; authors received **no funding and declared no conflicts** (which strengthens its weight relative to the conflicted positive trials).
- **[2] Lee 2024 (Tripep20, pangasius)** — confirmed: RDBPC, 85 enrolled/78 completed, 1 g/day × 12 wk, hydration/roughness/elasticity/crow's-feet results match; **most authors employed by Amicogen Inc.**, which funded and designed the study.
- **[16] Yamamoto 2014 (tilapia safety)** — confirmed: ISO safety battery (cytotoxicity 93.9%, 0/10 sensitization, intracutaneous −0.24); it is a **safety, not efficacy/anti-aging**, study, as the dossier already states.
- **[9] Shigemura 2009** — confirmed real (PMID 19128041; DOI 10.1021/jf802785h): Pro-Hyp 200 nmol/mL ↑ migration of mouse-skin fibroblasts; 0–1000 nmol/mL dose-dependent growth on collagen gel. In vitro, mouse, dipeptide.
- **[11] Virgilio 2024 (bioavailability)** — confirmed: n=6 crossover, single 10 g doses, Pro-Hyp the most abundant circulating peptide (~3.8 µg/mL porcine), fish/porcine/bovine absorption broadly comparable.

> Note: source **[6]** is self-flagged in the dossier as a non-peer-reviewed vendor write-up (mechanism claims treated as hypotheses), and **[20]/[21]/[22]** are flagged as canonical-but-unverified or secondary/industry — appropriate handling.

### Main caveats
1. **The oral↔topical leap is the central risk.** The dossier's glamour (hydration/elasticity/wrinkle RCTs, bioavailability, fibroblast signaling) is overwhelmingly **oral or in-vitro**. None of it substantiates a topical claim. The dossier already says this repeatedly; the grade simply enforces it.
2. **Industry funding is near-universal in the positive literature.** Every positive fish-CTP human trial cited is maker-funded; the one independent, no-conflict source (Myung & Park) is the *negative* one. Weight accordingly.
3. **"Anti-glycation" topically rests on a single uncontrolled ~4% autofluorescence drop.** Mechanistically plausible in vitro, but not a defensible human-topical claim in any quantified or therapeutic form.

### Claims to keep soft / already-defensible language
- Defensible topical register (unchanged): *"helps hydrate," "supports a smoother, more supple look," "visibly improves the appearance of fine lines."* These rest on humectancy, not on the borrowed data.
- **Softened in this pass:** the one-line summary's "genuinely *decent* human evidence" → now flags the oral evidence as **bias-fragile and largely industry-authored**; the §3 Hydration row now states the strong signal is **oral** (and bias-fragile), with only generic humectancy topically.
- **Do not** quantify collagen "rebuilding," anti-glycation, or AGE removal; **do not** cite oral RCTs for the topical product. The dossier's §8/§10/§11 already enforce this.

### Verdict
The dossier is **accurate, well-cited, and admirably self-critical** — its citations check out and its honesty framing is correct. The **D grade applies to the topical anti-aging proposition** (one tiny uncontrolled maker-funded pilot, ~6.7% penetration), which is exactly how the dossier positions the ingredient: a **supporting hydration/sensory active with a soft, non-quantified anti-glycation narrative — not a topical hero.** No overstated claims required reversal; two phrasings were tightened for defensibility. The ingredient is a materially stronger candidate for a future **ingestible** line (where the evidence, though contested, is at least on-route).
