# Ingredient: Revinage / Bidens pilosa extract (INCI: Bidens Pilosa Extract [+ carrier oils])

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⬜ considering / ✅ shortlisted (as a *conditional* gentle-alt-retinoid option) / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Retinoids & retinoid alternatives (botanical "retinol-like" / RXR-acting)

> **Bottom line up front:** Revinage has a genuinely interesting *mechanism* (its phytol metabolite, phytanic acid, is a real, if weak, RXR ligand) and a clean *tolerability* story. But the human topical efficacy evidence is **thin and largely supplier-generated**, the most-cited "independent human clinical trial" supporting it (**"Barbier et al. 2014, CCID 7:175–181"**) **appears to be a fabricated citation** circulated by AI-written skincare blogs (see §4 and §10), and the popular "retinol-equivalent, pregnancy-safe" framing **outruns the evidence**. Treat it as a *supporting* / *conditional* gentle actives play, not a hero anti-aging claim.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common / marketing names:** Revinage® (the branded Chemyunion ingredient), "bio-retinol," "phyto-retinol," "plant retinol alternative." Plant source: *Bidens pilosa* L. (Asteraceae) — Portuguese *picão-preto*, English "black jack" / "Spanish needle" / "beggarticks," a pantropical weed native to South America.
- **The plant vs. the branded ingredient — keep these distinct:**
  - **Generic INCI:** *Bidens Pilosa Extract.* Many suppliers sell *some* Bidens extract (Xi'an Teng Yun, Yangling Ciyuan, Hunan Nutramax, etc.); these are **not** standardized the same way and most efficacy data does **not** transfer to them.
  - **Revinage® (Chemyunion, Brazil)** is a specific **supercritical CO₂ (apolar) extract standardized to phytol**, blended into plant oils. INCI of the standard/RSPO grades: *Bidens Pilosa Extract (and) Elaeis Guineensis (Palm) Oil (and) Gossypium Herbaceum (Cotton) Seed Oil (and) Linum Usitatissimum (Linseed) Seed Oil.* The **palm-free grade (Revinage® WPO)** swaps palm for *Astrocaryum Murumuru Seed Butter.* [Chemyunion technical literature; UL Prospector]
- **Active marker / "key form":** **phytol**, a diterpene alcohol from the chlorophyll fraction. The proposed *active metabolite* is **phytanic acid** (formed from phytol after skin penetration), which is the actual RXR ligand. So "the active" is really *phytol → phytanic acid*, concentrated by the supercritical extraction.

---

## 2. Class & mechanism

Conventional retinoids (retinol → retinaldehyde → retinoic acid) act mainly through **RAR** (retinoic acid receptors), with RAR/AP-1 signaling driving both the benefits *and* the irritation ("retinoid dermatitis"). Revinage's pitch is a **different receptor route**:

1. Phytol penetrates the skin and is oxidized to **phytanic acid**.
2. Phytanic acid binds and activates **RXR** (retinoid X receptors) — confirmed pharmacology, see Kitareewan et al. 1996 (§4). RXR heterodimerizes with RAR (and other nuclear receptors) to drive transcription of extracellular-matrix and differentiation genes.
3. The claimed net effect: "retinol-like" downstream gene expression (pro-collagen, growth factors, reduced MMP-1) **without** strong direct RAR/AP-1-driven irritation. Chemyunion's own data report Revinage *raises* RXR expression and *lowers* RAR expression — the mechanistic basis for the "gentler than retinol" story.

**Honest caveat on mechanism:** phytanic acid is a **weak** RXR agonist — Kitareewan 1996 measured it as roughly **~200× less potent than 9-cis-retinoic acid** (Ki ~4 µM; EC50 ~20 µM). "Activates the same family of receptors" is true; "as strong as retinol" is not established. The mechanism is real but modest.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted (claimed)

| Hallmark | Claimed effect | Where the evidence sits |
|---|---|---|
| Collagen / ECM loss | ↑ pro-collagen, type I & IV collagen, elastin, fibronectin; ↓ MMP-1 | In vitro + ex vivo (supplier + Dieamant 2015); human = supplier only |
| Photoaging | ↓ AP-1 activity; ↓ UV-induced MMP-1 | Ex vivo (supplier); plausible, not independently confirmed in humans |
| Oxidative stress | ↑ SOD/CAT; ↓ free radicals, ↓ MDA (lipid peroxidation) | In vitro (supplier); Bidens *is* genuinely antioxidant-rich (independent reviews) |
| Cellular senescence | ↑ SIRT6 gene expression (DNA repair / telomere claim) | In vitro (supplier) only — **far from a human longevity claim** |
| Inflammaging | ↓ COX-2, PGE2, LTB4; macrophage/DC polarization to anti-inflammatory | In vitro (supplier + Carmona-Hernandez 2023 for non-polar extract) |
| Pigmentation / tone | ↓ melanin, ↓ α-MSH; ↓ facial dark spots | In vitro (supplier); human = supplier only |
| Hydration / barrier / TEWL | ↑ NMF, ceramide-3, cholesterol | Single supplier Raman study (lips) — weak |
| Sebum / oiliness | ↓ DHT | In vitro (supplier) only |
| Glycation/AGEs · mitochondria · microbiome | — | **No meaningful data** for topical use |

Genuine signal: **antioxidant + anti-inflammatory + RXR-mediated ECM support.** The breadth of the table above is mostly *Chemyunion's own marketing dossier*, not independent literature.

## 4. Evidence review by tier (the core)

> **Verification note (important for claims discipline):** I searched PubMed directly. For *"Bidens pilosa AND retinol"* PubMed indexes **exactly two** records: **Dieamant 2015** (PMID 25883669) and **Ye 2025** (PMID 40001599). Plus the foundational receptor paper (Kitareewan 1996). **There is no PubMed/Dovepress record of "Barbier et al. 2014, Clinical Cosmetic and Investigational Dermatology, 7:175–181"** — the human trial repeatedly cited across skincare blogs and retailer pages. It looks like an **AI-hallucinated citation** that has propagated through content-farm articles. **Do not cite it. Do not let it into copy.**

### Tier 1 — Foundational receptor pharmacology (not skin studies)
- **Kitareewan S, et al. (1996).** "Phytol metabolites are circulating dietary factors that activate the nuclear receptor RXR." *Molecular Biology of the Cell* 7(8):1153–1166. DOI: 10.1091/mbc.7.8.1153. — Established **phytanic acid as an RXR agonist** (Ki ~4 µM; transactivation 4–64 µM; EC50 ~20 µM for RXRα/β/γ), but **~200× weaker than 9-cis-RA**. This is the scientific bedrock under the whole "phytol → RXR" claim. Cell-based reporter assays, *not* skin.
- **Elmazar MM, et al. (2013).** "Phytol/Phytanic Acid and Insulin Resistance…" *PLoS One* 8(1):e45638. DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0045638. — Docking/biochemical work supporting phytanic acid's nuclear-receptor (RXR/PPAR) activity. Not skin; metabolic context.

### Tier 2 — In vitro (cell culture)
- **Dieamant G, Pereda M del C, Nogueira C, et al. (2015).** "Antiageing Mechanisms of a Standardized Supercritical CO₂ Preparation of Black Jack (*Bidens pilosa* L.) in Human Fibroblasts and Skin Fragments." *Evidence-Based Complementary and Alternative Medicine* 2015:280529. DOI: 10.1155/2015/280529. PMID 25883669.
  - **The one peer-reviewed paper that actually studies this extract.** Human dermal fibroblasts + keratinocytes; BPE-CO₂A at 0.05–0.20 mg/mL; comparators retinol & retinoic acid (10 µM); 48 h.
  - Results: ↑collagen ~+32.5%; ↑elastin ~+25%; ↑TGF-β1 ~+53%; ↑FGF ~+188%; **EGFr mRNA ~2.6-fold; RXR mRNA ~3.6–3.7-fold (vs ~1.5-fold for RA/retinol); RAR mRNA not significantly changed.** Effects "similar to or better than" retinol/RA on several markers.
  - **Limitations / bias:** **Co-funded by Chemyunion** (the commercial owner) alongside FAPESP; authors declared no conflict but the funding tie is a clear interpretive caveat. No human in-vivo arm. No limitations section. Small n (3 independent trials).
- **Carmona-Hernandez JC, et al. (2023).** "A *Bidens pilosa* L. Non-Polar Extract Modulates the Polarization of Human Macrophages and Dendritic Cells into an Anti-Inflammatory Phenotype." *(PMC10608814).* — Independent-ish immunomodulation data; supports the anti-inflammatory story for a non-polar extract, but it's immunology, not a skin-aging endpoint.
- **Chemyunion in-house in vitro (unpublished, in technical dossier):** keratinocytes/fibroblasts/melanocytes at 0.15% Revinage vs retinoic acid (30 µM) and retinol (0.15%): RXR +119% / RAR −20%; pro-collagen +159%; collagen +24%, elastin +26%; MMP-1 −33%; FGF +138%; SOD +247%; SIRT6 +72%; melanin −56%; DHT −64%. **Marketing-grade, not peer-reviewed.** Useful for hypotheses, not for claims.

### Tier 3 — Ex vivo (excised human skin fragments)
- **Dieamant 2015** also ran ex-vivo skin fragments (blepharoplasty tissue), BPE-CO₂A 0.5%, vs retinol 0.5% / RA 30 µM, 48 h — immunohistochemistry confirmed RXR>RAR modulation and ECM (collagen I/IV) staining.
- **Chemyunion dossier ex-vivo:** skin fragments (n=5 donors, 45–60 y) showed Revinage ≈ bakuchiol for type-I collagen, and Revinage > 1% bakuchiol for collagen IV / elastin / fibronectin; plus UV-MMP-1 reduction. Again **supplier data.**
- *Interpretation:* ex vivo is a step up from petri-dish but still **not** living-skin efficacy. Penetration, metabolism to phytanic acid, and 24–48 h windows don't equal weeks of real use.

### Tier 4 — Human ORAL
- **None relevant.** (Phytanic acid is dietary — from chlorophyll/ruminant fat — and *accumulates pathologically* in Refsum disease, an inborn error of phytanic acid oxidation. That's a metabolic-disease context, not an oral-supplement efficacy story, and is arguably a reason to *not* over-hype systemic phytanic acid.)

### Tier 5 — Human TOPICAL
- **RCT-grade, independent, peer-reviewed:** **none identified for Revinage / Bidens specifically.**
- **Ye S, Lee S, Kang S, Jun S-H, Kang N-G (2025).** "Improvement of Skin Condition Through RXR Alpha-Activating Materials." *Biomolecules* 15(2):296. DOI: 10.3390/biom15020296. PMID 40001599.
  - **Industry (LG Household & Health Care).** Tested a **combination of andrographolide + *Bidens pilosa* extract** for RXRα activation — in vitro (fibroblasts/keratinocytes), 3D skin models, **and a ~4-week human topical trial** reporting wrinkle/elasticity/hydration improvement with "no irritation" vs retinol.
  - **Why this is only partial support:** it is a **different formulation** (Bidens + andrographolide, not Revinage), **LG-funded**, short (4 wk), and does **not** isolate Bidens' contribution. It is the closest thing to a published human topical signal — but it is *adjacent*, not direct, evidence for Revinage.
- **Chemyunion in-house in vivo (unpublished, in technical dossier):** **40 women, 35–55 y, Fitzpatrick II–IV, 42 days**, 2% Revinage vs 0.3% **retinyl palmitate** (a *weak* retinoid comparator — note this, it's a soft benchmark), split-face/forearm, instrumental endpoints. Reported: elasticity +13–18%, dermal density +25%, roughness −36%, deep wrinkles ~−26–28%, UV dark spots −37–53%, "no statistical difference vs retinyl palmitate." **This is the strongest human dataset that exists for Revinage — and it is supplier-generated, unpublished, vs a weak comparator, with no independent replication.**

**What is actually *proven* for human TOPICAL use:** essentially **tolerability** (it's well tolerated) and *supplier-reported* efficacy comparable to a *weak* retinoid. Independent, published human RCT proof of anti-wrinkle efficacy: **absent.** Benefits like SIRT6/"senescence," sebum/DHT control, and barrier/NMF are **borrowed from in-vitro/supplier data**, not demonstrated for topical human use.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- The active is **lipophilic** (phytol/phytanic acid), so it partitions into the oily phase and the stratum corneum readily; the ingredient is delivered *in* plant oils, aiding penetration.
- Chemyunion's Raman-spectroscopy permeation study (n=2 volunteers, 3% Revinage) reported phytol peaking at **~65% present in the 1–60 µm depth at 4 h**, declining by 18–24 h. Plausible direction of travel, **tiny n, supplier study.**
- Mechanistic dependency worth noting: efficacy presumes **in-skin conversion of phytol → phytanic acid**; the rate/efficiency of that conversion in living human skin is **not well characterized independently.**

## 6. Formulation

- **Use level:** **1.0–2.0% w/w** (Revinage); efficacy studies used 2%. (Generic Bidens extracts vary wildly — don't assume parity.)
- **pH:** suggested **5.5–7.0** (forgiving; no acid activation needed, unlike L-AA).
- **Phase / processing:** **oil-soluble** — add to the **oil phase**, heat ≤85 °C. **Not** for hydroalcoholic systems.
- **Stability:** a real selling point. Supplier HPLC stress data show Revinage far more **photo- and oxidation-stable than retinol** (retinol ~−54% to −100% under UV/peroxide stress vs Revinage ~−3% to −8%); 24-month shelf life claimed. Even discounting supplier framing, phytol/oil chemistry is inherently more robust than retinol — credible.
- **Pairings:** comfortable with niacinamide, peptides, ceramides, HA, antioxidants (vitamin E often co-formulated). No pH conflict with most actives. Plays well in a **barrier-repair cream** (oil phase + ceramides) — relevant to our product thesis.
- **Conflicts:** none well-documented ("no incompatibilities evidenced" per supplier). The carrier **plant oils** (linseed, cotton, sunflower) are oxidation-prone, so antioxidant protection and packaging matter.
- **Sensory:** oil/butter-based, emollient, slightly herbaceous/green; contributes richness — fits a cream, not a clear gel/toner.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation:** consistently reported **low** — the core differentiator vs retinoids (no/low erythema, peeling, "retinoid dermatitis"). Supplier + Ye 2025 + dermatologist commentary align. But "gentle" ≠ "inert."
- **Sun sensitivity:** **not photosensitizing** in the way RAR-retinoids are (it actually shows photostability and AP-1/MMP-1 modulation). Still, daily SPF remains the #1 anti-aging step regardless.
- **Sensitization / allergy:** *Bidens pilosa* is an **Asteraceae (daisy/ragweed family)** plant — a botanical family with a known (if modest) contact-allergy/cross-reactivity profile. People with **Compositae/Asteraceae allergy** could react. Patch testing is prudent; "plant-based" is not automatically hypoallergenic (dermatologists explicitly caution this — Refinery29/Dr. Monica Li).
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** **This is the biggest claims trap.** Marketers routinely call it "pregnancy-safe retinol alternative." Reality: **there are no human pregnancy safety studies on topical Bidens/Revinage.** "Safe because it's not a RAR-retinoid" is a *mechanistic argument, not data.* Note also that phytanic acid is biologically active at nuclear receptors. **We should never state or imply pregnancy safety.** At most: "not a vitamin-A retinoid; consult your doctor." (Same posture mainstream sources take for bakuchiol.)
- **Sourcing / sustainability:** *Bidens pilosa* is a fast-growing, abundant **weed** — low cultivation pressure, a sustainability plus. Supercritical CO₂ extraction is **solvent-free** (green-chemistry talking point). The standard grade contains **palm oil** (RSPO grade available); the **WPO grade is palm-free** (murumuru butter) — choose WPO to avoid the palm-deforestation narrative.

## 8. Regulatory & claims

- **Cosmetic, not drug — as long as we talk about *appearance*.** Bidens Pilosa Extract is an established cosmetic INCI (listed in EU CosIng / COSMILE Europe; on US INCI). No special restriction identified in current EU Cosmetics Regulation annexes.
- **The line we must hold (per CLAUDE.md guardrails):**
  - ✅ Safer cosmetic language: "a gentle, plant-derived alternative to retinol," "helps visibly improve the look of fine lines / firmness / tone," "supports the skin's renewal processes," "well tolerated."
  - ⚠️ Drug-like / substantiation-risk: "retinol-equivalent," "as effective as retinol," "boosts collagen by X%," "repairs DNA," "anti-senescence / longevity," "treats hyperpigmentation," "**pregnancy-safe**." All of these either cross into drug territory, rest on supplier-only data, or are outright unsupported.
- **Substantiation reality:** if we ever quantify (% wrinkle reduction etc.), the *only* numbers available are **Chemyunion's unpublished in-house study** — acceptable as supplier substantiation in some markets *if disclosed as such*, but weak, and **not** something to present as independent science. The "Barbier 2014" citation must be treated as **non-existent / unverified** (see §4, §10).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Primary commercial source:** **Chemyunion (Brazil)** for the branded, phytol-standardized Revinage® (standard / RSPO / WPO grades), distributed via Knowde, UL Prospector, SpecialChem, etc. Functionally the **only** supplier whose efficacy data you can lean on.
- **Generic Bidens Pilosa Extract** is available from numerous Chinese/other botanical houses at lower cost — but **none carry the phytol standardization or the study package**, so they're not interchangeable for an evidence-led brand.
- **Cost tier:** mid-range specialty active (priced as a branded standardized botanical extract, well below growth factors/exosomes, above commodity oils). Used at 1–2%, cost-in-formula is modest. Exact pricing requires a supplier quote (not public).
- **Supply risk:** single-brand dependency (Chemyunion) is the main commercial risk; raw plant supply itself is low-risk (abundant weed).

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- 🚩 **Fabricated/unverifiable "human clinical trial."** The "**Barbier et al. 2014, *Clinical, Cosmetic and Investigational Dermatology*, 7:175–181**" study (0.5% Bidens, 8 weeks, improved elasticity/pigmentation) is cited all over retailer and "ingredient glossary" pages — **but it is not in PubMed or the Dovepress archive.** Strong signs of an AI-generated phantom citation. **Anyone citing it has not checked it.** Likewise, the much-repeated "*International Journal of Cosmetic Science* bakuchiol-vs-Bidens head-to-head" comparison could **not** be verified to a real article — treat as unverified.
- 🚩 **"As effective as retinol."** Mechanistically it's a *weaker* (RXR, ~200× lower potency) actor; the only head-to-head human data is supplier-run **vs retinyl palmitate (a weak retinoid)**, not vs retinol/tretinoin. "Comparable to a weak retinoid in one in-house study" is the honest version.
- 🚩 **"Pregnancy-safe."** No human pregnancy data. Mechanism ≠ safety. (See §7.)
- 🚩 **"Anti-senescence / longevity / DNA repair / SIRT6 / telomeres."** A single in-vitro gene-expression bump in a marketing dossier. Do **not** translate to human longevity language.
- ✅ **What's fair to say:** real, if modest, RXR-pathway mechanism; genuinely **antioxidant-rich plant**; **notably more stable than retinol**; **well tolerated**; a legitimate **gentle alternative** for retinoid-intolerant / sensitive users. The *tolerability + stability* story is the honest hero, not the efficacy magnitude.

---

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **Conditional / Supporting — not a hero.**
**One-liner:** A well-tolerated, stable, plant-derived RXR-acting "soft retinoid" with a real mechanism but **thin, mostly supplier-generated human evidence** — useful as a *gentle, sensitive-skin* supporting actor (and a clean story for a barrier-repair cream), **not** as the brand's flagship anti-aging claim, and **never** marketed as "retinol-equivalent" or "pregnancy-safe."

**Where it could fit:** a sensitive-skin / "retinoid-free" line extension, or a low-irritation evening step layered into a barrier-repair cream (oil phase + ceramides) — exactly the format it formulates best in. If the brand wants a *proven* retinoid hero, encapsulated retinol/retinal or bakuchiol (which has more independent human RCT data) are stronger bets; Revinage is the **"gentlest, but least-proven"** corner of that shelf.

**Longevity-relevance note:** The "skin-longevity" angle (SIRT6, senescence, telomeres, antioxidant defense) is **mechanistically on-theme but evidentially in-vitro only.** It's a compelling *narrative* hook for a longevity-positioned brand — which makes it *more* important, not less, to hold claims discipline so the story stays credible. Use it to *illustrate* the renewal mechanism, never to assert a longevity outcome.
