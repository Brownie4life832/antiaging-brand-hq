# Ingredient: Buddleja Extract (INCI: see §1 — multiple, e.g. Buddleja Officinalis Flower Extract; Buddleja Davidii Meristem Cell Culture)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Retinoids & retinoid alternatives (botanical antioxidant / photoprotective polyphenol active; **note:** the brief files this under retinoid alternatives, but on the evidence below it is **not** a retinoid mimetic — it is an antioxidant/photoprotectant. See §2 and §10.)

> **One-line honest summary:** Buddleja extract is a verbascoside-rich botanical whose real, repeatable signal is **antioxidant + UV/blue-light photoprotection**, demonstrated well in vitro and in mice. Its single best human datapoint (a 2024 menopausal-skin trial) is **open-label, no placebo, multi-active**, so it cannot prove Buddleja did the work. It belongs in the "environmental-defense / antioxidant booster" lane, **not** as a retinoid substitute and **not** as a standalone hero. Treat as a **supporting / conditional** active.

---

## 1. Identity

"Buddleja extract" is not one ingredient — it is a genus (*Buddleja*, family Scrophulariaceae; the butterfly bush), and at least four distinct cosmetic/research forms circulate, all united by one signature molecule: **verbascoside (= acteoside)**.

- **Common names:** Butterfly bush; butterfly-bush; (Chinese medicine) "Mi Meng Hua" for *B. officinalis* flower.
- **Genus / spelling:** *Buddleja* (also spelled *Buddleia*). Frequently mis-spelled both ways in INCI and labels.
- **INCI names actually seen on market / in research:**
  - **Buddleja Officinalis Flower Extract** — the SOLIBERINE® active (Greentech). Solvent extract of the flowers. [9][10]
  - **Buddleja Davidii Meristem Cell Culture** — CAS 94465-67-5, EC 305-351-6; a suspension of plant-cell-cultured meristem cells, standardized to verbascoside (the "plant stem cell" form). [7][8]
  - **Buddleja Cordata** leaf / cell-culture **methanolic extract (BCME)** — the form behind most of the published academic photoprotection science (Mexican research group). Research-grade, not a common commercial INCI. [1][2][3]
  - **Buddleja Scordioides / Buddleja Saligna** — appear in older photoprotection and other (e.g. anticancer patent) literature. [4]
- **Signature actives (across all forms):** **verbascoside / acteoside** (a phenylpropanoid / phenylethanoid glycoside) — plus **isoverbascoside, echinacoside, linarin (a flavonoid glycoside), leucosceptoside A, martinoside, syringin**, phytosterols (β-sitosterol), amino acids and polysaccharides. [1][2][6][7][9]
- **Verbascoside content (huge variation by form — verify on COA):**
  - SOLIBERINE NAT (B. officinalis): **verbascoside 10–20%**, echinacoside 1.5–3.5% (of dry matter). [10]
  - B. davidii meristem culture: standardized to verbascoside (vendor-dependent). [7][8]
  - BCME (B. cordata leaf): verbascoside ≈ **98.75 mg/g**, linarin ≈ 36.45 mg/g (research extract). [1]
- **Verbascoside identity:** CAS 61276-17-3; C₂₉H₃₆O₁₅; MW ≈ 624.6 g/mol; caffeoyl-phenylethanoid glycoside. Water-soluble, **moderately large and polar** — relevant to penetration (§5).
- **Physical form / carrier (commercial):** SOLIBERINE NAT is supplied in **water + propanediol**; the meristem culture is supplied as an aqueous suspension (often with glycerin + xanthan gum, e.g. "Buddleja Stems GX™"). [8][10]

## 2. Class & mechanism

Mechanistically Buddleja extract is an **antioxidant / photoprotective polyphenol active**, driven mostly by verbascoside. There are four converging mechanisms, each at a different evidence tier:

1. **Direct UV/HEV photon absorption ("organic-filter-like" behavior).** BCME and verbascoside absorb across the UVB–UVA range; BCME shows UV peaks at ~224, 290, 324 nm, and at 60 µg/mL its UV absorption was comparable to 10 µg/mL octyl methoxycinnamate (octinoxate). This is **passive screening, not an SPF**. [1][3][4]
2. **Free-radical scavenging / ROS quenching.** Verbascoside is a strong hydroxyl-radical scavenger; BCME hydroxyl-radical IC₅₀ ≈ 1.85 µg/mL (better than gallic acid control), DPPH IC₅₀ ≈ 64 µg/mL. Purified verbascoside antioxidant IC₅₀ ≈ 7.2 µg/mL. [1][6]
3. **NRF2 antioxidant-response activation.** *B. officinalis* extract upregulated NRF2/HO-1/NQO1 in UVB-stressed keratinocytes, restoring SOD/GSH and lowering MDA — i.e. it boosts the cell's *endogenous* antioxidant machinery, not just sponging radicals. [5]
4. **Anti-inflammatory + MMP/ECM protection.** Verbascoside suppresses UV-induced MMP secretion (e.g. MMP-9) and pro-inflammatory signaling in keratinocyte/fibroblast models, which is the plausible route to "less collagen breakdown." [6][12][13]

**Honest mechanistic translation:** this is a *defensive* mechanism set (block / scavenge / calm / protect ECM). There is **no credible evidence** it acts like a retinoid (no retinoic-acid-receptor agonism, no demonstrated keratinocyte differentiation / turnover induction). Classifying it under "retinoid alternatives" is a **marketing taxonomy, not a biological one** — see §10.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

**Bottom line up front:** the evidence is **deep on the in-vitro and mouse tiers, thin and confounded at the human-topical tier, and essentially absent at the human-oral tier (for skin).** Almost everything strong is *photoprotection in mice/cells*. There is **no published double-blind, placebo-controlled human topical RCT isolating a Buddleja extract** that I could verify.

### 4a. IN VITRO

- **Gómez-Hernández, Flores-Merino, Sánchez-Flores et al., 2021, *Plants (Basel)* 10(2):266. DOI 10.3390/plants10020266 (PMID 33573194).** [2]
  *B. cordata* **cell-culture** methanolic extract on UVB-irradiated 3T3-Swiss fibroblasts. UVB 293 mJ/cm² dropped viability to ~58–61%; extract at 2500 µg/mL restored to ~75%; **purified verbascoside (250 µg/mL) restored to ~78–85%** (verbascoside outperformed the whole extract). Note: at 2500 µg/mL the extract was **mildly cytotoxic itself** (87% viability at 24 h, no UV). LC/MS confirmed verbascoside as a major component. **Limitations:** single (mouse) cell line, ROS not directly measured, acute only, authors flag low polyphenol bioavailability and suggest encapsulation.
- **Zhu et al., 2025, *Pharmaceutical Biology* 63(1):567–581. DOI 10.1080/13880209.2025.2540348 (PMID 40771174).** [5]
  *B. officinalis* extract (BOE) on UVB-stressed **human HaCaT keratinocytes** (UVB 30 mJ/cm²). BOE reversed the UVB-induced drop in viability, rise in LDH/ROS/MDA and fall in SOD/GSH, **via NRF2 → HO-1/NQO1 upregulation** (qPCR + Western). **Limitations:** in vitro only, no topical/animal arm, concentration-response detail limited in abstract.
- **Vertuani, Beghelli, Scalambra et al., 2011, *Molecules* 16(8):7068–7080. DOI 10.3390/molecules16087068.** [6]
  Verbascoside (sourced from *Buddleia davidii* meristem cells) in real **dermo-cosmetic O/W emulsions** — antioxidant + **stability** data (see §6). Key for formulators, not an efficacy trial.
- **Korkina / "olive polyphenol" line, 2025, *Scientific Reports* 15, art. s41598-025-24845-4.** [12]
  Verbascoside (with oleuropein, hydroxytyrosol) reduced UVB-induced inflammation, oxidative stress, apoptosis and **collagen degradation / MMPs** in skin-cell models. Verbascoside is a contributor, not isolated.
- **Wound-healing/photoprotection via verbascoside-standardized *Acanthus ebracteatus*, 2024, *Scientific Reports* 14, s41598-024-52511-8 (Nature).** [13]
  Different plant, but **dose-dependent on verbascoside content**: scratch-assay wound closure rose with verbascoside; HaCaT/fibroblast co-cultures protected from UV; **MMP-9 secretion significantly suppressed (p<0.001)**. Useful corroboration that *the verbascoside* drives the effect.

### 4b. ANIMAL (in vivo, topical)

- **Avila-Acevedo, García-Bores, et al., 2014, *BMC Complementary and Alternative Medicine* 14:281. DOI 10.1186/1472-6882-14-281 (PMID 25086781).** [1] — *the anchor in-vivo study.*
  Topical **BCME (B. cordata leaf, 2 mg/mL, 200 µL)** on **SKH-1 hairless mice** (female, 5 groups, **n=5/group, 25 total**), acute UVB 6 mJ/cm², extract applied 15 min pre-irradiation, assessed 24 h later. Results vs UVB-only: **significantly reduced erythema, fewer sunburn cells, no vessel congestion, no edema, less epidermal thickening.** Skin penetration only **0.82% of applied dose (≈33 µg/cm²)**; non-genotoxic (*Vicia faba* micronucleus). **Limitations:** tiny n, acute single-dose model, **MMP/collagen NOT measured**, histology not molecular markers, mouse-skin penetration > human.
- **Espinoza-González, García-Bores, Benítez-Flores, Avila-Acevedo, 2016, *Bol. Latinoam. Caribe Plant. Med. Aromát. (BLACPMA)*.** [3]
  Follow-up: topical **verbascoside** (and BCME) in SKH-1 mice, **acute + chronic** UVB. Reported: acute — protected mice showed no sunburn/inflammation; **chronic — carcinoma/lesion incidence reduced ~90% vs unprotected (which reached ~100% lesions).** *[The dramatic ~90%/100% tumor figures come from the abstract/secondary summaries; full-text quantitative confirmation and per-group n were not machine-readable in the PDF — treat the tumor-prevention magnitude as **indicative, not independently verified**.]*
- **Avila-Acevedo et al., 2005, *Fitoterapia* 76(3-4):301–309. DOI 10.1016/j.fitote.2005.03.009 (PMID 15890460).** [4]
  *B. scordioides*: photoprotection in an *E. coli* UV-survival model + **SPF measured in guinea pigs**. **Verbascoside gave the largest SPF**; verbascoside and linarin acetate the highest protection. Old, crude SPF method, animal — supports the "verbascoside screens UV" claim only directionally.

### 4c. HUMAN — ORAL

- **No skin-relevant human oral trial of Buddleja extract was found.** Verbascoside oral pharmacology exists in other contexts (anti-inflammatory, neuro/hepatoprotective) but **not for skin appearance.** Do not borrow oral data for topical claims.

### 4d. HUMAN — TOPICAL (the tier that actually justifies cosmetic claims)

- **Korkina, Trombetta, et al., 2024, *Biomolecules* 14(9):1176. DOI 10.3390/biom14091176 (PMID via PMC11429794).** [11] — *the single best human datapoint, and it is confounded.*
  **Design:** randomized, **open-label, single-blind, NO placebo control**, 4 weeks. **n=104** Caucasian women (102 completed), ages 46–57 (mean 50.6), peri/post-menopausal. Four meristem-cell serums compared (B. davidii/**verbascoside 1.2–1.4%**, *Leontopodium*, *Centella*, *Echinacea*); each participant got **two** of the four — so the **Buddleja arm is only a fraction of 104**, and each serum **also contained hyaluronic acid + panthenol** (known to move hydration/elasticity on their own).
  **Pooled results (across all four serums):** elasticity 33.7→40.3 (p<0.01), moisture 49.0→56.9 (p<0.01), smoothness 12.2→18.0 (p<0.001), dermal thickness +233 µm (p<0.05), hydroxyproline ↑ (p<0.0068), MDA 26→15 µmol/L (p<0.01), "skin biological age" −~5 yrs.
  **Why this does NOT prove Buddleja works topically:** no placebo/vehicle arm; not single-active (HA + panthenol confounders); results reported pooled; open-label (expectancy bias); industry-aligned authors. It is **supportive, suggestive, hypothesis-generating** — not confirmatory.
- **Vendor "clinical" claims (SOLIBERINE / meristem actives):** suppliers reference face-serum testing and tolerance studies, but I found **no peer-reviewed, controlled, Buddleja-isolated human efficacy RCT.** Vendor anti-redness / anti-wrinkle claims are **unsubstantiated at the independent-evidence level.** [9][10]

**Tier verdict:** strongest real evidence = **animal-topical photoprotection** (mouse). Human-topical = **one confounded open-label multi-active trial.** No human-topical RCT isolating Buddleja.

## 5. Topical bioavailability

- **Penetration is modest.** Verbascoside is fairly large (MW ~625) and polar; in SKH-1 mice only **0.82% of the applied BCME dose (~33 µg/cm²) penetrated** by tape-strip assay — and **human stratum corneum is a tougher barrier than mouse**, so real-world penetration is likely lower. [1]
- **Implication:** the **UV-screening / surface-antioxidant** mechanisms (which work *at or above* the stratum corneum) are the most defensible; the **deep dermal collagen/MMP** mechanisms require it to reach living dermis, which is **not demonstrated in human skin**.
- **Formulation levers to help:** the 2021 authors explicitly recommend **encapsulation** (liposomes/nanocarriers) to offset low polyphenol bioavailability. [2] Lipophilic verbascoside derivatives (e.g. VPP) have been explored to improve skin partitioning. [6]
- **Stability is the bigger practical constraint than penetration** (see §6).

## 6. Formulation

- **Use level:**
  - *B. officinalis flower extract* (general): **0.5–2%** in a water base with a glycol (e.g. propanediol). [9]
  - SOLIBERINE NAT specifically: supplier recommends **5–7%** of the finished product (because verbascoside is only 10–20% of that raw material). [10]
  - Meristem-culture actives in the human trial delivered **~1.2–1.4% verbascoside** in serum. [11]
- **pH:** keep **acidic, ~4.4–6.4** (SOLIBERINE spec). [10] **This matters a lot:** verbascoside **fully degrades within ~3 weeks at pH 7 (room temp)** but lasts ~60 days at pH 5; it is far more stable in ethanol/water and in low-water systems. [6] **Do not formulate near neutral.**
- **Stability:** in an O/W emulsion verbascoside lost **~30% over 150 days at 40 °C** (accelerated) — manageable but real; protect from **light and heat**, consider antioxidants/chelators and opaque/airless packaging. [6]
- **Pairings (synergistic, evidence-aligned):**
  - **Vitamin C, vitamin E, ferulic acid, other polyphenols** — stacks the antioxidant/photoprotection story (olive-polyphenol combo data). [12]
  - **Niacinamide, panthenol, HA** — barrier/hydration partners (and the combo actually used in the human trial). [11]
  - **Sunscreen actives** — Buddleja is a *booster/antioxidant under* SPF, never a replacement (§8).
- **Conflicts / cautions:** avoid **high pH** systems and strong oxidizers; verbascoside is a catechol-type polyphenol so it can **discolor (browning/oxidation)** — chelation + antioxidants + airless packaging help.
- **Sensory:** water/glycol or aqueous-suspension forms are easy in serums/essences/lotions; can carry a faint botanical color/odor; low use levels are sensory-neutral.

## 7. Safety

- **Tolerance (commercial active data):** SOLIBERINE NAT at 5%: **"very good skin compatibility"** on patch test and HRIPT (no sensitization); **not mutagenic; not phototoxic.** HET-CAM eye test "slightly irritant" (typical for many actives; relevant to eye-area formulating). [10]
- **Irritation/sensitization:** generally **low** at cosmetic levels; no notable allergen flags in the meristem-culture safety listings reviewed. [7][8]
- **Sun:** **Not photosensitizing** per SOLIBERINE data; on the contrary the extract is photoprotective. *Caveat:* a general IFRA note exists that some *Buddleja* essential-oil / fresh-herb fractions could photosensitize — that concerns **essential oils**, not the polyphenol water/glycol extracts used here; keep to the standardized extract forms. [unverified — IFRA note surfaced in secondary search only]
- **Pregnancy:** **No topical-cosmetic human data.** The only reproductive-tox study is **verbascoside 1 g/kg/day intraperitoneal in mice (Etemad, Zafari, Moallem et al., 2016, *Iran. J. Pharm. Res.* 15(2):521–525)** — **no teratogenicity, no NOAEL exceedance** at that dose. [14] **Relevance to topical use is limited** (IP route, systemic, ≠ cosmetic exposure). Default to the cautious cosmetic line: educate, don't make pregnancy-safety promises.
- **Sourcing/biology caveat:** *Buddleja davidii* is an **invasive species** in parts of Europe/North America — a potential ESG/marketing nuance (meristem cell-culture sourcing actually side-steps wild harvest, which is a positive story). The genus also contains compounds studied for other bioactivities; stick to characterized, COA-backed cosmetic grades.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (cosmetic vs drug — per CLAUDE.md)

- **Status:** a **cosmetic ingredient** (skin conditioning / antioxidant). Listed in EU CosIng (e.g. Buddleja Davidii Meristem Cell Culture). Not a US OTC sunscreen monograph active.
- **Hard line — DO NOT imply it is a sunscreen / SPF.** Despite "absorbs UVA/UVB" lab data, claiming sun protection, SPF, or "protects from sunburn" turns a cosmetic into an **OTC drug (US FDA)** and is unsubstantiated for a finished product. Even the mouse SPF/erythema data must not be ported to a consumer SPF claim. This is the #1 compliance trap for this ingredient.
- **✅ Safer cosmetic phrasings:** "antioxidant," "helps defend the look of skin against environmental/oxidative stress," "supports skin's resilience to daily light exposure," "helps visibly calm look of redness," "supports a more even-looking tone." Pair with "use a broad-spectrum SPF."
- **⚠️ Risky / needs substantiation or legal review:** "sun protection," "SPF," "blue-light/HEV protection" as a hard claim, "boosts collagen," "anti-wrinkle," "prevents photoaging," "reduces skin cancer risk" (the mouse-carcinoma data must **never** appear in consumer copy — it is a drug-disease claim).
- **HEV/blue-light:** plausible but **mostly vendor lab data** — if used, keep to "helps defend against" soft language and hold substantiation on file; many markets now scrutinize blue-light claims.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Suppliers / trade names:**
  - **Greentech – SOLIBERINE® / SOLIBERINE NAT** (Buddleja Officinalis Flower Extract, verbascoside 10–20%). [9][10]
  - **Meristem cell-culture form** (Buddleja Davidii Meristem Cell Culture; e.g. "Buddleja Stems GX™" CM-Studio listing; associated with plant-stem-cell suppliers). [7][8]
  - Research-grade *B. cordata*/*B. scordioides* extracts are academic, not standard supply.
- **Cost tier:** **mid-to-premium.** Standardized verbascoside actives and especially **plant-cell-culture ("stem cell") meristem** materials sit above commodity botanical extracts (cell-culture biomanufacturing is costly); SOLIBERINE-type flower extracts are more moderate. Use levels (0.5–7%) keep cost-in-use reasonable for a serum, higher for the meristem form.
- **Supply considerations:** verbascoside content varies widely by species/form/lot — **demand a COA with verbascoside %.** Meristem cell-culture sourcing is consistent and avoids wild/invasive harvest (a clean sustainability narrative).

## 10. Hype vs evidence

- **Taxonomy hype:** filing Buddleja under **"retinoid alternatives" is misleading.** It has **no retinoid mechanism** (no RAR agonism, no turnover/differentiation data). It is an **antioxidant/photoprotectant.** If the brand wants a true retinoid alternative (bakuchiol, etc.), this isn't it.
- **"Plant stem cell" hype:** the meristem-cell-culture marketing leans on "stem cell" language. The cells are a **manufacturing method to produce verbascoside consistently** — the benefit is the **verbascoside**, not literal stem-cell magic transferred to your skin. Honest framing: "cell-cultured source of verbascoside."
- **"UV/SPF" hype:** real lab UV absorption ≠ a usable sunscreen in a finished cosmetic. The mouse photoprotection is genuine science; the leap to consumer SPF/blue-light protection is **overclaim.**
- **What's genuinely solid:** verbascoside is a **legitimately strong antioxidant + NRF2 activator + MMP/inflammation modulator** in cells, with consistent **mouse photoprotection.** That's a real, defensible *defense* story.
- **What's missing:** **a placebo-controlled human topical RCT isolating Buddleja/verbascoside.** Until that exists, human efficacy claims rest on one confounded open-label trial + mechanism.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL — not a hero.**

- **Why supporting:** the evidence is mechanistically sound and photoprotection in mice is real, but the **human-topical tier is a single open-label, multi-active, no-placebo trial.** That's enough to *support* an antioxidant/environmental-defense product, **not** enough to anchor a flagship claim or carry a hero SKU.
- **Best fit in the lineup:** an **antioxidant "daytime/environmental defense" serum or as a booster within the barrier-repair cream's antioxidant complex** — alongside vitamin C/E/ferulic, niacinamide — explicitly positioned as **"defense," paired with SPF messaging**, never as sun protection itself.
- **Conditions to upgrade toward "shortlisted":** (1) source a **COA-standardized verbascoside %** active; (2) formulate **acidic (pH ~5) + light/heat-protected + airless**; (3) keep claims to soft antioxidant/look-of-redness language; (4) ideally back with the brand's own simple instrumental (e.g. antioxidant capacity / ROS) data before any "defense" hero positioning.
- **Skip if:** the brand needs a *retinoid alternative* (wrong category) or wants an evidence-backed *collagen/wrinkle* hero (human RCT not there).
- **Longevity / skin-longevity note:** the strongest longevity-relevant angle is **photoprotection + oxidative-stress defense + ECM (MMP) protection** — i.e. *slowing extrinsic (photo)aging* rather than reversing intrinsic aging. That maps cleanly to a "protect the skin's future / preserve the barrier and matrix" thesis, which is a credible, honest longevity story **if framed as defense, not repair.** Verbascoside's NRF2-activating, photoprotective profile is a genuine fit for an "environmental-defense pillar," but it is a **complement to**, not a replacement for, the barrier-repair core.

Log under **considering** ([`../../ideas/considering.md`](../../ideas/considering.md)) as a supporting antioxidant/photo-defense active.
