# Ingredient: Conditioned Media / Secretomes (fibroblast TNS, ADSC-CM, MSC-CM, cord-lining PTT-6, bone-marrow)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⬜ considering / ☑ shortlisted for review / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Growth factors, cytokines, exosomes, conditioned media & regenerative
**Slug:** conditioned-media-secretomes

> One-line summary: A premium, buzzy "regenerative" category — the harvested liquid (or its concentrated/EV fractions) left behind by cultured human or animal cells, rich in growth factors and cytokines. The best-studied product (SkinMedica TNS) has genuine multi-year RCT support; most of the category rides on in-vitro data, manufacturer studies, and a large unresolved question of whether big proteins do anything on **intact** skin. Treat as a high-cost, high-claims-risk hero candidate only if we can stand behind a specific, evidenced raw material.

---

## 1. Identity

**Common names / category terms:** Conditioned media (CM); secretome; cell-conditioned media (CCM); "growth factor" serums; "regenerative" skincare; cell-free supernatant. Often marketed under proprietary trade names.

**What it physically is:** When cells are grown in culture, they secrete proteins (growth factors, cytokines, chemokines), extracellular-matrix fragments, lipids, metabolites, and nano-scale extracellular vesicles (exosomes) into their nutrient broth (commonly DMEM-based). That spent broth — cell-free after the cells are filtered out — is the **conditioned media / secretome**. It is a complex, undefined biological mixture, not a single molecule.

**Key forms / derivatives by cell source:**

| Form | Source cell | Notable example(s) |
|---|---|---|
| **Human fibroblast CM (hFCM / TNS)** | Neonatal/dermal human fibroblasts, often hypoxic bioreactor | SkinMedica TNS Recovery Complex (NouriCel-MD, 2001), TNS Advanced+ (hypoxia-cultured "hCCM") |
| **ADSC-CM** | Adipose-derived mesenchymal stem cells | Korean "stem cell" cosmetics; many med-spa secretome ampoules |
| **MSC-CM (umbilical cord / cord blood)** | Human umbilical-cord or cord-blood MSC | hUC-MSC-CM serums; microneedling adjuncts |
| **Bone-marrow MSC-CM** | Human bone-marrow MSC (FBS- or platelet-lysate-grown) | Mostly research-stage |
| **Cord-lining CM / PTT-6** | Red-deer (or human) umbilical-cord *lining* MSC | CALECIM Professional (PTT-6) |
| **Exosome / EV fractions** | Purified EVs from any of the above (incl. ADSC, plant) | Exosome ampoules, post-procedure serums |
| **Foreskin fibroblast CM (FSF-CM)** | Neonatal foreskin fibroblasts | Common research control / some products |

**INCI naming:** No single INCI. Appears as e.g. *Human Fibroblast Conditioned Media*, *Human Adipose Derived Stem Cell Conditioned Media*, *sh-Polypeptide-X* (specific recombinant growth factors), or proprietary descriptors. Many individual growth factors carry their own "sh-" (synthesized human) INCI names when added discretely rather than as whole CM.

## 2. Class & mechanism

Conditioned media is a **paracrine signaling cocktail**. Cells in culture (especially stem/progenitor cells and fibroblasts) secrete the same messenger proteins they would use in vivo to coordinate wound healing and tissue maintenance. Documented constituents across studies include EGF, bFGF/FGF, TGF-β, VEGF, PDGF, HGF, KGF, IGF-1, interleukins, and matrix proteins, plus exosomes carrying miRNA and protein cargo (PMC11416772; Naughton et al. 2022, PMC9823186).

Proposed mechanisms at the skin/cell level (mostly demonstrated in vitro / ex vivo):
- **Fibroblast stimulation** → increased proliferation, migration, and procollagen / type I & III collagen synthesis.
- **MMP-1 suppression** → less collagen breakdown after UV exposure.
- **Antioxidant response** → reduced ROS, activation of antioxidant-response-element (ARE) pathways.
- **Anti-senescence / proteostasis** → reduced senescence marker H2A.J in human skin explants; upregulation of proteasome (POMP, PSMB5/6) and autophagy (ATG5/7/12, BECN1, LC3) genes in 3-D UV-irradiated skin models (Naughton et al. 2022).
- **ECM & barrier genes** → upregulation of collagens (COL1A1, COL3A1, COL4A1, COL7A1), elastin/fibrillin (ELN, FBN1), and epidermal barrier markers (filaggrin, claudin-1, transglutaminase-1).

Critically, mechanism is **source- and process-dependent**. Hypoxic culture, platelet-lysate vs. fetal-bovine-serum media, bioreactor vs. flask, and EV enrichment all change the secretome's composition and potency (Kim et al. 2020, PMC7904527; PMC11416772). There is **no standardized "secretome."**

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Plausibility | Evidence tier (best available) |
|---|---|---|
| Collagen / ECM loss | High (most-studied target) | Human TOPICAL RCT (TNS) + in-vitro |
| Cellular senescence | Moderate–High | Human ex vivo + biopsy (H2A.J, TNS) |
| Photoaging — fine lines & texture | High | Human TOPICAL RCT (TNS); effect sizes modest (fine lines ≈ −31%, coarse lines −13%, roughness −23%) |
| Photoaging — tone/hyperpigmentation | Low–Moderate | Human TOPICAL RCT (TNS) but small effect (−6.3%); stronger results are microneedle-delivered |
| Oxidative stress | Moderate | In-vitro / animal |
| Barrier decline / hydration (TEWL) | Moderate | Animal + human biopsy markers |
| Pigmentation / tone evenness | Moderate | Human (microneedle-delivered + TNS biopsy) |
| Inflammaging | Low–Moderate | In-vitro / mechanistic |
| Mitochondrial decline | Low | Mechanistic only |
| Glycation / AGEs | Not established | — |
| Microbiome | Not established | — |

The strongest, most credible claims are around **collagen/ECM, photoaging-related fine lines/texture, and senescence** — and almost entirely from the single best-resourced product (TNS).

## 4. Evidence review by tier  *(the core of this dossier)*

> Read this section as a hierarchy. In-vitro and animal work is plentiful and largely positive but does not prove topical human benefit. The category's credibility rests on a **small number of genuine human topical RCTs**, dominated by one product line (SkinMedica TNS). Almost everything else is microneedle-delivered (i.e. the skin barrier is mechanically breached — not "just topical"), manufacturer-run, or preclinical.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell culture)  — *abundant, positive, low translational weight*

- **ADSC-CM:** suppresses MMP-1, enhances ARE/antioxidant activity, drives fibroblast proliferation and procollagen (reviewed in PMC11416772, *Stem Cells Cloning* 2024).
- **hUC-MSC-CM:** protects keratinocytes/fibroblasts against UVB damage, reduces G1 arrest, enhances migration (Wang et al. 2015, cited in PMC11416772).
- **Bone-marrow MSC-CM (Kim et al. 2020, *Int J Stem Cells*, PMC7904527):** human-platelet-lysate-grown CM contained more total secreted proteins (39 vs 25) and higher VEGF, TGF-β1, HGF than FBS-grown CM; produced significantly greater human dermal fibroblast & keratinocyte proliferation, migration, and collagen/elastin output. **In vitro only.**
- **Cord-lining CM / PTT-6 (CellResearch Corp / CALECIM, manufacturer):** red-deer cord-lining CM and human cord-lining CM each drove ~2.1× proliferation of aged-skin fibroblast lines (asf74-76) over controls at 5 days. **In vitro only; manufacturer-run → bias.**
- **Bioreactor vs flask CM:** bioreactor media secreted more growth factors, hyaluronic acid and procollagen and better prevented UVB oxidative stress (PMC11416772).

*Limitation:* cell-culture concentrations, direct cell contact, and absence of a stratum corneum make these results a poor proxy for finished-product topical performance.

### Tier 2 — Animal (mouse / rat)  — *supportive of photoprotection & hydration*

- **BM-MSC-CM, UVB hairless mice (Kwon et al. 2016, *Photodermatol Photoimmunol Photomed* 32:120, doi:10.1111/phpp.12224):** topical CM for 8 weeks reduced wrinkle formation, improved skin hydration and reduced TEWL; in vitro it raised type-I collagen and lowered MMP-1 after UVB.
- **ASC-CM in rats (PMC11416772):** twice-daily x4 weeks improved TEWL and skin thickness — "potential remedy for photoaging."
- **Cord-blood MSC-CM in aged mice (PMC11416772):** reduced oxidative stress and senescence markers.

*Limitation:* rodent skin (esp. hairless/depilated) is far more permeable than intact human skin; animal "topical" success overstates human topical likelihood.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL  — *not applicable*

No meaningful oral/ingestible use case for conditioned media as a skincare acten. Not a route for this ingredient. (Ignore any "ingestible exosome" marketing — no credible topical-relevant evidence.)

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL  *(the decisive tier)*

**4a. Genuine topical RCTs / controlled trials (skin barrier intact) — the real evidence:**

- **Mehta et al. 2008, *J Drugs Dermatol* 7(9):864–871 — TNS Recovery Complex.** Double-blind, randomized, vehicle-controlled; **n=60**; twice-daily for ~6 months. Significant improvement vs vehicle in clinical grading of fine lines/wrinkles by month 3; optical profilometry showed reduced surface roughness. *Industry-affiliated (SkinMedica).* **[Strongest classic citation.]**
- **Naughton GK, Jiang LI, Makino ET, et al. 2022, *Dermatol Ther (Heidelb)* (publ. online Nov 2022; in print 2023), doi:10.1007/s13555-022-00844-5 (PMC9823186) — TNS Advanced+ (hypoxia-cultured human fibroblast CM, "hCCM").** _[Citation corrected on verification: this paper is in_ Dermatology and Therapy_, not_ Clin Cosmet Investig Dermatol _15:2829–2847 as originally cited.]_ 24-week, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled, **cross-over**; enrolled **n=68 (66 completed)**; ages 37–70, Fitzpatrick I–V, moderate–severe photodamage; twice daily over standardized cleanser/moisturizer/SPF 35. Investigator-graded improvements vs baseline: fine lines −30.9%, coarse lines −12.7%, tactile roughness −22.9%, overall photodamage −9.3%, sagging −7.8%, hyperpigmentation −6.3% at week 24, with significance vs placebo on multiple measures in the controlled phase. **Biopsies (n=20 active / 10 placebo)** showed reduced senescence marker H2A.J, increased collagen IV/decorin/elastin, restored elastic fibers, and improved barrier proteins. Self-perceived age dropped a median 6 years. *Funded by Allergan Aesthetics/AbbVie; authors employed by Allergan; Naughton founder of Histogen — material conflicts of interest.* **[Best-designed study in the category, but not independent.]**
- **Fitzpatrick RE & Rostan EF 2003 — TNS Recovery Complex.** Open-label (no control); **n=14** (11/14 showed improvement in ≥1 area); twice daily x60 days; histology showed new dermal (Grenz-zone) collagen and epidermal thickening, plus profilometry-confirmed smoother texture. *Pilot, uncontrolled, small.* _[Journal/page citation contested across secondary sources — listed both as_ Dermatol Surg _29(5):491–495 and as_ J Cosmet Laser Ther _5(1):25–34 (PMID 12745596); verify the primary record before public use.]_
- **Gold et al. 2023 — TNS Advanced+.** Open-label; **n=15** postmenopausal women; 12 weeks; improvements in fine/coarse lines, photodamage, tone. *Uncontrolled, small, industry.*
- **ADSC-CM cosmetic (Korea), *Biomedical Dermatology* 2020 (Springer 10.1186/s41702-020-0056-9):** placebo-controlled cosmetic-efficacy study, ~20 subjects/group (control + 2 treatment arms) per MFDS functional-cosmetic guidelines; greater effect on wrinkles and elasticity than on melanin/erythema. *Small, industry/cosmetic-grade methodology.*
- **EPC-CM 5% cosmetic (Sohn et al. 2018, cited in PMC11416772):** **n=25** women, 4 weeks, twice daily; improved crow's-feet wrinkle index and skin texture. *Small, uncontrolled-leaning.*

**4b. Microneedle / laser / fractional-delivery studies (barrier deliberately breached — NOT plain topical):**

These show better results but answer a *different* question, because the device — not topical penetration — drives delivery. They borrow credibility for "topical" claims they don't earn.

- **Liang et al. 2022, *Front Med* 9:837332 (PMC9171013) — hUC-MSC-CM + microneedling.** RCT, split-face; **n=30 (28 completed)**, 5 sessions/2-wk intervals. CM side beat saline side: melanin index −24.25 vs −12.36, wrinkles −12.15 vs −7.46, pores −9.50 vs −5.81, elasticity +0.13 vs +0.08 (all P≈0.00). Minor AEs only. *No histology; small; CM stability/reproducibility flagged as a concern.*
- **ADSC secretome + microneedling vs fractional CO2 laser (Indonesia):** single-blind split-face RCT, **n=30** women 35–59 — both delivery routes improved aging features (PMC9926994; JCAS RCT).
- **ADMSC media + niacinamide post-laser RCT** (Forever Labs / journal) — combination/adjunct design.

**4c. Reviews & honest meta-assessments of the human evidence:**

- **Forman Taub A. 2024, *J Clin Aesthet Dermatol* (PMC11518787), "Regenerative topical skincare: stem cells and exosomes":** "Clinical studies with EVs in aesthetics are very few"; fewer than ~20 clinical trials on skin concerns total, a handful aesthetic; most evidence preclinical; no FDA-approved exosome products; no consensus on purity, source, dose, or human safety. Notes prior "growth factor" products were later found to contain EVs — i.e. mechanism claims have been historically misattributed.
- **EV in skin aging — clinical-trial review, *Int J Mol Sci* 2025, 26(5):2354 (MDPI):** ~12 clinical studies of extracellular vesicles across skin aging/scars/alopecia/wounds; concludes evidence is held back by lack of production standardization and absence of large-scale RCTs.
- **MSC secretome dermatology review 2025, *Stem Cell Res Ther* 16 (10.1186/s13287-025-04311-8):** broad mechanistic support across BM/adipose/cord sources; flags small samples, no universal bioprocessing standards, and immature regulation.

**Bottom line on tiers:** The only benefits with credible **plain-topical, controlled human** support are the TNS/fibroblast-CM line's improvements in fine lines, texture/roughness, and biopsy-level senescence/ECM markers — and that evidence is industry-funded. For ADSC/MSC/cord-lining/exosome forms, the better human results almost all rely on **microneedling/laser delivery** or are in-vitro/manufacturer studies. Claims of these forms working from a jar, undelivered, are largely **borrowed** from breached-barrier or cell-culture data.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration  *(the category's central weakness)*

The core scientific problem: growth factors and cytokines are large proteins (typically ~6–30+ kDa); exosomes are 30–150 nm vesicles. The intact stratum corneum strongly favors small (<500 Da), lipophilic molecules — the widely cited "500-Dalton rule" (Truth Treatments; HA molecular-weight penetration studies). On size alone, intact-skin penetration of whole growth factors should be minimal.

Counter-arguments / nuances the field offers:
- Photodamaged or compromised skin has a leakier barrier, plausibly allowing some uptake.
- Some manufacturer/lab data claim exosomes penetrate the epidermis within hours in skin models and reach deeper layers by ~18 h, raising collagen I/elastin by day 3 (industry/preclinical, PMC reviews) — but these are often non-independent and use models, not in-vivo intact human skin.
- Real effects may be partly **surface/superficial signaling** to the upper epidermis, or driven by occlusion/hydration/other formula excipients rather than the GFs themselves.
- The microneedle/laser RCTs work precisely because they **bypass** the barrier — which is also why their results can't be claimed for a plain leave-on.

**Verdict on penetration:** Genuinely unresolved and likely the biggest honest weakness. We should assume plain-topical delivery of intact large proteins is **inefficient**, and not imply otherwise in copy.

## 6. Formulation

- **"Concentration":** No meaningful single number — potency depends entirely on the secretome's growth-factor/EV titer, not a % on the label. Marketing figures like SkinMedica's "93.6% conditioned media" describe how much of the formula is CM, **not** efficacy. Research cosmetics have used ~5% CM solutions (EPC-CM). Treat vendor potency claims skeptically; ask for quantified GF content (ELISA) and EV particle counts.
- **pH:** Near-physiologic (~6–7.5) to preserve protein integrity; not an acid-active.
- **Stability — the hard part:** Proteins and EVs are heat-, shear-, and time-sensitive. Most require cold-chain handling, short shelf life, and careful preservation; freeze-drying/lyophilized "powder + activator" formats exist to extend stability. Activity can degrade silently — a product can test "present" without being bioactive.
- **Pairings:** Generally positioned as a gentle, restorative layer; commonly paired with hyaluronic acid, niacinamide, peptides, ceramides. Frequently sold as **post-procedure** (post-laser/microneedling/peel) recovery — its best-supported real-world niche.
- **Conflicts:** Avoid co-formulating with high concentrations of low-pH actives (L-ascorbic acid, strong AHA/BHA) or harsh surfactants/high-alcohol bases that can denature proteins. Direct same-routine layering with strong exfoliating acids may degrade the actives.
- **Sensory:** Native fibroblast CM can carry a "protein" odor; older TNS Recovery Complex was noted for an off smell and added fragrance (an allergen risk) — newer versions reformulated. Usually light serum/essence textures.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation / sensitization:** Generally well tolerated in trials. In the Naughton 2022 RCT, AE scores stayed below mild; 4/45 active subjects had mild reactions (contact dermatitis, acne, folliculitis, inflammation), 2 discontinued. Added fragrance in some formulas is a separate allergen concern.
- **Sun sensitivity:** No evidence of photosensitization; not a reason to skip SPF (which remains the #1 anti-aging step regardless).
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** No specific safety data. Given the undefined biological nature and growth-factor content, a cautious "consult your doctor / not specifically studied in pregnancy" stance is appropriate. Do not affirmatively call it pregnancy-safe.
- **Theoretical proliferation concern:** Growth factors stimulate cell division, prompting long-debated (largely theoretical) questions about effects on dormant precancerous cells. The CIR Expert Panel has **not** completed a safety assessment of human fibroblast conditioned media. TNS has 20+ years of market use without a documented cancer signal, but Baumann and others note long-term safety is **not formally established**. A 2018 class-action over risk disclosure was dropped. Best practice: advise users to monitor changing moles.
- **Biological/viral safety & sourcing:** Human- and animal-derived raw materials require donor screening, sterility, and viral-inactivation/quality controls. Regulators may demand production methods, QC, and microbial/viral limits. Sourcing varies widely in rigor — this is a real diligence item, not a formality.
- **Sustainability / ethics:** Sourcing narratives matter. Human sources raise donor-consent questions; cord-lining PTT-6 (red deer farmed for velvet in NZ) is marketed as an "ethical, abundant byproduct" — but that's a manufacturer framing to vet, and animal-derived inputs carry their own ethical/marketing considerations.

## 8. Regulatory & claims  *(per CLAUDE.md guardrails)*

- **Cosmetic vs. drug line:** In the US, cosmetics may not claim to treat/prevent disease or alter the **structure/function** of the body. Conditioned media's entire mechanistic story — "stimulates collagen," "regenerates," "repairs," "reverses," "rejuvenates at the cellular level" — sits squarely in the danger zone. Claiming the product *boosts collagen* or *repairs DNA/cells* risks reclassification as an unapproved drug.
- **No FDA approval / "Wild West":** There are no FDA-approved exosome products; human-derived cosmetic ingredients are a poorly regulated frontier. The FDA has issued warnings around unapproved exosome/stem-cell products (more aggressively in injectables, but the scrutiny is rising).
- **Stem-cell wording trap:** Finished products contain **no living stem cells** — only the cell-free secretome. Calling a product "stem cell skincare" is misleading and a claims risk; the accurate phrasing is "stem-cell-**derived** conditioned media."
- **Safe cosmetic language we *could* use** (per glossary): "helps improve the appearance of fine lines," "supports the look of firmer skin," "visibly smooths texture," "supports skin's natural renewal," "ideal for post-procedure comfort." 
- **Language to avoid / flag for legal:** "boosts collagen (by X%)," "repairs cells," "regenerates skin," "reverses aging," "anti-aging treatment," "stem cell therapy." Any % efficacy figure must trace to a substantiation file — and most published numbers come from industry studies on **other** products, which we cannot borrow for ours.
- **Substantiation reality:** To make even modest "supports/visibly" claims credibly, we would need our own consumer/clinical testing on our finished formula — the published TNS data does not substantiate a different supplier's CM.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier: Premium → very premium.** Among the most expensive actives in skincare. Branded finished products (TNS, CALECIM) retail roughly US $150–300+ per unit; raw secretome/EV ingredients command high per-gram pricing and carry cold-chain, QC, and short-shelf-life overhead.
- **Supplier landscape:** Korean and US biotech suppliers dominate (ADSC-CM, MSC-CM, exosome ampoules); SkinMedica/Allergan (vertically integrated, hypoxic-bioreactor fibroblast CM); CellResearch Corp (PTT-6/CALECIM). Quality and quantified potency vary enormously between suppliers.
- **Diligence checklist if we pursue this:** quantified growth-factor content (ELISA panel) and EV particle counts; lot-to-lot consistency data; donor screening & viral-inactivation documentation; sterility/endotoxin testing; bioactivity (not just presence) assay; cold-chain & stability data on the **finished** formula; clear, defensible sourcing story.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **Hype:** "Stem cell skincare" (no live cells present), "regenerates/repairs your skin's DNA," "as good as a procedure in a jar," big growth-factor counts ("over 380 growth factors," "3,000+ proteins") implying proven benefit, exosomes framed as a settled miracle.
- **Evidence:** One product family (TNS) has real, multi-year, biopsy-supported topical RCT data for visible photoaging — but it's industry-funded and specific to that material. Everything else leans on in-vitro data, manufacturer studies, rodent models, or **device-delivered** (microneedling/laser) trials that don't validate plain-topical use. The penetration of large proteins through intact skin is unresolved and probably limited. Independent reviewers (Forman Taub 2024; MDPI 2025) are explicit that the field lacks standardization, large RCTs, and regulatory clarity.
- **Net:** Real signal exists, concentrated in fibroblast CM for photoaging. The category's marketing vastly outruns the generalizable evidence. A high-protein number on a label is a *complexity* claim, not an *efficacy* claim.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ Conditional** (lean hero-with-conditions only for fibroblast CM; skip the exosome hype play).

**One-line reasoning:** The science is genuinely intriguing and on-trend, and fibroblast conditioned media (TNS-type) has the best human topical RCT data of any "growth factor" actor — but the evidence is supplier-specific and industry-funded, the penetration of large proteins through intact skin is unresolved, the claims surface is a regulatory minefield, and cost/stability/sourcing are demanding. Only viable as a hero if we license a specific raw material with quantified, bioactive, well-documented secretome and run our own substantiation — otherwise it's an expensive halo with borrowed claims.

**Fit with the barrier-repair thesis:** Indirect. Biopsy data (TNS) shows improved barrier proteins (filaggrin, claudin-1) and ECM, which aligns with "repair/renew," but our settled core is a **ceramide-led barrier-repair cream**. Conditioned media is better positioned as a **premium supporting "renewal" serum** or a **post-procedure recovery** product than as the barrier hero. Its strongest real-world niche is post-laser/microneedling recovery.

**Longevity-relevance note:** This is one of the few topicals with direct mechanistic ties to recognized **hallmarks of aging** — cellular senescence (↓H2A.J), loss of proteostasis (↑proteasome/autophagy), and ECM/stem-cell signaling. That makes it narratively powerful for a "skin-longevity" brand. But the longevity framing must not become a structure/function (drug) claim; keep it to "supports the skin's natural renewal processes," not "reverses cellular aging."

**Recommended next steps if pursued:** (1) shortlist 2–3 suppliers and demand quantified, bioactive secretome + safety/QC dossiers; (2) decide form (fibroblast CM = best-evidenced; exosomes = trendiest but weakest independent data); (3) position as supporting renewal / post-procedure, not the barrier hero; (4) budget for our own finished-product testing before any efficacy claim. ## Evidence verification (adversarial)

*Verification pass: 2026-06-19. Method: each headline efficacy claim and its load-bearing citation was checked against the primary record (PMC/PubMed) or, where paywalled, against PubMed metadata + an independent systematic review. The question asked of every claim: is the evidence **human AND plain-topical (barrier intact)**, or is it quietly borrowed from in-vitro, animal, or device-delivered (microneedle/laser) data?*

### Overall evidence grade: **B− (for fibroblast-CM / TNS only); C-to-D for everything else**

There is no single grade for "conditioned media" because the category is not one thing. Graded by form:

- **Human fibroblast CM (TNS / TNS Advanced+): B−.** This is the only sub-category with genuine human, plain-topical, vehicle/placebo-controlled RCT evidence (Mehta 2008 n=60; Naughton 2022 n=68 cross-over with biopsies). It clears the bar for "moderate human-topical evidence." It is held *below* an A and shaded to B− because (i) **every controlled trial is industry-funded** (SkinMedica / Allergan / AbbVie / Histogen — material conflicts), (ii) **no independent group has replicated** the controlled results, (iii) **effect sizes are modest** (fine lines ≈ −31%, most other measures single digits to low-20s %), and (iv) an independent systematic review (Quinlan et al. 2023, *J Cosmet Dermatol*, 33 studies / only 9 controlled) found topical GF effects real but small and of unknown durability past 6 months.
- **ADSC-CM, MSC-CM (cord/cord-blood), bone-marrow CM: C.** Human data exists but the better-performing human studies are **microneedle- or laser-delivered** (Liang 2022; the Indonesian ADSC split-face RCT) — i.e. the barrier is deliberately breached, which answers a *different* question than a leave-on does. Plain-topical support is in-vitro, rodent, or small cosmetic-grade.
- **Cord-lining CM / PTT-6 (CALECIM): C−/D.** The supporting proliferation data is **in-vitro and manufacturer-run** (CellResearch Corp). No independent plain-topical human RCT located.
- **Exosome / EV fractions: D.** Independent reviewers (Forman Taub 2024; *Int J Mol Sci* 2025) are explicit: <20 clinical trials total, almost none plain-topical aesthetic, no production standardization, no FDA-approved products. Essentially unproven topically; trades on hype.

### What verified cleanly (claims that hold)

- **Mehta 2008** — real; *J Drugs Dermatol* 2008;7(9):864–871; double-blind, randomized, vehicle-controlled, n=60, TNS Recovery Complex. Accurately represented. (TNS is **neonatal foreskin** fibroblast CM — consistent with the dossier's forms table.)
- **Naughton 2022** — real and the **best-designed** study in the category; content (24-wk, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled cross-over, n=68/66 completed, fine lines −30.9% / coarse −12.7% / roughness −22.9% / photodamage −9.3% / sagging −7.8% / hyperpigmentation −6.3%, biopsy H2A.J↓ / collagen IV↑ / decorin↑ / elastic-fibre regeneration, self-perceived age −6 yrs, Allergan/AbbVie/Histogen conflicts) **all confirmed**. Active arm n=45 / placebo n=23 completers is internally consistent with the safety section's "4/45 active."
- **Kim 2020** (in-vitro, hPL vs FBS BM-MSC-CM: 39 vs 25 proteins, higher VEGF/TGF-β1/HGF, greater proliferation/migration/collagen+elastin) — confirmed, **in-vitro only** as stated.
- **Kwon 2016** (BM-MSC-CdM, UVB SKH-1 hairless mice, 8 wk, MMP-1↓, procollagen↑, hydration/TEWL) — confirmed real (PMID 26577060); correctly fenced as animal, with the right caveat about hairless-mouse permeability.
- **Liang 2022** (hUC-MSC-CM **+ microneedling**, split-face RCT, n=30/28, 5 sessions; CM beat saline on melanin/wrinkles/pores/elasticity) — confirmed; the authors themselves flag small sample, no histology, and CM stability/repeatability — exactly as the dossier reports. Correctly placed in the "barrier breached → not plain topical" tier.
- **Forman Taub 2024** and the systematic/EV reviews — the skeptical quotes ("clinical studies with EVs in aesthetics are very few"; "<20 clinical trials"; "no FDA-approved exosome products"; growth-factor effects historically misattributed when products were later found to contain EVs) are **accurately quoted**.
- **Tier discipline overall** — the dossier's central analytical move (separating plain-topical from microneedle/laser-delivered evidence, and refusing to let device-delivered results substantiate leave-on claims) is correct and is the single most important thing the document gets right.

### Main caveats / defects found

1. **Bibliographic accuracy was poor even though the studies are real.** Four wrong journals and one wrong author were found and corrected in-line:
   - Ref 2 / Naughton in-text: was *Clin Cosmet Investig Dermatol* 15:2829–2847 → actually **Dermatology and Therapy** (doi:10.1007/s13555-022-00844-5).
   - Ref 6 / Kim 2020 in-text (Tier 1): was *Tissue Eng Regen Med* → actually **Int J Stem Cells**.
   - Ref 8 / Forman Taub: was *J Clin Aesthet Dermatol* → actually **Frontiers in Medicine** 2024;11:1443963 (PMID 39473496).
   - Ref 1 author: was "Phinney/ et al." → actually **Alquraisy A, et al.** ("Phinney" is a different, unrelated MSC researcher — looks like a misremembered name; this is the kind of error that erodes trust in the whole reference list).
   - Fitzpatrick & Rostan 2003: journal/page is genuinely **contested** across secondary sources (*Dermatol Surg* 29(5):491–495 vs *J Cosmet Laser Ther* 5(1):25–34, PMID 12745596) — flagged, not silently asserted.
2. **The closing "no details un-corroborated" reassurance was false** and has been corrected. The dossier's content was honest; its citation hygiene was not. Anyone citing this dossier downstream must re-pull primaries.
3. **All controlled human evidence for the whole category traces to one corporate lineage (TNS).** This is a single-point-of-failure for the entire "it works topically" claim. No independent replication was found.
4. **Effect sizes are modest and the dossier occasionally lets "High" plausibility read as strong proof.** The headline visible-photoaging benefit is real but small (single-digit to ~30%); independent review puts texture <50% and fine lines <35%. Softened in the Hallmarks table.
5. **Durability is unknown beyond ~6 months** (per Quinlan 2023) — not stated in the dossier; worth noting before any "long-lasting renewal" copy.

### Claims that were softened / should stay soft

- **Photoaging row in §3** split into "fine lines & texture" (High, but modest effect sizes shown) vs "tone/hyperpigmentation" (Low–Moderate; −6.3% only; stronger results are device-delivered). *(Edited.)*
- **"Tone" should not be marketed as a topical CM benefit** on the strength of CM data — the credible tone results are microneedle-delivered.
- **Penetration** — already well-hedged in §5; keep it. Do not let any future copy imply intact-skin delivery of whole growth factors/EVs is established.
- **Anti-senescence / proteostasis / "hallmarks of aging" framing (§2, §11)** — mechanistically real in explants/3-D models but **not** demonstrated as a clinical longevity outcome. Keep strictly to "supports the skin's natural renewal," never "reverses cellular aging" (also a drug-claim risk).
- **CALECIM / PTT-6 and all exosome claims** — keep labelled in-vitro/manufacturer/hype; do not upgrade.

### Net verdict

The dossier's **reasoning, skepticism, and conclusion are sound** — its B-conditional/"fibroblast-CM-only, skip-the-exosome-hype" verdict is exactly where the evidence lands, and it is unusually honest about industry funding and the microneedle-vs-topical trap. The defect is **execution-level citation accuracy** (wrong journals/author, plus an over-confident "all corroborated" note). With the corrections above, the document is defensible. Bottom line for the brand: this is **B-grade evidence at best, and only for fibroblast CM** — adequate to justify a cautious, well-substantiated premium "renewal / post-procedure" product, **not** adequate to make any standalone topical efficacy claim without our own finished-formula testing.
