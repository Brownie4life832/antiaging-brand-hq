# Ingredient: Reprogramming / Klotho-Mimetic Frontier Actives — Yamanaka-Factor concepts (OSK/OSKM), Metadichol® (policosanol nano-emulsion), Klotho (INCI: **none as a class — mostly not cosmetic ingredients**)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⬜ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ☑ rejected (as a *frontier reprogramming* play) / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Experimental / frontier & pharma-adjacent longevity actives · **Slug:** `reprogramming-klotho-actives`

> **Brutally honest headline up front.** This "ingredient" is really three loosely-related marketing umbrellas that get sold together as the "cellular reprogramming / klotho" anti-aging frontier:
> 1. **Yamanaka-factor / partial-reprogramming biology** (OCT4-SOX2-KLF4 ± c-MYC = "OSK/OSKM"). This is **genuinely excellent, Nature/Cell-tier science** — and it is **gene therapy** (viral vectors, doxycycline-inducible transgenes, mRNA) or **lab small-molecule cocktails**, delivered to *cells, mice, or eyes*, **never as a topical cosmetic**. It carries a real **teratoma / cancer / loss-of-cell-identity risk** and has **zero** human topical cosmetic evidence. No INCI. Not formulable.
> 2. **Metadichol®** (a patented nano-emulsion of **policosanol** long-chain alcohols, ~85% C28 octacosanol), marketed as a "natural ligand that expresses Yamanaka factors and the klotho gene." The reprogramming/klotho claims rest **almost entirely on a single self-funded author (P.R. Raghavan, Nanorx Inc.)**, in-vitro qPCR, often in low-tier journals — a textbook **low-evidence, high-conflict** signal. The *underlying* policosanol has only modest, ordinary topical data (sebum reduction, mild wound-healing/antioxidant).
> 3. **Klotho** itself — a ~130 kDa "longevity protein." A topical "Klotho serum" (Gail Humble / Klotho Skin) has **one small open-label, uncontrolled human pilot (n=16, JDD 2025)**, but the product is **stem-cell-conditioned medium + growth factors**, not the klotho protein delivered into skin (a 130 kDa protein does not penetrate the stratum corneum).
>
> **Net:** the *real* reprogramming science is not a cosmetic and may never safely be one topically; the *cosmetic* products flying the "reprogramming/klotho" flag are either single-author/self-funded (Metadichol) or thinly-evidenced rebrands of growth-factor/conditioned-media science (Klotho Skin). This dossier exists mostly to let the brand **speak intelligently about the frontier without buying the hype** — and to point at the *legitimate* cosmetic neighbour (senotherapeutic peptides like OS-01) that actually has controlled human topical data.

## 2. Class & mechanism

**A. Yamanaka-factor partial reprogramming (the real science).**
Takahashi & Yamanaka (2006/2007) showed that forcing four transcription factors (OSKM) converts adult somatic cells into induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs). The aging insight came later: **transient, sub-pluripotency ("partial") expression of OSK can reset age-associated epigenetic marks** — DNA-methylation patterns, histone modifications — toward a younger state **without erasing cell identity.** Mechanistically:
- OSK drives **active DNA demethylation** via **TET enzymes (TET1/TET2)** and TDG-mediated base excision, restoring "youthful" methylation at age-altered loci. [1]
- It reverses transcriptomic drift, improves regenerative capacity, and lowers epigenetic-clock age.
- **The danger is the same machinery:** push too far/too long and cells **dedifferentiate**, lose identity, and form **teratomas / cancers** (c-MYC is a frank oncogene; full OSKM can kill mice in days). [4][7] The entire field is about finding a **narrow safe window** between rejuvenation and tumorigenesis.

**B. Chemical reprogramming.** Small-molecule cocktails (HDAC inhibitors, GSK3 inhibitors, etc.) reproduce *part* of the OSK effect without viral genes — easier to dose, but still **lab/therapeutic** tools, not cosmetic actives. [3]

**C. Metadichol® → "expresses Yamanaka factors + klotho."** The vendor's mechanistic claim is that policosanol nano-emulsion acts as an **inverse agonist of the nuclear vitamin-D receptor (VDR)** and a pleiotropic gene-expression modulator that, in cell culture, raises OCT4/SOX2/KLF4/NANOG and KLOTHO mRNA. [9][10][11] **This is an in-vitro gene-expression claim, not demonstrated rejuvenation, and not from topical skin use.**

**D. Klotho.** α-Klotho is a bona fide longevity protein: it co-receptors **FGF23 signaling**, suppresses **IGF-1/insulin and Wnt** over-activation, raises **FOXO/SOD antioxidant defenses**, and its decline tracks aging. [12] In *skin*, the rationale is antioxidant/anti-senescence support. **But klotho is a large protein that does not cross intact skin**, so "topical klotho" is mechanistically a stretch unless reframed as "ingredients that *upregulate* skin klotho" (the conditioned-media/growth-factor route). [12][13]

## 4. Evidence review by tier ← core of this dossier

**Overarching honesty flag.** There are **two completely separate evidence worlds** here, and the marketing fuses them:
- **World 1 — Reprogramming biology:** strong-to-spectacular science, but **in cells, mice, and eyes via gene therapy / lab cocktails.** Human data are early *therapeutic* trials (gene therapy / injectables), **not cosmetics.** **Zero human topical-cosmetic evidence.**
- **World 2 — The cosmetic products:** Metadichol (single-author, self-funded, in-vitro) and Klotho Skin (one small open-label pilot). **No vehicle-controlled topical RCT** establishes any "reprogramming" benefit from a cream.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro & ex-vivo

**Reprogramming (legit but not cosmetic):**
- **Takahashi & Yamanaka (2006), *Cell*; (2007) human cells** [6] — foundational: OSKM converts fibroblasts to iPSCs. Establishes the factors; also establishes that *full* reprogramming = pluripotency = tumorigenic if uncontrolled.
- **Olova et al. (2019), *Aging Cell*** [2] — partial reprogramming of human fibroblasts: **epigenetic age declines steadily *before* loss of somatic identity**, defining the "safe window" concept. In-vitro proof that age and identity can be uncoupled.
- **Yang et al. / chemical reprogramming (2023), *Aging (Albany NY)*** [3] — screened small-molecule cocktails (**VC6TF**, "7c," etc.) that restore youthful transcriptome and nucleocytoplasmic compartmentalization in aged/senescent **human** cells in days, without reaching pluripotency. In-vitro; cocktails are pharmacological reagents.

**Metadichol (low-quality, in-vitro, single-author):**
- **Raghavan (2022), *Medical Research Archives* / related** [9][10] — Metadichol (1 pg–100 ng/mL) raised **OCT4 ~4-fold, SOX2 ~2.5-fold, KLF4 ~3.5-fold, NANOG ~1.3-fold** in fibroblasts (higher folds in cancer lines) by qRT-PCR + some western blots. **Single author (P.R. Raghavan, Nanorx Inc., the product's owner)**, no independent replication, limitations not discussed, journal not strongly indexed. Treat as **hypothesis-generating at best.**
- **Raghavan (2018/2019), klotho papers** [11] — reported ~70-fold KLOTHO upregulation over 8 days in cancer cell lines. Same single-author/self-funded pattern; in-vitro only.

**Klotho (the most skin-relevant in-vitro signal):**
- **Klotho-containing conditioned medium, UVA fibroblast study** [13 companion] — 1% klotho-containing media reduced **UVA-induced oxidative DNA damage** and endogenous DNA damage in fibroblasts; ↑collagen-III after UVB when combined with growth factors. In-vitro; uses conditioned medium, not isolated topical klotho.

**Bottom line Tier 1:** the *reprogramming* in-vitro science is real and important (World 1). The *Metadichol/klotho cosmetic* in-vitro data are weak (single-author/self-funded or conditioned-media), not the OSK gene-therapy science the branding implies.

### Tier 2 — Animal

**Reprogramming (the famous results — all gene therapy/transgenic, none topical):**
- **Ocampo, Reddy, Izpisua Belmonte et al. (2016), *Cell*** [4] — *the* in-vivo proof of concept. **Cyclic OSKM (2 days on / 5 days off)** in progeroid (LAKI) mice extended median lifespan ~**33%** and ameliorated aging hallmarks, **without** teratomas during the model's lifespan. Crucially also showed **continuous OSKM is lethal** (sickness/death within days, then teratomas) — defining the safety knife-edge. Doxycycline-inducible transgene, **systemic, not topical.**
- **Lu, Sinclair et al. (2020), *Nature*** [1] — **AAV-delivered OSK** (c-MYC omitted) to **retinal ganglion cells** restored youthful DNA-methylation, regenerated optic-nerve axons (RGC survival ~doubled), and **reversed glaucoma/age-related vision loss** in mice. Required TET1/TET2. **No tumors after 10–18 months** of induction. Landmark — but it is **intra-ocular gene therapy**, not skincare.
- **Browder, Izpisua Belmonte et al. (2022), *Nature Aging*** [5] — long-term (7–10 mo) and short-term (1 mo) **doxycycline-inducible OSKM in transgenic mice**; *epidermal* partial reprogramming conferred "healing-like" states, accelerated **re-epithelialization**, reduced scarring (incl. in hyperglycemic mice). Reported safe in wild-type mice over the windows tested. **The most skin-relevant animal study — and it is a transgenic gene-expression system, not a cream.**
- **Gene-therapy lifespan study (2023, bioRxiv/PMC)** [7] — AAV-OSK in aged wild-type mice; reported lifespan extension / reversal of age markers. Preprint-tier; systemic gene therapy.

**Metadichol/policosanol animals:** scattered vendor-associated reports; no rigorous independent animal skin-aging study. Policosanol oral animal data exist for lipids/antioxidant, not skin rejuvenation. [16]

### Tier 3 — Human, ORAL / systemic (exists for the *neighbours*, not for topical reprogramming)

- **Reprogramming in humans:** moving into **therapeutic** trials (e.g., **Life Biosciences** AAV-OSK for optic-neuropathy/vision, entering clinical development ~2024–2025; various biotech IND programs). These are **gene-therapy injections under medical supervision**, with the field's central caution = **oncogenic risk**. **No oral or topical cosmetic human reprogramming data.**
- **Metadichol / NANO SOMA (oral supplement):** marketed with sweeping claims across dozens of diseases (viral infection, cancer markers, diabetes, etc.), almost all **single-author/self-funded, small, in-vitro or open-label.** The breadth of disease claims from one funded author is itself a **major red flag** for evidence quality, not a strength.
- **Policosanol (oral):** decades of cardiovascular-lipid trials — **famously positive in Cuban-origin studies and largely null in independent replications** — a cautionary tale about source-dependent, conflicted evidence that maps directly onto the Metadichol situation. [16]

### Tier 4 — Human, TOPICAL

**Reprogramming (OSK/OSKM/chemical):** **None. Zero.** No RCT, pilot, or case series of topically-applied Yamanaka factors / partial reprogramming on human skin. **It is not technically possible** to deliver functional OSK gene therapy through a cosmetic, and doing so would be an unapproved gene-therapy drug, not a cosmetic.

**Metadichol (topical):** sold as a topical gel/spray/cream with skin claims (wound healing, barrier, "younger appearance"), but **no peer-reviewed, controlled human topical trial** substantiates a reprogramming or anti-aging benefit. Claims are vendor/marketing.

**Klotho serum — the *only* human topical cosmetic study in this whole space:**
- **Topical Klotho Anti-Aging Serum pilot (JDD, 2025; Gail Humble / Klotho Skin)** [13] — **open-label, uncontrolled, n=16 women aged 30–60** with mild–moderate photoaging; serum **twice daily ×12 weeks**; statistically significant (p<0.001) improvements from baseline in fine lines/wrinkles, photodamage, erythema, dyschromia; "exceptional" safety.
  - **Critical limitations:** **no vehicle control, no blinding/randomization, tiny n, baseline-only comparison** (regression-to-mean and placebo effects uncontrolled); **industry-affiliated**; and — decisively — **the active is "cell-conditioned medium" from genetically-modified mesenchymal stem cells** (25%/15%/10% in serum/eye/night), i.e., **a growth-factor/secretome product**, *not* purified klotho protein delivered into skin. The "klotho" framing is largely **branding on a conditioned-media/growth-factor base.** A 130 kDa protein won't penetrate intact stratum corneum. [13]

**What is actually proven for TOPICAL human cosmetic use of "reprogramming/klotho":** essentially **nothing rigorous.** The single human topical study is an **uncontrolled n=16 open-label pilot of a conditioned-media serum** — not evidence of epigenetic reprogramming or of klotho-protein delivery. Everything else is World-1 gene-therapy/cell science borrowed as marketing.

**Contrast — the legitimate cosmetic neighbour:** the **senotherapeutic peptide OS-01 (Prodermic/OneSkin)** has a **double-blind, vehicle-controlled clinical study** (Zonari et al., 2024, *J Cosmet Dermatol*) plus human-skin-model work showing reduced senescence/biological-age markers. [14] If the brand wants a credible "cellular longevity" actic with controlled human topical data, **that is the class to use — not reprogramming/klotho.**

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **OSK / Yamanaka factors:** N/A as a cosmetic — these are **genes** requiring viral vectors or transfection to enter the nucleus and be expressed. **No cosmetic delivery exists or is plausible.** mRNA/LNP delivery is being explored *therapeutically*, not cosmetically.
- **Klotho protein (~130 kDa):** **does not penetrate** intact stratum corneum (orders of magnitude above the ~500 Da practical limit). "Topical klotho" benefit, if real, must come from **other small molecules / growth factors** in the formula acting at the surface/upper epidermis, or from *upregulating* endogenous skin klotho — neither of which is the same as delivering klotho. [12][13]
- **Metadichol / policosanol:** nano-emulsion *does* improve dispersibility and skin contact of long-chain alcohols; octacosanol/policosanol can sit in and on the barrier (emollient, sebum-modulating). [15] But "penetrates cells, binds DNA, regulates genes" (vendor language) is **not established for topical human skin** and would, if true, raise drug-not-cosmetic questions.
- **No published human topical PK / penetration data** for any "reprogramming" claim in this class.

## 6. Formulation

Split by what is/ isn't formulable.

- **OSK / partial reprogramming:** **not formulable as a cosmetic.** Documented here only to say so.
- **Chemical reprogramming cocktails:** components (valproic acid, CHIR99021, tranylcypromine, forskolin, etc.) are **pharmacological reagents**; **not cosmetic actives**; forskolin appears cosmetically only at low levels for unrelated claims. Not a route to "reprogramming in a jar."
- **Metadichol / policosanol (the only genuinely formulable piece):**
  - **Use level:** policosanol/octacosanol typically **0.1–5%** in cosmetics (emollient, sebum-balancing); a topical study used **2–5% policosanol** (sebum ↓~11% at 2%, ~27% at 5%, safe over 7 days). [15]
  - **pH / stability:** waxy, lipophilic long-chain alcohols; stable; nano-emulsification needed for aqueous systems; standard emulsion handling.
  - **Pairings:** behaves like a fatty-alcohol emollient/co-emulsifier; pairs fine with barrier lipids, niacinamide, humectants.
  - **Sensory:** waxy/emollient slip; neutral.
  - **Reality:** you can absolutely formulate *policosanol* — but then you have an ordinary emollient/sebum active, **not** a "Yamanaka-factor/klotho reprogramming" active. The branding, not the chemistry, is doing the heavy lifting.
- **Klotho/conditioned-media route:** formulable as a **growth-factor/secretome** product (like other CM/exosome cosmetics) — but that's a *different* dossier (growth factors; see brand glossary "vet carefully"), with its own evidence and claim issues.

## 7. Safety

- **OSK / reprogramming — the headline risk is oncogenesis.** Forced reprogramming can cause **teratomas, cancer, organ failure, and loss of cell identity**; **continuous OSKM is lethal in mice within days.** [4][7] c-MYC is an oncogene (hence OSK-only variants). Even the optimistic studies emphasize a **narrow safe window.** This is **not** a "gentle" mechanism — it is the same machinery that makes stem cells and tumors. Categorically inappropriate to put anywhere near a consumer cosmetic.
- **Metadichol / policosanol:** **policosanol/octacosanol are generally well-tolerated** orally and topically in the modest data that exist (the 2–5% topical study reported local safety over 7 days). [15] The *safety* of policosanol isn't the problem; the **efficacy/reprogramming claims** are. NANO SOMA's sweeping multi-disease marketing is the concern (overclaiming), not acute toxicity.
- **Klotho-CM serum:** the pilot reported "exceptional" tolerability (n=16, 12 wks). [13] As with all conditioned-media/growth-factor cosmetics, watch for **sourcing/standardization, potential proliferative-signaling theoretical concerns**, and substantiation.
- **Sun / pregnancy:** no specific photosensitivity for policosanol; **reprogramming/gene-therapy concepts are categorically contraindicated** (and not cosmetics). Growth-factor/CM products are commonly cautioned in pregnancy by brands out of conservatism — flag for review.
- **Sensitization / HRIPT:** policosanol low-risk; no class-wide cosmetic sensitization data for the "reprogramming" actives because they aren't cosmetics.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

**This section largely settles the brand question.**

1. **"Reprogramming" claims are drug/gene-therapy claims.** "Reverses cellular age," "resets the epigenetic clock," "expresses Yamanaka factors in your skin," "partial reprogramming" all assert **structure/function changes** (and, for OSK, an **unapproved gene therapy**). There is no cosmetic monograph for this; saying it about a product is **drug (indeed biologic) territory** and unsubstantiable. Hard no.
2. **"Klotho gene expression" / "klotho-mimetic" are structure/function (drug-adjacent) claims.** Claiming a cream "increases klotho gene expression" or "mimics the longevity protein" implies altering gene expression / physiology → needs drug-level substantiation. The n=16 open-label pilot does **not** substantiate a klotho-mechanism claim.
3. **Metadichol's claim stack is a compliance minefield.** The brand-name actic comes wrapped in disease and gene-expression claims; adopting it would import those claims and their substantiation gap.
4. **What *is* sayable** — only if the brand used the formulable pieces honestly:
   - ✅ For **policosanol/octacosanol**: "emollient," "supports the look of a balanced, comfortable skin surface," "helps skin feel nourished." (Standard cosmetic emollient/sebum language — *not* reprogramming.)
   - ⚠️ **Forbidden:** "Yamanaka factors," "reprogramming," "resets/reverses epigenetic age," "expresses klotho," "klotho-mimetic," "rejuvenates cells," "younger cells." Each is an unsubstantiated structure/function or unapproved-drug claim.
   - **Editorial only:** the brand may *discuss* reprogramming as **the research frontier** ("scientists are exploring partial cellular reprogramming — currently gene therapy in the lab, not skincare"), clearly framed as **not our product.** This is the only legitimate touchpoint and a good credibility/education play.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **OSK / reprogramming / chemical cocktails:** sourced only as **gene-therapy reagents / research chemicals / pharmaceutical-grade compounds** — **not** cosmetic raw materials. Cost irrelevant; cannot be lawfully/appropriately formulated into a consumer cosmetic.
- **Metadichol® / NANO SOMA:** sold as a **proprietary branded supplement/topical** by Nanorx and licensees (single-source, proprietary, premium-priced). Adopting it ties the brand to one vendor's contested evidence and claim stack. **Plain policosanol/octacosanol**, by contrast, is a **cheap, widely-available commodity** cosmetic emollient (sugarcane/rice/wheat-derived) — but then it's just policosanol.
- **Klotho serum:** proprietary (Klotho Skin / conditioned-media); growth-factor/CM raw materials are a separate, vet-carefully supply category.
- **Cost tier:** policosanol = **low**; Metadichol/klotho-CM = **premium/proprietary** with weak ROI on evidence.

The "reprogramming/klotho" *skincare* on the market is **marketing arbitrage**: borrowing Nobel-tier vocabulary to sell conditioned media, growth factors, or policosanol.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️→❌ **SKIP as a "reprogramming / klotho-mimetic frontier" play. Keep only as (a) editorial/credibility material and (b) an optional plain-policosanol emollient with no reprogramming claims.**

- **Why skip the frontier framing (non-negotiable):**
  - **OSK / partial reprogramming is gene therapy, not a cosmetic** — no INCI, not formulable, real **teratoma/cancer** risk, **zero** human topical-cosmetic evidence. Any "reprogramming" claim is an unapproved drug/biologic claim. [1][4][5]
  - **Metadichol's** reprogramming/klotho story is **single-author, self-funded, in-vitro** with sweeping disease claims — fails the brand's evidence-over-hype bar and imports a claim-compliance minefield. [9][10][11]
  - **Topical "klotho"** has only an **uncontrolled n=16 open-label pilot** of a **conditioned-media/growth-factor** serum mis-framed as klotho-protein delivery; klotho can't penetrate skin. [12][13]
- **The one salvageable, honest piece:** **policosanol/octacosanol** is a fine, cheap, well-tolerated **emollient / sebum-balancing** ingredient with modest real data [15][16]. If it ever earns a slot, it goes in as **"policosanol emollient,"** with **standard cosmetic language and absolutely no reprogramming/klotho claims.** That is a *supporting commodity*, not a hero, and doesn't need the frontier branding at all.
- **Longevity relevance:** **High in the science, ~nil in a credible bottle.** Reprogramming is the headline act of longevity biology — which makes it **superb editorial fuel** ("here's the real frontier — gene therapy in labs — and here's why we won't fake it in a cream") and a **trust-builder** that contrasts the brand with hype merchants. Do **not** turn it into product copy or a SKU.
- **Strategic alternative (use this instead):** if the brand wants a defensible "cellular longevity / senescence" angle with **controlled human topical data**, use the **senotherapeutic peptide OS-01** (double-blind, vehicle-controlled study; human-skin-model biological-age data) [14] and/or evidenced senescence-adjacent actives — framed with honest "supports / visibly" language. Reference reprogramming/klotho as the *frontier we respect but don't oversell.*

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

_Verified 2026-06-19 by independent reading/cross-checking of primary sources: Lu/Sinclair 2020 Nature [1] (full PMC extraction — AAV-OSK, RGC, TET1/2, doxycycline, no-tumor safety, explicit ocular/CNS scope); Ocampo 2016 Cell [4] (cyclic OSKM ~33% lifespan, continuous-OSKM lethality/teratoma); Browder 2022 Nature Aging [5] (transgenic doxycycline OSKM, epidermal wound-healing); Olova 2019 [2] safe-window; Yang/Sinclair 2023 chemical cocktails [3]; Paine 2024 review [8] (tumorigenicity risk); Raghavan Metadichol/klotho papers [9][10][11] (single-author/self-funded, in-vitro qPCR, fold-changes extracted, journal-quality flagged); Klotho Skin JDD 2025 pilot + vendor tech page [13] (n=16 open-label, conditioned-media composition confirmed); OS-01 controlled study [14] as the cosmetic contrast. Goal: ensure no World-1 gene-therapy/cell result is laundered into a topical-cosmetic benefit, and that evidence-quality flags on Metadichol/klotho are explicit._

### Overall evidence grade for TOPICAL COSMETIC "reprogramming/klotho": **D–/F.**
- Topical *reprogramming*: **F** — impossible to deliver, none attempted, real cancer risk.
- Topical *Metadichol reprogramming/klotho*: **F** — single-author/self-funded, in-vitro only.
- Topical *klotho serum*: **D–** — one uncontrolled open-label n=16 pilot of a conditioned-media product mis-framed as klotho.
- Underlying *reprogramming biology* (non-cosmetic): **A** (Nature/Cell-tier, multi-lab) — but it's gene therapy.
- Underlying *policosanol emollient*: **C** — modest, ordinary cosmetic data.

### Load-bearing caveats (verified)
1. **Reprogramming = gene therapy with teratoma/cancer risk; not a cosmetic; no INCI; zero human topical evidence.** Continuous OSKM is lethal in mice [4] — the opposite of "gentle." 
2. **The marketed "reprogramming/klotho" cosmetics do not contain OSK or deliver klotho protein** — Metadichol is policosanol with single-author claims [9–11]; Klotho Skin is conditioned media/growth factors [13].
3. **Klotho (~130 kDa) cannot penetrate skin** — "topical klotho" is mechanistically reframed (upregulation/CM), not delivery. [12][13]
4. **Policosanol is the only legitimately formulable piece** — benign emollient/sebum active, no reprogramming claim defensible. [15][16]
5. **Single self-funded author + sweeping multi-disease claims (Metadichol)** = a recognized low-evidence/high-conflict pattern; do not treat fold-change qPCR as efficacy.
