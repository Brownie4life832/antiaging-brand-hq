# Ingredient: Sugar-alcohol humectants — Sorbitol, Xylitol, Maltitol, Mannitol, Aquaxyl (INCI: Sorbitol; Xylitol; Maltitol; Mannitol; Xylitylglucoside / Anhydroxylitol / Xylitol)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Humectants & hydration actives (polyols / sugar alcohols)

> **One-line honest summary:** Sugar alcohols are workhorse humectants — cheap, exceptionally well-tolerated, and supported by *human* topical data (small, mostly combination-with-glycerol studies) for **hydration and barrier support** (especially **xylitol** and the **Aquaxyl** trio). But they are not anti-aging actives in the collagen/senescence sense: the "anti-aging" story is essentially a *hydration + barrier + soothing + mild antimicrobial* story, with a thin extra layer of in-vitro differentiation/aquaporin signaling. **Xylitol/Aquaxyl** carry the best evidence; **sorbitol/maltitol/mannitol** are competent humectants with much weaker skin-specific clinical proof. Treat as a **supporting/base-layer hydration system**, never a hero.

---

## 1. Identity

This dossier covers a *family* of polyols (sugar alcohols) used as humectants, plus one branded multi-polyol blend:

| Name | INCI | CAS | Structure | MW (g/mol) | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sorbitol** (D-glucitol) | Sorbitol | 50-70-4 | Hexahydric alcohol (6 carbons, 6 –OH) | 182.17 | Reduction of glucose; naturally in Rosaceae berries (mountain ash, apples, pears) |
| **Xylitol** | Xylitol | 87-99-0 | Pentahydric alcohol (5 carbons, 5 –OH) | 152.15 | From xylan/birch & corncob hemicellulose |
| **Maltitol** | Maltitol | 585-88-6 | Disaccharide alcohol (4-O-α-D-glucopyranosyl-D-glucitol) | 344.31 | Hydrogenation of maltose (starch-derived) |
| **Mannitol** | Mannitol | 69-65-8 | Hexahydric alcohol (stereoisomer of sorbitol) | 182.17 | Marine algae, manna ash exudate, fungi; industrial from fructose |
| **Aquaxyl™** (Seppic) | Xylitylglucoside (+) Anhydroxylitol (+) Xylitol | (blend) | Xylitol + glucose-derived xyloside trio | mixed | Glucose + xylitol (plant-derived, biotech) |

- **Chemical class:** Polyols / sugar alcohols (polyhydroxy alcohols). Mannitol and sorbitol are **diastereomers** differing only in the orientation of one hydroxyl; xylitol is the same chemistry one carbon shorter. [1]
- **Physical form:** White crystalline powders / free-flowing granules (or aqueous syrups, e.g. "sorbitol 70%"). All highly water-soluble; mildly sweet. [1]
- **Aquaxyl specifics:** A patented blend marketed by Seppic positioned as a "moisturizing + restructuring" active that "harmonizes the skin's hydrous flow"; xylitylglucoside is the dominant component (glucoside of xylitol). Typical use **3–4%**. [9][12][14]
- **Why grouped:** Structurally and functionally near-identical small polyhydroxy humectants; the 2025 CIR safety review formally assessed mannitol, sorbitol, and xylitol *together* on structural similarity grounds. [1]

## 2. Class & mechanism

Sugar alcohols are **humectants**: small polyhydroxy molecules whose multiple –OH groups hydrogen-bond water, drawing it from the environment and deeper skin into the stratum corneum (SC) and holding it there. Mechanistically the family does several things, in descending order of evidence strength:

1. **Hydrogen-bond water binding (core humectant action).** Each hydroxyl coordinates water; one molecule binds several waters, creating a hydrated SC microenvironment. This is basic physical chemistry, shared with glycerol and shared with the skin's own **Natural Moisturizing Factor (NMF)** — of which polyols and small sugars are native components. [2][20]
2. **Barrier reinforcement / reduced TEWL.** By holding SC water and (for the Aquaxyl trio) reportedly stimulating barrier-lipid and protein synthesis, they reduce transepidermal water loss. Demonstrated in human use for the glycerol/xylitol combination and Aquaxyl. [4][9][12]
3. **Pro-differentiation signaling (in vitro).** Glycerol and especially **xylitol** upregulate epidermal differentiation/barrier genes (filaggrin, loricrin, involucrin, occludin) and activate MAPK signaling and (xylitol) PKCδ translocation in cultured keratinocytes — i.e., they are not purely "passive water sponges." [3][4]
4. **Aquaporin / water-channel modulation.** Aquaxyl is claimed (cosmetogenomics data, manufacturer) to upregulate **aquaporin-3 (AQP3)**, **claudin (CLDN)**, and **caspase-14** — channels/proteins governing water movement, tight junctions, and SC maturation. AQP3 is a legitimate, well-studied skin water/glycerol channel; the *Aquaxyl→AQP3* link is largely manufacturer/in-vitro. [9][14][16]
5. **Antioxidant (mannitol-specific).** **Mannitol is a recognized hydroxyl-radical (•OH) scavenger** — used precisely for this in HA dermal fillers (to protect HA from free-radical degradation) and explored with ectoine for photoprotection. Sorbitol/xylitol have weak/negligible direct antioxidant activity. [5][6][7]
6. **Microbiome / anti-biofilm (xylitol-specific).** **Xylitol inhibits *Staphylococcus aureus* glycocalyx/biofilm formation** and, with farnesol, selectively rebalances skin microflora without antibiotics — relevant to barrier-compromised/atopic skin. [10][11][13]
7. **Anti-irritant / anti-inflammatory.** Glycerol and xylitol blunt SLS-induced irritation (↓TEWL rise, ↓IL-1β/TNF-α, ↓inflammatory cell influx) in animal models. [8]

**Honest framing:** Items 1–2 are real and proven in humans. Items 3–4 are mechanistically plausible but rest on in-vitro/manufacturer data. Items 5–7 are specific to particular members (mannitol, xylitol) and mostly in-vitro/animal/adjacent-format.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** For sugar-alcohol humectants the human *topical* evidence is genuinely better than for many "longevity" actives — because the claim being tested (hydration/barrier) is modest and easy to measure. The strongest human data are for **xylitol (in a glycerol combination)** and for **Aquaxyl/xylitylglucoside**. **Sorbitol and maltitol have almost no skin-specific clinical literature** — they ride on (a) general humectant physics and (b) the family safety/efficacy umbrella. **Mannitol's** distinctive evidence is as an *antioxidant adjunct*, mostly in fillers, not as a facial anti-aging active.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell, biochemical, microbiology)

- **Páyer E. et al., 2018, *Experimental Dermatology* 27(3):280–284 (PMID 29520873).** Human epidermal keratinocytes treated with xylitol (0.0045–0.45%) and glycerol (0.0027–0.27%) at matched osmolarities. **Both polyols upregulated filaggrin, loricrin, involucrin, occludin** and stimulated **MAPK**; **xylitol additionally drove PKCδ translocation** (a differentiation promoter). Both showed anti-inflammatory activity in keratinocyte inflammation models. *Limitation: cultured cells; direct medium dosing, not transdermal; differential, not dramatic, effects.* [3]
- **Katsuyama M. et al., 2005 (Part 1), *Journal of Dermatological Science* 38(3) (PMID 15927813).** Screen of ~500 substances: **xylitol inhibited *S. aureus* glycocalyx (biofilm) formation**; farnesol dissolved fibrin and selectively suppressed *S. aureus* but spared *S. epidermidis*; combined they synergistically inhibited biofilm. *Limitation: in vitro microbiology; concentrations are culture-based.* [10]
- **Mannitol free-radical scavenging — André P., 2017, *International Journal of Cosmetic Science* 39(4) (review; DOI 10.1111/ics.12386).** Reviews mannitol as a **hydroxyl-radical scavenger**, the rationale for adding it to HA fillers (protects HA from oxidative degradation; reduces post-injection inflammation/swelling); cites limiting of oxidative DNA/lipid/protein damage. Roquette cell-free data report **~77% •OH inhibition at 2500 µg/mL, comparable to ascorbic acid**. *Limitation: review + cell-free/filler context; not facial topical efficacy; high concentrations.* [5][6]
- **Hyperosmotic-stress protection (keratinocytes).** Glycerol and xylitol protect keratinocytes against hyperosmotic stress (cited in the Hungarian group's body of work). *Limitation: in vitro.* [3]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)

- **Szél E. et al., 2015, *JEADV* 29(12) (PMID 26370610).** SLS-induced acute irritation, SKH-1 hairless mice. **10% glycerol and both xylitol concentrations inhibited the SLS-induced TEWL rise**, reduced dermal blood flow and leukocyte–endothelial interactions, improved hydration, and prevented lymphocyte accumulation. **↓IL-1β (10% glycerol), ↓TNF-α (10% glycerol and xylitol)**; higher polyol levels reduced SLS penetration into deeper SC. **Xylitol was effective at lower concentration than glycerol.** *Limitation: mouse model; acute chemical irritation, not chronological aging.* [8]
- **Mannitol in the imiquimod psoriasis model / glycerol psoriasis work (Hungarian group).** Glycerol improved lesion development in the imiquimod mouse psoriasis model. *Limitation: glycerol, not the sugar alcohols per se; disease model.* [3]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL / systemic (context, NOT skin efficacy)

- These polyols are mainstream **food sugar substitutes** (sorbitol, xylitol, maltitol, mannitol are GRAS / EU-approved sweeteners). The relevant human "oral" facts for a skincare brand are *safety* facts: high oral doses are **laxative/cause GI upset (osmotic)**, and **xylitol is dangerously toxic to dogs** (hypoglycemia/hepatic failure) — a household-safety note, not a skin issue. There is **no oral-to-skin anti-aging bridge** here; do not borrow "sugar substitute" data as skin efficacy. [1]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters)

**(a) Glycerol + Xylitol combination — the best controlled human skin data**

- **Korponyai C. et al., 2017, *Acta Dermato-Venereologica* 97(2):182–187 (PMID 27349297; DOI 10.2340/00015555-2493).** Within-subject controlled study, **n = 12** dry-skin volunteers (aged 50–60), three arm sites (untreated / vehicle / **5% glycerol + 5% xylitol**), twice daily for **14 days**, with biopsies. Results in the polyol-treated site vs control: **significantly increased hydration (p<0.05)**, **significantly reduced TEWL (p<0.05)**, **increased filaggrin-positive epidermal cells**, increased epidermal & dermal thickness, increased interdigitation index, and improved friction/elasticity (R0) parameters. **MMP-1 showed no significant change** (i.e., no anti-collagenase signal). *Limitations: small (n=12), combination (can't fully separate xylitol from glycerol), short, dry-skin-selected, single center; no restoration of elastic fibers/gross elasticity.* [4]

**(b) Aquaxyl / xylitylglucoside trio — manufacturer + one peer-reviewed comparison**

- **Leite e Silva V.R. et al., 2009, *Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology* 8(1):32–39 (PMID 19250164).** In-vivo comparison of humectant actives in hydrogels measured by corneometry/MoistureMeter. A formula with **4% Aquaxyl (xylitylglucoside trio) performed as well as urea and somewhat better than NMF-component or *Imperata cylindrica* extract** formulas (all with 5% glycerin base). *Limitations: small humectant-comparison design; Aquaxyl tested in a glycerin-containing base; modest journal; device-comparison focus.* [12]
- **Seppic/Aquaxyl manufacturer in-vivo data.** Claims for **3% Aquaxyl**: skin barrier reinforced in **24 h** (↓water loss); softer/smoother skin and smoothed fine lines at **28 days** with normalized desquamation; reported hand-study figures of **+~13.5% hydration at 24 h** and **−~14–15% TEWL at ~8–28 days**; cosmetogenomics showing **↑AQP3, ↑claudin, ↑caspase-14** and ↑HA/ceramide synthesis. *Limitations: manufacturer-generated, not independently peer-reviewed; methods/sample sizes not fully published; cosmetogenomic gene-expression endpoints ≠ clinical outcomes.* [9][12][14]

**(c) Xylitol in barrier-compromised / atopic skin**

- **Katsuyama M. et al., 2005 (Part 2), *Journal of Dermatological Science* 38(3):207–213 (PMID 15927814).** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled left/right study, **n = 17** dry-type atopic dermatitis patients; cream with **0.02% farnesol + 5% xylitol**, 1 week. **Skin conductance (hydration) significantly increased vs baseline (p=0.0001) and vs placebo (p=0.002)**; ***S. aureus* proportion significantly decreased (p=0.007 vs baseline; p=0.045 vs placebo)**; *S. epidermidis* unaffected. *Limitations: tiny (n=17), 1 week, combination with farnesol (can't isolate xylitol's hydration vs antimicrobial roles), AD population.* [11][13]

**(d) Mannitol in human skin (as an antioxidant adjunct, not a standalone facial active)**

- **Fontbonne A. et al., 2024, *Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology* 23(1):308–315 (DOI 10.1111/jocd.15893).** In-vivo, **n = 10 men**: cream with **0.1% ectoine + 0.1% mannitol ± UV filters**, applied twice daily over 3 days then irradiated. The active+UV-filter combination gave **significantly higher protection of three UV-altered natural defenses (squalene oxidation, catalase activity, trans-UCA preservation) vs UV filters alone**; in-vitro it preserved glutathione, reduced oxidative DNA strand breaks, and maintained Langerhans-cell function. *Limitations: tiny (n=10, men only), 3 days, ectoine+mannitol combination (mannitol's solo contribution unclear), photoprotection-adjunct context not anti-aging treatment.* [7]

**Bottom line by tier:** **Proven in humans → SC hydration ↑ and TEWL ↓ for xylitol (in glycerol combo) and Aquaxyl; filaggrin ↑ in one biopsy study; *S. aureus* ↓ for xylitol (with farnesol) in atopic skin.** **Plausible/in-vitro → differentiation-gene & aquaporin signaling, anti-irritant cytokine effects, mannitol antioxidant.** **NOT supported → collagen/ECM rebuilding (MMP-1 null), senescence, mitochondrial, glycation reversal, pigmentation.** Sorbitol and maltitol have essentially no standalone skin clinical data — they are competent humectants by physics and family analogy only.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Small and water-soluble**, but **highly hydrophilic and (for sorbitol/mannitol/maltitol) not membrane-permeant** — the same property that makes intracellular sorbitol an osmotic stressor in diabetes (it doesn't cross membranes well) means topically applied polyols largely act **at and within the stratum corneum**, which is exactly where a humectant should work. Deep dermal penetration is neither expected nor needed for the hydration/barrier mechanism. [17]
- **Xylitol/glycerol** can interact with **aquaglyceroporin (AQP3)** transport at the keratinocyte level (glycerol is a known AQP3 substrate; xylitol modulates AQP3 expression), giving a plausible route to *cellular* (not just surface) effects — but quantitative human transdermal data are absent. [3][16]
- **Maltitol** is the largest (disaccharide alcohol, MW 344) → most surface-confined; best thought of as a surface humectant/film-former, not an active that signals cells.
- **Practical read:** Bioavailability is essentially "stratum-corneum-level," which is appropriate and sufficient for the evidenced benefits. Claims that imply deep dermal/cellular remodeling are not supported by penetration data.

## 6. Formulation

- **Typical use levels (topical):**
  - **Sorbitol:** broadly used; CIR reports leave-on use up to ~**20%** (sorbitol's highest overall use is **70% in dentifrices**). Practical skincare humectant levels **~1–10%**. Often supplied as 70% syrup. [1]
  - **Xylitol:** leave-on up to ~**14%** reported; practical **~1–10%** (5% is the studied level). [1][4]
  - **Mannitol:** leave-on reported up to **~60.5%** (likely a specialty/exfoliant or texturizer context); as an antioxidant adjunct, fractions of a percent to a few percent. [1]
  - **Aquaxyl trio:** **3–4%** (manufacturer-recommended; matches the human data). [9][12]
  - **Maltitol:** typically low single-digit % as humectant/masking/film-forming agent.
- **pH:** Polyols are non-ionic and **pH-neutral / pH-tolerant** — a major formulation advantage. They do not shift formula pH and are compatible across acidic-to-neutral systems (unlike, e.g., spermidine). Stable across the usual cosmetic pH range (~3.5–7).
- **Stability:** Excellent. Chemically robust, non-volatile, oxidation-resistant (they're already reduced alcohols). Polyols can act as **cryoprotectants / freeze–thaw stabilizers**, help **plasticize films** (reduce flaking/tightness), and **lower water activity** (mild boost to preservation robustness). Sorbitol/xylitol can **crystallize** at high concentrations on drydown ("graining") — manage with humectant blends/solvents. Reducing-sugar Maillard browning risk is **low** (sugar *alcohols* lack the free aldehyde of reducing sugars), unlike glucose/fructose.
- **Sensory:** Light, non-greasy, water-thin feel; less sticky/tacky than glycerol at equal humectancy, so often blended **with glycerol to cut glycerol's tack** while keeping hydration. Mild sweetness (irrelevant on skin; matters for lip products). Can leave a faint smooth/cushioned afterfeel.
- **Pairings (sensible):**
  - **Glycerol** — the canonical partner; combination is what the best human data actually tested (Korponyai 2017). Polyols soften glycerol's stickiness. [4]
  - **Hyaluronic acid / NMF actives (amino acids, PCA, lactate)** — layered humectant + NMF-mimetic system; reinforces the barrier-repair thesis.
  - **Ceramides / cholesterol / barrier lipids** — humectant (water in) + occlusive/lipid (water held); Aquaxyl claims to upregulate these endogenously. [9]
  - **Niacinamide** — barrier/NMF synergy.
  - **Mannitol + antioxidants / under HA** — antioxidant-protective role; rational in an antioxidant or filler-adjacent context. [5][6]
  - **Xylitol + microbiome/soothing actives** — for barrier-compromised/sensitive positioning. [11]
- **Conflicts:** Few. Very high polyol loads can compete for water with other humectants and feel tight on drydown or crystallize; balance the humectant system. No notable actives incompatibility.

## 7. Safety

Sugar alcohols are among the **best-tolerated cosmetic ingredients**, with a formal modern safety review:

- **CIR Expert Panel, 2025 (Cherian, Bergfeld, Belsito et al.), *International Journal of Toxicology* 44(1_suppl):22S–43S.** Conclusion: **"Mannitol, Sorbitol, and Xylitol are safe in cosmetics in the present practices of use and concentration."** Functions: humectants, skin-conditioning agents, flavoring/binders. [1]
- **Irritation:** Xylitol at **5% and 10%** in gel and cream was **non-irritating** to rabbit skin (Draize). No meaningful human irritation signal at cosmetic levels. [1]
- **Sensitization:** **Negative.** HRIPT on 50 subjects (15% mannitol blend) — no reactions; HRIPT on 119 subjects (0.115% xylitol) — no sensitization (a few transient low-level erythema); **HRIPT on 110 self-identified sensitive-skin subjects (3% xylitol body lotion) — no sensitization.** Guinea-pig maximization (mannitol blend) negative. [1]
- **Phototoxicity / photosensitization:** Human test on 34 subjects (15% mannitol blend) — **no phototoxicity/photosensitization**. No photo-aggravation concern; polyols are photostable and do **not** increase sun sensitivity. [1]
- **Ocular:** Mannitol non-irritating to eyes in rabbit studies (relevant to eye-area products). [1]
- **Genotoxicity / repro-developmental:** Non-genotoxic in standard batteries; multi-generation oral repro studies (sorbitol, xylitol) showed no reproductive/teratogenic effects (some high-dose GI/osmotic effects only). [1]
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding (topical):** No topical concern flagged; polyols are food-grade and barrier-confined. (Default conservative copy still advisable.)
- **Sourcing/allergen:** Generally allergen-free; xylitol/sorbitol/maltitol are typically corn-, birch-, or starch-derived (note for corn-sensitive customers only at trace level). **Household caution (not skin):** **xylitol is highly toxic to dogs if ingested** — worth a note for any sweet-tasting product (e.g., lip care) in pet households. [1]
- **Oral GI note (context):** ingested in quantity these are osmotic laxatives — irrelevant to topical use but explains the "sugar-free gum" association.

**Net:** Excellent safety. No irritation, no sensitization, no photosensitivity, suitable for sensitive/atopic and eye-area use. This tolerability is itself a selling point for a barrier-repair brand.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic ingredient status:** All are long-established cosmetic ingredients with INCI names (Sorbitol, Xylitol, Maltitol, Mannitol, Xylitylglucoside, Anhydroxylitol) and a current CIR "safe" conclusion; also food-grade (GRAS / EU sweeteners). Routine cosmetic use, no special restriction. [1]
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** The **safe** claims map to genuine cosmetic territory — *hydration, moisturization, suppleness, barrier "support," smoothness.* The **risk zone** is importing the in-vitro/manufacturer mechanism language into structure/function (drug) claims.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "hydrates," "draws and holds moisture in skin's surface," "helps reduce moisture loss," "supports a healthy-looking moisture barrier," "leaves skin soft, smooth and supple," "well-suited to sensitive skin," "plant-derived humectant," "a moisture component related to skin's own NMF."
- ⚠️ **Risky / substantiation-or-legal-review:** "boosts collagen," "reduces wrinkles" (only "improves the look of dry-skin lines via hydration" is defensible), "anti-aging," "rebuilds the barrier," "increases aquaporins/hyaluronic acid in your skin," "antioxidant protection" (mannitol-specific and weak; needs its own substantiation), "antimicrobial / treats atopic skin" (xylitol's *S. aureus* data are adjunctive and drug-adjacent — avoid treatment claims).
- **Substantiation reality:** Generic *hydration/TEWL* claims are the easiest in skincare to substantiate with standard corneometry/Tewameter testing on the **finished formula** — and we should do that rather than lean on ingredient studies. Aquaxyl/AQP3/HA-synthesis claims should be attributed to the supplier's data or backed by our own testing, not stated as fact. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier:** **Low** (commodity). Sorbitol, xylitol, maltitol, and mannitol are large-scale food/industrial polyols — inexpensive and abundant. **Aquaxyl is a premium branded blend** (mid cost) — you pay for the trio + supplier efficacy data + marketing dossier.
- **Suppliers:** Commodity polyols from Roquette, Cargill, ADM, DuPont/IFF, and many others (food/pharma/cosmetic grades). **Aquaxyl exclusively from Seppic.** Mannitol cosmetic-antioxidant positioning is led by Roquette. [5][6][9]
- **Sustainability:** Generally favorable story — **plant/biotech-derived** (corn, birch, starch; mannitol from algae/fructose), biodegradable, often **preservative-free actives** (Aquaxyl markets this). Verify feedstock (corn vs birch vs algal) and any "upcycled/biotech" angle on the COA/supplier docs if used in sustainability messaging.
- **Supply risk:** Low; commodity scale. Aquaxyl is single-source (Seppic) — a minor dependency if it becomes a named hero in a SKU.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **"Anti-aging humectant."** The evidenced benefit is **hydration + barrier + soothing**, which *improves the appearance* of dry/crepey skin and fine "dehydration lines." That is real and worth saying — but it is **not** collagen, senescence, or biological-aging action. The one human biopsy study explicitly found **no MMP-1 change** (no anti-collagenase signal). [4]
- **"Boosts hyaluronic acid / ceramides / aquaporins in your skin."** These are **Aquaxyl manufacturer cosmetogenomics / in-vitro** endpoints, not validated clinical outcomes, and they're specific to the branded blend — not to plain sorbitol or maltitol. Attribute, don't assert. [9][14]
- **"Mannitol = antioxidant anti-aging."** Mannitol *is* a legit hydroxyl-radical scavenger, but the real-world evidence is in **HA fillers** and one tiny **photoprotection-adjunct** study (with ectoine, n=10, +UV filters) — not standalone facial antioxidant efficacy. Don't oversell. [5][6][7]
- **"Microbiome-balancing."** Xylitol's anti-*S. aureus* biofilm data are real but were generated **with farnesol** in **atopic** skin — adjunctive and drug-adjacent. A general "supports skin microbiome" leap is overreach. [10][11][13]
- **The grouping trap.** Marketing often lets **xylitol/Aquaxyl evidence halo onto sorbitol/maltitol/mannitol.** Sorbitol and maltitol have essentially **no standalone skin clinical data**; they're fine humectants by physics, nothing more. Be precise about *which* polyol earned which claim.
- **The glycation irony (important for a longevity brand).** The body's **endogenous polyol (sorbitol) pathway** — aldose reductase converting glucose→**sorbitol**→fructose — is implicated in **AGE formation and oxidative stress** in hyperglycemia/diabetic skin. This is *intracellular metabolic biology* and has **nothing to do with topically applied sorbitol as a surface humectant** (topical polyols don't meaningfully enter cells or feed this pathway). But it means we must **never** spin sorbitol as "anti-glycation," and should be ready to address the (incorrect) "isn't sorbitol bad/sugar→glycation?" objection with this distinction. [17]

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING — a reliable, well-tolerated hydration/barrier base layer, not a hero.**
**One-line:** Sugar alcohols (lead with **xylitol** and/or **Aquaxyl**) are an inexpensive, exceptionally safe, *genuinely human-evidenced* way to deliver hydration, lower TEWL, and support the barrier with sensitive-skin-friendly elegance — perfect supporting actives for the brand's barrier-repair thesis, but with no real claim to collagen, senescence, or "longevity" biology.

**Why supporting, not hero:** (1) The proven benefit (hydration/barrier) is valuable but commoditized — every competent moisturizer hydrates; this won't *differentiate* a premium brand on its own. (2) The deeper "active" mechanisms (differentiation genes, aquaporins, antioxidant, microbiome) are in-vitro/manufacturer/member-specific and won't support headline anti-aging claims. (3) They are most powerful as the **quiet, well-formulated foundation** under the real heroes (ceramides for barrier; retinoids/peptides/vitamin C for renewal) — improving sensory (cutting glycerol tack), tolerability, and the all-important "skin feels comfortable and looks plumper immediately" experience.

**Recommended use within the line:**
- **Xylitol (~3–5%)** and/or **Aquaxyl (3–4%)** as the lead polyol in the barrier-repair cream's humectant system, paired with glycerol + HA + ceramides — this mirrors the actual human evidence (Korponyai 2017; Leite e Silva 2009). [4][12]
- **Mannitol** only where an **antioxidant-adjunct / filler-adjacent / photoprotection-support** rationale exists, and copy it carefully.
- **Sorbitol / maltitol** as economical secondary humectants / sensory modifiers — not as named "stars."

**Longevity-relevance note:** Among the brand's actives, sugar alcohols have arguably the **weakest "longevity" pedigree** but among the **strongest hydration evidence-to-cost ratios**. For a skin-*longevity* brand, the honest role is foundational comfort and barrier support — and a small, defensible *prevention* angle: a well-hydrated, intact barrier is itself a real (if unglamorous) contributor to skin's long-term resilience. Lead the science storytelling with ceramides/retinoids/etc.; use sugar alcohols to make the formula feel and perform beautifully, with strictly hydration-framed copy.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Method: independent check of every load-bearing citation (PubMed/journal lookups) against the claims as written, and assessment of evidence tier (human-topical vs. borrowed in-vitro/animal/oral), study size, and funding/source independence.

### Overall grade: **B** (moderate human-topical) for the lead claim only — **hydration + reduced TEWL** from xylitol (in a glycerol combination) and Aquaxyl. Everything beyond that downgrades.

The grade is **claim-specific**, because this is really several ingredients with very different evidence:

| Claim | Grade | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Hydration ↑ / TEWL ↓** (xylitol-in-glycerol; Aquaxyl) | **B** | Real human-topical data, but small (n=12, n=17), combination-confounded, short, and dry-skin/AD-selected; one peer-reviewed Aquaxyl comparison (n-modest) plus manufacturer data. Not large independent RCTs (so not A). |
| **Filaggrin ↑ / barrier reinforcement (human)** | **B–C** | One biopsy study (Korponyai 2017, n=12, glycerol+xylitol) — real but single, small, combination. |
| **Anti-*S. aureus* / microbiome** | **C** | Real human RCT (n=17) but xylitol **+ farnesol**, AD population, 1 week — adjunctive, can't isolate xylitol; drug-adjacent. |
| **Differentiation genes / aquaporin (AQP3) / HA-ceramide synthesis** | **C** | In-vitro (Páyer 2018) + manufacturer cosmetogenomics (Seppic). Mechanistically plausible, not clinical outcomes. |
| **Mannitol antioxidant / photoprotection** | **C** | Real but: cell-free + filler-context review (André 2017) + one tiny in-vivo study (Fontbonne 2024, n=10 men, 3 days, mannitol+ectoine+UV filters). Not standalone facial efficacy. |
