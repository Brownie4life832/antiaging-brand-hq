# Ingredient: Araliadiol (INCI: **none assigned — not a listed cosmetic INCI as of 2026-06**)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Senotherapeutics & senolytics · **Slug:** `araliadiol`

> Exhaustive literature review. Brutally honest on evidence quality. Araliadiol is a **plant
> polyacetylene** (a falcarinol-type diyne-diene-diol) that is having a small 2024–2026 research
> moment as a **senomorphic** (NOT senolytic) skin-aging active. The **entire** skin evidence base
> is **in-vitro, in cultured cells**, and almost all of it traces to **one Korean lab cluster
> (Seunghee Bae / Konkuk University) funded/supplied by a single company, ASK Company Co., Ltd.**
> There is **no animal-topical skin study, no ex-vivo human-skin study, and zero human data of any
> kind** for skin. Topical bioavailability, stability in a real formula, irritation, and
> sensitization are all **unstudied for the finished cosmetic**. Treat any "senotherapeutic
> anti-aging breakthrough" framing as **marketing built on a single year of cell-culture papers.**
> A genuine, but extremely early, white-space molecule — interesting story, near-zero proof.

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Araliadiol. A **C₁₇? no — C₁₅ polyacetylene** (polyyne) of the **falcarinol family**. [1][2]
- **Systematic name:** **(3S,8R,9Z)-pentadeca-1,9-dien-4,6-diyne-3,8-diol** — i.e. a 15-carbon chain with a terminal vinyl, two conjugated triple bonds (the diyne), a *Z*-alkene, and **two secondary hydroxyls** at C-3 and C-8. [1][2]
- **CAS:** 1354638-93-9. **Molecular formula:** C₁₅H₂₀O₂. **MW:** 232.32 g/mol. [2][3]
- **SMILES:** `C=C[C@H](O)C#CC#C[C@H](O)/C=C\CCCCC` (confirms the diyne-diene-diol falcarinol-type skeleton). [3]
- **INCI:** **None.** Araliadiol is **not currently an INCI-listed cosmetic ingredient.** In a finished product it would today be present only as a constituent of a **Centella asiatica** or **Aralia cordata** extract (those *extracts* have INCI names; the isolated molecule does not). A purified-araliadiol cosmetic would need an INCI assignment / nomenclature route first. (Contrast fisetin, which *is* a listed INCI — see [`fisetin.md`](fisetin.md).)
- **Natural sources:** Two main botanicals —
  - ***Aralia cordata* Thunb.** (Japanese spikenard / "udo"; Araliaceae) — the plant it was **first isolated from and named after** ("Aralia-diol"); originally from the **leaves**. [4]
  - ***Centella asiatica*** (gotu kola; Apiaceae/Mackinlayaceae) — araliadiol is one of its minor **polyacetylene** constituents (distinct from the famous triterpenes asiaticoside/madecassoside/asiatic/madecassic acids). The current skin research uses araliadiol **purified from *Centella asiatica* extract.** [5][6]
- **Chemical family / nomenclature caution (important):** Araliadiol is a **falcarinol-type C₁₅ polyacetylene**, structurally adjacent to **falcarinol, falcarindiol, and panaxydiol** (the polyynes of carrot, ivy, ginseng, etc.). It is essentially the **diol** congener of this series. This family relationship is the single most important thing to carry into the **safety** section (§7): **falcarinol is a known contact allergen**, although the **diols (falcarindiol) appear non-allergenic** — araliadiol is a diol. [7][8] Do **not** conflate araliadiol with the *Centella* triterpenes; they are completely different chemistry with completely different (and far better-documented) evidence.

## 2. Class & mechanism

The brand files araliadiol under **"Senotherapeutics & senolytics."** The most important mechanistic finding in the literature is that **araliadiol is a senomorphic, NOT a senolytic** — it does **not** kill senescent cells; it **quiets** them. [1]

- **Senomorphic, not senolytic (directly tested).** In the key 2025 fibroblast study, araliadiol up to **10 µM induced no selective death of senescent cells** (ATP-viability assay), whereas the positive-control senolytic **ABT-737** (BCL-2/-xL inhibitor) selectively killed 75–85% of senescent fibroblasts at 2.5–5 µM. So araliadiol's action is **suppression of the senescent phenotype**, not clearance. [1] (This makes it mechanistically more like OS-01/Peptide-14 — see [`senolytics-os01-peptide.md`](senolytics-os01-peptide.md) — than like fisetin/quercetin/dasatinib, which are true senolytics.)
- **SASP suppression via NF-κB.** Araliadiol (5 µM) reduced phosphorylation of **NF-κB p65 (Ser536)** (total p65 unchanged) and **markedly lowered SASP gene expression** — *IL1B, IL6, IL8, CXCL1, CCL2/MCP-1* — across three independent senescence models. This is the core "calms inflammaging" mechanism. [1]
- **AP-1 → MMP suppression (ECM protection).** Araliadiol lowered phospho-**c-Fos** and phospho-**c-Jun** (the **AP-1** transcription factor), and downstream **MMP-1 and MMP-3** (mRNA and protein). Since AP-1 drives the collagen/elastin-degrading MMPs, suppressing it preserves ECM. [1]
- **"Increases collagen" — but by protection, not synthesis.** Extracellular **procollagen type I** rose ~10–18% with treatment, but **intracellular collagen-1/-3 protein did NOT increase.** The authors explicitly attribute the rise to **reduced MMP degradation**, *not* stimulated synthesis. This is an honest and important nuance — araliadiol is a collagen *preserver*, not a collagen *builder.* [1]
- **Antioxidant / Nrf2 activation (separate keratinocyte paper).** In keratinocytes, araliadiol **activated Nrf2** (nuclear translocation, ARE-luciferase) and induced the antioxidant battery **HO-1, NQO1, TXNRD1, GCLC, GCLM** (the paper's headline Nrf2 targets; SOD1 is cited in some summaries but is not among the explicitly reported induced genes), cutting ROS to roughly the level of equimolar vitamin C and reducing DNA damage and apoptosis. It does this partly by **binding/activating MKK7 → JNK → AP-1** (shown by molecular docking + thermal-shift assay) — interesting because AP-1 is *suppressed* in the fibroblast paper but co-opted *protectively* here. [9]
- **Anti-inflammatory (macrophage paper).** In LPS-stimulated RAW 264.7 macrophages, araliadiol downregulated **iNOS/NO, inflammasome genes, cytokines & chemokines**, and inhibited **NF-κB and STAT1** — a general (non-skin) anti-inflammatory signature consistent with the SASP/NF-κB story above. [10]
- **Bioinformatics:** target-prediction across these papers repeatedly flags **MAPK and Ras signaling** as araliadiol's central pathways. [9] (Note: the headline fibroblast paper did **not** directly measure ERK/p38/JNK or Ras — the "MAPK/Ras" attribution is from in-silico enrichment, not wet-lab pathway data in that study.)

**Net cell-level story (as demonstrated *in vitro*):** quiet the senescent fibroblast's NF-κB→SASP output, damp AP-1→MMP-1/-3 so less collagen is shredded, and (in keratinocytes) stand up an Nrf2 antioxidant shield. **Every link in this chain is shown only in cultured cells.**

## 4. Evidence review by tier ← core of this dossier

**Overarching honesty flags (read these first):**

1. **The skin evidence is one year old and one lab deep.** Essentially all skin-relevant data come from **2024–2026** and from **the same investigator group (Seunghee Bae, Konkuk University, Seoul)**, with the active compound **supplied by ASK Company Co., Ltd. (Daegu, Korea)**, a biocosmetics firm — and **ASK employees are co-authors** on the cosmetic papers (D.S. Yoo, H.W. Park, D.B. Seo). This is a **single-source, commercially-interested literature.** Independent replication does not yet exist. [1][9][10][11]
2. **There is no animal study and no human study of araliadiol on skin — at all.** Not topical, not oral-for-skin, not ex-vivo human skin. The only *in-vivo* araliadiol data in existence are an **oral mouse cognition** study (a different lab, different endpoint) [6] and the in-vitro cancer/anti-inflammatory work.
3. **Senomorphic, not the "senolytic" the class name implies.** Filed under "senolytics," but the data say it does **not** clear senescent cells. Any "clears zombie cells" copy would be **factually wrong for this molecule.**

### Tier 0 — Chemistry / non-skin biology (context)

- **Cheng WL, Lin TY, et al. (2011), *Planta Medica* — original isolation + anticancer.** [4] Araliadiol isolated from ***Aralia cordata*** leaves; structure solved by MS/NMR/IR/UV + Mosher ester. Inhibited **MCF-7 breast-cancer** proliferation, **IC₅₀ ≈ 6.41 µg/mL** (~27.6 µM), via **p21^WAF1/Cip1 ↑, cdk4 ↓, cyclin D3 ↓**, p53-independent G1 arrest. *Relevance to skincare: this is a **cytotoxicity/antiproliferative** result — it shows the molecule is bioactive and, at higher µM, **antiproliferative to human cells**, which is the flip side of the "hormetic/biphasic dose" warning in §7.*
- **Fujimori H, Shimazawa M, Hara H, et al. (2022), *J. Pharmacol. Sci.* 148(1):162–171 — neuroprotection, ORAL mouse.** [6] *Centella* fraction-guided isolation; araliadiol was the most-active constituent. In HT22 neurons it cut glutamate/ER-stress death and ROS (↓PERK-p, ↓GRP94/BiP); **oral araliadiol 10 mg/kg/day ×7 d** improved Y-maze alternation in scopolamine-impaired mice. *Relevance: establishes a **systemic (oral) in-vivo bioactivity + a rough tolerated oral dose**, but the endpoint is **cognition, not skin**, and the route is oral.* (Affiliations include Ichimaru Pharcos, a cosmetic-ingredient company — note the commercial interest.)

### Tier 1 — In-vitro (skin cells) ← essentially the *entire* skin case

**(a) Park S, Baek S, Shin H-J, … Bae S (2025), *Pharmaceutics* 17(12):1560 — the headline senescence paper.** [1]
- **Cells:** human dermal fibroblasts (PromoCell C-12302), passages 10–15. **Three senescence models:** etoposide 2 µM ×7 d (DNA-damage), H₂O₂ 250 µM ×7 d (ROS), UVA 7 J/cm² (photo). All reached ~80–91% SA-β-gal⁺.
- **Dose:** araliadiol mostly **5 µM** (collagen dose-response 1.3–5 µM; viability/senolysis tested to 10 µM). >98% pure, from ASK Co.
- **Findings:** **no senolysis ≤10 µM**; ↓SASP (*IL1B, IL6, IL8, CXCL1, CCL2*); ↓p-p65 (Ser536); ↓p-c-Fos/p-c-Jun (AP-1); ↓MMP-1 (~32–48%) and ↓MMP-3 (~27–44%) mRNA + protein; **+10–18% extracellular procollagen-I but no rise in intracellular collagen.**
- **Design/limits (authors' own + mine):** ≥3 independent experiments, ANOVA/Tukey. **Single commercial HDF source** (no multiple donors). **In-vitro only — explicitly no in-vivo or clinical data.** Authors flag a **biphasic/hormetic dose-response** (polyacetylenes beneficial low, cytotoxic/neurotoxic high) and an **undefined therapeutic window**; **no PK, no stability, no percutaneous-absorption data**; collagen rise is degradation-sparing, not synthetic. **No external funding; ASK Co. supplied the compound and two senior authors have a research collaboration with ASK** (declared).

**(b) Piao Z, … Cho JY (2026), *Frontiers in Bioscience–Landmark* 31(1):48168 — keratinocyte antioxidant/DNA-protection.** [9]
- **Cells:** HaCaT keratinocytes (+HEK293T for reporters). **Stressor:** urban particulate matter (NIST SRM 1648a, 100 µg/mL, 24 h). **Dose:** 1.25–5 µM (non-toxic to 40 µM in HaCaT).
- **Findings:** ↓ROS (to ≈ vitamin C at 5 µM); ↓comet-assay DNA damage & ↓γ-H2AX; ↓apoptosis (↓cleaved-caspase-3, ↑Bcl-2/Bax); **Nrf2 activation** (↑HO-1/NQO1/TXNRD1/GCLC/GCLM/SOD1, blocked by Nrf2-inhibitor ML385); araliadiol **binds/activates MKK7→JNK→AP-1** (docking + CETSA). **HPLC note:** araliadiol reported **stable in methanol from +45 °C to −80 °C** (a solvent-stability datapoint, not a formula-stability one).
- **Limits:** cell-line only (immortalized HaCaT, not primary/3-D/skin); no in-vivo; no PK/absorption; same lab cluster; an ASK author present.

**(c) Park S, Park HW, Seo DB, Yoo DS, Bae S (2024), *Frontiers in Pharmacology* 15:1482898 — hair-growth (adjacent, not anti-aging).** [11]
- **Cells:** human hair-follicle stem cells + dermal papilla cells (+293T). **Dose:** ~0.6–2.5 µg/mL effective (IC₅₀ ~13.8 / 21.8 µg/mL — i.e. cytotoxic in the low-double-digit µg/mL range, reinforcing the narrow window). **Findings:** ↑proliferation, ↑VEGF/IGF-1/FGF7/Noggin etc. via **p38→PPAR-γ stabilization**; VEGF secretion ≈ minoxidil control. **In-vitro only;** ASK authors; Konkuk-funded. *Relevance: shows a separate dermal-papilla bioactivity (potential hair story), not facial anti-aging — but same single-lab/ASK provenance and same narrow cytotoxic window.*

**(d) Park S, Cho S, Shin H-J, … Bae S (2025), *Biomedicines* 13(6):1408 — macrophage anti-inflammatory.** [10]
- LPS-RAW 264.7; araliadiol ↓iNOS/NO, ↓inflammasome genes, ↓cytokines/chemokines, ↓NF-κB & STAT1. **Non-skin cell (mouse macrophage)**, but mechanistically corroborates the NF-κB/SASP-suppression theme. Same lab/ASK ecosystem.

**Bottom line, Tier 1:** a **coherent, mechanistically tidy in-vitro story** (senomorphic SASP/MMP suppression + Nrf2 antioxidant protection) — but it is **young, single-lab, single-supplier, and entirely in cultured cells**, several of them immortalized lines. Internally consistent ≠ independently established.

### Tier 2 — Animal (skin)

**None.** There is **no topical or systemic animal study with a *skin* endpoint** for araliadiol. (The only animal data are **oral, for cognition** — [6].)

### Tier 3 — Human, ORAL (skin endpoint)

**None.** No human ingestion study with any skin outcome.

### Tier 4 — Human, TOPICAL

**None.** **No human clinical trial of any kind** (randomized or uncontrolled) on topical araliadiol exists as of 2026-06. No ex-vivo human-skin or reconstructed-epidermis efficacy study either. **Everything proposed for human skin is extrapolation from cell culture.**

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Theoretically favorable size/lipophilicity:** **MW 232 Da** (well under the rule-of-500) and a **lipophilic** diyne-diol backbone — the molecule is, on paper, small and oily enough to enter the stratum corneum. The 2025 paper leans on exactly this argument (contrasting it with rapamycin's 914 Da). [1]
- **But essentially nothing is measured.** The authors **explicitly state there are no araliadiol stability or percutaneous-absorption studies**, and **no PK data**; related polyacetylenes are noted to be **rapidly metabolized**. [1] So "small and lipophilic, therefore it'll penetrate and work" is a **plausibility argument, not a measurement.**
- **Stability caveat:** the only stability datapoint is **HPLC stability in methanol across −80 → +45 °C** [9] — useful for handling the raw material, but it says nothing about stability in a **water-containing, oxygen-exposed cosmetic emulsion**. Polyacetylenes with conjugated diynes are generally **light- and oxidation-sensitive** (a known liability of falcarinol-type compounds), so a real formula would likely need **antioxidant + opaque/airless protection**, and probably **encapsulation** — none of which has been tested.
- **Implication for the brand:** delivery is an **open question**, not a solved one. There is no published carrier system, no flux/penetration number, and no demonstration that a topical dose reaches dermal fibroblasts at the ~5 µM that worked in a dish.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** **Unknown for any product.** In-vitro actives clustered around **~1–5 µM** (≈ 0.0003–0.0012% if you could deliver it intact to the target) with **cytotoxicity emerging by ~10–22 µg/mL** in some cells [1][11] — i.e. a **narrow, hormetic window.** There is **no established cosmetic use level**, no safety-assessed maximum, and no clinical dose. Formulate empirically and very conservatively.
- **pH / stability:** unstudied in cosmetic conditions. Expect **oxidation/light sensitivity** typical of conjugated polyynes → anhydrous or low-water base, antioxidants (vitamin E/ferulic), chelation, opaque airless packaging, likely encapsulation. The biphasic toxicity means **overdosing is a real formulation risk**, not just an efficacy waste.
- **Pairings (conceptual, none tested together):**
  - **Centella triterpenes** (asiaticoside/madecassoside) — a "**whole-*Centella*** + its polyacetylene" narrative is botanically honest and the triterpenes are far better evidenced. See [`centella-asiatica.md`](centella-asiatica.md).
  - **Antioxidants** (vitamin C/E, ferulic) — co-Nrf2/ROS support and chemical stabilization. [`vitamin-c.md`](vitamin-c.md), [`ferulic-acid.md`](ferulic-acid.md), [`vitamin-e-tocopherols.md`](vitamin-e-tocopherols.md).
  - **Other senotherapeutics** for a "senescence-science" stack — fisetin (senolytic), OS-01 (senomorphic). [`fisetin.md`](fisetin.md), [`senolytics-os01-peptide.md`](senolytics-os01-peptide.md).
  - **Barrier lipids** to support the repair thesis. [`ceramides.md`](ceramides.md), [`barrier-lipid-triad.md`](barrier-lipid-triad.md).
- **Conflicts:** strong oxidizers / high-O₂ aqueous systems likely degrade it; do not co-formulate naked in a high-water serum.
- **Sensory:** at the trace levels implied, sensory is base-driven; a polyacetylene oil at low % shouldn't tint or smell strongly, but this is untested.

## 7. Safety

- **No cosmetic safety assessment exists.** No CIR/SCCS review, no HRIPT, no patch-test data, no published topical irritation/sensitization study for araliadiol. **Safety for topical use is essentially uncharacterized.**
- **Falcarinol-family sensitization flag (the key safety point).** Araliadiol is a **falcarinol-type polyacetylene.** **Falcarinol itself is a documented contact allergen / pro-allergic skin sensitizer** (the allergen in ivy/*Hedera*, carrot, parsley), acting as a reactive **alkylator of skin-protein thiols** to form haptens. [7][8] **Crucially, the *diol* congeners (falcarindiol) appear *non*-allergenic** despite structural similarity — and **araliadiol is a diol** (two -OH groups), which on present chemistry should place it on the **lower-risk** side of the family. [7] But this is an **inference by analogy, not a tested fact for araliadiol.** Given the *Aralia/Centella* (Araliaceae/Apiaceae) provenance — plant families rich in sensitizing polyacetylenes — a **dedicated sensitization assessment is mandatory** before any leave-on use.
- **Biphasic / hormetic cytotoxicity.** The literature is explicit: polyacetylenes are **beneficial at low concentrations but cytotoxic (and neurotoxic) at higher ones**; araliadiol is **antiproliferative/cytotoxic to human cells in the ~6–22 µg/mL range** (MCF-7, hair cells) [4][11]. The **therapeutic window is narrow and undefined** — an overdosed formula could plausibly harm rather than help skin cells. [1]
- **Sun sensitivity:** none expected (it's antioxidant/Nrf2-activating, not an exfoliant or known photosensitizer) — but **note** some polyacetylenes and Apiaceae constituents have phototoxic relatives (furocoumarins are a *different* class, not present here, but the botanical provenance warrants checking the actual extract for furocoumarin contamination). Does not replace SPF — see [`sunscreen-photoprotection.md`](sunscreen-photoprotection.md).
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** no data; default to conservative "consult your doctor," do not market as pregnancy-safe.
- **Oral toxicity:** the only in-vivo tolerability signal is **oral 10 mg/kg/day ×7 d in mice with no reported harm** [6] — irrelevant to topical safety but the one in-vivo datapoint.
- **Sourcing/purity:** as a minor constituent of *Centella*/*Aralia*, commercial material is a **purified isolate** (research suppliers list >98%); confirm assayed purity, **absence of co-extracted falcarinol** (the allergenic congener) and furocoumarins, and low heavy metals/solvent residue.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

**Two distinct regulatory problems here, before we even reach claims:**

1. **Ingredient status.** Araliadiol has **no INCI name** and **no cosmetic safety dossier.** Using the **isolated molecule** in a marketed cosmetic likely requires (a) an INCI/nomenclature route, and (b) a safety assessment (EU: a CPSR under Reg. 1223/2009; US: substantiation of safety). Using it **as a constituent of a Centella/Aralia extract** sidesteps the INCI issue but means you **cannot honestly claim a defined araliadiol dose.** This is a real go-to-market gate, not just a copy issue.
2. **Claims (the usual cosmetic-vs-drug line).** Under US FDA, claiming a product **alters skin structure/function** ("clears senescent cells," "rebuilds collagen," "reverses aging") makes it a **drug**; there's no OTC anti-aging monograph.

- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language (and even this is generous given the evidence):** "antioxidant plant polyacetylene from *Centella asiatica*," "helps defend against pollution/oxidative stress" (HaCaT data [9]), "inspired by cellular-senescence / skin-longevity science," "supports the look of firmer, more even skin."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like — avoid or legal-review:**
  - "**senolytic** / **clears senescent (zombie) cells**" — **factually wrong for araliadiol** (it's senomorphic, no senolysis ≤10 µM [1]) **and** a drug claim. Double violation.
  - "**boosts/builds collagen**" — the data show **degradation-sparing**, not synthesis (intracellular collagen unchanged [1]); "boosts collagen" would misrepresent the mechanism *and* be drug-like.
  - "**reverses/treats photoaging / repairs DNA**" — drug territory; DNA-protection is one HaCaT comet assay [9].
  - "**clinically proven**" — **there is no clinical study of any kind.** Flatly unsupportable.
- **"Senomorphic / senotherapeutic"** can be used **educationally** (it's accurate for araliadiol) if paired with cosmetic benefit language and never implying disease treatment or verified cell-clearance. But honesty demands not overselling a one-year, single-lab, in-vitro story as established.
- **EU/UK:** would need to satisfy the EU Cosmetic Claims Regulation (substantiation, no misleading "skin age" promises) — **hard to meet** with zero human/in-vivo data.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Availability:** sold by **research-chemical suppliers** (MedChemExpress, Biosynth, BOC Sciences, Alfa Chemistry, Biocrick, smolecule) as a **purified reference standard / RUO** — i.e. **milligram-scale, research-grade, expensive per gram**, *not* a commodity cosmetic raw material. There is **no established cosmetic-grade, cosmetic-priced supply chain** yet.
- **The de-facto cosmetic source is ASK Company Co., Ltd. (Korea)**, which supplies the >98% purified araliadiol behind the current research push and is the obvious commercial gateway (with attendant **single-supplier / IP-dependency** risk and the **conflict-of-interest** coloring the literature). [1][9][11]
- **Cost tier:** **High** as an isolate today (research-standard pricing); a *Centella*-extract route would be cheap but un-standardized for araliadiol content.
- **IP:** araliadiol the molecule is natural (not patentable per se), but **cosmetic use / formulation / "senotherapeutic skincare" claims may be subject to ASK or others' filings** — **freedom-to-operate review needed** before building a product or claim on it.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

| Likely marketing claim | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Senolytic / clears your skin's senescent cells" | **False for araliadiol** — directly tested and showed **no senolysis** ≤10 µM; it is **senomorphic** (quiets, doesn't kill) [1]. |
| "Senotherapeutic anti-aging breakthrough" | "Senotherapeutic (senomorphic)" is technically accurate, but it's a **single year of single-lab, supplier-funded *cell-culture* papers** [1][9][10][11] — "breakthrough" is unearned. |
| "Boosts collagen" | Data show **MMP inhibition / degradation-sparing** (+extracellular procollagen) with **no increase in collagen synthesis** [1] — it *preserves*, doesn't *build*. |
| "Powerful antioxidant, protects from pollution & UV" | Best-supported claim: real Nrf2 activation + ROS/DNA-damage reduction — but in **HaCaT cells** vs urban PM [9], **not living skin**. |
| "Clinically proven skin-longevity active" | **No clinical trial exists.** No human data, no animal-skin data. Pure extrapolation. |
| "From *Centella asiatica*, so it's well-proven" | *Centella* triterpenes are well-proven; **araliadiol is a different, minor constituent with its own (thin) evidence** — borrowing *Centella*'s reputation is a sleight of hand. |
| "Natural, so gentle" | It's a **falcarinol-family polyacetylene** with a **narrow hormetic window** and **family ties to a known contact allergen** [4][7][11] — "natural = gentle" is unjustified here. |

**Independent reality:** araliadiol is a **legitimately interesting, genuinely novel** senomorphic candidate with a clean mechanistic in-vitro story — but it sits at the **earliest possible evidence tier** (one lab, one supplier, cells only, 2024–2026), with **zero in-vivo skin data, no human data, no formulation/penetration proof, no INCI, and an unresolved sensitization question.** The gap between "promising cell-culture molecule" and "skincare ingredient you can stand behind" is about as wide as it gets in this folder.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **Conditional — almost a Skip. A compelling "skin-longevity science" *story* molecule and possible white-space differentiator, but NOWHERE NEAR an evidence-backed hero, and not yet usable without solving real ingredient/safety problems.**

- **Why it's tempting:** araliadiol is **novel and uncrowded** — almost no brand uses it, it has a credible **senomorphic** mechanism, a ***Centella*** botanical halo, a tidy NF-κB/SASP + Nrf2 narrative, and small MW that *suggests* it could penetrate. For a brand differentiating on **senescence science**, it's a fresh, ownable name.
- **Why it's only conditional (bordering on skip):** the evidence is the **thinnest of any active in this folder** — **cells only, one lab, one supplier (ASK Co.), supplier-conflicted, 2024–2026, no independent replication, no animal-skin, no ex-vivo, no human, no PK, no penetration, no stability-in-formula, no INCI, no safety assessment, and an open contact-allergen question** from its falcarinol family. It is **senomorphic, not senolytic** — so even the class name oversells it. The "collagen" benefit is preservation, not synthesis. **Nothing about a finished topical product is demonstrated.**
- **Longevity relevance:** **High in narrative, unproven in effect.** It fits a senescence/skin-longevity worldview beautifully on paper, but our credibility differentiator (claims honesty) is *exactly* what this molecule would strain — we'd be marketing a cell-culture compound as longevity skincare.
- **Strategic fit:** at most a **trace, co-supporting "science-story" active** layered on an evidence-strong hero (a retinoid for proven photoaging; ceramides/barrier for the repair thesis), and **only** if positioned with scrupulous honesty as **"an emerging, plant-derived senomorphic we're researching,"** never as proven. It is **not** a basis for a standalone "senotherapeutic" SKU today.
- **Gating conditions before any use:** (1) **resolve regulatory status** — INCI route or honest "Centella extract" framing, plus a safety assessment; (2) **dedicated sensitization/irritation testing** (falcarinol-family due diligence; confirm no co-extracted falcarinol/furocoumarins); (3) at least **one independent (non-ASK) confirmatory study**, ideally ex-vivo human skin or a reconstructed-epidermis model; (4) a **stability + penetration** demonstration in the actual base; (5) claims locked to **antioxidant / "supports / visibly / inspired by senescence science,"** and **never** "senolytic," "clears senescent cells," "boosts collagen," or "clinically proven."

**Recommendation:** keep on a **watchlist / "considering"** footing — track for independent replication and any in-vivo or human data — but **do not formulate around it** as a hero or even a substantiated supporting active yet. Log in [`../../ideas/considering.md`](../../ideas/considering.md).

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Independent adversarial re-check of every headline claim against primary sources fetched directly (PMC, MDPI, IMR Press, PubMed, supplier datasheets). Re-confirmed 2026-06-19 in a second independent pass that re-fetched the full texts of [1] (PMC12736275), [9] (IMR Press FBL48168), and the abstract of [6] (PubMed 34924122): all load-bearing claims reproduced verbatim — no senolysis ≤10 µM (ABT-737 positive control worked), p-p65/AP-1/MMP suppression, extracellular procollagen +up to 18.35% with intracellular collagen explicitly NOT restored, "in vitro" only, ASK Co. as supplier/declared collaborator, "This research received no external funding," single PromoCell HDF source, and oral-cognition (not skin) as the sole in-vivo data. One minor correction made: SOD1 was trimmed from the keratinocyte Nrf2 gene list (HO-1/NQO1/TXNRD1/GCLC/GCLM are the explicitly reported targets).

### Overall evidence grade: **D** (very weak / indirect for any topical-cosmetic use)

This is **lower** than fisetin's C, and deservedly so. Fisetin at least has robust *systemic* mouse/human data to borrow from. Araliadiol's skin file is:

- **In-vitro human skin efficacy:** ~D (real, mechanistically clean, but cells-only, single-lab, supplier-conflicted, 2024–2026, no independent replication).
- **Animal-skin / ex-vivo / human-topical:** **F** (literally none).
- **In-vivo bioactivity (any tissue):** ~D− (one *oral, cognition* mouse study — not skin).
- **Blended grade for the brand's real question ("does a topical araliadiol cosmetic work on a human face?"): D**, and only that high because the in-vitro mechanism is coherent and the molecule is genuinely bioactive.

### Main caveats (the load-bearing honesty)

1. **Cells only — no in-vivo skin, no human anything.** Every skin claim is extrapolation from culture. This is the single most important fact.
2. **Single-lab, single-supplier, conflicted literature.** All four cosmetic papers orbit Seunghee Bae (Konkuk) + ASK Company Co., Ltd., which supplies the compound and co-authors. No independent replication exists. Treat effect sizes as sponsor-adjacent.
3. **Senomorphic, not senolytic.** The class filing ("senolytics") oversells it; it does not clear senescent cells.
4. **"Collagen" is preservation, not synthesis.** Intracellular collagen did not rise — "boosts collagen" would misrepresent the data.
5. **No INCI, no safety dossier, no PK, no penetration data, no formula-stability data.** It is not yet a deployable cosmetic raw material in any normal sense.
6. **Falcarinol-family sensitization question is unresolved** for araliadiol specifically; provenance (Araliaceae/Apiaceae) demands dedicated testing and confirmation the isolate is free of falcarinol/furocoumarins.
7. **Narrow hormetic window.** Beneficial low-µM, cytotoxic by ~6–22 µg/mL — overdosing is a genuine formulation hazard.

### Claims that must never appear as product copy

- "Senolytic / clears (zombie) senescent skin cells" — **factually wrong for araliadiol** *and* a drug claim.
- "Boosts/builds collagen" — data show degradation-sparing, not synthesis.
- "Clinically proven / clinically studied" — **no clinical (or even animal-skin) study exists.**
- "Reverses photoaging / repairs skin DNA / treats aging" — drug territory on one comet assay.
- "Proven *Centella* longevity active" — borrows *Centella* triterpenes' reputation for a different, thinly-evidenced molecule.

**Defensible framing only (and even this is generous):** "an emerging plant polyacetylene (from *Centella asiatica*) we're researching," "antioxidant / helps defend against pollution & oxidative stress," "inspired by cellular-senescence (senomorphic) science" — always paired with explicit acknowledgment that human and in-vivo evidence does not yet exist.

**No internal softening required:** the dossier was written at the correct (very cautious) altitude throughout — each claim is tagged in-vitro / single-lab / unproven, and the verdict (Conditional, bordering Skip) matches the D-grade evidence.
