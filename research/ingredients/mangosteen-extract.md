# Ingredient: Mangosteen / α-Mangostin (INCI: Garcinia Mangostana Peel Extract; α-Mangostin)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Slug:** mangosteen-extract · **Class:** Botanicals & plant bioactives (xanthone-rich pericarp extract; lead actives α-mangostin / γ-mangostin)

> **One-line honest summary:** Mangosteen pericarp ("peel"/rind) extract — and its lead xanthone **α-mangostin** — is a genuinely strong **antioxidant and anti-inflammatory** botanical with a coherent, mechanism-rich anti-photoaging story (potent in-vitro **anti-collagenase**, **MMP-1/-9 suppression**, ROS scavenging, NF-κB/MAPK/NLRP3 inhibition, and a remarkably potent **TRPV3 block**). The catch for a *skin-longevity* brand is the usual one, only sharper here: the deepest data are **in-vitro and animal**, and **what little human topical evidence exists is almost entirely for ACNE, not anti-aging.** There are **two real human topical RCTs** — but both are acne trials (split-face, 12-week), plus **one controlled human UVB-erythema study** (n=31) and a **2-person** anti-age-spot pilot. There is **no controlled human topical trial for wrinkles/elasticity/firmness.** The anti-aging case is built on excellent cell/mouse work plus borrowed-format human acne/photoprotection data. α-Mangostin is also **poorly water-soluble** (delivery matters), and oral mangosteen carries a real (if rare) **lactic-acidosis** safety signal — irrelevant topically but a reason to keep the brand topical-only. A credible **supporting** antioxidant/soothing/anti-photoaging botanical, not a proven anti-aging hero.

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it works)

Mangosteen sits in **botanicals & plant bioactives** as a **xanthone-rich antioxidant/anti-inflammatory** extract. Its skin biology is unusually well-characterized for a botanical, and the mechanisms are real and convergent:

**Mechanistic pillars (well-supported in cells/animals):**

1. **Direct antioxidant / ROS scavenging + endogenous-antioxidant upregulation.** α-Mangostin and the pericarp extract scavenge ROS and raise the cell's own defenses — **SOD, glutathione peroxidase (GPx), GSH, catalase** — in multiple models (keratinocytes, fibroblasts, retinal cells). [1][6][7][8] In UVA-stressed human fibroblasts, mangosteen pericarp extract (MPE) sharply cut H₂O₂-induced ROS. [3]
2. **Anti-collagenase / anti-elastase / MMP suppression (the anti-photoaging core).** α-Mangostin is a **potent collagenase inhibitor in vitro (IC₅₀ ≈ 9.75 µg/mL)** and the whole extract inhibits elastase and hyaluronidase; molecular docking shows α-mangostin binds **MMP-1 (collagenase)** strongly (−8.9 kcal/mol). [1] In UVB-photoaged mice and UVA-stressed fibroblasts, it **suppresses MMP-1 and MMP-9** and **raises type-I procollagen.** [2][3]
3. **Anti-inflammatory: NF-κB / MAPK / NLRP3 inhibition (inflammaging).** Across many models α-mangostin suppresses **NF-κB (p65) activation, MAPK (ERK/p38/JNK) phosphorylation, and the NLRP3 inflammasome**, lowering TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6, IL-8/IL-18. [2][5][9] This is the most robust, repeatedly replicated mechanism.
4. **TRPV3 channel block (a standout, skin-specific mechanism).** α-Mangostin is a **remarkably potent and selective TRPV3 inhibitor (IC₅₀ ≈ 0.077 µM)** in human keratinocytes — ~100× more potent at TRPV3 than at TRPV4/TRPA1 — cutting keratinocyte IL-8/IL-6 release and calcium overload. TRPV3 is implicated in itch, inflammation, and barrier/skin homeostasis (Olmsted syndrome). This is a genuinely interesting, differentiated cutaneous mechanism. [12]
5. **Anti-melanogenic (tone) — for the PERICARP/α-mangostin.** α-Mangostin inhibits **tyrosinase** and downregulates **MITF / TYR / TRP-2** (via GSK3β/ERK and RAR/RXR signaling) in B16 melanoma cells, reducing melanin. [13][14] The pericarp extract is anti-tyrosinase. [1] **But note the leaf contradiction in §3.**
6. **Senescence / SASP attenuation (longevity-adjacent).** MPE pretreatment **reduced UVA-induced fibroblast senescence** (~41% fewer SA-β-gal⁺ cells; 12.0% → ~7.1%). This is the closest mechanism to a true "skin-longevity" lever, but it's a single in-vitro study. [3]

**Skin-relevant translation (honest):** Pillars 1–4 are robustly demonstrated *in skin cells and/or animal skin* and are mutually reinforcing (antioxidant + anti-MMP + anti-inflammatory + TRPV3 = a clean anti-photoaging/soothing rationale). Pillar 5 (tone) is solid in cells for the pericarp/α-mangostin. Pillar 6 (senescence) is promising but single-study. **None of these mechanisms has been demonstrated in living human skin for an anti-aging endpoint** — the human data are acne and UV-erythema (see §4).

| **Photoaging (UV)** | **Moderate–strong mechanistically** (↓UVB wrinkles + reduced UVB-induced epidermal hyperplasia in mice; ↓UVA fibroblast senescence; human UVB-erythema protection) | Animal (oral) [2] + in vitro [3] + **1 controlled human UVB-erythema study** [16] |

| **Pigmentation / tone** | Moderate (anti-tyrosinase; ↓MITF/TYR/TRP-2) — **pericarp/α-mangostin only** | In vitro (B16) [13][14] + 2-person serum pilot [4] |

| **Mitochondrial decline / NAD⁺** | Not a documented skin mechanism (some oral metabolic data, not skin) | — |

> **Pigmentation is a real source-dependent contradiction.** The **pericarp/α-mangostin** is **anti-melanogenic** (tyrosinase↓, MITF↓) [1][13][14], but **mangosteen LEAF extract has been shown to *increase* melanogenesis** in B16F1 cells (stimulating tyrosinase activity and upregulating the tyrosinase gene). [18] So "mangosteen evens tone" is only defensible for **pericarp/α-mangostin**, and only as cell-level evidence. Specify the plant part; do not let leaf data and peel data be conflated in copy.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** Mangosteen's anti-aging *mechanisms* are strong in cells and mice, but the **human topical evidence is overwhelmingly for ACNE, not anti-aging.** There are **two genuine human topical RCTs** — both **acne** (split-face, 12 weeks). For *photoaging/wrinkles* there is **one controlled human UVB-erythema study** (prevention of redness, not wrinkle reversal) and a tiny **2-subject** anti-age-spot serum pilot. **No randomized, controlled human trial has tested mangosteen for wrinkles, firmness, elasticity, or dermal density.** The wrinkle/MMP/procollagen story that powers an "anti-aging" pitch is **in-vitro + a single ORAL mouse study** — i.e., the right *mechanism* but the wrong *route and species* for a topical anti-aging claim.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell & biochemical)

- **Widowati W. et al., 2020, *Trop. Life Sci. Res.* 31(3):127–144 (PMC7652251).** Mangosteen peel extract (MPE) + four isolated xanthones tested for anti-aging enzyme inhibition + antioxidant + molecular docking. **α-Mangostin anti-collagenase IC₅₀ = 9.75 µg/mL** (most potent); MPE anti-elastase IC₅₀ = 7.40; α-mangostin anti-hyaluronidase IC₅₀ = 16.19; γ-mangostin anti-tyrosinase IC₅₀ = 50.35 (α-mangostin 70.92). FRAP/H₂O₂-scavenging confirm antioxidant capacity. Docking: α-mangostin–MMP-1 −8.9 kcal/mol. *Limitation: purely biochemical/cell + in-silico; isolated enzymes, not skin.* [1]
- **Im A.-R. et al., 2017 (in-vitro arm), *Int. J. Mol. Med.* 40(6):1941–1949 (DOI 10.3892/ijmm.2017.3188).** Mechanistic support for ↓MMP-1/-9, ↓IL-1β/IL-6/TNF-α, ↓ERK/p38/JNK phosphorylation, ↑SOD/catalase. [2]
- **Kongpol K. / "Anti-Senescence and Anti-Photoaging…", 2025, *Cosmetics* 12(3):108 (DOI 10.3390/cosmetics12030108).** Primary human dermal fibroblasts, UVA model; MPE (α-mangostin 60.9 ± 1.2% by HPLC). **Senescent cells 12.0% → ~7.1% (↓~41%); MMP-1 ↓~39% (38,929 → 23,873 pg/mL); procollagen-I ↑~52% (37,137 → 56,443 pg/mL); ROS sharply reduced under H₂O₂.** *First report of MPE photoprotection on UVA-induced senescence. Limitation: in vitro, one lab, pretreatment design.* [3]
- **Dang Y. et al., 2023, *Int. J. Mol. Sci.* 24(16):12930 (DOI 10.3390/ijms241612930; PMC10455244).** α-Mangostin is a **potent, selective TRPV3 inhibitor (IC₅₀ 0.077 ± 0.013 µM**; TRPV4 8.47 µM; TRPA1 5.09 µM); in carvacrol-stimulated keratinocytes it markedly cut **IL-8** and modestly **IL-6**; rescued Olmsted-mutant TRPV3 toxicity. *Limitation: in vitro; channel/cytokine endpoints, no skin/clinical.* [12]
- **Abate M. et al., 2022, *Pharmaceuticals (Basel)* — mangostanin in HaCaT keratinocytes.** Mangostanin (1.25–40 µM; 10 µM for mechanism) both **protected** (pre-treat) and **repaired** (post-treat) H₂O₂-oxidative damage; ROS 89.9% → 64.4%; blocked AKT/ERK/p53/p38/EGFR phosphorylation and caspase-3/9 cleavage. *Limitation: a different xanthone (mangostanin), in vitro.* [15]
- **Anti-melanogenic mechanism (B16):** α-mangostin ↓tyrosinase, ↓MITF/TYR/TRP-2 via GSK3β/ERK and RAR/RXR. [13][14] **α-Mangostin as a "safe natural skin-whitening agent," 2025, *Sci. Rep.* (s41598-025-31047-5)** — tyrosinase inhibition / melanin reduction, MITF-protective antioxidant framing; cell/lower-organism models. [13]
- **Antibacterial (acne-relevant):** pericarp extract / α-mangostin active vs *Cutibacterium acnes*, *S. epidermidis*, *S. aureus* (incl. MRSA). [17]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)

- **Im A.-R. et al., 2017, *Int. J. Mol. Med.* 40(6):1941–1949 — the headline photoaging study (but ORAL).** HR-1 hairless mice, **oral α-mangostin 100 mg/kg/day** through 12 weeks of UVB. **Blocked UVB wrinkle formation; reduced UVB-induced epidermal hyperplasia (UVB-vehicle ~221 µm → α-mangostin ~118.7 µm, toward the non-irradiated control ~55 µm — i.e. it *attenuated* UVB thickening, not "doubled thickness"); ↓MMP-1/-9; ↓IL-1β/IL-6/TNF-α mRNA; ↑SOD/catalase; ↓ERK/p38/JNK.** *Critical limitation: n=5/group, and **administered ORALLY, not topically** — this is the study most often (mis)cited as topical anti-aging proof.* [2]
- **MRSA superficial skin-infection model (mouse).** Topical *G. mangostana* pericarp extract → complete wound healing by day 10 with epidermal regeneration and reduced inflammatory infiltrate. *Anti-infective/wound, not anti-aging.* [15][17]
- **Photoprotective-cream / 8-OHdG mouse study (albino mice, UVB).** MPE photoprotective cream reduced UVB-induced 8-OHdG (oxidative DNA damage marker). *Animal; DNA-damage marker, supportive of photoprotection.* [16-related; details partly unverified]
- **Anti-skin-cancer (mouse/cell).** Phenolic-rich pericarp extract showed anti-skin-cancer activity (chemoprevention framing). *Disease/cancer model — do not import into cosmetic claims.* [19]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL / systemic (NOT skin; included for honesty + the safety flag)

- **Functional-beverage antioxidant study (PMC4304560).** Mangosteen beverage raised plasma antioxidant capacity in healthy adults. *Oral; plasma marker, not skin.* [20]
- **Gingivitis RCT (mangosteen + propolis complex, multi-center).** Improved clinical/immunological gingivitis measures. *Oral cavity, not facial skin.* [21]
- **The oral SAFETY signal — Wong & Klemmer, 2008, *Am. J. Kidney Dis.* 51(5):829–833 (PMID 18436094).** **Single case report** of **severe lactic acidosis** in a man drinking mangosteen juice as a daily supplement (~>1 year). Rare and oral-route-specific, but a real reason to keep mangosteen **topical-only** in this brand and never make oral-beauty claims. [22]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters — and it's ACNE, not anti-aging)

- **Lueangarun S. et al., 2019, *J. Cosmet. Dermatol.* 18(5):1395–1403 (PMID 30688020) — ACNE RCT #1.** **12-week, split-face, double-blinded, randomized, controlled trial**, n=28 (24 female; mean age 25.1). **0.5% mangosteen extract nanoparticle gel (α-mangostin)** vs **1% clindamycin**, twice daily (both sides also got 2.5% BPO once daily, 5 min). **Comedones ↓66.86%, inflammatory lesions ↓67.05% (both P<0.001); improvement from week 2; mangosteen ≥ clindamycin; no severe AEs.** *Strong design but: small, acne not aging, and a BPO co-treatment confounds the absolute effect.* [17]
- **Mangosteen 1% extract gel vs benzoyl peroxide 5% — ACNE RCT #2 (J. Philippine Dermatological Society).** Randomized comparative trial; mangosteen 1% gel vs BPO 5% in mild-to-moderate acne; reported comparable efficacy/safety. *Acne, not aging; full text not retrieved (journal site down at access) — design verified via abstract/index.* [23 — details partly unverified]
- **Djawad K. et al., 2020, *J. Clin. Aesthet. Dermatol.* 13(10):E59–E65 — controlled human UVB-erythema study (the closest thing to a human anti-photoaging trial).** **Double-blind, randomized, controlled**, n=31 healthy subjects (Fitzpatrick III–IV). **5/10/20% MPE nano-liposomal cream** vs base cream vs UVB-only, erythema by chromameter Δa* after 2 MED UVB. **10% (Δa* −0.07) and 20% (Δa* 0.16) creams ≈ fully prevented UVB erythema vs UVB-only Δa* 5.585; 5% less protective (Δa* 1.03).** *Real controlled human topical data — but the endpoint is acute redness PREVENTION, not wrinkle/firmness improvement; it is a sunburn-protection signal, not an anti-aging-treatment signal.* [16]
- **Chairunisa et al., 2022, *Pharmaceutics* 14(12):2741 (DOI 10.3390/pharmaceutics14122741) — anti-age-spot serum PILOT.** Ultra-small unilamellar carrier (USUC/Nanotope™) α-mangostin serum, particle ~16.5 nm, EE 72.5%. **Human pilot: only n=2 women (57 & 40), 2 weeks nightly → significant ↓ spot intensity/area, ↑brightness (p<0.05).** *This is the only human "anti-aging-adjacent" topical data and it is n=2 — essentially anecdotal, not a trial.* [4]

**Bottom line by tier:** Proven for mangosteen/α-mangostin specifically → potent antioxidant + anti-collagenase/anti-MMP + anti-inflammatory (NF-κB/MAPK/NLRP3) + potent TRPV3 block (cells), and UVB-photoaging reversal in **orally-dosed** mice. For **human topical** use: **two solid acne RCTs**, **one controlled UVB-erythema (redness-prevention) study**, and a **2-person** age-spot pilot. **There is no controlled human topical trial for wrinkles, elasticity, firmness, or dermal density.** The anti-aging positioning is mechanistically credible but **clinically unproven in humans by the topical route for aging endpoints.**

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **The core problem: α-mangostin is poorly water-soluble and lipophilic.** [10] A plain water serum cannot carry meaningful dissolved α-mangostin; undissolved xanthone is inactive (and gritty/tinting).
- **Penetration is plausible *with* delivery, and the literature leans hard on delivery systems.** Every promising topical study used an engineered carrier: **nanoparticle gel** (acne RCT) [17], **nano-liposomal cream** (UVB-erythema) [16], **ultra-small unilamellar vesicles ~16.5 nm** (age-spot pilot) [4], plus nanoemulsions and nanostructured lipid carriers in formulation papers. The ~16–40 nm vesicle work explicitly argues sub-50 nm particles slip through intercorneocyte channels. [4]
- **No human quantitative penetration/flux data** establishing how much α-mangostin reaches viable epidermis/dermis from a finished consumer formula, and **no evidence** that a topical product reproduces the cell/mouse mechanisms (MMP↓, senescence↓) in living human skin.
- **Practical read:** Treat delivery (encapsulation, oil/ester or glycol/ethanol solubilization, or a standardized solubilized extract) as a **formulation requirement**, not an afterthought. The pericarp **extract** (xanthone blend at low total %) is easier to formulate than high-% pure α-mangostin.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** No validated anti-aging dose exists. Reference points from human topical work: **0.5% extract (nanoparticle) for acne** [17]; **10–20% MPE nano-liposomal cream for UVB-erythema** (5% under-performed) [16]; cosmetic extracts are commonly used at **~0.1–5%** [11]. For a leave-on antioxidant/soothing role, a **solubilized/encapsulated pericarp extract standardized to α-mangostin, used at low total %**, is the pragmatic path; do not imply a proven anti-aging dose-response.
- **Solubilization is mandatory.** Use oil/ester phase, glycol/ethanol carriers, or encapsulation (liposome/nanoemulsion/USUC). Undissolved α-mangostin recrystallizes and won't perform. [4][10]
- **pH:** No narrow published optimum; xanthone phenolics are generally more stable mildly acidic-to-neutral and can oxidize/darken under alkaline conditions. Target **~5–6** and run stability.
- **Stability / color:** α-Mangostin is a **yellow pigment** — expect color contribution and watch for drift/oxidation with light, heat, and metals. Protect with antioxidants/chelators and opaque packaging; verify color over time. It is itself an antioxidant, which helps formula resilience.
- **Pairings (conceptual):** Niacinamide (barrier/tone, well-tolerated, complementary anti-inflammatory) [glossary]; vitamin C / E and other antioxidants (network defense — mind vitamin C's low-pH need); ceramides/barrier lipids (the brand's barrier thesis); SPF systems (mangosteen's strongest human signal is UV-redness prevention — pairs naturally with, **but never replaces**, sunscreen). For acne formats, the evidence pairs it with BPO/clindamycin. [17]
- **Conflicts:** Strong oxidizers; high-pH systems (discoloration). Co-formulating with low-pH vitamin C is workable but watch the solubility/stability of both.
- **Sensory:** Yellow tint; pericarp extracts can carry a faint earthy/astringent botanical note; keep levels modest in delicate textures.

## 7. Safety

- **Topical irritation/sensitization:** Generally well-tolerated at cosmetic levels; **EWG hazard ~1**; the human topical studies (acne RCTs, UVB-erythema, age-spot pilot) reported **no severe adverse events.** [11][16][17] α-Mangostin is itself **anti-inflammatory** in skin (TRPV3 block, NF-κB↓). No CIR monograph specific to purified topical α-mangostin was located — generate **finished-formula tolerability (HRIPT/patch) testing**. [no CIR monograph found — partly unverified]
- **Allergen/sourcing note:** Rare allergic reactions possible in those sensitive to tropical fruits; patch testing advised. [11]
- **Sun:** No photosensitization signal; mechanistically **photoprotective** (human UVB-erythema prevention) [16] — but **not a sunscreen and must not be implied as one.**
- **Dose-dependent cytotoxicity / paradoxical effects (mostly oral/high-dose context).** At high in-vitro concentrations α-mangostin can be **cytotoxic** (e.g., >8 µg/mL antiproliferative in some cell lines), can **raise TNF-α** in unstimulated macrophages, and **worsened DSS-colitis** in one dietary study — i.e., its anti-inflammatory effect is **context/dose-dependent**, not universal. [9] Relevant mainly to high oral doses; keep topical levels modest and avoid systemic positioning.
- **Oral safety flag (keep the brand topical-only):** A **case report of severe lactic acidosis** from chronic mangosteen-juice supplementation. [22] Mangosteen may also slow clotting and interact with chemotherapy in oral contexts. None of this applies to a low-% topical, but it's a clear reason **not** to make ingestible-beauty / "drink your mangosteen" claims.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** No topical pregnancy data; given oral cautions, advise conservative copy ("consult your doctor"). [22]
- **Pigmentation caution:** Tone benefit is **pericarp/α-mangostin-specific**; **leaf extract can increase melanin** — don't generalize "evens tone" across plant parts. [18]

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic ingredient status:** **Garcinia Mangostana Peel Extract** is an established cosmetic ingredient (COSING 56319; skin-conditioning/antioxidant/soothing). [11] α-Mangostin appears in finished cosmetics as the standardized active. Confirm legality and any limits per target market before formulating.
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** "Treats acne," "antibacterial," "heals wounds," "anti-inflammatory," "boosts collagen," "reverses photoaging," "protects DNA," "anticancer/chemoprevention" all push into **structure/function or disease (drug) claims** — and the strongest mangosteen evidence (acne, antibacterial, wound healing, UVB DNA-damage, cancer) is exactly the disease-claim territory we must avoid for a cosmetic.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "antioxidant-rich xanthones from mangosteen peel," "helps defend the look of skin against environmental/UV-induced stress (in lab studies)," "helps calm the look of stressed/red-looking skin," "supports a more even-looking tone," "helps improve the appearance of an aged-looking complexion," "inspired by photoaging-defense science."
- ⚠️ **Risky / substantiation-or-legal-review:** "clears acne / antibacterial / kills *C. acnes*," "anti-inflammatory," "reduces wrinkles by X%," "boosts collagen," "repairs sun damage / protects DNA," "SPF / sun protection," any acne/eczema/cancer **treatment** claim. The acne RCTs are real but are **drug-territory** efficacy and were on **BPO-combined / comparator** regimens.
- **Substantiation reality:** The human topical wins are **acne** and **UV-erythema prevention**, not anti-aging. Any **anti-aging** performance claim for our product would need our **own** placebo-controlled, blinded finished-formula testing on aging endpoints — the existing literature does not provide it. ## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Forms/suppliers:** Standardized **pericarp extracts** (often specified to α-mangostin %) from botanical-extract houses (heavily Thailand/SE-Asia sourced — mangosteen is a regional crop); **purified α-mangostin** from fine-chemical/actives suppliers (CAS 6147-11-1); **encapsulated/nano grades** from specialty cosmeceutical suppliers. Pericarp is an **agricultural by-product ("upcycled" peel)** — a real sustainability angle.
- **Cost tier:** **Mid to mid-high.** Crude pericarp extract is moderately priced; **high-α-mangostin standardized, high-purity, and especially delivery-enhanced (liposomal/nanoemulsion/USUC) grades cost more** — and a delivery system is effectively required, raising true formulated cost.
- **Supply/standardization risk:** α-Mangostin content varies widely by part, cultivar, ripeness, and extraction; **require a COA with quantified α-mangostin and confirmed plant part (pericarp, not leaf).** Color/odor batch variability is real.
- **Upcycling story:** Pericarp is normally discarded fruit waste — a credible, low-greenwash sustainability narrative if sourced transparently.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **The route + indication switch.** The most-cited "anti-aging" mouse study dosed α-mangostin **orally**, not topically [2], and the strongest *human* topical data are for **acne** and **UV-redness prevention**, not wrinkles. Selling a topical "mangosteen anti-wrinkle" product as if proven imports the wrong route *and* the wrong indication.
- **"Clinically proven anti-aging."** There is **no controlled human topical trial for wrinkles/firmness/elasticity.** The only anti-aging-adjacent human topical data is an **n=2** age-spot pilot. [4] Calling that "clinically proven" is indefensible.
- **"Two clinical trials!"** True — but both are **acne** RCTs (and one combined BPO on both sides). [17][23] They support an acne/antibacterial story (drug territory), not a cosmetic anti-aging story.
- **"Powerful antioxidant — proven on skin."** The antioxidant/anti-MMP data are excellent but **in vitro/animal**; no human study shows a finished topical reproduces them in living skin.
- **"Evens skin tone."** Only for **pericarp/α-mangostin**, and only cell-level — and **leaf extract does the opposite** (↑melanin). [18] Source-specific and unproven in human skin.
- **Solubility is the inconvenient truth.** α-Mangostin barely dissolves in water; a plain "X% mangosteen" water serum may carry mostly inactive, undissolved (and tinting) xanthone. [10]
- **"Mangosteen = the fruit's health halo."** The XanGo-style oral hype is a different product, and oral mangosteen carries a **lactic-acidosis** case report. [22] Don't borrow the juice halo for a topical.

---

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING — a genuinely strong antioxidant / anti-inflammatory / anti-photoaging botanical with rich mechanism and a real (upcycled) sustainability story, but its only solid human-topical proof is for ACNE and UV-redness, not anti-aging.**
**One-line:** Mangosteen pericarp / α-mangostin is one of the better-characterized antioxidant botanicals for skin — potent in-vitro **anti-collagenase + MMP-1/-9 suppression + procollagen↑ + NF-κB/MAPK/NLRP3 + a standout TRPV3 block**, with **two real human acne RCTs** and a **controlled human UVB-erythema** study — but it has **no controlled human topical trial for aging endpoints**, depends on a **delivery system** (poor solubility), and carries a **plant-part pigmentation contradiction** plus an **oral-route safety flag**. Use it as a science-forward **supporting** antioxidant/soothing/anti-photoaging active with strictly honest, mechanism-framed copy and an upcycling angle — not a proof-backed anti-aging hero.

**Longevity-relevance note:** For a *skin-longevity* brand, mangosteen's most on-thesis levers are **oxidative-stress defense, inflammaging suppression (NF-κB/NLRP3/TRPV3), MMP-driven ECM protection, and a single promising UVA-induced-fibroblast-senescence signal.** That's a coherent "defend the dermis from photoaging" story — but it is **borrowed-format** (cells/oral mice + human acne/sunburn data), not demonstrated for human topical aging. It slots cleanly into an **antioxidant + barrier + photoprotection** routine (pair with niacinamide/ceramides; let SPF do the sun work). If the brand ever wants real topical anti-aging claims, the honest path is a **delivery-optimized, α-mangostin-standardized extract + our own placebo-controlled human testing on aging endpoints** — the literature gives mechanism and a great narrative, not the clinical proof.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

_Verified 2026-06-19 by independent re-check of primary sources (PubMed, PMC, publisher full text, MDPI, journal indexes). Re-checked against full text: Widowati 2020 (PMC7652251), Im 2017 (Spandidos full text), Lueangarun 2019 (PubMed 30688020), Dang 2023 (PMC10455244), Djawad 2020 (JCAD full text). Each headline claim tested for: (a) HUMAN + TOPICAL vs borrowed in-vitro/animal/oral; (b) citation real and faithfully represented; (c) industry-funded or tiny. **One factual correction made on this pass: the Im 2017 epidermal-thickness figure (see audit table).**_

### Overall grade: **C+ (moderate mechanism; human-topical proof exists but is OFF-TARGET for anti-aging)**

The antioxidant/anti-inflammatory/anti-MMP/TRPV3 **mechanisms are genuine, replicated, and well-cited** (would be B+/A− as *mechanistic* science). But for a **skin-longevity / anti-aging** brand the relevant endpoint is human topical *aging* benefit, and there the evidence is **absent**: the only human topical RCTs are **acne** [17][23], the only controlled human anti-photoaging data is **UVB-erythema prevention** [16], and the only anti-aging-adjacent topical data is an **n=2** pilot [4]. The flagship "anti-wrinkle" mouse study is **oral** [2]. So this is a **strong mechanistic supporting active with an off-target human-evidence base** — graded C+ for the anti-aging use case specifically.

### Funding / size flags
- **Acne RCTs (n=28; comparative):** small, single-center, cosmetic/derm trials commonly sponsor-associated (not verified); BPO co-treatment confounds absolute effect. [17][23]
- **UVB-erythema (n=31):** controlled but a short acute-exposure redness study, not a treatment trial. [16]
- **Age-spot pilot (n=2):** delivery-system paper; the human arm is anecdotal. [4]
- **Mechanistic work:** multiple independent academic labs (Thailand/Korea/Italy) — credible, but cell/animal.

### Claims to keep softened (do NOT overstate in marketing)
1. **No "clinically proven anti-aging / reduces wrinkles."** Zero controlled human topical aging trials; the n=2 spot pilot is not proof.
2. **No "treats acne / antibacterial / anti-inflammatory."** The acne RCTs are real but **drug-territory** and BPO-combined.
3. **No "sun protection / SPF."** The human signal is UVB-redness *prevention* in a study, not a sunscreen.
4. **"Evens tone" only for pericarp/α-mangostin, cell-level — leaf does the opposite.** Specify plant part; don't claim human tone benefit.
5. **Keep it topical.** Do not borrow the oral "mangosteen superfruit" halo; oral carries a lactic-acidosis case report.
6. **No "thickens / rebuilds the epidermis."** The mouse data show α-mangostin *attenuating* UVB-induced epidermal hyperplasia (a return toward normal), not growing thicker skin — and it was oral. Do not frame the 118.7 µm figure as an increase.

**Net:** A legitimately strong **antioxidant / anti-inflammatory / anti-photoaging botanical** with rich, well-cited mechanism, a standout TRPV3 action, and a real upcycling story — but its human topical proof is for **acne and sunburn redness, not aging**, it needs a **delivery system**, and it has a **plant-part pigmentation contradiction** and an **oral safety flag**. Genuine **supporting** active; mechanism-and-narrative, not anti-aging performance. Graded **C+** for the anti-aging use case.
