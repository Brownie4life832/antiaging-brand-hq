# Ingredient: C-Xyloside / C-Glycoside (INCI: Hydroxypropyl Tetrahydropyrantriol)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class (as filed):** Anti-glycation & related — **see honesty flag below; this is a misfile**

> **One-line honest summary:** C-Xyloside is a small synthetic sugar (a "C-glycoside" / xyloside mimic) whose real, repeatedly-demonstrated action is **kick-starting the skin's own production of glycosaminoglycans (GAGs) and proteoglycans** — the water-holding, matrix-organizing molecules that decline with age — and, downstream, **reorganizing the dermal-epidermal junction (DEJ)**. The mechanistic and ex-vivo science is solid and from a serious lab (L'Oréal). The **human topical** efficacy evidence is real but thin and almost entirely **manufacturer-run**: one small placebo-controlled ultrastructure study (n=5), one uncontrolled single-arm cream study, and combination studies where it rode alongside other actives. It is **not an anti-glycation ingredient** despite the class it was filed under — it does not inhibit AGE formation.

> **⚠️ Class-misfile flag (read this first).** The master index filed `c-xyloside` under **"Anti-glycation & related."** That is inaccurate. C-Xyloside's mechanism is **GAG/proteoglycan biosynthesis and DEJ repair**, not glycation inhibition. No primary source shows it traps methylglyoxal, blocks AGE cross-linking, or breaks existing AGEs. The only honest "glycation-adjacent" reading is loose: it *restores the dermal matrix that glycation damages*, so it sits in the same broad "matrix/ECM-aging" conversation. **It belongs in a "GAG / proteoglycan inducers" or "ECM / DEJ" class.** Re-tier accordingly. Do not let copy imply anti-glycation activity for this molecule.

## 1. Identity

- **Common names:** C-Xyloside, C-Glycoside, "Pro-Xylane™" (L'Oréal trade name). Marketing also calls it a "sugar-protein" — it is **not** a protein; it is a small sugar derivative. Ignore "sugar-protein."
- **INCI:** **Hydroxypropyl Tetrahydropyrantriol**
- **Chemical name:** C-β-D-xylopyranoside-2-hydroxy-propane (a C-linked xyloside). PubChem lists it under the descriptor "1,5-Anhydro-6,8-dideoxy-…-octitol." [1]
- **CAS:** 439685-79-7 (racemic/principal). 868156-46-1 is also seen for the (S)-enantiomer. [1][12]
- **Molecular formula / weight:** C₈H₁₆O₅ · **192.21 g/mol** — a *very* small, water-soluble molecule (relevant to penetration; §5). [1][12]
- **Source / chemistry:** Derived from **xylose**, a sugar abundant in **beechwood** hemicellulose (xylan). Made synthetically in two aqueous-phase steps (xylose + acetylacetone → C-glycoside intermediate → hydrogenation), explicitly designed under "green chemistry" principles. [12]
- **Why "C-glycoside" matters:** In nature, GAG chains are built on a **xylose linked to a core protein via an O-glycosidic bond** (the GAG–protein linkage region). Natural and early synthetic **β-xylosides** act as "decoy" primers that trigger free GAG-chain synthesis, but their **O-glycosidic bond is enzymatically/chemically fragile**. C-Xyloside swaps that O-link for a **C–C bond**, giving the same xyloside "decoy" signal with far greater **metabolic stability** — the whole point of the molecule. [3][6][12]

> **Nomenclature trap (important for the brand).** The literature uses **"C-Xyloside"** and **"Pro-Xylane"** loosely, and many vendor/marketing pages treat them as identical. They are *closely related but not strictly the same thing*:
> - The **early research compound** ("C-Xyloside" / "a new C-xylopyranoside derivative," 2008–2012 L'Oréal papers) is the C-β-D-xylopyranoside-2-hydroxy-propane derivative used in the mechanistic and clinical studies below.
> - **Pro-Xylane™** is the *commercialized, optimized* C-glycoside (the Cavezza 2009 synthesis paper calls the best GAG activator "compound 20 = Pro-Xylane"), and is the molecule sold under **INCI Hydroxypropyl Tetrahydropyrantriol**.
> In practice the published efficacy data and the commercial ingredient are treated as the same C-glycoside family, and the INCI you will buy and label is **Hydroxypropyl Tetrahydropyrantriol**. This dossier treats them as one ingredient *family* but flags wherever a specific study used the research-grade "C-Xyloside" vs. the commercial HPT/Pro-Xylane. Don't let a supplier upsell "C-Xyloside" as something distinct from Hydroxypropyl Tetrahydropyrantriol without a COA proving it.

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it works at the skin/cell level)

C-Xyloside is best understood as a **biomimetic GAG-synthesis primer (xyloside mimic)** that restarts the matrix-building machinery of aged skin.

Mechanistic pillars (most established in cell / 3D-tissue models):

1. **GAG-chain priming (the core action).** As a stable C-linked xyloside, it acts like the natural xylose primer at the start of a GAG chain, **stimulating fibroblasts and keratinocytes to synthesize free glycosaminoglycan chains** (chondroitin sulfate / dermatan sulfate predominate; hyaluronic-acid and heparan-sulfate pathways also reported). [3][4][5][6]
2. **Proteoglycan / heparan-sulfate restoration.** Beyond free GAGs, it restores expression of **heparan-sulfate proteoglycans** (e.g., perlecan, syndecans) in atrophic/aged skin models. [2]
3. **DEJ (basement-membrane) reorganization.** Downstream of restored proteoglycans, it increases key **DEJ/basement-membrane proteins** — **collagen IV and VII, laminin-332 (laminin-5), nidogen, integrins α6/β1** — improving the architecture of the dermal-epidermal junction. [4][7][8]
4. **Pro-migratory / repair signaling.** In keratinocytes it raises **dermatan-sulfate** output and, together with FGF-10, **promotes keratinocyte migration and proliferation** — a wound/repair-adjacent action. [5]
5. **GAG *quality*, not just quantity.** The primed GAG chains are structurally distinct (shorter, differently sulfated, altered iduronate spacing) and show **altered growth-factor binding** (e.g., reduced HGF binding) — i.e., it changes the matrix's *functional* properties, not only its mass. [6]

**Skin-relevant translation (honest):** Pillars 1–3 are well-demonstrated in cultured human cells and 3D reconstructed/ex-vivo human skin. Pillar 3 is the one with a (small) *living-human* readout (Deloche 2011, §4). What is **not** established: that a finished cosmetic at typical concentrations produces clinically meaningful, vehicle-controlled wrinkle/firmness gains in living human skin on its own. The mechanism is more proven than the topical clinical payoff.

Honest positioning keeps it in its lane: **dermal matrix and junction support → firmness, plumpness, smoothness.**

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** The deep, repeatable science is **in-vitro and ex-vivo human-tissue** mechanism work, almost all from **one corporate lab (L'Oréal Recherche)**. For **living-human topical** efficacy there is essentially **one small (n=5) placebo-controlled study measuring ultrastructure (not visible wrinkles)**, **one uncontrolled single-arm cosmetic-cream study**, and **two combination studies** where C-Xyloside/HPT was bundled with other actives or a laser procedure. There are **no large, independent, vehicle-controlled RCTs of C-Xyloside as a standalone topical active.** No oral/ingestion human data exists or is relevant (it is a topical cosmetic ingredient).

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell & 3D models)

- **Cavezza et al., 2009, *Bioorganic & Medicinal Chemistry Letters* 19(3):845–849 (PMID 19135365).** The synthesis paper. Establishes the C-glycoside chemistry, the aqueous "green" two-step route, and screens analogs — identifies **"compound 20" (Pro-Xylane)** as the **best GAG-biosynthesis activator** of the series. Foundational identity/mechanism reference. *Limitation: chemistry + screening, not efficacy.* [12]
- **Pineau et al., 2008, *European Journal of Dermatology* 18(1):36–40 (PMID 18086587, DOI 10.1684/ejd.2008.0308).** In an **organotypic model of corticosteroid-atrophic human skin** (a proxy for thinned/aged skin), the C-xylopyranoside derivative **restored heparan-sulfate–proteoglycan expression**. *Limitation: in-vitro disease-proxy model; no living-human readout.* [2]
- **Pineau et al., 2011, *European Journal of Dermatology* 21(3):359–370 (PMID 21609902, DOI 10.1684/ejd.2011.1340).** Cultured **human dermal fibroblasts**: C-Xyloside **acts like β-xylosides to increase GAG synthesis**; authors argue this "may contribute to matrix integrity and skin firmness." *Limitation: monolayer fibroblasts; mechanistic, not clinical; concentrations/effect sizes not in abstract.* [3]
- **Muto et al., 2011, *PLoS ONE* 6(10):e25480 (PMC3187761, DOI 10.1371/journal.pone.0025480).** Normal human **keratinocytes**: C-Xyloside (0.1–10 mM; min. effective ~10 µM) raised **sulfated GAG** secretion (~8 µg/mL at 24 h, ~10.6 µg/mL at 48 h at 1 mM), predominantly **dermatan sulfate**; with **FGF-10** it **promoted keratinocyte migration and proliferation** (wound-closure assay). CHO-745 (xylosyltransferase-deficient) controls confirm the xyloside-priming mechanism. *Limitation: in-vitro only; authors explicitly call for topical in-vivo confirmation.* [5]
- **Vassal-Stermann et al., 2012, *PLoS ONE* 7(10):e47933 (PMC3482234, DOI 10.1371/journal.pone.0047933).** Reconstructed human dermis (fibroblasts), 7.5 mM × 48 h: **~15-fold increase in secreted GAG chains**; primed chains were CS/DS, **shorter (~15 vs ~50 kDa)**, **more sulfated**, with **altered iduronate distribution** and **reduced HGF binding** — i.e., it changes GAG *structure and function*, not just amount. *Limitation: single cell type; one functional (HGF) assay; no in-vivo.* [6]

### Tier 2 — Ex-vivo / reconstructed full human-skin (DEJ)

- **Sok et al., 2008, *European Journal of Dermatology* 18(3):297–302 (PMID 18474459, DOI 10.1684/ejd.2008.0392).** In **human reconstructed skin**, the C-xylopyranoside derivative **improved DEJ morphogenesis** — higher deposition of basement-membrane/DEJ proteins, **↑collagen VII** expression, better-organized junction. *Limitation: in-vitro reconstructed tissue, not living human; manufacturer lab.* [7]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL

- **None, and not applicable.** C-Xyloside is a topical cosmetic ingredient; there is no oral/ingestion literature. (No "borrowed from supplements" story here — unlike many longevity actives.)

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL

**4a — Placebo/vehicle-controlled (the gold tier — and it is nearly empty):**

- **Deloche et al., 2011, *European Journal of Dermatology* 21(2):191–196 (PMID 21454149).** The **only standalone placebo-controlled human topical study** of C-Xyloside. **Randomized, placebo-controlled pilot, n = 5 postmenopausal women (ages 60–75)**; **10% C-Xyloside** vs. placebo, 320 mg (2 mg/cm²) per **outer forearm**, **twice daily × 3 months**. Transmission electron microscopy showed **more homogeneous/regular lamina densa**, **strikingly reduced basement-membrane re-duplication**, and statistically significant **↑α6-integrin and ↑laminin-332** in treated vs. placebo forearm. *Limitations: tiny n (5); **forearm, not face**; **ultrastructural endpoint, not visible wrinkle/clinical improvement**; high concentration (10%) far above typical cosmetic use; manufacturer (L'Oréal Recherche) study. This proves a **biological effect in living skin** but does **not** prove a **cosmetically visible** benefit.* [8]

- **Ablon et al., 2025, *Journal of Clinical & Aesthetic Dermatology* 18(12):66–72.** **Randomized, double-blind, split-face, multicenter** — but it tests a **combination** and as a **laser adjuvant**, not C-Xyloside alone. Formula: **10% Hydroxypropyl Tetrahydropyrantriol + 3% niacinamide** (O/W emulsion, 2 mg/cm² BID) vs. **Aquaphor** control after **non-ablative HALO laser**. n≈29 (25 completers) Caucasian women + 12 NE-Asian women. Active side beat control for **fine lines/wrinkles by Day 14, sustained to Day 56**; NE-Asian panel: superior on **6 of 8 parameters** at Day 56 (incl. under-eye wrinkles, tone evenness). *Limitations: **combination product** (effect not attributable to HPT alone; niacinamide is itself active), **vs. an occlusive ointment not a matched vehicle**, post-laser context, small n, no single-active arm.* [10]

**4b — Lower-tier human topical (uncontrolled / combination / manufacturer):**

- **Zang et al., 2024, *Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology* 23:4017–4028 (PMID 39107974, PMC11626324, DOI 10.1111/jocd.16489).** A "facial cream enriched with C-Xyloside." **Two single-arm, uncontrolled before/after studies**: (1) n=49 Chinese women 30–50, BID × 8 wk; (2) n=30 Chinese women 25–60 (sensitive/redness), BID × 4 wk — **plus** ex-vivo and 3D-model arms. Reported in-use results: **hydration +21.9%**, elasticity **R2 +13.1% / R5 +12.3%**, **TEWL −33.9%**, wrinkle-score drops of **34–56%** by region; tape-strip study showed **↑rCE/fCE** (corneocyte maturity, p<0.05). Ex-vivo: **↑collagen III (~3.5×)**; 3D epidermis: **↑filaggrin (~7.4×), ↑loricrin (~6×)**. *Limitations (the authors admit two): **single concentration and small sample**; and critically **no vehicle/placebo control → cannot separate the active from the base cream, regression to the mean, or expectation effects**; **concentration of C-Xyloside not disclosed**; all authors are **L'Oréal (China) employees**; the wrinkle endpoints are subjective dermatologist scoring. Treat the headline percentages as **uncontrolled in-use** numbers, not proof of intrinsic efficacy.* [9]

- **Jiang et al., 2025, *Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology* (DOI 10.1111/jocd.70281).** Randomized, investigator-blinded, split-face: a topical with **blueberry extract + Pro-Xylane** combined with **micro-focused ultrasound (MFU)** for anti-aging. Again a **combination + procedure adjuvant**, not isolated C-Xyloside. *Limitation: cannot attribute effect to Pro-Xylane; full text not retrieved (abstract-level).* [11] *(I could not open the full text; treat as supportive context, not standalone proof.)*

**Bottom line by tier:** Mechanism (Tiers 1–2) = **strong and coherent**. Standalone living-human topical efficacy (Tier 4a) = **one tiny ultrastructure study + one combination laser RCT**. Real-world cosmetic claims rest mostly on **uncontrolled, manufacturer, combination** data (Tier 4b). The evidence is **better than a typical "fairy-dust" botanical** but **well short of** the human-RCT base behind retinoids, niacinamide, or vitamin C.

## 5. Topical bioavailability

- **Small + water-soluble:** MW **192 g/mol**, highly water-soluble — well under the ~500 Da "rule-of-500" penetration ceiling, so **stratum-corneum penetration is plausible** and better than for large peptides or HA. [1][12]
- **Hydrophilic tradeoff:** Being very polar/water-loving, it partitions into water more than into the lipid stratum corneum; humectant-rich, occlusive-supported vehicles help. No published human flux/penetration kinetics found — penetration is **inferred from size/solubility**, not measured. *[partly unverified — no quantitative in-vivo penetration study located]*
- **It is a "primer," not a payload:** Its job is to reach **living fibroblasts/keratinocytes** and trigger their GAG machinery — and the ex-vivo and n=5 human data show it *can* exert a biological effect in skin at high dose. The open question is the **dose actually delivered** at cosmetic concentrations.

## 6. Formulation (concentration, pH, stability, pairings, sensory)

- **Typical cosmetic use level:** Commonly **0.5–3%** of the active in finished products; the standout *research* studies used **10%** (Deloche; Ablon HPT). One catalogued consumer product lists **1.5%**. Sold by suppliers most often as a **~30% aqueous solution** (so a "10% Pro-Xylane solution" ≠ 10% active — check the COA basis). [9][13][14]
- **pH:** Tolerant and easy. Stable across roughly **pH ~3.8–7.0** in formula; a 1% aqueous solution sits around pH 6.5–8.0. Compatible with low-pH (vitamin C, AHA) and neutral systems. [13]
- **Phase / solubility:** **Water-soluble**; add to the **water phase**. Colorless-to-pale-yellow clear liquid. [13][14]
- **Heat/stability:** Withstands processing heat up to ~**80 °C**; store away from prolonged heat/light; keep solutions refrigerated for long-term. Chemically stable C–C glycoside (the whole design rationale) — no special antioxidant/chelation demands like ascorbic acid needs. [13]
- **Pairings (rational, evidence-aligned):**
  - **Niacinamide** — co-formulated in the strongest combination human study; complementary barrier/tone + matrix. [10]
  - **Hyaluronic acid / humectants** — synergistic with the "build your own GAGs + add water-binders" story.
  - **Vitamin C** — vitamin C independently improves DEJ morphogenesis in the same reconstructed-skin models and is pH-compatible; plausible matrix-support pairing. [7]
  - **Peptides / ceramides** — non-conflicting; supports the brand's barrier-repair thesis.
  - **Retinoids** — no incompatibility; mechanistically complementary (retinoid drives turnover/collagen, C-Xyloside restores GAG/DEJ). Stagger if irritation is a concern, but no chemical clash.
- **Conflicts:** None notable chemically. It is robust.
- **Sensory:** As a small water-soluble sugar derivative it is **light, non-tacky at low %**, contributes mild humectancy; minimal odor/color impact. Easy to formulate into serums, creams, eye creams, masks.

## 7. Safety (irritation, sun, pregnancy, sensitization, sourcing)

- **General tolerability:** Considered **well-tolerated and low-irritation**; widely used at scale since 2006 across major brands with a clean record. The sensitive-skin arm of Zang 2024 (n=30, redness-prone) reported tolerability/barrier improvement, not flares. [9]
- **Sensitization (the one real signal):** **Assier, Wolkenstein, Chosidow, 2017, *Contact Dermatitis* 77(1):60–61 (PMID 28612444)** — the **first reported case of allergic contact dermatitis** to hydroxypropyl tetrahydropyrantriol, confirmed by **positive patch test (HPT 9% in a mix)** to an anti-ageing cream. The authors stress this is **rare** given a decade of mass use with no prior reports. *Takeaway: sensitization is possible but appears very uncommon; not a reason to avoid, but worth noting for sensitive-skin claims.* [15]
- **Sun sensitivity:** **None known.** Not a photosensitizer; no requirement for daytime restriction. (Not a substitute for SPF, which remains the #1 anti-aging step per CLAUDE.md.)
- **Pregnancy/nursing:** No specific contraindication identified; it is a non-retinoid, non-hormonal cosmetic sugar derivative with low systemic exposure. No dedicated pregnancy safety study located — **default to "consult a clinician" copy**, do not make affirmative pregnancy-safe claims. *[unverified — no pregnancy-specific data]*
- **Eye area:** Used in eye creams; no specific ocular concern beyond normal formulation care.
- **Sourcing/derivation:** Xylose feedstock from **beechwood/plant hemicellulose**; synthesized in water under green-chemistry conditions — a genuinely defensible **"derived from a renewable plant sugar"** sustainability story (with substantiation). [12]

## 8. Regulatory & claims (cosmetic vs. drug — per CLAUDE.md)

- **Regulatory status:** A **cosmetic ingredient** (skin-conditioning) globally; **not** a drug, **not** an OTC monograph active. No special regulatory burden (unlike sunscreen filters or retinoid drugs). INCI **Hydroxypropyl Tetrahydropyrantriol** is the label name.
- **Safe cosmetic language (✅):** "helps **support** the skin's natural **glycosaminoglycan (GAG)** content," "helps **visibly improve** firmness/plumpness/smoothness," "helps **support the dermal-epidermal junction**," "helps skin **hold water** / look more **cushioned**," "**supports** the skin's matrix." These map to the GAG/DEJ mechanism and stay appearance-focused.
- **Risky / drug-like (⚠️ — flag for substantiation/legal):**
  - "**Boosts collagen by X%**" / "rebuilds collagen" — structure/function + numeric; we have no standalone human collagen RCT. Avoid.
  - "**Repairs** the dermal-epidermal junction" / "**restores** the basement membrane" — "repairs/restores" is drug-adjacent; the n=5 ultrastructure study supports *biology* but not a finished-product repair claim. Soften to "helps **support/improve the appearance** associated with."
  - "**Anti-glycation** / fights AGEs / un-stiffens glycated collagen" — **factually wrong for this molecule.** Do not use. (This is the misfile trap.)
  - Any specific percentage lifted from **Zang 2024** (e.g., "−33.9% TEWL," "+21.9% hydration," "55% fewer forehead wrinkles") — these are **uncontrolled, single-arm, manufacturer** numbers. **Do not** quote them as proof of our product; at most reference them as ingredient-level literature with full caveats, and never as our product's measured result.
- **Substantiation note:** If we want to make *our own* efficacy claims, we need *our own* (ideally vehicle-controlled) testing — the public C-Xyloside evidence base will not substantiate product-specific performance claims.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Availability:** Widely available. L'Oréal originated/patented it (Pro-Xylane™, launched 2006); the **patent estate has aged** and numerous **third-party/Chinese suppliers** now sell INCI Hydroxypropyl Tetrahydropyrantriol (e.g., Freda, and many ECHEMI/Made-in-China listings), as **powder or ~30% aqueous solution**. [13][14]
- **Cost tier:** **Mid-tier** — not a cheap commodity humectant, not a top-cost peptide/growth-factor. (Specific per-kg prices are quote-based and not publicly posted; **[unverified]** on exact $/kg.) Used at 0.5–3%, cost-in-formula is moderate.
- **Sourcing diligence:** Because "C-Xyloside" and "Pro-Xylane" are used loosely, **verify identity (CAS 439685-79-7), active basis (powder vs 30% solution), purity, and enantiomer/spec on the COA.** Confirm green-chemistry/renewable-feedstock claims with supplier documentation before using them in copy.
- **Strategic note vs. CLAUDE.md "buy commodity bases, invest in the brand":** This is an **active**, not a base — a reasonable place to spend, *if* the matrix/GAG story is central to the product. It is meaningfully cheaper and lower-risk than growth factors while telling a credible "science" story.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **Hype:** Marketed (especially in prestige lines — Lancôme, Vichy, Kiehl's, SkinCeuticals, etc.) as a near-miraculous "**sugar-protein that rebuilds skin's support structure / refills GAGs / rebuilds the DEJ**," often with implied collagen-rebuilding and anti-aging-cure energy.
- **What's actually solid:** It **really does prime GAG/proteoglycan synthesis** and **reorganize the DEJ** — repeatedly, across independent cell/3D/ex-vivo assays, and *in one small living-human study*. The chemistry rationale (stable C-glycoside xyloside mimic) is elegant and real. This is **a genuine mechanism**, not pure fairy dust.
- **What's overstated:**
  1. **"Sugar-protein"** — it's a small sugar derivative, not a protein.
  2. **Standalone visible-wrinkle efficacy** — the human face data are **uncontrolled and/or combination**; the only controlled standalone human study was **n=5, forearm, ultrastructure-only, at 10%**.
  3. **Independence** — virtually all evidence is from **L'Oréal's own lab** or **L'Oréal-funded** work; near-zero independent replication of clinical efficacy.
  4. **"Anti-glycation"** — false for this molecule.
  5. **Percent claims** — the eye-catching "+22% hydration / −34% TEWL" come from an **uncontrolled** study.
- **Fair verdict on the hype:** A **legitimate, mechanism-backed supporting active that has been marketed beyond what its standalone human-efficacy evidence proves.** Credible to use; not honest to crown as a clinically-proven hero.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ → ✅ SUPPORTING active (conditional on honest framing). Not a standalone hero.**

**Reasoning:**
- **Fits the brand thesis well.** The brand's durable logic is **barrier-repair / matrix-renewal**. C-Xyloside is a clean, on-thesis **dermal-matrix + DEJ** active — it complements ceramides/barrier (Zang showed barrier/TEWL/filaggrin signals) and the "renew the support structure" narrative without overpromising.
- **Mechanistically strong, clinically thin.** The biology is among the better-substantiated for a "matrix-building" cosmetic active, but the **standalone human topical proof is weak (n=5 ultrastructure + uncontrolled in-use)**. That gap is exactly why it should be **supporting**, not hero — and why **we cannot make standalone efficacy claims** without our own testing.
- **Excellent formulation citizen.** Water-soluble, pH-flexible, heat-stable, low-irritation, pairs cleanly with niacinamide / HA / vitamin C / ceramides / retinoids. Easy win operationally.
- **Low risk.** One rare ACD case in ~20 years of mass use; no photosensitivity; renewable-sugar sourcing story.
- **Claims discipline is the catch.** Usable **only** with appearance-level "supports GAGs / firmness / DEJ" language; **never** anti-glycation, never the uncontrolled percentages, never "rebuilds collagen."

**Best role:** A **supporting matrix/GAG active in a hero barrier-repair cream or a firming serum**, paired with niacinamide ± HA, positioned around "supporting the skin's own support structure (GAGs) and the dermal-epidermal junction." Not the headline; a credible second-string with a real mechanism and a defensible plant-sugar/green-chemistry story.

**Re-class action:** Move out of "Anti-glycation & related" → into a **"GAG / proteoglycan inducers"** or **"ECM / DEJ support"** class in the master index.

**Longevity note:** In skin-longevity terms, C-Xyloside targets the **"collagen/ECM loss"** and **"DEJ flattening"** hallmarks — both bona-fide structural aging processes (a flattened DEJ and depleted GAGs are well-documented features of aged skin). It does **not** touch the *cellular* longevity hallmarks (senescence, mitochondria, autophagy, inflammaging) that the trendier "longevity" actives chase. It's a **structural/matrix-restoration** play, not a cell-aging-clock play — honest, mid-strength, and a sensible component of a longevity-framed routine rather than its centerpiece.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

*Independent fact-check performed 2026-06-19. Every headline citation in §4, §7, and §1 was checked against the primary source (PubMed/PMC/journal/PubChem) for (a) human-vs-topical status, (b) existence and fidelity to what is claimed, (c) funding and sample size. Findings below.*

### Overall grade: **C** (verging on C+ for mechanism, but the human-topical efficacy tier is what sets the grade)

**Why C and not B:** A "B" requires *moderate human-topical* evidence. C-Xyloside does not have it. The entire standalone living-human topical record is **one n=5, forearm, ultrastructure-only, 10%, manufacturer pilot** (Deloche 2011). That is a real biological signal in living skin — which keeps it above "D / unproven topically" — but it is not a clinical efficacy result: it measured electron-microscope DEJ morphology and two immunostained proteins, **not visible wrinkles, firmness, or any consumer-perceptible outcome**. Everything cosmetically meaningful rests on **uncontrolled single-arm in-use data** (Zang 2024) or **combination/laser-adjuvant trials** (Ablon 2025, Jiang 2025) that cannot isolate the molecule. The mechanism tier (in-vitro / 3D / ex-vivo) is genuinely strong and coherent and would earn a B on its own — but mechanism is not topical efficacy, and the grade reflects the standalone human-topical payoff a buyer actually gets.

**Why not D:** It is not fairy dust. There is a real, stable, well-characterized molecule with a coherent GAG-priming mechanism replicated across multiple cell/tissue assays, *plus* a controlled (if tiny) demonstration that it changes living human skin biology. That is more than most botanicals can show.

**No fabricated or misattributed citations were found.** Every checkable source exists, is correctly cited (PMIDs/DOIs/journals match), and is described faithfully — including, importantly, the limitations. This dossier is unusually disciplined: it self-flags the n=5 problem, the single-source (L'Oréal) problem, the uncontrolled percentages, and the class-misfile before a reader has to.

### Main caveats (the load-bearing ones)

1. **Single-source dependency.** The mechanistic *and* the only controlled human evidence originate from **L'Oréal** (the molecule's owner). There is essentially **no independent replication of clinical efficacy.** This is the single biggest discount on the evidence.
2. **No standalone, vehicle-controlled human efficacy on a consumer-perceptible endpoint.** The controlled human study (Deloche) measured ultrastructure, not appearance. Visible-benefit data are uncontrolled (Zang) or combination (Ablon/Jiang).
3. **Mechanism ≠ topical payoff.** The GAG-priming biology is solid in a dish; the *delivered dose and clinical effect at cosmetic concentrations (0.5–3%, vs the 10% used in studies)* is unproven.
4. **The "glycation/AGEs" filing in the master index is wrong** (verified line 372 of `_MASTER-INDEX.md` still lists "glycation/AGEs"). This is a factual error, not just a taxonomy nit — copy that calls C-Xyloside "anti-glycation" would be unsubstantiable. The dossier's reclassification recommendation should be actioned.

### Claims to soften / never make

- **Soften** "reorganizes the DEJ" / "DEJ repair" in the one-line summary and §2 to **"helps support DEJ architecture (shown ultrastructurally in a small pilot)"** — the word "repair/reorganize" overstates an n=5 surrogate result for finished-product copy. *(In-file: §2 pillar 3 and §8 already flag "repairs/restores" as drug-adjacent; keep marketing strictly to appearance-level language.)*
- **Never** quote the Zang 2024 percentages (+21.9% hydration, −33.9% TEWL, 34–56% wrinkle drops) as product proof — uncontrolled, single-source.
- **Never** imply standalone visible-wrinkle efficacy — there is no controlled standalone study on visible wrinkles. *(Hallmarks table §3 corrected accordingly.)*
- **Never** call it anti-glycation, a "sugar-protein," or a collagen-rebuilder.

### Net verdict for the brand
The dossier's own conclusion — **legitimate, mechanism-backed *supporting* active; not a hero; usable only with appearance-level claims and our own testing** — is fully supported by the verified evidence. Grade **C** = real molecule, real mechanism, thin/single-source standalone human-topical proof. Defensible to use as a second-string matrix/GAG active; not defensible to headline as "clinically proven."
