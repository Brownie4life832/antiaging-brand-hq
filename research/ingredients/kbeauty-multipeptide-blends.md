# Ingredient: K-beauty recombinant multi-peptide blends (INCI: e.g. Tripeptide-46 / Hexapeptide-40 / Oligopeptide-96 / Decapeptide-27 / Methionyl Oligopeptide-91 / Tetrapeptide-38 / sh-Oligopeptide-1 fusion blends; sh-Polypeptide-121; Oligopeptide-29)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering (leaning ⚠️ conditional / supporting) · **Class:** Peptides — recombinant single-chain *fusion* peptides spanning signal, carrier, enzyme-inhibitor and (occasionally) neurotransmitter-inhibitor functions; in practice these are **biotech growth-factor / collagen-mimetic blends**

> **Scope note.** This is a *class / format* dossier, not a single molecule. It covers the family of **recombinant (fermentation-made) multi-peptide actives** that dominate current K-beauty "peptide complex" and "growth factor" launches. They appear on labels as long compound INCI strings — e.g. *"Tripeptide-46, Hexapeptide-40, Decapeptide-27, Methionyl Oligopeptide-91, Tetrapeptide-38, sh-Oligopeptide-1"* (the Abib Hypoderma SP1-2GF system) or *"Tripeptide-46, Hexapeptide-40, Oligopeptide-96, Oligopeptide-91, Tetrapeptide-38, Rh-Polypeptide-9"* — plus standalone recombinant actives like **sh-Polypeptide-121 (HumaColl21®, a biodesigned vegan human collagen)** and **Oligopeptide-29**. The single most important fact for honest copy: **most of these compound INCI names are not "peptides" in the small Matrixyl/Argireline sense — they are recombinant proteins or protein fragments, frequently growth factors (EGF, FGF, IGF, VEGF, KGF) or collagen sequences, manufactured by genetic engineering in *E. coli* or yeast (or, increasingly, plants).** That changes the entire evidence and penetration story.

## 1. Identity

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Marketing / class names | "Multi-peptide complex," "peptide cocktail," "growth factor complex / GFC," "recombinant peptides," "biomimetic / bioengineered peptides," "vegan collagen," "K-beauty peptide blend," "SP1-2GF" (Abib) |
| What the compound INCI strings *are* | A **single-chain synthetic fusion peptide/protein** produced by **fermentation in *E. coli*** (or yeast/plant). A fusion gene is built by joining the genes for several functional sequences, with an initiator methionine; the host cell expresses one continuous chain. The Abib-type fusion contains **up to ~98 amino acids**. (incidecoder; CosIng) |
| The "sh-" / "rh-" prefix | **sh- = "synthetic human"**, **rh- = "recombinant human"** — both denote a sequence copied (or close to) an endogenous human protein, made by recombinant DNA technology, *not* extracted from people. |
| Decoding the components (key) | Many numbered INCI fragments map to **growth factors or structural proteins**: **sh-Oligopeptide-1 = rh-EGF** (epidermal growth factor, 53 aa, ~6 kDa); **sh-Oligopeptide-2 = rh-IGF-1**; **sh-Polypeptide-1 = bFGF (FGF2)**; **sh-Polypeptide-11 = aFGF (FGF1)**; **sh-Polypeptide-9 / Rh-Polypeptide-9 = VEGF**; **sh-Polypeptide-121 = recombinant human collagen type XXI (HumaColl21®)**. The Tripeptide-46 / Hexapeptide-40 / Oligopeptide-96 / Tetrapeptide-38 / Decapeptide-27 fragments are proprietary signal/collagen-mimetic sequences (e.g. Gly-Pro-Hyp-type collagen triplets) bundled into the fusion. |
| sh-Polypeptide-121 (HumaColl21®) | Single-chain recombinant peptide **100% bio-identical to human collagen α-1(XXI)** (COL21A1); up to ~957 aa; may carry disulfide bonds/glycosylation; made by **fermentation on plant-based inputs** (vegan). Supplier: **Geltor**. INCI functions: film forming, skin conditioning. |
| Oligopeptide-29 | A numbered oligopeptide listed with **antioxidant, chelating, binding, skin-protecting** functions; thin public characterization; appears in <0.1% of products. |
| CAS / MW | Varies by exact construct; no single CAS. Sizes range from genuinely small oligopeptides (<1 kDa) to **full recombinant proteins of 6 kDa (EGF) up to ~90+ kDa (collagen-scale)** — a >100-fold spread that the single "peptide" label conceals. |
| Origin | Modern biotech (recombinant DNA / fermentation / molecular farming). This is the **current commercial frontier** of cosmetic peptides, heavily driven by Korean and Chinese suppliers and brands. |

Plain language: these are **lab-grown protein blends**. A microbe (or plant) is genetically programmed to print one long chain that strings together several "active" sequences — often skin growth-factor copies and collagen-like bits — then it is purified and dropped into a serum/cream. The marketing frames them as "next-generation peptides"; biochemically several are **growth-factor proteins**, which is a different (and more regulated, less skin-penetrant) thing than a classic cosmetic peptide.

## 2. Class & mechanism

These blends are deliberately **multi-mechanism** — that is their selling point. Mapped to the four classical cosmetic-peptide categories:

- **Signal peptides (the bulk of the story).** The collagen-mimetic fragments (Gly-Pro-Hyp triplets, palmitoyl-type signal motifs embedded or co-formulated) and especially the **growth-factor components (EGF/FGF/IGF/VEGF/KGF)** are meant to *signal* fibroblasts and keratinocytes to proliferate and to synthesize collagen, elastin, fibronectin, laminin and hyaluronic acid. Growth factors bind their cognate receptors (EGFR, FGFR, IGF-1R, VEGFR) to drive proliferation, migration and matrix production — the same biology exploited in wound healing.
- **Carrier peptides.** Some constructs include or are paired with **cell-penetrating peptides (CPPs/TD1-type)** specifically to drag the cargo across the stratum corneum (an explicit research direction — see molecular-farming work). A few numbered fragments also act as copper/metal carriers (GHK-Cu lineage).
- **Enzyme-inhibitor peptides.** Certain fragments are pitched as MMP (matrix-metalloproteinase) inhibitors — i.e., *protect* existing collagen from breakdown — overlapping the "clear ECM waste" narrative of regimen studies.
- **Neurotransmitter-inhibitor ("neuro") peptides.** Less central here, but some K-beauty "multi-peptide" labels also fold in Argireline-class (Acetyl Hexapeptide-8) or SNAP-8 for an expression-line claim. (See [`argireline-neuropeptides.md`](argireline-neuropeptides.md) — that mechanism is weak topically.)

**The mechanistic catch (be honest).** Mechanistic *plausibility* is high — growth-factor and collagen-signal biology is real and well-mapped in cell culture and wound healing. But two things break the chain for a **topical leave-on cosmetic**:

1. **Delivery.** The headline actives are large and hydrophilic. EGF is ~6 kDa; FGF/IGF ~17–22 kDa; recombinant collagen tens of kDa. The classic dermatologic rule of thumb is that molecules **>500 Da do not meaningfully cross intact stratum corneum**. So the dose reaching living fibroblasts in the dermis is, at best, a small fraction entering via follicles/sweat ducts (Section 5).
2. **Identity inflation.** The fusion-blend genius is also its evidence problem: a single proprietary chain is **not** the molecule studied in the growth-factor literature, so efficacy is *borrowed* from rh-EGF/recombinant-collagen studies done with different molecules, often by injection or microneedling, not from the actual fusion product on intact skin.

Defensible framing: **a biotechnologically sophisticated, multi-signal active whose cell-level mechanism is real, but whose topical-on-intact-skin benefit is modest, mostly extrapolated, and gated by poor penetration.**

## 4. Evidence review by tier  *(the core)*

> **Honest headline.** The *cell-biology* evidence is strong; the *topical-human* evidence for **these specific recombinant blends** is **sparse, mostly manufacturer-linked, and largely borrowed** from (a) the broader topical-growth-factor literature and (b) injectable recombinant-collagen trials. There is **no robust independent RCT on the named fusion blends** (Tripeptide-46/Hexapeptide-40 complexes, sh-Polypeptide-121) applied topically to intact skin. Treat headline percentages ("+197% collagen," etc.) as **in-vitro or manufacturer figures**, not clinical outcomes.

### Tier 1 — In vitro / mechanistic (strong, but cell-level only)
- **He Q, Liao Y, Wu Y, et al. 2025.** *Bioactive oligopeptides and the application in skin regeneration and rejuvenation.* J Appl Biomater Funct Mater (SAGE). DOI: 10.1177/22808000251330974. Review: numbered oligopeptides regulate collagen synthesis, antioxidant defense and melanin; emphasizes small size, biocompatibility, modifiable groups — *and* flags penetration/stability as the practical bottleneck.
- **Geltor HumaColl21® (sh-Polypeptide-121) supplier data.** *Third-party in-vitro* assays reported as **collagen +197%, fibronectin +151%, elastin +133%** in fibroblasts; "superior collagen/elastin/laminin/fibronectin boosting vs marine collagen." **These are in-vitro / supplier figures — not human clinical wrinkle outcomes**, and "elasticity +107%" is also presented without an accessible peer-reviewed protocol.
- **Recombinant humanized collagen, in-vitro/ex-vivo** (e.g., rh type I/III/XVII complexes) upregulate collagen IV, laminin and other basement-membrane proteins in damaged keratinocyte/fibroblast models (PMC11856143). Mechanistically supportive of the *class*; not the fusion blends specifically.
- **Molecular-farming fusion designs** (EGF-CPP/TD1 fusions): double-TD1-fused EGF reported **~5× more efficient transdermal delivery** than control in model systems — but the review concludes effects on "skin repair, melasma, whitening, anti-aging are *poorly explored*" and frames this as a *future* direction (Su/Ye et al. 2024, PMC11416094).

### Tier 2 — Animal
- Recombinant growth factors (EGF, FGF, PDGF, KGF) and recombinant humanized collagen show wound-healing, fibroblast-secretion and basement-membrane reinforcement effects in rodent models (e.g., PMC11856143 rat skin). **Wound/regeneration models, not cosmetic intact-skin aging**, and not the K-beauty fusion blends.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL
- **Not applicable / irrelevant to topical.** Oral "collagen peptides" (hydrolyzed collagen) are a *different category* with their own ingestion RCTs; **recombinant topical peptides are not the same molecules and oral data do not transfer.** Do not borrow oral-collagen trials to support topical recombinant-peptide claims.

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL  *(the only tier that matters for our product)*

**4a. Proxy evidence — the topical-growth-factor literature (the strongest real data, but on different products)**
- **Quinlan DJ, Ghanem AM, Hassan H. 2023.** *Topical growth factor preparations for facial skin rejuvenation: a systematic review.* J Cosmet Dermatol. 2023;22(7):2023–2039. PMID: 37222303. DOI: 10.1111/jocd.15644. **The key citation.** 33 studies (**9 RCTs**, 24 uncontrolled case series), **1,180 participants**, 23 different preparations. By investigator assessment: **modest** gains — skin texture median <50%, fine lines/wrinkles median **<35%**, overall facial appearance median **<20%**; participant self-ratings higher; **low adverse-event risk**. *Limitations (severe):* huge heterogeneity in GF source/number, undisclosed co-ingredients, non-standardized outcomes, and **durability beyond 6 months unknown.** Conclusion: appear effective by reported measures, but **evidence quality modest.**
- **Miller-Kobisher B, Suárez-Vega DV, Velazco de Maldonado GJ. 2021.** *Epidermal Growth Factor in Aesthetics and Regenerative Medicine: Systematic Review.* J Cutan Aesthet Surg. 2021;14(2):137–146. DOI: 10.4103/JCAS.JCAS_25_20. 49 articles, **821 patients**; rh-EGF (~6 kDa) topical formulations gave statistically significant improvements in periocular wrinkles, hydration, elasticity, density; notes EGF entry via **hair follicles/sweat glands** and that "quasi-experimental, uncontrolled, or [non-]randomized clinical trials still abound" — i.e., **evidence base methodologically weak.**
- **Topical GF + microneedling (delivery-assisted):** small case series (e.g., Pamela RD 2018, *J Clin Aesthet Dermatol*, retrospective n=8, four-GF gel after microneedling: 7/8 physician-rated texture/wrinkle improvement) and RCTs show better results **when the barrier is breached** — underlining that intact-skin topical delivery is the weak link.

**4b. The closest thing to direct evidence on a tripeptide/hexapeptide *regimen* (still not the recombinant fusion blends, and manufacturer-funded)**
- **Widgerow AD, Jiang LI, Calame A. 2019.** *A single-center clinical trial to evaluate the efficacy of a tripeptide/hexapeptide antiaging regimen.* J Cosmet Dermatol. 2019;18(1):176–182. PMID: 29504212. DOI: 10.1111/jocd.12507. **Open-label, n=22, 12 weeks**, tripeptide/hexapeptide regimen (Alastin) "to clear ECM waste + stimulate collagen/elastin." Statistically significant improvement across clinical-grading and 3D-imaging wrinkle metrics; **biopsies (n=5)** showed collagen stimulation in all, elastin in 3/5. *Limitations:* small, **no control group**, single-center, **funded by Alastin (author affiliation)**, and a *different* peptide system than the recombinant K-beauty fusions. Useful as directional support for "tripeptide/hexapeptide regimens can improve wrinkle appearance," **not** as proof for any specific fusion-blend product.

**4c. Recombinant *collagen* human trials — promising, but the positive RCTs are INJECTED, not topical**
- **Huang et al. 2025.** *Translational Application of Recombinant Humanized Type III Collagen in Facial Rejuvenation: A Randomized Controlled Trial.* J Cosmet Dermatol. 2025. PMID: 41208340; PMC12598374. DOI: 10.1111/jocd.70529. RCT, **n=55 (52 completers; 39 treatment / 13 control)**; significant GAIS and VISIA improvements (spots, wrinkles, texture, porphyrins) at day 90; well tolerated. **Crucial caveat: the product was delivered by intradermal "water-light" injection, not topical leave-on** — so it supports recombinant collagen *as an injectable*, and tells us little about a recombinant-collagen *cream*.
- **NCT06854796** (registered RCT, recombinant collagen *gel* for nasolabial wrinkles) — again an aesthetic-procedure context; results pending.

**4d. The named K-beauty fusion blends themselves**
- **No independent peer-reviewed topical RCT located** for the specific compound-INCI fusions (Tripeptide-46/Hexapeptide-40/Oligopeptide-96/Decapeptide-27/Tetrapeptide-38/sh-Oligopeptide-1, etc.) or for sh-Polypeptide-121 as a finished topical. Marketing relies on (i) in-vitro/supplier data and (ii) the proxy GF/collagen literature above. Finished products (Abib Hypoderma SP1-2GF, Geltor-based "vegan collagen" creams, Youth to the People Future Cream uses Geltor's polypeptide line) carry brand efficacy claims, not published independent trials on the exact formula.

**Tier-4 verdict.** For **topical human use of these specific recombinant blends**, evidence is **borrowed and modest**. The best *real* data (Quinlan 2023 systematic review of topical GFs) shows **modest** investigator-rated improvement (wrinkles median <35%), low harm, and unknown durability — and even that is on *other* GF products of varying composition. The eye-popping percentages in marketing are **in-vitro/supplier** numbers. The strongest recombinant-collagen RCT is **injectable**. Net: real but unspectacular, and over-extrapolated.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration  *(the crux)*

This is where biotech ambition meets skin physics:

- **The 500-Da rule.** Dermatologic consensus: molecules **>500 Da rarely cross intact stratum corneum** in meaningful amounts. (Bos & Meinardi's "500 Dalton rule.") Most headline actives here are far larger: **EGF ~6 kDa; FGF ~17 kDa; IGF-1 ~7.6 kDa; VEGF ~38 kDa; recombinant collagen tens of kDa; the Abib-type fusion ~98 aa (~9–11 kDa).** By this rule, dermal delivery of the *intact* protein is minimal.
- **The follicular/appendageal loophole.** Reviews argue a *small fraction* of ~6 kDa rh-EGF enters via **hair follicles and sweat glands**, enough (it is hypothesized) to start a keratinocyte→fibroblast signaling cascade. This is plausible but quantitatively small and not well measured for finished cosmetics.
- **Surface/film effects are real and immediate.** Recombinant collagen (HumaColl21) and large peptides form a **breathable, water-binding film** → instant smoothing, soft-focus, hydration, better makeup wear. This is a genuine *cosmetic* benefit but is a **surface effect, not dermal remodeling.**
- **Delivery hacks change the math.** Microneedling, fractional laser, encapsulation/liposomes, and **CPP/TD1 fusion** all raise flux (TD1-EGF reported ~5× in models; GF-after-microneedling studies are the positive ones). The very existence of these strategies concedes that **plain topical penetration is the bottleneck.**
- **Stability.** Recombinant proteins are **fragile** — sensitive to heat, pH extremes, proteolysis and oxidation. Activity in a jar over shelf life is a real and under-disclosed question; many "growth factor" products may contain denatured/inactive protein by the time of use.

**Conclusion:** On *intact* skin, the large recombinant components deliver **mostly surface/upper-epidermal benefit**; true dermal collagen remodeling from a leave-on cream is **largely unproven and physically constrained.** The smaller embedded oligopeptide fragments (sub-kDa) have a better penetration chance, more like classic signal peptides.

## 6. Formulation

| Parameter | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Typical use level | Actives are extremely potent per-molecule but used at **trace concentrations** (often ppm to low % of a dilute trade solution). Recombinant collagen/film-formers used higher for sensory. Label position is usually **mid/low** in the INCI list. |
| pH | Keep **mild/near-physiologic (~5.5–6.5)**; recombinant proteins denature at pH extremes. Avoid co-processing with very low-pH actives in the same phase. |
| Stability / packaging | **The hard part.** Proteins degrade with heat, light, oxidation, microbial proteases. Favor **airless/opaque packaging, cool storage, antioxidant systems, robust preservation**, and consider encapsulation/lyophilized two-part ("activator") formats. Anhydrous or low-water systems can improve stability for some constructs. Do not assume label presence = active presence at point of use. |
| Pairings (sensible) | Humectants/HA and barrier lipids (carry the surface-hydration story honestly); niacinamide (well-tolerated, complementary); antioxidants (protect the protein + skin); penetration-supportive delivery (encapsulation). Pleasant with ceramide/barrier creams — fits the brand's barrier-repair core. |
| Conflicts | **Direct acids / very low-pH vitamin C / strong exfoliants in the same layer** (denaturation, salt-out of charged peptides); high-ionic-strength systems; strong oxidizers. Avoid pairing growth-factor proteins with aggressive actives that could degrade them. |
| Sensory | Generally elegant — recombinant collagen gives a silky, film-forming, "instant plumped/soft-focus" feel that consumers reward; aqueous serums are light. |

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation / sensitization:** Generally **well tolerated** in the GF literature (Quinlan 2023: low adverse-event risk; Miller-Kobisher 2021 similar). Recombinant proteins are designed for biocompatibility. As proteins, there is a **theoretical sensitization/allergenicity** consideration with repeated exposure, but reported rates are low.
- **The growth-factor / proliferation–cancer question (the real debate):** Because growth factors drive cell proliferation, there is a long-standing **theoretical concern** that chronic topical use could promote unwanted proliferation (including of dysplastic cells) — voiced by some dermatologists. **Current evidence does not show that topical rh-EGF/GF cosmetics cause cancer**, and a 2014 lawsuit raising the issue was **dismissed (2018)**. Still: **no long-term (multi-year) safety data**, mechanism is pro-proliferative, and this warrants caution in marketing and in candidate populations (avoid implying use over actively dysplastic/lesional skin). For a *longevity* brand, the pro-proliferation logic also sits awkwardly with senescence-focused theory (proliferation ≠ rejuvenation).
- **rhPDGF-BB precedent (reassuring for that molecule):** Pharmacokinetics show **negligible systemic exposure** after repeated topical application; non-cytotoxic/non-irritating/non-sensitizing/non-mutagenic/non-carcinogenic in studies (PMC12754275) — but that is one specific, well-characterized GF, not a blanket clearance for all fusion blends.
- **Sun sensitivity:** None known; not photosensitizers.
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** **No specific safety data.** Given pro-proliferative mechanism and absent evidence, **do not make pregnancy-safe claims**; standard cosmetic caution applies.
- **Sourcing / sustainability:** Fermentation-made (microbial/yeast) or plant-molecular-farming → **animal-free / vegan-compatible** (HumaColl21 explicitly vegan, plant-input fermentation). No wild/animal sourcing concerns. Ordinary biotech-manufacturing footprint. **Purity/endotoxin control** (E. coli expression) is a quality consideration — specify low-endotoxin grades.

## 8. Regulatory & claims  *(per [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md) guardrails)*

- **Status:** Cosmetic ingredients in **EU** (Reg. (EC) 1223/2009; listed in CosIng with skin/hair-conditioning, film-forming functions) and **US** (FDA: cosmetic *unless* claims cross into "drug"). "Cosmeceutical" has **no legal standing.**
- **The drug line is sharp — growth-factor and "collagen-boosting" language is high-risk.** Claiming a product **"boosts collagen," "stimulates fibroblasts," "regenerates," "repairs," "heals," "rebuilds the dermis,"** or contains **"growth factors" that act on cells** describes **altering the structure/function of the body** → can reclassify the product as an **unapproved drug** (US) and is **unsubstantiated** for a topical cosmetic given the penetration/evidence gaps. The word **"growth factor"** itself invites scrutiny.
- ❌ Do NOT say (without substantiation/legal review): "boosts collagen by X%," "+197% collagen," "stimulates fibroblasts," "regenerates/repairs skin," "growth factors rebuild your skin," "clinically proven to remodel the dermis," "anti-aging treatment," anything implying drug-like cellular action.
- ✅ Defensible cosmetic language: "advanced **bioengineered/recombinant peptide** complex," "helps **visibly** improve the look of firmness and fine lines," "**supports** the appearance of smoother, plumper-looking skin," "vegan biotech collagen for an **instantly smoother, more hydrated** feel," "helps skin **look** more even." Keep it **appearance-based and surface-honest**; reserve mechanism language for educational content, hedged.
- **Any in-vitro percentage in copy must be labeled as such** ("in laboratory testing on skin cells…") and flagged for substantiation. Do not present in-vitro or injectable-study results as topical clinical outcomes. **Flag every "growth factor," "boost," "stimulate," "regenerate," and percentage claim for legal review.**

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Suppliers:** A crowded, fast-moving field. **Geltor** (HumaColl21® / sh-Polypeptide-121, biodesigned vegan collagen); **Milestone Pharmtech / numerous Chinese & Korean biotech suppliers** for rh-EGF (sh-Oligopeptide-1), FGF, IGF, KGF and the compound fusion blends; Korean ODM/OEMs build the finished "SP1-2GF"-type systems. Commodity-ish at the rh-EGF end; more proprietary/premium for branded recombinant collagens and patented fusions.
- **Cost tier:** **Moderate to high.** Recombinant proteins cost more than classic synthetic peptides (Argireline/Matrixyl); branded vegan collagens (Geltor) and patented fusions carry a premium. Because effective use levels are low, **cost-in-formula can be manageable**, but the *story* (and stability engineering) is where you pay.
- **Supply:** Generally stable and scalable (fermentation), multi-source at the commodity end; branded actives are single-source. **Quality variance is real** — endotoxin, purity, and *actual retained bioactivity* differ widely between suppliers; demand CoA/activity data.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

| Marketing claim | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Boosts collagen +197% / elastin +133%" | **In-vitro / supplier** fibroblast figures (HumaColl21), **not** human topical wrinkle outcomes. Not a measured in-use result. |
| "Recombinant growth factors regenerate your skin" | GF biology is real *in cells*; on intact skin, **penetration is the bottleneck** (>500 Da rule; ~6 kDa EGF only trickles in via follicles). Best human GF data (Quinlan 2023) = **modest**, heterogeneous, durability unknown. |
| "Clinically proven multi-peptide complex" | The **named fusion blends have no independent topical RCT**; evidence is borrowed from other GF/collagen products and from **manufacturer-funded** regimen studies (Widgerow 2019, open-label, n=22). |
| "Vegan collagen rebuilds the dermis" | Topically it forms a **hydrating surface film** (real, nice) — it does not "rebuild the dermis." The positive recombinant-collagen RCT (Huang 2025) used **injection**, not a cream. |
| "Next-generation, so better than old peptides" | More sophisticated *production*, not better *delivery*. Larger recombinant proteins penetrate **worse** than small classic peptides. Novelty ≠ efficacy. |
| "Growth factors are proven anti-aging" | Not FDA-approved as anti-aging; mechanism is pro-proliferative (a theoretical safety/longevity tension); long-term safety unstudied. |

This class is **prestige-coded and over-extrapolated**: genuine biotech, genuine in-vitro activity, real but modest proxy clinical data — wrapped in claims (percentages, "boosts collagen," "growth factors," "regenerates") that are **the highest-risk language in cosmetic regulation** and not substantiated for the actual topical products.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ CONDITIONAL — at most a SUPPORTING "biotech credibility" active, formulated for surface/appearance benefits with disciplined claims; not a hero, and never marketed with growth-factor/percentage/"boosts collagen" language.**

One-line reasoning: The cell-level science is real and the sensory/film benefits (especially recombinant vegan collagen) are genuine and on-brand, but **topical-on-intact-skin efficacy for these specific blends is unproven, penetration-limited, and the only honest claims are appearance-based** — meanwhile the category's natural marketing vocabulary ("growth factors," "boosts collagen," "regenerates," "+197%") is exactly the **drug-claim / substantiation minefield** [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md) tells us to avoid.

**How to use it if we use it:**
- Position as a **premium supporting active** inside a barrier/repair cream or serum — paired with the better-evidenced core (retinoids, niacinamide, ceramides, vitamin C, peptides with collagen-signaling data).
- Lead with **vegan biotech provenance + instant smoothing/hydration (film effect)**, which is defensible, plus a hedged "supports the appearance of firmness" — *not* growth-factor mechanism.
- Insist on **stability-engineered formats** (airless, encapsulated/activator) and supplier **activity data**, or the active is theater.
- Keep **all percentages and "growth factor" mentions out of consumer claims** (educational, hedged only); flag for legal.

**Longevity-relevance note:** **Mixed.** It targets the right *cluster* (collagen/ECM, photoaging, barrier renewal) and signals cutting-edge science, which suits a skin-longevity brand's credibility. **But** (1) topical delivery undercuts the deep-remodeling promise; (2) it does **not** touch senescence, mitochondria or glycation; and (3) its **pro-proliferative** mechanism sits in philosophical tension with a senescence/longevity worldview (more growth ≠ more youthful aging). For durable longevity positioning, spend the **hero** budget on better-penetrating, better-evidenced actives; let recombinant peptides/vegan collagen play a **supporting, sensory-and-story** role with honest, appearance-only claims.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

*Verified 2026-06-19 against PubMed/PMC records, INCI/supplier references, and the cited systematic reviews. Re-checked 2026-06-19 with primary-source fetches of the four load-bearing citations (Quinlan 2023, Widgerow 2019, Huang 2025, Geltor/HumaColl21).*

### Overall evidence grade (for the brand's purpose — topical, intact skin, objective endpoint): **C / "borrowed and modest"** (mechanistic/in-vitro tier B+; topical-human tier weak)

The **mechanistic and in-vitro** tier is genuinely strong (B+): recombinant growth factors and collagen sequences do drive fibroblast/keratinocyte activity, and the production technology is real and scalable. The **topical-human** tier — the only one that matters for a leave-on cosmetic — is **weak**: (1) **no independent RCT on the actual named fusion blends**; (2) the best real human data (Quinlan 2023 systematic review of *other* topical GF products) is **modest, heterogeneous, durability-unknown**; (3) the standout recombinant-collagen RCT (Huang 2025) is **injectable, not topical**; (4) the closest topical regimen trial (Widgerow 2019) is **open-label, n=22, manufacturer-funded, and a different peptide system**; (5) the marquee percentages are **in-vitro/supplier**. Penetration physics (>500 Da rule vs 6–90 kDa actives) caps the plausible dermal effect from a cream. Net usable grade for claims: **C− topically** — real surface/sensory benefit and credible science, but **not** a foundation for "boosts collagen / regenerates / growth factor" claims.

### What checks out
- **All cited studies are real and correctly attributed.** Primary-source re-verified (2026-06-19):
  - **Quinlan 2023** (PMID 37222303) — confirmed: J Cosmet Dermatol; 33 studies / **9 RCTs** / **1,180 participants**; investigator-rated **fine lines/wrinkles median <35%**, texture <50%, overall <20%; 3 comparative RCTs showed **no significant between-group difference**; durability >6 months unknown. Numbers in the dossier are exact.
  - **Widgerow 2019** (PMID 29504212) — confirmed: **open-label, n=22, 12 weeks, no control group, funded by Alastin** (author affiliation Alastin/UC Irvine), 5 biopsies. Dossier's "manufacturer-funded, uncontrolled, different peptide system" framing is accurate.
  - **Huang 2025** (PMID 41208340; PMC12598374) — confirmed real and confirmed **delivered by intradermal "water-light" injection, NOT topical** ("syringe preloaded with 3 mL of rhCol III lyophilized fibers… injections in a… 'carpet-like' pattern"). n=52 completers (39 treatment / 13 control), GAIS ~71.8% vs 0%. The dossier's single most important caveat — that the standout recombinant-collagen RCT is injectable — is **verified correct**.
  - Miller-Kobisher 2021 (JCAS; 49 articles/821 patients) and molecular-farming review (PMC11416094) consistent with prior check.
  - **No fabricated citations found.**
- **Geltor / HumaColl21 percentages re-checked at source:** the supplier page (Knowde) confirms the framing "superior collagen, elastin, laminin, fibronectin-boosting vs marine collagen" and states efficacy is "supported by **in vitro and third-party clinical study results**." The page does **not** surface the specific numerics (+197% / +151% / +133% / +107%) in retrievable text, so those figures are **supplier-disclosed and may not be independently verifiable**; they must never be presented as human topical wrinkle outcomes. Dossier handles this correctly (labeled [supplier/in-vitro]).
- **The recombinant/fusion identity is accurate:** these compound INCI strings are single-chain fusion proteins made by fermentation; sh-/rh- = synthetic/recombinant human; sh-Oligopeptide-1 = rh-EGF; sh-Polypeptide-121 = recombinant human collagen XXI (Geltor HumaColl21).
- **Penetration critique is well-founded:** the >500 Da rule and EGF's ~6 kDa size are correctly stated; follicular entry is a real but small route; delivery hacks (microneedling/CPP) are the positive-result settings.
- **Claims-risk framing is correct and important:** "growth factor / boosts collagen / regenerates" is precisely the structure-function language that risks drug reclassification.

### Caveats / honest uncertainties to carry forward
- **"≤98 aa fusion" and the exact component→molecule maps** come from INCI databases and supplier literature, not always primary peer-reviewed characterization — treat component identities (esp. proprietary Tripeptide-46/Hexapeptide-40/Oligopeptide-96 sequences) as **supplier-disclosed, not independently verified**. Marked accordingly.
- **The HumaColl21 percentages and "elasticity +107%"** are supplier in-vitro/3rd-party figures without an accessible peer-reviewed protocol — labeled [supplier/in-vitro], not clinical.
- **Bos & Meinardi 2000 (Source #19)** is cited from established secondary use of the "500 Dalton rule"; the principle is well established even though exact penetration depends on vehicle/formulation. [primary reference well-known; not re-fetched in full]
- **Safety:** absence of evidence of carcinogenicity ≠ evidence of long-term safety; the pro-proliferative mechanism + missing multi-year data justify the cautious tone, especially for a longevity brand.
- **No oral tier applies** — do not let "collagen peptide" oral RCTs leak into topical claims; they are different molecules and categories.

### Claims to soften / never make (consumer-facing)
These are the specific phrasings the evidence does **not** support topically. The dossier body already enforces this (Sections 8, 10); restated here as a checklist:
1. **"+197% collagen / +133% elastin / +151% fibronectin / +107% elasticity"** — supplier in-vitro figures, possibly not independently verifiable. Never in consumer copy; if used in education, label "in laboratory testing on isolated skin cells" and flag for legal.
2. **"Boosts / stimulates collagen," "stimulates fibroblasts," "regenerates / repairs / rebuilds the dermis"** — structure-function (drug) language; unsubstantiated for these topicals. Soften to appearance-based ("helps **visibly** improve the look of firmness / fine lines").
3. **"Growth factors [verb] your skin," "growth factor complex"** as a benefit claim — highest regulatory-risk wording; the word "growth factor" itself invites drug scrutiny. Reserve mechanism talk for hedged educational content.
4. **"Vegan collagen rebuilds the dermis"** — topically it forms a hydrating **surface film** only; the positive collagen RCT was **injected**. Soften to "instantly smoother, more hydrated feel."
5. **"Clinically proven multi-peptide complex"** — no independent topical RCT exists on the named fusion blends; evidence is borrowed + manufacturer-funded. Drop "clinically proven."
6. **"Next-generation / better than old peptides"** — larger recombinant proteins penetrate **worse**; novelty ≠ efficacy. Avoid superiority claims.

### Bottom line for the brand
Evidence verification **passes** — the dossier is honest, well-sourced, and its central caveats (no independent topical RCT on the fusion blends; best RCT is injectable; marquee percentages are in-vitro/supplier; >500 Da penetration cap) are all confirmed against primary sources. Usable as a **conditional / supporting** "biotech-credibility + surface-sensory" active with **appearance-only** claims; **not** a hero, and **never** marketed with growth-factor / percentage / "boosts collagen" language. Grade **C** topically (B+ mechanistic).
