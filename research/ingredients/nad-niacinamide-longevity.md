# Ingredient: NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide) & the NAD+ longevity axis vs. plain Niacinamide

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering (niacinamide) / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Slug:** nad-niacinamide-longevity · **Class:** Antioxidants (NAD+/redox metabolism)

> **Reading note.** The brief asks for an exhaustive dossier on **NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)**. NMN cannot be assessed honestly in isolation, because in skincare it is sold as part of one biochemical story — the **NAD+ "cellular-energy" longevity axis** — alongside its far better-evidenced cousin **niacinamide (vitamin B3 / nicotinamide)**. This file therefore covers the whole NAD+ precursor family but keeps a hard, explicit line between what is proven and for which *format*. The marketing blurs these together; we will not.

---

## 0. The honest punchline (read this first)

- **Niacinamide** is one of the most evidence-backed cosmetic actives in existence: multiple human topical RCTs for barrier, tone/pigment, and aging appearance. It is *also*, biochemically, an NAD+ precursor — so it lets us tell an honest "NAD-supporting vitamin B3" story grounded in real data.
- **NMN** is the trendy, science-forward face of the "recharge your skin's NAD+" narrative. Its anti-aging evidence is currently **in-vitro (cultured human fibroblasts) and animal (mostly *oral/injected* mice)**. There is **no published human topical RCT** demonstrating that an NMN cosmetic visibly reduces wrinkles or measurably raises skin NAD+ in people.
- Even for niacinamide, the literature is explicit: **there is no convincing evidence that topical application raises skin NAD+ levels** in vivo, and nicotinamide has **no identified specific molecular target** for its anti-aging effects (Boo 2021 [1]).
- **Net:** niacinamide = proven workhorse with a legitimate longevity *halo*. NMN = a credible mechanism and a strong *story*, but the human topical proof is not there yet. Treat NMN as an optional "story garnish," never as substantiated efficacy.

## 1. Identity

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Common name (brief target)** | NMN — Nicotinamide Mononucleotide (β-NMN, β-nicotinamide mononucleotide) |
| **INCI (NMN)** | No single harmonized INCI in wide use; cosmetic listings appear as **Nicotinamide Mononucleotide** (sometimes within a fermented carrier, e.g. *Saccharomyces* ferment filtrate / "yeast-fermented filtrate"). Verify the exact INCI on any supplied raw material. |
| **Common name (workhorse)** | Niacinamide / Nicotinamide (Vitamin B3 amide) |
| **INCI (niacinamide)** | **Niacinamide** |
| **Related family members** | **NR** — Nicotinamide Riboside (INCI often "Nicotinamide Riboside Chloride"); **NAD+** itself (large, charged, unstable); **Niacin / Nicotinic Acid** (the acid form — flush-causing, regulated differently) |
| **CAS** | NMN 1094-61-7; Niacinamide 98-92-0 |
| **Forms/derivatives in market** | NMN powder; NMN in ferment filtrate; NR salts; NAD+ topicals; niacinamide (free, stable powder). "Reduced" forms (NMNH/NADH) are emerging but not cosmetically established. |

**Biochemical relationship (why the marketing groups them):** all are precursors that feed the **NAD+ salvage pathway**. Niacinamide → (via NAMPT) → **NMN** → (via NMNAT) → **NAD+**. NR → (via NRK) → NMN → NAD+. So niacinamide is literally one enzymatic step "upstream" of NMN; NMN is the immediate precursor to NAD+. This is the kernel of truth the longevity story is built on.

## 2. Class & mechanism

**Class:** NAD+ metabolism / cellular-energy & redox actives. (Filed under Antioxidants per the taxonomy, though the mechanism is broader than free-radical scavenging.)

**The mechanistic thesis:**
- **NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide)** is a coenzyme central to mitochondrial energy production (ATP), DNA repair, and as the substrate for **sirtuins** (SIRT1–7, "longevity enzymes") and **PARPs** (DNA-repair enzymes).
- **NAD+ declines with age** across tissues including skin; declining NAD+ is a proposed driver of mitochondrial decline, impaired DNA repair, and cellular senescence. In skin, reduced NAD+ stores have been reported alongside higher oxidative-stress markers and higher NAD-consuming PARP activity, and **NAMPT** (the salvage-pathway rate-limiter) is required to restore keratinocyte NAD+ after UV and to delay fibroblast senescence (Boo 2021 [1]; NAMPT/keratinocyte work [11]).
- **The intervention logic:** supply a precursor (NMN/NR/niacinamide) → raise intracellular NAD+ → re-fuel sirtuins/PARPs/mitochondria → push cells back toward a "younger" state (more ECM synthesis, less senescence, better repair).

**At the skin/cell level (what is actually demonstrated, by format):**
- **Niacinamide** restores NAD+/NADP(H) pools *in cultured cells*, scavenges ROS and upregulates antioxidant enzymes, suppresses inflammatory cytokines (IL-6/IL-8/TNF-α), increases collagen (I/III/V), elastin and fibrillin while lowering MMPs in dermal fibroblasts, stimulates **ceramide + barrier lipid** synthesis, and reduces **melanosome transfer** from melanocytes to keratinocytes (the basis of its pigment effect) (Boo 2021 [1]; Tanno 2000 [4]; Hakozaki 2002 [5]).
- **NMN** *in cultured human fibroblasts* raises NAD+, activates SIRT1/autophagy, improves mitochondrial function, suppresses senescence, promotes proliferation, supports ECM, and accelerates scratch-wound closure (Biomedicines 2025 [2]); separately increases collagen type I in fibroblasts (Betsuno 2025 [3]).
- **Crucial caveat:** raising NAD+ is the *hypothesized* mechanism. Boo 2021 [1] states plainly that **no specific molecular target** has been confirmed for niacinamide's clinical skin effects, and it is **not established that topical application raises NAD+ in living human skin**. The benefit may come from niacinamide itself, its metabolites, or general redox buffering — not necessarily NAD+ elevation.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Niacinamide (topical, human) | NMN/NR (in-vitro / animal) |
|---|---|---|

| Barrier decline | **Yes — strongest, direct human data** (↑ceramides, ↓TEWL) | ↑barrier function in UVB mice (oral) |
| Oxidative stress | Yes (ROS scavenging, ↑antioxidant enzymes) | Yes (↓ROS/mtROS in fibroblasts; ↑SOD in mice) |
| Glycation / AGEs | Indirect/weak | Not a primary documented effect |
| Cellular senescence | Plausible (NAD+/SIRT pathway) | Yes in-vitro (↓SA-β-gal, ↓senescence under PM/oxidative stress) |
| Mitochondrial decline | Restores NAD+ pools in cells | **Core claimed mechanism** — ↑mitochondrial function in fibroblasts |
| Inflammaging | Yes (↓IL-6/IL-8/TNF-α) | ↓TNF-α/IL-6 in UVB mice |

| Microbiome | Not established | Not established |
| Hydration / water loss | **Yes — ↓TEWL, ↑SC hydration** (topical & oral RCT) | ↑hydration, restored TEWL in UVB mice (oral) |

## 4. Evidence review by tier (the core of this dossier)

> Tiers, strongest format first within the longevity story: **in-vitro → animal → human ORAL → human TOPICAL (RCT vs lower)**. For each NMN/NAD claim I flag whether the benefit is **proven for topical human use** or **borrowed from another format**.

### TIER A — In-vitro (cell/biochemistry)

**A1. NMN in human skin fibroblasts — the flagship "longevity" paper.**
- Zhou et al. (or listed authors), *Biomedicines* 2025;13(10):2395. "Distinctive Gene Expression Profiles and Biological Responses of Skin Fibroblasts to Nicotinamide Mononucleotide: Implications for Longevity Effects on Skin." DOI 10.3390/biomedicines13102395; PMC12561839. [2]
- **Design:** cultured **human skin fibroblasts** ± oxidative-stress / photoaging-type insults, treated with NMN; transcriptomic (gene-expression) profiling plus functional assays.
- **Findings:** ↑cellular NAD+; activation of **sirtuin & autophagy** pathways; ↑mitochondrial function; ↓cellular senescence; ↑proliferation; supported ECM integrity; accelerated wound (scratch) closure.
- **Limitations:** **in-vitro only — no human or even animal skin.** Cultured fibroblasts bathed in NMN are not skin under a cream. Mechanistic support, not efficacy proof. Single-lab, not yet independently replicated for skin.

**A2. NMN protects fibroblasts from pollution-driven senescence.**
- *Int J Mol Sci* 2022; PMC9319393. NMN (with CoQ10) reduced excess ROS/mtROS and lowered SA-β-gal senescence in fibroblasts exposed to particulate-matter-activated mast-cell medium. [6]
- **Limitation:** in-vitro; combination with CoQ10 confounds attribution.

**A3. NMN permeation + collagen (artificial membrane).**
- Betsuno et al., *J Cosmet Dermatol* 2025;24(5):e70222. [3]
- NMN (in a yeast-fermented filtrate) permeated a **Strat-M artificial membrane**, detected (MALDI-IMS) only in the **papillary-dermis region**, not subcutis; ↑collagen type I in human fibroblasts; NMN reasonably stable (~7-month half-life at 20 °C, degrades to nicotinamide).
- **Limitation: NOT human skin.** Strat-M is a synthetic surrogate; the authors explicitly state human skin permeation/efficacy is unexplored. Useful as a *suggestive* penetration signal, nothing more.

**A4. Niacinamide mechanism (foundational).** Boo 2021 review [1] aggregates the in-vitro basis: NAD+/NADP(H) restoration, antioxidant-enzyme induction, anti-inflammatory cytokine suppression, ↑collagen/elastin/↓MMP, and ceramide/barrier-lipid synthesis. Tanno 2000 [4] (cultured keratinocytes): nicotinamide 1–30 µM ↑ceramide biosynthesis 4–5.5-fold, glucosylceramide 7.4-fold, sphingomyelin 3.1-fold. Hakozaki 2002 [5]: niacinamide reduces melanosome transfer ~35–68% (pigment mechanism). NAMPT/keratinocyte survival work supports the NAD+→SIRT1 axis under UV [11].

### TIER B — Animal (mostly oral / injected, NOT topical)

**B1. Oral NMN attenuates UVB photoaging in mice.**
- *(2025/2026)* "β-Nicotinamide Mononucleotide Enhances Skin Barrier Function and Attenuates UV-B-Induced Photoaging in Mice," PMC12729516. [7]
- **Design:** SKH-1 hairless mice, **oral** NMN, UVB exposure, ~10 weeks.
- **Findings:** ↓epidermal hypertrophy, ↓wrinkles/roughness, ↑hydration/elasticity, restored TEWL; preserved collagen density; ↓MAPK phosphorylation (ERK/JNK/p38), ↓TNF-α/IL-6, ↓MMP-1; restored HAS-1/HAS-2 and SOD.
- **Limitation:** **oral, in mice.** Says nothing about a topical product in humans; benefits are *borrowed from another format AND another species*.

**B2. Intraperitoneal NMN protects against UVB skin damage in mice.**
- *J Inflamm Res* 2021;14:5165–5179; PMC8504657. **Injected** NMN preserved collagen, normalized epidermal/dermal thickness, reduced mast cells. [8]
- **Limitation:** injection route; mouse; not a cosmetic scenario.

**B3. NMN blocks UVB collagen loss via ROS/MAPK/AP-1 + mitochondrial proline.**
- *(2025)* PMID 40025354 (mechanistic; cells/animal). Supports the anti-photoaging mechanism. [9]

### TIER C — Human ORAL (real RCTs — but for systemic endpoints, not skin appearance)

> Oral NMN has the **best human data in the family**, but almost none of it is dermatological. This is the classic "borrowed format" trap.

**C1. NMN raises blood NAD+ but clinical benefits are modest.**
- Systematic review + meta-analysis (RCTs), *Crit Rev Food Sci Nutr* 2024; PMID 39116016 — 12 studies, **513 participants**. **Significant rise in blood NAD+**, but **most clinically relevant outcomes were NOT significantly different vs placebo**; authors warn of "exaggeration of the benefits." [10]
- **Skin relevance:** essentially none measured. Raising *blood* NAD+ ≠ visibly better skin.

**C2. NMN oral safety.**
- *Sci Rep* 2022;12:14442; PMC9400576 — single doses to 1250 mg/day up to 4 weeks well tolerated, no severe AEs. [12] Multiple RCTs (300/600/900 mg ×60 d; 250 mg ×12 wk; 1000 mg ×14 d) report good tolerability; common AEs mild GI/nausea. (Renue/aggregate trial lists [13].)
- **Skin relevance:** establishes oral safety only.

**C3. Oral NICOTINAMIDE (not NMN) — the one hard clinical skin win: ONTRAC.**
- Chen et al., *NEJM* 2015;373:1618–1626; PMID 26488693. Phase 3 RCT, **386** high-risk patients, nicotinamide **500 mg BID ×12 mo**: **23% fewer nonmelanoma skin cancers**, ~30% fewer SCC, ~20% fewer BCC, fewer actinic keratoses. [14]
- *(Caveat: a later organ-transplant-recipient RCT (ONTRANS) did NOT replicate the benefit in that immunosuppressed population — so the effect is population-dependent and not a general "anti-cancer" claim we could make.)*
- Chen et al., *Br J Dermatol* 2016 — oral nicotinamide **reduced TEWL** in an RCT (barrier endpoint). [15]
- **Skin relevance:** strong — but it's **oral nicotinamide**, a DRUG-adjacent chemoprevention finding, **not a cosmetic claim** and **not NMN**.

### TIER D — Human TOPICAL

**D1. Topical NIACINAMIDE — extensive, genuine RCT evidence.**

| Study | Conc. | n | Duration | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bissett 2004, *Int J Cosmet Sci* [16] | 5% | 50 (split-face, vehicle-controlled, F 40–60) | 12 wk | ↓fine lines/wrinkles, hyperpigment spots, red blotchiness, sallowness, texture |
| Bissett 2005, *Dermatol Surg* [17] | 5% | 50 | 12 wk | Confirms improved aging facial appearance |
| Kawada et al. (in [1]) | 4% | 30 | 8 wk | ↓wrinkle grade & roughness (p<0.001) |
| Chiu et al. (in [1]) | 4% | 27 | 12 wk | ↓pores/unevenness (8 wk), ↓wrinkles (12 wk) |
| Fu et al. (in [1]) | niacinamide regimen vs 0.02% tretinoin | 99 | 8–24 wk | Wrinkle improvement comparable to tretinoin |
| Hakozaki 2002 [5] | 5% (also 2% no effect) | 18 (+dose-response) | 8 wk | ↓hyperpigmented area; 5% works, 2% does not |
| Navarrete-Solís (melasma, in [1]) | 4% vs 4% hydroquinone | 27 | 8 wk | 44% vs 55% good-excellent; fewer side effects with niacinamide |
| Tanno 2000 [4] (in-vivo arm) | topical | — | — | ↑SC ceramide/FFA, ↓TEWL in dry skin |

- **Bottom line:** topical niacinamide at **~4–5%** has *strong* RCT support for **barrier/TEWL/hydration** and **pigment/tone**, and *moderate* support (smaller studies, often in regimens) for **fine lines/texture**. 2% underperforms; 4–5% is the evidence sweet spot.
- **Honest caveat:** many "anti-aging" wins are in **multi-ingredient regimens** (with peptides/retinyl/sunscreen), so niacinamide's solo contribution to wrinkle reduction is harder to isolate (Boo 2021 [1]).

**D2. Topical NMN / NR / NAD+ — the gap.**
- **No published, peer-reviewed human topical RCT** was found demonstrating that an **NMN (or NR, or NAD+) cosmetic** visibly reduces wrinkles, evens tone, or measurably raises skin NAD+ in living humans. Human topical evidence is **sparse to absent**; the supporting data is in-vitro (D-tier A1/A3) and animal-oral (B-tier).
- Brand/marketing summaries (e.g. Medik8's NAD+ explainer [18]) present the *narrative*; they are framing, not clinical evidence for the topical format.
- **Verdict on the longevity story:** the NAD+ mechanism is real biology; the *topical human proof* for NMN is **borrowed** (from cells and from oral mice). Any "topical NMN recharges your skin's NAD+" claim is currently **unsubstantiated for that format**.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Niacinamide:** small (MW 122), water-soluble, penetrates the stratum corneum well; the dominant, well-characterized skin-conditioning vitamin. Demonstrated in-vivo functional effects (↓TEWL, ↑ceramides) confirm it reaches living epidermis (Tanno [4]).
- **NMN:** larger, charged (phosphorylated nucleotide) — penetration is the central open question. The only direct penetration signal is the **Strat-M artificial-membrane** study (Betsuno [3]), where NMN reached the "papillary dermis" *of a synthetic membrane*. **No human-skin penetration data.** NMN also degrades to nicotinamide over months, so part of any in-use effect could simply be... niacinamide.
- **NR / NAD+:** NR is a salt of a smaller nucleoside (more plausible than NAD+); **NAD+** itself is large, charged and unstable — poor candidate for passive penetration. None have human topical-penetration RCTs.

## 6. Formulation

**Niacinamide (the practical active):**
- **Concentration:** evidence sweet spot **4–5%**. 2% underperforms for pigment (Hakozaki [5]); ≥10% adds irritation/flushing risk without proportional benefit.
- **pH:** stable across a broad range; best around **pH 5–7**. At low pH / heat / over time it can hydrolyze to **nicotinic acid (niacin)**, which causes flushing — formulate near-neutral and avoid prolonged heat.
- **Stability:** excellent; water-soluble powder, long shelf life.
- **Pairings:** plays well with almost everything — HA, ceramides, peptides, ectoin, panthenol, most antioxidants, and (modern data) vitamin C and retinoids (the old "don't mix with vitamin C" caution is largely outdated for finished cosmetics).
- **Conflicts:** the only real watch-outs are low-pH systems and very high niacinamide % stacked with other potential irritants.
- **Sensory:** light, non-tacky, easy in serums/creams; high-quality grades are odorless.

**NMN / NR / NAD+ (story actives):**
- **Concentration:** no established efficacious topical level in humans; cosmetic use levels are vendor-driven, not RCT-derived.
- **Stability:** NMN is moderately stable but **degrades to nicotinamide** over time (Betsuno [3]); NAD+ is the least stable. Carrier/ferment systems are used to stabilize.
- **Pairings:** typically layered onto a niacinamide base; pairs conceptually with mitochondrial/antioxidant stories (CoQ10 [6]).
- **Practical reality:** because NMN decays to niacinamide, a "premium NMN" cream may partly be delivering a more expensive route to vitamin B3. Spend the money where the proof is.

## 7. Safety

- **Niacinamide:** one of the best-tolerated actives. CIR safety assessment (Cosmetic Ingredient Review; *Int J Toxicol* 2005, PMID 16596767) — niacinamide/niacin **not significant irritants, sensitizers, or photosensitizers** at cosmetic levels [19]. Pooled RCT data (2–10%): no serious AEs; mild transient flushing in ~2–4% of users at ≥5%; **zero photosensitivity**. Accepted in EU and Japan.
- **Flushing risk source:** comes from the **niacin (nicotinic acid)** contaminant/degradant, not niacinamide itself — source pure raw material and formulate near-neutral pH.
- **Sun sensitivity:** none (unlike retinoids/AHAs). Compatible in daytime products.
- **Pregnancy:** niacinamide (vitamin B3) is generally considered low-concern topically; no specific contraindication flag, but defer to medical advice and avoid disease claims.
- **NMN/NR:** **oral** human safety is well documented (to ~1250 mg/day, [12][13]); **topical** safety data is thin but no specific signals — conservative use levels and patch-test logic apply. As food-derived nucleotides, low intrinsic irritancy is expected.
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** niacinamide is produced at industrial scale (often synthetic or fermentation-derived); minimal sustainability concern. NMN is increasingly made by **fermentation/enzymatic** routes (greener than early chemical synthesis); quality varies widely with the supplement-market boom — demand CoA, purity, and absence of nicotinic acid.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

**The cosmetic/drug line.** In the US/EU, cosmetics may improve *appearance* but may not claim to treat/prevent disease or alter the body's structure/function — that crosses into **drug** territory.

- ✅ **Safe cosmetic language:** "supports the skin barrier, tone and resilience," "helps visibly improve fine lines, texture and even tone," "supports skin's natural renewal/energy processes," "formulated with niacinamide, an **NAD-supporting** form of vitamin B3," "helps reduce the appearance of hyperpigmentation."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like / unsubstantiated — flag for legal:**
  - "**recharges your skin's NAD+**" / "**boosts cellular energy/ATP**" — NAD+ elevation is **not demonstrated for topicals**; mechanism, not efficacy.
  - "**activates sirtuins / longevity genes in your skin**" — not shown in human skin in vivo.
  - "**reverses cellular aging**" / "reverses wrinkles" — structure/function + overreach.
  - "**prevents skin cancer**" / "treats melasma" — the skin-cancer data is **oral nicotinamide** (drug-adjacent, ONTRAC) and population-dependent; not a cosmetic claim, and not NMN.
  - any **quantified** "boosts collagen by X%" without our own substantiation.
- **Key compliance nuance:** the strongest "anti-aging" longevity headline (skin-cancer reduction) belongs to **ORAL nicotinamide** and must never be implied by a **topical** product. Keep oral findings clearly labeled as oral, and as education — not product efficacy.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **"NMN recharges your skin's cellular battery / NAD+."** — *Mechanism plausible; topical-human proof absent.* No RCT shows a cosmetic raises NAD+ in living human skin. Overreach.
- **"Clinically proven NAD+ anti-aging."** — Usually leaning on **in-vitro fibroblast** data [2] or **oral mouse** data [7][8], not human topical RCTs. The "clinical" framing is borrowed across format and species.
- **"NMN = the longevity breakthrough in a jar."** — The genuine human NMN data is **oral and systemic**, and even there a 513-subject meta-analysis found NAD+ rises but **most clinical endpoints unchanged** [10]. "Exaggeration of benefits" is the literature's own phrase.
- **"Premium NMN does what cheap niacinamide can't."** — Unproven topically; and NMN partly **degrades into niacinamide** anyway [3]. The proven actor in the family is the cheap one.
- **What's NOT hype:** niacinamide's barrier/TEWL/ceramide and pigment/tone benefits at 4–5% are real, RCT-backed, and well-tolerated. The NAD+ decline-with-age biology is real. Oral nicotinamide's skin-cancer chemoprevention (high-risk patients) is real (but oral, drug-adjacent).

## 11. Verdict for the brand

- **Niacinamide: ✅ HERO-CAPABLE / strong supporting.** Rare ingredient that is *both* genuinely evidence-backed (barrier, tone, appearance) *and* on-trend for a longevity narrative (it's a real NAD+ precursor). Best longevity-story-to-risk ratio in the entire set. Anchor any "NAD/cellular-energy" positioning here.
- **NMN (the brief's target): ⚠️ CONDITIONAL / supporting "story" garnish — not a hero.** The mechanism is compelling and the in-vitro/animal data is real, but **there is no human topical RCT**, penetration is unproven in real skin, and it decays toward niacinamide. Use it, if at all, as a small narrative inclusion layered on a niacinamide base — **never** claim it raises skin NAD+ or "recharges" cells.
- **NR / NAD+ topical: ⚠️→❌** — weaker/again-unproven topically (NAD+ especially); NR carries IP friction. Skip unless a specific stabilized, substantiated raw material emerges.

**One-line reasoning:** Build the NAD+/longevity story on **niacinamide** (proven, cheap, safe, on-trend); treat **NMN** as an honest mechanistic garnish, not as substantiated efficacy.

**Longevity-relevance note:** This is the cleanest bridge in the whole portfolio between the **longevity/hallmarks-of-aging** science narrative and **defensible cosmetic claims** — *if* we keep the NAD+/sirtuin mechanism as education and let niacinamide's RCTs carry the efficacy. The temptation to over-claim NMN is exactly where credibility (our core asset) would be lost.
