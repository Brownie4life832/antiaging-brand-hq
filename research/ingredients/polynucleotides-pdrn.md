# Ingredient: Polynucleotides / PDRN (salmon DNA) (INCI: Sodium DNA; also "Polydeoxyribonucleotide (PDRN)," "Polynucleotides (PN)," "Salmon DNA")

**Researched:** 2026-06-18 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product

## What it is
Purified, low-molecular-weight DNA fragments — most famously derived from salmon (hence the viral "salmon sperm facial" nickname; the DNA is typically sourced from salmon milt/sperm or trout). PDRN is the best-known fraction. Mechanistically it is thought to act via **adenosine A2A receptor** activation plus a "salvage pathway" supply of nucleotides, producing anti-inflammatory and tissue-repair effects. This is currently one of the hottest K-beauty / longevity-adjacent actives.

## What it does (claimed → evidenced)
| Benefit | Evidence strength | Source(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Wrinkle reduction, improved texture & elasticity — **injected** (intradermal PN/PDRN) | moderate–strong (for injectables: ~9 studies / 219 patients pooled; periorbital cohort) | Systematic review & cohort [4][1] |
| Skin healing / anti-inflammatory / wound & post-procedure recovery | moderate (mostly clinical/injectable contexts) | [2][3] |
| Same benefits from **topical** serum (no needles) | weak / largely undocumented — penetration of large DNA fragments is the bottleneck | [1][2] |
| Hydration, "glass skin," soothing from topical use | anecdotal–weak | [2][3] |

## How it fits Kintsugi
On-thesis for "repair / renew / mend" and culturally hot (strong search and social demand under "salmon DNA / polynucleotide skincare," giving SEO upside). The honest problem: the **real evidence is for injectables**, a regulated aesthetic procedure we will not be doing. As a topical cosmetic active it has a great story and momentum but thin proof of dermal delivery. Good **supporting** ingredient with a trend tailwind; not a defensible hero on evidence.

## Formulation notes
Water-soluble; used in serums/ampoules/masks/eye products. Pairs well with HA, peptides, niacinamide, panthenol, centella. The central formulation challenge is molecular size and skin penetration — fragment size and delivery system matter a lot, and most topical products do not document how much active reaches living skin. Keep claims to comfort/appearance/soothing for a topical.

## Concerns & cautions
- **Fish/marine allergen flag** — salmon/trout-derived; relevant for fish-allergic users; disclose source.
- Sustainability/sourcing: marine-derived; check supplier provenance and purity (endotoxin, protein contamination).
- **Regulatory:** *injectable* PDRN/PN is a medical product and is **not broadly FDA-approved** in the US (widely used in South Korea/EU aesthetics). *Topical cosmetic* polynucleotides are regulated as cosmetics, but borrowing the injectable's clinical claims for a topical is the trap.
- Pregnancy: no specific topical flag identified, but conservative copy advised.

## Claims caution
- ✅ Safe cosmetic language: "helps skin look smoother and more hydrated," "supports the look of skin renewal," "soothing," "helps improve the appearance of fine lines." Disclose marine source.
- ⚠️ Risky / drug-like: "regenerates tissue," "heals," "stimulates fibroblasts/collagen," "repairs DNA," or importing injectable-study efficacy numbers onto a topical product. Avoid implying drug/medical effect. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## Cost / sourcing
Mid-to-high cost tier for cosmetic-grade polynucleotides; widely available from Korean and EU suppliers (booming category). Cosmetic-grade is far cheaper than medical/injectable-grade. Verify purity and source documentation.

## Verdict for Kintsugi
⚠️ — **Supporting, trend-led, not a hero.** Strong cultural momentum and an on-brand "renewal" story, but topical efficacy is unproven and the compelling clinical data belong to injectables we won't offer. Use as a supporting hydrating/soothing/"renewal-appearance" active with honest cosmetic claims, or skip if we want to avoid riding a hype wave.
