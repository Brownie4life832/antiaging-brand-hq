# Ingredient: Polynucleotides / PDRN (salmon DNA, PN-HPT, salmon roe DNA) (INCI: Sodium DNA)

**Slug:** polynucleotides-pdrn · **Class:** Growth factors, cytokines, exosomes, conditioned media & regenerative
**Researched:** 2026-06-19 (exhaustive upgrade) · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product

> **How to read this dossier.** This is an evidence-graded literature review, not marketing copy. The core is the **tiered evidence review (§4)**. The single most important honest finding, stated up front: **essentially all of the strong human efficacy data for PDRN / polynucleotides is for INJECTABLES** (intradermal "skin boosters" like Rejuran, Plinest/PN-HPT, Placentex) — a regulated aesthetic procedure this brand will not perform. The flagship 2024/2025 systematic review (Lampridou et al., 9 studies / 219 patients) included **zero topical studies — all injectable** [1]. The **topical-cosmetic** evidence is genuinely thin: a handful of small, short, mostly manufacturer-adjacent studies, several on *plant-derived* PDRN rather than salmon, and the molecule's **large size makes dermal penetration the central unsolved problem** (§5). Treat "PDRN works" (true, by injection) and "a PDRN serum works" (largely unproven) as **two separate questions**. This is the most hype-laden active in the current K-beauty / longevity conversation, so the discipline here matters more than usual.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common names:** Polynucleotides (PN); polydeoxyribonucleotide (PDRN); "salmon DNA"; "salmon sperm"/"fish sperm" facial; salmon roe / salmon egg DNA; trout DNA. Branded injectables: **Rejuran®/Rejuvenex®** (Korea), **Plinest®** and **Placentex®** (Mastelli, Italy, "PN-HPT®"), Pluryal Silk, Newdien, Juvelook-adjacent products.
- **INCI (topical/cosmetic):** **Sodium DNA** (the cosmetically stable salt form). Also seen: **Hydrolyzed DNA**, "DNA," "Salmon DNA." (Note: "PDRN" and "Polynucleotides" are pharmaceutical/medical-device terms, *not* INCI names — a topical product legally labels the ingredient as **Sodium DNA / Hydrolyzed DNA**.)
- **What it physically is:** purified, fragmented DNA — a mix of nucleotide chains. **Source matters and is the marketing hook:** classically extracted from the **gonadal tissue (milt/sperm or roe/eggs) of salmonid fish** (*Oncorhynchus mykiss* trout, *O. keta* chum salmon). Highly purified to strip out proteins, lipids and peptides (the allergenic fraction). Historically, the original Placentex was **human-placenta-derived** (1950s–80s, Mastelli) before being reformulated to fish DNA [12].
- **PN vs PDRN — a real, often-blurred distinction** [4]:
  - **PN (polynucleotide):** **longer chains, higher molecular weight**; forms a viscous 3-D hydrogel; acts partly as a **structural/rheological "scaffold" + slow-release biostimulator** (the logic behind injectable "skin boosters" like Plinest/Rejuran).
  - **PDRN (polydeoxyribonucleotide):** **shorter chains, lower MW** (commonly cited ~50–1,500 kDa; fragments ~200–800 bp); more fluid; positioned as the **pharmacologically active** fraction (A2A-receptor + salvage pathway). Placentex is a registered PDRN drug.
  - In practice cosmetic marketing uses "PDRN," "PN," "polynucleotides" and "salmon DNA" interchangeably; the science does not fully support treating them as identical.
- **Plant / microbial-derived "PDRN" (emerging):** non-fish DNA fragments from **Panax ginseng adventitious root** [6], **Paeonia lactiflora (peony)** [9], and **microbial (Lactobacillus rhamnosus)** sources [7] — developed to dodge the fish-allergen, sustainability and supply problems. These are **chemically analogous but not the same molecule** and carry their own (early) evidence.

## 2. Class & Mechanism

PDRN/PN is a **regenerative/biostimulatory** active. Its proposed mechanisms (well-characterised in injectable/wound contexts; see §4 for the topical caveat):

- **Adenosine A2A receptor agonism.** PDRN's degradation products selectively activate the **A2A adenosine receptor** → ↑cAMP → PKA signalling, which is **anti-inflammatory, pro-angiogenic and pro-repair**. This is the best-defined molecular mechanism [3][4][5].
- **Nucleotide "salvage pathway."** PDRN is enzymatically broken down into **nucleosides/nucleotides**, supplying raw material for DNA/RNA synthesis in cells that are dividing or under metabolic/hypoxic stress — sparing cells the energy cost of de-novo synthesis and supporting repair/proliferation [3][4].
- **Fibroblast & ECM stimulation.** Increases dermal fibroblast metabolic activity and **collagen I/III synthesis** (via ERK signalling), increases fibronectin, and **inhibits elastase** (preserving elastin). Downregulates **MMP-1** (less collagen breakdown) [4].
- **Angiogenesis.** Upregulates **VEGF**, promoting microvascular/granulation-tissue formation — central to its wound-healing identity [3][4].
- **Anti-inflammatory cytokine shift ("inflammaging" relevance).** ↑IL-10 (anti-inflammatory), ↓TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β, HMGB1; suppresses NF-κB and MAPK [3][4].
- **Antioxidant.** Some models show NRF2/HO-1 pathway engagement and ROS reduction [4][9].

**The mechanism-vs-delivery gap (read this twice):** every mechanism above was characterised where the molecule **reaches living dermis** — i.e. injected, or applied to cultured cells / 3-D skin models with no intact stratum-corneum barrier. Whether a topical serum delivers enough intact DNA to the dermis to trigger any of this is the unresolved question (§5).

## 3. Hallmarks of Skin Aging Targeted

| Hallmark | PDRN/PN relevance | Strength (for *topical human* use) |
|---|---|---|
| **Collagen / ECM loss** | Core mechanism: ↑collagen I/III, ↓MMP-1, fibroblast activation, ↓elastase | **Moderate–strong by injection; weak topically** |
| **Inflammaging** | A2A-mediated ↓TNF-α/IL-6/IL-1β, ↑IL-10 | Strong mechanistically; topical clinical = weak |
| **Barrier decline / hydration / water-loss (TEWL)** | ↑filaggrin/involucrin/occludin in models; a single n=10 *plant*-PDRN pilot showed faster TEWL recovery after induced irritation + small elasticity gain | **Weak topically** (the least-thin topical signal, but it rests on one tiny uncontrolled pilot) |
| **Photoaging** | A2A repair + antioxidant; UV-damage models; injectable trials in photoaged skin | Moderate by injection; weak topically |
| **Oxidative stress** | ROS reduction, NRF2/HO-1, antioxidant assays | In-vitro mostly |
| **Pigmentation / tone** | In-vitro tyrosinase/MITF suppression; some erythema/melanin improvement in injectable trials | Weak |
| **Cellular senescence** | Salvage pathway "prevents apoptosis/senescence" framing in repair models | Unproven for skin aging — mechanistic only |
| **Mitochondrial decline / glycation (AGEs) / microbiome** | No meaningful direct evidence | None established |

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (core of this dossier)

**Reading guide.** The evidence pyramid for this ingredient is **upside-down relative to the brand's needs**: the strongest human data is for a delivery route (injection) we won't use; the weakest is for the route we'd actually sell (topical). Be ruthless about not borrowing injectable efficacy for a serum.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro (abundant, mechanistically convincing, least relevant to consumer claims)
- **Mechanistic literature (Galeano, Squadrito, Bitto et al., 25 yrs of work; reviewed in Galeano et al. 2021, *Pharmaceuticals* 14(11) [3] and Kim 2025, *Pharmaceutics* 17(8):1024 [4]).** Cultured fibroblasts/keratinocytes/macrophages: PDRN ↑collagen, ↑VEGF, ↑fibroblast proliferation/migration, ↓pro-inflammatory cytokines, all **blocked by A2A antagonists** — solid evidence the A2A mechanism is real.
- **Plant-PDRN in-vitro:** Lee et al. 2023, *Molecules* 28(21):7240 [6] — *Panax ginseng* adventitious-root PDRN on HaCaT/HDF and a 3-D KeraSkin model: dose-dependent ↑filaggrin, E-cadherin, fibronectin, Ki-67; **92% re-epithelialisation at 50%** in a model wound; A2A + FAK-AKT-MAPK engaged. Microbial L-PDRN (Chae et al. 2025 [7]): smaller fragments (<100 bp), **>2× SOD and ~5× DPPH antioxidant activity vs salmon PDRN** in vitro.
- **Limitation:** cultured cells and barrier-free 3-D models at high concentrations (5–50%) — they demonstrate *intrinsic activity*, not that a finished serum delivers it through real skin.

### Tier 2 — Animal
- **Wound-healing / tissue-repair models** (rat/mouse incisional, ischaemic, diabetic, burn, flap, tendon, bone): consistent acceleration of healing, angiogenesis, collagen deposition — the foundation of PDRN's regenerative reputation [3]. Galeano 2021 synthesises ~25 years of these [3]; Colangelo/Galli/Guizzardi 2020 (*Regen Med*, 34 studies across in-vitro/in-vivo/clinical) reach the same conclusion [8].
- **Limitation:** these are **injury models with systemic or local injection**, not cosmetic anti-aging, and rodent skin ≠ human skin. Not transferable to a topical anti-aging claim.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL / systemic
- **Not an oral/ingestible ingredient.** No credible human oral RCT for skin aging. PDRN's human use is **injectable (intradermal/intra-articular/IV in wound/OA contexts)**, e.g. diabetic-foot-ulcer and knee-osteoarthritis RCTs and meta-analyses [3]. Any "oral salmon DNA longevity" framing is unsupported and not transferable to a topical cosmetic.

### Tier 4a — Human INJECTABLE (strong-ish, but NOT the brand's route)
*This tier is where the real clinical signal lives. It is included to be honest about what the molecule can do — and to mark clearly that none of it is a topical-cosmetic result.*
- **Lampridou et al. 2025 (online 2024), *J Cosmetic Dermatology* 24:e16721 — the key systematic review.** 9 studies, **219 patients**, 2014–2022 (7 Korea, 2 Italy); 4 RCTs + 4 case studies + 1 prospective exploratory. **All injectable; no topical.** Outcomes: wrinkle reduction (6 studies), texture (5), elasticity (3), hemoglobin/melanin (5), hydration (1). Reported signals incl. Pak et al. "95.7% crow's-feet improvement at 12 wk." **Authors' own verdict: low-to-moderate quality — "none of the included studies achieved full scores"; small, heterogeneous, not generalisable.** Adverse events mild/transient (edema, erythema, pruritus, pain) [1].
- **Cavallini et al. 2022 (PN-HPT, facial middle-third), *J Cosmetic Dermatology* — RETRACTED.** Originally cited as evidence; the journal **retracted it**. Do **not** cite as support [11]. A cautionary flag on the quality/independence of parts of this literature.
- **Araco et al. 2023 (PN-HPT vs cross-linked HA, nasolabial folds), *J Cosmetic Dermatology* 22 [10].** Split-face exploratory RCT, **n=20** women 40–65. Intradermal PN-HPT significantly improved wrinkle appearance/texture vs saline at 6 wk; combo prolonged HA persistence. No side effects. **Limitation:** tiny; **Mastelli (the PN-HPT patent holder) paid publication costs** — sponsorship-adjacent.
- **Ziade et al. 2026, *J Cosmetic Dermatology* (Pluryal Silk, periorbital) [2].** Prospective observational case series, **n=42**, salmon-sperm PN 7.5 mg/mL, 2 mL/session intradermal. FACE-Q crow's-feet +10.57 and lower-eyelid +9.52 at 3 mo (p<0.001); benefit peaked at 3 mo. **Limitation:** uncontrolled, unblinded, single-clinic, no objective imaging.
- **Crow's-feet split-face RCTs (Rejuran):** e.g. PN vs polycaprolactone (Yoo et al. 2024, *J Cosmetic Dermatology*, PMC11754925) and PN vs HA fillers — generally find PN improves elasticity/hydration/roughness, sometimes **no statistically significant difference vs comparator on primary wrinkle endpoint**, with good tolerability [secondary syntheses].
- **Injectable bottom line:** intradermal PN/PDRN has a *plausible, repeatedly-observed* benefit on fine lines, elasticity, texture and hydration over ~12 weeks, but the evidence base is **small, short, geographically narrow (Korea/Italy), partly industry-supported, includes a retraction, and has no large independent RCT.** Even on its home turf it is "promising," not "proven."

### Tier 4b — Human TOPICAL (the tier that actually matters for the brand — and it is THIN)
- **Bak et al. 2025, *Int J Mol Sci* 27(1):220 — the most relevant topical human study, and it's small + plant-derived.** Low-MW **Paeonia lactiflora (peony) PDRN**, not salmon. In-vitro arm + **human arm: n=10**, 4-week topical **1% cream**. Results: periorbital **elasticity +4.25% vs baseline (p<0.001)** — a within-subject before/after change with no placebo arm, so the absolute effect is tiny and not control-adjusted; **TEWL −21.01% at 2 wk after SLS-induced irritation** (i.e. barrier-recovery, not baseline TEWL); upregulated collagen/elastin/barrier genes in vitro [9]. **Limitations:** n=10, 4 weeks, **no placebo/control arm** (so even the significant elasticity result cannot be separated from vehicle/moisturiser effect), modest effect sizes, plant-source (not salmon DNA), industry author (NewLife BST Co.). This is the single best "a PDRN cream did something measurable on real human skin" datapoint — and it is **a 10-person pilot**.
- **Lee 2023 (Panax PDRN) [6] and Chae 2025 (microbial PDRN) [7]:** topical/3-D-model and in-vitro only — **no human topical RCT**.
- **Salmon-DNA cosmetic serums specifically:** despite the enormous market, **independent peer-reviewed human RCTs on a topical *salmon* DNA serum are essentially absent.** Marketing leans on injectable data, in-vitro data, and consumer/before-after content. Topical "PDRN" claims are frequently propped up by **device-assisted delivery** (microneedling, "spicule" micro-channelling, microinfusion) — which is no longer a passive cosmetic.
- **Honest topical bottom line.** *Proven for topical human use:* at most, **modest short-term improvements in elasticity and barrier recovery/hydration** from small pilot studies (and the best one used *peony*, not salmon). Wrinkle/collagen "regeneration" claims for a topical are **borrowed from injectable/in-vitro data and are not established.** A topical salmon-DNA serum is best understood today as a **hydrating, soothing, barrier-supportive** active with a strong story and weak proof.

## 5. Topical Bioavailability / Penetration

This is the decisive issue and the reason injectables dominate the evidence.

- **Molecular size is the barrier.** PDRN/PN are **large, hydrophilic, polyanionic** DNA fragments (PDRN ~50–1,500 kDa; PN higher still). The stratum corneum is specifically evolved to exclude large hydrophilic molecules; the practical passive-permeation ceiling is ~**500 Da**. DNA fragments are **orders of magnitude too big** to cross intact skin in meaningful amounts [secondary technical sources; consistent across reviews].
- **Implication:** a passive topical serum likely deposits most of its PDRN in the **upper stratum corneum**, where it can act as a humectant/film-former and *possibly* let salvage-pathway nucleotides diffuse — but delivering **intact, A2A-active DNA to dermal fibroblasts is not demonstrated** for a leave-on cosmetic.
- **Why injection exists at all:** the entire injectable category (Rejuran/Plinest/Placentex) is, in effect, an admission that **you have to bypass the barrier** to get reliable PDRN bioactivity. That is the honest subtext of the whole field.
- **Cosmetic workarounds (each a flag):**
  - **Smaller fragments** (low-MW / oligonucleotide / <100 bp microbial or plant PDRN) penetrate *better* but **are a different molecule** with less clinical data and a weaker "salmon DNA" story.
  - **Encapsulation / nano-emulsion** (e.g. "PDRN-NE" products) — plausible but product-specific and rarely independently validated.
  - **Device-assisted delivery** (microneedle/spicule/microinfusion) genuinely improves delivery — **but converts a cosmetic into a procedure** and exits passive-cosmetic claim territory.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** No established efficacy threshold for topicals. Market products advertise **0.05–5% Sodium DNA**, with "5,000 ppm" (0.5%) a common headline. Because penetration is the bottleneck, **higher labelled % ≠ more dermal effect** — much of it is a marketing number.
- **pH / form:** Water-soluble salt (Sodium DNA); typically formulated **mildly acidic to neutral (~pH 5–7)**. DNA is reasonably robust in this range but is degraded by nucleases/contaminants and by extremes of pH/temperature — **purity and preservation matter**.
- **Stability:** Susceptible to microbial nuclease degradation; needs sound preservation and clean sourcing (endotoxin/protein-free). Generally stable in well-made water-phase serums.
- **Sensory:** Light, water-phase; can feel **slightly tacky/film-forming** (humectant-like). Plays well in "glass-skin" serum/ampoule/essence/eye formats.
- **Pairings (good):** HA, **polyglutamic acid**, glycerin, panthenol, **niacinamide**, centella/madecassoside, ceramides/barrier lipids, peptides — all consistent with the "soothing barrier-support hydrator" framing.
- **Conflicts:** Avoid co-formulating in the **same low-pH layer as strong acids (AHA/BHA) or L-ascorbic acid** (degradation/destabilisation risk; not a hard chemistry rule like copper-vit C, but prudent). Don't stack with high-irritation actives in the same step.

## 7. Safety

- **Overall:** Reassuring. Across injectable trials and reviews, adverse events are **mild and transient** (injection-site redness, swelling, bruising, pruritus); no serious AEs in the systematic-review cohort [1][3]. Purified Sodium DNA is considered **low-irritant, low-sensitiser** for topical use [secondary INCI sources].
- **Fish / marine allergen — the key topical flag.** Salmon/trout-derived. Purification removes the **protein/peptide fraction that drives fish allergy**, so well-purified Sodium DNA is *generally* considered low-risk even for fish-sensitive users — but this is **not a guarantee**, residual protein varies by supplier, and **fish allergy is potentially serious**. **Disclose the marine source prominently; advise fish-allergic users to patch test or avoid; do not claim "allergen-free."**
- **Irritation/sensitisation (topical):** Low reported rates; patch testing advised for sensitive skin. No established photosensitisation.
- **Sun sensitivity:** None established (still pair any anti-aging routine with SPF — the #1 evidence-based anti-aging step).
- **Pregnancy/nursing:** No specific topical red flag identified, but data are limited — **use conservative copy; defer to a clinician; do not make pregnancy-safe claims.**
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** Marine-derived from fish by-products (often framed as upcycling milt/roe). Concerns: **wild-vs-farmed salmonid sourcing, traceability, seasonal/limited supply, and purity (endotoxin, residual protein)** [7]. Microbial and plant PDRN are explicitly proposed to solve sustainability/supply/allergen issues [6][7][9].

## 8. Regulatory & Claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

**This ingredient sits exactly on the cosmetic/drug-device fault line — the single biggest compliance trap in the dossier.**

- **Two regulatory worlds:**
  - **Injectable PDRN/PN** = **medicine or medical device**, *not* a cosmetic. **Placentex** is a registered PDRN **drug** (EU); **Rejuran/Rejuvenex** are **Korean MFDS-approved** (since 2014) [12]. In the **US, injectable PDRN is NOT FDA-approved** for aesthetics — used off-label/imported [secondary]. In the **UK/EU, polynucleotide injectables sit in an under-defined zone** (often CE/UKCA medical devices, with the *Journal of Aesthetic Nursing* explicitly noting "the regulatory framework... is currently not well-defined" and no MHRA-specific guidance) [13]. **The brand will not touch this category.**
  - **Topical Sodium DNA** = a **cosmetic ingredient** (no pre-market approval needed in US; cosmetic under EU Reg. 1223/2009 with the usual finished-product safety assessment). Legally sellable as a serum/cream.
- **The trap:** the compelling *science* (regenerates tissue, heals wounds, stimulates collagen, A2A pharmacology) belongs to the **drug/injectable** story. Importing it onto a topical = both a **drug claim** and a **format-mismatch overreach**.
- **✅ Safer cosmetic language:** "helps skin look smoother, plumper and more hydrated," "supports a healthy-looking moisture barrier," "soothing/comforting," "helps improve the appearance of fine lines," "salmon-derived polynucleotides (Sodium DNA)." Always **disclose the marine source**.
- **⚠️ Risky / drug-like — needs substantiation or legal review:** "regenerates/heals skin," "repairs DNA," "stimulates fibroblasts/collagen," "wound healing," "skin booster," importing **injectable trial numbers** (e.g. "95.7% wrinkle improvement"), or "like Botox/filler in a bottle." Avoid "PDRN" on-pack as an efficacy promise if the legal active is Sodium DNA. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & Cost

- **Cost tier:** **Mid-to-high** for cosmetic-grade Sodium DNA (a booming, demand-pulled category), but **far cheaper than medical/injectable-grade** PDRN. Plant/microbial "PDRN" alternatives are emerging and may shift pricing.
- **Supply:** Widely available from **Korean and Italian/EU** suppliers; raw-material supply is real but subject to **salmonid seasonality and provenance concerns** [7]. Specify **purity/endotoxin/residual-protein testing** and source documentation in QC.
- **Provenance/ethics:** Marine-derived (fish by-product). For a brand wary of allergen/sustainability optics, **plant-derived (ginseng/peony) or microbial Sodium-DNA-analogues** are a credible — if less proven and less "iconic" — alternative.

## 10. Hype vs. Evidence

Where marketing overreaches the data (this ingredient is the worst offender in the set):

1. **"Salmon DNA repairs your skin's DNA / regenerates skin."** False framing. The salvage pathway *supplies nucleotides*; it does not "repair your DNA." "Regenerates" is borrowed from **wound-healing/injectable** science and is a drug claim.
2. **"Clinically proven anti-aging serum."** The strong clinical data are **injectable**; the systematic review had **zero topical studies** [1]. The best topical human study is a **10-person, 4-week pilot on *peony* PDRN** [9]. "Clinically proven" for a salmon-DNA serum overstates the evidence badly.
3. **"Like Botox/filler in a bottle."** No — topical can't reproduce intradermal injection; even injectable PN often shows **no significant difference vs comparator on primary wrinkle endpoints**.
4. **Importing injectable percentages** ("95.7% crow's-feet improvement") onto a topical product — a direct evidence-tier laundering. Don't.
5. **Penetration glossed over.** Marketing rarely admits DNA fragments are far too large to cross intact skin (§5) — the gating variable for any real dermal effect.
6. **"Hypoallergenic / fish-allergy-safe."** Purification *reduces* risk; it doesn't license a "safe for fish allergy" claim.
7. **A genuine retraction exists** in the injectable literature (Cavallini 2022) [11] and several supportive studies were **manufacturer-supported** [10] — the evidence base is not as clean as the hype implies.

What's *genuinely* good: a real, mechanistically-grounded regenerative biology (by injection), an excellent safety record, a strong soothing/hydration/barrier signal that's plausible for topical, and **enormous cultural/SEO momentum** ("salmon DNA," "PDRN," "polynucleotide skincare" are trending hard) — a marketing tailwind even where the topical proof is thin.

---

## 11. Verdict for the Brand

**⚠️ Conditional / supporting — a trend-led hydration-and-barrier active with a great story and thin topical proof; never a hero on evidence, and a real claims-discipline hazard.**

One-line reasoning: The compelling efficacy is for **injectables we won't offer**; as a **topical cosmetic** PDRN/Sodium DNA is, on current evidence, a **soothing, hydrating, barrier-supportive** ingredient with only small/pilot human topical data (the best of which used *plant* PDRN) — so use it, if at all, as a **supporting "renewal-appearance" active with rigorously honest cosmetic claims and prominent marine-source disclosure**, riding the trend for SEO/demand without importing injectable science.

**Longevity-relevance note:** Mechanistically on-theme for skin-longevity (ECM support, A2A-mediated anti-inflammaging, salvage-pathway "fuel for repair"), but **topically unproven** — it is a **narrative/credibility bridge** to the regenerative-aesthetics conversation, not a demonstrated topical skin-longevity intervention. If the brand wants the story without the fish-allergen/sustainability baggage, **plant- or microbial-derived Sodium DNA** is the hedge. ## Evidence verification (adversarial)

*Independent re-check performed 2026-06-19 against primary sources. Each headline claim was tested for: (a) is the evidence human AND topical, or quietly borrowed from in-vitro/animal/oral/injectable data? (b) are the cited studies real and do they say what is claimed? (c) are key studies industry-funded or very small?*

### Overall evidence grade (for TOPICAL human anti-aging use): **D / D+**

**D = essentially unproven topically / hype-adjacent.** This grade is for the route the brand would actually sell — a leave-on topical salmon-DNA cosmetic. To be explicit about the split the dossier itself draws:
- **Topical human salmon-DNA serum: D.** No independent peer-reviewed human RCT exists. Verified.
- **Topical human PDRN (any source): D+.** The entire topical-human evidence base is *one* study — a 10-person, 4-week, **uncontrolled** pilot on *plant* (peony) PDRN from an industry author [9]. One tiny uncontrolled pilot on a different molecule cannot lift the grade past D+.
- **Injectable human PDRN/PN: ~B−** (for contrast only — not the brand's route). Real but small, short, geographically narrow, partly industry-funded, and includes a retraction.

The dossier's own framing already lands at this conclusion; this verification confirms it rather than revising it.

### Verification findings (what I checked at primary-source level)
- **[1] Lampridou 2025 systematic review — VERIFIED and accurately represented.** Confirmed at PMC11845969: 9 studies, 219 patients, **all injectable / zero topical**, 4 RCTs + 4 case studies + 1 prospective, databases Embase/Medline/Cochrane, search window 2010–2024. The authors' verdict "none of the included studies achieved full scores in the quality appraisal" is a real quote. The "95.7% crow's-feet (Pak et al.)" figure does appear *inside* the review and is correctly flagged in the dossier as second-hand, not independently verified. **No overstatement.**
- **[9] Bak peony-PDRN — VERIFIED; this is the load-bearing topical datapoint and it is correctly hedged.** Confirmed: n=10, 4-week topical, plant (not salmon) source, industry author (NewLife BST Co.), elasticity +4.25% vs baseline, TEWL −21.01% explicitly in an **SLS-induced irritation** model (barrier *recovery*, not baseline TEWL). Critically, **there is no placebo/control arm**, so even the p<0.001 elasticity result is a within-subject before/after change that cannot be separated from a vehicle/moisturiser effect — this dossier now states that explicitly.
- **[10] Araco 2023 — VERIFIED.** n=20, **intradermal (injectable)**, split-face randomized vs saline, significant at 6 wk; Mastelli (PN-HPT patent holder) paid the article-processing charges. Sponsorship caveat is accurate.
- **[11] Cavallini 2022 — VERIFIED RETRACTED.** Retraction confirmed (jocd.14578 → retraction jocd.15936, 2024). Retraction reason was **image-integrity / possible figure duplication** (before/after images), which is even more reason to keep it out of the efficacy base. Correctly excluded.
- **[13] Titcomb 2025 (J Aesthetic Nursing) — VERIFIED.** Real; the "regulatory framework… currently not well-defined" (UK/MHRA) characterisation is accurate.

### Main caveats (the things a buyer/regulator would attack)
1. **Evidence-tier laundering is the central risk.** All the persuasive efficacy (collagen, regeneration, wrinkle reduction, the 95.7% figure) is **injectable or in-vitro**. None of it transfers to a topical. The dossier handles this correctly; any downstream marketing copy must not undo it.
2. **The single topical human study is plant-derived, n=10, and uncontrolled.** It is the *only* human-topical datapoint and cannot support a "clinically proven topical salmon-DNA serum" claim — nor even a confident "reduces TEWL / improves elasticity" claim, because there was no control arm and the source molecule differs.
3. **Penetration is unsolved, not merely unaddressed.** DNA fragments (~50–1,500 kDa) are orders of magnitude above the ~500 Da passive-permeation ceiling. Absent device-assisted delivery (which exits cosmetic territory) or validated encapsulation, dermal delivery of intact A2A-active DNA from a leave-on serum is **undemonstrated**. This is correctly treated as the gating variable.
4. **Industry fingerprints across the base.** Mastelli funded [10]; NewLife BST authored [9]; a retraction sits in [11]. The independent, large, topical RCT does not exist.
5. **Best defensible topical positioning = hydration / soothing / barrier-support + story/SEO**, not anti-wrinkle or "regeneration." The dossier's Verdict (§11) and Claims (§8) already say this.
