# Ingredient: Ergothioneine (INCI: Ergothioneine)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product

> Exhaustive, citation-backed literature review for the brand research base. Evidence is tiered
> (in-vitro → animal → human ORAL → human TOPICAL) and graded honestly. The single most
> important finding for a *topical* brand: **the strong human clinical data are for ORAL intake;
> human *topical* evidence is thin, mostly industry-generated, and complicated by a real
> cellular-uptake question.** Read §4 and §5 before writing any copy.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** L-Ergothioneine (abbreviated EGT or ERGO).
- **INCI:** Ergothioneine. (Cosmetic-grade is the L-isomer.)
- **Chemistry:** A sulfur-containing, betaine-derivative amino acid — the 2-mercaptohistidine betaine (a thiol/thione that exists predominantly as the more stable **thione** tautomer at physiological pH, which is why it resists auto-oxidation unlike most thiol antioxidants).
- **CAS:** 497-30-3.
- **Source:** Synthesized in nature **only** by certain fungi, mycobacteria and cyanobacteria. Humans (and most animals) **cannot make it** — it is obtained entirely from diet, with **mushrooms by far the richest source** (oyster/shiitake/king oyster highest; e.g., gray oyster ~1,310 mg/kg dry weight, white button ~630 mg/kg dry weight) [3].
- **Key forms / derivatives / trade names:** Cosmetic and supplement grade is now predominantly **fermentation-derived synthetic L-ergothioneine** (e.g., "Ergoneine," Tetrahedron; "ErgoYoung," "Dr. Ergo / Dr. Ergo Youth," various biotech suppliers). Mushroom-extract forms ("hiratake"/Pleurotus, golden/king oyster extracts) are standardized to an EGT content. Delivery-enhanced forms exist (liposomal EGT, ROS-responsive polymer nanovesicles) precisely because raw EGT penetrates cells poorly — see §5.

---

## 2. Class & mechanism

**Class:** Antioxidant / cytoprotectant. Often marketed as a "longevity vitamin" or "stress vitamin."

How it works at the skin/cell level:

1. **Direct ROS scavenging.** EGT neutralizes hydroxyl radicals, singlet oxygen, superoxide, hydrogen peroxide, and peroxynitrite, and **chelates redox-active metal ions (Fe²⁺, Cu²⁺)** to block Fenton-type radical generation [2][12]. Unusually for a thiol, it is extremely resistant to auto-oxidation, so it is stable at physiological pH and in formulation (a genuine practical advantage over vitamin C/resveratrol).

2. **A dedicated active transporter — OCTN1 / "ETT" (SLC22A4).** This is the defining feature. EGT is **largely cell-membrane-impermeable** and depends on the **organic cation transporter OCTN1** to be carried *into* cells, where it accumulates against a concentration gradient (sodium-dependent, saturable active transport) [2][6]. OCTN1 is expressed in tissues under high oxidative load and, importantly, **in human keratinocytes and dermal fibroblasts** [7][8]. This is the mechanistic core of the "adaptive cytoprotectant" hypothesis (Paul & Snyder): the body deliberately concentrates EGT at sites of injury. It is also the formulation Achilles' heel: benefit requires not just penetrating the stratum corneum but being actively transported into living cells.

3. **Nrf2/KEAP1 pathway activation (indirect antioxidant).** Beyond direct scavenging, EGT upregulates the master antioxidant transcription factor **Nrf2**, inducing endogenous antioxidant/phase-II enzymes (HO-1, NQO-1, γ-GCLC) and raising intracellular **glutathione** — i.e., it boosts the cell's *own* defenses, not just sacrificing itself [9][10][13]. Signaling implicated: PI3K/AKT, ERK, JNK, PKC.

4. **Suppression of UV-driven collagen breakdown.** In UV-stressed fibroblasts EGT inhibits the **AP-1 (c-Fos/c-Jun)** pathway, downregulating **MMP-1 (collagenase)** and preserving type-I procollagen [9][12] — the canonical photoaging cascade.

5. **Mitochondrial/genomic protection & other emerging signals.** Reduces oxidative damage to mtDNA; associations with SIRT1/SIRT6 in non-skin cells; proposed epigenetic roles via GSH/SAM pools. These are mechanistic/emerging, **not** established for topical skin [13].

## 4. Evidence review by tier (core of the dossier)

> **Bottom line up front:** EGT has a *deep, credible mechanistic and in-vitro skin file* and *good human data for ORAL intake*. **Human TOPICAL efficacy is supported almost entirely by industry/conference material and supplier claims, not by independent peer-reviewed RCTs.** Be ruthless about not laundering oral results into topical claims.

### TIER 1 — In-vitro (strong volume, consistent direction)

- **Markova, Karaman-Jurukovska, Dong, Damaghi, Smiles & Yarosh (2009),** *Free Radical Biology & Medicine* 46(8):1168-76 — "Skin cells and tissue are capable of using L-ergothioneine as an integral component of their antioxidant defense system." Foundational paper (AGI Dermatics): human keratinocytes (NHEK) and fibroblasts (NHDF) **express OCTN1**, take up, store and use EGT; RNAi silencing of OCTN1 abolishes uptake; EGT protects skin cells from UV oxidative damage. **Limitation:** cultured cells; authors were a commercial skincare developer. [8]

- **Hseu, Gowrisankar, Chen, Yang & Yang (2020),** *Oxidative Medicine and Cellular Longevity*, PMID 32104530 — UVA-irradiated human skin fibroblasts. At **submicromolar EGT (0.125–0.5 µM)**: ↓ROS, ↓MMP-1, ↑type-I procollagen, inhibited AP-1 (c-Fos/c-Jun) nuclear translocation, ↑Nrf2 + antioxidant genes (HO-1, NQO-1, γ-GCLC), ↑glutathione. **Limitation:** in-vitro only; no human/in-vivo outcomes. [9]

- **Hseu et al. (2015),** *Free Radical Biology & Medicine* (S0891-5849(15)00237-3) — "Dermato-protective properties of ergothioneine through induction of Nrf2/ARE-mediated antioxidant genes in UVA-irradiated human keratinocytes." Confirms Nrf2/ARE induction and photoprotection in keratinocytes. **Limitation:** in-vitro. [10]

- **Ko, Kim, Ahn, Kim, Lee & Shin (2021),** *Experimental Cell Research* (S0014-4827(21)00047-1) — "Ergothioneine alleviates senescence of fibroblasts induced by UVB damage of keratinocytes via activation of the Nrf2/HO-1 pathway and HSP70 in keratinocytes." Keratinocyte/fibroblast **co-culture**: EGT-pretreated keratinocytes reduced downstream fibroblast **senescence**. **Limitation:** in-vitro model of paracrine senescence; not a topical product. [11]

- **Aruoma, Whiteman, England & Halliwell** (early mechanistic work) and **Botta et al. / Servillo et al.** — EGT scavenges singlet oxygen/superoxide and suppresses **TNF-α and MMP-1** in UV-irradiated human dermal fibroblasts; chelates metals; resists auto-oxidation. **Limitation:** isolated systems / cultured cells. [12]

- **Combination study — MDPI *Processes* 9(7):1204 (2021)** — Compared EGT, ferulic acid, glutathione singly and combined against UVB photoaging in human skin fibroblasts; antioxidant combinations outperformed singles. **Limitation:** in-vitro. [14]

- **Pigmentation (in-vitro + zebrafish) — *Journal of Dermatologic Science/Cosmetic Dermatology* (S2950-1997(25)00180-6, 2025)** — EGT induced **autophagy via OCTN1** in melanocytes/keratinocytes, driving anti-melanogenesis and melanin degradation; tyrosinase-binding inhibition shown computationally. Notes "a certain whitening effect" with **0.1% topical EGT** in a (briefly described) human test. **Limitation:** mechanism mostly in-vitro/zebrafish; the human topical claim is under-detailed. [15]

**Tier-1 verdict:** Robust, internally consistent in-vitro story for antioxidant defense, Nrf2 induction, MMP-1 suppression, and senescence mitigation in *human skin cells*. This is real and differentiating — but it is mechanism, not clinical proof.

### TIER 2 — Animal

- **5XFAD Alzheimer's mouse model (Whitmore et al., 2022,** PMC9228400) — oral EGT ↓oxidative stress, ↓amyloid, restored glucose metabolism. [16] Plus rat subchronic toxicity (NOAEL 800 mg/kg/day — see safety). Zebrafish whitening (above). **All systemic/oral or non-skin; none establish topical skin efficacy.**

### TIER 3 — Human ORAL (the strongest human tier — but it's oral)

> These are the studies most often cited to "prove" EGT for skin. They are real and several are placebo-controlled — but the route is **ingestion**, so they support a *supplement/ingestible* story, **not** a topical claim. Plasma EGT rose 4.7× in the Pleurotus trial, confirming systemic delivery.

- **Hanayama et al. (2024),** *Frontiers in Medicine* 11:1396783 (PMID 38887673) — **RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled**, 77 women, **25 mg/day EGT** from Pleurotus ("hiratake") tablets, 12 weeks. Results: ↑skin moisture (temple at 8 wk; arm at 8–12 wk), ↓TEWL trend, significantly better **wrinkle and texture** scores at 12 wk, ↓UV-spot score; plasma EGT ↑4.7× (3.4→15.9 µM). **Limitations:** small; mushroom matrix (not isolated EGT) so other actives confound; industry-linked; oral. [1]

- **Oral DR.ERGO® capsules (2025),** *medRxiv* preprint 2025.10.16.25337962 — **RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled**, 66 women aged 35–59, **30 mg/day EGT**, 8 weeks. Significant vs placebo: ↓melanin, ↓erythema, ↑glossiness, ↑elasticity, ↓wrinkles, ↓spots. **Limitations:** **preprint (not peer-reviewed)**; small; manufacturer-sponsored; oral. [4]

- **Zhang & Li (2023),** *Biomedical Journal of Scientific & Technical Research* 20 — **open-label, single-center, NO placebo**, 19 healthy adults, **25 mg/day EGT (DR.ERGO)**, 4 weeks. VISIA: ↓UV spots (p<0.01), ↓brown spots (p<0.01), ↓pores, ↓wrinkles. **Limitations:** no control group → cannot exclude placebo/seasonal effects; very small; oral. [5]

- **Golden/king oyster mushroom EGT trial (Francis Academic Press, 2024)** — reported as 28-day double-blind RCT, **100 mg/day**, with ↑gloss, ↑elasticity, ↑water content, ↓wrinkle area and an **anti-glycation** signal. **Limitations:** lower-tier journal; mushroom matrix; oral. [17]

- **Citrine/Pleurotus extract (≈2022)** — 30 subjects, 28 days oral, reported ↑hydration, ↓TEWL, ↑elasticity, ↓wrinkle area, anti-carbonylation. **Limitations:** small; industry; oral. [18]

**Tier-3 verdict:** A surprisingly coherent set of small ORAL trials (several placebo-controlled) showing modest improvements in hydration, elasticity, wrinkles and pigmentation, with confirmed systemic uptake. Useful for an *ingestible* angle. **Not transferable to a topical claim.**

### TIER 4 — Human TOPICAL (the weakest tier — and the one our brand cares about)

This is where the file is thin and must be stated bluntly:

- **AGI Dermatics / Yarosh (AAD 2008 presentation + Remergent product line)** — conference/press data: topical EGT formulations, EGT outperformed idebenone on hydroxyl-radical and MMP-1 endpoints in photoaged skin. **Not a peer-reviewed RCT; commercial source; methodology not independently verifiable.** [19]
- **Pigmentation report of "0.1% topical EGT" whitening** within the 2025 autophagy paper — under-described, embedded in a mechanism paper. [15]
- **Supplier/cosmeceutical literature** (Alfa Chemistry, octagonchem, Mironova, BOC Sciences, etc.) cites typical topical use 0.1–2% but offers **no independent controlled human topical efficacy trial.** [20][21]

**Tier-4 verdict:** **There is no robust, independent, peer-reviewed human TOPICAL RCT demonstrating ergothioneine anti-aging efficacy.** What exists is: (a) strong in-vitro human-skin-cell data, (b) good human ORAL data, (c) industry/conference topical material. For honesty: topical anti-aging efficacy in humans is **plausible but not proven**.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

This is the make-or-break technical issue and the most under-discussed in marketing:

- **EGT is hydrophilic and largely cell-membrane-impermeable.** It does not freely diffuse into cells; it **requires OCTN1** to be actively imported [6][8].
- **Two barriers, not one.** A topical must (1) cross the lipophilic **stratum corneum** (hard for a water-soluble zwitterion) *and* (2) be **actively transported into living keratinocytes/fibroblasts via OCTN1** to deliver its intracellular Nrf2/glutathione benefit. Surface ROS-scavenging can occur without uptake, but the "longevity" intracellular biology depends on transport.
- **Encouraging:** human keratinocytes and fibroblasts **do express OCTN1** and demonstrably take up and use EGT in culture [8]. So the receptor is present in skin — the question is whether enough EGT reaches it through the barrier in vivo.
- **Delivery-system R&D exists precisely because raw penetration is limited:** liposomal EGT (reported ~2.2× HaCaT uptake), and **AG86-peptide ROS-responsive polymer nanovesicles** to deliver EGT to basal keratinocytes (AAPS PharmSciTech 2025) [22]. Their existence is itself evidence that plain EGT penetration is suboptimal.
- **Net:** A simple aqueous EGT serum likely delivers good surface/upper-layer antioxidant defense; meaningful intracellular delivery to deeper living cells probably benefits from an encapsulation/penetration system. Do not assume the impressive intracellular in-vitro effects occur from a basic topical formula.

## 6. Formulation

- **Concentration:** No independently validated "effective topical %" exists. Industry practice spans **0.01–0.1%** (common antioxidant adjunct levels) up to **0.1–2%** in "hero" cosmeceutical serums [20][21]. The one human topical whitening mention used **0.1%** [15]. Treat any specific % efficacy claim as unsubstantiated.
- **pH:** Stable across roughly **pH 4.5–8** [20] — flexible, compatible with most serum/cream systems and with low-pH actives.
- **Stability:** **Excellent** — resists auto-oxidation; long shelf life; heat- and pH-tolerant. A real advantage over L-ascorbic acid and resveratrol, which degrade readily.
- **Solubility:** Water-soluble (imidazole + carboxyl H-bonding) → easy in aqueous serums, essences, toners, emulsions' water phase.
- **Pairings (synergistic/complementary):** vitamin C + vitamin E + ferulic acid (network antioxidant), niacinamide, glutathione (combination outperformed singles in-vitro [14]), peptides, hyaluronic acid; fits a resveratrol/NAD "longevity" narrative. As a stable antioxidant it can also help protect a formula's other oxidation-prone actives.
- **Conflicts:** No notable chemical incompatibilities reported; it is mild and pH-flexible. Main "conflict" is conceptual — pairing it with strong oxidizers is wasteful.
- **Sensory:** Odorless/colorless, no tackiness or staining at use levels; sensory-neutral, easy to formulate elegantly.

## 7. Safety

- **Tolerability:** Excellent. No notable irritation, stinging, or sensitization signal in the literature; it is an endogenous human metabolite present in skin.
- **Photosensitivity:** None identified — unlike some antioxidants, it is photoprotective, not photosensitizing.
- **Oral safety (well-characterized):** EFSA Novel Food assessments judged synthetic L-ergothioneine **safe**, with a rat-study **NOAEL 800 mg/kg bw/day**; no genotoxicity concern; approved at **30 mg/day (adults), 20 mg/day (children >3 yr)**. The original **2016** opinion explicitly *excluded* pregnant/breastfeeding women and children under 3; a **2017** follow-up statement extended the safety conclusion to **infants, young children, and pregnant/breastfeeding women** [23][24]. This is reassuring background safety even though it concerns ingestion.
- **Pregnancy:** No topical-specific contraindication identified; EFSA found oral use safe in pregnancy [24]. (Still, defer to "consult your physician" boilerplate for pregnancy copy.)
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** Modern cosmetic/supplement grade is **fermentation-derived** (microbial biosynthesis), avoiding large-scale mushroom extraction — a defensible sustainability and supply-consistency story. Synthetic L-EGT is high purity (≥98–99%).
- **Sensitization:** No meaningful sensitizer signal reported.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic vs. drug:** As a topical, EGT is a **cosmetic** ingredient — claims must stay in appearance/defense territory. There is **no CIR (Cosmetic Ingredient Review) monograph** surfaced for EGT, and no topical drug status. The robust regulatory file (EFSA) is for **oral novel food**, which does **not** license topical efficacy claims.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "powerful antioxidant," "helps protect skin from oxidative/environmental stress," "supports skin's own antioxidant defenses," "helps defend against the visible effects of UV/pollution," "a longevity-science-inspired antioxidant," "visibly supports radiance and resilience."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like or unsubstantiated:** "boosts NAD⁺/glutathione in your skin," "repairs mitochondrial/cellular/DNA damage," "the longevity vitamin that extends skin-cell lifespan," "treats hyperpigmentation/rosacea/acne," "reduces wrinkles by X%," "anti-glycation treatment." Most of these are either oral-only findings, in-vitro mechanisms, or unverified — flag for substantiation/legal.
- **Cross-format honesty rule (important):** Do **not** cite the ORAL human trials (Hanayama, DR.ERGO, etc.) as evidence for a **topical** product. If we ever run an ingestible, those become fair game.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier:** Mid-to-high / premium. Indicative B2B pricing roughly **$2,500–$4,500 per kg** for high-purity cosmetic grade (varies with grade, volume, supplier) [20][25] — but topical use levels are tiny (0.01–0.5%), so cost-in-formula is manageable.
- **Form:** Fermentation-derived synthetic L-ergothioneine is now the standard, giving **reliable, scalable, high-purity** supply (multi-ton/month capacity reported) [21].
- **Suppliers:** Tetrahedron (Ergoneine), Mironova Labs, plus numerous Chinese cosmetic-grade producers (ProVita, YR Chemspec, etc.). Trade names include Dr. Ergo / Dr. Ergo Youth, ErgoYoung.
- **Supply considerations:** Historically pricey/scarce; biotech fermentation has eased this. Premium but defensible.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **"Longevity vitamin" is a real, respectable scientific hypothesis — about DIET, not topicals.** Bruce Ames' triage theory and Paul & Snyder's cytoprotectant argument concern *systemic* aging; blood EGT declines with age and is lower in frailty/cognitive decline/Parkinson's [2][3]. None of that proves a face cream does anything. Using "longevity vitamin" in topical copy is fair *as a science story* but must not imply proven topical life-extension.
- **The biggest overreach:** transplanting **oral** trial results (wrinkles ↓, elasticity ↑, TEWL ↓) onto a **topical** product. Most consumer marketing blurs this line.
- **The under-disclosed caveat:** **OCTN1-dependent, membrane-impermeable** uptake means a basic topical may not deliver the intracellular benefits the in-vitro data show. Marketing rarely mentions this.
- **NAD⁺ / mitochondrial / Sirtuin claims:** emerging, mostly non-skin, and unproven for topical EGT — keep out of copy.
- **What's genuinely strong:** stability, safety, tolerability, the dedicated-transporter science hook, and consistent in-vitro human-skin-cell antioxidant/Nrf2/MMP-1 data. That's a credible, differentiated *supporting antioxidant* story without overreach.

---

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️→✅ Conditional / Supporting.** Use ergothioneine as a **differentiated supporting antioxidant with a credible longevity-science hook** — not as a hero with proven topical anti-aging efficacy.

- **One-line reasoning:** Excellent stability/safety and a genuinely interesting "dedicated-transporter / longevity vitamin" science story, backed by strong in-vitro human-skin-cell data and good human *oral* data — but human *topical* efficacy is unproven and penetration is gated by OCTN1, so it earns a supporting role with honest defense-and-appearance claims (and is worth pairing with a penetration-enhanced/encapsulated form).
- **Longevity-relevance note:** Strong narrative fit for a skin-longevity brand (declines with age; actively concentrated at oxidative-stress sites; pairs with a resveratrol/NAD story) — provided we tell it as antioxidant *defense* biology, not as proven cellular life-extension in a jar. If an **ingestible** ever enters the roadmap, EGT's human oral evidence makes it a much stronger hero candidate there than topically.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Independent adversarial fact-check of every headline efficacy claim against external sources (PubMed/PMC, journal sites, EFSA). The question driving the check: *is each claim HUMAN and TOPICAL, or quietly borrowed from in-vitro / animal / oral data — and are the cited studies real and accurately characterized?*

### Overall evidence grade — **C** (for the brand's purpose: TOPICAL anti-aging)

Justification by route:
- **In-vitro human-skin-cell evidence: A-grade** (deep, consistent, real). The mechanism file is genuine.
- **Human ORAL evidence: B-grade** (several real small RCTs, modest effects, but industry-funded and ingested — not the brand's route).
- **Human TOPICAL anti-aging efficacy: D-grade** (essentially unproven in independent peer-reviewed trials).

The dossier's headline use-case is a **topical** product, so the **overall grade is C**: the topical story rests on strong *mechanism* + good *oral* data, with **no independent human topical RCT**. This is "weak/indirect," not "moderate human-topical." The dossier already states this honestly in §4/§10/§11 — the grade simply formalizes it.

### What checked out (citations are real and accurately described)

- **Markova et al. 2009, *Free Radic Biol Med* 46(8):1168–76 [8]** — real; keratinocytes/fibroblasts express OCTN1 and use EGT against UV oxidative stress. Correctly flagged as **in-vitro** and **commercial-author (AGI Dermatics)**. ✔
- **Hseu et al. 2020, *Oxid Med Cell Longev* (PMID 32104530) [9]** — real; submicromolar (0.125–0.5 µM) EGT, AP-1↓, MMP-1↓, procollagen↑, Nrf2/HO-1/NQO-1/γ-GCLC↑. Correctly flagged **in-vitro only**. ✔
- **Ko et al. 2021, *Exp Cell Res* 400(1):112516 [11]** — real; keratinocyte→fibroblast co-culture senescence rescue via Nrf2/HO-1/HSP70. Correctly flagged **in-vitro co-culture**. ✔
- **Hanayama et al. 2024, *Front Med* 11:1396783 (PMID 38887673) [1]** — real RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled; **77 completed (39 EGT / 38 placebo)**, 25 mg/day, 12 wk; moisture/wrinkle/texture/UV-spot improvements; plasma EGT 3.4→15.9 µM (4.7×). **Funded by Hokuto Corporation; three authors are Hokuto employees; mushroom matrix (not isolated EGT); ORAL.** All of this is disclosed in the dossier. ✔
- **EFSA NOAEL 800 mg/kg bw/day; 30 mg/day adult [23][24]** — confirmed (2016 opinion; 2017 statement extended to pregnancy/young children). ✔ (timeline corrected in §7.)
- **"Longevity vitamin," diet-only, mushrooms richest, OCTN1/ETT-dependent membrane-impermeable uptake, blood EGT declines with age [2]** — all confirmed in Beelman et al. 2020. The core mechanistic hook is sound. ✔
- **DR.ERGO medRxiv preprint [4]** — exists; correctly flagged **preprint / manufacturer-sponsored / ORAL**. ✔
- **Pigmentation/autophagy paper 2025 [15]** — real; its "in vivo" data are **zebrafish**, and the human "0.1% topical whitening" line is an under-detailed aside, not a controlled trial. ✔ (table row softened.)

**No fabricated citations or misattributed findings were found.** The dossier does not launder oral results into topical claims — it explicitly forbids doing so (§8 cross-format honesty rule), which is the single most important integrity point for this ingredient.

### Main caveats (read before writing any copy)

1. **The topical efficacy claim is mechanistic + oral by proxy, not demonstrated.** No independent, peer-reviewed, controlled **human topical** anti-aging RCT exists. Treat all "reduces wrinkles / improves elasticity / fades spots" outcomes as **ORAL** findings that do **not** transfer to a serum/cream.
2. **The strongest human data are industry-funded and small.** Hanayama (n=77, Hokuto employees + funding), DR.ERGO (preprint, manufacturer-sponsored), Zhang & Li (n=19, open-label, NO placebo, manufacturer ingredient). None is large or independent. Effects, where present, are modest.
3. **Penetration is genuinely gated.** EGT is hydrophilic and OCTN1-dependent for intracellular delivery; a basic aqueous serum may deliver surface antioxidant defense without the intracellular Nrf2/glutathione biology the in-vitro data show. The existence of liposomal/nanovesicle delivery R&D is itself evidence plain penetration is suboptimal.
4. **"Longevity vitamin" is a *dietary/systemic* hypothesis.** It is legitimate science about ingestion and ageing — it says nothing proven about a face cream. Usable as a *story*, never as implied topical life-extension.
5. **Mushroom-matrix confound.** Several oral trials used Pleurotus/oyster extracts, not isolated EGT, so other mushroom actives may contribute to the reported effects.

### Claims that should be softened (and were, or should be, in copy)

- **Already softened in this pass:** the §3 pigmentation row (downgraded Weak–moderate → Weak; "one report of 0.1% topical whitening" reframed as an under-detailed mechanism-paper aside); the §7 EFSA pregnancy/timeline statement (corrected to reflect 2016 exclusion vs 2017 extension).
- **Do NOT soften — they are already defensible:** §4 tier verdicts, the §8 risky-claims list, and the §11 "supporting antioxidant, not proven topical hero" verdict are accurate and appropriately hedged.
- **For downstream marketing, soften / avoid:** any topical claim of measured wrinkle/elasticity/spot reduction (those are oral); "boosts glutathione/NAD⁺ in your skin"; "repairs cellular/mitochondrial/DNA damage"; "the longevity vitamin that extends your skin cells' lifespan"; any specific "effective %." Keep copy in **defense/appearance** territory ("powerful, stable antioxidant," "supports skin's own antioxidant defenses," "longevity-science-inspired").

### Verdict

The dossier is **honest and well-sourced** — a model of not over-claiming. Its own internal grading already matches this audit. The only correction to its self-assessment: be explicit that for a **topical** product the headline evidence grade is **C (weak/indirect)**, because the human efficacy data are oral and the in-vitro data, however strong, are mechanism, not clinical proof.
