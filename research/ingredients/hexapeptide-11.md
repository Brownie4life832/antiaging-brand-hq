# Ingredient: Hexapeptide-11 / Peptamide-6 (INCI: Hexapeptide-11)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⚠️ considering (CONDITIONAL — interesting mechanism, thin human topical proof)
**Class:** Peptides (yeast-derived signal / "senomorphic-leaning" oligopeptide)

> Scope note: This is an exhaustive, citation-backed literature review for the brand's research base. Evidence is separated strictly by tier (in-vitro → animal → human oral → human topical). Where a benefit is borrowed from a non-topical format, an in-vitro model, or supplier marketing, it is flagged. Claims discipline per [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md) and [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md). See also the class overview in [`peptides.md`](peptides.md).

## 1. Identity

- **Common / trade name:** **Hexapeptide-11**; sold mainly as **Peptamide™ 6** (Arch Personal Care Products, now part of **Lonza**; the line traces to Croda/Arch heritage suppliers). Misspelled "Pentamide-6" / "Pentamide 6" appears widely in retail copy — it is the *same* material. [1][2][6][9]
- **INCI:** **Hexapeptide-11** (the synthetic/purified peptide). A related but **distinct** INCI exists: **Saccharomyces/Hexapeptide-11 Ferment Filtrate Extract** — a yeast *ferment filtrate* containing the peptide in a fermentation matrix, not the isolated peptide. Do not treat the two as interchangeable on a label. [2][4][12]
- **Sequence:** **Phe-Val-Ala-Pro-Phe-Pro (FVAPFP)** — a six-residue oligopeptide of phenylalanine, valine, alanine and proline. [1][6][9]
- **CAS:** **161258-30-6**. **Molecular formula** C₃₆H₄₈N₆O₇; **MW ≈ 676.8 g/mol**. White to off-white solid; water-dispersible cosmetic grades are typically supplied as dilute aqueous solutions. [9]
- **Origin / how it's made:** Originally **isolated from *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* (yeast) ferment** fractions (Lupo et al. 2007, cited in the originating patent); the dominant bioactive fraction was identified by gene-microarray screening, then reproduced by **solid-phase peptide synthesis** to high purity for commercial use. So although it is "yeast-derived" in heritage, the cosmetic-grade **Hexapeptide-11** is normally **synthetic**. [3][5][6]

## 2. Class & mechanism

Hexapeptide-11 is a **signal/messenger peptide** — it is proposed to act not by being incorporated structurally but by modulating gene expression and intracellular stress-response pathways in dermal cells. Its reported mechanisms (almost all from **in-vitro** work, much of it tied to the manufacturer or academic collaborators) are unusually "longevity-biology-flavoured" for a cosmetic peptide:

- **Proteostasis (protein-quality-control) activation.** In normal human diploid fibroblasts, hexapeptide-11 caused **dose- and time-dependent up-regulation of the proteasome, autophagy genes, molecular chaperones, and antioxidant-response genes**, with **increased nuclear Nrf2 accumulation**, higher proteasomal subunit expression, and measurably higher proteasome peptidase activity. (Sklirou et al. 2015) [5]
- **Anti-senescence / senomorphic signalling.** It **delays the onset of cellular senescence** in intrinsically aged and oxidatively/UV-stressed cells, read out as reduced **SA-β-galactosidase** staining. The proposed upstream mechanism is **reversible down-regulation of ATM and p53** (the DNA-damage → senescence axis) plus increased expression of the DNA-repair glycosylase **OGG1**. (Gruber et al. 2013; originating patent) [3][6]
- **ECM / dermal-support gene signalling.** Gene-microarray surveys of fibroblasts treated with ~1% Peptamide-6 reported up-regulation across five functional clusters: **transmembrane proteins, growth factors, matrix proteins (collagen/elastin-related), skin-lipid development, and cell-stress/heat-shock proteins** — interpreted as a "firming / renewal / water-retention" signature. [2][6]
- **Reported on the cell-behaviour side:** induced extracellular MMP activity while *suppressing* cell migration in one in-vitro arm — a mixed signal noted but not fully resolved. [5]

**Bottom line on mechanism:** The mechanistic story (Nrf2 → proteasome/autophagy/chaperones; ATM/p53 → senescence delay) is **biologically coherent and genuinely aligned with aging-hallmark science** — more so than most "collagen peptides." But it is built on **fibroblast/keratinocyte/dermal-papilla cultures**, not on proof that topical application reproduces these pathways in living human skin.

## 4. Evidence review by tier  *(the core of this dossier)*

### Tier 1 — In-vitro (cell & cell-stress models) — *the strongest, most credible body of data*

- **Sklirou AD, Ralli M, Dominguez M, Papassideri I, Skaltsounis AL, Trougakos IP. 2015.** *Hexapeptide-11 is a novel modulator of the proteostasis network in human diploid fibroblasts.* **Redox Biol. 5:205–215.** doi:10.1016/j.redox.2015.04.010. Models: human diploid **lung (IMR90)** and **skin (BJ)** fibroblasts; concentrations **1%, 2%, 5% (v/v)** and **300 µM**. Findings: non-toxic; **dose-/time-dependent activation of proteasome, autophagy, chaperone and antioxidant-response genes**; **nuclear Nrf2 ↑**; proteasome subunit expression + peptidase activity ↑; **suppression of oxidative-stress-induced premature senescence** (SA-β-Gal ↓). *Limitations:* in-vitro core; led by an academic group (Trougakos lab, Univ. of Athens) **but the author affiliations include the supplier — Lonza — and Korres S.A. Natural Products, so this is a supplier-collaborative paper, not independent**, and it frames the material as an anti-aging agent; the included human arm is small (see Tier 4). **This is the best peer-reviewed paper on the ingredient — but treat its independence as compromised.** [5]
- **Gruber JV, Ludwig P, Holtz R. 2013.** *Modulation of cellular senescence in fibroblasts and dermal papillae cells in vitro.* **J Cosmet Sci. 64(2):79–87.** PMID:23578831. Showed hexapeptide-11 (FVAPFP) **delays senescence onset** in intrinsically aged fibroblasts, extrinsically (stress-)aged fibroblasts, and extrinsically aged dermal papilla cells; effects **reversible and dose-dependent** between **0.1%–1.0%**; mechanism tied to **reversible ATM and p53 down-regulation.** *Limitations:* **Gruber is the inventor/Arch (manufacturer) scientist; Holtz is at a contract testing lab (BioInnovation Labs)** — i.e., **manufacturer-originated** data; *J Cosmet Sci* is an industry-facing journal; no independent replication located. [6]
- **Originating patent — US 2011/0052676 A1 (Arch Personal Care Products; inventor J.V. Gruber).** Claims FVAPFP at **0.01–5 wt%** for delaying intrinsic/stress-induced senescence; data: passage-18 fibroblasts with **SA-β-Gal ↓**; keratinocytes at 1% with **ATM/p53 ↓**; UV-stressed dermal papilla cells with SA-β-Gal ↓ at 0.5–1.0%; **OGG1 ↑ at 0.1%.** *Limitation:* a **patent**, not peer-reviewed; inherently promotional. [3]

**Verdict (Tier 1):** Mechanistically rich and internally consistent (proteostasis + senescence + Nrf2/antioxidant). **Caveat: heavily manufacturer-originated**; even the most academic-looking paper (Sklirou 2015) carries **supplier (Lonza) and Korres co-authorship** and uses the supplier's material and conclusions — so there is **no fully independent body of efficacy data** at any tier.

### Tier 2 — Animal

- **No published animal efficacy studies** for topical skin-aging endpoints were located. [unverified — none found]

**Verdict (Tier 2):** Essentially **no animal evidence**.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL

- **None.** Hexapeptide-11 is a topical cosmetic ingredient; there is **no oral human trial** and no basis for any ingestible claim. (Do **not** borrow from the unrelated *oral collagen-peptide* literature — different molecules entirely.) [11]

**Verdict (Tier 3):** **No oral evidence; do not borrow oral-peptide claims.**

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL  *(the only tier that can support a product claim — and it is thin)*

**4a. Controlled / RCT-grade human data:**
- **None that qualifies as an independent randomized controlled trial.** A 2026 systematic review & meta-analysis of RCTs of topical/oral peptides for skin aging (Nukaly et al., *Front Med* 13:1618306) **does not include hexapeptide-11 at all** — it surfaces only when peptides have qualifying RCTs, and this one has none. [11]

**4b. Lower-tier human topical (uncontrolled / small / supplier-linked):**
- **Human in-vivo arm within Sklirou et al. 2015** — a **skin-deformation/elasticity assay in 25 volunteers, twice daily for 4 weeks**, using a **2.8% (v/v)** preparation. Reported **significant improvement in the initial elastic response** (Ue) and deformation parameters. *Limitations:* **small (n=25), short (4 wk), single readout (cutometer elasticity, not wrinkles/histology), embedded in a supplier-aligned paper, design not described as a double-blind vehicle-controlled RCT.** This is the **single most relevant human topical data point — and it is weak.** [5]
- **Supplier (Peptamide-6) efficacy claims** — "increases elasticity, firmness, regulates collagen, supports renewal/water retention," anchored to the n=25 study and to the in-vitro gene-microarray work at **~1%** use level. **Marketing-grade, not independent.** [1][2][6]
- **Retail "up to +30% collagen/elastin after 7 days" / "firmness after 4 weeks" type claims** — these circulate in product copy and ingredient-blog write-ups (e.g. peptide-supplier blogs and retail ingredient pages); the **+30% figure traces to in-vitro/ex-vivo gene-expression data, not a human clinical wrinkle/collagen measurement** (the retail sources that quote it explicitly attribute it to *in-vitro and ex-vivo* tests). Treat as **in-vitro, repackaged as if clinical.** Note also that some retail copy mislabels hexapeptide-11 a "lipopeptide" — it is **not** lipidated (see §5). [2][10]

**Verdict (Tier 4):** Topical human evidence is **one small (n=25), short, single-endpoint, supplier-aligned elasticity study** — no independent RCT, no wrinkle/photoaging clinical endpoint, no published vehicle-controlled trial. **Far below the bar of retinoids, niacinamide, or even copper peptides.**

#### What is proven for TOPICAL human use vs. borrowed

- **Plausibly supported (weak human topical):** a **measurable short-term improvement in skin elasticity** (cutometer) at ~2.8% over 4 weeks, single small study. [5]
- **Borrowed from in-vitro (NOT proven topical-clinical endpoints):** senescence delay, ATM/p53 down-regulation, Nrf2/proteasome/autophagy activation, antioxidant defence, "+30% collagen/elastin," "regulates collagen." [3][5][6][10]
- **Overreaching / unsupported for topical human use:** "reverses aging," "removes wrinkles," "boosts collagen by X%," any oral/systemic benefit, mitochondrial/"oxygen consumption" benefit as a finished-product claim. [1][10]

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **MW ≈ 677 Da is above the ~500-Dalton rule of thumb** for efficient passive stratum-corneum penetration. Like most cosmetic peptides (Matrixyl ~802 Da, Argireline ~889 Da), unmodified hexapeptide-11 is **not expected to cross intact stratum corneum efficiently by passive diffusion** in large amounts. [7][8]
- It is **hydrophilic and unacylated** (no palmitoyl/myristoyl lipid tail), so unlike lipidated peptides it has **no built-in lipophilic carrier** to aid penetration — a genuine delivery weakness. [7][8]
- Penetration claims therefore rest on **formulation-assisted delivery** (penetration enhancers, liposomes/encapsulation, follicular route) or on signalling at the upper-epidermal level. **No published human penetration/pharmacokinetic study for hexapeptide-11 was located** — its in-vivo effect in the n=25 study is empirical, not mechanistically traced through the barrier. [5][7][8] [unverified — no PK data found]

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective / studied concentration:** **0.1–1.0%** drove the in-vitro effects [6]; the **human elasticity arm used 2.8% (v/v)** of the supplied preparation [5]. Note suppliers ship dilute solutions, so "2.8% of the preparation" is **not** 2.8% active peptide — read the COA for actual peptide loading.
- **pH:** No specific optimum published; peptides of this type are generally formulated in the **mildly acidic-to-neutral (~5–7)** range. Predicted pKa ≈ 3.4. [9] [partly unverified]
- **Stability:** Synthetic, purified material is reasonably stable as a solid; in finished aqueous formulas, standard peptide caveats apply (avoid extreme pH, high heat, and incompatible strong oxidants/reducers). No notable photosensitivity. [supplier-level; specifics unverified]
- **Pairings:** Compatible with the usual hydrators/conditioners — **hyaluronic acid, glycerin, niacinamide, panthenol (B5), vitamin E**, other signal peptides. Often blended into multi-peptide "cocktails." [4][12]
- **Conflicts:** No well-documented hard incompatibilities; as with most peptides, **avoid co-formulating in very low-pH or strongly oxidising systems** (e.g., high-dose L-ascorbic acid bases) where degradation is plausible.
- **Sensory:** Supplied as a low-odour aqueous solution; negligible impact on texture at typical use levels.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation / sensitization:** Reported **low irritation potential**; non-cytotoxic to normal human fibroblasts in vitro at tested levels. [5][10] No notable sensitization signal in available sources, but **independent clinical safety data are limited**.
- **Sun sensitivity:** **None known** — not a photosensitiser; usable AM/PM.
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** **No specific safety data.** As a non-retinoid, non-hormonal topical peptide it is *generally considered low-risk*, but the brand must **not state "pregnancy-safe"** — use "consult your doctor" framing. [no dedicated data — unverified]
- **Regulatory safety reviews:** **No dedicated CIR (Cosmetic Ingredient Review) report** specific to Hexapeptide-11 (CIR has covered *other* hexapeptides, e.g., Hexapeptide-12). **No EWG Skin Deep hazard score** is assigned (low data volume). Absence of a formal panel review is a **data-gap flag**, not evidence of harm. [11]
- **Sourcing / sustainability:** Synthetic (solid-phase) production avoids wild-harvest concerns; the **ferment-derived** INCI variant is a fermentation product (renewable feedstock). Verify **purity, COA, and actual peptide concentration** per supplier. [3][4]

## 8. Regulatory & claims

- **Cosmetic vs drug line:** As a cosmetic ingredient, Hexapeptide-11 may carry **appearance/cosmetic** claims (look of firmness, smoothness, "supports skin's renewal," "helps improve the appearance of elasticity"). The moment copy says it **"delays senescence," "repairs DNA," "activates the proteasome," "boosts collagen by 30%,"** it asserts a **structure/function / drug-like** effect (US FDA framing per [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md)) **and** rests on **in-vitro data only** — a double problem (overreach + drug-adjacent). Flag any such language for substantiation/legal review.
- **EU:** Hexapeptide-11 is permitted as a cosmetic ingredient under Regulation (EC) 1223/2009; **no specific Annex restriction** identified. Verify the current CosIng entry before label claims. [2][12] [confirm with regulatory counsel — unverified for specific market]
- **US:** Regulated as a cosmetic ingredient; **no monograph drug status**, no CIR conclusion of safety on record (see §7). [11]
- **Safe cosmetic language:** "a yeast-derived signal peptide," "helps support the look of firmer, more elastic skin," "studied for its effects on skin-cell stress-response pathways" (kept appearance-level), "in a small 4-week user study, skin showed improved elasticity readings." Always pair the senescence/proteostasis science with an explicit **"shown in laboratory (in-vitro) studies"** qualifier.
- **Risky / overreaching:** "anti-senescence / senolytic," "reverses cellular aging," "repairs DNA," "boosts collagen +30%," "activates autophagy in your skin," "pregnancy-safe."

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier: moderate (per active), but cheap per finished formula** because it is used at fractions of a percent. Less premium-priced than branded copper peptides or growth factors, more than commodity humectants. [9][10]
- **Suppliers:** **Peptamide™ 6 (Arch Personal Care / Lonza)** is the heritage branded grade; multiple peptide houses (Active Peptide Company, Creative Peptides, BOC Sciences, others) offer synthetic Hexapeptide-11 and acetate salt forms. The **Saccharomyces/Hexapeptide-11 Ferment Filtrate Extract** is a separate ferment product. **Verify INCI exactly, peptide purity/loading, and COA.** [1][2][4][9]

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **"Yeast longevity peptide / activates autophagy & the proteasome":** the **most scientifically interesting** part of the story — and **entirely in-vitro**. Real cell biology, **unproven as a finished-product human skin benefit.** [5][6]
- **"Anti-senescence / senomorphic":** based on **SA-β-Gal + ATM/p53 in cultured cells**, not human skin. Calling a cosmetic "senolytic/anti-senescence" is both overreach and drug-adjacent. [3][6]
- **"+30% collagen and elastin in 7 days":** an **in-vitro/ex-vivo gene-expression figure** recycled as if it were a clinical result. [10][2]
- **"Clinically proven to firm skin":** there is **one small (n=25), 4-week, single-endpoint elasticity study** — "clinically studied," not "clinically proven," and **no independent RCT**. [5][11]
- **"Pentamide-6":** a persistent **misspelling** of Peptamide-6 in retail listings; same ingredient. [1]
- **Independence problem:** the favourable data is **substantially manufacturer-originated** (Gruber/Arch patent + paper) or supplier-aligned (Sklirou). No independent confirmatory human trial located.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **CONDITIONAL / SUPPORTING-at-most.** Hexapeptide-11 has the **most longevity-biology-aligned mechanism of the common cosmetic peptides** (Nrf2 → proteasome/autophagy/chaperones; ATM/p53 → senescence delay), which makes it an attractive *story* ingredient for a skin-longevity brand. **But** the human topical evidence is a **single small, short, single-endpoint, supplier-aligned elasticity study with no independent RCT**, penetration is questionable (MW ~677, no lipid tail, no PK data), and most "proof" is in-vitro and manufacturer-originated.

**One-line reasoning:** A genuinely interesting *proteostasis/senescence-signalling* peptide whose mechanism fits the brand's thesis far better than its human proof does — usable as a **supporting "renewal-science" accent**, never as a hero or as a clinically-proven anti-wrinkle claim.

**Longevity-relevance note:** Mechanistically this is one of the *better-aligned* topicals to the actual hallmarks-of-aging framework (proteostasis loss, cellular senescence, oxidative stress / Nrf2 — see [`senolytics-os01-peptide.md`](senolytics-os01-peptide.md), [`spermidine.md`](spermidine.md), [`nad-niacinamide-longevity.md`](nad-niacinamide-longevity.md) for adjacent mechanisms). That makes it a strong *narrative* fit for a longevity positioning — **but the gap between mechanism and human-proven topical benefit is exactly the credibility trap this brand must avoid.** If used, frame the science honestly ("studied in the lab for skin-cell stress-response pathways"), keep claims at appearance/elasticity level, and pair it with better-evidenced actives. Log in [`../../ideas/considering.md`](../../ideas/considering.md).

## 13. Evidence verification (adversarial)

*Independent adversarial review of this dossier's claims, 2026-06-19. Method: each headline efficacy claim was checked against primary sources for (a) HUMAN + TOPICAL vs. borrowed in-vitro/animal/oral data, (b) whether the cited study is real and says what is claimed, and (c) study size / independence / funding. Sources re-checked: Sklirou 2015 (Redox Biol), Gruber 2013 (J Cosmet Sci), Nukaly 2026 meta-analysis (Front Med), ChemicalBook identity record, the Peptamide-6 supplier guide, and the retail pages cited for the "+30%" claim.*

### Overall evidence grade: **D / D-plus** (essentially unproven topically in humans; mechanism is real but in-vitro)

- **Human-topical clinical evidence: grade D.** The entire human-topical case is **one** open-label-style elasticity arm (n=25, 4 weeks, twice daily, cutometer Ue) embedded in a **supplier-co-authored** paper — no independent RCT, no vehicle control described, no wrinkle/photoaging/histology endpoint. That is the definition of "weak/indirect," and because the single study is not independent and not a controlled trial, it sits at the **D/C boundary rather than a clean C**. A 2026 systematic review of peptide RCTs for skin aging (Nukaly et al.) **does not include hexapeptide-11 at all**, confirming the absence of qualifying human-topical RCT evidence.
- **Mechanism (in-vitro): would be grade B in its own right, but it is in-vitro, so it does NOT lift the topical grade.** The proteostasis/Nrf2/senescence biology is genuine and internally consistent — but it is fibroblast/keratinocyte culture work, not proof of effect in living human skin through the barrier.

### What the adversarial check confirmed (the dossier is honest here)

1. **No silent borrowing of in-vitro/animal/oral data as human-topical proof.** This dossier is unusually disciplined: every senescence / ATM-p53 / Nrf2 / proteasome / "+30% collagen" claim is already explicitly tagged **in-vitro**, and the §4b / "hype vs evidence" sections already call out the in-vitro-repackaged-as-clinical move. No animal data exists and none is borrowed; no oral data exists and the dossier explicitly forbids borrowing the unrelated oral collagen-peptide literature. **Good.**
2. **Cited studies are real and say what is claimed.** Sklirou 2015 (Redox Biol 5:205-215) and Gruber 2013 (J Cosmet Sci 64(2):79-87) both verified — titles, journals, findings, concentrations and the n=25 / 2.8% / 4-week / Ue human arm all match. Chemical identity (CAS 161258-30-6; C₃₆H₄₈N₆O₇; MW ~676.8; FVAPFP) verified exactly. The MW-above-500-Da penetration concern and "no lipid tail" point are sound (the supplier guide confirms no lipid modification — so retail copy calling it a "lipopeptide" is wrong).
3. **Independence problem is real and, if anything, was understated.** Verified author affiliations on the "best" paper (Sklirou 2015) **include Lonza (the supplier) and Korres S.A.** — i.e., it is a supplier-collaborative paper, not an independent academic one. Gruber 2013 + the patent are manufacturer-originated (Arch). **There is no fully independent efficacy study at any tier.** (The dossier's Tier-1 verdict has been edited to state this plainly.)
4. **All key human-topical data is small / short / single-endpoint.** n=25, 4 weeks, one cutometer parameter. Nothing approaches RCT grade.

### Main caveats

- Treat hexapeptide-11 as a **mechanism-rich but human-topically-unproven** ingredient. The gap between "interesting longevity biology in a dish" and "does something visible on a person's face" is **not closed by any independent controlled human study.**
- The favourable evidence base is **not independent** — supplier/manufacturer fingerprints are on every supporting study.
- Penetration is genuinely doubtful (MW ~677, hydrophilic, no lipid carrier, **zero published PK/penetration data**), so even the mechanism may not be reaching the cells it acts on in a finished topical.

### Claims that should be softened (in this dossier or any downstream copy)
