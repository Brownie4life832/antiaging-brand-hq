# Ingredient: Senolytic / Senotherapeutic Peptide — OS-01 ("Peptide 14") (INCI: typically listed as a proprietary peptide blend; OneSkin discloses it as a 14-amino-acid peptide)

**Researched:** 2026-06-18 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product

## What it is
A short synthetic peptide (OneSkin's OS-01, marketed as "Peptide 14") positioned as a topical **senotherapeutic** — i.e., an active that targets cellular senescence, the build-up of damaged, non-dividing "zombie" cells that secrete inflammatory signals (the SASP) and are a hallmark of skin aging. It is the flagship active of the "skin longevity" category and the single most-cited ingredient when people talk about longevity skincare.

Important nuance: OneSkin's own published data suggest OS-01 acts more as a **senomorphic / senopreventive** (reducing markers of senescence and slowing cells entering the senescent state) than as a true **senolytic** (selectively killing existing senescent cells). The "kills zombie cells" framing is marketing-forward shorthand.

## What it does (claimed → evidenced)
| Benefit | Evidence strength | Source(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Reduces molecular markers of senescence in cultured skin / ex-vivo skin models | moderate (in-vitro / ex-vivo) | OneSkin lab data, Lifespan.io [1][4] |
| Improves skin barrier function & hydration / reduces TEWL (12-wk RCT) | moderate (one company-run RCT, active control not placebo) | OneSkin 12-wk study; Lifespan.io [1][3] |
| Lowers some inflammatory cytokines (e.g., IL-8) vs. control | weak–moderate | Lifespan.io summary [1] |
| Visibly improves wrinkles / firmness | weak (largely self-report + one vehicle-controlled trial reported by company) | Zonari et al. 2024, J Cosmet Dermatol [2] |
| "Reverses skin's biological age" | anecdotal / marketing — based on a proprietary molecular-clock readout, not an accepted clinical endpoint | OneSkin claims [4] |
| Actually destroys existing senescent cells in human skin in vivo | not established | [1][4] |

## How it fits the brand
Extremely on-thesis. "Repair / renew" maps almost perfectly onto the senescence-clearance / cellular-renewal story, and "skin longevity" is exactly the science-forward layer our positioning calls for ([`../../brand/positioning.md`](../../brand/positioning.md)). This is the most credible candidate for a **signature longevity hero** narrative — IF we can source a defensible senotherapeutic peptide and stay disciplined on claims. The catch: OS-01 itself is OneSkin's proprietary, patent-protected molecule; we cannot simply buy it. We would need either a licensed supply, a different commercially available senotherapeutic peptide, or to build the story around a peptide blend with our own (modest, honest) substantiation.

## Formulation notes
Peptides are generally water-soluble, used at low percentages, and want near-neutral to mildly acidic pH and cool, stable, low-oxidation environments. Avoid co-formulating with high concentrations of strong acids or harsh oxidants that can cleave/denature peptides. Pairs well with humectants (HA, glycerin), barrier lipids (ceramides), and niacinamide. Stability and real delivery into living epidermis/dermis are the open formulation questions for any "longevity peptide" — penetration of larger peptides is limited.

## Concerns & cautions
- Generally well tolerated in reported studies; low irritation expected for a low-% peptide.
- **Evidence is thin and largely in-house.** The strongest human data are company-run, use an active comparator rather than a true placebo, and have conflict-of-interest (authors hold commercial stakes). Independent replication is lacking.
- "Senolytic" as a drug-class term carries pharmacological baggage — overclaiming invites scrutiny.
- No specific pregnancy/sun-sensitivity flags identified for the peptide itself.

## Claims caution
- ✅ Safe cosmetic language: "supports skin's natural renewal," "helps visibly improve firmness, hydration and barrier resilience," "inspired by skin-longevity science," "helps skin look and feel younger."
- ⚠️ Risky / drug-like — avoid or legal-review: "senolytic," "kills/clears senescent (zombie) cells," "reverses aging," "reduces your skin's biological age by X years," "repairs DNA." These are structure/function or disease-adjacent claims and, with "senolytic," edge toward drug territory. Any in-vivo senescence-clearance claim is not substantiated. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## Cost / sourcing
High cost tier. True OS-01 is proprietary to OneSkin (not openly sold to formulators). Alternative routes: license; or source a different senotherapeutic/senomorphic peptide or "longevity peptide" blend from a premium peptide supplier (expect specialty-peptide pricing and MOQ). Supply, IP/patent landscape, and substantiation files all need diligence before committing.

## Verdict for the brand
⚠️ — **Strategically the best fit for a "signature longevity hero," but only viable with a sourceable, defensible peptide and rigorously honest claims.** The category-defining narrative is real; the independent evidence is not yet strong, and the marquee molecule is locked up by a competitor. Pursue as a hero *candidate*, contingent on sourcing + a claims strategy that sells the longevity story without drug-like overreach.
