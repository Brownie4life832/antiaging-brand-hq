# Ingredient: Carnosine relatives — Carcinine (Alistin), Anserine, Homocarnosine (INCI: Decarboxy Carnosine HCl; et al.)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Antioxidants (histidine-containing dipeptides / "imidazole peptides"; carbonyl-quenching anti-glycation agents)

> **One-line honest summary:** This is a *family* of carnosine cousins whose appeal is being **carnosinase-resistant** (they survive in the body where carnosine is destroyed in ~1 minute). The biochemistry — antioxidant, lipid-aldehyde quenching, anti-glycation — is real and decades-old. But the **skin-specific, human-topical** evidence is thin and heavily conflicted: **Carcinine (Alistin)** rests almost entirely on its Monaco supplier (Exsymol) and on one prolific, commercially-conflicted author (Babizhayev); **Anserine** and **Homocarnosine** are essentially **oral/research compounds with no meaningful standalone topical-cosmetic record**. Treat carcinine as a credible **supporting** anti-glycation/antioxidant active, not an evidence-proven hero — and treat anserine/homocarnosine as **narrative cousins**, not formulation candidates.

---

## 1. Identity

This dossier covers four related molecules. The first (carnosine) is the parent reference; the latter three are the title "relatives."

| **L-Carnosine** (parent) | β-alanyl-L-histidine | **Carnosine** | 305-84-0 | C₉H₁₄N₄O₃ / 226.23 |
| **Carcinine** (= "Alistin", decarboxy-carnosine) | β-alanyl-**histamine** (carnosine minus the carboxyl group) | **Decarboxy Carnosine HCl** | free base **56897-53-1**; **2HCl salt 57022-38-5** | C₈H₁₄N₄O / 182.22 (free base); 255.14 (2HCl) |
| **Anserine** | β-alanyl-**N(π)-methyl**-L-histidine (methylated carnosine) | (no established cosmetic INCI in common use) | 584-85-0 | C₁₀H₁₆N₄O₃ / 240.26 |
| **Homocarnosine** | **γ-aminobutyryl** (GABA)-L-histidine | (no established cosmetic INCI in common use) | 3650-73-5 | C₁₀H₁₆N₄O₃ / 240.26 |

**Key identity facts:**
- **Carcinine = "Alistin."** "Alistin" is the trade name of Exsymol (Monaco), sold as a **10% aqueous solution** (or as the anhydrous powder "Carcinine 2HCl"). Its INCI is **Decarboxy Carnosine HCl**. [13][1][2] It is the **only** member of this family with a real, registered cosmetic supply chain and TDS. The FDA UNII (6X7K9I5QR7) lists first marketing in a derm serum ca. 2014. [11]
- **Carcinine is decarboxylated carnosine** — chemically it is **β-alanyl-histamine** (the histidine of carnosine has lost its carboxyl group and become histamine). This has a real pharmacological consequence (see §7): carcinine is a **selective histamine H3-receptor antagonist** (Ki ≈ 0.29 µM). [12][10]
- **Anserine** is methyl-carnosine; it is the dominant histidine-dipeptide in poultry/fish ("chicken essence"). **Homocarnosine** is a GABA-histidine dipeptide found mainly in **brain/CSF**, not skin. [8][9][6]
- **Forms you'll actually encounter:** Carcinine as Alistin (10% soln) or Carcinine 2HCl powder. Anserine only as a research chemical / oral nutraceutical (often co-sold with carnosine as "chicken-meat extract"). Homocarnosine only as a research chemical. There is **no mainstream topical anserine or homocarnosine cosmetic raw material** — an honest red flag for anyone marketing them as topical actives.

## 2. Class & mechanism

All four are **histidine-containing dipeptides (HCDs / "imidazole peptides")**. Their bioactivity comes from the **imidazole ring of histidine** plus the dipeptide backbone — isolated β-alanine, histidine or imidazole alone are inactive, confirming the intact dipeptide is required. [4][5]

Shared mechanistic pillars (well-established *biochemistry*, mostly in non-skin systems):

1. **Direct free-radical scavenging & peroxyl-radical trapping.** Carnosine, anserine and homocarnosine all trap peroxyl radicals and act as electrochemical reducing agents at physiological concentrations (the classic Kohen/Ames 1988 PNAS demonstration). [5]
2. **Lipid-peroxidation / lipid-aldehyde quenching.** They inhibit lipid peroxidation and — importantly — quench **reactive carbonyl/aldehyde species** (4-HNE, MDA, acrolein) generated when membrane lipids oxidize. Carcinine specifically forms stable adducts with 4-HNE and reduces lipid hydroperoxides (LOOH → harmless alcohol LOH), a "lipid-peroxidase-like" action that Babizhayev's group reported exceeds vitamin E in their assays. [3][10][13]
3. **Anti-glycation ("sacrificial" carbonyl trapping).** Reducing sugars and reactive carbonyls (glyoxal, methylglyoxal) preferentially react with the dipeptide instead of with collagen/elastin lysine residues — so the dipeptide is "sacrificed" to spare structural proteins, slowing **Advanced Glycation End-product (AGE)** crosslinking. Carnosine can also act on already-formed early adducts ("transglycation"/reverse-glycation). [14][15][7]
4. **Transition-metal chelation.** They chelate Cu²⁺ (and to a lesser extent Fe²⁺/Zn²⁺), shutting down metal-catalysed Fenton chemistry. Carnosine is a notably better copper- than iron-driven antioxidant. [5][7]
5. **The carcinine/anserine USP — carnosinase resistance.** Plasma **carnosinase (CN1)** destroys carnosine extraordinarily fast (human plasma half-life ≈ **1.2 min**; ~99% hydrolysed within hours of an oral dose). [16] Carcinine (no carboxyl → not a carnosinase substrate) and anserine (methylated → poor substrate) **survive** where carnosine does not. This single fact is the entire strategic rationale for the "relatives": same chemistry, far better persistence. [10][16]

**Carcinine-specific extras (per supplier/Babizhayev):** "peroxidase-like" membrane repair, faster DNA recovery after UV, and (in vitro) up-regulation of **sirtuins** and pro-collagen, plus suppression of pro-inflammatory cytokines. [13]

**Honest translation:** pillars 1–4 are solid, decades-old biochemistry, but were established in muscle/brain/eye/liposome systems and in cultured cells — **not** in living human skin via topical delivery. Pillar 5 (carnosinase resistance) is real but is an *oral/systemic* argument; it is less relevant to a topical product, where carnosinase in the stratum corneum/epidermis is a much smaller factor than skin penetration.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER

> The honest headline: **the strongest skin-relevant data in this whole family belong to the *parent* carnosine, not to the three "relatives."** Carcinine's skin file is supplier- and single-author-dominated. Anserine/homocarnosine have essentially **no topical skin literature** — their human data are *oral* and non-cutaneous.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (mechanism; strongest, oldest, but skin-translation unproven)

- **Kohen R, Yamamoto Y, Cundy KC, Ames BN (1988), *PNAS* 85(9):3175–79.** The foundational antioxidant paper for the *whole family*: carnosine, **homocarnosine and anserine** all trap peroxyl radicals and are electrochemically active reducing agents at physiological concentrations; carnosine inhibits Cu/ascorbate oxidation of deoxyguanosine. Establishes them as endogenous antioxidants of muscle/brain. *Limitation:* pure chemistry, no skin, no cells. [5]
- **Babizhayev MA, Seguin M-C, Gueyne J, et al. (1994), *Biochem J* 304:509–516.** The original **carcinine** antioxidant paper: L-carnosine and carcinine scavenge OH• (deoxyribose assay) and reduce/deactivate lipid hydroperoxides (free and membrane-bound) at 10–25 mM, with peroxidase activity reportedly exceeding glutathione peroxidase; isolated imidazole/β-alanine were inactive. *Limitation:* very high mM concentrations; liposome/biochemical, not skin; first paper of a long self-referential program. [3]
- **Babizhayev MA (2006), *Life Sci* 78(20):2343–57.** Review positioning N-acetylcarnosine, carcinine and L-carnosine for **eye and skin care**; carcinine "efficient deactivation of lipid hydroperoxides," antioxidant "superior to vitamin E." *Limitation:* narrative review by the conflicted inventor. [10]
- **Exsymol/Alistin in-vitro dossier (TDS, internal study codes DO_0751/DO_1044/DO_1133/DO_1157; Fréchet et al. 2010 "A3_1295").** On reconstructed human epidermis & fibroblasts, Alistin reportedly: prevents glucose-induced protein cross-linking (outperforming vitamin E and aminoguanidine); cuts MDA production; restores GSH/GSSG redox within ~8 h post-UV vs ≥24 h untreated; reduces TT-dimers; ↑ sirtuins and pro-collagen dose-dependently; accelerates collagen-lattice contraction; ↓ TNF-α/IL-1α/IL-8. *Limitation:* **manufacturer data, not peer-reviewed, no raw n's**; treat as directional marketing, not proof. [13]
- **Wu H-C, Shiau C-Y, et al. (2003), *J Food Drug Anal* 11(2):148–153.** Comparative antioxidant assay (linoleic-acid autoxidation, DPPH, reducing power, Cu²⁺-chelation): carnosine and anserine are both effective antioxidants, constituent amino acids are ineffective, and **no synergy** was found in combination. The load-bearing point: the relatives are **not *more* potent** than carnosine in vitro — their edge is stability, not raw antioxidant power. (Verified abstract confirms both dipeptides are active but does not unambiguously rank carnosine above anserine.) [4]

**Verdict for Tier 1:** the *class* antioxidant/anti-glycation biochemistry is well-proven. The *carcinine-specific* skin biology is real but rests on one author + the supplier.

### Tier 2 — Animal (sparse for skin)

- Carcinine has animal pharmacology data for **retina (light-damage/retinopathy)** and **brain histaminergic** systems (Chen Z et al., 2004, *Br J Pharmacol* 143:573–80 — carcinine lowers brain histamine, an H3-antagonist effect). [12] **None of this is dermatological.**
- Anserine improved neurovascular function/memory in an **Alzheimer's mouse model** (Kaneko J et al., 2017, *Sci Rep* 7:12571) — again, not skin. [demonstrates systemic antioxidant/anti-AGE plausibility, not topical efficacy]
- **No credible animal *skin/photoaging* model isolating carcinine, anserine or homocarnosine** was found.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL (real RCTs exist — but oral, and not isolating skin)

- **Babizhayev MA et al. (2012), *J Dermatolog Treat* 23(5):345–84.** A 4-month randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial (n≈42) of **oral non-hydrolyzed carnosine ("Can-C Plus")** reporting continuous, significant improvement in skin appearance/fine lines vs placebo. *Limitations:* **oral not topical; same conflicted author/company; sprawling combined biochem+clinical paper; carnosine, not the relatives.** [20]
- **Alkhatib A, Feng W, et al. (2020), *Nutrients* 12(4):1146 (PMC7231017).** RCT crossover, **n=10 healthy men**: oral **anserine** (15 & 30 mg/kg) post-exercise raised **SOD ~50%** and preserved catalase, but also raised GSSG / lowered GSH:GSSG ratio (mixed). *Limitations:* tiny n, oral, exercise-stress model — **no skin endpoint at all.** [17]
- Carnosine oral supplementation has broader (still modest) human longevity/metabolic literature, but bioavailability is gutted by carnosinase (half-life ~1.2 min). [16]

**Verdict for Tier 3:** human oral data exist and are *suggestive* of systemic antioxidant/skin benefit for carnosine (and antioxidant-enzyme benefit for anserine), but **none of it proves topical skin efficacy**, and the strongest "skin" RCT is conflicted.

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier a skincare brand actually needs — and it is weak)

- **RCT (parent carnosine, multi-ingredient): Kaczvinsky JR, Griffiths CEM, Schnicker MS, Li J (2009), *J Cosmet Dermatol* 8(3):228–33.** Two double-blind, randomized, **split-face controlled** studies (n=42 and n=35) of Olay (P&G) anti-aging products that **contained carnosine** alongside niacinamide + peptides (Pal-KT, Pal-KTTKS); 4 weeks twice-daily; 3-D periorbital imaging showed significant Ra/Rz improvement vs no-treatment. *Limitation:* **carnosine's contribution cannot be isolated** — it's one of several actives, and the dominant evidence-bearing ingredient (niacinamide/peptides) is the more likely driver. [21]
- **Ex vivo human skin (parent carnosine; best topical-mechanism data): Narda M, Peño-Mazzarino L, Krutmann J, Trullas C, Granger C (2018), *Skin Pharmacol Physiol* 31(6):324–331.** Topically applied **0.2% carnosine** (aqueous and in a facial cream, 2 mg/cm²) on MGO-glycated human skin **explants** reduced **CML −64%** and **pentosidine −48%** in epidermis (aqueous); the full cream did even better. *Limitations:* **single 41-y-old donor (n=1, triplicate)**; ex vivo not in vivo; **ISDIN-funded** (3 authors employees, Krutmann advisor); and again **carnosine, not the relatives.** [14]
- **Topical carcinine (Alistin) human data = supplier only.** Exsymol's TDS reports: 28-day in-vivo wrinkle reduction (21 volunteers, 67% showing visual improvement); a 56-day brightening study where Alistin 1% *boosted* the brightener Albatin (−28% melanin density, +7% luminosity vs Albatin alone); and a 6-month hair-density combo study. [13] *Limitations:* **manufacturer in-house, small, not peer-reviewed, frequently as a combo "booster" rather than as a standalone active.**
- **Topical anserine: none found.** **Topical homocarnosine: none found.** No peer-reviewed human topical study isolates either as a skincare active. This is the single most important honesty point of the dossier.

**Verdict for Tier 4:** For a topical brand, the *defensible* human evidence is (a) ex vivo + multi-ingredient RCT data for the **parent carnosine's anti-glycation**, and (b) **supplier-grade** data for **carcinine/Alistin**. **Anserine and homocarnosine have no topical evidence and should not be claimed as topical actives.**

## 5. Topical bioavailability

- **Small, water-soluble, hydrophilic.** Carcinine free base MW 182; carnosine 226; anserine/homocarnosine 240. All are small enough to be *plausible* penetrants but are **hydrophilic, charged at skin pH** (imidazole + amine), which limits passive stratum-corneum diffusion — the classic problem for water-loving small molecules.
- **Carcinine's claimed edge:** Exsymol states Alistin shows "good bioavailability and localizes on the keratinocyte cell membrane" on reconstructed human epidermis (immunohistochemistry). [13] This is supplier data; independent skin-penetration pharmacokinetics are not available.
- **Stability advantage is the real selling point, not penetration.** The family's headline benefit (carnosinase resistance) governs *systemic/oral* persistence; topically, the relevant advantages are (i) carcinine's better air/enzymatic stability vs carnosine in-formula, and (ii) being water-phase friendly. [13][1]
- **Net:** Topical delivery is *plausible but unquantified*. No human topical PK exists for carcinine, anserine or homocarnosine. Assume modest epidermal availability and formulate to support penetration (humectant base, adequate concentration).

## 6. Formulation

Based primarily on the Alistin TDS (the only real raw-material datasheet in this family). [13]

- **Concentration:** Alistin (10% carcinine solution) **advised dose 0.5–1.5%** of the finished formula (= ~0.05–0.15% active carcinine). In-vitro/in-vivo work used 0.7–1% Alistin. For powder (Carcinine 2HCl) match the active equivalent.
- **pH:** Alistin solution is **~pH 5**; "no particular formulation restriction." Compatible with mildly acidic anti-aging systems. Carcinine is a base (imidazole/amine) so it can nudge pH up at high loads — check final pH.
- **Solubility / phase:** **Water-soluble; miscible with water, alcohol, glycols; NOT miscible with hexane, mineral or vegetable oils.** It is a **water-phase** active — keep it out of the oil phase.
- **Stability:** Carcinine is "relatively stable in contact with air" and **substantially more stable than carnosine** (the whole point). Still, as an amine-bearing actic it can interact with formaldehyde-releasers and may discolour with strong oxidizers/aldehydes over time — pair carefully.
- **Pairings (rational):**
  - **Niacinamide** — co-present in the carnosine RCT and the ISDIN cream; complementary barrier/tone; both water-phase. [21][14]
  - **Other anti-glycation actives** (e.g., other carbonyl scavengers) for a coherent "anti-glycation" story.
  - **Antioxidant network** — fits alongside vitamin C / E / ferulic narrative; carcinine reportedly handles lipid hydroperoxides that lipophilic antioxidants miss. [13]
  - **A brightener** (Alistin was sold as a *booster* for melanogenesis inhibitors). [13]
- **Conflicts / cautions in-formula:** avoid strong aldehyde donors; recheck pH with high-amine loads; do not expect oil-phase incorporation.
- **Sensory:** Alistin is a "limpid, colorless liquid," density ~1.0, easy to add to the water phase; low expected impact on texture/scent at use levels.

## 7. Safety

- **General tolerance:** Exsymol reports Alistin "perfectly tolerated" in in-vitro (cell culture, reconstructed epidermis) and in-vivo (human volunteer) tolerance/toxicity testing; supplier even frames it as soothing/anti-inflammatory at use levels. [13] Histidine dipeptides are endogenous human molecules with a benign general profile.
- **Irritation/sensitization:** No notable irritation/sensitization signal in available data; but independent dermatological safety data on topical carcinine specifically are sparse (rely largely on supplier testing). Patch-test per normal new-active practice.
- **The histamine-H3 caveat (worth flagging honestly):** Carcinine is **β-alanyl-histamine** and a **selective histamine-H3 antagonist** (Ki ~0.29 µM); systemically it lowers brain histamine. [12][10] At cosmetic topical use levels (~0.05–0.15% active) systemic histaminergic effects are not expected, and no topical histamine-related adverse signal is documented — but it is a real pharmacological identity that distinguishes it from inert carnosine and should be noted for due diligence (and avoid implying any histamine/allergy "treatment" benefit, which would be drug-like).
- **Sun:** No photosensitization concern; mechanistically *photoprotective* (antioxidant), but **does not replace SPF** and we must not imply it does.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** No specific contraindication identified; these are endogenous dipeptides. As with any newer active and absent dedicated data, default to "consult a clinician" language rather than an affirmative safety claim.
- **Sourcing/animal origin:** Carcinine and anserine occur naturally in animal tissue (anserine is literally "chicken essence"); **cosmetic carcinine is synthetic / nature-identical** (Exsymol) or made by **microbial biotransformation in E. coli** (see §9) — so vegan/animal-free grades are achievable, but **verify the grade** if vegan positioning matters. Anserine nutraceuticals are typically animal-derived.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (cosmetic vs drug — per CLAUDE.md)

- **Status:** Cosmetic ingredient. **Decarboxy Carnosine HCl** is an established INCI; **Carnosine** is an established INCI. **Anserine and homocarnosine lack common cosmetic INCI usage** — using them topically would mean an unusual, hard-to-substantiate raw material (another reason to lean on carnosine/carcinine).
- **Safe cosmetic phrasing (✅):** "helps defend against oxidative stress," "supports skin's defenses against glycation," "helps protect the look of firmness and elasticity," "antioxidant," "helps visibly smooth," "supports a more even-looking tone." Keep to *appearance*/*supports* language (see glossary). [glossary]
- **Risky / drug-like (⚠️ flag for substantiation/legal):**
  - "**Reverses glycation**" / "breaks AGE crosslinks" / "**repairs** collagen" — Exsymol literally markets "reverse-glycation," but as *structure/function* claims these need strong substantiation and edge toward drug territory. Soften to "helps defend against glycation."
  - "**Boosts collagen / sirtuins X%**", "anti-inflammatory comparable to corticoids" (Exsymol's phrasing) — do **not** repeat; corticoid comparison is overtly drug-like.
  - "Protects DNA," "anti-tumor," "treats retinopathy/cataract" — these come from the pharmacology/ophthalmology literature and are **off-limits** for a cosmetic.
  - Any **histamine/allergy** benefit — drug claim; avoid.
- **Substantiation reality:** Most efficacy numbers we have are **supplier or single-conflicted-author** data. If we make an anti-glycation claim, anchor it to the *parent carnosine* ex-vivo data we can cite [14] and our own consumer/instrumental testing, not to Exsymol's brochure figures.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Carcinine (Alistin):** sole high-profile supplier **Exsymol S.A.M. (Monaco)**, distributed via Biosil Technologies / Tri-Iso and others. Sold as 10% solution (1/5/30/60 kg drums) or Carcinine 2HCl powder. **Single-supplier concentration is a real risk** (price/availability leverage; "single specialty molecule" caution — see decisions log on patented/single-source molecules). Premium specialty-active pricing tier.
- **Industrial carcinine production is now feasible at scale:** whole-cell **biotransformation in *E. coli*** has been published for large-scale carcinine manufacture (Sci­enceDirect, *J Biotechnol* 2022), which over time should improve supply/cost and enable animal-free grades. [22]
- **Anserine:** sourced as chicken/fish extract (nutraceutical) or research chemical (Selleck, etc.); **no cosmetic-grade topical supply chain** of note.
- **Homocarnosine:** **research-chemical only** (MedChemExpress, Cayman, GlpBio); not a cosmetic raw material.
- **Cost tier:** carnosine itself is moderately priced and widely available; **carcinine/Alistin sits at a premium, near-single-source tier.** If the goal is the anti-glycation story on a budget, **carnosine is the cheaper, better-supplied, better-evidenced** entry point; carcinine is the premium "stability-upgraded" version.

## 10. Hype vs evidence

| Marketing claim you'll see | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Carcinine is a more stable, more bioavailable carnosine" | **True for oral/systemic** (carnosinase resistance is real). Less meaningful topically, where penetration — not carnosinase — is the limiter. [16][10] |
| "Clinically proven anti-glycation & anti-wrinkle" (carcinine) | **Overstated.** The human topical data are **supplier in-house** (Exsymol) or from a **commercially-conflicted author** (Babizhayev, whose flagship NAC eye-drop claims a Cochrane review found unsupported). Independent peer-reviewed topical RCTs on carcinine are essentially absent. [13][23] |
| "Reverses glycation / repairs collagen" | Mechanistically plausible (transglycation) but **not demonstrated in vivo human skin**; a drug-adjacent claim. [13][14] |
| "Anserine / homocarnosine anti-aging skincare actives" | **Hype.** Their human data are **oral and non-cutaneous** (anserine: a 10-man exercise SOD study; homocarnosine: brain/CSF). **No topical skin evidence; no real cosmetic supply.** [17][5] |
| "Stronger antioxidant than the others" | Head-to-head in vitro, the relatives are **not more potent** than carnosine; their edge is *stability*, not raw antioxidant power. [4] |
| "Outperforms vitamin E / aminoguanidine" | From Babizhayev/Exsymol assays at high mM concentrations; directional, not a finished-product claim. [3][13] |

**The single biggest evidence-quality problem:** the carcinine-specific skin literature is **dominated by two interested parties** — the supplier (Exsymol) and one prolific author with a documented conflict of interest (Babizhayev / Innovative Vision Products), whose related eye-drop claims were judged unsupported by an independent Cochrane review. [23] That doesn't make the chemistry wrong — but it means we should price the *efficacy* claims as **low-independence evidence**.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ CONDITIONAL — "supporting" actor, not a hero. Lead with the parent (carnosine); use carcinine only as a deliberate premium stability upgrade; skip anserine & homocarnosine as topical actives.**

Reasoning:
- The **anti-glycation angle is genuinely on-trend and on-thesis** for a skin-longevity brand — glycation/AGE accumulation is a real hallmark of aged skin, and a credible "anti-glycation defense" pillar pairs well with the barrier-repair core. [15][14]
- But for a brand whose credibility rests on **evidence over hype**, this family's **independent topical human evidence is thin**, and the carcinine-specific record is **conflicted**. We cannot honestly call any of these a clinically-proven hero.
- **Best path:** if we want the anti-glycation story, **make carnosine the workhorse** (cheaper, better-supplied, has the strongest *topical-relevant* data — ex vivo CML/pentosidine reductions and a multi-ingredient periorbital RCT [14][21]) and consider **carcinine (Alistin) as a premium, stability-upgraded supporting active at 0.5–1.5%** in a water-phase, niacinamide-friendly system — framed as "antioxidant + anti-glycation support," never as a corticoid-like or collagen-"repair" hero.
- **Anserine & homocarnosine: skip as topical ingredients.** Keep them only as **narrative cousins** ("the carnosine family of longevity dipeptides") in education content — not on an INCI list and not in efficacy claims.
- **Single-source caution:** Alistin is effectively one-supplier (Exsymol). Per our stance on single-specialty molecules, that's a tolerable strategic option but a supply/pricing risk to weigh; the emerging *E. coli* biomanufacture of carcinine may de-risk this over time. [22]

**Longevity note:** Conceptually this family is a clean fit for a *skin-longevity* worldview — histidine dipeptides are bona-fide endogenous "geroprotective" antioxidants that **decline with age**, and **glycation is a core aging hallmark**. The longevity *story* is strong and authentic; the topical *proof* is not yet there. Use the family to enrich the brand's longevity narrative and as supporting anti-glycation/antioxidant chemistry — but anchor hard claims to carnosine's citable data and our own testing, and stay disciplined on language.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

_Independent verification pass — 2026-06-19. Each headline citation was checked for: (a) is the evidence HUMAN and TOPICAL, or borrowed from in-vitro / animal / oral? (b) is the cited study real and does it say what's claimed? (c) is it industry-funded or tiny?_

**Overall grade: C** (weak / indirect for the *topical* skincare use this brand needs).
- **Carcinine (Alistin) topically → C/D.** Human topical efficacy rests entirely on supplier (Exsymol) and a single commercially-conflicted author (Babizhayev). No independent peer-reviewed topical RCT exists.
- **Parent carnosine topically → C+ (borderline B−).** Best evidence is *ex vivo* anti-glycation (n=1 donor, industry-funded) plus a *multi-ingredient* RCT where carnosine cannot be isolated. Better than the relatives, still not standalone-proven.
- **Anserine & homocarnosine topically → D.** No topical skin evidence of any kind; their human data are *oral* (anserine) or *brain/CSF* (homocarnosine).

### Main caveats (what a buyer/regulator would attack first)
1. **No independent human topical RCT exists for carcinine, anserine or homocarnosine.** The single best topical datapoint in the whole file is for the *parent* (carnosine), is *ex vivo*, **n=1 donor**, and **industry-funded**.
2. **Two interested parties dominate carcinine's efficacy record** — the sole supplier (Exsymol) and one author with a Cochrane-documented conflict (Babizhayev). Treat all carcinine efficacy numbers as **low-independence evidence**.
3. **Carnosinase-resistance — the family's headline USP — is an oral/systemic argument and is largely irrelevant topically**, where penetration (not enzymatic destruction) is the limiter. Do not let it carry a topical claim.
4. **Anserine/homocarnosine have no cosmetic supply chain and no topical evidence.** Marketing them as topical actives is unsupported.

### Claims to soften / already softened
- ✅ The dossier already self-grades carcinine as "supporting, not a hero," skips anserine/homocarnosine as topical actives, and brackets every supplier/Babizhayev figure. No overstated *headline* claim was found that the dossier itself hadn't already flagged.
- ✏️ **Softened in this pass:** the Tier-1 line "carnosine > anserine across DPPH/reducing power/Cu-chelation" — the verified abstract confirms both are active but does not clearly rank one above the other, so the wording was changed to the defensible point (the relatives are *not more potent* than carnosine).
- ⚠️ **Keep watching:** any repetition of "superior to vitamin E," "exceeds glutathione peroxidase," or "comparable to corticoids" must stay attributed to high-mM in-vitro / supplier assays and must never appear as a finished-product or structure/function claim.

_Bottom line: citations are real and accurately represented; the dossier's own framing is unusually honest. The evidence simply does not support a topical "hero" claim for any member of this family — carnosine is the only one with even borderline-defensible topical data, and that data is ex vivo, n=1, and industry-funded._
