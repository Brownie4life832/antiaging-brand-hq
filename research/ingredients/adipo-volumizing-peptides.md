# Ingredient: Volumizing / Adipo Peptides — Adifyline® (Acetyl Hexapeptide-38) & Silusyne® (Acetyl Hexapeptide-39)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⬜ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ☑ rejected-leaning / ⬜ in a product
**Slug:** adipo-volumizing-peptides
**Class:** Peptides — signal peptide (adipocyte-signaling / metabolic-modulating subclass). *Not* a carrier, enzyme-inhibitor, or neuro-inhibitor peptide despite the umbrella class label in the brief.

> **One-line honest summary:** These are synthetic hexapeptides marketed to *change the volume of subcutaneous fat* under the skin — Adifyline to **add** facial/breast fat (PGC-1α agonist), Silusyne to **remove** it (PGC-1α antagonist, anti-cellulite). The biology is real in cultured fat cells, but the entire human-efficacy case rests on **manufacturer-run, unpublished, non-peer-reviewed in-vivo studies (n≈22)** with implausibly large headline numbers, and there is a **fundamental delivery problem**: these molecules must reach the *hypodermis* (the fat layer beneath the dermis) to work, which topical cosmetics do exceptionally poorly. Treat as **marketing-grade, not evidence-grade**, for skin-longevity purposes.

> **CRITICAL FRAMING — the brief groups two opposite ingredients.** The prompt pairs "Adifyline + Silusyne" as if one category, but **they are mechanistic opposites from the same supplier (Lubrizol/Lipotec):** Adifyline (Acetyl Hexapeptide-38) *up*-regulates PGC-1α to *grow* fat; Silusyne (Acetyl Hexapeptide-39) *down*-regulates PGC-1α to *shrink* fat. They share a chemistry, a supplier, an unproven delivery story, and a single regulatory target — so they're reviewed together — but you would never use both for the same goal. For a *facial anti-aging* brand the relevant one is **Adifyline** (volume restoration); Silusyne is a body-contouring/anti-cellulite tool.

## 1. Identity

### Adifyline® (the "volumizing"/fat-adding peptide)
- **Common/trade name:** Adifyline® (Lipotec / Lubrizol Life Science Beauty). Won *in-cosmetics* Innovation Zone "Best Ingredient" Award (Silver) 2011. [1][2]
- **INCI (active):** **Acetyl Hexapeptide-38**
- **INCI (trade solution):** Butylene Glycol, Water (Aqua), Acetyl Hexapeptide-38 — a transparent solution containing **0.05% active peptide**. [1]
- **CAS:** 1400634-44-7 · **FDA UNII:** XUJ27AR5LA [3][4]
- **Sequence:** Ac-Ser-Val-Val-Val-Arg-Thr-NH₂ (N-acetylated, C-amidated hexapeptide) [3]
- **Formula / MW:** C₃₀H₅₅N₉O₁₀ · **~701.8 g/mol** [4]
- **Origin:** Synthetic (solid-phase peptide synthesis). ISO 16128 natural-origin index assigned by supplier. [2]

### Silusyne® (the "anti-adipo"/fat-shrinking peptide)
- **Common/trade name:** Silusyne® (Lipotec / Lubrizol Life Science Beauty) — positioned for **cellulite / slimming / body contouring**. [5][6]
- **INCI (active):** **Acetyl Hexapeptide-39**
- **INCI (trade solution):** Soybean (Glycine Soja) Oil, Sorbitan Sesquioleate, Isohexadecane, Sodium Hyaluronate, Lauryldimonium Hydroxypropyl Hydrolyzed Soy Protein, Acetyl Hexapeptide-39 (a soy-based delivery emulsion). [7]
- **CAS:** 1382088-38-1 [8]
- **Sequence:** Ac-Ser-Ile-Tyr-Val-Ala-Thr-NH₂ [6]
- **Origin:** Synthetic. [6]

> **Name-confusion warning (important for copy & sourcing):** Do **not** confuse Acetyl Hexapeptide-38 (Adifyline, fat-volumizing) with **Acetyl Hexapeptide-8** (Argireline, the "Botox-like" wrinkle peptide, seq Ac-Glu-Glu-Met-Gln-Arg-Arg-NH₂) — these are completely different molecules with different jobs. Several secondary web sources (and at least one chemical-vendor article) **conflate -38 with -8** and even mislabel -38 as a "botulinum-toxin alternative," which is wrong. [9][16] Verify INCI + CAS on every COA.

## 2. Class & mechanism

Both are **synthetic signal peptides that act on adipocyte (fat-cell) gene regulation**, specifically the **PGC-1α / PPARγ adipogenic axis** in subcutaneous white adipose tissue (sWAT) — *not* on dermal collagen/elastin like classic matrikine "anti-aging" peptides (Matrixyl, etc.).

**The shared target — PGC-1α/PPARγ:**
- **PPARγ** is the master transcription factor of adipogenesis; **PGC-1α** (PPARγ coactivator-1α) is a transcriptional coactivator that interacts with PPARγ and modulates adipocyte differentiation and lipid handling. [10][11][12]

**Adifyline (Acetyl Hexapeptide-38) — agonist / "more fat":**
1. Identified by screening "millions" of hexapeptides via a reporter-gene assay. [3]
2. **Increases PGC-1α mRNA expression** (manufacturer in-vitro: +25.6% at 0.1 mg/mL, **+61.1% at 0.5 mg/mL** in human preadipocytes). [1]
3. → raises **adipogenesis rate** and **intracellular lipid accumulation** (+27.9% to +32.4% lipid droplets in vitro). [1]
4. Net intended effect: thicker subcutaneous fat → restored facial/breast/hand volume. [1][2]

**Silusyne (Acetyl Hexapeptide-39) — antagonist / "less fat":**
1. Acts as a **PGC-1α antagonist**: **reduces PGC-1α mRNA**, lowering preadipocyte→adipocyte differentiation and lipid accumulation. [5][6]
2. Claims to improve uniformity of the dermo-hypodermal junction and adipose "inner organisation" → smoother, less-dimpled (anti-cellulite) appearance. [5][6]

> **A real scientific tension worth flagging (honesty point):** In the canonical metabolic literature, **PGC-1α is best known for driving mitochondrial biogenesis, fatty-acid OXIDATION, and "browning" of white fat — processes that *reduce* white-fat lipid storage**, and its role specifically in *white* adipogenesis is described as **"contentious."** [10][11] Adifyline's marketing story ("more PGC-1α → more stored fat") runs against the grain of that mainstream view; Silusyne's story ("less PGC-1α → less fat") is arguably more consistent with classic PGC-1α biology but contradicts Adifyline's own premise. Both can't be straightforwardly "PGC-1α = lipid storage." This inconsistency is a flag that the proposed mechanism is, at minimum, oversimplified and not independently established for these specific peptides. Treat mechanism as *supplier hypothesis*, not settled science.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

The honest mapping is narrow. These peptides target essentially **one** hallmark — *subcutaneous fat-compartment volume loss* — which is a genuine and important driver of the **aged facial appearance** but is **not** one of the canonical molecular hallmarks of aging, and is mostly addressed clinically by **fillers/fat grafting**, not topicals.

| Hallmark | Relevance of these peptides | Evidence tier |
|---|---|---|
| **Subcutaneous fat / volume loss ("deflation")** | The actual target. Facial fat-compartment atrophy is real and central to looking older (Adifyline aims to reverse; Silusyne aims to reduce excess/cellulite) | Mechanism: in-vitro cell. Outcome: manufacturer in-vivo only [1][13][14] |
| **Collagen/ECM loss** | Indirect at best (some vendor copy claims "collagen synthesis / subcutaneous fat thickening," unsupported by primary data) | Weak / vendor claim [4] |
| **Barrier decline / hydration** | Not a mechanism; Silusyne's vehicle contains HA (hydration is from the vehicle, not the peptide) | None for peptide |
| **Oxidative stress** | Not targeted | None |
| **Glycation / AGEs** | Not targeted | None |
| **Cellular senescence** | Not targeted | None |
| **Mitochondrial decline** | Tangential (PGC-1α governs mitochondrial biogenesis) but not framed/tested as a skin benefit | None for skin |
| **Inflammaging** | Not targeted | None |
| **Photoaging (UV)** | Not targeted | None |
| **Pigmentation / tone** | Not targeted | None |
| **Microbiome** | Not targeted | None |

**Skin-longevity translation:** The "skin longevity" framing here is **anatomical/cosmetic** (restore lost fat volume) rather than **biological** (slow molecular aging). That's a meaningful distinction for a *longevity* brand: these are cosmetic-contour actives wearing peptide/longevity clothing, not geroprotectors.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core)

> **The single most important honesty point:** For both peptides, *all* of the human ("in-vivo") data is **manufacturer-generated, unpublished, and not peer-reviewed** — it lives in marketing brochures and trade-press articles, not in PubMed-indexed journals. I could find **no independent, peer-reviewed clinical trial** of Acetyl Hexapeptide-38 or -39 on humans. The strongest *independent* science is the *background* anatomy (facial fat compartments deflate with age — well established) and the *general* cell biology of PGC-1α/PPARγ — neither of which validates that *these topical peptides* do what's claimed in living skin.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell models) — *manufacturer data*
- **Adifyline, PGC-1α expression (Lubrizol/Lipotec brochure).** Human preadipocytes differentiated with PDM-2 medium ± Adifyline; after 10 days, qRT-PCR showed PGC-1α mRNA **+25.6% (0.1 mg/mL)** and **+61.1% (0.5 mg/mL)** vs differentiated controls. *Limitations: manufacturer study, no published methods/stats/replicate count, direct medium dosing (not transdermal), in vitro only.* [1]
- **Adifyline, lipid accumulation (same source).** Intracellular lipid droplets (fluorescence) **+27.9% / +32.4%** at 0.1 / 0.5 mg/mL. *Same limitations.* [1]
- **Silusyne, PGC-1α/lipid (Lubrizol).** Reported **reduction** of PGC-1α mRNA and lower lipid accumulation in preadipocyte-derived adipocytes. *No public quantitative dataset located; trade-press summary only.* [5][6]
- **PGC-1α/PPARγ adipogenesis biology (independent, peer-reviewed background).** PPARγ is the master adipogenic TF; PGC-1α coactivates it but is principally a regulator of mitochondrial biogenesis / fatty-acid oxidation / browning, with a "contentious" role in white adipogenesis. *Supports that the axis is real; does NOT validate the peptides' claimed direction of effect.* [10][11][12]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo) — *none located*
- **No published animal (rodent/other) studies** on Acetyl Hexapeptide-38 or -39 were found. Lubrizol holds patents titled *"Peptides that stimulate subcutaneous adipogenesis"* (US 10,494,402; US 10,766,927) describing the concept, but patents are **IP claims, not peer-reviewed efficacy evidence.** [15]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL — *not applicable*
- These are topical cosmetic actives; there is no oral format and no oral/systemic human data. (Unlike, e.g., spermidine, there's no borrowed oral-longevity literature to lean on.)

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters) — *manufacturer in-vivo only, unpublished*
- **Adifyline — cheek volume.** 22 female volunteers, **aged 50–60**, applied a cream with **2% Adifyline solution** (= 0.001% active peptide) to the cheeks **twice daily for 14 days**; cheek volume by Fringe Projection (3D morphology, mm³). Reported **+11.9% (≈+12%) cheek volume at 14 days.** *Limitations: n=22, no placebo/control arm described for the cheek study, 14 days only, single-center, manufacturer-run, unpublished/not peer-reviewed, no statistics or variance reported.* [1]
- **Adifyline — breast volume.** 22 women aged 25–40 (bra 80–90), split-body (2% Adifyline cream on one breast, placebo on the other), **twice daily for 56 days**; FOITS topometry at days 14/28/56. Reported **breast volume "+30-fold vs placebo" at 56 days.** *Limitations: the "30-fold vs placebo" framing is a relative-to-a-near-zero-placebo artifact (placebo change was minimal/near zero, inflating the ratio); absolute mm³ change is what matters and is not transparently reported; n=22, manufacturer-run, unpublished. A "30×" headline on n=22 with no peer review is a textbook over-claim.* [1]
- **Silusyne — cellulite/skin.** Trade-press states it "remarkably reduces the thickness of the dermo-hypodermal junction, cellulite, and abnormalities" and improves skin/adipose uniformity. *No sample size, duration, %-change, or statistics located in any accessible source; manufacturer-derived.* [5][6]

**Independent peer-reviewed clinical efficacy for either peptide: NONE FOUND.** A 2025 peer-reviewed review of the *neighboring* peptide Acetyl Hexapeptide-8 (Argireline) concluded its own evidence is limited and penetration-constrained, and that even for that far-better-studied cosmetic peptide, small studies are inconsistent and of "very low" quality — a sobering benchmark for the much-less-studied -38/-39. [16]

**Bottom line by tier:** Established (independent) → facial fat compartments deflate with age [13][14]; PGC-1α/PPARγ govern adipogenesis [10][11]. NOT established → that topical Acetyl Hexapeptide-38/-39 measurably and durably changes human subcutaneous fat volume. Every human number in market is manufacturer-supplied, unpublished, small-n, and (for breast "30×") rhetorically inflated.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration — *the fatal-flaw section*

This is the crux, and it is worse for these peptides than for ordinary dermal anti-aging peptides:

- **Wrong-depth target.** Classic anti-aging peptides need to reach the **dermis** (fibroblasts). Adipo-peptides must reach the **hypodermis / subcutaneous adipose layer** — *beneath* the dermis — where adipocytes and preadipocytes live. That is the deepest skin compartment and the hardest to reach by passive topical diffusion. [17][18]
- **Size.** Adifyline is **~702 Da**, comfortably **above the ~500 Da "rule"** for passive stratum-corneum penetration. Hydrophilic peptides at/above 500 Da penetrate poorly; the canonical Argireline (889 Da) is the textbook example of a cosmetic peptide that struggles to get through. [16][19]
- **Charge/polarity.** The Arg residue gives Adifyline a cationic, hydrophilic character — further reducing partitioning into the lipophilic stratum corneum. [3]
- **No published human penetration data** for -38 or -39 quantifying how much (if any) intact peptide reaches the viable epidermis, let alone the hypodermis. Even for the better-studied AH-8, penetration is "limited" and mechanism-in-skin is "rarely addressed." [9][16]
- **Why "browning/adipo peptide" delivery is genuinely hard.** Independent work that *did* get an adipocyte-targeting peptide into subcutaneous fat had to use **microneedles + iontophoresis** (active transdermal delivery), not a simple cream — underscoring that passive topical delivery to fat is not a solved problem. [17]
- **Practical read:** For a leave-on cream, the probability that a meaningful dose of intact Acetyl Hexapeptide-38 reaches subcutaneous adipocytes and shifts their PGC-1α/lipid program is **low and unproven.** The supplier's own in-vivo creams used 2% trade solution = **0.001% active**, an extraordinarily low concentration to be driving a "+12% volume" tissue change through the full skin thickness — another reason for skepticism.

## 6. Formulation

- **Use level (Adifyline trade solution):** **2%** recommended by supplier (= **~0.001% active peptide**, since the solution is 0.05% active). Water-soluble; incorporate in the water phase, post-emulsification, low/no heat. [1]
- **Use level (Silusyne):** Supplier emulsion; follow COA (typically a few %). Soy-oil/HA-based vehicle. [7]
- **pH / stability:** No public pH or stability dossier located. As acetylated/amidated short peptides they are reasonably robust vs simple hydrolysis, but: keep formulas **cool during addition**, avoid strong oxidizers and extremes of pH, run standard preservative-challenge + accelerated stability. The Arg/Thr/Ser residues are not notably reactive but **Maillard browning** with reducing sugars/aldehydes is a generic peptide watch-item. *Treat pH/stability as a known unknown; confirm with supplier TDS.*
- **Pairings (conceptual, for a volume-restoration story):** humectants/HA (immediate plumping while the long-game "fat" story is unproven), barrier lipids/ceramides, niacinamide, classic dermal-firming matrikines (Matrixyl-type) to cover the dermis while Adifyline (notionally) covers the fat layer. Penetration enhancers / liposomal or microneedle delivery would be *necessary*, not optional, to make the mechanism plausible.
- **Conflicts:** No specific incompatibilities documented; cationic Arg-containing peptide could in principle interact with strongly anionic systems — test.
- **Sensory:** At fractional-percent actives, no expected color/odor contribution from Adifyline (clear aqueous solution). Silusyne's soybean-oil vehicle adds emolliency and a faint oil note.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation/sensitization:** No specific published human irritation/sensitization (HRIPT/patch) data for Acetyl Hexapeptide-38 or -39 were located. At the tiny actual active concentrations used, systemic/irritation risk is presumably low, but **we'd generate our own finished-formula tolerability data.** [no primary safety study found — generate in-house]
- **Soy allergen (Silusyne specifically):** Silusyne's vehicle contains **Glycine Soja (soybean) oil and hydrolyzed soy protein** → relevant for **soy-allergic** consumers and labeling/transparency. Adifyline's vehicle (butylene glycol/water) has no such allergen flag. [7]
- **Sun sensitivity:** No photosensitivity signal; not a mechanism concern.
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** No data. Default to conservative copy ("consult your doctor"). The *concept* (modulating fat-cell differentiation) is biologically unremarkable at these doses but untested in pregnancy.
- **Theoretical mechanism caution:** A peptide intended to *promote* adipogenesis (Adifyline) is, in principle, doing the opposite of what one wants in fat-related metabolic dysfunction; there's no evidence of systemic effect from topical use at 0.001%, but the *narrative* ("grows fat") can read oddly and warrants careful framing.
- **CIR / formal safety review:** The Cosmetic Ingredient Review has assessed **Acetyl Hexapeptide-8 / -8 Amide** (concluded safe ≤0.005%) — but has **NOT** issued a specific safety assessment for Acetyl Hexapeptide-38 or -39. Do not borrow the -8 conclusion; -38/-39 are unreviewed by CIR. [20]
- **Sourcing purity:** Verify peptide identity (CAS), purity (HPLC), and residual-solvent/TFA-counterion data on COA from any supplier.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **The drug/cosmetic line is the central trap here.** Claims that the product **changes the body's structure** — *"increases fat,"* *"grows volume,"* *"enlarges/firms breasts,"* *"reduces cellulite,"* *"builds subcutaneous tissue"* — are **structure/function claims** that read as **drug** claims (US FDA) and likely cross the cosmetic line in the EU/UK as well. Breast-enhancement and cellulite-reduction claims are classic enforcement targets. This is higher-risk claim territory than most anti-aging actives.
- **The supplier itself disclaims the data for claims use.** Lubrizol's own brochure states the information is **not** to be presented "as evidence of finished product claim substantiation to any regulatory authority," and that the user is "solely responsible" for compliance. In other words, *the manufacturer's clinical numbers are explicitly not a substantiation package.* [1]
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language (if used at all):** "helps improve the appearance of facial volume," "for a smoother, more cushioned look," "visibly plumper-looking skin," "inspired by the science of facial fat compartments." Even these should be hedged given the evidence.
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation + legal review:** "restores lost facial fat," "increases cheek volume by X%," "+30× breast volume," "reduces cellulite," "rebuilds subcutaneous tissue," "non-surgical filler." None are supportable from the available (manufacturer, unpublished) evidence.
- **Substantiation reality:** To say *anything* performance-related we'd need our **own** finished-formula human study with a credible delivery system — and even then, durable subcutaneous-fat change from a topical is a high bar. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

- **Branded sources:** Adifyline® and Silusyne® are **Lubrizol Life Science (Lipotec)** trade products — buy the standardized solutions (defined active %, supplier TDS/COA, IP cover). [1][2][5]
- **Generic actives:** Acetyl Hexapeptide-38 (CAS 1400634-44-7) and -39 (CAS 1382088-38-1) are sold as raw peptides by research/peptide-chemical suppliers (BOC Sciences, GlpBio, MedChemExpress, TargetMol, and various Chinese cosmetic-peptide houses). - **Cost tier:** **Mid-to-high** on a finished-formula basis once you account for the branded actives — but note you're paying for a story more than a proven outcome. Peptides in general are dosed at fractional %, so raw-material cost-in-use is modest; the *value question* is efficacy, not price.
- **IP note:** Lubrizol patents on subcutaneous-adipogenesis peptides may constrain copycat positioning/claims — relevant if formulating around the concept. [15]

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (the overreach)

- **"Topical filler / non-surgical volume."** The headline promise — replace lost facial fat with a cream — is exactly the claim with the **least delivery plausibility** (must reach the hypodermis) and the **weakest evidence** (n=22, unpublished, 14 days). Fat grafting and fillers exist precisely because *getting durable subcutaneous volume change is hard.*
- **The "+30× breast volume" number.** A 30-fold-vs-placebo figure on n=22, unpublished, is a **ratio artifact** (tiny placebo denominator) and a marketing flourish, not a credible effect size. Real, durable breast enlargement from a 0.001%-active cream is not biologically believable.
- **"PGC-1α boosts fat storage."** Oversimplified and arguably backwards relative to mainstream PGC-1α biology (oxidation/browning). That the *same supplier* sells one peptide that raises PGC-1α to *add* fat and another that lowers it to *remove* fat should itself prompt skepticism about how cleanly the axis maps to a topical outcome. [10][11]
- **Borrowed legitimacy from "peptides" and "longevity."** Riding the credibility of well-evidenced matrikine peptides (Matrixyl) and the longevity-science halo, despite a totally different (and far less proven) mechanism and target depth.
- **Name-confusion halo.** Some marketing leans on confusion with Argireline (Acetyl Hexapeptide-8) to imply a "Botox-like," well-known pedigree — but -38 is a different, less-studied molecule. [9][16]
- **Genuinely real part:** Facial fat-compartment deflation *is* a true and underrated driver of looking older [13][14]; PGC-1α/PPARγ *are* real adipogenic regulators [10][11]. The hype is the leap from "real biology in a dish + real anatomy problem" to "this cream fixes it."

## 11. Verdict for the brand

❌→⚠️ **SKIP as a hero; CONDITIONAL/SKIP as supporting.**

**One-line:** Adipo/volumizing peptides are a clever, science-flavored *story* (restore the facial fat that deflates with age) attached to **weak, unpublished, supplier-only human evidence** and a **serious delivery problem** (the active must reach the subcutaneous fat layer, which topicals barely penetrate) — so for an evidence-led skin-longevity brand they are **hard to justify as a hero and risky to claim on at all.**

**Reasoning:**
1. **Evidence quality is the lowest tier we'd accept for a hero** — no independent peer-reviewed human (or even animal) trials; all numbers are manufacturer brochures the manufacturer *itself* says aren't substantiation. The "+30×" breast figure actively erodes credibility.
2. **Delivery is the dealbreaker** — a ~702 Da hydrophilic peptide reaching the hypodermis from a leave-on cream is implausible without microneedle/iontophoresis tech we're not building.
3. **Claims risk is high** — volume/breast/cellulite claims drift into drug territory and are exactly what regulators police.
4. **Mechanism is internally inconsistent** across the supplier's own two peptides and against mainstream PGC-1α biology.

**Where it could earn a *conditional* role:** Adifyline (not Silusyne) *could* be a **minor supporting "story" ingredient** in a face cream **only if** (a) copy is strictly appearance-based and hedged ("helps improve the look of volume"), (b) we never cite the supplier's numbers as proof, and (c) ideally we pair it with a real delivery system and our own tolerability testing. Even then it's a narrative asset, not a proven one. **Silusyne is off-brief** (body/cellulite, not facial anti-aging) and should be set aside for this brand unless we ever pursue body-contouring SKUs.

**Longevity-relevance note:** This is *cosmetic-anatomy* "longevity" (replacing lost fat volume), not *biological* longevity (slowing molecular aging). For a brand staking credibility on real longevity science, leaning on adipo-peptides would **dilute the evidence story** — the facial-fat-deflation *narrative* is compelling and true, but the *topical-peptide solution to it* is not yet real. Better to address visible volume honestly (HA for temporary plumping; acknowledge fillers exist) than to imply a cream rebuilds fat.
