# Ingredient: Coenzyme Q10 / Ubiquinone (+ Ubiquinol) (INCI: Ubiquinone; Ubiquinol)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Antioxidants · **Slug:** coenzyme-q10

> **One-line read:** A genuine endogenous mitochondrial antioxidant with a solid mechanistic story and *real but thin* topical human evidence (a handful of small studies, much of it industry-run). Credible **supporting** antioxidant with a "cellular energy / mitochondrial" narrative — not a hero, and the marketing hype runs well ahead of the published topical data.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common names:** Coenzyme Q10, CoQ10, Q10, ubiquinone, ubidecarenone, ubiquinone-10, "Q-10."
- **INCI:** **Ubiquinone** (oxidized form) and **Ubiquinol** (reduced form). Some derivatives appear under other INCI names (see Idebenone, separate dossier).
- **CAS:** 303-98-0 (ubiquinone). **Formula:** C59H90O4. **Molecular weight: ~863 g/mol** — large. **Highly lipophilic / oil-soluble** (a long isoprenoid tail). [10]
- **Key forms / derivatives:**
  - **Ubiquinone** — oxidized form. The standard, most stable, cheapest; what "CoQ10" usually means in a jar.
  - **Ubiquinol** — reduced form; the body's "active" antioxidant form. More potent in cell studies but **chemically unstable — oxidizes back to ubiquinone on air exposure**, which complicates topical use. [6][8]
  - **Idebenone** — a *synthetic short-chain analog* of CoQ10 (Hydroxydecyl Ubiquinone). Smaller, more soluble, designed to penetrate better and resist pro-oxidant behavior. **Tracked in its own dossier ([`idebenone.md`](idebenone.md)) — do not conflate.**
  - Delivery-engineered grades: water-soluble/solubilized CoQ10 (e.g. "Q10Vital" used orally), nanoemulsions, microemulsions, liposomes/ethosomes, cyclodextrin and phytosome complexes — all attempts to fix penetration/bioavailability.

## 2. Class & mechanism

CoQ10 is a **fat-soluble quinone** that sits in cell membranes (especially the **inner mitochondrial membrane**) and does two related jobs:

1. **Bioenergetics (mitochondrial electron transport).** CoQ10 is an obligatory electron carrier in the respiratory chain (Complexes I/II → III), enabling ATP production. The "energizer" framing: more available CoQ10 → more ATP for energy-hungry repair processes (collagen/elastin synthesis, barrier lipid production). Keratinocyte studies show topical CoQ10 raising cellular oxygen-consumption rate (~38% in Knott 2015: 2.79→3.84 fmol/min/cell), consistent with restored respiration. [1][4]
2. **Lipophilic antioxidant.** In its **reduced (ubiquinol) form** it scavenges reactive oxygen species (ROS), terminates lipid-peroxidation chain reactions in membranes, and **regenerates oxidized vitamin E (and indirectly vitamin C)** back to their active forms — a "network antioxidant." [3][1]

**Downstream skin-relevant effects observed in models:**
- Suppresses **UVA-induced collagenase (MMP-1)** in dermal fibroblasts (via MAPK-pathway dampening) → less ECM breakdown. [1]
- Reduces UVA-driven oxidative DNA damage, thiol depletion, and tyrosine-kinase activation in keratinocytes. [1]
- Reduces keratinocyte cytokine output, lowering MMP induction (inflammaging link). [4][2]
- Protects/stabilizes mitochondrial function against UV. [2]

**Why it matters for "skin longevity":** Skin CoQ10 **declines with age** (roughly across ages 20→66) and is depleted by UV exposure. The mitochondrial-decline-as-aging-hallmark thesis makes "topping up" CoQ10 conceptually attractive. This is the *premise*; whether topical application meaningfully restores it where it counts is the open question (Sections 4–5). [2]

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Relevance | Evidence anchor |
|---|---|---|
| **Mitochondrial decline** | Core mechanism — CoQ10 is literally a respiratory-chain component; skin levels fall with age | Strong mechanistically [1][2][4] |
| **Oxidative stress** | Lipophilic ROS scavenger; regenerates vit E/C; cuts UVA-induced free radicals in skin | Strong mechanistically; shown in human skin [1][5] |
| **Photoaging** | Suppresses UVA-induced MMP-1/collagenase; protects mitochondria & DNA from UV | Moderate (mostly in-vitro + small topical) [1][2] |
| **Collagen/ECM loss** | Reduces collagenase; restores collagen/elastin gene+protein in senescence model; oral RCT showed collagen-synthesis & elastic-fiber benefit | Moderate (cell/oral); weak topical [3][7][9] |
| **Cellular senescence** | Reverted senescent phenotype (β-gal, p21) in CoQ10-deprived fibroblasts (esp. ubiquinol) | Moderate in-vitro only [3] |
| **Inflammaging** | Lowers keratinocyte cytokines → less MMP | Weak–moderate (model) [2][4] |
| **Pigmentation / tone** | Oral RCT showed reduced hyperpigmentation/erythema; brightening otherwise rests on cell-line/zebrafish data | Weak (topical) [5][9] |
| **Hydration / TEWL** | Mixed: one topical "data on file" study reported ↑moisturization, but the main oral RCT (Žmitek 2017) found **no significant effect on skin hydration** | Not established (conflicting) [5][7] |
| Glycation/AGEs | Not a recognized CoQ10 mechanism (this is more an idebenone/carnosine story) | Not established |
| Microbiome | No meaningful evidence | None |

## 4. Evidence review by tier (the core of this dossier)

**Honest framing up front:** CoQ10 has a *large* literature, but most of it is **oral supplementation** (cardiovascular, statin myopathy, fertility, general antioxidant status) and **in-vitro skin** work. The **topical human anti-aging** evidence — the only tier that justifies putting it in a face cream and making appearance claims — is **small, old, and substantially industry-generated** (Beiersdorf/Eucerin/Nivea lineage). Treat the cellular and oral wins as *supportive context*, not proof of topical benefit.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro (cell / biochemical)
- **Hoppe et al., 1999 (BioFactors)** — *Coenzyme Q10, a cutaneous antioxidant and energizer.* In cultured human keratinocytes, CoQ10 reduced UVA-mediated oxidative stress (thiol depletion, phosphotyrosine-kinase activation, oxidative DNA damage) and **significantly suppressed UVA-induced collagenase expression in human dermal fibroblasts.** No cytotoxicity. This is the foundational mechanistic paper. [1]
- **Marcheggiani et al., 2021 (Free Radic Biol Med, 165:282–288)** — Statin-induced CoQ10-deprivation **senescence model in human dermal fibroblasts.** Adding back CoQ10 reverted the senescent phenotype (↓β-gal, ↓p21; ↑collagen I, ↑elastin at gene + protein level). **Ubiquinol was more bioavailable and more effective than ubiquinone** at equal dose. *Limit:* an artificial depletion model in a dish, not aged human skin. [3]
- **Mine et al., 2022** — Cultured fibroblasts: CoQ10 increased proliferation and mRNA for collagen (I/III/VII), elastin, and HSP47. *Limit:* in-vitro. [2]
- **Inui et al., 2008** — In-vitro/in-vivo: CoQ10 decreased keratinocyte cytokine production, reducing MMPs. [2]
- **Prahl et al., 2008** — CoQ10 protected/stabilized mitochondrial function against UV in skin cells. [2]
- **Brightening (depigmentation):** rests on **cell-line and zebrafish** assays only — preliminary at best, per independent appraisal. [8]

### Tier 2 — Animal
- **Ashida et al. (hairless mice)** — Dietary/topical CoQ10 raised epidermal CoQ10 levels. Confirms uptake, not anti-aging efficacy. [2]
- Animal data for CoQ10 *skin anti-aging specifically* is sparse; most rodent CoQ10 work is systemic.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL (supplement)
*(Relevant as "skin can benefit from CoQ10 when it actually reaches the dermis" — but a DIFFERENT delivery route. Does NOT substantiate a topical product.)*
- **Žmitek et al., 2017 (BioFactors, 43(1):132–140)** — RCT, **double-blind, placebo-controlled. n=33** healthy women ~45–60 yrs. 50 mg or 150 mg/day **water-soluble CoQ10 (Q10Vital), 12 weeks.** Both doses **significantly reduced periorbital wrinkles and microrelief lines** and improved skin smoothness/firmness; doses performed similarly. *Limits:* small; oral; manufacturer-affiliated (authors linked to the Q10Vital producer). [7]
- **JAAD 2023 (S0190-9622(23)02166-7)** — RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled. **n=41** women **60–76 yrs**, **100 mg or 200 mg/day, 12 weeks.** Reported benefits (esp. at 200 mg) in facial **erythema, hyperpigmentation, viscoelasticity, epidermal cellular area, collagen synthesis, preservation of elastic fibers**, plus molecular markers (↑Nrf2, ↓p53). No adverse effects. *Limits:* small; conference-abstract-level detail; oral. [9]
- **Žmitek et al., 2020 (Cosmetics; PMC7146335)** — RCT of **water-soluble CoQ10 + collagen** oral combo improving skin parameters. *Limit:* combo product, can't isolate CoQ10. [11]
- *Broader oral literature:* a 2020 systematic review/meta-analysis (PMC7174219) confirms oral CoQ10 measurably **reduces systemic oxidative-stress markers** in RCTs — supports the antioxidant mechanism generally, not skin-topical efficacy. [12]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL
**(a) Published**
- **Hoppe et al., 1999 (BioFactors)** — The classic topical claim. **n=20**, CoQ10 applied around the eyes for **6 months**; reported **~27% reduction in mean wrinkle depth** vs control side, and confirmed CoQ10 **penetrated into viable epidermis** (measured by reduced ultra-weak photon emission = lower oxidation). *Limits:* very small, old, industry (Beiersdorf), single site, methods light by modern standards. This single study underpins most "clinically proven wrinkle reduction" copy you'll see. [1]
- **Knott et al., 2015 (BioFactors, 41(6):383–390)** — **Best controlled topical mechanistic study. In-vivo, n=73** healthy women (20–66 yrs), twice-daily for **2 weeks**, two formulas (348 µM and 870 µM ubiquinone), plus in-vitro keratinocytes. Topical CoQ10 **raised skin-surface and epidermal Q10 levels (ubiquinone AND ubiquinol detected → cellular uptake + conversion)** and, in 16 high-oxidative-stress volunteers, **significantly reduced free radicals** and raised antioxidant capacity (FORT/FORD). Keratinocyte O2-consumption +38%. *Limits:* short (2 wk), endpoints are antioxidant-status/biomarker — **not** clinical wrinkle outcomes; industry-run (all authors Beiersdorf). [4]
- **Split-face 5% CoQ10 vs 0.025% tretinoin (2024, periorbital wrinkles)** — double-blind split-face comparison reporting CoQ10 as a gentler alternative with wrinkle benefit. *Status:* small, recent; full text behind ResearchGate/paywall — **[unverified — abstract-level only]**; do not lean on it for claims until the full RCT is confirmed. [13]
- **Nanoemulsion CoQ10 anti-wrinkle (Pubmed 29096550)** — formulation-optimization study showing improved permeability and anti-wrinkle efficacy. *Limit:* primarily a delivery/formulation paper. [2]

**(b) Unpublished / "data on file"** (cited in the JCAD 2024 review — Beiersdorf internal):
- STDR-071541C: n=30, ↓UVA-induced photon emission + ↑moisturization (1–2 wk).
- STDR-072057C: n=41, ↓periorbital wrinkle roughness/depth (4 wk).
- STDR-077530C: n=36, ↑elasticity / ↓crepey skin on hand (2–4 wk).
- **These are not peer-reviewed or publicly available** — the review authors explicitly flag this as a limitation. Treat as marketing-grade, not evidence-grade. [2]

**Bottom line on tiers — what is actually PROVEN for TOPICAL human use:**
- ✔ Topical CoQ10 **does raise skin Q10 levels and demonstrably lowers oxidative-stress markers in the epidermis** (Knott 2015 — reasonable n, biomarker endpoints). This is the strongest, most defensible claim.
- ◐ **Wrinkle reduction** from topical use rests largely on **one small 1999 study (n=20)** + unpublished internal data + one unverified 2024 split-face study. Real signal, weak evidentiary base.
- ✖ **Brightening, "collagen boosting," firmness, anti-senescence** in *topical human* skin are **borrowed** from oral RCTs, cell models, or zebrafish — **not proven for the topical format.** Do not claim them as topical outcomes.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

This is CoQ10's central weakness and the part marketing ignores.
- **CoQ10 is big (~863 Da) and very lipophilic** — physicochemically poor at crossing the stratum corneum. [10]
- **Tessema et al., 2020 (Skin Pharmacol Physiol; PMC7949203)** — ex-vivo human skin, Franz cells. Vehicle matters enormously: a **hydrophilic cream with a penetration enhancer (2-ethylhexyl laurate) drove >90% penetration with peak accumulation in the *viable epidermis*** (the target), whereas a lecithin microemulsion only ~60% penetrated and over-solubilized the active. **Negligible transdermal (systemic) delivery** in both — it stays in the skin. [5]
- **Depth ceiling:** independent appraisal notes CoQ10 concentrates in the **epidermis (~10× more than dermis)** — so the dermal collagen/ECM benefits seen in *cell* and *oral* studies are exactly the ones a topical may *not* reach well. [8]
- **Practical implication:** efficacy is **formulation-dependent**. Generic "CoQ10 in a heavy occlusive base" may underperform; an engineered vehicle (optimized cream, nano/microemulsion, liposome, cyclodextrin/phytosome complex) is needed to get it into viable epidermis. This is a real formulation cost/competence requirement, not a given.

## 6. Formulation

- **Typical use level:** commonly **~0.3%–1%** in skincare; some studies/products go to **~5%** (split-face study). Knott's effective formulas were ~348–870 µM (~0.03–0.075% w/v) but in optimized vehicles — concentration alone is a poor proxy; **delivery system dominates.** [2][4][13]
- **Solubility:** **oil-soluble** — naturally suits anhydrous oils, balms, oil-in-water emulsions, and the oil phase of creams; water-dispersible/solubilized grades exist for serums.
- **pH:** not pH-driven for activity (unlike L-ascorbic acid); stable across normal cosmetic pH. Tolerant formulation partner.
- **Color/sensory:** ubiquinone is a **bright yellow-orange powder** — it tints products yellow (a recognizable "CoQ10 glow" cue, but a formulation/aesthetic consideration). Heavy oil-phase loading can feel rich/occlusive.
- **Stability:**
  - **Ubiquinone (oxidized) is the stable, formulator-friendly form** — good shelf life, lower cost. Preferred for most topicals. [6]
  - **Ubiquinol (reduced) is unstable — air-oxidizes back to ubiquinone**, so it needs protective packaging/encapsulation or it converts in the jar. Marketing "ubiquinol is the superior active form" is true *in a cell*, but in a tube it largely reverts; **and skin can reduce ubiquinone → ubiquinol in situ** (Knott detected both after applying ubiquinone), so the practical advantage of paying for ubiquinol topically is modest. [4][6][8]
  - A 2021 QC study found notable **variability/degradation of CoQ10 (and vitamins A/E) in commercial anti-aging products** — i.e. label claim ≠ active content. Stability/packaging discipline is a real quality risk. [14]
- **Pairings (synergy):**
  - **Vitamin E (tocopherol):** strong rationale — CoQ10 regenerates oxidized vitamin E; classic membrane-antioxidant pairing.
  - **Vitamin C:** complementary network antioxidant; one 2025 paper formulated a CoQ10 + HA + vitamin C serum. [6]
  - **Niacinamide, ceramides, peptides, hyaluronic acid:** compatible, no known conflict — fits a barrier-repair cream cleanly.
  - **Idebenone:** a *substitute*, not a partner — same family, different molecule.
- **Conflicts:** no notable incompatibilities. Not a sensitizing/irritating active, so it layers easily (including with retinoids/acids) — useful as a "soothing antioxidant" alongside harsher actives.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation:** **Very low.** Well tolerated across skin types including sensitive skin; in Hoppe's work irritancy was "similar to vehicle." [1][2]
- **Sensitization / allergy:** rare; CoQ10 is endogenous (the body makes it), which lowers immunogenic risk. CIR mitigated concern partly on this natural-occurrence basis. [9-CIR]
- **Photosensitivity:** Not a photosensitizer — it's photoprotective in models. Still: **not a sunscreen** and no SPF claim.
- **Pro-oxidant caveat:** quinones *can* in principle cycle to pro-oxidant behavior under some conditions; not flagged as a practical problem for topical CoQ10 at cosmetic levels, but a reason not to overstate "pure protection." (Idebenone was specifically engineered to reduce this risk.)
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** no specific topical contraindication identified; endogenous compound. Use standard "consult your doctor if pregnant" boilerplate — do not assert "safe in pregnancy."
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** Modern cosmetic/supplement CoQ10 is overwhelmingly **yeast-fermentation-derived** (e.g. Kaneka), bio-identical all-trans isomer, GMO-free strains, fewer impurities than older synthetic routes — a reasonable sustainability/clean-sourcing story. [15]

## 8. Regulatory & claims

- **Classification:** **Cosmetic ingredient** (antioxidant / skin conditioning), not a drug. INCI Ubiquinone/Ubiquinol; widely permitted globally.
- **CIR (Cosmetic Ingredient Review):** assessed Ubiquinone ingredients **safe** in present practices of use and concentration; concerns mitigated by natural occurrence, food/supplement history, and negative developmental findings. [9-CIR]
- **Claims line (per CLAUDE.md guardrails):**
  - ✅ **Defensible cosmetic language:** "antioxidant defense / helps protect skin from oxidative and environmental (UV-driven) stress," "supports skin's natural cellular energy," "an antioxidant naturally present in skin that declines with age," "helps improve the appearance of fine lines / smoothness" *(softer wording; the wrinkle data are thin)*, "helps maintain a radiant, even-looking complexion."
  - ⚠️ **Risky / would need substantiation or cross into drug territory:** "reduces wrinkles by 27%" (cites one tiny 1999 study — don't quantify), "boosts collagen," "reverses cellular aging / senescence," "repairs mitochondria," "brightens/fades dark spots" (topical brightening = zebrafish/cell only), "energizes your skin cells" stated as fact, "replaces sunscreen," any structure/function claim about altering the body.
  - **Key compliance trap:** much of the strongest CoQ10 data is **oral**. Do **not** import oral-RCT outcomes (collagen synthesis, elastic-fiber preservation, hyperpigmentation reduction) into copy for a *topical* product. That's an unsubstantiated-claim risk and an honesty risk for an evidence-led brand.
- See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md) claim cheat-sheet.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier: low–moderate.** Plain ubiquinone is an inexpensive, commodity, well-supplied raw material (fermentation-scaled). **Ubiquinol and engineered-delivery grades (nanoemulsion, liposomal, cyclodextrin/phytosome) cost meaningfully more.**
- **Suppliers:** Kaneka (Kaneka Q10 / Kaneka Ubiquinol — the dominant fermentation producer), plus numerous cosmetic-grade suppliers (Gfn-Selco, etc.). Easy to source. [15]
- **The real cost is formulation, not the active:** getting CoQ10 to actually penetrate the viable epidermis (good vehicle + stability + opaque/airless packaging to protect against oxidation) is where the spend and the competence go.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

| Marketing claim you'll see | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Clinically proven to reduce wrinkles" | Rests on **one small 1999 study (n=20)** + unpublished internal data. Real but weak. Don't quantify ("27%"). |
| "Powers your skin cells' energy / recharges mitochondria" | True mechanism *in cells*; topical penetration to where it'd matter (dermis) is limited and the in-vivo bioenergetic benefit is biomarker-level (Knott), not a proven visible outcome. |
| "Ubiquinol — the superior active form" | More potent in a dish, but **unstable** topically (reverts to ubiquinone) and **skin converts ubiquinone → ubiquinol anyway.** The premium is largely a marketing differentiator. |
| "Brightens / fades dark spots" | **Topical** brightening = cell-line + zebrafish only. Not substantiated for human topical use. |
| "Boosts collagen, reverses senescence" | From **cell models and oral RCTs**, not topical human skin. Off-limits for topical copy. |
| "Anti-aging powerhouse / hero antioxidant" | It's a legitimate antioxidant, but the topical human evidence base is **smaller and weaker than vitamin C's** and much of it is industry-run. Supporting, not headline. |

**Net:** CoQ10 is one of the more *over-marketed* antioxidants relative to its published *topical* evidence. The biology is real and the mechanism is sound; the gap is clinical topical proof and penetration to the dermis.

---

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **CONDITIONAL → SUPPORTING.** A credible, well-tolerated, premium-feeling antioxidant with a genuine "cellular energy / mitochondrial" longevity narrative — but **not a hero** (topical human evidence is thin and partly industry-sourced, and penetration to the dermis is limited). **Use it as a supporting antioxidant in a well-engineered vehicle (optimized cream/encapsulated), pair it with vitamin E/C, lead claims with the strongest proven benefit — epidermal antioxidant defense / lowering oxidative stress (Knott 2015) — and keep wrinkle language soft and appearance-based.** Skip it if the alternative is a better-evidenced antioxidant slot (vitamin C is the stronger hero) unless the differentiated "mitochondrial longevity" story is strategically wanted.

**Longevity relevance:** High *thematically* (mitochondrial decline is a bona fide hallmark of aging; CoQ10 declines with age and is depleted by UV), modest in *proven topical payoff*. Strong narrative ingredient for a longevity-positioned line, provided claims stay honest. Compare/contrast with **idebenone** (engineered analog, better penetration, separate dossier) before finalizing the antioxidant lineup.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

_Verified 2026-06-19 against primary sources. Method: for each headline claim, checked (a) whether the evidence is **human + topical** or quietly borrowed from in-vitro/animal/oral routes; (b) whether the cited studies are real and say what is claimed; (c) whether they are industry-funded or under-powered._

### Overall evidence grade: **C (topical), with one B-grade biomarker exception**

- **Topical anti-aging *appearance* outcomes (wrinkles, firmness, brightening, "collagen boost"): C** — weak / indirect. The only topical *clinical wrinkle* outcome rests on a single n=20 Beiersdorf study from 1999 (Hoppe) plus unpublished Beiersdorf "data on file" and one unverified 2024 split-face abstract. That is not a defensible evidentiary base for quantified appearance claims.
- **Topical *mechanistic / biomarker* outcomes (raises epidermal Q10, lowers epidermal oxidative-stress markers): B (borderline B/C)** — Knott 2015 is a genuinely controlled in-vivo study (n=73) with objective biomarker endpoints. It is the one claim that earns moderate human-topical support. Caveat: short (2 weeks), endpoints are antioxidant-status biomarkers (FORT/FORD, surface/epidermal Q10), **not** clinical anti-aging outcomes, and **all authors are Beiersdorf employees**.
- **Collagen synthesis, anti-senescence, elastic-fiber preservation, hyperpigmentation reduction: borrowed.** Real and well-conducted, but the evidence is **oral RCT** (Žmitek 2017 n=33; JAAD 2023 n=41) or **in-vitro** (Marcheggiani 2021 statin-depletion fibroblast model; Mine 2022). None of it is topical-human. The dossier already says this; the grade reflects that these must **not** migrate into topical copy.

**Net:** the *narrative* (mitochondrial decline is a real aging hallmark; skin Q10 falls with age; CoQ10 is a legitimate antioxidant) is sound and well-sourced. ### Main caveats

1. **Industry concentration is even higher than first stated.** Three of the load-bearing topical/secondary sources — Hoppe 1999, Knott 2015, and the JCAD 2024 review — all trace to **Beiersdorf**. An evidence-led brand should assume the topical wrinkle story is, at present, substantially a Beiersdorf-authored story.
2. **No independent, peer-reviewed, adequately-powered topical RCT with clinical anti-aging endpoints exists** as of this check. The strongest *independent* topical work (Tessema) is a penetration study, not an efficacy study.
3. **Oral ≠ topical.** The most impressive outcomes (collagen synthesis, elastic-fiber preservation, dyspigmentation) are all **oral**. Importing them into topical copy is the central compliance/honesty trap.
4. **Hoppe's headline numbers are not abstract-verifiable.** The widely-quoted "27% wrinkle reduction, n=20, 6 months" could not be confirmed from the abstract; do not quantify in any claim.
