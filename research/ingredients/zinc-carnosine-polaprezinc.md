# Ingredient: Carnosine zinc complex (Polaprezinc) (INCI: see §1 — usually "Carnosine" / "Zinc" in cosmetics; "Polaprezinc" is a drug name)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Anti-glycation & related (zinc–dipeptide chelate; carbonyl/AGE scavenger + zinc cofactor)

> **One-line honest summary:** "Polaprezinc" is a genuinely-studied molecule — but it's an **oral gastric-ulcer drug**, and essentially **all** of its human evidence is **oral** (gastric ulcer, oral mucositis, pressure ulcers, taste/zinc deficiency). The interesting *anti-aging* science (anti-glycation, telomere/senescence rescue, carbonyl quenching) belongs to **carnosine the dipeptide**, and that science is overwhelmingly **in-vitro / cell-culture**, with **one ex-vivo human-skin study** (confounded by other actives and run by a cream manufacturer). There is **no human RCT** showing a topical **zinc-carnosine** complex does anything for facial skin aging. Treat the *carnosine* half as a credible **supporting anti-glycation/antioxidant** active; treat the "polaprezinc" framing as mostly **borrowed (oral/drug) authority**.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common names:** Polaprezinc; Zinc L-carnosine; zinc-L-carnosine (ZnC); N-(3-aminopropionyl)-L-histidinato zinc. Trade name in Japan: **Promac®** (Zeria Pharmaceutical). [1][2][11]
- **What it is chemically:** A **1:1 chelate complex of zinc(II) and L-carnosine**, described as a **quadridentate, polymeric** structure — i.e., it is a discrete molecule, *not* a physical blend of zinc + carnosine. By mass it is ~**23% zinc / ~77% L-carnosine**. [1][11]
  - **L-carnosine** = the dipeptide **β-alanyl-L-histidine** (an endogenous dipeptide concentrated in muscle and brain). This is the bioactive partner most relevant to anti-aging chemistry. [3][11]
- **CAS:** 107667-60-7 (polaprezinc); L-carnosine CAS 305-84-0. [1][11]
- **Molecular formula / weight:** Empirical unit ~**C₉H₁₂N₄O₃Zn**, MW ≈ **289.6** (the complex is polymeric, so this is the repeat/empirical unit). L-carnosine: C₉H₁₄N₄O₃, MW 226.23. [1][11]
- **Physical/solubility:** Polaprezinc is a white–yellowish crystalline powder, **almost insoluble in water**, methanol, ethanol, ether (it adheres to and slowly releases at ulcer sites — central to its drug mechanism). [1] L-carnosine alone is highly **water-soluble and hydrophilic**. This solubility split matters a lot for topical formulation (§5–6).
- **INCI / cosmetic naming reality (important):**
  - **"Carnosine"** is an established **INCI** name (skin-conditioning / antioxidant function in EU CosIng / COSMILE). [12] Derivatives in cosmetics include **Acetyl Carnosine** (skin-protecting) and **Decarboxy Carnosine HCl / "Carcinine"** (a non-hydrolyzable, more carnosinase-resistant analogue). [12][17]
  - **"Polaprezinc" / "zinc-L-carnosine" is primarily a *pharmaceutical* identity**, not a routine cosmetic INCI. In a cosmetic you would most plausibly declare the parts (e.g., **Carnosine + a zinc salt**) or use the complex where locally permitted. **Confirm INCI/legality per market before formulating** — see §8. [13][2]
- **Key forms/derivatives relevant to a brand:** L-carnosine (plain), zinc L-carnosine/polaprezinc (the complex), Acetyl Carnosine, **Carcinine/Decarboxy Carnosine HCl** (stability/penetration play), N-acetylcarnosine; plus delivery-enhanced carnosine (liposomes, magnesium-carnosine complex, glycol-enhanced). [9][14][17]

## 2. Class & mechanism

This molecule has **two cooperating actives**, so the mechanism is best read as carnosine-driven + zinc-driven.

**Carnosine (the dipeptide) — the anti-aging chemistry:**
1. **Anti-glycation / carbonyl quenching.** Carnosine's free amino group competitively reacts with reactive dicarbonyls (notably **methylglyoxal**) and other reactive aldehydes (e.g., MDA) to form Schiff bases — **out-competing lysine residues on proteins** so they don't get cross-linked. Net effect in models: less AGE formation (CML, pentosidine), fewer protein carbonyls, **reduced collagen cross-linking** (improved collagen solubility). [4][5][6][7][8]
2. **Antioxidant / metal-chelation.** Scavenges ROS/RNS and chelates transition metals (Zn²⁺, Cu²⁺); chelation to zinc/copper **enhances** its superoxide-scavenging activity. [3][6]
3. **Senescence / telomere effects (cell models).** Carnosine retards replicative senescence of cultured human fibroblasts and can shift late-passage cells back toward a "juvenile" morphology; it slows telomere shortening and telomeric DNA damage. [18][19][20]
4. **Senescent-cell clearance (newer mechanism).** Carnosine boosts macrophage phagocytosis of senescent skin cells via **AKT2 → CD36/RAGE** (an in-vitro "senolytic-assist" idea). [21]

**Zinc (the cofactor) — supportive skin biology:**
5. **Enzyme cofactor for repair/proliferation**, antioxidant defense (SOD), and modulation of **MMPs** (MMPs are zinc-dependent — a double-edged point: zinc is *structurally* required by the very collagen-degrading enzymes, so the benefit is about zinc *status/signaling*, not blocking MMPs). [22]
6. **Anti-inflammatory:** the complex suppresses NF-κB and pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6) and upregulates SOD-1/2, HO-1, VEGF in wound/mucosa models. [2][10]
7. **Barrier/tight-junction protection & pro-migratory wound effects** (shown in GI mucosa and cell models). [2]

**Honest translation to *facial skin*:** Items 1–2 are solid, generalizable chemistry and the strongest reason to consider carnosine. Items 3–4 are real but **in-vitro**. Items 5–7 are genuine for **GI mucosa / wounds / oral mucositis**, and it is **not established** that a topical cosmetic dose reproduces them in living facial skin.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** Polaprezinc is an **approved oral drug** (gastric ulcer; Japan/Korea). Its **human** evidence base is **oral** — gastric ulcer, oral mucositis (a mucosal "topical" rinse/lozenge, *not* facial skin), pressure ulcers (oral), taste disorders, zinc deficiency. The **skin-aging** mechanisms come from **carnosine** and are mostly **in-vitro**. The closest thing to topical human-skin data is **one ex-vivo skin-explant study of a carnosine *cream*** that also contained niacinamide + exopolysaccharides and was run by the manufacturer. **No RCT** tests a topical zinc-carnosine complex for facial aging.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell & biochemical)

- **Shao, Li, Tan (2004), *Biochem Biophys Res Commun* (PMID 15474517).** Human fetal-lung fibroblasts grown continuously in **20 mM carnosine** showed **slower telomere shortening**, less telomeric DNA damage, and **extended lifespan in population doublings**. *Limitation: very high culture concentration; cell line; not skin-in-vivo.* [20]
- **McFarland & Holliday (1994), *Exp Cell Res*; and McFarland & Holliday (1999), *Exp Gerontol* 34(1):35–45 (PMID 10197726).** Carnosine (up to ~50 mM) retards senescence and **reverts late-passage human fibroblasts (HFF-1, MRC-5) to a more juvenile morphology**; phenotype switches back when carnosine is removed. *Limitation: classic but high-dose culture work; reversibility implies it's a maintenance effect, not a permanent "rejuvenation."* [18][19]
- **Li et al. (2020), *Front Pharmacol* 11:593832.** Carnosine (10–30 mM) drives **macrophage clearance of senescent HaCaT/HFF-1 cells via AKT2→CD36/RAGE** in co-culture. *Limitation: in vitro only; authors note in-vivo relevance unproven; 3 independent experiments.* [21]
- **Carnosine + AGEs — systematic review: Hipkiss-adjacent literature synthesized by the 2018 *Amino Acids* review (PMID 29858687).** 36 studies (**19 in vitro, 15 animal, only 2 human**); **all but two** showed carnosine **prevents AGE formation** (carbonyls, sugar-induced cross-links); authors stress **few human studies** and unclear precise mechanism. [4]
- **Zinc vs AGEs — Kheirouri, Alizadeh, Maleki (2018), *Clin Exp Pharmacol Physiol* (PMID 29220086).** Review of 5 studies: zinc shows **anti-glycation, anti-oxidative, anti-apoptotic** effects; zinc *insufficiency* promotes AGE formation. *Supports the "zinc half" of the rationale; not skin-specific, not topical.* [22]
- **Cripps et al. (2017), *Sci Rep* — carnosine mechanism on methylglyoxal cross-linking** (and broader carbonyl-quenching literature): carnosine markedly reduces MGO-driven protein-protein cross-linking. [5][8]
- **Caruso et al. (2022), *Antioxidants* (PMC8836079).** Proteomic study: carnosine modulates oxidative stress in a **scaffold-free human dermis spheroid** model. *3D human-cell model — a step above monolayer, still not in-vivo.* [23]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)

- **Senescence-accelerated mice (SAM) & rodent glycation models.** Carnosine delays features of aging and **lowers urinary AGEs / protein carbonylation** and improves **collagen solubility** (less cross-linking) with oral dosing. *Animal, oral; supports systemic anti-glycation, not topical skin.* [4][5]
- **GI/wound models for polaprezinc** (gastric ulcer, intestinal injury): the complex accelerates mucosal repair, scavenges radicals, ↓inflammatory cytokines. *Animal; GI mucosa, not facial skin.* [2]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL (where the real human data live — and it's not facial skin)

- **Gastric ulcer / *H. pylori* (the approved indication).** Multiple RCTs + meta-analyses: adding polaprezinc to triple therapy improves *H. pylori* eradication and ulcer healing. **150 mg/day** is the standard dose (≈34 mg zinc + 116 mg carnosine). *Oral; GI — establishes safety & the drug's pedigree, not skin claims.* [2][25]
- **Oral mucositis (head & neck radiotherapy / chemo / pediatric HSCT).** Polaprezinc suspension/lozenge **reduces severity of radiation- and chemo-induced oral mucositis**; mechanism tied to zinc + radical scavenging + ↓TNF-α/IL-1β/IL-6. *This is "topical" only in the sense of an oral-mucosa rinse — it is mucosa, not skin, and the format is a mouthwash/lozenge.* [10][26]
- **Pressure ulcers — Sakae et al. (2013), *Nutr Clin Pract* 28(5):609–616.** **Non-randomized** controlled trial, **n = 42** long-term-care patients (stage II–IV ulcers ≥4 wk): control (n=14, untreated), **oral** polaprezinc 150 mg/d (n=10), **oral** carnosine 116 mg/d (n=18). At 4 weeks, **PUSH** healing was significantly better in both active groups vs control; PLZ ≈ CAR (no significant difference). *Oral; non-randomized; small; wounds in compromised patients, not cosmetic aging.* [27]
- **Pressure ulcers — open-label 8-week trial (Biol Trace Elem Res, 2014).** Oral polaprezinc; supportive of healing. *Open-label, oral.* [27b]
- **Taste disorders & zinc deficiency.** Dose–response meta-analysis (IPD) shows polaprezinc improves zinc status and zinc-deficiency endpoints. *Oral.* [28]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier a skincare brand needs — essentially absent for the *complex*; thin for carnosine)

- **Narda, Peño-Mazzarino, Krutmann, Trullas, Granger (2018), *Skin Pharmacol Physiol* 31(6):324–331 — the headline "topical" study, and it's carnosine, not polaprezinc.** **Ex-vivo human skin explants** from **a single 41-y-old donor** (abdominoplasty); 3 explants/condition; topical **0.2% carnosine** as aqueous solution vs a **finished facial cream (FC-CARN)** also containing **niacinamide + bacterial exopolysaccharides**, applied days 0/2/4/6 over 9 days. By immunostaining, the **cream reduced CML and pentosidine** AGE staining in epidermis and dermis. *Critical caveats: (a) the active was a **multi-ingredient cream**, so benefit can't be attributed to carnosine alone (niacinamide is itself relevant); (b) **n = 1 donor**, ex-vivo explant (not living, perfused skin); (c) **manufacturer-conducted/funded** — three authors are ISDIN employees, one is an ISDIN advisor; (d) AGE staining is a surrogate, not a clinical wrinkle/elasticity outcome.* This is **the** most-cited "carnosine works on human skin" reference — and it does **not** validate a stand-alone carnosine (or zinc-carnosine) topical claim. [7]
- **Carnosine-loaded delivery systems vs UVB (2025), *Int J Pharm* / related.** Carnosine in **hya-ascorposomes**, liposomes, or magnesium complexes showed anti-photoaging/antioxidant effects **in vitro and in animal skin** — but these test **engineered carriers**, not plain carnosine, and aren't human RCTs. [9][14][15]
- **Polaprezinc topical for facial skin aging:** **none found.** Polaprezinc topical use in humans = **oral-mucosa mucositis rinses** and **wound dressings inspired by polaprezinc** (research-stage nanofibers), not cosmetic facial products. [2][16]

**Bottom line by tier:** Proven (human, but oral) → gastric ulcer, oral mucositis, pressure ulcers, taste/zinc deficiency. Proven (in vitro) → carnosine's anti-glycation, antioxidant, anti-senescence, telomere effects. **Not proven** → any topical facial-skin anti-aging benefit of zinc-carnosine/polaprezinc in a human RCT. The one ex-vivo human-skin signal is for a **carnosine-containing cream** (multi-active, n=1 donor, manufacturer-run).

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Carnosine alone penetrates poorly.** It is small but **hydrophilic and charged**, so the lipophilic stratum corneum resists it; it is also a substrate for **carnosinases (CNDP1/CNDP2)** that can degrade it. Plain aqueous carnosine reaches viable skin layers inefficiently. [14][17]
- **Penetration *can* be engineered.** Goebel, Schmaus, Neubert, Wohlrab (2012), *Skin Pharmacol Physiol* 25(6):281: adding **1,2-pentylene glycol** raised carnosine in the stratum corneum and viable layers **~6-fold** vs no enhancer; a microemulsion did *not* outperform the simple glycol-enhanced base. **Magnesium-carnosine complexation** and **liposomal carnosine** likewise increase delivery to lower skin layers. [14][15]
- **The polaprezinc complex is *less* topical-friendly than carnosine.** Polaprezinc is **near-insoluble in water** (that insolubility is the basis of its GI mucosal-adhesion drug action) — which is a liability, not an asset, for a clear/elegant facial formula. To get carnosine + zinc benefits topically you would more realistically formulate **carnosine (with penetration help) + a soluble zinc source**, rather than the polymeric drug complex.
- **Practical read:** Topical activity is *plausible but unproven in humans*; if pursued, it **requires a delivery strategy** (glycol enhancer, liposome, Mg-complex, or the carcinine derivative) and honest, hedged claims. No published data quantify how much intact zinc-carnosine reaches living human dermis.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** **No validated topical efficacy concentration in humans.** Cosmetic carnosine is typically used at **~0.05–0.5%** (the one ex-vivo skin study used **0.2%**). In-vitro actives were **10–50 mM** — *culture* doses, not formulation guidance; do not imply a proven dose-response. [7][18][20]
- **pH:** Carnosine is generally formulated around **pH ~6.0–7.5** (some patents use alkaline vehicles up to pH 8–9). [Note: this is *above* the skin's acid mantle and conflicts with low-pH actives.] The polaprezinc complex's stability is pH-dependent and the chelate can dissociate at low pH (releasing zinc + carnosine). Buffer thoughtfully. [Formulation sources, including 17][1]
- **Stability:** Carnosine is **carnosinase- and oxidation-sensitive**; reactive amines can participate in Maillard-type discoloration with reducing sugars/aldehydes. **Carcinine (Decarboxy Carnosine HCl)** is the industry's stability/penetration workaround. Zinc salts can interact with anionic systems and chelators. Run stability + challenge testing; watch color/odor. [17]
- **Pairings (conceptual):** **Niacinamide** (barrier/tone; co-used in the one human ex-vivo study), **other anti-glycation actives** (alistin/blueberry, l-carnosine's cousins), **antioxidants** (vitamin C/E — but mind pH and metal interactions), **ceramides** (barrier thesis). Anti-glycation makes a clean conceptual pair with NAD/sirtuin and barrier stories.
- **Conflicts:** Avoid raw co-formulation with **strong low-pH actives** (L-ascorbic acid, AHAs) that can dissociate the complex / sit far from carnosine's preferred pH; watch **chelator/zinc** and **anionic** interactions; metals can catalyze oxidation of sensitive partners.
- **Sensory:** At low use levels, carnosine is largely odor/color-neutral in solution; the **insoluble polaprezinc complex** can cause haze/grittiness — favors carnosine + soluble zinc, or an encapsulated form, for elegant texture.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation/sensitization:** Carnosine in cosmetics is generally **well tolerated**, with dermatological testing typically negative for irritation/sensitization at cosmetic levels; it is endogenous to the body. No specific topical sensitization signal found for the zinc-carnosine complex — but **generate our own patch-test/HRIPT data** on the finished formula. [12][17]
- **Sun sensitivity:** No photosensitivity flag; mechanistically anti-glycant/antioxidant actives are not phototoxic. No human topical photo-safety data specific to the complex. [9]
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** **Oral polaprezinc** is a *drug* and oral zinc has an upper limit; oral data don't transfer cleanly to a low-dose cosmetic. No topical pregnancy data → advise conservative copy ("consult your doctor") and keep zinc contribution low.
- **Zinc / copper balance:** The relevant safety nuance for the **oral drug** is that **chronic high zinc can cause copper deficiency** (seen at higher oral doses; 300 mg/day gave more adverse events without added benefit). For a **topical** cosmetic at fractional-percent levels this systemic risk is negligible, but it's a reason **not** to chase high zinc loads. [11][28]
- **Cancer-patient context:** Polaprezinc is *used* (orally) to *reduce* radiotherapy mucositis, so there's no oncology contraindication for that use — but we are not making medical claims; keep cosmetic positioning clearly cosmetic. [10][26]
- **Sourcing/allergen:** Carnosine is usually **synthetic/fermentation-derived** (β-alanine + L-histidine) → low allergen profile; confirm vegan/non-animal source on COA (carnosine is named for *Carnis*/meat but commercial cosmetic grade is synthetic). Specify heavy-metal-tested zinc.
- **General tolerability (oral, as context):** Decades of oral use at 150 mg/day with a good safety record. [2][11][25]

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **The core regulatory trap: "Polaprezinc" is a DRUG identity.** In Japan/Korea it is a **prescription anti-ulcer drug**; elsewhere the *complex* is sold as a **dietary supplement / natural health product** (US NDI, AU complementary medicine, CA NHP). [11] Marketing a topical product as containing "Polaprezinc" risks importing **drug** associations and inviting a structure/function reading. **Prefer the cosmetic INCI "Carnosine" (± a declared zinc salt)** and verify the chosen identity is permitted as a cosmetic in each target market. [12][13]
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** "Heals," "repairs ulcers/wounds," "anti-glycation *treatment*," "reverses cellular aging," "boosts collagen," "clears senescent cells" all flirt with **structure/function (drug) claims**. The big trap here is importing the **wound-healing / ulcer / mucositis (medical, oral)** literature into a **topical cosmetic** story.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "antioxidant," "helps protect against environmental stress," "supports skin's defense against glycation (sugar-related dullness/'glycation glow loss')," "helps maintain the look of firmness and even tone," "skin-conditioning." Even the anti-glycation wording should be hedged ("helps defend against") given only ex-vivo human data.
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation or legal review:** "anti-glycation that erases AGEs," "rejuvenates/reverses aging at the cellular level," "lengthens telomeres," "removes senescent cells," "heals/repairs skin," "boosts collagen by X%." None are supported for **topical human facial** use.
- **Substantiation reality:** To make even modest topical performance claims we'd want our **own finished-formula human testing**; the literature substantiates **oral** medical uses and **in-vitro** mechanisms, not a topical anti-aging cream. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Forms/suppliers:** **L-carnosine** (cosmetic-grade) is widely available from peptide/fine-chemical suppliers (e.g., Creative Peptides, Youchang, plus general suppliers); **polaprezinc/zinc-L-carnosine** powder is sold by chemical houses (Haihang, Stanford Chemicals, GlpBio, Medichem, MedChemExpress) largely for pharma/supplement use. **Carcinine/Decarboxy Carnosine HCl** and **Acetyl Carnosine** are the cosmetically-optimized derivatives. [13][17]
- **Cost tier:** **Low–mid** for plain L-carnosine (commodity dipeptide); **mid** for the zinc complex and for stability/penetration-enhanced derivatives (carcinine, liposomal, Mg-complex).
- **Supply/standardization:** Specify **synthetic/fermentation** origin (vegan, low allergen), heavy-metal-tested zinc, and require COA for identity/purity. If using the **complex**, plan for its **water-insolubility** in formulation (encapsulation or in-situ carnosine + soluble zinc may be more practical).

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **The "drug halo."** "Polaprezinc" sounds clinical and proven — because it **is**, but as an **oral gastric-ulcer drug**. That credibility does **not** transfer to a topical anti-aging cream. This is the central marketing trap.
- **Format and tissue switch.** Human data are **oral** and largely **GI / oral-mucosa / wound** — not facial skin. "It heals ulcers" ≠ "it rejuvenates your face."
- **"Anti-glycation hero."** Carnosine's anti-glycation chemistry is real and well-cited — but ~**19 in-vitro to 2 human** studies in the systematic review, and the lone human-skin study is **ex-vivo, n=1 donor, multi-ingredient cream, manufacturer-run**. [4][7]
- **"Lengthens telomeres / reverses cellular aging."** From **20–50 mM** carnosine in cell culture; effects are **reversible** on removal (maintenance, not permanent rejuvenation), and there's no evidence a topical dose achieves intracellular concentrations remotely near those. [18][19][20]
- **"Clears senescent cells (senolytic)."** One **in-vitro** co-culture mechanism; authors themselves flag in-vivo relevance as unknown. [21]
- **Penetration glossed over.** Plain carnosine penetrates poorly and is carnosinase-degraded; the **insoluble polaprezinc complex** is *worse* for elegant topical delivery — yet copy rarely mentions delivery is the bottleneck. [14][17]

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL — use *carnosine* (delivery-optimized) as a credible anti-glycation supporting active; do NOT lean on the "Polaprezinc drug" framing.**

**One-line:** The genuinely useful asset here is **carnosine** — a real, well-studied **anti-glycation + antioxidant + anti-senescence** molecule with strong *in-vitro/oral* science and a clean longevity narrative — but its **topical human facial evidence is essentially one confounded ex-vivo study**, and the "zinc-carnosine/polaprezinc" identity mainly imports **oral-drug** authority that doesn't apply to skin. Adopt **carnosine (or carcinine) with a real delivery system** as a **supporting** anti-glycation active under honest, hedged copy; treat a *topical zinc-carnosine complex* specifically as **conditional** (formulation-hostile because it's water-insoluble, and no human topical evidence) unless we run our own testing.

**Longevity-relevance note:** Anti-glycation is a legitimately under-served, **on-thesis** longevity axis (AGEs stiffen and dull skin with age), and carnosine is one of the most-cited anti-glycation/antioxidant dipeptides — making it an excellent **education/differentiation** ingredient for a skin-*longevity* brand. The honest gaps are **(1) format/tissue** (human data are oral/mucosal/wound, not topical facial), **(2) delivery** (penetration must be engineered), and **(3) attribution** (the human-skin signal came from a multi-active cream). If we want real topical claims, the path is **carnosine in a proven delivery vehicle + our own finished-formula human study**, not the polaprezinc drug literature.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Independent check of the headline claims against primary sources (titles, study design, doses, tissue, funding). This dossier was written in an already-skeptical voice; verification largely **confirms** its honesty rather than overturning it.

### Overall grade: **C (weak / indirect for the topical anti-aging use case)**

- For the *advertised use* — a **topical facial anti-aging** active — the evidence is **C**: mechanistically plausible, well-cited in vitro, but with **no human RCT**, and the single human-skin signal is an ex-vivo, n=1-donor, multi-active, manufacturer-run study.
- For *carnosine's general anti-glycation chemistry* in isolation, the in-vitro/animal base is genuinely strong (would be B-grade), but that is **not** topical-facial efficacy.
- For *polaprezinc as an oral drug* (gastric ulcer, oral mucositis, zinc deficiency) the human evidence is **A/B** — but it is the **wrong tissue and route** for this brand's claim, so it does not lift the topical grade. The dossier already states this; the grade reflects the use case, not the molecule's overall pedigree.

### What checked out (claim → verification)

- **Narda et al. 2018 (the lone "human skin" reference) — VERIFIED, exactly as the dossier caveats it.** Confirmed: **single donor, 41-y-old** Caucasian woman (abdominoplasty); **3 explants per condition** (triplicate); **0.2% carnosine** as AQ-CARN solution and as a finished cream **FC-CARN that also contained niacinamide and Alteromonas Ferment Extract (bacterial exopolysaccharides)**; outcome was **CML/pentosidine immunostaining** (a surrogate, not clinical wrinkle/elasticity); disclosure states authors MN, CT, CG are **ISDIN employees** (manufacturer of the tested cream) and JK is an **ISDIN scientific advisor**. Every confounder the dossier flags is real. [7]
- **Ghodsi & Kheirouri 2018 systematic review — VERIFIED.** Real paper (Amino Acids 50:1177–1186); the exact tally **36 studies = 19 in vitro + 15 animal + 2 human**, "all but two show carnosine prevents AGE formation," and the authors' own "few human studies" caveat are accurate. [4]
- **Shao, Li, Tan 2004 (telomere) — VERIFIED.** Real (BBRC, PMID 15474517); **20 mM** carnosine in **human fetal-lung fibroblasts**; slower telomere shortening + extended population doublings. (Corrected a stray author typo in this dossier: the Tier-1 line had read "Shao, Li, **Cai**"; the correct third author is **Tan**, matching source [20].)
- **McFarland & Holliday 1994/1999 (senescence/rejuvenation) — VERIFIED.** Real; **~20–50 mM** carnosine; HFF-1/MRC-5; juvenile↔senescent phenotype **reverses when carnosine is removed** — i.e., maintenance, not permanent rejuvenation, as the dossier honestly notes. [18][19]
- **Li et al. 2020 (senolytic-assist) — VERIFIED.** Real (Front Pharmacol 11:593832); **in-vitro co-culture only**, THP-1 macrophages + tBHP-senesced HaCaT/HFF-1; AKT2→CD36/RAGE. In-vivo relevance unproven. [21]
- **Goebel et al. 2012 (penetration) — VERIFIED.** Real (Skin Pharmacol Physiol 25(6):281); hydrophilic carnosine penetrates poorly; 1,2-pentylene glycol enhances delivery; microemulsion did not outperform the glycol base. [14]
- **Sakae et al. 2013 (pressure ulcers) — VERIFIED.** Real; **non-randomized**, **n=42**, **oral** 150 mg/d PLZ (116 mg carnosine + 34 mg zinc) vs oral carnosine vs untreated control; PUSH score; 4-week. Oral and in compromised patients — not cosmetic facial aging. [27]
- **Hewlings & Kalman 2020 (mechanism/oral) — VERIFIED.** Real (Nutrients 12(3):665); covers oral gastric/mucositis/taste uses and 150 mg/day dosing. Note it does mention "enhancing skin," but in the context of **oral/systemic** supplementation, not topical — consistent with this dossier's framing. [2]

**No fabricated or misrepresented citations were found.** Every spot-checked source is real and says what the dossier claims.

### Main caveats (the load-bearing honesty points, restated)

1. **Tissue/route mismatch is the whole ballgame.** All *human* evidence is **oral** and mostly **GI / oral-mucosa / wound** — none is topical facial skin. "Polaprezinc" is an **oral drug** identity; its clinical credibility does not transfer to a cream.
2. **The one human-skin study cannot carry a stand-alone claim:** ex-vivo explant, **n=1 donor**, **multi-active cream** (niacinamide + exopolysaccharides confound attribution), **manufacturer-conducted**, surrogate (AGE-staining) endpoint.
3. **Cell-culture doses (20–50 mM) are not formulation guidance** and there is no evidence a topical dose reaches comparable intracellular levels; senescence effects are **reversible** (maintenance, not rejuvenation).
4. **Delivery is an unsolved bottleneck** for plain carnosine, and **worse** for the water-insoluble polaprezinc complex.
5. **Anti-glycation as a *topical* benefit is itself only ex-vivo-supported** — even the hedged "helps defend against glycation" wording rests on that single confounded study.

### Claims to soften / keep softened

The dossier's own claim language (§8, §10–11) is already appropriately hedged and **defensible as written** — keep it. The verification confirms these must **stay** softened and never be upgraded without our own finished-formula human data:

- Do **not** say or imply topical carnosine/zinc-carnosine is "**clinically proven**," "**proven anti-glycation**," lengthens telomeres, clears senescent cells, reverses cellular aging, heals/repairs skin, or boosts collagen — none is supported for **topical human facial** use.
- Keep anti-glycation copy at "**helps defend against**"-level hedging (ex-vivo human data only).
- Do **not** import the **polaprezinc / oral-drug / wound-healing** evidence into topical cosmetic copy.
