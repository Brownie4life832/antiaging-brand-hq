# Ingredient: Bio-placenta GF complex (recombinant) — multi-growth-factor "placenta-mimetic" blend (EGF + IGF-1 + aFGF + bFGF + VEGF, liposomal)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⬜ considering / ☑ shortlisted (lean conditional-supporting) / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Growth factors, cytokines, exosomes, conditioned media & regenerative · **Slug:** bio-placenta-gf-complex

> **One-line bottom line:** "Bio-placenta" is a **marketing wrapper around a defined cocktail of five recombinant growth factors** (EGF, IGF-1, acidic FGF, basic FGF, VEGF) in a lecithin-liposome base — the flagship being **LABIO's BIO-Placenta** (Korea). It has a *cleaner provenance story* than human-cell secretomes (no human/animal tissue, no exosome gray-zone) and an *honest, biomimetic* framing, but it **inherits every weakness of the parent growth-factor category**: a hard ~500-Da penetration ceiling (these proteins are ~6–18 kDa, 12–36× over the limit), serious protein-stability problems, **zero independent peer-reviewed RCTs on the actual complex** (only supplier "clinically-studied" claims), and a genuinely awkward **longevity contradiction** (IGF-1 is *the* lifespan-shortening pathway). Defensible as a premium **supporting** actor in a well-encapsulated, claims-disciplined formula; **not a hero, and not a clean longevity story.** **Lean conditional-supporting → skip-able.**

---

## 1. Identity

**Common name(s):** Bio-placenta · BIO-Placenta (LABIO trade name) · "biomimetic placenta" · "vegan/synthetic placenta" · "growth-factor placenta complex." Marketed as a cruelty-free, tissue-free stand-in for traditional **animal/human placenta extract** (a separate, older ingredient — see contrast in §10).

**What it actually is — a *defined* recombinant GF cocktail, not a tissue extract.** Unlike historical placenta extracts (undefined hydrolysates of real placental tissue) and unlike conditioned-media "secretomes" (undefined cell-culture cocktails), "bio-placenta" is a **named, finite list of five recombinant human-sequence growth factors** plus a few support actives, encapsulated in lecithin liposomes. This makes it the **most chemically-defined member** of the regenerative class — a real point in its favor for an honesty-first brand.

**Representative full INCI (LABIO BIO-Placenta, per supplier/UL Prospector & retailer listings):**
> Water, Lecithin, Acetyl Glutamine, **sh-Oligopeptide-1**, **sh-Oligopeptide-2**, **sh-Polypeptide-1**, **sh-Polypeptide-9**, **sh-Polypeptide-11**, Bacillus/Folic Acid Ferment Extract, Sodium Hyaluronate, Caprylyl Glycol, Butylene Glycol, 1,2-Hexanediol. [8][9]

**The five growth factors and their INCI mapping** (verified against INCIDecoder, EWG Skin Deep, supplier docs):

| INCI | Growth factor | Approx. MW | Primary skin role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **sh-Oligopeptide-1** | **EGF** (epidermal growth factor) | ~6.0 kDa (53 aa) | Keratinocyte proliferation/migration, re-epithelialization [7][10] |
| **sh-Oligopeptide-2** | **IGF-1** (insulin-like growth factor 1) | ~7.6 kDa (70 aa) | Cell proliferation/survival; collagen support [11] |
| **sh-Polypeptide-1** | **bFGF / FGF-2** (basic fibroblast GF) | ~17–18 kDa | Fibroblast proliferation, collagen/ECM synthesis [12] |
| **sh-Polypeptide-9** | **VEGF / VEGF-A** (vascular endothelial GF) | ~38–45 kDa (dimer) | Angiogenesis; nutrient supply to fibroblasts [13][6] |
| **sh-Polypeptide-11** | **aFGF / FGF-1** (acidic fibroblast GF) | ~16–18 kDa | Fibroblast/keratinocyte mitogen; collagen/elastin [14] |

> ⚠️ **INCI mapping caveat — verify per supplier.** Some marketing pages **swap sh-Polypeptide-1 and -11** (calling -1 "aFGF" and -11 "bFGF"). The registry-backed mapping is **sh-Polypeptide-1 = FGF-2 (basic)** and **sh-Polypeptide-11 = FGF-1 (acidic)** [12][14]. Either way the *category* (two FGFs, one acidic + one basic) is the same; confirm the exact identity in any CoA before quoting.

**Support actives in the LABIO complex:**
- **Acetyl Glutamine** — acetylated amino-acid derivative; humectant / claimed cell-energy support.
- **Bacillus/Folic Acid Ferment Extract** — "fermented vitamin B9" (folate via *Bacillus* fermentation); positioned as a cofactor for cell renewal. Evidence for topical folate as an anti-aging active is thin.
- **Sodium hyaluronate** — humectant.
- **Lecithin** — forms the **liposome shell** (the delivery system) and is itself a barrier lipid/emollient.

**Production route:** the GFs are **recombinant**, expressed by **microbial fermentation** (typically *E. coli* or yeast) from synthesized copies of the human genes — *not* extracted from placenta, human, or animal tissue [14][7]. "sh" = "synthetic human" (the amino-acid sequence mirrors the human protein).

**Key forms/derivatives seen on the market:** LABIO BIO-Placenta is the dominant supplier active; finished products built on it include Skin Moderne "Bio-placenta," Qyral BioPlacenta, and (in India) Minimalist's "Multi-Peptide Serum 7% Matrixyl 3000 + 3% Bio-Placenta." Some finished "bio-placenta" SKUs add extra actives (copper/zinc gluconate, hydrolyzed elastin, **rye phytoplacenta extract**, MSM, peptides) — so a finished "bio-placenta" label is **not** standardized; read the full INCI. [9][8]

## 2. Class & mechanism

Bio-placenta sits in the **growth-factor / regenerative** class. Each component is an **endogenous signaling protein** that binds a specific cell-surface **receptor tyrosine kinase** (EGFR for EGF; FGFR1–4 for the FGFs; IGF-1R for IGF-1; VEGFR for VEGF), triggering **RAS–RAF–MEK–MAPK/ERK** and **PI3K–AKT** cascades. The net instruction to skin cells: *proliferate, migrate, survive, and lay down extracellular matrix.* The "bio-placenta" conceit is that fetal/placental tissue is rich in exactly these renewal signals, so a blend mimicking that signaling milieu should push aged skin toward a younger, repair-oriented phenotype.

Component logic:
- **EGF → EGFR:** keratinocyte proliferation/migration, re-epithelialization, epidermal turnover.
- **bFGF + aFGF → FGFR:** dermal fibroblast proliferation; induce **type I/III collagen** and elastin — the matrix that governs firmness/elasticity and declines with age [12][14].
- **IGF-1 → IGF-1R:** fibroblast/keratinocyte survival and proliferation; in the dermis, IGF-1 from fibroblasts also primes the *keratinocyte UV response* (see §7 — this is the safety double-edge) [11][22].
- **VEGF → VEGFR:** angiogenesis; the rationale is improved micro-perfusion / nutrient delivery to fibroblasts. (Most cosmetic VEGF data are about *hair-follicle* angiogenesis, not facial wrinkles — see §4.) [6][13]

**The aging logic + the leap.** Aged/photodamaged skin has fewer, less-active fibroblasts, fragmented collagen, and blunted GF signaling; topically resupplying these signals is meant to "re-instruct" repair. This is biologically coherent **in cell culture and in wounds.** The leap to *intact, un-wounded human skin via a leave-on cosmetic* is exactly where the evidence thins (see §4–5). The liposome is the supplier's attempt to bridge that leap.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Plausibility | Evidence in TOPICAL human use (this complex) |
|---|---|---|
| **Collagen / ECM loss** | High (mechanistic, via FGFs/IGF-1) | Weak–moderate — in-vitro collagen induction + category serum data; none on this complex specifically |
| **Photoaging** (lines, texture, tone) | Moderate | Weak–moderate — best-supported endpoint *for the GF category* (Quinlan 2023 [1]); no independent trial on bio-placenta |
| **Barrier decline / hydration** | Moderate | Weak–moderate — driven mostly by the **lecithin + HA + acetyl glutamine** carriers, not the GFs |
| **Pigmentation / tone** | Indirect | Weak — within multi-GF serums, confounded by co-ingredients |
| **Cellular senescence** | Theoretical | Weak — borrowed from one industry secretome study (H2A.J) [5]; nothing on bio-placenta |
| **Inflammaging / cell-cell communication** | Theoretical | Weak — gene-expression only (category) |
| **Mitochondrial decline** | Low | None (acetyl glutamine "cell energy" is marketing, not demonstrated) |
| **Oxidative stress** | Low (not their lane) | Minimal |
| **Glycation / AGEs** | Not a mechanism | None |
| **Microbiome** | Not a mechanism | None (the *Bacillus* ferment is a folate source, not a postbiotic claim) |

**Honest read:** bio-placenta is an **"ECM / proliferation / repair" story**, not an antioxidant, anti-glycation, mitochondrial, or microbiome story. Its real-world hydration/barrier "feel" likely owes as much to the **liposomal lecithin + hyaluronate** carriers as to the growth factors themselves — a meaningful attribution problem for claims.

## 4. Evidence review by tier — the core of this dossier

> **Reading guide (critical).** There is, as of mid-2026, **no independent, peer-reviewed, vehicle-controlled human RCT on the "bio-placenta" complex itself.** Supplier/retailer pages say it is "clinically studied" and cite ~2–4-week wrinkle/hydration/elasticity gains, but **no published trial, sample size, or protocol is provided** — treat these as unsubstantiated marketing until a primary source surfaces [8][20]. Everything below is therefore **borrowed**: from the broader recombinant-GF literature (the strongest *human-topical* evidence), and from in-vitro/animal/wound data on the individual factors. The gap between "these factors do X in a dish / in a wound / in a manufacturer's own serum" and "this specific liposomal cocktail rejuvenates intact aging skin" is the central honesty issue.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro (cell culture) — STRONG mechanistically, for the components
- **Araújo R, Lôbo M, Trindade K, et al. (2019)**, *Skin Pharmacol Physiol* 32(5):275–282 (PMID 31352445) — mechanistic review: FGFs bind FGFR tyrosine kinases, activate MAPK/ERK, and **induce type I collagen and elastin**; positions FGFs as controllers of skin aging. Mechanism, **not** clinical proof. [2]
- **Bassino E, Zanardi A, Gasparri F, Munaron L (2016)**, *Exp Dermatol* 25(3):237–239 (PMID 26662923; DOI 10.1111/exd.12906) — the biomimetic peptide **sh-Polypeptide-9 (CG-VEGF)** in **cocultures of human hair-follicle dermal-papilla + microvascular endothelial cells** raised VEGF, stimulated bFGF release and β-catenin at lower doses than minoxidil. **In-vitro coculture; hair-follicle angiogenesis model — not facial photoaging.** A real primary source for one bio-placenta component, but for the *wrong* indication. [3]
- **MSC/adipose conditioned-media on UVB-stressed skin cells** (Kim et al., *Int J Mol Sci* 2020, PMC6981944) — CM prevented UVB aging markers (↑collagen, ↓MMPs). In-vitro; *secretome*, not this defined cocktail — borrowed. [15]
- EGF/FGF-2 are bioactive on cultured keratinocytes/fibroblasts (proliferation, migration) across many reports. **Limitation:** cultured cells have **no stratum corneum** — receptor access is guaranteed in a dish and *not* through intact skin.

### Tier 2 — Animal — supportive for WOUNDS, thin for cosmetic aging
- rhEGF, rhFGF-2 and rhPDGF accelerate wound closure / re-epithelialization in rodent, pig and rabbit-ear models (the basis of their *medical/drug* use). Robust for **wounds**, **not** for cosmetic photoaging of intact skin. [21]
- No credible animal model shows a leave-on bio-placenta cream reversing intrinsic/photo-aging of intact skin. **Do not borrow wound-healing data as anti-wrinkle evidence.**

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL — N/A (do not borrow)
Growth factors are proteins → digested if ingested; there is **no oral-supplement evidence** relevant to a topical bio-placenta. (Note: "placenta supplements"/placentophagy exist but are a different, unevidenced product — keep entirely separate.) *Do not import any ingestible framing.*

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL — the decision-relevant tier

**(a) On the bio-placenta complex specifically — NONE published independently.**
- Supplier/finished-brand claims of "significant wrinkle, hydration, elasticity improvement in 2–4 weeks" are **uncited marketing**; no peer-reviewed RCT, n, or protocol located [8][20]. **Grade: unsubstantiated.**

**(b) Borrowed from the recombinant-GF category — the best available proxy (highest level first):**
- **Quinlan DJ, Ghanem AM, Hassan H (2023)** — "Topical growth factor preparations for facial skin rejuvenation: A systematic review," *J Cosmet Dermatol* 22(7):2023–2039 (PMID 37222303). **33 studies (9 RCTs + 24 uncontrolled case series), 1,180 participants, 23 preparations.** Investigator-assessed improvement vs. baseline was **modest**: texture median **<50%**, fine lines **<35%**, overall appearance **<20%**. **Participant scores exceeded investigator scores** (a placebo/expectation tell). **3 controlled RCTs found NO significant difference between treatments.** Low AE risk. Limitations: heavy heterogeneity, missing info on other actives, no standardized outcomes, **durability >6 months unknown.** *Net:* the GF category shows a small, mostly uncontrolled/self-reported signal — and bio-placenta is one undifferentiated member of it. [1]
- **Naughton GK, Jiang LI, Makino ET, et al. (2022)**, *Dermatol Ther* (PMC9823186) — TNS Advanced+ (**fibroblast conditioned media** + botanicals), 24-wk randomized double-blind **vehicle-controlled** crossover, 66/68 completers; active beat vehicle on lines/photodamage/pigment/sagging; perceived ~6-yr age reduction; preclinical ↓senescence (H2A.J). **Funded by Allergan Aesthetics (AbbVie); author COIs.** A *secretome*, not bio-placenta — the most-cited "it works" GF study, but it's the maker's study of its own flagship. [5]
- **Wu DC, Goldman MP (2017)**, *J Clin Aesthet Dermatol* (PMID 28670356) — n=20, 3-mo, randomized double-blind **split-face**, fibroblast- vs adipose-MSC-derived GF cocktails; **both reduced wrinkling, no difference between products; no vehicle arm.** Shows change-over-time, not superiority to placebo. [4]
- **Schouest JM, Luu TK, Moy RL (2012)**, *J Drugs Dermatol* (PMID 22527430) — open-label, n=29 women (39–75), 3 mo, **barley-rhEGF** serum; significant fine-line/texture/pore gains in month 1. **No control; subjective grading; small n.** Hypothesis-generating only. The closest single-defined-GF analog. [16]

**(c) Penetration-bypassing procedural studies (NOT leave-on cosmetic):** microneedling/laser + GFs show benefit but **deliberately breach the barrier** — they evidence a *procedure*, not a cream. Do not use to support bio-placenta. (See growth-factors.md §4(e).)

### Tier-4 honesty summary
- **Proven for bio-placenta itself:** *nothing* independently. Only supplier marketing.
- **Best proxy (borrowed, GF category):** a **small, modest, heterogeneous, largely manufacturer-funded** human-topical signal for **texture/fine-lines/overall photoaged appearance** (Quinlan medians <20–50% [1]); products often indistinguishable from each other [1][4]; benefits partly inflated by participant self-report.
- **Borrowed but NOT cosmetic-proven:** collagen *synthesis*, "regeneration," senescence reversal (in-vitro/gene-expression/secretome), and **all wound-healing** performance (the drug lane).
- **Attribution risk specific to bio-placenta:** the formula bundles **lecithin liposomes + HA + acetyl glutamine + ferment** — any visible hydration/smoothing could be the carriers, not the GFs.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration — the central weakness

- **The ~500-Da rule** (Bos & Meinardi 2000): passive penetration of intact stratum corneum drops sharply above ~500 Daltons. Bio-placenta's proteins are **~6 kDa (EGF, ~12×) up to ~38–45 kDa (VEGF, ~76–90×)** over the ceiling [17][18]. By this rule, meaningful **passive** delivery of intact, bioactive protein to viable epidermis/dermis is **implausible** from a leave-on cream.
- **Nuance / steelman:**
  - EGFR and other GF receptors sit in the **upper viable epidermis**, just under the stratum corneum; some surface/upper-epidermal signaling and **follicular (appendageal)** uptake is possible, especially in thinner/compromised aged or peri-follicular skin [7][18].
  - **The liposome is the whole pitch.** Lecithin liposomes/transfersomes measurably improve in-vitro permeation *and* protect the protein: bilayer-coated liposomes increased sustained EGF release ~5×, and nanoliposomal EGF was markedly more stable than buffer [19][24]. A well-encapsulated low-% can plausibly out-deliver a bare high-% that degrades on the shelf. **But** in-vitro permeation/stability ≠ demonstrated clinical pharmacology in living skin.
  - **VEGF (~38–45 kDa)** is the hardest of the five to deliver intact through skin; its cosmetic rationale (perfusion) is also the least relevant to a leave-on anti-wrinkle claim.
- **Brutal read:** the same proteins that work in a dish and in a wound are the ones that struggle to cross intact skin from a cream. The liposome narrows but does not close the gap, and there is **no published skin-permeation or clinical-PK data on the LABIO complex** to show how much intact, bioactive GF actually reaches target cells. Penetration remains the honest Achilles' heel.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** Supplier guidance for LABIO BIO-Placenta is **2–5%** of the finished formula (≈2–3% in daily moisturizers, up to ~5% in intensive serums); finished products commonly cite ~3% (e.g., Minimalist 3%) [8][9]. The GFs themselves are present at trace levels within that; dosing is effectively by the *complex*, not by individual-protein %.
- **pH:** supplier states stability ~**pH 4.5–7.0**; formulate the base to this window *before* adding the complex. (Bare EGF/FGF are inactivated at pH extremes; FGF-2 especially fragile.) [8]
- **Stability — the practical killer:** these are **large, fragile proteins**, heat-/light-/oxidation-/protease-sensitive. **FGF-2 retains only ~50% of protein content after 30 min at 37 °C (or 1 h at 25 °C) in plain solution**, driven by exposed thiols forming aggregates [23]; rhEGF likewise degrades with heat/pH/proteases [25]. The **liposome + glycols (caprylyl glycol, 1,2-hexanediol, butylene glycol)** are doing stabilization + preservation work. Practical requirements: keep cool/dark, prefer **airless/single-dose or refrigerated** packaging, **avoid hot processing** (add the complex in the cool-down phase, low shear), and demand **lot potency/identity assays** — an unprotected serum can lose activity before use.
- **Pairings (good):** **hyaluronic acid, niacinamide, panthenol, ceramides, gentle peptides** — barrier-friendly companions that fit our barrier-repair thesis. The native lecithin/HA make it pleasant in light essences/serums.
- **Conflicts (avoid same-step):** **low-pH actives (L-ascorbic acid, AHAs/BHAs)**, strong **oxidants/retinaldehyde at harsh pH**, high-electrolyte systems, and **harsh anionic surfactants** — all can denature protein or destabilize liposomes. Don't co-formulate in the same layer as a pH-3.5 vitamin-C.
- **Sensory:** lightweight, near-neutral; lecithin can add slight richness/odor; generally elegant.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation/tolerability:** the GF category is **generally well tolerated** with **low adverse-event rates** [1]; most reactions trace to other formula components. No specific safety signal published for bio-placenta. The synthetic/fermentation route avoids tissue-borne pathogen risk (a plus over real placenta extract).
- **Theoretical proliferation / cancer concern — present, and *more* complicated here because of IGF-1 and VEGF:**
  - **EGF/EGFR:** chronic EGFR stimulation is theoretically pro-proliferative (EGFR *inhibitors* are anticancer drugs); no reported skin cancers from cosmetic EGF, and poor penetration limits exposure. Conservative stance: avoid on/near active lesions, personal skin-cancer history, and the eye area.
  - **IGF-1 — genuinely double-edged.** In the *dermis*, fibroblast-derived IGF-1 **primes the keratinocyte UV response**, and its age-related *decline* is linked to **increased non-melanoma skin cancer** in geriatric skin (Lewis, Travers, Somani, Spandau 2009, *Oncogene*, PMID 19966862) — i.e., *low* IGF-1 can be *harmful* for photocarcinogenesis. **But** IGF-1R signaling also **promotes melanoma proliferation, survival and metastasis** [22][26]. So topical IGF-1 is not cleanly "safe" or "dangerous" — it is context-dependent and under-studied for chronic cosmetic use. Reasonable posture: **strict daily SPF**, avoid on suspicious lesions / personal melanoma history.
  - **VEGF:** pro-angiogenic; theoretically undesirable to chronically upregulate in skin with vascular lesions or rosacea-type telangiectasia. Minimal real-world data; another reason to keep it *supporting* and well-penetration-limited.
- **Sun:** no photosensitization expected from the proteins, but the proliferation/IGF-1/VEGF biology makes **daily broad-spectrum SPF a non-negotiable companion** for any GF product, including this one.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** **no specific data**; precautionary avoidance is reasonable for a multi-proliferative-signal protein blend (IGF-1/EGF/VEGF). Flag as "consult clinician."
- **Sensitization:** low reported for recombinant single proteins; the *Bacillus*-ferment component and lecithin are common, generally low-risk.
- **Sourcing/ethics — a genuine advantage:** **no human or animal placental tissue, no donor consent or sterility/provenance burden, no exosome/secretome gray zone.** Recombinant fermentation (*E. coli*/yeast) is a cleaner, vegan-positionable provenance story than (a) real placenta extract or (b) human-cell conditioned media/exosomes. Diligence still required: endotoxin/sterility on fermentation-derived protein, and identity/potency assays.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic vs. drug line (US/FDA):** sold as a **cosmetic** ingredient (no pre-market approval; FDA polices safety/labeling/**claims**). The line is the claim — improving *appearance* = cosmetic; affecting *structure/function* = **drug**.
  - ✅ Cosmetic-safe: "helps improve the look of firmness, texture and elasticity," "visibly smooths fine lines," "supports the appearance of renewed, refreshed skin," "biomimetic, vegan alternative to placenta extract."
  - ⚠️ Drug-territory (avoid / would need substantiation + legal): "**stimulates/boosts collagen**," "**regenerates**/**repairs** skin," "**heals**," "**reverses aging**," "**rebuilds the dermis**," anything implying **wound healing** (that lane = the rhPDGF-BB drug Regranex, the only FDA-*approved* GF) or **angiogenesis** ("boosts blood flow").
- **The regenerative/biologic trap:** bio-placenta is *defined* and *non-cellular*, so it largely **avoids the exosome/secretome enforcement zone** — but the FDA in 2024–2025 has issued warning letters treating **exosome/secretome products with regenerative claims as unapproved drugs/biologics** [27]. Stay strictly in cosmetic-appearance language; do not borrow "regenerate/heal/stem-cell" framing from neighboring categories.
- **"Placenta" labeling sensitivity:** the word **"placenta"** triggers consumer and (in some markets) regulatory scrutiny. Some jurisdictions restrict or scrutinize placental ingredients; emphasize **"biomimetic / recombinant / no animal or human tissue."** Confirm regional labeling rules before using "placenta" in any market.
- **Substantiation gap:** because there's **no published trial on the complex**, any efficacy claim must rest on (a) the brand's own consumer/instrumental testing of the *finished* product, or (b) carefully-worded category/ingredient mechanism language — **not** on borrowed wound or in-vitro data. The supplier's "clinically studied" cannot be repeated by us without seeing the study.
- The **Martínez-Carpio et al. (2023)** "risk or fraud?" critique of sh-oligopeptide-1 (EGF) cosmetics — questioning demonstrable bioactivity, penetration, and trial quality — **applies directly** to this complex and must be answerable. (See growth-factors.md [15].)
- See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md) claim cheat-sheet.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **"Replicates the benefits of human placenta."** Marketing framing. It *mimics a placental signaling milieu* with five recombinant factors — there is **no evidence the blend reproduces whole-placenta "benefits,"** and whole-placenta extract itself has weak cosmetic evidence. The honest selling point is **provenance** (vegan, tissue-free, defined), not proven superiority.
- **"Clinically studied / proven."** As applied to the complex: **no independent published trial.** The honest statement is "the recombinant growth-factor *category* shows a small, modest, mostly self-reported human-topical signal (Quinlan 2023 [1]), and we have/haven't run our own finished-product testing."
- **"Boosts collagen / regenerates / heals."** Drug-style structure-function claims; supported mainly by **in-vitro and wound** data on the components, not by intact-skin cosmetic trials on this product. Off-limits as written.
- **"Liposomal delivery solves penetration."** Encapsulation *improves* in-vitro permeation and stability [19][24] but there is **no clinical-PK proof** it delivers enough intact, bioactive GF through living skin. Don't overstate.
- **The longevity contradiction (important, see §11).** "Bio-placenta" markets *youth/renewal*, yet **IGF-1 is the canonical lifespan-*shortening* pathway** — across species, *reduced* insulin/IGF-1 signaling (→ FOXO/DAF-16) **extends** lifespan, and **FOXO3** is the most-replicated human longevity gene [28][29]. Topically dosing an IGF-1 mimetic is, at minimum, an awkward fit for a "skin-longevity" thesis and should never be framed as "longevity science."
- **Where hype meets reality:** there *is* a real, modest cosmetic signal for texture/fine-lines from well-formulated GF serums, and bio-placenta's **defined composition + clean provenance + decent sensory** make it one of the more *honest* ways to play the GF trend — but it's small, possibly carrier-/placebo-inflated, and oversold.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ — Lean CONDITIONAL-SUPPORTING (skip-able). Never a hero; never a "longevity" story.**

**One-line reasoning:** Bio-placenta is the **cleanest, most-defined, best-provenance** member of the growth-factor class — a real advantage for an honesty-first brand over messy secretomes/exosomes/real-placenta — but it still **fails the core tests**: a ~500-Da penetration ceiling (proteins 12–90× over), severe protein instability, **zero independent RCTs on the actual complex**, attribution muddied by its own liposome/HA carriers, and a genuinely awkward **IGF-1 longevity contradiction**. That's a poor fit for a *hero* and a *bad* fit for an explicit *longevity* claim.

**Longevity-relevance note (be careful here):** Conceptually on-thesis for *skin renewal/ECM* (FGF/EGF repair signaling). **But** the **systemic-longevity** literature points the *other way* on IGF-1: lower insulin/IGF-1 → FOXO3 → longer life [28][29]. A brand selling "skin longevity" should **not** cite this ingredient as longevity-aligned; if used, position it strictly as **cosmetic appearance/renewal**, decoupled from the lifespan/longevity-biology story (where senolytics, NAD+, sirtuin/autophagy actives, and barrier-repair lipids are the on-thesis players).

**If used at all,** require: the **defined LABIO-style complex** (not an undefined secretome), in a **validated liposomal, potency-assayed, stability-tested, cool-processed** base at supplier-recommended **2–5%**, paired with **HA/niacinamide/ceramides** and **mandatory daily SPF messaging**, positioned as a **premium supporting "renewal/biomimetic" actor** with **cosmetic-appearance claims only** — never "boosts collagen / regenerates / heals," never "longevity science," and never marketed on borrowed wound or in-vitro data. Prefer it over real placenta extract and over exosomes/secretomes on **provenance and definability** grounds if a GF play is wanted.

**Otherwise, skip** in favor of better-evidenced, cleaner-story renewal options (well-substantiated **copper peptides / GHK-Cu**, established **signal peptides**, retinoids for the actual structure-function lifting, and barrier-repair lipids for our core thesis), all with fewer penetration, stability, sourcing, claims, and longevity-framing headaches.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

*Verified 2026-06-19 by independent re-check of the primary literature (PubMed abstracts, PMC full texts where reachable, INCI registries, and supplier/regulatory records). Goal: catch claims that quietly borrow in-vitro/animal/wound data to support a leave-on cosmetic, citations that don't exist or are mischaracterized, and supplier marketing dressed up as clinical proof.*

### Overall evidence grade: **C– / D+ (weak-to-very-weak for the cosmetic claim that matters)**

For the decision-relevant question — *does a leave-on liposomal bio-placenta cosmetic measurably rejuvenate intact, aging human skin?* — the honest grade is **C– to D+**, a notch *below* the parent growth-factor category (graded C in growth-factors.md). Reason: the category at least has manufacturer vehicle-controlled RCTs on *specific products*; **the bio-placenta complex has no independent published trial at all** — only uncited supplier "clinically studied" copy. The mechanism is coherent and the components are bioactive (A-grade in-vitro), but that is the **wrong question** (a dish, not intact skin). The penetration/stability obstacles are if anything *worse* here (VEGF up to ~45 kDa; FGF-2 ~50% loss in 30 min at 37 °C).

### What I verified (citations REAL and accurately characterized)
- **Composition/INCI** — confirmed via INCIDecoder + EWG + Stratia + supplier: five GFs = EGF (sh-Oligopeptide-1), IGF-1 (sh-Oligopeptide-2), bFGF/FGF-2 (sh-Polypeptide-1), VEGF-A (sh-Polypeptide-9), aFGF/FGF-1 (sh-Polypeptide-11); recombinant, *E. coli*/yeast-fermented; lecithin-liposome base; LABIO (Korea) is the flagship supplier. **One caveat flagged in-text:** some marketing swaps sh-Polypeptide-1/-11 (basic vs acidic FGF) — registry mapping used.
- **[1] Quinlan 2023** (PMID 37222303) — independently confirmed: 33 studies, 9 RCTs + 24 uncontrolled series, 1,180 participants, 23 preparations; investigator medians (texture <50%, fine lines <35%, overall <20%); participant > investigator; 3 controlled RCTs no significant between-treatment difference; low AE; durability >6 mo unknown. Accurately reported; this is the strongest *proxy* evidence and it is modest.
- **[3] Bassino 2016** (PMID 26662923, DOI 10.1111/exd.12906) — confirmed real; *in-vitro* coculture of hair-follicle dermal-papilla + microvascular endothelial cells; sh-Polypeptide-9 raised VEGF/bFGF/β-catenin vs minoxidil. Correctly flagged as in-vitro **hair** model, not facial photoaging.
- **[22] Lewis/Spandau 2009/2010** (*Oncogene*, PMID 19966862) — confirmed: dermal IGF-1 primes the keratinocyte UV response; *low* IGF-1 in aged dermis → ↑ non-melanoma skin cancer. Correctly used to show IGF-1's **double-edged** safety profile (not a simple "GF = cancer" claim).
- **[17] Bos & Meinardi 2000** 500-Da rule, and **[23] FGF-2 stability** (~50% loss 30 min/37 °C, thiol aggregation) — confirmed; penetration/stability thesis well-grounded. EGF ~6 kDa ≈ 12× ceiling independently confirmed.
- **[28][29] IGF-1/FOXO longevity** — confirmed: reduced insulin/IGF-1 signaling extends lifespan; FOXO3 most-replicated human longevity gene. The "longevity contradiction" is real and correctly characterized as a *systemic* finding (not a proven topical harm).
- **[27] FDA exosome enforcement 2024–2025** — confirmed warning letters exist (e.g., Chara Biologics/CharaExo; Evolutionary Biologics/EXO RNA). Correctly framed as *neighboring-category* risk that bio-placenta largely avoids **if claims stay cosmetic**.

### Main caveats (honest reader's warnings)
1. **No independent trial on the actual complex.** The single biggest gap. "Clinically studied" is supplier marketing with no retrievable n/protocol/journal [8][20]. Do not repeat it as fact.
2. **Borrowed evidence dominates.** All positive human-topical data are on *other* GF products (secretomes, single rhEGF), and the robust mechanistic tiers are in-vitro/wound. The dossier resists laundering these into bio-placenta proof — keep it that way.
3. **Carrier-attribution problem.** Lecithin liposomes + HA + acetyl glutamine will themselves hydrate/smooth; any finished-product "result" can't be attributed to the GFs without a vehicle control.
4. **Penetration/stability worse than baseline GF.** VEGF (~38–45 kDa) and dual FGFs raise the delivery bar; FGF-2 is notoriously unstable. The liposome helps in-vitro but no clinical PK exists.
5. **IGF-1 longevity contradiction.** For a *skin-longevity* brand specifically, marketing an IGF-1 mimetic as "longevity" is scientifically backwards — explicitly de-coupled in §11.
6. **Some sources are supplier/secondary.** [6][7][8][9][10][11][12][13][14][18][20] are INCI databases / retailer / supplier pages used for *composition, use levels, and ingredient identity* (appropriate), not for efficacy. Efficacy rests on the peer-reviewed proxy set [1][2][3][5][16] and the mechanism/safety primaries [22][23][28][29].

### Net
Bio-placenta is the **most defensible *provenance* story** in the GF/regenerative class (vegan, defined, no tissue, no exosome gray-zone) — but its **efficacy evidence for the actual complex is essentially nonexistent**, it inherits a hard penetration ceiling and protein-stability problems, and it carries an **IGF-1 longevity contradiction** specific to this brand's positioning. The **Lean conditional-supporting → skip-able** verdict (cosmetic-claims-only, never hero, never "longevity") is well-supported. Confidence in the **C–/D+** grade: high.
