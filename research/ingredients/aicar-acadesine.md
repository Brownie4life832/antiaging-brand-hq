# Ingredient: AICAR (Acadesine) (INCI: none established — see §1)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⬜ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ☑ rejected (topical) / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** NAD+ / sirtuin / AMPK / cellular-energy actives (specifically: a direct **AMPK activator** — an AMP-mimetic nucleoside)

> **One-line honest summary:** AICAR (acadesine) is the *textbook* pharmacological AMPK activator — the molecule researchers reach for to switch on the cell's master energy/longevity sensor, which is genuinely upstream of several real skin-aging hallmarks (autophagy, mitochondrial biogenesis, mTOR restraint, lipid synthesis, senescence). On paper it is the most "longevity-mechanism-pure" active in this whole class. **But it is not a cosmetic ingredient.** It has **no established cosmetic INCI**, it is a **failed investigational cardiac drug** (the Phase 3 RED-CABG trial was stopped for futility), it is a **WADA-banned doping agent** ("exercise in a pill"), and — critically for safety — it is a **cell-cycle-arresting, pro-apoptotic compound used in oncology research** whose AMPK activation is *context-dependent* (pro- AND anti-tumor depending on setting). There is **essentially zero human topical skin evidence**; the skin story is in-vitro fibroblast/melanocyte work plus borrowed AMPK biology. For a credible skincare brand this is a **skip as a topical active** — it belongs in the dossier as the mechanistic "north star" that *safe, formulatable* AMPK actives (metformin, ferments, plant AMPK activators) gesture toward, not as something to put in a jar.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** AICAR (the most common research name); **Acadesine** (the drug/INN name); **AICA riboside** / **AICA ribonucleoside**. (Strictly: "AICAR" technically denotes the *ribonucleotide/ribotide*, i.e. the intracellular phosphorylated form ZMP; "acadesine" / "AICA riboside" denotes the administered *riboside*. In practice the literature uses "AICAR" loosely for the administered nucleoside — this dossier follows that loose usage and flags ZMP where the distinction matters.)
- **Other synonyms:** 5-aminoimidazole-4-carboxamide-1-β-D-ribofuranoside; 5-amino-4-imidazolecarboxamide riboside; NSC 105823; GP-1-110.
- **The active intracellular species (ZMP):** Once inside a cell, adenosine kinase phosphorylates AICA-riboside to **ZMP (AICA ribotide / 5-aminoimidazole-4-carboxamide ribonucleotide monophosphate)**, an **AMP analogue** that allosterically activates AMPK *without* changing the actual AMP:ATP ratio. ZMP is the business end. [1][2]
- **INCI status (decisive nuance):** **There is no established, recognised cosmetic INCI for AICAR / acadesine, and it is not a conventional cosmetic ingredient.** It is sold as a **research biochemical** (Sigma-Aldrich A9978, Selleck, Abcam ab120358, MedChemExpress, Cayman, etc.) and was developed as a **pharmaceutical**. It is also a **WADA-prohibited substance** (see §8). Do **not** assume it can be lawfully or safely used in a cosmetic in major markets — treat it as **not cosmetic-permitted** absent specific, market-by-market confirmation that almost certainly does not exist. [3][4][13][14]
- **CAS:** **2627-69-2** (AICA riboside / acadesine). (The monophosphate ZMP has its own CAS, 3031-94-5.)
- **Molecular formula / weight:** **C₉H₁₄N₄O₅, MW ≈ 258.23 g/mol** (the riboside). A small, **highly water-soluble, polar nucleoside** (insoluble in ethanol; ~20–52 mg/mL water; ~51 mg/mL DMSO). [3][5]
- **Chemical class:** A **purine-precursor nucleoside** — the riboside of an intermediate (AICA ribotide/ZMP) in the *de novo* purine (inosine monophosphate) biosynthesis pathway. So it is an **endogenous metabolite** in the sense that ZMP is a natural purine-synthesis intermediate; the administered *riboside* is a pharmacological tool.
- **Key forms/derivatives:** Essentially one molecule of interest (the riboside). Note related/adjacent AMPK activators that ARE more formulatable — **metformin**, **salicylate/A-769662-class direct activators (research)**, and natural AMPK-activators (berberine, certain ferments, plant extracts) — these are the practical alternatives the brand should look at instead (see this folder's `berberine.md`, `b-vitamin-anti-glycation.md`, `calcium-akg.md` for adjacent energy/longevity actives).

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it works at the skin/cell level)

AICAR is the **prototype direct pharmacological activator of AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK)** — the cell's central low-energy / "fuel gauge" sensor and a recognised longevity node. Mechanistically it is clean and well-characterised:

1. **AMPK activation via ZMP (the core mechanism).** AICA-riboside enters cells, is phosphorylated to **ZMP**, an **AMP-mimetic** that binds the AMPK γ-subunit and activates the kinase *as if* energy were low — but without depleting ATP. [1][2] This is why it became the canonical "is this effect AMPK-dependent?" research probe.
2. **mTORC1 restraint.** Active AMPK inhibits mTORC1 (via TSC2 and Raptor). In skin, **mTOR hyperactivation is linked to accelerated collagen degradation and stem-cell exhaustion**; restraining it is a plausible geroprotective lever. [11][12]
3. **Autophagy / mitophagy induction.** AMPK activates ULK1 and promotes autophagic flux and mitophagy — clearing damaged mitochondria and protein aggregates. Declining autophagy is a hallmark of aged skin; AMPK-driven autophagy reverses replicative and ROS-induced senescence markers in human dermal fibroblasts (shown with *other* AMPK activators). [6][9]
4. **Mitochondrial biogenesis & fatty-acid oxidation (the "exercise mimetic" axis).** AICAR ↑ PGC-1α / PPARδ signalling, ↑ mitochondrial biogenesis, ↓ acetyl-CoA carboxylase → ↓ malonyl-CoA → ↑ fatty-acid oxidation. This AMPK–PPARδ axis is exactly why AICAR is doping-banned ("exercise in a pill"). [7][13]
5. **Lipid-synthesis / barrier relevance (skin-specific, indirect).** AMPK activation is implicated in **keratinocyte lipid (ceramide/fatty-acid) synthesis** and barrier repair; barrier disruption itself activates AMPK as part of the repair program. (Most direct keratinocyte-lipid data are for *other* AMPK/SIRT1 activators, not AICAR specifically.) [8][10]
6. **Anti-inflammatory / inflammaging.** AICAR inhibits NF-κB-driven transcription and suppresses iNOS/COX-2 and pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1) in immune cells/macrophages. [1][15]
7. **Hyaluronan & melanin modulation (the two most *direct* skin-cell findings).** AICAR directly ↑ **HAS2** transcripts and hyaluronan synthesis in **human dermal fibroblasts** (AMPK/PPARα-dependent) [16]; and AICAR ↓ MITF/tyrosinase/TRP-1/DCT and melanin in **human and mouse melanocytes** (AMPK/CRTC pathway) [17]. These are the closest things to "AICAR did something in a skin cell."

**Skin-relevant translation (honest):** AMPK is a *legitimately* central skin-aging/longevity hub — restraining mTOR, fuelling autophagy/mitophagy, supporting barrier lipids, and damping inflammaging are all real, on-thesis levers. AICAR is the purest way to *probe* that hub. **The problem is not the mechanism — it's the molecule.** "AMPK is good for skin" does not make "put the doping-banned, cell-cycle-arresting research nucleoside on your face" a sound or legal product decision. The same hub is reachable by safer, cosmetic-legal actives.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** AICAR's deep, real evidence is as a **research AMPK probe** and as a **systemic (intravenous/oral/injected) metabolic agent** — in cell biology, oncology, metabolism, cardiology and exercise physiology. Its **best-known human data are a *failed* Phase 3 cardiac-surgery drug trial** and its **most famous "result" is endurance-doping in athletes**. For **human topical skin**, the literature is **effectively empty**. A "topical AICAR serum" today would rest on **borrowed AMPK biology + two in-vitro skin-cell papers** — and would be selling a *doping-banned, cell-cycle-arresting research drug*. Be brutal about this.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell models) — *this is where almost all skin-relevant AICAR data live*

- **Yamane T., Kobayashi-Hattori K., Oishi Y. (2011), *Biochem Biophys Res Commun* 415(2):235–238 (DOI 10.1016/j.bbrc.2011.10.008).** In **human dermal fibroblasts**, adiponectin ↑ hyaluronan synthesis and **HAS2** mRNA via an **AMPK/PPARα-dependent** pathway; **chemical AMPK activation with AICAR also ↑ HAS2 mRNA** (described as a *slight* induction, with the authors noting complex/cell-specific HAS2 regulation). *Limitation: in-vitro only; AICAR's own effect was modest; primary actor in the paper is adiponectin; no topical/in-vivo arm; no measure of dermal hyaluronan in living skin.* [16] — **the single most direct "AICAR did X in a human skin cell" datapoint, and it's modest.**
- **Bang S., Won K.H., et al. (2017), *Pigment Cell & Melanoma Research* — "Novel regulation of melanogenesis by adiponectin via the AMPK/CRTC pathway" (PubMed 28481450).** In **normal human and mouse melanocytes**, **adiponectin and AICAR both ↓ MITF, tyrosinase, TRP-1, DCT and melanin content**, via AMPK→inhibitory CRTC phosphorylation→CREB suppression. Authors frame adiponectin/analogues as a strategy for **hyperpigmentation**. *Limitation: in-vitro melanocytes; AICAR is the tool compound not the proposed product; no human topical/skin-tone outcome.* [17] — **the second direct skin-cell finding (pigment).** (Note: the AMPK–melanogenesis literature is *directionally mixed* across studies/cell types — some report AMPK suppressing, the picture is not monolithic — which itself argues against over-claiming a depigmenting benefit.)
- **AMPK-autophagy-senescence in dermal fibroblasts (mechanistic anchor, mostly *other* activators).** AMPK-induced autophagy + NRF2 antioxidant signalling can reverse replicative and ROS-induced senescence and "rejuvenate" human dermal fibroblasts; e.g. **Kim H. et al. (2021), *Clinical & Translational Medicine* 11(7):e521 (DOI 10.1002/ctm2.521)** — Ca²⁺-AMPK-mediated autophagy (ginsenoside Rg3) attenuates senescence in HDFs. *Limitation: establishes the AMPK→autophagy→anti-senescence route in skin cells but with a different activator; supports the **pathway**, not AICAR-the-ingredient.* [6][9]
- **Oncology / cell-cycle in-vitro (the safety-relevant body of work).** AICAR repeatedly **inhibits proliferation, arrests the cell cycle (G1/S), and induces apoptosis** across many tumour lines (prostate, hepatocellular, multiple myeloma, B-CLL [EC₅₀ ~380 µM], glioblastoma, ALL, colon, breast, retinoblastoma). [1][3][19][20] *Relevance: this is real, reproducible biology — and it means AICAR is a **cytostatic/pro-apoptotic agent**, which is the opposite of what you want a leave-on facial active doing to keratinocytes/fibroblasts at uncontrolled doses. It is exactly why AMPK activation is described as a double-edged sword.*

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo) — *systemic/injected, essentially never topical skin*

- **Narkar V.A., Downes M., Evans R.M., et al. (2008), *Cell* 134(3):405–415 (DOI 10.1016/j.cell.2008.06.051).** The landmark "exercise pill" study: **AICAR (injected) increased running endurance ~44% in sedentary mice** via the AMPK–PPARδ axis (≈77% combined with GW501516). *Limitation: systemic injection; muscle/metabolic endpoint; nothing to do with skin — but it is the reason AICAR is doping-banned and the source of most of its fame.* [7]
- **AICAR-dependent AMPK activation & scar/collagen maturation — Mia M.M., et al. / Dobaczewski-adjacent (2013), *J Mol Cell Cardiol* 62:24–35 (DOI 10.1016/j.yjmcc.2013.04.027; PubMed 23871790).** In a murine reperfused-MI model, AICAR-driven AMPK ↑ (myo)fibroblast recruitment and **enhanced collagen deposition/fibre maturation** in cardiac scar. *Limitation: heart, not skin; pro-fibrotic context (collagen deposition in a scar is not the same as cosmetically desirable dermal remodelling — and pro-fibrotic signalling is a caution, not a selling point, for skin).* [18]
- **AICAR vs diabetic polyneuropathy via mitophagy (2025), *Int J Mol Sci* 26(1):80 (DOI 10.3390/ijms26010080).** Systemic AICAR prevented/reversed diabetic neuropathy by restoring AMPK-mediated mitophagy. *Limitation: systemic, nerve tissue; cited only to show AICAR's mitophagy mechanism is reproducible in vivo — not skin.* [21]
- **Anti-tumour in vivo (retinoblastoma, etc.).** AICAR reduced tumour growth via ↓ angiogenesis + ↑ apoptosis. [20] *Same double-edged-sword caveat as Tier 1 oncology.*

### Tier 3 — Human (systemic/oral/IV) — *the real human data are pharmacological, and the flagship trial FAILED*

- **RED-CABG trial — Newman M.F., et al. (2012), *JAMA* 308(2):157–164 (DOI 10.1001/jama.2012.7633; PubMed 22782417).** The definitive human trial of acadesine: **randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled, ~3,080 patients (planned 7,500), 300 sites/7 countries**, IV acadesine (0.1 mg/kg/min ×7 h + in cardioplegia) in CABG surgery. **Stopped early for futility** — no reduction in death/MI/stroke vs placebo. Earlier (1990s) meta-analyses had been encouraging; the large rigorous trial was negative. *Relevance: this is the highest-quality human evidence on acadesine and it is a **negative efficacy result** for its intended (cardioprotective) use. Tolerability was acceptable IV, but efficacy failed.* [22][23]
- **Hematology/oncology Phase I/II (acadesine in B-CLL).** Acadesine reached clinical trials in chronic lymphocytic leukaemia (its pro-apoptotic AMPK biology). *Relevance: confirms it is developed and dosed as a **drug**, and that its lead activities are anti-proliferative/pro-apoptotic.* [1][19]
- **Exercise/doping context (human).** AICAR is on the **WADA Prohibited List** (added 2009; class S4 Hormone & Metabolic Modulators) precisely because of human endurance-enhancing potential and detectability work in doping control. *Relevance: regulatory reality for any "exercise mimetic" framing.* [13][14]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters for a skincare brand)

- **None identified.** After multiple targeted searches, **no peer-reviewed human topical/clinical skin study of AICAR/acadesine was found** — no RCT, no lower-tier clinical, no published cosmetic finished-product efficacy study, no transdermal/penetration study on human skin. AICAR is **not** an established cosmetic ingredient and does not appear in mainstream cosmetic actives portfolios. "Topical AMPK / longevity skincare" discussion in the literature centres on **metformin, rapamycin/rapalogs, and natural activators** — *not* AICAR. [10][24]
- **Implication:** Any topical AICAR claim would be **100% borrowed** from (a) systemic/animal AMPK pharmacology and (b) two in-vitro skin-cell papers [16][17]. There is **no human topical efficacy or safety dataset** to stand on.

**Bottom line by tier:** Mechanistically excellent and *the* canonical AMPK activator (in-vitro/animal/human-systemic). Two genuine in-vitro human-skin-cell findings (HAS2↑ in fibroblasts; melanin↓ in melanocytes), both modest and tool-compound in nature. **Best human evidence overall = a FAILED Phase 3 cardiac trial.** **Human topical skin evidence = zero.** And its dominant, reproducible cellular activity is **growth arrest / apoptosis**.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Against (significant):** AICAR is a **small but very polar, highly water-soluble nucleoside (MW ~258, insoluble in ethanol)** — exactly the physicochemical profile that **crosses the lipophilic stratum corneum poorly** without a delivery system. Nucleosides are not natural skin-penetrants.
- **Activation requires intracellular machinery:** AICAR is inert until **phosphorylated to ZMP by adenosine kinase inside viable cells**. So topical activity needs the molecule to (a) penetrate to viable epidermis/dermis in meaningful amounts AND (b) be taken up and phosphorylated by skin cells. Neither step has any published human-skin data.
- **No human transdermal/PK data:** There are **no published human (or even ex-vivo human skin) penetration data** for AICAR quantifying delivery to viable skin or intracellular ZMP generation. Systemic studies dosed it **IV or by injection** precisely because oral/passive delivery is inefficient.
- **Practical read:** Topical penetration of plain AICAR is **likely poor and entirely unproven**; meaningful skin activity would require an unproven delivery/penetration system — which, given the safety profile (§7), is not a problem worth solving for this molecule.

## 6. Formulation

> Provided for completeness only — the verdict is **skip** (§11). There is no validated cosmetic formulation pathway.

- **Effective concentration:** **No established topical efficacy concentration.** In-vitro AMPK-activating doses are typically **0.1–2 mM (≈0.026–0.5%)**, with oncology effects often at **0.5–2 mM** (EC₅₀ ~380 µM in B-CLL) — i.e. *millimolar* concentrations achieved in cell culture, which say nothing about a topical dose-response and sit near anti-proliferative ranges. [3][19]
- **pH / solubility:** Water-soluble, near-neutral handling; insoluble in ethanol. No specific cosmetic stability data published.
- **Stability:** As a nucleoside it is reasonably stable as a dry powder; aqueous behaviour in a finished cosmetic is uncharacterised (no published cosmetic stability work).
- **Pairings:** Not applicable as a recommended active. *If* one were exploring the AMPK *theme* (with legal actives, not AICAR), the coherent stack is **AMPK/SIRT/NAD-axis** (niacinamide, Ca-AKG, berberine, ferments) + **barrier lipids** — see this folder's adjacent dossiers.
- **Sensory:** Unknown in cosmetic context.

## 7. Safety

This is where AICAR fails hardest for a leave-on consumer cosmetic.

- **Cytostatic / pro-apoptotic activity (the headline concern).** AICAR's most robust, reproducible cellular effect is **cell-cycle arrest and apoptosis** across many cell types [1][3][19][20]. AMPK over-activation can also be **anti-regenerative** (loss/over-restraint of mTOR impairs keratinocyte/stem-cell proliferative capacity — "accelerated aging" phenotype in some models). This bidirectionality means uncontrolled topical AMPK activation is **not self-evidently safe or beneficial** for actively renewing skin. [11][12]
- **The AMPK "double-edged sword."** AMPK activation can be cytoprotective OR pro-death/pro- or anti-tumour depending on tissue, dose and context. A consumer leave-on product offers none of the dosing control of a clinical/research setting. [25]
- **Doping-banned (regulatory, not toxicology, but disqualifying).** AICAR is on the **WADA Prohibited List** (S4). Selling a topical product containing a WADA-banned metabolic modulator is reputationally and practically untenable for any athlete-adjacent consumer and is a compliance landmine. [13][14]
- **Clinical safety context:** IV acadesine was *tolerated* in the large RED-CABG surgical population (efficacy, not safety, was the failure) [22] — but IV tolerability in monitored surgical patients does **not** transfer to chronic leave-on topical use, and tells us nothing about skin irritation/sensitisation.
- **Irritation / sensitisation / photo-safety:** **No human topical patch-test/HRIPT/photo-safety data found.** Unknown.
- **Endogenous-metabolite caveat:** ZMP is a natural purine-synthesis intermediate, and inborn ZMP-accumulation (ATIC/AICA-ribosiduria-type defects) is associated with serious developmental disease — i.e. the body tightly regulates this metabolite; flooding cells with it pharmacologically is not "just a vitamin." [unverified — specific clinical-genetics detail not re-fetched]
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** No topical data; given the cell-cycle/apoptotic and metabolic-modulator profile, default to **avoid**.
- **Sourcing:** Research-grade biochemical (Sigma/Selleck/Abcam/MCE). No cosmetic-grade supply chain.

**Safety read:** Even setting aside legality, the combination of **(reproducible growth-arrest/apoptosis) + (no human skin safety data) + (WADA ban) + (no cosmetic-grade supply)** makes AICAR unsuitable for a consumer cosmetic.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic ingredient status:** **No established cosmetic INCI; not a recognised cosmetic ingredient.** It is a **research chemical / investigational drug**. Using a pharmacologically active, formerly-clinically-developed AMPK-activating drug in a cosmetic would very likely render the product an **unapproved drug** (US FDA structure/function framing) and/or an unauthorised ingredient (EU/UK and most markets typically restrict pharmacologically active/medicinal substances in cosmetics). **Do not assume it is cosmetic-legal anywhere.** [3][4]
- **The drug/cosmetic line (decisive):** AICAR's *entire* value proposition — "activates your skin's longevity energy sensor / exercise-mimetic / autophagy / reverses senescence" — is **structure/function (drug) language**. There is no honest cosmetic claim available because there is no human topical evidence to substantiate even a modest appearance claim, and the molecule itself is drug-like.
- **WADA:** Banned substance (S4). Any "exercise mimetic" / "exercise in a pill / jar" framing is doping-adjacent and off-limits. [13][14]
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** *None recommended for AICAR.* If the brand wants the **AMPK / cellular-energy narrative**, attach it to **cosmetic-legal AMPK-adjacent actives** (e.g. niacinamide/NAD-axis, ferments, plant AMPK activators) with their own substantiation — see glossary and adjacent dossiers.
- ⚠️ **Risky / disqualifying:** "AMPK activator," "exercise mimetic," "activates autophagy/longevity pathways in your skin," "reverses cellular senescence," "AICAR/acadesine" on a cosmetic label — all unsupported for topical use and/or drug-/doping-coded. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **"AMPK is the longevity master switch, so a topical AMPK activator is anti-aging."** AMPK *is* a legitimate longevity hub, but (a) AICAR's AMPK activation is documented **systemically/in-vitro**, not in human skin, and (b) "more AMPK activation" is **not monotonically good** — over-activation is anti-proliferative and context-dependently pro-death. Mechanism ≠ safe topical outcome. [11][12][25]
- **"Exercise in a jar."** The "exercise mimetic" fame comes from **injected mice (endurance +44%)** [7] and is the reason it's **doping-banned** [13]. None of it is a topical skin claim.
- **"Reverses cellular senescence / boosts autophagy in skin."** The AMPK→autophagy→anti-senescence route in skin cells is real **but was shown with *other* activators** [6][9]; AICAR-specific human-skin senescence reversal is **unproven**.
- **"Clinically studied molecule."** True — and the **flagship clinical study (RED-CABG) FAILED** [22], and the clinical development was for **cardiac/leukaemia**, not skin. "Clinically studied" here is a *caution*, not an endorsement.
- **The two real skin-cell findings are modest.** HAS2 induction in fibroblasts was described as *slight* [16]; the melanocyte/pigment effect is tool-compound and the AMPK-melanogenesis literature is **directionally mixed** [17]. Neither supports a finished-product claim.
- **The safety inversion.** The most reproducible thing AICAR does to cells is **stop them dividing / kill them** (its oncology rationale) [19][20] — the opposite of a reassuring leave-on-active profile.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

❌ **SKIP as a topical active — outstanding *mechanism story*, unusable *molecule*.**
**One-line:** AICAR (acadesine) is the most mechanistically "pure" longevity active imaginable — the canonical AMPK activator sitting directly on the autophagy / mTOR / mitochondrial / senescence hub — but it is a **doping-banned, cell-cycle-arresting investigational drug with no cosmetic INCI, no cosmetic-grade supply, poor/unproven skin penetration, zero human topical evidence, and a FAILED flagship clinical trial.** For a credibility-first skincare brand it is a **clear skip for use in product**; its value to us is purely **educational / directional** — it marks the *mechanism* (AMPK / cellular-energy / autophagy) that we should pursue through **safe, cosmetic-legal actives** instead.

**Why not "conditional":** Unlike Ca-AKG or spermidine (oral-data-rich, topical-data-poor, but at least benign and formulatable), AICAR carries **active disqualifiers**: (1) **WADA ban / drug status** — likely not cosmetic-legal; (2) **no cosmetic supply chain**; (3) a **reproducible anti-proliferative/pro-apoptotic safety signal**; (4) **no human topical data at all**. Any one of these is enough to bench it; together they make it a firm skip.

**Longevity-relevance note:** For a skin-*longevity* brand, AICAR is genuinely useful as a **map, not a destination**. It demonstrates *why* the AMPK / cellular-energy axis belongs in our worldview (mTOR restraint, autophagy/mitophagy, inflammaging, barrier-lipid synthesis) — and it lets us tell that story credibly *if* we deliver it through **cosmetic-appropriate AMPK-adjacent actives** (niacinamide/NAD-axis, Ca-AKG, berberine, fermentation-derived activators, plant AMPK activators) and through the **#1 evidence-based AMPK-friendly behaviours** (UV protection, the real "exercise" being lifestyle, not a banned nucleoside). Watch the broader **topical-geroprotector** field (topical metformin/rapamycin research) for the *legitimate* version of this idea — AICAR itself is not it.

Log in [`../../ideas/not-considering.md`](../../ideas/not-considering.md) (topical use) — reason: no cosmetic INCI/legal status, WADA-banned, anti-proliferative/pro-apoptotic safety signal, no human topical evidence, no cosmetic-grade supply. Retain in research as the AMPK-mechanism reference.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified 2026-06-19** by independent re-check of the load-bearing citations against primary literature (PubMed/JAMA/Cell/journal originals) and supplier/WADA references. For each headline claim: is the evidence (a) HUMAN + TOPICAL, or borrowed from in-vitro/animal/human-systemic; (b) a real study that says what's claimed; (c) industry-funded/tiny/negative?

### Overall grade: **D for the topical hero use any copy would imply (and "skip" overall)**

- **The molecule's *mechanism* grade is high (A/B) — its *topical-skincare* grade is D.** AICAR is the genuine, canonical AMPK activator with deep in-vitro/animal/human-systemic pedigree. But the **human topical skin evidence is zero**, the **best human evidence is a FAILED Phase 3 trial**, the molecule is **WADA-banned and drug-classed with no cosmetic INCI/supply**, and its dominant cellular activity is **growth-arrest/apoptosis**. As a topical cosmetic active it is **not viable** — hence the overall **skip**, which is *more* negative than Ca-AKG/spermidine (those are benign-but-unproven; AICAR is disqualified on legality + supply + safety grounds, not merely thin data).

### Main caveats (what could mislead a reader or copywriter)

1. **Mechanism-vs-molecule trap.** Everything attractive ("AMPK longevity switch / autophagy / exercise mimetic") is **AMPK biology**, mostly demonstrated **systemically or in cell culture** — not topical AICAR on human skin. Don't let "AMPK is good for skin" launder "AICAR is a good skincare ingredient."
2. **The best human data are negative.** RED-CABG [22] failed. "Clinically studied" is a *caution* here.
3. **AICAR is cytotoxic by design** [19][20] — its oncology use is precisely growth-arrest/apoptosis; the safety prior for a leave-on facial active is poor.
4. **Legal/supply disqualifiers** are independent of efficacy: **WADA ban [13], no cosmetic INCI, no cosmetic-grade supply** [3][4].
5. **The two real skin-cell findings are modest and tool-compound** [16][17]; HAS2 induction was "slight," and the AMPK-melanogenesis direction is mixed across studies.

### Claims to never make (topical product)

- ❌ "AMPK activator," "exercise mimetic / exercise in a jar," "activates autophagy/longevity/mitophagy in your skin," "reverses cellular senescence," "acadesine/AICAR" on a cosmetic — unsupported topically and/or drug-/doping-coded.
- ❌ Any "clinically proven" framing (the flagship clinical trial failed; none of it was skin).
- ✅ Defensible path: pursue the **AMPK / cellular-energy narrative** via **cosmetic-legal AMPK-adjacent actives** with their own substantiation — AICAR stays a *research reference*, not an ingredient.
