# Ingredient: Carrot Seed Extract / Oil (INCI: Daucus Carota Sativa Seed Oil)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Retinoids & retinoid alternatives (positioned here as a marketing "natural retinol / pro-vitamin A" adjacent — see honesty note)

> **One-line honest summary:** "Carrot seed extract" is three chemically different materials sold under one folk name — a **carotol-rich essential oil**, a **fatty seed oil (petroselinic acid)**, and a beta-carotene-rich **carrot ROOT macerate** (a different plant part, different INCI). It is a pleasant antioxidant emollient with real but **modest, mostly in-vitro/animal** evidence. Its placement in the "retinoid alternative" class is **marketing, not pharmacology** — the seed material contains essentially **no retinol and little-to-no beta-carotene** (that's the root), and there is **no good human evidence it acts like a retinoid topically**. Two specific claims are actively false-or-overstated: the famous **"SPF 38-40"** and the **"natural retinol"** framing.

---

## 1. Identity

There are **three distinct commercial materials** loosely called "carrot oil / carrot seed extract." Conflating them is the single biggest source of misinformation about this ingredient. The brief's slug ("carrot-seed-extract") and class ("retinoid alternative") sit awkwardly across them, so this dossier covers all three and is explicit about which evidence belongs to which.

| Material | INCI | Plant part / process | Dominant chemistry | "Vitamin A" content |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Carrot seed essential oil** | *Daucus Carota Sativa Seed Oil* (also *Daucus Carota Sativa Seed Extract*) | Seeds, **steam-distilled** | **Carotol ~50-66%**, daucol, daucene, β-bisabolene, sesquiterpenes [3][6][7] | ~None (no carotene; volatile sesquiterpenes) |
| **B. Carrot seed fixed/CO₂ oil** | *Daucus Carota Sativa Seed Oil* | Seeds, **cold-pressed / CO₂** | **Petroselinic acid ~60%**, linoleic ~12%, palmitic ~10%; tocopherols; some carotol/daucol [2][8] | Trace (mostly fatty acids + tocols) |
| **C. Carrot ROOT macerate ("carrot oil")** | *Daucus Carota Sativa (Carrot) Root Extract* / Root Oil | **Root** macerated in a carrier oil (sunflower/olive) | Carrier-oil fatty acids + **β-carotene (pro-vitamin A)** + vitamin E | The only one rich in **β-carotene** [2][9] |

- **Common name(s):** Carrot seed oil; carrot seed essential oil; carrot oil; carrot root oil/macerate; "wild carrot" oil.
- **Botanical:** *Daucus carota* L. (Apiaceae). Both wild (subsp. *carota*) and cultivated (subsp. *sativus*) sources are used; **chemotype/composition vary enormously** by subspecies, geography and process (see §6 stability/sourcing).
- **CAS / EC:** Daucus Carota Sativa Seed Oil — CAS 8015-88-1 (also 84929-61-3); essential oil registries vary. [unverified — confirm against supplier COA]
- **Physical:** Yellow/amber to pale orange-brown; **distinctive earthy, woody-sweet odor** (strong in the essential oil; milder in fixed/CO₂; faint in root macerate). [Wikipedia/Eden Botanicals]
- **Key actives of interest:** *Carotol* (sesquiterpene alcohol, the marker of the essential oil), *daucol*, *petroselinic acid* (fatty acid of the seed fixed oil), *tocopherols/tocotrienols* (~258 mg/kg total tocols in cold-pressed seed oil [8]), plus minor flavones (luteolin glycosides) and, **only in the root material**, β-carotene.

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it plausibly works at the skin/cell level)

The brief classes this as a **"retinoid / retinoid alternative."** That label needs to be challenged head-on, because it drives the most overreaching marketing.

**Mechanistic pillars that are genuinely supported (mostly in-vitro / animal):**
1. **Antioxidant / free-radical scavenging.** Carotol, tocopherols, phenolics and (in the root) β-carotene give moderate DPPH/ABTS/NO-scavenging activity — i.e., it can quench ROS at the skin surface. This is the best-supported mechanism, but the activity is **moderate, not standout** (the wild-carrot EO study found in-chemico antioxidant capacity "not relevant" [4]). [1][4][8]
2. **Anti-inflammatory.** Wild-carrot EO reduced NO release ~20% in LPS-stimulated macrophages at non-toxic doses [4]; in a rat wound model the seed EO lowered TNF-α and IL-1β [5]. Plausible soothing/anti-inflammaging signal, in models only.
3. **Emolliency / barrier-feel (fixed oil only).** The petroselinic/linoleic/oleic fatty acid profile of the *fixed* seed oil behaves like a lightweight occlusive-emollient; linoleic acid is barrier-relevant. This is ordinary plant-oil behavior, not unique to carrot.
4. **Antimicrobial / antifungal.** Carotol and the EO inhibit dermatophytes and some bacteria/fungi [3][4][7] — relevant to preservation/skin-flora narratives, not anti-aging per se.

**The "retinoid" mechanism — what's real vs. wishful:**
- **Retinol content: essentially none.** Carrot *seed* materials do not contain pre-formed vitamin A. The "vitamin A" association comes from **β-carotene, which is in the ROOT**, not the seed.
- **β-carotene → retinol in skin is not established topically.** The popular claim that topical β-carotene is converted by skin into retinol is **not well supported**; evidence is conflicting and there is no human data showing carrot-derived β-carotene drives retinoid-receptor (RAR/RXR) signalling, epidermal turnover, or procollagen the way true retinoids do. Treat "carrot = natural retinol" as **marketing analogy, not pharmacology.** [longevity.technology; multiple vendor sources — none primary]
- **Net:** Carrot seed extract is an **antioxidant/emollient botanical that is *adjacent* to the retinoid story by association, not by mechanism.** A real retinoid-alternative claim belongs to bakuchiol (and arguably the root's β-carotene as a mild pro-vitamin-A antioxidant), not to carrot seed oil.

| **Mitochondrial decline** | No direct skin evidence | — |
| **Glycation / AGEs** | No direct evidence | — |

**Honest read:** Carrot seed extract is best understood as a **broad, gentle antioxidant + soothing emollient** with a side of antimicrobial activity. The headline "anti-aging" hallmarks people *want* it to hit (collagen, photoprotection, retinoid-like renewal) are the ones with the **weakest or actively contradicted** evidence.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** There is **no rigorous human topical RCT** demonstrating that carrot seed extract reduces wrinkles or improves dermal aging endpoints. The human data that exist are **small, single-arm or vehicle-uncontrolled cosmetic-cream studies** in minor journals where carrot's effects were **not statistically distinguishable from the cream base**. The mechanistic and efficacy depth is **in-vitro and animal**, and much of the "anti-aging" reputation rests on a **debunked SPF figure** and a **species/plant-part conflation** (root β-carotene → "natural retinol").

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell, chemical assays, SPF spectrophotometry)

- **Alves-Silva et al., 2016, *Evidence-Based Complementary and Alternative Medicine* (DOI 10.1155/2016/9045196; PMC4769755) — "New Claims for Wild Carrot EO."** Carotol-rich wild-carrot seed EO: antibacterial (Gram-positive MIC 0.32-0.64 µL/mL), antifungal vs *Cryptococcus*/dermatophytes; **anti-inflammatory** (~20% ↓NO in LPS-stimulated macrophages at non-toxic dose); **antioxidant capacity "not relevant"** by two in-chemico methods. Cytotoxicity profiled on **keratinocytes (HaCaT), A549, RAW 264.7 macrophages, HepG2** — safe **below 0.64 µL/mL**. *Limitation: cell/biochemical models; modest antioxidant; no aging endpoints.* [4]
- **Sieniawska et al., 2016, *Industrial Crops and Products* (S0926669016305027) — "Carrot seed essential oil—source of carotol and cytotoxicity study."** Carotol the main constituent across Moroccan/French/Polish oils; **moderate, non-selective cytotoxicity** on VERO (monkey kidney) and FaDu (pharynx carcinoma): Moroccan/French ~35.3-46.1 µg/mL, Polish ~70.9-96.3 µg/mL. *Relevance: defines a no-effect/irritation ceiling — keep use levels low; carotol is not selectively "anti-cancer," it's broadly cytotoxic at high dose.* [6]
- **Singh et al., 2019, *Journal of Cosmetic and Laser Therapy* (PubMed 29737890) — cosmetic emulsions.** Carrot seed oil emulsions at 2/4/6% w/v: good colloidal stability (zeta −43 to −48 mV), DPPH/NO antioxidant activity, and **in-vitro SPF max 6.92 at 6%**. *This is the key, credible SPF datapoint — it reassessed the molecule in isolation and found ~7, not ~40.* [1]
- **Akhtar/Saraf-lineage nanoemulgel work (Int. J. Applied Pharmaceutics, 2022) — "Carrot Seed Oil Nanoemulgels as natural sunscreen and anti-aging."** 6% formulation: SPF 6.92, UVB "medium," UVA PA++; small droplet size (~338 nm) claimed to aid delivery. *Limitation: in-vitro SPF/photometry only; "anti-aging" is inferred, not measured on humans; corroborates the ~7 SPF ceiling, not the 38-40 myth.* [11]
- **Tyrosinase/melanin screens (various).** *Daucus carota* materials (often **root/shoot**, not seed) show melanin-synthesis/diphenolase inhibition in screening panels — a weak pigmentation signal that is **not specific to the seed oil**. [tyrosinase literature; not carrot-seed-specific]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)

- **Venkatesan et al., 2025, *Pharmaceuticals (Basel)* 18(12):1905 (DOI 10.3390/ph18121905; PMC12735670) — rat excision wound.** Carrot seed EO (carotol 21.9%) as **10% w/w ointment** in albino rats (n=6/group, 21 d): 100% wound closure by d21 (vs 99.12% silver sulfadiazine); ↓ROS/MDA, ↑GSH/SOD; ↓TNF-α/IL-1β/CD68; restored caspase-3-mediated apoptotic balance. *Limitation: rat acute wound (not chronic photoaged human skin); single 10% dose, no dose-response; Nrf2/NF-κB not directly measured; no human data.* [5]
- **Daucus carota vs UV-DMBA skin carcinogenesis in mice (ResearchGate; verify primary).** Carrot extract reported protective against UV/chemical (DMBA) skin carcinogenesis in mice — supports an **antioxidant/chemoprotective** narrative, not a cosmetic anti-wrinkle claim. *Limitation: chemoprevention model, mouse; not the seed oil specifically; [partially unverified].* [12]
- **Antifertility/reproductive-toxicity animal work (oral).** *D. carota* **seed** extracts (oral, rats/mice) show anti-implantation, anti-estrogenic/estrogen-receptor-modulating and abortifacient effects — historically used as a folk contraceptive. *Not a skin study, but a real safety flag for the pregnancy section (§7).* [13][14]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL

- **No meaningful human oral skin-aging trials for carrot *seed*.** β-carotene (the **root/dietary** form) has its own large oral literature (antioxidant; the ATBC/CARET lung-cancer signal in smokers is a famous *caution* for high-dose β-carotene supplementation), but that belongs to dietary β-carotene, **not topical carrot seed extract**, and should not be imported into skin claims. [general β-carotene literature]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters — and it's thin and low-quality)

- **Carrot seed oil anti-aging cream (Indonesian pharmacy study, ~2022, ResearchGate 366002785).** A **9% carrot seed oil** cream was the best-performing of the concentrations tested, with reported ↑skin water content, ↓skin hardness, ↓pore size, ↓blemishes, ↓wrinkles over the test period. *Limitation: small, instrumental-only, design/controls weak; minor journal; treat as preliminary, not proof.* [10]
- **Carrot *extract* cream, 4-week volunteer study.** Reported improvements — hydration +76.74%, pore −63.15%, spot −62.22%, wrinkle −63.63% — **BUT explicitly "not statistically significant vs the cream base,"** and the authors conclude antioxidant activity is "relatively mild" and that "further controlled trials, including placebo groups, are needed." *This is the most honest human datapoint: the benefits did not beat vehicle.* [1/related]
- **No published double-blind, vehicle-controlled, adequately powered human RCT** demonstrating carrot seed extract reduces wrinkles, improves elasticity, or remodels dermal collagen. The commonly cited human "studies" are cosmetic-formulation evaluations, not efficacy RCTs.

**Bottom line by tier:** Proven → in-vitro antioxidant/antimicrobial/anti-inflammatory; animal wound healing (rat). **Not proven for human topical anti-aging** — the human work is small, mostly uncontrolled, and at least one study found **no statistical separation from vehicle**. Any anti-wrinkle/retinoid/photoprotection claim today is borrowed from mechanism, animals, or marketing.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Fixed seed oil (B):** Behaves like other plant oils — fatty acids partition into the stratum corneum lipids and deliver emolliency at the surface/upper SC. Standard, well-understood; no special penetration claims warranted.
- **Essential oil (A) / carotol:** Carotol is a small, lipophilic sesquiterpene alcohol → reasonable skin partitioning *prospects*, but **no published human transdermal/penetration quantification** showing how much reaches viable epidermis/dermis or what it does there at cosmetic levels. Lipophilicity that aids penetration also raises the **irritation/sensitization** ceiling (see §7).
- **β-carotene (root material, C):** Large, highly lipophilic, **poorly penetrating and unstable**; even if some enters, **topical conversion to retinol is unproven**. This is the crux of why the "natural retinol" claim fails at the bioavailability step, not just the content step.
- **Practical read:** Penetration is *plausible-but-unproven* for the actives that matter; the realistic delivered benefit is **surface antioxidant + emollient feel**, not deep dermal retinoid-style remodeling.

## 6. Formulation

- **Which material to use:** For a longevity/anti-aging cream, the **cold-pressed/CO₂ fixed seed oil (B)** is the most sensible base-friendly choice (emollient, antioxidant, low scent). The **essential oil (A)** is a potent aromatic active — use at **fraction-of-a-percent** levels for scent/antioxidant accent, never as a "treatment dose." The **root macerate (C)** is the one to reach for if you specifically want the β-carotene color/antioxidant story (and it tints product orange).
- **Effective concentration:** No validated human efficacy dose. Formulator references suggest **fixed seed oil 1-10%**, root macerate **5-20%**, and essential oil **well under 1%** (often 0.1-0.5%) for scent/irritation control. In-vitro/animal work used 6-10% of the seed oil/EO in finished forms [1][5][10]. Formulate conservatively; do not imply a proven dose-response.
- **pH:** Oils are not pH-active; finished carrot-seed emulsions in studies sat around **pH 6.7-7.7** [1] — fine for an emollient cream but note that's above the skin's ideal acid-mantle range; buffer the overall formula to ~5-5.5.
- **Stability:** **This is a real weakness.** Unsaturated fatty acids (petroselinic, linoleic) + carotenoids/sesquiterpenes are **oxidation-prone** → rancidity, color shift, odor drift. Tocopherols help but aren't enough. **Composition varies wildly by chemotype/origin/process** (wild vs cultivated; oleic- vs petroselinic-dominant), so two "carrot seed oils" can perform differently. Mitigate: antioxidant package (added tocopherol/rosemary), opaque/airless packaging, ~**12-month** shelf-life expectation, real-time + accelerated stability and challenge testing. [2][8][formulator guide]
- **Pairings (conceptual):** Other antioxidant/emollient oils (rosehip, sea buckthorn, argan, jojoba), **bakuchiol** (the actual retinoid-alternative if you want that story), niacinamide, vitamin E. Its earthy scent pairs into "botanical/herbal" sensory directions.
- **Conflicts:** No hard chemical incompatibilities, but its **strong earthy odor** can fight a clean/fragrance-free positioning, and its oxidative instability argues against pairing with other highly unsaturated oils unless well-stabilized.
- **Sensory:** Earthy/woody/carroty; can read "natural/herbal" to some, "off/vegetal" to others. Keep low; consider masking. Comedogenicity is **disputed** — formulator references rate the fixed oil low (~1), while some popular sources cite 4-5; treat as **uncertain** and test on acne-prone panels. [formulator guide vs popular sources]

## 7. Safety

- **General topical safety:** Broadly considered safe as a cosmetic ingredient at low levels; CIR/plant-derived-fatty-acid-oil and EWG entries treat carrot seed oil as low-concern. Cytotoxicity studies put a **no-irritation ceiling well below ~0.64 µL/mL for the EO** in cell models [4][6] — i.e., **the essential oil is bioactive enough to be cytotoxic at high concentrations**, reinforcing low use levels.
- **Irritation / sensitization:** The **essential oil** is a fragrant, terpene-rich material → real potential for irritation/sensitization in sensitive skin; the fixed/macerate forms are gentler. No robust human HRIPT/patch data found for cosmetic use — **generate our own**.
- **Phototoxicity / sun:** *Daucus carota* is in the **Apiaceae** family (which famously includes furanocoumarin-containing, phototoxic plants like bergamot/angelica/parsnip). Carrot seed oil is generally regarded as **non-phototoxic**, but (a) **confirm furanocoumarin levels per COA**, and (b) crucially, **do NOT position it as sun protection** — its real SPF is ~6-7 [1][11], far below the debunked "38-40," and relying on it as a sunscreen is a genuine consumer-safety problem.
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** **Meaningful caution.** *D. carota* **seed** has documented **anti-implantation, anti-estrogenic and abortifacient activity in animals** (oral) and a folk-contraceptive history [13][14]; aromatherapy references (Tisserand) contraindicate **carrot seed essential oil** in pregnancy/breastfeeding. Topical cosmetic exposure is far lower than those oral animal doses, but the prudent stance is **avoid the essential oil in pregnancy-targeted products and advise "consult your doctor."**
- **Sourcing/allergen:** Botanical, variable; terpenes (e.g., limonene/linalool depending on chemotype) may be EU-labelable fragrance allergens — check COA. Apiaceae cross-sensitivity is theoretically possible for carrot-allergic individuals.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Status:** A recognized cosmetic ingredient (INCI *Daucus Carota Sativa Seed Oil* / *...Root Extract*); functions = skin-conditioning/emollient, antioxidant, fragrance. The essential oil also functions as fragrance — triggers allergen labeling rules.
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** The two danger zones are unique to this ingredient:
  1. **SPF/sun-protection claims** = **drug** claims (and factually false at ~SPF 6-7). **Never** claim or imply carrot seed oil is a sunscreen or has "SPF 38-40." This is the #1 legal-and-truth trap.
  2. **"Natural retinol" / "boosts collagen" / "anti-wrinkle"** = structure/function (drug-adjacent) claims that the **evidence does not support** for carrot seed material.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "antioxidant-rich botanical oil," "nourishing/emollient," "helps protect the look of skin from environmental stress," "supports a healthy-looking, soft skin feel," "rich in carotol and skin-loving fatty acids." Hedge even these (mechanistic/marketing framing, not proven outcomes).
- ⚠️ **Risky / substantiation-or-legal-review:** "natural SPF," "natural sunscreen," "SPF 38-40," "natural retinol," "boosts collagen," "reverses wrinkles," "converts to vitamin A in your skin." None are supported; the first three are also **false/dangerous**.
- **Substantiation reality:** To make even mild topical performance claims, we'd need **our own finished-formula human testing**; the literature won't substantiate anything beyond "antioxidant/emollient feel." See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Forms/suppliers:** Widely available from natural-oil/aromatherapy houses (Eden Botanicals, etc.) and cosmetic raw-material suppliers; essential oil, cold-pressed/CO₂ fixed oil, and root macerate are sold separately — **specify exactly which** on the spec sheet (different INCI, different chemistry).
- **Cost tier:** **Low-to-mid.** The essential oil (high carotol, labor-intensive distillation, low yield) is the priciest per gram but used at tiny levels; fixed seed oil and root macerate are inexpensive.
- **Supply/standardization risk:** **High variability** — wild vs cultivated, geography, chemotype (carotol- vs β-bisabolene-dominant; oleic- vs petroselinic-dominant fatty profile) [2][3][6][8]. **Require a COA** quantifying carotol (for EO) / fatty-acid profile (for fixed oil) / furanocoumarins, and pick **one consistent supplier+chemotype**. Cosmetic-grade, sustainably sourced lots preferred.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **"SPF 38-40 natural sunscreen" — FALSE and unsafe.** Traces to **Kapoor & Saraf 2009**, which tested a **multi-ingredient commercial herbal sunscreen** (with mineral filters), not carrot in isolation. Isolated reassessment (Singh 2019) puts carrot seed oil at **SPF ~6.9** [1][11]. The Tisserand Institute and formulator references explicitly debunk this and warn against relying on it as sun protection. [Tisserand; 1; 11]
- **"Natural retinol" — category error.** Carrot **seed** has no retinol and little β-carotene (that's the **root**); topical β-carotene→retinol conversion is unproven; no human data show retinoid-receptor or turnover effects. It is *adjacent by association*, not by mechanism.
- **"Rich in vitamin A" — usually the wrong material.** The vitamin-A (β-carotene) story belongs to **carrot root macerate**, yet it's routinely slapped onto **seed oil** products.
- **"Clinically proven anti-aging."** The human cream studies are small, mostly uncontrolled, and at least one found **no significant difference vs the cream base** [1] — the opposite of "proven."
- **"Powerful antioxidant."** Moderate at best; one wild-carrot EO study found antioxidant capacity "not relevant" by in-chemico methods [4]. Real, but not a standout.
- **"Anti-cancer carotol."** Carotol's cytotoxicity is **moderate and non-selective** (it kills normal cells too) [6] — not a cosmetic benefit and not a marketing point.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL — a pleasant, on-trend botanical accent, NOT an evidence-backed anti-aging hero, and NOT a true retinoid alternative.**

**One-line:** Use carrot seed **fixed oil** (or root macerate for the β-carotene color/story) as a **secondary antioxidant-emollient** with honest "nourishing/environmental-defense feel" copy — but do **not** build the product or its claims around it, **never** imply SPF/sun protection, and **don't** sell it as "natural retinol."

**Why supporting/conditional, not hero or skip:**
- *Reasons to include:* clean-beauty appeal, gentle, low cost, real (if modest) antioxidant/anti-inflammatory/emollient activity, good sensory "botanical" story, pairs into a natural anti-aging oil blend.
- *Reasons not to lead with it:* no human RCT support; weakest evidence is on the exact hallmarks marketing assigns it (collagen, photoprotection, retinoid-like renewal); oxidative-stability and scent headaches; two of its famous selling points are **false** (SPF) or **unsupported** (natural retinol); pregnancy caution for the EO.
- *Condition for any "retinoid alternative" claim:* don't make one for carrot seed. If the brand wants a credible natural-retinoid story, that is **bakuchiol's** lane — pair them and let bakuchiol carry the claim while carrot plays sensory/antioxidant support.

**Longevity-relevance note:** For a *skin-longevity* brand whose credibility is "evidence over hype," carrot seed extract is a **cautionary mascot of the natural-skincare myth machine** (the SPF and natural-retinol legends are textbook overreach). It can earn a quiet supporting role as an antioxidant emollient, but its highest-value use to *this* brand might actually be **editorial** — a credibility-building "here's what the evidence really says about carrot oil" myth-buster that demonstrates our claims discipline.
