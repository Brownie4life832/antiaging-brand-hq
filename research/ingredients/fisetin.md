# Ingredient: Fisetin (INCI: **Fisetin**)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** NAD+ / sirtuin / AMPK / cellular-energy & senolytic actives · **Slug:** `fisetin`

> Exhaustive literature review. Brutally honest on evidence quality. Fisetin's "fame" rests on
> **oral, systemic, mostly-mouse** senolytic data (lifespan extension in aged mice; Mayo Clinic
> human frailty/COVID trials). Its **skin** evidence is real but sits almost entirely at the
> **in-vitro and animal (mouse) topical** tiers — there is **no published human topical RCT** on a
> fisetin cosmetic as of June 2026. Most "removes 66% of senescent skin cells / +41% collagen"
> marketing is **borrowed from in-vitro + mouse-graft data**, not earned in living human skin.
> Treat every "senolytic skincare that clears your zombie skin cells" line as marketing-grade.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Fisetin. Chemically **3,3′,4′,7-tetrahydroxyflavone** — a **flavonol** (the same flavonoid sub-class as quercetin, myricetin, kaempferol). [1][2]
- **INCI:** **FISETIN** (recognized cosmetic INCI; EINECS 208-434-4). Listed cosmetic functions: *skin conditioning* and *cosmetic colorant* (it is a yellow pigment). [10]
- **CAS:** 528-48-3. **MW:** ~286.2 g/mol. **LogP ≈ 2.0–3.2** (lipophilic); aqueous solubility very low (~10 µg/mL). [8]
- **Natural sources:** strawberries (the richest common source, ~160 µg/g), apples, persimmons, grapes, onions, cucumbers, kiwi. Typical dietary intake is tiny (~0.4 mg/day estimated in Japan). [1] Commercial fisetin is most often extracted from the **smoke tree / *Rhus* / *Cotinus coggygria*** (a traditional yellow dye plant) or from *Toxicodendron* species, then purified.
- **Key forms / derivatives:**
  - **Free fisetin (aglycone)** — the form in nearly all studies and supplements.
  - **Liposomal / hybrid-hydrogel / cyclodextrin-complexed / nano-encapsulated fisetin** — bioavailability-boosting formats developed because raw fisetin is so poorly absorbed and unstable (see §5, §6). [8][12]
  - **Fisetin glycosides** (e.g. fisetin-4′-glucoside) exist in plants but are not the marketed actives.
- **Nomenclature caution:** "Fisetin" is sometimes loosely lumped with **quercetin** (its close structural cousin and the partner senolytic in the famous "D+Q" — Dasatinib+Quercetin — cocktail). They are *different molecules*; do not conflate their data.

## 2. Class & mechanism

Fisetin is a **polyphenolic flavonol** that the longevity field classifies as a **senolytic** — i.e. a compound that *selectively kills existing senescent ("zombie") cells* — in contrast to a senomorphic (which merely quiets the senescent phenotype). This is the opposite of how OS-01/Peptide-14 works (that one is senomorphic; see [`senolytics-os01-peptide.md`](senolytics-os01-peptide.md)), so the two are mechanistically complementary, not interchangeable. [1][6]

**Proposed mechanisms (multi-target — fisetin is "dirty" in the pharmacological sense, hitting many pathways):**

- **Senolysis — selective apoptosis of senescent cells.** Senescent cells survive by upregulating anti-apoptotic "SCAP" (senescent-cell anti-apoptotic pathway) networks (BCL-2/BCL-xL, PI3K/AKT, etc.). Fisetin transiently disables these so the senescent cell tips into its own pre-loaded apoptosis. In the skin-specific work, fisetin triggered **caspase-3/8/9-mediated intrinsic and extrinsic apoptosis** selectively in senescent dermal fibroblasts. [6] This is the "hit-and-run" model: a short pulse clears cells, and benefit persists after the drug is gone (consistent with fisetin's very short half-life). [1]
- **Nrf2/ARE antioxidant activation.** Fisetin is described as a *master* Nrf2 activator: it stabilizes/translocates Nrf2 to the nucleus, switching on endogenous antioxidant enzymes (SOD, catalase, glutathione system, HO-1). This is its dominant antioxidant mechanism — indirect (boosting the cell's own defenses) more than direct radical scavenging. [13][3]
- **Anti-inflammatory: NF-κB / MAPK suppression.** Fisetin inhibits NF-κB and the MAPK (p38/JNK/ERK)–AP-1 axis, lowering COX-2, IL-6, TNF-α, PGE₂, and NO. In aged tissue this also means **reduced SASP** (senescence-associated secretory phenotype: MMPs, IL-6, IL-8, MCP-1) — the inflammatory broth senescent cells pump out. [3][6][9]
- **MMP suppression / ECM protection.** By blocking ROS→MAPK→AP-1, fisetin **downregulates MMP-1, MMP-2, MMP-3, MMP-9** (the collagen/elastin-degrading enzymes driven by UV), preserving dermal collagen. [4][9]
- **Cellular-energy / longevity signaling.** Reviews report fisetin modulates **SIRT1, AMPK, and mTOR** and supports mitochondrial function — the reason it is filed under the NAD+/sirtuin/AMPK "cellular-energy" class — though these are mostly non-skin/systemic findings. [1]

**Net cell-level story (as claimed):** clear a fraction of senescent fibroblasts → quiet the SASP → less MMP-driven collagen loss + less inflammaging → plus a standing Nrf2 antioxidant shield against UV/ROS. Most of this chain is demonstrated in **cells and mice**, not in human topical skin (§4).

## 4. Evidence review by tier  ← core of this dossier

**Overarching honesty flag.** Fisetin has an unusually *deep* preclinical and systemic-human file (it is a leading geroscience candidate, championed by Mayo Clinic's Kirkland/Robbins groups), but a **shallow skin-topical file**. The deep data are **oral/systemic and mouse**; the skin data are **in-vitro + mouse-topical + one mouse/human chimeric graft**. There is, as of 2026-06, **no published randomized human topical trial of a fisetin cosmetic.** Keep the tiers strictly separated.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro (cells)

**Yousefzadeh et al. (2018), *EBioMedicine* — the seminal senolytic-screening paper.** [1]
- Screened **10 flavonoids** (resveratrol, fisetin, luteolin, rutin, EGCG, curcumin, myricetin, apigenin, catechin, quercetin) for senolytic potency. **Fisetin was the most potent**, beating quercetin at 5 µM at reducing SA-β-gal+ cells.
- Dose-dependent reduction of senescence markers in murine embryonic fibroblasts (oxidative-stress-induced) and human **IMR90** fibroblasts (etoposide-induced) across ~1–15 µM.
- *Limitation here:* IMR90 are lung, not skin, fibroblasts.

**Jang et al. (2023), *Int. J. Molecular Sciences* — UVA in human skin cells.** [9]
- **Human dermal fibroblasts (HDFs) + human epidermal keratinocytes (HEKs)**, fisetin at **2.5–20 µM** (no cytotoxicity <10 µM).
- ↓ UVA-induced **MMP-1 and MMP-3**; downregulated **NOX1-5** and **ROS** (comparable to NAC/DPI controls); inhibited **p38/JNK phosphorylation** and **AP-1** nuclear translocation, dose-dependently.
- *Limitation:* **cell culture only**, no animal/human validation; authors note critical-ROS-threshold caveats.

**Mechanistic antioxidant / anti-inflammatory cell work.** Multiple papers: Nrf2/ARE as the "master pathway" for fisetin's cellular antioxidant activity [13]; ↓ TNF-α-induced inflammation and ↓ H₂O₂ damage in keratinocytes [9 context]; anti-melanogenesis in melanoma cells (↓ tyrosinase, MITF, melanin at 20–40 µM via PKCα/β-catenin/ERK) — **Ei et al. (2025), *IJMS***. [14]

**Bottom line, Tier 1:** strong, internally consistent in-vitro story — fisetin is a genuine senolytic, a real Nrf2/antioxidant activator, and an MMP/SASP suppressor in human skin cells. All at micromolar concentrations *in a dish*.

### Tier 2 — Animal (mouse)

**Yousefzadeh et al. (2018), *EBioMedicine* — systemic, oral.** [1]
- Aged wild-type and progeroid *Ercc1*⁻^/Δ mice; fisetin dietary (500 ppm ≈ 60 mg/kg/day) or gavage (100 mg/kg × 5 days).
- **Extended median and maximum lifespan** when started late in life (~85+ weeks); restored tissue homeostasis; reduced senescence markers (p16, p21) and SASP (IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α, MCP-1, PAI-1) across fat, spleen, liver, kidney, and immune cells.
- This is the headline "fisetin extends lifespan ~10–20%" data — **oral, systemic, not skin.**

**Wu et al. (2017), *Int. J. Molecular Sciences* — TOPICAL, UVB hairless mice.** [3]  *(The single best topical efficacy study; still rodent.)*
- BALB/c hairless mice, **5 groups of 5** (control / UVB / UVB+vehicle [PEG 400] / UVB+50 µM fisetin / UVB+200 µM fisetin). Fisetin applied **topically, 50 µL daily** after UVB, **3×/week for 10 weeks** (escalating dose to 108 mJ/cm²).
- **Wrinkle score:** UVB 4.6 ± 1.5 → fisetin-200 µM **2.5 ± 1.8** (significant).
- **Epidermal thickness:** UVB 81 µm → fisetin-200 µM reduced by ~62%.
- **Collagen (Masson trichrome):** UVB depleted dermal collagen; topical fisetin **increased** it.
- **Erythema (a\*):** reduced weeks 6–10. **TEWL:** reduced. **Filaggrin & aquaporin:** increased.
- **Mechanism in vivo:** ↑ **Nrf2**, ↓ **MMP-1/MMP-2**, ↓ **COX-2/IL-6/NF-κB** (200 µM > 50 µM).
- *Limitations (honest):* tiny N (5/group); **the PEG 400 vehicle alone showed modest protection** (↓MMP-1/-2, ↓IL-6, ↑filaggrin), confounding the fisetin-specific delta; hairless-mouse skin ≠ human skin; preventive (applied with the UV insult), not a treatment of pre-aged skin.

**Sundarraj et al. (2018) — fisetin-loaded binary ethosomes, UV-exposed mice (skin cancer model).** [12] — demonstrates topical fisetin delivery is feasible and bioactive in mouse skin, but the endpoint is anti-tumor, not cosmetic aging.

**Topical-delivery formulation animal work (2025).** [11] Fisetin nanoparticles/limosomes (Ascorbyl-Palmitate + Hyaluronan-modified) applied to UVB mice: ↓ TNF-α, NF-κB, MMP-9; ↑ SOD, CAT. Confirms the in-vivo mechanism *with* an engineered carrier (see §5).

**Bottom line, Tier 2:** the systemic *lifespan* data are robust and famous (oral). The **topical** mouse data (Wu 2017) are genuinely encouraging for photoaging/barrier — but small, vehicle-confounded, and rodent.

### Tier 3 — Human, ORAL

This is where fisetin has *real* human data — but **none with a skin endpoint.**

- **Mayo Clinic senolytic program (AFFIRM, NCT03675724 and successors).** Oral fisetin pulsed **20 mg/kg/day × 2–3 days** for frailty/inflammation in older adults. Reported **no severe/serious adverse events** at these doses across >50 subjects; efficacy results were not fully unblinded/published as of mid-2026. [5]
- **COVID-FIS (NCT04537299) / COVFIS-HOME — Verdoorn, Kirkland et al., *Aging Cell* (2021), design paper.** [5] Phase-2 placebo-controlled pilots of oral fisetin in older nursing-home adults with COVID-19. Establish a human **safety/tolerability** track record for short-pulse high-dose fisetin. The COVID-FIS efficacy trial was **terminated for futility** (DSMB/NIA, primary completion Aug 2024) — i.e. no COVID benefit; this is a *negative* clinical result worth citing for honesty, though it's a COVID endpoint, not aging/skin.
- **Krishnakumar et al. (2022), *J. Nutritional Science* — human PK crossover.** [8] N=15 healthy adults; single dose; a hybrid-hydrogel fisetin (FF-20) gave **~23× higher Cmax** (238.2 vs 9.97 ng/mL) and **~27× higher AUC** (AUC₀–₁₂ₕ 341.4 vs 12.67) than unformulated fisetin, with **t½ ≈ 1.1–1.5 h** and **no adverse events** (minor bloating in both arms). Confirms (a) fisetin's poor native absorption and (b) very short half-life. *COI: funded by Akay Natural Ingredients; formulation commercialized by Life Extension.*
- **Newer healthy-aging trials in progress** (e.g. 100 mg/day × 7 weeks for inflammation/senescence markers; an industry 108-subject dermatology trial combining fisetin + urolithin A + ergothioneine). [results pending / not yet published — treat as forthcoming, not evidence].

**Bottom line, Tier 3:** humans tolerate short-pulse oral fisetin well; **no published human study shows an oral-fisetin *skin* benefit.** Any "improves skin from within" claim is currently **unsubstantiated**.

### Tier 4 — Human, TOPICAL

**None located.** As of 2026-06 there is **no published randomized (or even uncontrolled) human clinical trial of a topical fisetin cosmetic** with skin-aging endpoints. The closest skin-relevant human-tissue datapoint is **ex-vivo/graft**, not a topical trial:

- **Yamada/Keio group — "Fisetin, a potential skin rejuvenation drug that eliminates senescent cells in the dermis," *Biogerontology* (2023; epub Sep 2023).** [6] **In-vitro + a mouse/human chimeric model**: senescence induced in primary **human dermal fibroblasts** (replicative + ionizing radiation); fisetin selectively eliminated **~66% of senescent HDFs** via caspase-3/8/9 apoptosis. Then **whole-skin grafts from aged human donors were transplanted onto nude mice** and the mice treated with fisetin → grafts showed **↑ collagen density (~+41%)** and **↓ SASP (MMPs, ILs)**; no apparent adverse events.
  - **Crucial honesty point:** this is the source of the viral "removes 66% of senescent skin cells / +41% collagen" numbers. It is **(a) human cells in a dish and (b) human skin grafts on mice treated systemically — NOT a topical product on living human faces.** The route of fisetin in the graft model is not a finished cosmetic.

**What is actually proven for TOPICAL human use:** **nothing yet.** Every topical efficacy datapoint is **rodent** (Wu 2017 [3]; nanocarrier studies [11][12]) or **cell/graft** (Jang [9]; Biogerontology [6]). The human evidence that exists is **oral and non-skin** (safety, frailty, COVID). Bridging "fisetin clears senescent skin cells" to a human face cream is, today, **an inference, not a demonstrated result.**

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

This is fisetin's **central formulation problem** and the biggest gap between hype and deliverable benefit.

- **Poorly water-soluble (~10 µg/mL), lipophilic (logP ~2–3), and chemically unstable** — a tough trio for stable, penetrating delivery. [8]
- **MW ~286 Da** is *below* the rule-of-500, so the molecule itself is small enough to cross the stratum corneum in principle — the limiter is **solubility, loading, and stability**, not size.
- **Native fisetin in a simple base penetrates poorly and degrades**; nearly all serious topical work uses **engineered carriers**:
  - **Ascorbyl-Palmitate + Hyaluronan-modified Poloxamer-407 nanoparticles (2025):** size ~214 nm, entrapment ~96%, **flux ~9.2 µg/cm²/h**, confocal penetration to **~80 µm**. [11]
  - **AP+HYA limosomes (D-limonene phospholipid vesicles, 2025):** size ~82 nm, entrapment ~99%, flux ~10.5 µg/cm²/h, penetration to **~128 µm**. [11]
  - **Transethosomes / binary ethosomes:** improve dermal deposition in optimization + mouse studies. [12]
- **Implication for the brand:** an *off-the-shelf, just-dissolve-some-fisetin* serum will likely deliver little active, oxidize, and discolor. A credible topical fisetin product needs a **solubilization/encapsulation system** (cyclodextrin, liposome/ethosome, or AP/HYA nanocarrier) — which raises cost and formulation complexity. None of these delivery systems has a **human** efficacy trial.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** **Unknown for human topical use.** In-vitro active range is ~2.5–20 µM in cells [9]; mouse-topical used 50–200 µM solutions [3]. There is **no established human cosmetic use level**; current cosmetic use is rare and low (fisetin appears in ~0.01% of products). [10] Formulate empirically and conservatively; do not claim a "clinical dose."
- **pH / stability — the hard part:** fisetin is a **catechol-bearing flavonol that oxidizes readily** (it "decomposes within minutes in solution in the presence of air"), is **light-sensitive**, and is a **substrate for polyphenol oxidase**; its oxidation rate peaks around **pH 5.5**. [15] Practically: formulate **anhydrous or low-water**, with **antioxidant/chelator protection** (it pairs naturally with vitamin C derivatives like ascorbyl palmitate, vitamin E, ferulic acid), **airless/opaque packaging**, and ideally **encapsulation**.
- **Color caveat:** fisetin is a **yellow pigment** (INCI-listed as a colorant) — at meaningful levels it can tint a formula yellow and may stain. [10]
- **Pairings (well-matched):**
  - **Vitamin C derivatives / ferulic acid / vitamin E** — co-antioxidants that also stabilize it (the published nanocarriers literally use ascorbyl palmitate). [11] See [`vitamin-c.md`](vitamin-c.md), [`ferulic-acid.md`](ferulic-acid.md), [`vitamin-e-tocopherols.md`](vitamin-e-tocopherols.md).
  - **Niacinamide / NAD story** — narrative + barrier fit (same cellular-energy class). [`nad-niacinamide-longevity.md`](nad-niacinamide-longevity.md), [`niacinamide.md`](niacinamide.md).
  - **Quercetin** — its senolytic cousin; a "senolytic flavonoid" pairing is conceptually coherent.
  - **Hyaluronan / barrier lipids / ceramides** — used in the delivery systems and supports the barrier-repair thesis. [`ceramides.md`](ceramides.md).
  - **Spermidine / resveratrol** — co-"longevity-science" actives for narrative. [`spermidine.md`](spermidine.md), [`polyphenols-resveratrol.md`](polyphenols-resveratrol.md).
- **Conflicts:** strong oxidizers and high-pH systems accelerate degradation; don't co-formulate naked in a high-water, oxygen-exposed serum. Standard "antioxidant in a protective base" rules apply.
- **Sensory:** as a low-% encapsulated active, sensory is driven by the base; watch for yellow tint and potential gritty feel from undissolved actives if not properly solubilized.

## 7. Safety

- **Oral human safety:** good short-term tolerability at high pulsed doses (20 mg/kg/day × 2–3 days; ~100 mg/day chronic) — **no serious adverse events** reported in Mayo/COVID trials; minor GI effects only. [5][8] (Genotoxicity has been flagged *in vitro at high concentrations* in some reviews — relevant to high oral dosing, not low topical levels.)
- **Topical irritation/sensitization:** **no published human topical safety data** (HRIPT, patch testing) located for a fisetin cosmetic. Plant-flavonol; generally expected low-irritant, but **unproven** — note that commercial fisetin is often **sourced from *Rhus*/*Toxicodendron* (sumac/smoke-tree)** plants whose families include known contact allergens (urushiol), so **botanical-source purity and an allergen/sensitization assessment matter.**
- **Sun sensitivity:** none expected — fisetin is **photoprotective/antioxidant**, not a photosensitizer or exfoliant. (It still does not replace SPF — see [`sunscreen-photoprotection.md`](sunscreen-photoprotection.md).)
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** **no topical safety data**; default to conservative "consult your doctor" copy. Do not market as pregnancy-safe.
- **Drug interactions (oral context):** fisetin is glucuronidated and may interact with warfarin/CYP substrates — a *supplement* caution, largely moot for low topical use but worth knowing if a brand ever considers an ingestible.
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** plant-derived (smoke tree / sumac / fruit by-products); broadly a clean, plant-provenance story **if** source botanical and purity are controlled.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

**The line:** under US FDA, intended use decides cosmetic vs drug. Claiming a product **alters the structure/function of skin** ("removes senescent cells," "clears zombie cells," "rebuilds collagen," "reverses aging") makes it a **drug** requiring approval — there is **no OTC monograph for anti-aging/anti-wrinkle**. Fisetin is a legitimate **cosmetic INCI** (skin-conditioning/colorant) [10], but the *claims* are the exposure.

- ✅ **Safe cosmetic language:** "antioxidant-rich flavonol," "helps defend against environmental/oxidative stress," "supports the look of firmer, more even-toned skin," "helps visibly improve photo-aged skin's appearance," "inspired by cellular-senescence / skin-longevity science."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like — avoid or legal-review:**
  - "**senolytic**" / "**clears/removes senescent (zombie) cells from your skin**" — pharmacological structure/function claim; and in humans-topical it's **unproven** → doubly risky.
  - "**removes 66% of senescent skin cells / boosts collagen 41%**" — these numbers are from **in-vitro + mouse-graft** work [6]; quoting them on a face cream is misleading substantiation.
  - "**extends your skin's lifespan / reverses skin aging**" — structure/function + borrows oral/mouse lifespan data.
  - "**reduces inflammation / repairs DNA**" — drug-adjacent.
- **"Senolytic" / "senotherapeutic"** are science/education terms, not regulated cosmetic claims — usable *educationally* if paired with cosmetic-appropriate benefit language and never implying disease treatment or a verified clearing of cells in human skin.
- **EU/UK:** FISETIN is in CosIng; claims must meet the EU Cosmetics Claims Regulation (evidential support, no misleading "biological/skin age" promises). [10]

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Raw material:** widely available as **≥98% fisetin powder** from nutraceutical/cosmetic suppliers (extracted from smoke tree/*Cotinus*, sumac, or synthesized). **Mid cost tier** as a raw flavonol — far cheaper per gram than peptides/exosomes — **but** the cost-relevant unit is the *delivered, stabilized* active, which requires encapsulation/solubilization and pushes formulation cost up.
- **Identity/purity caveat:** confirm botanical source and a **urushiol/allergen-free, low-heavy-metal** spec (sumac-family provenance). Standardize on assayed fisetin %, not just "smoke-tree extract."
- **Supply:** riding the oral-senolytic supplement boom (Life Extension, DoNotAge, etc.), so raw supply is healthy; cosmetic-grade, encapsulated forms are less commoditized.
- **No dominant IP lock on fisetin itself** (it's a natural molecule), though specific **delivery systems/formulations** and the senolytic *use* may carry patents — FTO review needed if leaning on a specific carrier or a senolytic-skincare claim.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

| Marketing claim | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Senolytic skincare — clears the zombie/senescent cells in your skin" | Fisetin **is** a senolytic in cells/mice [1][6], but **no human topical study** shows it clears senescent cells in living skin. **Unproven for the format.** |
| "Removes 66% of senescent skin cells / +41% collagen" | From **in-vitro HDFs + aged-human-skin grafts on mice** (systemic treatment) [6] — **not a topical cream on human faces.** |
| "Extends your skin's lifespan" | Lifespan extension is **oral, in mice** [1]; no skin-lifespan endpoint in humans. |
| "Reverses photoaging / rebuilds collagen" | ↓ wrinkles + ↑ collagen shown **topically in hairless mice** [3] (small N, vehicle-confounded); promising but **rodent**, and "rebuilds collagen" is a drug claim. |
| "Powerful antioxidant that protects skin" | Best-supported claim: real Nrf2/antioxidant + MMP/NF-κB suppression in cells and mouse skin [13][3][9] — but still not human-trial-confirmed for a finished product. |
| "Clinically proven anti-aging skincare ingredient" | **No human topical clinical trial exists** (2026-06). "Clinically studied" applies to **oral** fisetin for frailty/COVID — and the COVID trial **failed for futility.** |
| "Better than retinol" | No head-to-head human data; unsupported. |

**Independent reality:** fisetin is one of the most *credible* longevity molecules at the **systemic/oral** level — but its **skincare** story is **mechanistically strong, clinically (topically) unproven.** The category is "exciting preclinical senolytic," and marketing has run far ahead of human topical evidence.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **Conditional — excellent science-forward *supporting* / narrative active and a strong antioxidant; NOT an evidence-backed topical hero (yet).**

- **Why it's tempting:** fisetin is *the* most decorated natural **senolytic**, with genuine mouse-lifespan and human-safety pedigree from Mayo Clinic — an unbeatable "skin-longevity science" hook. It maps perfectly onto a repair/renewal/anti-senescence thesis and pairs cleanly with our antioxidant and barrier actives. As an **antioxidant (Nrf2 activator) + MMP/SASP suppressor**, it has real, if non-human-trial, photoaging rationale.
- **Why it's only conditional:** the headline senolytic numbers are **in-vitro and mouse-graft**, the lifespan/safety data are **oral**, and there is **zero published human topical efficacy.** On top of that, fisetin is **hard to formulate** (oxidizes fast, light-sensitive, poorly soluble, yellow-staining) and likely needs **encapsulation** to deliver anything. The honest human-topical story today is: *"a promising antioxidant flavonol; senolytic skin benefits are unproven in people."*
- **Longevity relevance:** **Very high in narrative, unproven in topical effect.** It is arguably the single best *educational/story* asset in the senolytic class (real, famous, Mayo-backed longevity molecule) — but our differentiation (claims honesty) demands we sell the **antioxidant + science-inspired senescence** angle, never "clears your senescent cells / +41% collagen."
- **Strategic fit:** best deployed as a **co-supporting longevity active** alongside an evidence-stronger hero (e.g. a retinoid for proven photoaging, or GHK-Cu/OS-01 for the peptide-longevity angle), contributing antioxidant defense + a credible senescence narrative. Could anchor a "senolytic-inspired antioxidant" SKU **only with** (1) an **encapsulated/stabilized** delivery system, (2) our own modest substantiation, and (3) strictly cosmetic claims.
- **Gating conditions before any "senolytic" framing:** (1) lock a **stable, penetrating delivery** form; (2) confirm **urushiol/allergen-free, assayed** raw material; (3) claims strategy that uses "antioxidant / supports / visibly / inspired by senescence science" and **never** "clears senescent cells / reverses skin aging / +X% collagen."

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Independent adversarial re-check of every headline claim and its cited source (primary sources fetched directly: PMC, PubMed, ClinicalTrials.gov, Cambridge Core).

### Overall evidence grade: **C** (weak / indirect for the topical-cosmetic use that matters)

Split by route, because the dossier's central honesty move — separating the tiers — holds up under scrutiny:

- **Oral / systemic human safety:** ~B (real human trials, decent N, consistent tolerability).
- **Systemic mouse lifespan / senolysis:** ~B (robust, famous, reproduced).
- **Topical / skin efficacy in humans:** **D** (zero human topical trials; everything is in-vitro, mouse-topical, or graft).
- **Blended grade for the brand's actual question ("does a topical fisetin cosmetic work on a human face?"): C**, and only that high because the *mechanistic* and *mouse-topical* scaffolding is genuinely strong. There is **no human-topical RCT**, so it cannot reach A or B.

This dossier is, unusually, **not** guilty of the borrowing it warns about — it leads with the caveat that the famous numbers are oral/mouse/graft and repeatedly re-flags it. The grade is low because the *underlying evidence* is thin for the format, not because the write-up is dishonest.

### Main caveats

1. **No human topical evidence exists.** The single most important fact. Every topical efficacy datapoint is rodent or graft; every human datapoint is oral and non-skin. The bridge from "senolytic in a dish/mouse" to "works on a human face" is **inference, not data**.
2. **The viral marketing numbers are borrowed.** "66% senescent cells cleared / +41% collagen" come from in-vitro HDFs + **intraperitoneally**-dosed human-skin grafts on mice [6] — not a cream, not a human face, not even a topical route. Quoting them on a product would be misleading substantiation.
3. **The best topical study is small and confounded.** Wu 2017 [3]: N=5/group, hairless-mouse skin, preventive (co-applied with UV), and the PEG 400 **vehicle alone** lowered MMP-1/-2 and raised filaggrin — eroding the fisetin-specific delta.
4. **The strongest human data are oral — and the marquee oral efficacy trial FAILED.** COVID-FIS was terminated for futility. Human trials establish *safety/tolerability*, not skin (or even COVID) *efficacy*.
5. **Key human PK data are industry-funded and tiny.** [8] is N=15, funded by the ingredient maker (Akay) whose formulation Life Extension sells. Treat the bioavailability multipliers as sponsor-reported.
6. **Formulation reality undercuts even the mechanistic case.** Fisetin oxidizes within minutes in air, is light-sensitive, poorly soluble, and yellow-staining; meaningful topical delivery needs encapsulation — and **no encapsulated delivery system has a human efficacy trial** either.
7. **Sourcing allergen flag is legitimate.** *Rhus/Toxicodendron* (sumac/smoke-tree) provenance warrants a urushiol-free, assayed spec — correctly noted.

### Claims that should be softened (and the standard to hold)

The dossier's own §8/§10 already rule these out for marketing; restating as the verification standard, **none of the following is defensible** and must never appear as product copy:

- "Senolytic skincare / clears (zombie) senescent cells in your skin" — unproven for topical human use; structure/function (drug) claim.
- "Removes 66% of senescent skin cells / +41% collagen" — borrowed in-vitro + IP-graft numbers; misleading on a cream.
- "Clinically proven anti-aging ingredient" — **no human topical trial exists**; the only human trials are oral, and the efficacy one failed.
- "Extends your skin's lifespan / reverses skin aging / rebuilds collagen" — borrows oral mouse-lifespan data; drug-territory.
- "Better than retinol" — no head-to-head data.

**Defensible framing only:** "antioxidant flavonol (Nrf2 activator)," "helps defend against oxidative/environmental stress," "inspired by cellular-senescence / skin-longevity science," "supports the look of firmer, more even-toned skin" — paired with explicit honesty that human-topical senolytic benefit is not yet demonstrated.

**One internal-text softening applied:** the PK figure "~24× Cmax" was corrected to "~23×" (238.2 vs 9.97 ng/mL) to match the source. No efficacy claim in the dossier body required softening — the document was already appropriately hedged ("animal," "in-vitro," "unproven," "[unverified]") at each tier, which the audit confirms is the correct posture.
