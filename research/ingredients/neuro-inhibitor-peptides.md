# Ingredient: Other neuro-inhibitor peptides — Leuphasyl, Syn-Ake, Vialox, "Tripeptide-3" (INCI: Pentapeptide-18 · Dipeptide Diaminobutyroyl Benzylamide Diacetate · Pentapeptide-3 · Tripeptide-3)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering (leaning ⚠️ conditional / supporting — same conclusion as the Argireline class) · **Class:** Peptides (neurotransmitter-inhibiting / "neuromodulator" / "topical Botox-mimic" peptides)

> **Scope & relationship to the Argireline dossier.** This is the *companion class dossier* to [`argireline-neuropeptides.md`](argireline-neuropeptides.md). Argireline (Acetyl Hexapeptide-8) and its SNAP-25-mimic cousins (SNAP-8, AH-30) are covered there. **This file covers the *other* synthetic "muscle-relaxing" peptides** that are routinely stacked with Argireline in "peptide complex" marketing but act on **different molecular targets**:
> - **Leuphasyl® / Pentapeptide-18** — *enkephalin (opioid-receptor) mimic*, presynaptic.
> - **Syn-Ake® / Dipeptide Diaminobutyroyl Benzylamide Diacetate** — *waglerin-1 (snake-venom) mimic*, postsynaptic nicotinic-receptor antagonist.
> - **Vialox® / Pentapeptide-3** — *tubocurarine (curare) mimic*, postsynaptic nicotinic-receptor antagonist.
> - **"Tripeptide-3"** — included in the brief, but **the name is a mess** (see §1). It is *not* a single agreed molecule; in marketing it is most often used as a loose synonym for Syn-Ake, while the official INCI "Tripeptide-3" is a different (Gly-Ser-Val) peptide with essentially no neuro literature.
>
> **One-sentence honest headline:** these are mechanistically clever, well-tolerated peptides whose entire human efficacy record is **supplier-generated** (Lipotec, Pentapharm/DSM, Centerchem) — there is **no independent, peer-reviewed, placebo-controlled topical RCT** isolating any one of them, and the same near-zero topical penetration problem that sinks Argireline applies in full here.

### 1d. "Tripeptide-3" — naming caution (read this before using the term)

- The **official INCI "Tripeptide-3"** is a tripeptide of **glycine-serine-valine** (Gly-Ser-Val per INCI Beauty/SpecialChem/COSMILE) with **essentially no published neuromodulator literature.** It is a generic skin-conditioning peptide.
- In **marketing and several ingredient encyclopedias**, "Tripeptide-3" is used **loosely as a synonym for Syn-Ake** (the snake-venom peptide), which is *chemically not a tripeptide* and has the INCI **Dipeptide Diaminobutyroyl Benzylamide Diacetate.** A related but **distinct** Lubrizol active, **Tripeptide-10-citrulline** (Decorinyl®, a collagen-organising peptide, *not* a neuromodulator), adds to the confusion.
- **Bottom line:** "Tripeptide-3" is **not a clean, single ingredient.** If a supplier offers "Tripeptide-3 (Syn-Ake)," treat it as Syn-Ake and demand the CAS/INCI. Do **not** put the bare term "Tripeptide-3" on a label or in copy expecting it to mean a proven neuromodulator — it doesn't reliably mean anything specific.

Plain language: four lab-made short peptides that each *copy a different natural "muscle off-switch"* (an opioid signal, two snake/curare-style nerve blockers) with the goal of getting facial muscles to contract a little less so expression lines soften. None is botulinum toxin; none is injected — all are surface cosmetics.

## 2. Class & mechanism

All are pitched as **topical neuromodulators** — interrupt the nerve→muscle signal so mimic-muscle micro-contractions (which etch crow's feet, forehead and frown lines) relax. They split into two mechanistic camps, **both different from Argireline's SNAP-25/SNARE story**:

**Presynaptic (block acetylcholine *release*) — Leuphasyl/Pentapeptide-18.**
Mimics **enkephalin**. Binds neuronal **enkephalin/opioid receptors**; receptor activation releases inhibitory G-protein subunits that **close voltage-gated Ca²⁺ channels and open K⁺ channels**, lowering neuronal excitability and Ca²⁺-dependent vesicle fusion, so **less acetylcholine is released** at the neuromuscular junction. Because it acts at a *different point in the cascade* than Argireline (which destabilises the SNARE fusion machinery), the two are marketed as **synergistic** (the "Argirelox" combo).

**Postsynaptic (block the muscle's acetylcholine *receptor*) — Syn-Ake and Vialox.**
- **Syn-Ake** mimics **waglerin-1**, a **reversible antagonist of the muscle-type nicotinic acetylcholine receptor (mnAChR)** — it sits on the receptor so acetylcholine can't trigger the muscle, keeping the fibre relaxed.
- **Vialox** mimics **tubocurarine/curare**, also a **competitive postsynaptic nicotinic-receptor antagonist** ("curare-like effect").

**The mechanistic catch (be brutally honest — identical to the Argireline problem).** Every one of these mechanisms is demonstrated, at best, **in isolated cells / muscle preparations**, and its relevance to **topical** use is unproven. To work *as advertised* the peptide must cross the stratum corneum, traverse epidermis and dermis, and reach the **neuromuscular junction inside facial muscle** at an active concentration. These are **hydrophilic, charged, ~480–500 Da** molecules — the exact profile the lipophilic skin barrier resists (see §5). The 2024 critical review (Nguyen et al.) explicitly states that for this whole peptide class the ability "to reach neuromuscular junctions remains uncertain" and the mechanism of topical contraction-inhibition is "incompletely understood." So the defensible framing is the same as for Argireline: **peptides with real in-vitro receptor activity whose topical anti-wrinkle benefit — where claimed — is modest, supplier-reported, and probably *not* delivered via true muscle relaxation.**

## 4. Evidence review by tier *(the core)*

> **Honest headline:** the independent evidence base is **thinner than Argireline's** (which at least has a couple of independent controlled tests — both null/weak). For Leuphasyl, Syn-Ake and Vialox, the human efficacy numbers are almost entirely **manufacturer/supplier-generated** (Lipotec, Pentapharm/DSM, Centerchem), frequently **not published in indexed peer-reviewed journals**, and **never** isolated in an independent placebo-controlled RCT. Grade the *topical human* evidence **D**.

### Tier 1 — In vitro / mechanistic (plausibility only)
- **Syn-Ake:** supplier in-vitro data report **~82% inhibition of muscle (myotube/HDF) contraction at 0.5 mM after ~2 h**, and "blocks contraction up to ~80% by disabling muscular nACh receptors" (Pentapharm/DSM-Firmenich technical data). Mechanistically consistent with waglerin-1 antagonism. *Limitation:* isolated cells, supplier source, not skin, not topical.
- **Leuphasyl:** enkephalin-receptor binding / reduced neuronal Ca²⁺ influx and acetylcholine release in neuronal models (Lipotec data). *Limitation:* supplier, in-vitro.
- **Vialox:** competitive nicotinic-receptor antagonism (tubocurarine-like) in muscle-cell assays (Centerchem/Pentapharm data). *Limitation:* supplier, in-vitro.
- **Pentapeptide-18 biophysics:** a 2026 spectroscopy/molecular-interaction paper characterises Pentapeptide-18 as an "anti-aging candidate" (binding/conformation work) — *mechanistic/biophysical, not a clinical efficacy study* (ScienceDirect S1476927126000770) **[abstract-level; efficacy not addressed]**.

These establish **mechanistic plausibility, not clinical proof.**

### Tier 2 — Animal
- **None of substance identified** for these three specific peptides via topical route in peer-reviewed sources. (Contrast: the Argireline class has the disputed Wang 2013 aged-mouse collagen arm.) Treat the animal tier as **empty** here.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL
- **None / not applicable.** These are not used orally; oral peptides would be digested. No oral efficacy use case.

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL *(the only tier that matters)*

**4a. Supplier / low-tier human data (the source of every headline number)**

- **Leuphasyl® / Pentapeptide-18 (Lipotec in-house):**
  - **5% Leuphasyl alone → ~11–11.6% wrinkle-depth reduction at 28 days** (twice daily; digital imaging + silicone replicas).
  - **5% Leuphasyl + 5% Argireline ("Argirelox") → ~24–25% average, up to ~47%** wrinkle reduction. The headline "synergy" claim. *Limitations:* manufacturer-originated, the standout numbers are **combination** results that can't isolate Leuphasyl, small/under-reported design.
  - **Dose-finding (often cited via Dragomirescu et al. / "Topical Peptide Treatments," Cosmetics 2017):** three formulations at **0.5% / 1% / 2%**, **n = 20**, twice daily ~2 months, Pro-Derm imaging — **only the 2% concentration reached significance** ("minimal efficient concentration = 2%"), establishing a **threshold dose** and implying lower-% label claims are likely inactive. *Limitation:* small, peptide-supplier-aligned, single study.

- **Syn-Ake® / Dipeptide Diaminobutyroyl Benzylamide Diacetate (Pentapharm/DSM in-house):**
  - Crow's-feet study, **twice daily × 28 days**, profilometry (Ra smoothing; Rz/Rt anti-wrinkle): **wrinkle depth reduced ~"up to 25%"** with "visible deep-wrinkle smoothing after 4 weeks." DSM's product page references **100 volunteers (25/group)**; other secondary summaries cite a **36-volunteer (age 35–60)** dataset with effect detectable from ~15 min (acute) and ~25% at 4 weeks. *Limitations:* manufacturer source, profilometry endpoints, no independent replication; the "15-minute effect" is an acute surface/film observation, not durable neuromodulation.
  - **Comparative supplier study, n = 45, twice daily × 28 days (Syn-Ake vs Argireline vs placebo):** Syn-Ake outperformed Argireline — e.g. **~20–21% wrinkle-depth/smoothing reduction for Syn-Ake vs ~2–4% for Argireline**; before/after significant **for Syn-Ake only**. A "**up to 52%** improvement in forehead fine lines" figure also circulates from supplier/secondary literature. *Limitations:* supplier-run comparative, the dramatic Syn-Ake-vs-Argireline gap is exactly what a maker would want to show, not independently reproduced.

- **Vialox® / Pentapeptide-3 (Centerchem in-house):**
  - Half-face study, **women 30–60, twice daily × 28 days:** **~49% reduction in wrinkle "size" and ~47% reduction in roughness.** *Limitations:* manufacturer marketing data, not located in any indexed peer-reviewed journal; among the **least independently substantiated** of the group despite carrying the **largest** headline percentages.

**4b. The closest thing to independent controlled data (and why it does NOT rescue these four)**
- **Raikou V, Varvaresou A, Panderi I, Papageorgiou E. 2017.** *J Cosmet Dermatol* 16(2):271–278 (PMID 28150423; DOI 10.1111/jocd.12314). Prospective **randomized controlled**, **n = 24**, 4 groups (combo / **Tripeptide-10-citrulline** alone / **Acetyl Hexapeptide-3** alone / control), 60 days, microtopography (cR2/cR3) + TEWL. Concluded it "**confirms the antiwrinkle activity of acetyl hexapeptide-3**" with reduced TEWL; peptide effects were **modest and group-dependent**. **Why it doesn't count for us:** it tested **Argireline and Tripeptide-10-citrulline (a collagen-organising peptide), *not* Leuphasyl, Syn-Ake or Vialox.** It's the same study cited in the Argireline dossier — included here only to show that even the one indexed RCT in this neighbourhood is **not about these four molecules.**
- Multi-peptide eye-serum / combo RCTs (e.g. Li 2023, *J Cosmet Dermatol*, DOI 10.1111/jocd.15849) often *contain* Leuphasyl or Syn-Ake but **cannot isolate** their contribution.

**4c. Current critical synthesis**
- **Nguyen TTM, Yi E-J, Jin X, Zheng Q, Park S-J, Yi G-S, Yang S-J, Yi T-H. 2024.** "Sustainable Dynamic Wrinkle Efficacy: Non-Invasive Peptides as the Future of Botox Alternatives." *Cosmetics* 11(4):118. DOI 10.3390/cosmetics11040118. Reviews Argireline, Syn-Ake, Leuphasyl, Vialox etc.; repeats the supplier efficacy figures (incl. "up to 52%") **but explicitly flags** poor stratum-corneum permeability, that reaching the neuromuscular junction "**remains uncertain**," and that the topical contraction-inhibition mechanism is "**incompletely understood**," calling for better delivery and validation. Net: optimistic framing, honest caveats, **no new independent RCT evidence.**
- **Zdrada-Nowak J, et al. 2025** (*Int J Mol Sci* 26:5722) and **Lum K, et al. 2025** (*J Drugs Dermatol* 24(4)) — focused on Acetyl Hexapeptide-8 but their **penetration/efficacy conclusions generalise** to this larger, equally hydrophilic class.

**Tier-4 verdict:** For **topical human use**, every efficacy number for Leuphasyl, Syn-Ake and Vialox is **supplier-sourced and unreplicated**; there is **no independent placebo-controlled RCT** isolating any of them; the one nearby indexed RCT (Raikou 2017) tested **different** peptides. The Leuphasyl dose-finding data even suggest **<2% is likely inactive.** Treat all percentages (11/25/47/49/52%) as **marketing-tier, not substantiated.**

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration *(the crux of the skepticism)*

Same fatal profile as Argireline, arguably worse for the charged members:
- All four are **hydrophilic and ~480–500 Da**; Vialox (GPRPA) and Syn-Ake (Dab, benzylamide) carry **charged/polar groups** that the lipophilic stratum corneum strongly resists.
- The rigorous **independent FDA-lab penetration work on the analogous Acetyl Hexapeptide-8** (Kraeling 2015, *Cutan Ocul Toxicol* 34(1):46–52): only **~0.22%** reached the stratum corneum, **~99.7% washed off**, and **none** reached receptor fluid. There is **no published independent reason** to expect Leuphasyl/Syn-Ake/Vialox to do better; the 2024 review treats limited permeability as a class problem.
- **No independent permeation study** specific to these three was located. Supplier "it penetrates" assertions are not equivalent to peer-reviewed flux data.
- **Implication:** by the advertised mechanism (reach the neuromuscular junction in muscle), topical delivery is **functionally implausible** without a delivery hack (microneedling, etc.). Any visible benefit is almost certainly **upper-epidermal surface smoothing / humectancy / film-forming**, not neuromodulation.

## 6. Formulation

| Parameter | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Effective concentration | **Leuphasyl: ~2–5%** of trade solution (note: dose-finding says **2% is the minimum that did anything**; 5% in the synergy studies). **Syn-Ake: ~4%** (commonly cited use level; DSM recommends ~4%). **Vialox: ~2–5%** of supplied solution. These are % of *dilute trade solutions*, so true peptide content is far lower. |
| pH | Mildly acidic, ~5.0–6.0 for solubility/stability; avoid extremes that harm tolerance or salt out the charged peptides. |
| Solubility / phase | Water-soluble; deliver in aqueous serums or O/W (W/O performs worst for any chance of penetration). |
| Stability | Reasonably stable in water at proper pH; aqueous systems need robust preservation; protect from heat. |
| Pairings (sensible) | Routinely **stacked together and with Argireline/SNAP-8** as a "peptide neuro-complex" (Argirelox = Leuphasyl + Argireline). Humectants (HA, glycerin), niacinamide, signal peptides (Matrixyl). Honestly, most of the perceived result is plausibly **hydration/film**. |
| Conflicts | Very low-pH formulas (strong direct acids in the same step), high-ionic-strength systems (can precipitate the cationic peptides), and anhydrous-only systems (need water for any claimed activity). |
| Sensory | Light, water-thin; arginine-/amine-rich members can leave slight tack at higher loads; easy to make pleasant serums/gels. |

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation / sensitization:** Generally **excellent tolerability** across supplier and combo human studies; "free of side effects" noted in the Leuphasyl dose-finding work; no significant adverse events reported for Syn-Ake/Vialox at use levels. Low allergenicity expected for short synthetic peptides.
- **Sun sensitivity:** None known; not photosensitisers.
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** No specific human safety data for any of the four. Systemic absorption is negligible (§5), so topical use is generally low-concern, but with **no pregnancy-specific evidence**, apply standard cosmetic caution and make **no pregnancy-safety claims.**
- **"Snake venom" framing (Syn-Ake):** it is a **fully synthetic mimic** of waglerin-1, **not** actual venom — no envenomation risk topically. Don't let marketing imply real venom.
- **Regulatory safety reviews:** The **CIR** has assessed various pentapeptides (e.g., Sept 2024 myristoyl/palmitoyl-pentapeptide assessment) and tripeptides; CIR generally finds reviewed peptides **safe at current use levels** but those documents focus on *other* pentapeptide ingredients — do not over-read them as bespoke clearances for Pentapeptide-3/-18 or Syn-Ake. **[CIR coverage of these exact actives unverified — cite cautiously]**
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** All fully synthetic (solid-phase peptide synthesis); no animal/wild sourcing (despite the "snake venom" branding), vegan-compatible, ordinary synthetic-chemistry footprint.

## 8. Regulatory & claims *(per CLAUDE.md guardrails)*

- **Status:** Cosmetic ingredients in **EU** (Reg. (EC) 1223/2009) and **US** (FDA: cosmetic unless claims cross into "drug"). "Cosmeceutical" has **no legal standing.**
- **The drug line is sharp — and these are higher-risk than average.** The whole pitch — "relaxes facial muscles," "blocks nerve signals," "neuromuscular blocker," "curare-/snake-venom-like," "topical Botox / Botox alternative" — describes **altering the structure/function of the body** and **mimics a drug** (Botox/curare/waglerin are drugs/toxins). Such claims can **reclassify the product as an unapproved drug (US)** and are **unsubstantiated** given the supplier-only, unreplicated, penetration-implausible evidence.
- ❌ Do NOT say: "Botox in a bottle/alternative," "relaxes/freezes/paralyses muscles," "blocks nerve signals," "neuromuscular blocker," "inhibits acetylcholine," "snake-venom power," "curare effect," "reduces wrinkles by 25/47/49/52%," "synergistic muscle relaxation."
- ✅ Defensible cosmetic language: "peptide blend that helps **visibly soften the look of** expression lines," "helps **smooth the appearance of** fine lines," "smooths and hydrates," "supports a smoother-looking complexion." Appearance-based only; no body-mechanism claims; no efficacy percentages we can't substantiate.
- Any "%", "muscle," "nerve," "venom" or "Botox" claim → **flag for legal/substantiation.** We hold no data that would survive a challenge.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Suppliers:** **Leuphasyl** — Lipotec/Lubrizol (originator) + generics. **Syn-Ake** — Pentapharm/**DSM-Firmenich** (originator) + many generics (often China). **Vialox** — Centerchem/Pentapharm lineage + generics. All widely available as commodity raws / dilute trade solutions.
- **Cost tier:** **Low–moderate** as supplied; modest cost-per-finished-product at typical inclusion. Branded grades (Leuphasyl®, Syn-Ake®, Vialox®) cost more than generics. Fits "buy commodity actives, invest in the brand," but the **value is questionable given unproven efficacy.**
- **Supply:** Stable, multi-source, no scarcity or ethical-sourcing flags (synthetic; no actual snake venom).

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

| Marketing claim | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Topical Botox / snake-venom Botox / curare in a cream" | Same *targets* in vitro (opioid / nicotinic receptors); **not** shown to relax muscle when applied topically. Reaching the neuromuscular junction is "uncertain" (Nguyen 2024); analogous peptide penetrates ~0.22% (Kraeling 2015). |
| "Reduces wrinkles 25% / 47% / 49% / 52%" | **All supplier-generated, unreplicated**; several not in indexed journals. The biggest numbers (Vialox 49%, Syn-Ake 52%) are the **least** independently substantiated. |
| "Leuphasyl synergises with Argireline (Argirelox)" | The ~25–47% figure is a **combination** result that can't isolate Leuphasyl; dose-finding shows **<2% Leuphasyl is likely inactive.** |
| "Syn-Ake works in 15 minutes" | An **acute surface/film** observation, not durable neuromodulation. |
| "Clinically proven" | **No independent placebo-controlled RCT** isolates any of these four. The one nearby indexed RCT (Raikou 2017) tested **different** peptides. |
| "Tripeptide-3 = proven snake-venom neuromodulator" | "Tripeptide-3" is a **naming mess** — officially a Gly-Ser-Val peptide with no neuro data; only colloquially equated with Syn-Ake. **Don't rely on the term.** |

This class is, alongside Argireline, among the **most over-claimed** in skincare. "Muscle/nerve/venom/Botox/%" language is precisely the kind that draws **regulatory and substantiation risk**.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ CONDITIONAL — at most a SUPPORTING "feel-good" peptide blend; never a hero, never marketed as a Botox/snake-venom/curare alternative. If forced to pick within the group, Syn-Ake has the *relatively* least-bad (still supplier-only) human signal; "Tripeptide-3" as a bare term should be avoided.**

One-line reasoning: the headline mechanism (topical muscle relaxation) is **even less independently supported than Argireline's** — every human number is supplier-generated and unreplicated, penetration to muscle is implausible, and the naming around "Tripeptide-3" is unreliable — so the only honest play is a well-tolerated, hydrating expression-line *appearance* blend with conservative, appearance-only claims; it earns no hero status and carries real claims risk if oversold.

**Longevity-relevance note:** **Low** — same as the Argireline class. These target a single cosmetic symptom (dynamic lines) via an unproven topical route and engage **none** of the durable skin-aging hallmarks (ECM/collagen synthesis, barrier repair, senescence, mitochondrial decline, glycation, structural photoaging) that anchor the brand's skin-longevity / barrier-repair thesis. Spend the formulation budget and hero narrative on better-evidenced actives (retinoids, niacinamide, ceramides/barrier lipids, vitamin C, collagen-signalling peptides like Matrixyl); if any neuro-peptide is included at all, keep it a quiet supporting "looks smoother" note — and prefer the better-documented Argireline over the thinner-evidence Leuphasyl/Vialox.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

*Verified 2026-06-19 against primary chemistry databases (PubChem, ChemicalBook), the 2024 Nguyen review, the indexed Raikou 2017 RCT, INCI references, and manufacturer technical pages.*

### Overall evidence grade: **D (essentially unproven topically; mechanism-as-marketed implausible; weaker independent base than Argireline)**

For the only tier that matters (human, topical, objective, isolated), **no independent placebo-controlled RCT exists for Pentapeptide-18, Syn-Ake or Vialox.** All headline numbers are supplier-generated and unreplicated; the nearest indexed RCT (Raikou 2017) tests **other** peptides. Penetration to the neuromuscular junction is implausible (class-level; analogous AH-8 ~0.22% per Kraeling 2015) and the current review (Nguyen 2024) concedes NMJ delivery is "uncertain." Grade **D**; it avoids "F" only because tolerability is genuinely good and a thin surface-smoothing/hydration "appearance" benefit is plausible (which would not exceed C even if cleanly shown).

### What checks out
- **Chemistry is solid and verified:** Syn-Ake CAS 823202-99-9 / β-Ala-Pro-Dab-NHBzl·2AcOH / MW ≈ 495.6 (ChemicalBook); Vialox/Pentapeptide-3 CAS 135679-88-8 / GPRPA-NH₂ / C₂₁H₃₇N₉O₅ / MW ≈ 495.6 (PubChem CID 11605666, keyorganics); Pentapeptide-18 = Tyr-Ala-Gly-Phe-Leu, Leu-enkephalin analogue (multiple INCI sources).
- **Distinct mechanisms confirmed:** Leuphasyl = presynaptic enkephalin/opioid; Syn-Ake = postsynaptic waglerin-1/nicotinic antagonist; Vialox = postsynaptic tubocurarine/curare-like antagonist — genuinely different from Argireline's SNAP-25/SNARE route.
- **The "Tripeptide-3" naming problem is real and important:** official INCI Tripeptide-3 = Gly-Ser-Val (no neuro data); "Tripeptide-3 = Syn-Ake" is a marketing conflation; Tripeptide-10-citrulline is yet another, separate molecule. The dossier flags this correctly.
- **Penetration skepticism is well-founded** and inherited from the strongest (independent) AH-8 data.
- **Nguyen 2024** is a real, current peer-reviewed review and its caveats are accurately represented.

### Errors / overstatements explicitly avoided or flagged
1. **Raikou 2017 is NOT evidence for these four** — it tested Argireline + Tripeptide-10-citrulline. Flagged so it isn't mis-cited as a Syn-Ake/Vialox/Leuphasyl RCT.
2. **Syn-Ake sample size is inconsistent across sources** (DSM page implies ~100 volunteers / 25-per-group; secondary sources say 36). Reported as a range with the discrepancy noted; not asserted as a single fact.
3. **"Tripeptide-3"** is not treated as a clean ingredient; the dose/efficacy figures attributed to "Tripeptide-3" in some sources are really Syn-Ake's.
4. **The Pentapeptide-18 biophysics paper (Source 15)** is abstract-level and biophysical; not represented as clinical efficacy, author list marked unverified.
5. **CIR coverage** of Pentapeptide-3/-18/Syn-Ake specifically is **unverified** (the located CIR doc addresses other pentapeptides); flagged, not over-read.

### Main caveats to carry forward
- **Independent evidence is essentially absent** — this class is *more* marketing-driven than Argireline, not less.
- **Penetration is the recurring fatal flaw**; the advertised muscle-relaxation mechanism cannot plausibly operate topically.
- **"Tripeptide-3" should not be used as a label/copy term** without a CAS/INCI, because it does not reliably denote a proven neuromodulator.
- Internally, treat all four as **grade D (unproven topically)**; the §11 "conditional/supporting" verdict holds **only** under appearance-only/humectant framing, never as efficacy, and never with the "Botox/venom/curare/%" language.
