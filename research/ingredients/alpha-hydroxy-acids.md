# Ingredient: Alpha-Hydroxy Acids — Glycolic & Lactic Acid (INCI: Glycolic Acid; Lactic Acid)

**Researched:** 2026-06-18 · **Status:** ✅ shortlisted (SUPPORTING)

## What it is
AHAs are water-soluble exfoliating acids derived from (or nature-identical to) fruit/milk sugars. They loosen the "glue" between dead surface cells, prompting gentle desquamation — revealing smoother, more even skin and, at higher exposure/over time, supporting epidermal renewal and dermal changes. **Glycolic acid** (smallest molecule, deepest penetration, more potent/irritating) and **lactic acid** (larger, gentler, also a humectant) are the two workhorses.

## What it does (claimed → evidenced)
| Benefit | Evidence strength | Source(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Smooths texture, improves appearance of photodamaged skin | **Moderate–strong** — double-blind vehicle-controlled trial: ~71–76% reported improvement at 8% over 22 wks | [1](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8651713/) |
| Reduces appearance of fine lines / increases skin thickness & firmness (higher exposure) | **Moderate** — histologic studies show increased epidermal/dermal thickness; lactic 12% improved firmness | [2](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8642081/) |
| Evens tone / reduces look of discoloration | **Moderate** | [3](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9117988/) |
| Supports collagen / epidermal renewal (glycolic at pH 4) | **Moderate** — explant study: stimulates collagen + renewal without raising TNF-α | [4](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7891644/) |
| Overall efficacy & safety profile | **Moderate** — confirmed in recent comprehensive review | [5](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39050562/) |

## How it fits the brand
**Good supporting fit.** "Reveal renewed skin" / gentle resurfacing is on-thesis (renewal, not aggression). Lactic acid especially — being a gentler humectant-acid — suits a premium, kind-to-skin brand better than harsh high-% glycolic peels. AHAs make a strong **supporting "smoothing/radiance" active or a standalone gentle exfoliant SKU**, complementing (not competing with) a retinoid hero.

## Formulation notes
- **Effective concentration:** Leave-on consumer products commonly **5–10%** (8% is the classic studied dose); lactic 5–12%. Higher % / lower pH = professional-peel territory (out of scope for premium daily use).
- **pH:** Efficacy depends on free-acid availability — typically **pH ~3.5–4** for leave-on. (Glycolic at pH 4 still stimulates renewal while limiting inflammation [4].)
- **Stability:** Stable, easy to formulate; partial neutralization buffers irritation.
- **Pairs well with:** Hyaluronic acid + ceramides (offset dryness), niacinamide. Good in AM or PM, but **PM preferred** due to sun sensitivity.
- **Conflicts:** Don't stack same-step with strong retinoids or high-strength vitamin C (irritation); alternate. Lactic acid pairs nicely with hydrators because it's also a humectant.
- **Sensory:** Can tingle; well-formulated versions feel light and "polishing."

## Concerns & cautions
- **Irritation:** Dose/pH-dependent stinging, redness, dryness; sensitive skin needs lower %.
- **Sun sensitivity:** **AHAs increase UV sensitivity** — US labeling guidance recommends a sunscreen warning on AHA products. Daily SPF messaging is mandatory.
- **Pregnancy:** Topical AHAs generally considered acceptable (low systemic absorption); standard boilerplate.
- **Sourcing:** Lactic acid is often fermentation-derived (nice natural-story angle).

## Claims caution
- ✅ **Safe cosmetic language:** "gently exfoliates," "smooths and refines the look of skin texture," "reveals brighter, renewed-looking skin," "improves the appearance of dullness and uneven tone."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like:** "peels away years," "increases collagen/skin thickness by X%" (structure/function), "treats sun damage." Also: **must include the FDA-recommended sun-sensitivity/sunscreen warning** on AHA products in the US.

- **Inexpensive**, abundant, very stable. Lactic acid's fermentation origin supports a natural/sustainable narrative. Low supply risk.
