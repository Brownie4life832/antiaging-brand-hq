# Ingredient: Hyaluronic Acid (INCI: Sodium Hyaluronate; Hyaluronic Acid; Hydrolyzed Sodium Hyaluronate)

**Researched:** 2026-06-18 · **Status:** ✅ shortlisted (SUPPORTING)

## What it is
Hyaluronic acid (HA) is a humectant naturally present in skin that binds large amounts of water (up to ~1000× its weight). Topically it draws and holds moisture in the upper skin layers, giving immediate hydration and a temporary "plumped," smoother look. It comes in a range of **molecular weights (MW)** — high-MW sits on the surface for a moisture film; low-MW penetrates a bit deeper. "Sodium hyaluronate" is the more stable, formulator-friendly salt form.

## What it does (claimed → evidenced)
| Benefit | Evidence strength | Source(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Increases skin hydration | **Strong** | [1](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10078143/) |
| Improves appearance of fine lines/wrinkles (temporary, hydration-driven plumping) | **Moderate** — RCT: 0.1% over 60 days improved hydration/elasticity; low-MW reduced wrinkle depth most | [2](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10078143/) |
| Improves skin elasticity/suppleness | **Moderate** | [1](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10078143/) |
| Multi-MW blends give both surface + deeper hydration | **Moderate** | [3](https://jcadonline.com/clinical-evaluation-hyaluronic-acid-skin-rejuvenation/) |
| "Long-term anti-wrinkle / collagen building" from topical HA | **Weak** — benefit is largely surface hydration & temporary plumping, not structural remodeling | [1](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10078143/) |

## How it fits Kintsugi
**Solid supporting fit.** HA is the comfort/hydration backbone — it makes skin look immediately smoother and "dewier in gold," and it buffers the dryness from actives like retinoids and AHAs (helping adherence). It is not a deep "repair" actor on its own, so it's a **supporting hydrator across the line**, not a hero claim. Honest framing (visible plumping/hydration vs. structural change) protects brand credibility.

## Formulation notes
- **Effective concentration:** Low — typically **0.1–2%** total HA; multi-MW blends are common (e.g., high + low MW) for surface + slightly deeper hydration.
- **pH:** Compatible across a broad range; very easy to formulate.
- **Stability:** Sodium hyaluronate is stable and widely used; HA itself can be sensitive but the salt form is robust.
- **Pairs well with:** Basically everything — niacinamide, peptides, ceramides, vitamin C, retinoids (helps offset their dryness). Best applied to **slightly damp skin and sealed with an occlusive/moisturizer** so it draws water *into* skin rather than *out* in dry climates.
- **Conflicts:** None meaningful.
- **Sensory:** Light, "slip-y," fast-absorbing; great for elegant serum textures.

## Concerns & cautions
- **Irritation:** Very low; among the most tolerable cosmetic ingredients.
- **Dry-climate caveat:** In low humidity without an occlusive top layer, a pure humectant can theoretically pull moisture from deeper skin — mitigate with occlusives in the formula/routine.
- **Sun sensitivity:** None.
- **Pregnancy:** Considered safe topically.
- **Sourcing:** Modern HA is mostly **bio-fermentation** derived (vegan-friendly, good sustainability story) rather than animal-derived.

## Claims caution
- ✅ **Safe cosmetic language:** "intensely hydrates," "holds moisture for plumper-, smoother-looking skin," "visibly improves the look of fine lines from dehydration," "for a dewy, replenished look."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like:** "rebuilds your skin's hyaluronic acid," "fills wrinkles" (implies filler/structural), "boosts collagen." Keep plumping framed as **appearance/temporary hydration**, not structural.
- See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## Cost / sourcing
- **Low to moderate** (varies by MW spec and grade). Abundant, fermentation-derived, vegan, low supply risk. Premium multi-MW or specialty (e.g., cross-linked / "hybrid") grades cost more and suit premium positioning.
