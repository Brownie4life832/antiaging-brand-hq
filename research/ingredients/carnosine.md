# Ingredient: Carnosine (& N-Acetyl Carnosine) (INCI: Carnosine)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Class:** Peptides · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product

> Brutally honest one-liner: carnosine has a genuinely interesting and well-characterized **anti-glycation + antioxidant mechanism** and a long bench/longevity pedigree, but its **human topical evidence is thin, almost entirely industry-sponsored, uncontrolled, and confounded by multi-ingredient formulas** — and its biggest selling point (anti-glycation in the dermis) runs straight into a **poor-penetration problem**. A credible *supporting* anti-glycation/antioxidant story, not a hero.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Carnosine (L-carnosine).
- **Chemistry:** A naturally occurring **dipeptide**, β-alanyl-L-histidine. MW ≈ 226.2 Da. Endogenous to humans (highest in skeletal muscle and brain); declines in tissue with age. Water-soluble, near-neutral isoelectric behavior.
- **INCI:** **Carnosine** (often labeled "L-Carnosine" colloquially; the standardized INCI is *Carnosine*).
- **Key forms / derivatives:**
  - **L-Carnosine** — the bioactive natural enantiomer (D-carnosine is largely inactive in the rejuvenation work; see §4).
  - **N-Acetyl Carnosine (NAC)** — an acetylated, more lipophilic/stable prodrug form. **Famous almost exclusively as an *eye-drop* (e.g. "Can-C") for cataract**, NOT as a skin active. Included here because the brief asks, and because marketing sometimes blurs the two — its skin evidence is essentially nil and its eye-drop evidence is weak (see §4).
  - **Carcinine** (β-alanyl-histamine) — a related non-hydrolyzed analog promoted in some "oral skin beautification" papers by the NAC eye-drop patent holder; fringe.
  - Related histidine dipeptides: **anserine**, **homocarnosine** (different molecules; not interchangeable — see §4 specificity note).
  - **Beta-alanine** — the rate-limiting *oral precursor* used to raise muscle carnosine; relevant to oral, not topical.

---

## 2. Class & mechanism

Carnosine is a multifunctional small peptide. Its proposed skin-relevant mechanisms (mostly from chemistry / in-vitro work):

1. **Anti-glycation (its signature claim).** Carnosine's free amino group acts as a **sacrificial nucleophile / carbonyl scavenger**, reacting with reactive carbonyl species (RCS) such as **methylglyoxal (MG)** and intermediates before they cross-link proteins into **advanced glycation end-products (AGEs)** like carboxymethyl-lysine (CML) and pentosidine. Because dermal **collagen and elastin** are long-lived proteins that stiffen and yellow as they glycate, anti-glycation is the mechanistic basis for carnosine's "anti-aging" pitch [1][2][8].
2. **Antioxidant / free-radical scavenging** — direct ROS quenching and lipid-peroxidation defense (the imidazole ring of histidine is the active moiety) [3][7].
3. **Transition-metal chelation** — sequesters Cu⁺ and Fe²⁺, attenuating the **Fenton reaction** and hydroxyl-radical generation [3][7].
4. **Anti-carbonylation** — traps protein carbonyls, limiting downstream protein damage [1][3].
5. **Cellular / replicative effects** — at high millimolar concentrations in culture it delays fibroblast senescence and reduces telomere shortening (mechanism not fully resolved; see §4) [9][10][11].

Net framing: carnosine is best understood as a **defensive/protective antioxidant-antiglycation agent**, not a "signaling peptide" that instructs the skin to build collagen (unlike Matrixyl-type peptides or GHK-Cu). It protects existing matrix proteins rather than demonstrably stimulating new synthesis in human skin.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Mechanistic rationale | Strength of *skin* evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Glycation / AGEs** | Carbonyl scavenging; ex-vivo human-skin AGE reduction | Mechanism strong; *skin* evidence weak-indirect (human data ex-vivo only, single industry study, penetration unverified) |
| **Oxidative stress** | ROS scavenging, metal chelation | Moderate-mechanistic; weak clinical |
| **Collagen / ECM loss** | *Protects* (not proven to build) collagen by preventing cross-linking | Weak-indirect |
| **Inflammaging** | Anti-inflammatory signals in wound/animal models | Weak (mostly non-skin) |
| **Cellular senescence** | Delayed fibroblast senescence in vitro (high mM) | In-vitro only |
| **Photoaging** | Antioxidant/anti-glycation contribution in combo creams | Weak (confounded combos) |
| **Hydration / water-loss** | Improvements seen in combo night cream | Weak (combo, uncontrolled) |
| **Pigmentation / tone** | Minor brown/UV-spot reductions in combo cream | Weak (combo, uncontrolled) |
| Barrier decline | Not a primary mechanism | Negligible |
| Mitochondrial decline | Theorized in longevity literature | Not skin-relevant |
| Microbiome | No relevant data | None |

**Carnosine's distinctive lane is glycation** — a hallmark that few mainstream actives address head-on. That is its strategic differentiator if the brand wants an "anti-glycation/anti-sugar-aging" angle.

## 4. Evidence review by tier (the core)

> **Read this first:** Carnosine's evidence is *inverted* relative to what marketing implies. The deepest data is **in-vitro and animal**; the **oral** human data is modest; the **topical** human data is **shallow, uncontrolled, industry-funded, and almost always tests carnosine inside a multi-ingredient formula** — so almost nothing is attributable to carnosine *alone* applied to *human skin in a controlled trial*. There is **no published independent topical-carnosine RCT** found in this review.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro / chemistry (strongest, most consistent)

- **Hipkiss / Hobart and reviews — anti-glycation chemistry.** Carnosine reliably inhibits AGE formation and protein carbonylation in cell-free and cell systems. Synthesized in the systematic review by **Ghodsi & Kheirouri (2018), *Amino Acids* 50(9):1177–1186** [1]: of **36 studies** (19 in-vitro, 15 animal, **only 2 human**), **all but two** showed carnosine prevents AGE formation. Authors' own caveat: "*there were few human studies*" and the mechanism "*needs further investigation.*" This is the single best evidence summary — and it underlines how little human work exists.
- **McFarland & Holliday (1994), *Experimental Cell Research* 212(2):167–175** [9] — the foundational "rejuvenation" paper. Carnosine at **20–50 mM** retarded senescence and preserved non-senescent morphology in human diploid fibroblasts (HFF-1, MRC-5). Removing carnosine reverted cells to a senescent phenotype. **Specificity is important and double-edged:** D-carnosine, homocarnosine, anserine, and β-alanine did NOT reproduce the effect — so the effect is specific to L-carnosine, but it required **millimolar (20–50 mM) culture concentrations** that are not realistically achievable in living dermis from a topical cream.
- **McFarland & Holliday (1999), *Exp Cell Res* / "further evidence"** [10] — replicated and extended the rejuvenation findings.
- **Shao, Li & Tan (2004), *Biochem Biophys Res Commun* 324:931–936** [11] — fibroblasts grown in **20 mM** carnosine showed slower **telomere shortening**, less telomeric DNA damage, and extended population-doubling lifespan.
- **Narda et al. (2018), *Skin Pharmacol Physiol* 31:324–331 — ex-vivo human skin explants** [8] — see Tier 4 (the most skin-relevant in-vitro/ex-vivo work; counted there because it used human skin and a topical product).

**Verdict on Tier 1:** Mechanism is real and well-replicated. **Caveat:** the cellular-longevity effects depend on **high millimolar concentrations** sustained in the medium — a bench condition, not a topical reality.

### Tier 2 — Animal

- **Ansurudeen et al. (2012), *Amino Acids* — db/db diabetic mouse wound healing** [5]. Carnosine (≈100 mg/kg, local + injected) accelerated wound closure and raised IGF-1, TGF-β, FGF7, and Col-1a expression vs. controls; in-vitro it improved fibroblast/endothelial viability under high glucose. **Limitations:** disease (diabetes) model, injection + local route, animal — not a cosmetic-use analog.
- Various rodent oxidative-stress/anti-glycation models feed the **Ghodsi & Kheirouri** review's 15 animal studies [1], broadly supportive of anti-AGE activity systemically.

**Verdict on Tier 2:** Supportive of anti-glycation and pro-healing biology, but **systemic/injected dosing and disease models** — not transferable to a topical anti-aging claim.

### Tier 3 — Human, ORAL

- **Guaitolini et al. (2019), *J Clin Aesthet Dermatol* 12(4):40–45** [6] — **randomized, placebo-controlled, single-blind**; **50 women, 40–65**, 60 days, oral combo of **HA 200 mg + L-carnosine 500 mg + MSM 400 mg**. Results: glabella hydration +15.2% (p=0.03), elasticity +22.6% (p=0.004), periocular wrinkle improvement (p=0.043) vs. no change on placebo. **Limitations:** small, single-blind, short, and a **three-ingredient combo** — carnosine's individual contribution is unknowable; oral, not topical.
- **Beta-alanine / muscle-carnosine literature** (e.g., Harris et al.; Sale et al. reviews [12]) — robust evidence that **oral β-alanine raises muscle carnosine 30–80%** and modestly aids exercise capacity. **Relevance to skin: essentially none** — this is muscle physiology, cited only to show carnosine *is* orally bioavailable via its precursor and to debunk skin claims built on muscle data.

**Verdict on Tier 3:** One small combo RCT with skin endpoints; suggestive but not carnosine-specific. Oral bioavailability is established for muscle, irrelevant to skin appearance.

### Tier 4 — Human, TOPICAL (the tier that matters for the brand — and the weakest)

**No independent, double-blind, placebo-controlled topical-carnosine RCT was found.** What exists:

- **Narda, Peno-Mazzarino, Krutmann, Trullas & Granger (2018), *Skin Pharmacol Physiol* 31:324–331** [8] — **ex-vivo human skin explants** (not living subjects). Glycation induced with **500 µM methylglyoxal**; **0.2% carnosine** applied as aqueous solution (AQ-CARN) or in a facial cream (FC-CARN). Anti-glycation vs. control:
  - AQ-CARN: CML −64% (epidermis) / −41% (dermis); pentosidine −48% (epidermis) / −42% (dermis, ns).
  - FC-CARN: CML −150% / −122%; pentosidine −108% / −136% (values exceed 100% because the cream pushed AGE markers *below* untreated baseline).
  - **Critical caveats:** (a) **ex-vivo explants, not in-vivo** humans; (b) **skin penetration was NOT measured** — a major gap given carnosine's known poor permeation (§5); (c) the >100% "super-reductions" are biologically odd and suggest the **cream base/other components** contributed; (d) **funded/authored by ISDIN** (Narda, Trullas, Granger employees; Krutmann a paid scientific advisor) — direct manufacturer conflict.
- **Granger, Brown, Aladren & Narda (2020), *Dermatol Ther* — melatonin + carnosine + *Helichrysum italicum* night cream** [4] — **four single-center, OPEN-LABEL (uncontrolled) trials, 117 subjects**, up to 12 weeks. Reported hydration +64.4% (1 h), TEWL −10.0%, wrinkle counts up to −18.9%, brown spots −5.5%, UV spots −13.2%, calmer-skin self-report 86.7%. **Caveats:** **open-label, no placebo/control**, **three actives** (carnosine cannot be isolated; melatonin is itself an antioxidant and the obvious driver), **ISDIN-sponsored**. This is product-marketing-grade evidence, not efficacy proof for carnosine.
- **Wang et al. (2021), *Aesthetic Plast Surg* — HA + L-carnosine (2.0 mg/mL) for horizontal neck wrinkles** [13] — **open-label, n=13, intradermal INJECTION/mesotherapy (NOT topical).** 12/13 improved ≥1 grade at 1 month; mild transient injection-site AEs. **Borrowed format warning:** this is an injectable amino-acid/HA "skin booster," not a leave-on cosmetic; route bypasses the stratum corneum entirely and the result cannot be claimed for a cream.
- **JAAD 2024 conference abstract — retinol + carnosine + soothing combo** [2] — conference abstract (not a full peer-reviewed paper). It does describe a **14-week in-vivo study in 34 Chinese women (36–60)** with a retinol serum containing carnosine + a soothing complex, reporting improvement vs. baseline in wrinkles, hydration, elasticity, firmness, and an AGE measure. **Low evidentiary weight regardless:** abstract-only (not peer-reviewed), **no placebo/vehicle control** (improvements are vs. baseline), and a **multi-active formula led by retinol** — carnosine cannot be isolated and retinol is the obvious driver.

**What is actually proven for human TOPICAL carnosine use:**
- **Shown once (ex-vivo, low confidence):** topically applied 0.2% carnosine reduced MG-induced AGEs in **ex-vivo human skin explants** in a **single manufacturer-funded study with penetration unmeasured** [8]. Not "proven" in any in-vivo or independent sense.
- **Suggested but confounded:** hydration, TEWL, wrinkle, and spot improvements — all from **uncontrolled, multi-ingredient, manufacturer-run** studies [4].
- **NOT established:** any benefit of carnosine *alone* in a *controlled in-vivo* human topical trial; collagen "boosting" in human skin; anti-wrinkle efficacy independent of the formula it rode in on.

### Side note — N-Acetyl Carnosine (NAC) eye drops

Included for completeness because the slug names it. **Dubois & Bastawrous (2017), Cochrane Database Syst Rev** [14]: only **2 eligible trials (~114 participants), both "awaiting classification"** for lack of obtainable methodology; conclusion: "*no convincing evidence that NAC reverses cataract, nor prevents progression.*" Earlier positive trials (Babizhayev et al. [15]) are by the **patent holder**, with conflict-of-interest and methodology concerns. **NAC has no meaningful topical-skin evidence base.** Do not let the eye-drop "cataract" story leak into skin copy.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

This is carnosine's **central practical weakness** and must temper every claim:

- Carnosine is **small (≈226 Da)** and water-soluble — small enough in principle to permeate — **but in practice penetration is reported as poor**, with limited stratum-corneum partitioning and minimal systemic absorption [16][17].
- Its hydrophilicity and charge at skin pH limit passive diffusion through the lipid barrier; it is also subject to **hydrolysis by carnosinase** in tissue.
- The signature anti-glycation benefit needs carnosine in the **dermis** (where long-lived collagen glycates). Getting an intact, sufficient dose to the dermis from a leave-on cream is **unproven** — the ex-vivo study that showed dermal AGE reduction [8] **did not measure penetration**, so we cannot confirm the carnosine reached the dermis vs. the cream base acting at the surface.
- **Delivery R&D exists but is early:** liposomal, ultradeformable (transfersome), and ceramide-containing liposomal carnosine systems have been developed in vitro to improve permeation/stability [17][18]; a biomimetic quercetin+carnosine liposome showed enhanced transdermal penetration and NRF2 antioxidant effects in a 2025 paper [18]. None is a validated, off-the-shelf cosmetic standard yet.

**Implication:** if the brand uses carnosine, an **encapsulated/liposomal** form materially strengthens both the mechanism story and the claim defensibility; plain aqueous carnosine is the weakest option.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** Cosmetic use typically **0.05–2%**; the only human-skin (ex-vivo) data used **0.2%** [8][16]. There is no established dose-response in vivo. Note the chasm between the **20–50 mM (~0.45–1.1% in *culture medium bathing the cells*)** of the longevity studies and what reaches living dermis from a cream — do not equate the two.
- **pH:** Best held around **~6.0–7.5** (near-neutral) for stability and activity [17]. Comfortably compatible with barrier-friendly near-neutral formulas.
- **Stability:** Reasonably stable as a raw material but **hydrolysis-prone in biological tissue (carnosinase)** and benefits from encapsulation for skin delivery; protect from oxidation; keep in well-buffered, low-pro-oxidant systems.
- **Pairings (synergistic / sensible):**
  - **Antioxidant network** — pairs naturally with **niacinamide, vitamin E, ergothioneine, melatonin** (the night-cream combo) for layered oxidative defense.
  - **Other anti-glycation actives** — blueberry/blackberry extracts, alistin, aminoguanidine-type cosmetic actives, or **spermidine** for an "anti-glycation + autophagy" longevity narrative.
  - **HA + humectants** — supports the hydration story; the injectable/oral studies pair it with HA.
  - **Retinol** — combined in the JAAD 2024 abstract [2]; carnosine's soothing/antioxidant profile is a logical buffer for retinoid irritation (plausible, not proven).
- **Conflicts:** No strong documented incompatibilities. As a metal chelator it could theoretically interact with **copper peptides (GHK-Cu)** or high-dose metal-dependent systems — separate or formulate deliberately (low-confidence caution). Low-pH vitamin C systems are sub-optimal for carnosine's preferred near-neutral pH; layer at different steps.
- **Sensory:** Water-soluble, no color or odor issues, no inherent tackiness at low %; formulation-neutral and easy to include.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation/sensitization:** **Very well tolerated.** Endogenous human molecule; cosmetic use at 0.05–2% has low irritancy. Reported adverse reactions are rare, mild, transient (occasional redness) [16][19]. Standard patch-test advice applies for sensitive skin.
- **Sun sensitivity:** No photosensitization signal; it is an *antioxidant*, plausibly photoprotective-adjacent (do not over-claim).
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** No specific red flags; carnosine is naturally present in the body and diet (meat). Considered generally low-risk topically; as always, defer to "consult your physician" for pregnancy copy rather than asserting safety.
- **Sensitization potential:** Low; it is a simple natural dipeptide. The broader peptide-cosmetics safety framework [19] treats short endogenous peptides like carnosine as low-concern.
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** Produced by **synthesis or fermentation** (not animal-extracted for cosmetic grade), so no major ethical/provenance issues — an advantage over biologically-derived actives (exosomes, growth factors). Vegan-compatible when synthetically/fermentation-sourced (confirm with supplier).

## 8. Regulatory & claims

- **Cosmetic, not drug** — carnosine is a recognized cosmetic ingredient (INCI: Carnosine). Topical use sits in cosmetic territory **provided claims stay appearance-based.**
- **NAC eye drops are a different regulatory animal** (ophthalmic/medical-device or unapproved-drug depending on market) — keep that story entirely out of skin marketing.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "helps defend against glycation (sugar-related dullness and stiffness in the look of skin)," "antioxidant support," "helps protect the look of skin from environmental and lifestyle stress," "supports a more even, radiant-looking complexion," "helps visibly improve skin's resilience."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like or unsubstantiated — flag for legal/substantiation:**
  - "Reverses/erases wrinkles," "boosts collagen," "rebuilds the matrix" — carnosine *protects* collagen mechanistically; it has **not** been shown to build collagen in human skin.
  - Any **anti-glycation efficacy %** ("reduces AGEs by 64%") quoted as a consumer benefit — those numbers are **ex-vivo, MG-induced, manufacturer-run, penetration-unverified** [8]; using them as on-skin claims needs substantiation/legal review.
  - "Anti-aging," "treats," "prevents aging," "cataract"/"cellular rejuvenation"/"telomere" claims — drug-adjacent and/or built on in-vitro or eye-drop data; do not use.
  - "Clinically proven to reduce wrinkles" — the supporting clinical data is open-label and multi-ingredient; not defensible as carnosine-specific.
- See claims guardrail in [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md) and [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier:** **Low-to-moderate** as a raw material — carnosine is a commodity peptide, widely available from cosmetic and nutraceutical suppliers; far cheaper than proprietary/biologic actives. Encapsulated/liposomal grades cost more and carry MOQ/formulation complexity.
- **Supply:** Stable, broadly available (synthetic/fermentation). No scarcity or provenance concerns. Confirm cosmetic-grade purity and vegan status with supplier.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

| Marketing claim seen in the wild | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Carnosine reverses cellular aging / rejuvenates skin cells" | Based on **1990s fibroblast cultures at 20–50 mM** [9][10][11] — bench conditions unachievable in living dermis from a cream. Over-extrapolated. |
| "Reduces skin AGEs by up to 64%" | **Ex-vivo explant** number, MG-induced model, **penetration never measured**, **manufacturer-funded** [8]. Not a validated in-vivo consumer benefit. |
| "Clinically proven anti-wrinkle / hydration" | From **open-label, uncontrolled, 3-ingredient** night cream [4]; melatonin is the likelier driver; carnosine not isolated. |
| "The anti-glycation 'sugar-aging' hero" | Best *mechanistic* lane, genuinely differentiated — but human topical proof is **thin**, and penetration to the dermis (where it'd matter) is **unproven**. |
| "Carnosine for cataracts/eyes shows it works on skin" | Conflation of **NAC eye drops** (weak, COI-laden, Cochrane-negative [14]) with skin. Irrelevant and misleading. |
| "Boosts collagen" | No human-skin evidence of collagen *synthesis*; mechanism is **protection**, not stimulation. |

**Bottom line:** carnosine is **over-marketed relative to its topical human evidence**, but it is **not snake oil** — the mechanism is real and rare (anti-glycation), and it's cheap, safe, and easy to formulate. The honest pitch is "protective antioxidant + anti-glycation support," not "rejuvenation."

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING (conditional) — not a hero.** Use carnosine as a **credible, safe, low-cost antioxidant + anti-glycation supporting actor** that lets the brand own the underexploited "**glycation / sugar-aging**" angle — *but only* (a) in an **encapsulated/liposomal** form to make the penetration story defensible, (b) paired with stronger-evidenced actives (niacinamide, ergothioneine, retinoid, HA), and (c) with **strictly appearance/"helps protect/defend"** copy, never collagen-boost or %-AGE claims.

- **One-line reasoning:** Real, differentiated mechanism (anti-glycation) + excellent safety + low cost, undercut by thin, confounded, industry-only human topical evidence and a genuine penetration problem — so it earns a supporting role, not the headline.
- **Longevity-relevance note:** Strong *conceptual* fit with skin-longevity (glycation, senescence, telomeres are all longevity hallmarks it touches in vitro), which makes it useful **narrative scaffolding** for a longevity brand — provided we keep the in-vitro longevity science clearly separated from what's proven on human skin. Pairs naturally with the brand's other longevity actives (spermidine, NAD/niacinamide, GHK-Cu) under an "anti-glycation + antioxidant defense" sub-theme.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Independent adversarial fact-check of every headline claim and citation in this dossier (sources cross-checked against PubMed / publisher pages / Cochrane).

### Overall evidence grade: **C** (weak / indirect for the use that matters)

Grading is **for the brand-relevant question: does *topical* carnosine work on *living human* skin?** On that question the evidence is genuinely weak/indirect — hence **C**, not higher. (If the question were "is the anti-glycation *mechanism* real?" the answer would be A-grade; if it were "does it work in a controlled in-vivo human topical RCT?" the answer would be D — there is none.) The dossier's own one-liner and Tier-4 framing already land at roughly this grade; this verification confirms it rather than overturning it.

- **A (strong human-topical RCT):** none exists. No independent, double-blind, placebo/vehicle-controlled topical-carnosine RCT was found in this check — the dossier's central claim, and it holds.
- **B (moderate human-topical):** not met. The only in-vivo human topical data are open-label, uncontrolled, multi-active, manufacturer-run.
- **C (weak / indirect):** **this is where carnosine sits.** One ex-vivo human-skin study + suggestive (confounded) in-vivo combo studies + strong non-human mechanistic data.

### Main caveats (the real risk surface)

1. **The headline benefit (dermal anti-glycation) collides with a penetration unknown.** The single human-*skin* study [8] never measured whether carnosine reached the dermis; its biologically impossible >100% AGE reductions point to the **cream base / other actives** doing work, not carnosine alone. This is the load-bearing weakness, and the dossier flags it correctly.
2. **Industry-funded monoculture.** The entire human topical/ex-vivo signal traces to **ISDIN-affiliated authors** ([8] and [4]; Narda, Trullas, Granger employees, Krutmann paid advisor). There is **no independent confirmation**.
3. **Combo confounding everywhere.** Every in-vivo human study (oral [6], topical [4], abstract [2], injection [13]) tests carnosine **inside a multi-active formula** (HA, MSM, melatonin, *Helichrysum*, retinol). Carnosine's individual contribution is **unknowable** from any of them; in the night cream [4], melatonin is the more plausible driver, and in [2] retinol is.
4. **The "rejuvenation/telomere" longevity story is bench-only at 20–50 mM** — concentrations unachievable in living dermis from a cream. Genuine science, but not a topical claim.
5. **NAC eye-drop evidence is Cochrane-negative and COI-laden, and has ~zero skin relevance.** Correctly quarantined; keep it out of skin copy.
6. **Sample sizes are small** (oral RCT n=50; injection n=13; abstract n=34) and study durations short (≤12 weeks) even before the control/funding problems.
