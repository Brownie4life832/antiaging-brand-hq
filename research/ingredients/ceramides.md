# Ingredient: Ceramides (INCI: e.g. Ceramide NP, Ceramide AP, Ceramide EOP, Ceramide NG; often with Phytosphingosine, Cholesterol, Fatty Acids)

**Researched:** 2026-06-18 · **Status:** ✅ shortlisted (HERO candidate for the barrier story)

## What it is
Ceramides are the skin's own barrier lipids — they make up roughly **50% of the lipid "mortar"** in the outer skin layer (stratum corneum), arranged with cholesterol and free fatty acids in a brick-and-mortar structure that locks moisture in and irritants out. Ceramide levels decline with age and in compromised/dry skin; topical ceramides (ideally in a physiological **ceramide + cholesterol + fatty-acid blend**) help replenish that pool and restore barrier function.

## What it does (claimed → evidenced)
| Benefit | Evidence strength | Source(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Restores/strengthens skin barrier, reduces transepidermal water loss (TEWL) | **Strong** — RCTs in eczema-prone & compromised skin show barrier restoration | [1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8459234/), [2](https://academic.oup.com/bjd/article/193/4/729/8142502) |
| Rebalances stratum corneum ceramide profile (physiological lipid supplementation) | **Strong** | [2](https://academic.oup.com/bjd/article/193/4/729/8142502) |
| Hydration / reduces dryness & roughness | **Strong** | [1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8459234/) |
| Improves comfort/resilience in aged, depleted skin | **Moderate** — strong mechanistic basis (age-related ceramide decline); fewer dedicated anti-aging RCTs | [3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5801391/) |
| Directly reduces wrinkles / "anti-aging" by itself | **Weak** — value is barrier/hydration/comfort, not direct wrinkle reduction | [3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5801391/) |

## How it fits the brand
**A strong candidate molecule.** Ceramides *are* barrier-replenishing lipids — they restore the skin's own building blocks. They also make every other active more tolerable (a resilient barrier tolerates retinoids/acids better). Strong candidate to **anchor a signature barrier-renewal moisturizer/cream as a hero**, and a supporting lipid base across the line.

## Formulation notes
- **Effective concentration:** Used at low total % but **ratio matters more than dose** — the evidence-backed approach is a **physiological blend of ceramides + cholesterol + free fatty acids** (often cited around a 3:1:1 / 1:1:1 family ratio); cholesterol-dominant blends repair fastest.
- **pH:** Compatible with normal skin-care pH (~5–6).
- **Stability:** Lipids; require proper emulsification and antioxidant protection. Generally robust in well-made creams/emulsions.
- **Pairs well with:** Niacinamide (which *itself* upregulates ceramide synthesis — strong synergy story), cholesterol & fatty acids, hyaluronic acid, fatty alcohols/occlusives, and as a buffer alongside retinoids/AHAs.
- **Conflicts:** None significant. Best in richer emulsion/cream formats rather than watery serums.
- **Sensory:** Rich, cushioning, comforting — premium "balm/cream" feel.

## Concerns & cautions
- **Irritation:** Very low; suitable for sensitive/compromised skin.
- **Sun sensitivity:** None.
- **Pregnancy:** Considered safe topically.
- **Sourcing/allergens:** Modern ceramides are typically **synthetic/bio-identical or plant-derived** (compatible with vegan/clean formulation); some legacy ceramides were animal-derived — specify plant/synthetic sourcing. Low allergen risk.

## Claims caution
- ✅ **Safe cosmetic language:** "helps restore and strengthen the look and feel of the skin barrier," "replenishes skin's essential lipids," "locks in moisture / reduces moisture loss appearance," "comforts dry, compromised-looking skin," "for resilient, renewed-feeling skin." (Note: "barrier repair" is widely used cosmetic language — keep it tied to support/appearance, not disease.)
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like:** "treats eczema/atopic dermatitis" (a disease claim — even though much evidence comes from eczema studies, marketing must not claim to treat it), "cures dryness," "rebuilds your skin." Cite eczema data internally for credibility, not in consumer claims.

## Cost / sourcing
- **Moderate** (true ceramides cost more than basic emollients; physiological blends + good emulsion add cost). Worth it for the central barrier story. Widely available (Evonik, Croda, others); low supply risk.
