# Ingredient: Saccharide Isomerate (INCI: Saccharide Isomerate)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Humectants & hydration actives (skin-conditioning agent — humectant; "skin-identical" carbohydrate complex; trade name **Pentavitin®**)

> **One-line honest summary:** Saccharide Isomerate is a genuinely useful, very well-tolerated, substantive humectant whose distinctive selling point — it *binds to keratin and resists wash-off, delivering longer-lasting hydration* — is real and mechanistically sensible. But it is fundamentally a **hydration ingredient, not an anti-aging actives**. The "72-hour hydration" and most efficacy data come from the **supplier (DSM/dsm-firmenich)**; independent peer-reviewed human topical data are thin (essentially one 2023 scalp RCT and one 2025 post-IPL split-face study, both small). The 2023 scalp study is authored entirely by DSM employees (direct conflict of interest); the 2025 post-IPL study declares **no conflict of interest** but still tests a commercial Pentavitin-containing formula. It has **no credible evidence for collagen, glycation, senescence, photoaging reversal, or any longevity hallmark.** Treat it as a strong **supporting humectant**, not a hero anti-aging claim.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Saccharide Isomerate. Best-known trade name **Pentavitin®** (dsm-firmenich, originally Pentapharm/DSM). Also marketed by others as generic "saccharide isomerate" and in "72-hour hydration" blends.
- **INCI:** **Saccharide Isomerate**
- **CAS:** **100843-69-4** [10]
- **Chemical class / definition:** Per CIR, "a carbohydrate complex formed from a base-catalyzed rearrangement of a mixture of saccharides." [10] The Pentavitin grade is made by **base-catalyzed isomerization of plant-derived D-glucose from corn kernels**, yielding a **mixture of mono- and disaccharides — mainly glucose and fructose** — that mimics the carbohydrate fraction of the skin's natural moisturizing factor (NMF). [9][10]
- **Not a single molecule.** It is a defined *mixture*. The classic low-MW Pentavitin grade has **MW ~120–400 Da**; CIR notes other "saccharide isomerate" trade materials exist at much higher MW (**15,000 Da, 20,000 Da, >1.4 MDa**) with different osidic compositions (e.g., rhamnose/glucose/galactose/galacturonic acid/N-acetylglucosamine). [10] So "saccharide isomerate" on a label is *not* guaranteed to be Pentavitin or the same composition — verify on the COA.
- **Composition of the Pentavitin grade (DSM data):** carbohydrate fraction broadly mirroring stratum-corneum sugars — approx. glucose 57%, fructose 26.5%, mannose 4.2%, psicose 3.9%, galactose 3.3%, various 5% (compared with SC's glucose 46.7%, fructose 31.1%, etc.); the finished raw material also contains **water, citric acid, and sodium citrate** and is **produced without solvents** (no residual solvent). [9][10]
- **Physical/chemistry:** Water-soluble (miscible with water at all ratios); the low-MW form is a hygroscopic sugar mixture. Highly hydrophilic. **Stable pH 3–6** (per DSM); typically supplied as an aqueous solution. [9]
- **Origin / certifications:** 100% natural origin; the Pentavitin grade is Ecocert/COSMOS-approved, NATRUE-certified, Halal-certified, preservative-free, China-listed, low carbon footprint. [9][1]

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it works)

Saccharide Isomerate is a **substantive humectant** — its whole differentiation rests on *how* it holds water, not on any signaling/anti-aging activity.

1. **Keratin binding → substantivity.** The sugars bind to the **free ε-amino groups of lysine** in keratin of the cornified envelope (a Maillard-type interaction), giving it strong affinity for the stratum corneum. Once bound, it is **not easily washed off** and is removed only by **natural desquamation** — the basis of the "moisture magnet"/"long-lasting" positioning. [9][10][11]
2. **NMF mimicry / water reservoir.** Because its carbohydrate profile resembles the SC's endogenous carbohydrate complex, it acts as a humectant reservoir that "regulates and retains moisture" even under low humidity, where simple humectants (glycerin, urea) lose efficacy. [9]
3. **Substrate-quality hydration > osmotic pull.** Unlike glycerin/HA which mostly hold free water osmotically, the binding mechanism means hydration persists between applications (the "72-hour" claim is built on this retention + buildup with repeat use). [9][12]
4. **Claimed secondary/barrier signaling (weaker tier).** Supplier/in-vitro material reports that it upregulates **filaggrin** (a key NMF precursor) and barrier genes (e.g., **hyaluronan synthase-3 / HAS3**, occludin) in keratinocyte models — proposed to indirectly boost endogenous NMF and tight-junction integrity. [4][12] These are **mechanistic/in-vitro claims, largely supplier-driven**, not established human outcomes.

**Honest mechanistic read:** Pillars 1–2 are well-characterized and physically plausible — this is a legitimately *better-than-average humectant for durability*. Pillar 4 (filaggrin/HAS3 "boosting") is the marketing bridge toward "barrier/anti-aging" language and rests on weaker, mostly proprietary data.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Plausibility for Saccharide Isomerate | Evidence tier |
|---|---|---|
| **Hydration / TEWL / dryness** | **Strong (this is its job)** — durable SC hydration, reduced TEWL, less flaking/itch | Human topical (supplier RCT-grade + 2 peer-reviewed studies) [5][9][13][14] |
| **Barrier decline** | Plausible/moderate — reinforces SC water-holding; claimed filaggrin/occludin support | Human topical (TEWL endpoints) + in-vitro gene data [9][12][13][14] |
| **Inflammaging / soothing** | Weak-moderate — reduces *dryness-driven* stinging/irritation (e.g., AHA, post-cleanse, post-IPL discomfort) | Human/supplier (anti-irritation, post-IPL comfort) [9][14] |
| **Microbiome** | Speculative — sugars are theoretically substrate; no real skin-microbiome efficacy data | None for SI specifically |
| **Pigmentation / tone** | Not a mechanism — only "tone looks better when hydrated" | None |
| **Collagen / ECM loss** | **No** — no collagen/elastin mechanism or data | None |
| **Glycation / AGEs** | **No** (and note irony: it is a *sugar mixture* that binds via Maillard chemistry — the opposite of an anti-glycation actives) | None |
| **Cellular senescence** | **No** | None |
| **Mitochondrial decline** | **No** | None |
| **Photoaging (UV reversal)** | **No** — not photoprotective/repair; only supports recovery indirectly via hydration | None (post-IPL recovery ≠ photoaging reversal) |
| **Oxidative stress** | **No** (some saccharide humectants are *listed* as antioxidant function, but SI has no antioxidant efficacy data) | None |

**Net:** It targets exactly **one** real aging-relevant axis — **hydration and the dryness/TEWL component of barrier decline** — plus a soft "soothing/comfort" benefit. It does **not** touch the structural or cellular hallmarks of skin aging.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** The efficacy story is dominated by the **manufacturer (DSM / dsm-firmenich) and trade literature**, much of it in supplier brochures and trade magazines (Cosmetics & Toiletries, Personal Care), not independent peer-reviewed journals. There are now **two** genuinely peer-reviewed human topical studies (a 2023 scalp RCT and a 2025 post-IPL split-face study), but **both are small** and both test a commercial Pentavitin-containing formula: the 2023 study is authored entirely by DSM employees (direct COI), while the 2025 study declares **no conflict of interest** (its authors are an independent clinical group, not DSM). There is essentially **no independent, large, peer-reviewed RCT** of saccharide isomerate on facial photoaging. Crucially, **none of the evidence is about anti-aging** — it is all about **hydration/barrier/comfort**.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell models)

- **Keratinocyte gene-expression / DNA-microarray (supplier-attributed).** Saccharide isomerate reported to upregulate **filaggrin** and **hyaluronan synthase-3 (HAS3)** in normal human epidermal keratinocytes (NHEK), interpreted as boosting NMF + hyaluronan; separate bioassay data cite **filaggrin +~28%** and **occludin +~170%**. *Limitation: these figures circulate via trade/marketing channels (Cosmetics & Toiletries and supplier material), are proprietary, lack independent replication, and are direct-dosing on cells (not transdermal).* [4][12]
- **General humectant in-vitro behavior.** DSM "Moisture Regulation Test" (in-vitro, 1.5% active across relative humidities at 37 °C) showed **better moisture regulation than glycerin or urea, especially at low RH.** *Supplier method.* [9]

> Note: CIR's broader saccharide-humectant review includes in-vitro work on *related* sugars (e.g., anti-melanogenesis of Anhydrogalactose, barrier-recovery of Psicose) — **these are different ingredients** and should NOT be borrowed as saccharide-isomerate evidence. [10]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)

- **No efficacy animal studies** specific to skin aging or hydration mechanism. Animal data on saccharide isomerate are **toxicology only** (acute dermal/oral, irritation, sensitization, photo — see §7). [10] (A tape-strip barrier-recovery study exists for **Psicose** in hairless mice, but that is a different ingredient. [10])

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL

- **None / not applicable.** Saccharide Isomerate is a topical cosmetic ingredient; it is not used or studied as an oral supplement. (Its constituent sugars — glucose/fructose — are dietary, but that is irrelevant to skin claims.)

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters — and what's actually here)

**(a) Foundational supplier efficacy (in-vivo human, supplier-run, non-peer-reviewed):**
- **DSM "Chronic Moisturization" in-vivo test.** Vs glycerin, saccharide isomerate gave **up to +43% more moisturization on dry skin and +42% on normal skin (p<0.05).** *Supplier brochure; small/undisclosed n; comparator and methods supplier-defined.* [9]
- **DSM "Moisture Accumulation Test" (in-vivo).** Lower surface moisture accumulation = better retention into the skin vs control/glycerin. *Supplier method, proprietary.* [9]
- **DSM "Anti-irritation"/lactic-acid sting study.** Reduced AHA-induced stinging score over ~28 days vs placebo. *Supplier; supports a "reduces dryness/irritation from AHAs" claim.* [9]
- **"72-hour hydration" origin.** The headline "hydration lasts 72 h after a single leave-on application" traces to **Cosmetics & Toiletries 2012;127(9):636–643** (DSM-authored trade-journal article), built on the buildup/substantivity mechanism. *This is the most-cited claim and it is **trade-journal/supplier**, not an independent RCT.* [4][9]
- **Rinse-off clinical studies (Rosenberger et al., Personal Care magazine, 2021, DSM authors).**
  - *Study 1:* RCT, **n=50 Caucasian women, 25–45, dry skin**, soap-free shower gel (pH~5) with **0.2% or 0.5%** SI vs placebo; volar forearm, twice daily D1–D6, evaluated D0/1h/24h/D7 (DermaLab conductance, Tewameter). SI arms **preserved/boosted hydration for 24h and through D7** and **reduced TEWL** (barrier reinforcement) vs placebo (placebo *lost* hydration). Self-report: 75% softer/less tight, 71% more moisturized. *Trade magazine, not peer-reviewed; industry authors.* [13]
  - *Study 2:* **n=33 Asian women, 21–65, dry skin**, soap-based body wash (pH~9), 0.2%/0.5% SI; MoistureMeter SC at D0/1h/D7. SI moved skin **from dry to normal within 1h, maintained to D7 (p<0.001)** vs placebo. *Same caveats.* [13]

**(b) Peer-reviewed human topical (the two real journal studies — both small; one DSM-authored, one independent):**
- **Martin, Zhang & Campiche, 2023, *Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology* 22(1):262–266 (PMID 35279940; DOI 10.1111/jocd.14913).** Randomized, placebo-controlled clinical study in **adult Chinese subjects with dandruff/flaky scalp**, formulations with saccharide isomerate (Pentavitin), **28 days**; measured **TEWL, sebum, scalp flaking.** SI formulations **significantly improved all parameters over time and vs placebo** (one secondary write-up cites ~28% TEWL reduction). *Limitations: scalp not face, modest n, primary endpoints are cosmetic-scalp condition; **all three authors are DSM employees** (direct conflict of interest).* [5]
- **Shao et al., 2025, *Journal of Dermatological Treatment* 36(1):2585243 (DOI 10.1080/09546634.2025.2585243).** **Randomized split-face, n=30** subjects with skin aging receiving a **single IPL (intense pulsed light) treatment**; a saccharide-isomerate facial moisturizer on one cheek vs a control moisturizer on the other, **twice daily × 7 days.** The SI side showed **significantly greater improvement in all biometric parameters (corneometry/hydration, TEWL/barrier, erythema) — all p<0.05 — and significantly less subjective discomfort.** Authors conclude it accelerates barrier recovery post-photoelectric procedures. *Limitations: very small n=30, only 7 days, **post-procedure recovery context (not chronic anti-aging)**, "test moisturizer" is a full formula (effect not isolated to SI alone), and the comparator is another moisturizer — so this measures a finished-formula effect, not the ingredient in isolation. On independence: the paper **declares no conflict of interest** and the authors are a clinical group (not DSM employees), which is a genuine point in its favor versus the 2023 study; but it still evaluates a commercial Pentavitin-containing product, so it is not fully ingredient-independent. Read as **a hydration/recovery result, not a wrinkle/photoaging result.*** [14]

**Bottom line by tier:** **Proven (reasonably) for SI:** durable topical **hydration**, **reduced TEWL**, **reduced dryness/flaking/sting**, including in rinse-off and post-IPL recovery — but mostly via **supplier data** plus **two small peer-reviewed studies (one DSM-authored, one independent-COI but on a commercial formula).** **Not proven for SI:** anything structural or "anti-aging" — no collagen, no wrinkle, no photoaging reversal, no senescence/mitochondrial/glycation effect.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **It is designed NOT to penetrate deeply — it works at the surface/stratum corneum.** Its mechanism is **corneocyte/keratin binding in the SC**, persisting until desquamation; "deep hydration" in marketing refers to **the lower scaly layers of the stratum corneum**, not the dermis. [9][10]
- **Penetration prospects (low-MW grade, 120–400 Da):** small and water-soluble in principle, but **highly hydrophilic** (sugar mixture, many H-bond donors/acceptors) → poor passage through the lipophilic SC, like other small hydrophilic sugars/humectants. CIR notes the supplier **did not perform dermal-absorption studies** (deemed unnecessary as it is sugars + water + citrate). [10]
- **High-MW "saccharide isomerate" grades (15k–>1.4 MDa)** would not be expected to be percutaneously absorbed at all (CIR). [10]
- **Practical read:** Bioavailability isn't the relevant question — SI is a **surface/SC-resident humectant by design**, and that's where its benefit lives. Don't claim dermal/cellular delivery.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** Typical/recommended **1–5%** (DSM suggested range); CIR survey shows real-world leave-on use **up to ~2.8%** (max reported was face/neck care) and rinse-off up to ~0.7% (clinical rinse-off studies used 0.2–0.5%). Eye-area products up to ~1%. **A practical, well-supported leave-on level is ~2–5%; rinse-off ~0.2–0.5%.** [9][10][13]
- **pH:** **Stable pH 3–6** (DSM). Good fit for low-pH systems (vitamin C, AHAs, exfoliating toners) — and it can *reduce* AHA-related stinging, a nice pairing rationale. [9]
- **Phase / processing:** Water-soluble; add to the **aqueous phase**. Can be processed warm (**<80 °C**) or cold. Easy to incorporate, including in surfactant systems. [9][13]
- **Stability:** Robust as an aqueous sugar solution within pH 3–6; preservative-free as supplied so **the finished formula must be adequately preserved** (sugars are microbial substrate). Standard discoloration vigilance for sugar-containing systems (Maillard browning risk with amines/high heat over time) — run stability + challenge testing.
- **Pairings (sensible):** Glycerin and **hyaluronic acid** (complementary humectant kinetics — fast/osmotic HA + durable/substantive SI), **ceramides + niacinamide** (barrier thesis), **panthenol/allantoin** (soothing), **AHAs/retinoids** (offsets dryness/sting). In rinse-off it can even **boost foam volume** (~+43% in one DSM test). [9][13]
- **Conflicts:** No notable incompatibilities reported; very forgiving. Avoid unnecessary high-heat hold with reactive amines to limit browning.
- **Sensory:** Light, non-tacky at use levels, water-thin; pleasant "slip"; contributes a soft, hydrated after-feel without the stickiness of high glycerin. Good for a premium sensorial profile.

## 7. Safety

Saccharide Isomerate has a **strong, well-documented safety profile** — one of the cleaner humectants on this axis. Primary source: **CIR Final Report, 2021** (Expert Panel for Cosmetic Ingredient Safety; assessment of saccharide humectants including Saccharide Isomerate). [10]

- **CIR conclusion:** **Safe in cosmetics in the present practices of use and concentration.** [10]
- **Irritation:** Non-irritating/non-corrosive in animal (OECD TG 404) and **human occlusive patch** tests (20% v/v and 0.5–1.5% trade materials all negative). [10]
- **Sensitization:** Non-sensitizer in guinea-pig maximization (OECD TG 406). **Multiple HRIPTs negative**, including a large **HRIPT of an eye cream with 2.75% SI in 213 subjects** (no irritation/sensitization), plus HRIPTs of 100/102/109/52 subjects on other SI trade materials — **no contact allergy.** [10]
- **Genotoxicity:** **Non-genotoxic** — negative Ames (OECD TG 471, ±metabolic activation) and micronucleus (OECD TG 487) for the low-MW grade and multiple high-MW grades. [10]
- **Phototoxicity / sun:** **Not phototoxic / not photosensitizing** (in-vitro OECD TG 432 and guinea-pig studies negative). **No photosensitivity concern; does not increase sun sensitivity.** (It is also not a sunscreen — provides no UV protection.) [10]
- **Ocular:** Practically non-irritating to eyes (animal OECD TG 405); a 2.75% SI eye cream showed **no ocular irritation potential** in 53–56 female subjects (incl. contact-lens and sensitive-eye wearers). Suitable for eye-area products. [10]
- **Acute toxicity:** Oral **LD50 > 2 g/kg** (undiluted, OECD TG 401). [10]
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** **No specific reproductive/developmental data exist** (CIR notes the absence), but the Panel judged concerns **mitigated because the ingredient is predominantly glucose + fructose** (ubiquitous, well-understood sugars) and is not meaningfully absorbed. No pregnancy red flag for topical cosmetic use; still use conservative "consult your doctor" copy as a default. [10]
- **Sensitive skin / allergen:** Very low sensitization signal; no fragrance/allergen content inherent to the pure ingredient. Theoretical caution only: rare sugar-related contact urticaria has been reported for *other* sugars (e.g., a psicose case report), not for saccharide isomerate. [10]
- **Sourcing caution:** Corn-derived — note for corn-allergy-aware customers (contact allergy risk is very low, but relevant to trust/labeling). Panel flagged generic **heavy-metals/cGMP** diligence for any saccharide. [10]

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Status:** Established cosmetic ingredient; INCI **Saccharide Isomerate**, function **skin-conditioning agent — humectant.** **Not restricted in the EU** (CosIng / Reg. 1223/2009). CIR "safe as used." China-listed; widely used (CIR cites 352 cosmetic products in 2021). [10]
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** Hydration/moisturization/barrier-*appearance* language sits comfortably in **cosmetic** territory. The risk is **over-reaching into anti-aging structure/function claims the evidence does not support** (collagen, wrinkle "treatment," "anti-aging at the cellular level") — those would be unsubstantiated and/or drug-like.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language (well-supported):** "delivers long-lasting hydration," "helps skin hold onto moisture," "binds to skin for moisture that resists wash-off," "helps reduce the look of dryness, flaking and tightness," "supports the skin's moisture barrier," "helps keep skin comfortable" (and, with our own data, "hydration that lasts up to X hours"). The **"72-hour"** number is defensible only if we either (a) cite/own data for our finished formula or (b) clearly attribute it to the ingredient supplier — **don't state it as our product's proven performance without our own test.**
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation or rephrase:** "anti-aging," "boosts collagen," "reverses wrinkles," "repairs the barrier" (prefer "supports"), "boosts hyaluronic acid by X%" / "increases filaggrin by 28%" (proprietary in-vitro — flag for substantiation/legal before use), "heals," "treats eczema/dry-skin disease."
- **Substantiation reality:** Ingredient-level hydration/TEWL claims are reasonably backed; **any number we print** (hours of hydration, % TEWL/hydration improvement, filaggrin %) should be tied to a citable source or our **own finished-formula testing.** See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Forms/suppliers:** Branded **Pentavitin®** from **dsm-firmenich** (the reference grade, MW 120–400 Da, corn-derived, multiple certifications). Generic "saccharide isomerate" available from many distributors (e.g., specialty actives suppliers, makingcosmetics-type houses) — **but composition/MW can differ from Pentavitin**, so spec it on the COA. [9][10]
- **Cost tier:** **Low–moderate.** It is essentially a refined sugar complex in water; raw-material cost is modest. The branded, certified Pentavitin grade carries a premium over commodity humectants (glycerin) but is far cheaper than peptides/growth factors. Good value for the durability benefit.
- **Supply/standardization risk:** Main risk is **ingredient ambiguity** ("saccharide isomerate" ≠ guaranteed Pentavitin). For a premium brand, specify the **Pentavitin grade** (or an equivalent with a defined glucose/fructose profile and low MW) for the substantivity story to hold. Sustainability is a selling point (natural, low carbon footprint, Ecocert/COSMOS/NATRUE). [9]

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **"72-hour hydration."** Real *concept* (substantivity + buildup) but the headline figure is **supplier/trade-journal-derived** (C&T 2012) and applies to *the ingredient under DSM's test conditions*, not automatically to our formula. Print it only with attribution or our own data. [4][9]
- **"Hydration hero / moisture magnet."** Fair as positioning — it genuinely is more *durable* than glycerin in supplier comparisons (+42–43%). Just keep it a **hydration** claim. [9]
- **Creeping "anti-aging" framing.** Some retailers file it under "anti-ageing." This is **a stretch** — there is **no collagen, wrinkle, or photoaging evidence.** The only aging link is "dry skin looks older; hydrated skin looks better," which is real but cosmetic and temporary.
- **"Boosts hyaluronic acid 66%" / "filaggrin +28%" / "occludin +170%."** These are **proprietary in-vitro figures** from supplier/trade material — not independently validated human outcomes. Use with caution and substantiation; don't imply they happen in the consumer's skin.
- **Ironic glycation angle.** It binds keratin via **Maillard (glycation) chemistry** and is a **sugar mixture** — so it is emphatically **not** an anti-glycation/anti-AGE actives. Never let copy imply otherwise.
- **Post-IPL study ≠ photoaging reversal.** The 2025 split-face result is a **barrier-recovery/comfort** finding after a procedure, n=30, 7 days — do not translate it into "repairs sun damage." [14]
- **Independence gap.** Most efficacy traces back to **DSM** (brochures, trade articles, and the 2023 peer-reviewed scalp RCT, which is authored entirely by DSM employees). The 2025 post-IPL study is the most independent piece of evidence — it declares no COI and its authors are not DSM staff — but it still tests a commercial Pentavitin formula and is tiny (n=30, 7 days). Net: solid for hydration; still thin on large, fully ingredient-independent data.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING — an excellent, low-risk hydration workhorse; not an anti-aging hero.**
**One-line:** Saccharide Isomerate (Pentavitin) is a premium-feeling, exceptionally well-tolerated, *substantive* humectant that delivers genuinely **durable hydration and TEWL reduction** — a great **supporting** ingredient for a barrier-repair cream — but it carries **no real anti-aging/longevity mechanism**, and its strongest data are supplier-generated, so it should never be positioned as a hero "anti-aging actives."

**Where it fits our thesis:** It maps cleanly onto the **barrier-repair / hydration** pillar (alongside ceramides, niacinamide, HA, glycerin), adds a differentiated, *evidence-light-but-plausible* "long-lasting / wash-resistant moisture" story, pairs well with potentially-drying actives (retinoids/AHAs) by **reducing their sting/dryness**, and is **eye-area and sensitive-skin safe**. It is cheap-to-moderate, sustainable, and easy to formulate — a high-ROI supporting pick.

**Longevity-relevance note:** For a *skin-longevity* brand, be disciplined: this is a **"keep skin hydrated and comfortable so it looks healthier today"** ingredient, not a hallmark-of-aging actives. Its honest role is to **protect/maintain the barrier** (which *is* part of healthy aging skin) and to **make the actual anti-aging actives more tolerable**. Frame it as comfort/hydration/barrier support — not as cellular or structural rejuvenation.
