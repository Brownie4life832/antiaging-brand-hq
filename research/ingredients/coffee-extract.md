# Ingredient: Coffee / Green Coffee (Chlorogenic acid, Caffeine) — and green coffee seed oil

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Slug:** coffee-extract · **Class:** Botanicals & plant bioactives

> **Reading note.** "Coffee extract" in cosmetics is not one ingredient — it is an umbrella over at least four distinct materials that behave very differently: (1) **chlorogenic acid (CGA)**, the dominant water-soluble polyphenol antioxidant of the green (unroasted) bean; (2) **caffeine**, a methylxanthine alkaloid with its own pharmacology (lipolysis, vasoconstriction, ATR-kinase inhibition); (3) **green coffee seed oil** (*Coffea arabica* seed oil), a linoleic-acid-rich emollient with a separate ECM/hydration story; and (4) whole-bean/by-product extracts (silverskin, spent grounds) that contain a mix of all of the above plus melanoidins. The marketing collapses these into "antioxidant coffee glow." This dossier keeps them separate and is explicit about which format each claim is actually proven in. **Headline honesty: the mechanistic and in-vitro evidence is genuinely strong and the antioxidant chemistry is real, but rigorous human TOPICAL RCTs on facial photoaging endpoints are thin-to-absent — most human data is either ORAL (coffee/polyphenol intake) or in adjacent indications (cellulite, hair, dark circles).**

---

## 0. The honest punchline (read this first)

- **Chlorogenic acid** is a legitimately potent antioxidant polyphenol with consistent, mechanistically coherent in-vitro and animal data for photoaging (↓ROS, ↓MMP-1/3, ↑collagen I, ↓UV-induced apoptosis/DNA damage). What it largely **lacks is a published facial-photoaging human topical RCT**. The human signal that exists is **dietary/oral** (coffee/polyphenol intake correlating with fewer pigment spots and better barrier).
- **Caffeine** is the best-characterized member for *penetration* and has real, peer-reviewed mechanisms (ATR/Chk1-mediated UVB-damage clearance; phosphodiesterase inhibition → lipolysis; adenosine-receptor-mediated vasoconstriction). Its human evidence is strongest in **cellulite (moderate, small trials), hair loss, and periorbital dark circles/puffiness** — *not* in facial wrinkle reduction. Two cautions matter: in vitro, caffeine **inhibits keratinocyte proliferation and collagen biosynthesis** — i.e. it is not a collagen-builder and could theoretically work against barrier renewal at high doses.
- **Green coffee seed oil** rests on essentially **one industry/in-house paper** (Velazquez Pereda 2009, a manufacturer-affiliated *J Cosmet Dermatol* study) showing dose-dependent collagen/elastin/GAG synthesis and a striking AQP-3 (aquaporin/hydration) upregulation in-vitro/ex-vivo, plus a small TEWL-reduction in-use signal at 15%. Single-source and un-replicated, it is best read as a **good emollient with an antioxidant halo**, not a proven actives delivery system.
- **Net:** Coffee is a **credible supporting-tier botanical antioxidant with an excellent brand/sustainability story** (upcycled silverskin/spent grounds), strong mechanism, but **borrowed-format human evidence**. Treat it as a supporting antioxidant / sensory-and-story ingredient, not as a hero "anti-wrinkle" claim driver. Caffeine specifically earns its keep in **eye/contour and de-puffing** contexts.

## 1. Identity

| **Key actives** | **Chlorogenic acid** (a family of caffeoylquinic / feruloylquinic acids; the main one is 5-O-caffeoylquinic acid) and **caffeine** (1,3,7-trimethylxanthine, a methylxanthine alkaloid). |
| **INCI (common forms)** | **Coffea Arabica (Coffee) Seed Extract**; **Coffea Arabica Seed Oil** (green coffee seed oil); **Caffeine**; **Chlorogenic Acids** (or listed as part of a standardized extract); **Coffea Arabica Seed Powder** (physical exfoliant); silverskin appears as **Coffea Arabica Seed Coat / Husk Extract** depending on supplier. |
| **CAS** | Caffeine 58-08-2; Chlorogenic acid 327-97-9; *Coffea arabica* seed oil 84650-00-0 / 8001-67-0 (EC 283-481-1). |
| **Forms in market** | Water/glycol or ethanol extracts standardized to CGA and/or caffeine; isolated caffeine (USP); isolated/standardized CGA; **green coffee seed oil** (cold-pressed, linoleic-rich emollient); **upcycled by-product extracts** — coffee silverskin (CGA ~1–6%, caffeine ~0.8–1.25%, melanoidins ~17–23%) and spent coffee grounds; ground-bean exfoliant powder. |

**Why they are grouped (and why that's lazy):** the green bean is the natural co-source of both CGA (the antioxidant) and caffeine (the methylxanthine), so a single "green coffee extract" can carry both. But their mechanisms, evidence, formulation behavior and even *direction of effect on collagen* differ. We will not let a single "coffee antioxidant" claim quietly borrow caffeine's penetration data, CGA's antioxidant data, and the oil's hydration data all at once.

## 2. Class & mechanism

**Class:** Botanicals & plant bioactives — specifically a **polyphenol antioxidant (CGA)** + **methylxanthine alkaloid (caffeine)** + **fatty/emollient oil (green coffee seed oil)**.

**Chlorogenic acid — mechanism:**
- Direct **free-radical scavenging** (DPPH, superoxide, hydroxyl radicals) and quenching of intracellular ROS from H₂O₂ and UV (Cha 2014 [4]).
- **UV absorption** in the UVB range (~280–320 nm) — i.e. mild intrinsic photofilter behavior, not a sunscreen (Cha 2014 [4]).
- **ECM protection:** in UVA-irradiated human dermal fibroblasts, CGA ↑collagen I (via TGF-β/Smad2/3), ↓MMP-1 and ↓MMP-3, ↓ROS, ↓γ-H2AX (DNA damage), ↑Rad51 (repair), and reduced late apoptosis from 18.4%→1.8% at 30 µM (Xue 2022 [1]).
- **Anti-inflammatory:** suppresses NF-κB translocation, COX-2, iNOS, IL-6, TNF-α in UV/TNF-α-stressed fibroblasts (Wu 2017 [5]; Peucedanum-CGA study [16]).
- **Anti-melanogenic:** modest tyrosinase/melanogenesis effects on B16 melanoma cells (cytostatic + signaling), supporting a "tone/spots" narrative (B16 study [10]).

**Caffeine — mechanism:**
- **ATR/Chk1 inhibition** → abrogates the UVB-induced cell-cycle checkpoint, forcing DNA-damaged keratinocytes into apoptosis/"lethal mitosis," clearing precancerous cells (Conney 2013 [6]; Kawasumi/Nghiem [7]).
- **Phosphodiesterase inhibition** → ↑intracellular cAMP → **lipolysis** in adipocytes (the cellulite/slimming rationale).
- **Adenosine-receptor antagonism** → transient **vasoconstriction** of superficial capillaries (the dark-circle / de-puffing / redness rationale).
- **Antioxidant / lipid-peroxidation inhibition** and **sunscreen-adjuvant** behavior (photostabilizes filters; ~25% UVB-protection boost when added at 2.5% to a filter system) (Rosado/Frontiers 2019 [8]).
- **Caution — anti-proliferative:** in vitro caffeine *restricts* keratinocyte proliferation and migration and **inhibits collagen biosynthesis dose-dependently** (Elias 2023 [9]). So caffeine is a damage-clearer / circulatory agent, **not** a collagen-builder.

**Green coffee seed oil — mechanism:** linoleic-acid-rich triglyceride emollient; in fibroblasts it dose-dependently ↑collagen, elastin, GAG and growth factors (TGF-β1, GM-CSF) and strongly ↑**AQP-3** (aquaporin-3 water channel) mRNA (up to 6.5× control), the basis of a hydration/barrier story (Velazquez Pereda 2009 [3]).

## 4. Evidence review by tier (the core of this dossier)

> Tiers, weakest-to-strongest in rigor for *cosmetic relevance*: **in-vitro → animal → human ORAL → human TOPICAL (RCT vs lower)**. For each claim I flag whether it is **proven for human topical use** or **borrowed** from another format/route/indication.

### TIER A — In-vitro (cell / biochemistry) — *the strongest part of coffee's evidence*

**A1. CGA prevents UVA photoaging in human dermal fibroblasts (flagship CGA paper).**
- Xue N, Liu Y, Jin J, Ji M, Chen X. *Int J Mol Sci* 2022;23(13):7691. PMC9266774. [1]
- **Design:** human dermal fibroblasts (HDF-α, CCC-ESF-1), UVA 12 J/cm², CGA 0.1–50 µM (focus 3–30 µM); plus BioMAP profiling.
- **Findings:** ↑Col I (via TGF-β/Smad2/3, ERK unchanged), ↓MMP-1, ↓MMP-3 (p≤0.01), ↓ROS at 30 µM (p<0.05), ↓γ-H2AX, ↑Rad51, late apoptosis 18.4%→1.8% at 30 µM.
- **Limitations:** in-vitro only; single cell type; no epidermal-dermal interaction; **no human/in-vivo validation.**

**A2. CGA attenuates UVB oxidative stress in HaCaT keratinocytes.**
- Cha JW, et al. *Biomol Ther (Seoul)* 2014;22(2):136–142. PMC3975475. [4]
- **Findings:** scavenges DPPH/superoxide/hydroxyl/intracellular ROS; absorbs UVB (280–320 nm); pre-treatment ↓comet-assay DNA damage, ↑viability, ↓apoptotic bodies, preserved mitochondrial membrane potential, modulated Bcl-2/Bax/caspase-3.
- **Limitations:** immortalized keratinocyte line; mechanistic, not efficacy.

**A3. CGA anti-aging/anti-inflammatory in UV- and TNF-α-stressed fibroblasts.**
- "Anti-inflammatory and antiaging properties of chlorogenic acid on UV-induced fibroblast cell." *PeerJ* 2021. PMC8272460. [13]
- CGA-from-*Peucedanum japonicum* in TNF-α-stimulated NHDFs: ↓intracellular ROS, ↓MMP-1, ↑COL1A1 — "most potent" of six compounds tested. *Front Pharmacol/related* 2025. PMC12734488. [16]
- **Limitations:** in-vitro; some are plant-extract (CGA as marker), confounding attribution to CGA alone.

**A4. CGA anti-melanogenesis.** Effect of CGA on B16 melanoma melanogenesis (cytostatic at 30–60 µM; tyrosinase-pathway signaling). PMC6271456. [10] — supports a *tone* story but is mouse-melanoma in-vitro.

**A5. Caffeine mechanism (in-vitro / cell).** ATR depletion abolishes caffeine's pro-apoptotic effect in primary human keratinocytes (caffeine >2× apoptosis after 75 mJ/cm² UVB) — Heffernan/Kawasumi work [7]. **Caution:** caffeine inhibits keratinocyte proliferation/migration and collagen biosynthesis in vitro (Elias 2023 review [9]) — i.e. not a builder.

### TIER B — Animal (topical and oral)

**B1. *Coffea arabica* extract (CGA 48.3 mg/g) attenuates UVB photodamage — fibroblasts + TOPICAL in hairless mice.**
- Wu PY, et al. *Int J Mol Sci* 2017;18(4):782. PMC5412366. [5]
- **Mouse arm:** BALB/c hairless mice, **topical** CAE (100–200 µg/mL in glycerol, 50 µL) 10 wk + UVB.
- **Findings:** ↓epidermal thickness (84.6→35.4–41.5 µm), restored dermal collagen, ↓wrinkles, ↓TEWL, ↓erythema (a*), ↓MMP-1/iNOS/IL-6/NF-κB.
- **Limitations:** mouse; small; not human; topical dose not directly translatable.

**B2. *Aronia*/CGA-rich extract reduces UVB skin damage (TOPICAL, mice).**
- Topical application of *Aronia melanocarpa* extract rich in CGA + rutin. *Antioxidants/Nutrients* 2020. PMC7582310. [12]
- ↑Col I/III, ↓MMP-1/3 immunoreactivity, ameliorated epidermal damage.
- **Limitations:** multi-polyphenol extract (rutin co-present); mouse.

**B3. Caffeine inhibits UVB carcinogenesis (oral + topical, mice; mechanistic).**
- Conney AH, Lu YP, Lou YR, Kawasumi M, Nghiem P. *Front Oncol* 2013;3:144. PMC3683821. [6]
- Topical/oral caffeine ↓thymine dimers, ↑apoptosis via ATR/Chk1; transgenic low-ATR mice had **69% fewer tumors**.
- **Limitations:** anti-cancer endpoint, not cosmetic anti-aging; mouse + transgenic.

### TIER C — Human ORAL (real signal, but dietary/systemic, not a topical product)

**C1. Oral coffee/polyphenol intake ↔ fewer UV pigment spots (cross-sectional).**
- Fukushima Y, et al. "Skin photoprotection and consumption of coffee and polyphenols in healthy middle-aged Japanese females." *Int J Dermatol* 2015;54(4):410–418. PMID 25041334. [11]
- Higher coffee/CGA polyphenol consumption correlated with **lower UV pigmented-spot scores** (statistically significant); measured hydration, TEWL, elasticity.
- **Limitations:** **observational/correlational**, not interventional; female Japanese cohort; oral; confounded by overall diet/lifestyle. Cannot support a topical claim.

**C2. Oral coffee polyphenols improve barrier & microcirculation (RCT).**
- Fukagawa S, et al. "Coffee polyphenols extracted from green coffee beans improve skin properties and microcirculatory function." *Biosci Biotechnol Biochem* 2017;81(9):1814–1822. [14]
- **Design:** double-blind, placebo-controlled, **n=49** women, **8 weeks**, oral CPP.
- **Findings:** ↓skin dryness clinical score, ↓TEWL, ↓skin-surface pH, ↑SC hydration, ↑skin blood-flow responsiveness to warming, ↑SC free fatty acids and lactic acid.
- **Limitations:** **ORAL** intake — says nothing about a cream; moderate n; manufacturer-adjacent.

> The honest read on Tier C: coffee's *best human data is what you drink*, not what you apply. This is the classic "borrowed-format" trap and must never be implied to support a topical product.

### TIER D — Human TOPICAL

> This is where coffee is weakest *for facial photoaging*, and strongest in *adjacent indications*.

**D1. Facial anti-aging / wrinkle — essentially no standalone RCT for CGA or coffee extract.**
- No well-powered, peer-reviewed, vehicle-controlled human RCT was found showing a topical **coffee/CGA** product reduces facial wrinkles or measurably rebuilds collagen in people. The facial-aging case is **in-vitro + mouse + oral**, not human topical. **[gap]**

**D2. Topical caffeine — barrier/elasticity (small RCT).**
- "The Comparison of the Effects of Caffeine Topical 0.25% and 0.5% as Anti-wrinkle Therapy." *(SCITEPRESS / RCD 2018)* [2]
- 4-week, double-blind, randomized; caffeine ↓TEWL (improved barrier) and improved elasticity; full PDF text not machine-readable this session — **[partially unverified; confirm n and effect sizes before use in copy]**.

**D3. Topical caffeine — cellulite/circumference (moderate, small trials).**
- Systematic/PRISMA review of topical cellulite cosmetics: **moderate efficacy at best**, mainly small ↓thigh circumference; "no clear evidence of good efficacy"; **no FDA-approved topical cellulite treatment** (Cosmetics 2024 review [15]; Elias 2023 [9] cites 3.5% caffeine, 15–44 participants, 6–12 wk, ↓circumference).
- "Clinical Effectiveness of a Novel Caffeine Nano-Cream for Cellulite Reduction: A Randomised Double-Blind Trial." *Pharmaceutics* 2026;18(2):151. [17] — nano-cream (lanolin) gave highest caffeine permeation (8.83 vs ~2.5–2.8 µg/cm²/h for aqueous/commercial); 12-wk twice-daily vs placebo.
- **Limitations:** small, short, formulation-dependent; cellulite ≠ facial aging.

**D4. Topical caffeine — periorbital dark circles / puffiness (small trials).**
- Multiple small caffeine-gel studies: transient ↓under-eye darkness and oedema via vasoconstriction; one 3% gel reported ~17% ↓under-eye fluid, ~14% ↑periorbital elasticity vs placebo at 4 wk; but a 34-volunteer study found significant de-puffing in only ~24% of users (high inter-individual variability). Summarized in Elias 2023 [9] and a multicorrective-eye-product RCT (Rajabi-Estarabadi 2024 [18], caffeine in combination).
- **Limitations:** small, often combination products (caffeine + HA/ruscogenin), effect transient.

**D5. Topical caffeine — hair loss (best human topical signal in the family).**
- 0.2% caffeine solution statistically non-inferior to 5% minoxidil in a >200-male RCT for androgenetic alopecia (cited in Elias 2023 [9]; systematic review PMC11855793 [19]).
- **Relevance to us:** off-target (scalp/hair), but demonstrates real human topical bioactivity of caffeine.

**D6. Green coffee seed oil — ex-vivo human skin + small in-use TEWL.**
- Velazquez Pereda M del C, et al. *J Cosmet Dermatol* 2009;8(1):56–62. PMID 19250168. [3]
- In-vitro/ex-vivo human skin: dose-dependent ↑collagen/elastin/GAG, ↑TGF-β1/GM-CSF, ↑AQP-3 mRNA (up to 6.5×); **15% GCO formulation ↓TEWL in volunteers at 24 & 48 h.**
- **Limitations:** single lab, **manufacturer-affiliated and un-replicated**; small volunteer arm; TEWL is an emollient/occlusion effect as much as an "active" effect; no wrinkle endpoint. Treat the 6.5× AQP-3 figure as a single-source in-vitro signal, not an established human benefit.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Caffeine:** the **most penetration-favorable** of the group. Small (MW 194), amphiphilic; documented transdermal flux (~2.24 µg/cm²/h mean maximal in human comparison; higher in optimized vehicles). Penetrates hair follicles within ~2 min and persists ~48 h (Elias 2023 [9]). Penetration is **vehicle-dependent** — nano/lanolin systems multiply flux 3× over aqueous [17].
- **Chlorogenic acid:** **hydrophilic and poorly skin-permeable** as the free acid; its delivery is the central formulation problem. Penetration/deposition improved markedly by **O/W microemulsions, ethosomes, liposomes, phospholipid complexes, and nanovesicles** (>25× solubility increase reported) [20]. Without an enabling carrier, much CGA likely sits on the surface acting as a topical antioxidant rather than reaching the dermis.
- **Green coffee seed oil:** an emollient — it works at/within the stratum corneum (occlusion, lipid replenishment, AQP-3 signaling) rather than needing deep penetration.

## 6. Formulation

**Concentrations (typical / evidence-aligned):**
- **Caffeine:** commonly **1–5%** (CIR notes use up to 6% in body/hand products [21]); eye/de-puffing products ~3%; cellulite trials ~3.5%; hair 0.2%. For facial antioxidant/contour use **~1–3%** is rational.
- **Chlorogenic acid / coffee extract:** standardized extracts dosed to deliver low-µM-to-low-% CGA; in-vitro activity is in the **3–30 µM** range, so a meaningful delivered dose + a penetration-enabling carrier matters more than a big label number.
- **Green coffee seed oil:** emollient phase, **1–15%** (15% used in the TEWL study).

**pH & stability:**
- **CGA is acid-stable, base-unstable** — stable at slightly acidic **pH ~4.5–6.0**, degrades at neutral/alkaline pH (and to light/heat/oxygen) [20][22]. Formulate acidic, protect from light/air, consider encapsulation.
- **Caffeine** is stable and robust across typical cosmetic pH and processing; high water-solubility can require solubilization or recrystallization control at higher %.
- **Green coffee oil** oxidizes (high linoleic content) — needs antioxidant protection (e.g. tocopherol) and opaque/airless packaging; can influence emulsion stability [23].

**Pairings:**
- CGA + caffeine are natural co-actives (whole-bean story).
- Pairs conceptually with other antioxidants (vitamin C/E, ferulic acid — also a hydroxycinnamic acid), niacinamide (barrier/tone), peptides, HA. Caffeine + HA/ruscogenin is the standard eye-contour stack.
- **Watch-outs:** CGA (a polyphenol) can interact with metal ions/oxidize and may discolor; high caffeine % can recrystallize; the anti-proliferative caffeine signal argues against pairing high caffeine with a barrier-renewal hero claim.

**Sensory:** green coffee oil is a light, fast-ish emollient (linoleic-rich) — pleasant in serums/creams; coffee extracts can carry **color (tan/brown)** and faint coffee odor that must be managed for a premium aesthetic; ground-bean powder gives a physical-scrub texture (off-strategy for a lean barrier-repair line).

## 7. Safety

- **Caffeine:** **CIR Expert Panel — Caffeine (and methylxanthines theobromine/theophylline) "safe in the present practices of use and concentration"** [21]; used up to 6% in body/hand products; low irritation/sensitization at cosmetic levels; systemic absorption from topical use is far below dietary exposure. A 12-volunteer compatibility study showed no erythema, no TEWL impairment, no SC-hydration impact at 2.5% [8].
- **Chlorogenic acid:** generally well-tolerated topically; it is a known **occasional contact allergen in occupational/dietary contexts (e.g. green coffee dust, some plant foods)** — sensitization risk is low but non-zero; patch-test logic applies, especially with high-load botanical extracts.
- **Green coffee seed oil:** low-irritancy emollient; main concern is **oxidative rancidity** (linoleic-rich) → protect and date-control.
- **Sun:** CGA and caffeine are **photoprotective/photo-neutral**, not photosensitizing — fine for daytime. (Caffeine is even a sunscreen adjuvant.) Do **not** position as a sunscreen substitute.
- **Pregnancy:** topical exposure is negligible systemically; no specific topical contraindication, but avoid disease/structure-function claims and defer to medical advice.
- **Anti-proliferative caveat:** the in-vitro keratinocyte-proliferation/collagen-inhibition finding for caffeine [9] is not a demonstrated in-use safety problem, but it argues against very high caffeine loads and against claiming caffeine "builds collagen."
- **Sourcing/allergen:** coffee is a common food; airborne green-coffee allergen is an occupational (manufacturing) concern, not a finished-product one. Standardize CoA for caffeine/CGA content, heavy metals, solvent residues, and (for oil) peroxide value.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

**The cosmetic/drug line.** Cosmetics may improve *appearance*; they may not claim to treat/prevent disease or alter the body's structure/function (US FDA; EU). Coffee has several tempting-but-risky claims:

- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "antioxidant-rich green coffee," "helps defend against environmental/oxidative stress," "helps visibly brighten and even tone," "helps reduce the look of puffiness and under-eye shadows" (caffeine, appearance-only, transient), "emollient green coffee oil helps skin feel hydrated / look smoother," "upcycled coffee silverskin."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like / unsubstantiated — flag for legal:**
  - "**boosts/builds collagen**" — in-vitro/mouse only for CGA; caffeine actually *inhibits* collagen in vitro. Quantified collagen claims need our own substantiation.
  - "**reduces cellulite / slims / burns fat**" — "cellulite reduction" and lipolysis claims edge toward structure/function; evidence is moderate-at-best and FDA-unapproved [15]. High legal-substantiation bar.
  - "**reverses wrinkles / treats photoaging**" — overreach; no human topical RCT.
  - "**prevents skin cancer / protects from UV / SPF**" — the skin-cancer data is **oral/animal caffeine** (ATR mechanism) [6]; never imply on a topical cosmetic. CGA's UVB absorbance is not an SPF.
  - "**clinically proven anti-aging**" when leaning on oral [11][14] or in-vitro [1] data — borrowed-format framing.
  - "**stimulates hair growth**" — that's a drug-adjacent claim (minoxidil comparator) [9][19]; off-strategy and high risk.

- **Key compliance nuance:** coffee's most quotable human findings are **dietary** (Fukushima [11], Fukagawa [14]) — keep them clearly labeled as oral/ingestion *education*, never as topical-product efficacy.

| Material | Cost tier | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Caffeine (USP/isolated)** | **Low** | Commodity, abundant, cheap, well-specified; can be synthetic or extracted (e.g. green-coffee-derived "natural" caffeine at a premium). |
| **Coffee/green coffee extract (std. CGA/caffeine)** | **Low–mid** | Widely available; price scales with standardization (% CGA) and "natural/upcycled" positioning. |

| **Green coffee seed oil** | **Mid** | Cold-pressed specialty oil; quality/peroxide value varies; premium "virgin green coffee oil" grades exist. |
| **Upcycled by-products (silverskin / spent grounds)** | **Low (feedstock) but variable processed cost** | ~50% of coffee fruit is waste; strong **circular/sustainability story** (e.g. SLVR/coffee-silverskin actives, Koffee'Up spent-grounds oil) [24][25]. Standardization and odor/color control are the cost drivers. |

**Sourcing rule:** caffeine and standardized coffee extract are cheap and easy; the value-add (and cost) is in **delivery (encapsulated CGA)**, **oil quality**, and the **upcycled provenance** that supports a sustainability narrative.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **"Coffee = powerful antioxidant anti-aging."** *Half true.* The antioxidant chemistry (CGA) is real and well-documented in vitro; the **anti-aging-on-human-skin** part is borrowed from cells, mice, and diet studies.
- **"Caffeine firms and de-puffs / builds collagen."** *De-puffs: yes, transiently (vasoconstriction). Builds collagen: no* — caffeine inhibits collagen synthesis in vitro [9]. Conflating the two is a common marketing error.
- **"Caffeine cream beats cellulite."** *Overstated.* Systematic reviews find moderate, mainly circumference effects; no FDA-approved topical; results are formulation- and individual-dependent [9][15][17].
- **"Coffee protects against sun / skin cancer."** *Borrowed and dangerous to imply.* The chemoprevention data is **oral/animal caffeine** (ATR pathway) [6]; not a topical cosmetic claim and not an SPF.
- **"Drink-your-glow proof that our coffee cream works."** *Non sequitur.* Fukushima [11]/Fukagawa [14] are **oral** — they cannot substantiate a topical product.
- **What's NOT hype:** CGA is a genuinely strong antioxidant with coherent photoaging mechanism; caffeine genuinely penetrates and genuinely causes transient periorbital vasoconstriction; green coffee oil is a genuinely good AQP-3-supporting emollient; and the **upcycled-coffee sustainability story is real and differentiating.**

## 11. Verdict for the brand

- **Overall: ⚠️ SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL — not a hero.** Coffee actives are a credible, on-trend, sustainability-friendly **supporting antioxidant + sensory/story layer**, but the human topical efficacy evidence for *facial anti-aging* is borrowed (in-vitro/mouse/oral), so they cannot honestly headline an anti-wrinkle hero claim.
- **Best-fit roles for this brand:**
  - **Caffeine in an eye/contour context** = its strongest defensible cosmetic role (transient de-puffing/shadow reduction) — *conditional* on appearance-only claims.
  - **Standardized CGA (encapsulated) as a supporting antioxidant** in a daytime/environmental-defense serum or in the barrier-repair cream — *supporting*, framed as "antioxidant defense," not "collagen boost."
  - **Green coffee seed oil as an emollient** with an AQP-3 hydration micro-story — *supporting*, fits the barrier thesis.
  - **Upcycled coffee (silverskin/spent-grounds) as the sustainability hook** — strong brand-story value regardless of which active it carries.
- **Skip / avoid:** cellulite/slimming and hair-growth positioning (off-strategy, high claims risk, off the lean-routine thesis); ground-bean physical scrub (off-strategy for a premium barrier line); any "builds collagen / SPF / cancer-protective" claim.

**One-line reasoning:** Coffee is a great **supporting antioxidant with a best-in-class sustainability narrative** — use CGA for antioxidant defense, caffeine for eye-area appearance, the oil for emollient hydration; do **not** let it carry the brand's primary efficacy claims.

**Longevity-relevance note:** Mechanistically, CGA touches several hallmarks of aging that matter to a skin-longevity story (oxidative stress, inflammaging, MMP-driven ECM loss, UV-induced DNA damage/senescence) — so it's a legitimate *narrative* bridge to the longevity thesis. But the bridge is built on cells and mice; the credibility move is to keep the hallmark mechanism as **education** and let better-evidenced actives (e.g. niacinamide, retinoids, sunscreen) carry the hard efficacy claims, with coffee as the antioxidant + sustainability supporting cast.
