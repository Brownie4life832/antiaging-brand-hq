# Ingredient: Granactive Retinoid — Hydroxypinacolone Retinoate (HPR) (INCI: Hydroxypinacolone Retinoate; Dimethyl Isosorbide)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⬜ considering (leaning SUPPORTING / conditional)
**Class:** Retinoids & retinoid alternatives

> **Read this first — what "Granactive Retinoid" actually is.** "Granactive Retinoid" is **not an ingredient — it is a branded trade blend** from Grant Industries. It is an anhydrous matrix of **10% hydroxypinacolone retinoate (HPR)** dissolved in **90% dimethyl isosorbide (DMI)**, a penetration-enhancing solvent. The *active* is HPR. This matters enormously for label math (see below) and for reading the evidence. Where this dossier says "HPR" it means the active; "Granactive Retinoid" means the 10% blend. This file is filed under the requested slug `granactive-retinoid` but the science is the science of **HPR**.
>
> **Companion file:** the standalone, deep evidence-by-tier dossier on the active molecule lives at [`hydroxypinacolone-retinoate.md`](hydroxypinacolone-retinoate.md). This Granactive file is the *trade-blend / formulation* view; the HPR file is the *molecule / literature* view. Keep them consistent.

## 1. Identity

- **Common / marketing names:** Granactive Retinoid (Grant Industries trade name); also sold as Granactive Retinoid Pro+, PromaCare-HPR, and generically as HPR. Marketed as a "next-generation," "fourth-generation," or "ester" retinoid.
- **Active INCI:** **Hydroxypinacolone Retinoate** (HPR). CAS **893412-73-2**. Molecular formula ~C26H38O3. [1][2]
- **Trade blend INCI:** *Hydroxypinacolone Retinoate (and) Dimethyl Isosorbide* — i.e. the label of a finished product using the blend will list both. [3][4]
- **Chemistry:** HPR is an **ester of all-trans retinoic acid (ATRA / tretinoin)** — specifically the pinacolyl-type ester of retinoic acid. It is a *cosmetic-legal* derivative; the free acid (tretinoin) is a prescription drug. [1][3][5]
- **Key forms / relatives in the same family** (cross-reference [`retinoids.md`](retinoids.md), [`bakuchiol.md`](bakuchiol.md)):
  - Retinyl esters (retinyl palmitate/propionate) — weakest.
  - Retinol → retinaldehyde (retinal) — the classic OTC ladder.
  - **HPR** — an *ester of the acid itself* (different branch of the family from retinol).
  - Tretinoin (ATRA) — prescription gold standard (drug, not cosmetic).
- **Label-math warning (critical):** a product advertising **"2% Granactive Retinoid" contains only ~0.2% actual HPR** (the rest is DMI solvent). "5% Granactive Retinoid" ≈ 0.5% HPR. Marketing and competitor packaging routinely cite the *blend* percentage, which looks far higher than the real active dose. [3][6]

## 2. Class & mechanism

HPR is a **retinoid** — a vitamin A derivative. The whole retinoid class works by ultimately engaging nuclear **retinoic acid receptors (RAR) / retinoid X receptors (RXR)**, which act as transcription factors that upregulate genes governing keratinocyte differentiation/turnover, dermal procollagen synthesis, and downregulate matrix-degrading enzymes (MMPs). Conventional retinoids (retinyl esters → retinol → retinal) must be **enzymatically converted up the ladder to retinoic acid** inside the skin to act; each step is rate-limiting, which is why retinol is milder (and slower) than tretinoin.

**The HPR pitch — and the honest caveat:** Grant Industries and most secondary sources state that because HPR is *already structurally a retinoate (an ester of the acid)*, it possesses "innate retinoic-acid activity" and **binds retinoid receptors directly without needing metabolic conversion.** [3][7][8]

- **Honest read:** This "no-conversion / direct-binding" claim originates from the manufacturer and the manufacturer-affiliated in-vitro work (see §4); it is repeated widely but has **not been independently confirmed by receptor-binding affinity studies in the public peer-reviewed literature.** Pharmacologically, an *ester* ("…retinoate") would normally be expected to require hydrolysis (cleavage by esterases) to release the active acid before receptor binding — so the "binds directly, intact" mechanism should be treated as a **plausible but unproven marketing-framed mechanism**, not settled science. The functional output (RAR-mediated gene transcription and procollagen induction) *is* demonstrated in cell/tissue models; the precise molecular route is less certain than the copy implies. [3][7]

Net: mechanistically HPR is a genuine retinoid acting through the RAR/RXR pathway. Whether its advantage is "direct binding" or simply "a stable, well-penetrating ester that is gently converted/hydrolyzed in situ" is not resolved.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | HPR relevance | Strength (for TOPICAL human use) |
|---|---|---|
| **Collagen / ECM loss** | Primary target — procollagen induction shown in *reconstructed-skin models* (in-vitro); multi-active clinical formulas *containing* HPR report improved wrinkles/elasticity, but HPR is never isolated | Moderate-to-weak (mechanism shown in-vitro only; human data confounded by co-actives — see §4) |
| **Photoaging** (lines, roughness, mottled pigment) | The headline use; retinoid-class benefit | Moderate (multi-active, uncontrolled human trials) |
| **Pigmentation / tone** | Retinoids normalize turnover and pigment transfer; clinical formulas show brightening | Moderate–weak (confounded) |
| **Barrier decline / hydration / TEWL** | Some formulas reported improved hydration & barrier (atypical for retinoids, likely from co-formulation) | Weak (confounded by other actives) |
| **Inflammaging** | In-vitro: HPR did not provoke more inflammation than vehicle (a *tolerability* point, not an anti-inflammatory claim) | Weak |
| Oxidative stress | Not a meaningful direct antioxidant; class is pro-renewal, not primarily antioxidant | Not applicable / weak |
| Glycation / AGEs | No specific evidence | None |
| Cellular senescence | No specific evidence (do **not** conflate with senolytics — see [`senolytics-os01-peptide.md`](senolytics-os01-peptide.md)) | None |
| Mitochondrial decline | No specific evidence | None |
| Microbiome | No specific evidence | None |

**Bottom line on hallmarks:** HPR's legitimate territory is the **retinoid core — turnover, ECM/collagen support, photoaging and tone** — the same hallmarks as retinol, just (claimed) with better tolerability. Everything beyond that is borrowed from co-formulated ingredients.

## 4. Evidence review by tier — THE CORE OF THIS DOSSIER

> **Headline honesty statement.** HPR has a **thin and conflicted-of-interest evidence base.** The mechanistic/in-vitro work is industry-affiliated; the human clinical work is **open-label, brand-funded, and uses HPR *combined* with other actives (retinol, peptides, etc.) — never HPR monotherapy against a vehicle control.** There is, to date, **no independent, randomized, vehicle-controlled trial of HPR alone** in the public literature. Compare this with retinol (multiple independent RCTs) and tretinoin (decades of RCTs + meta-analyses). HPR's reputation as "retinoic-acid-strength without irritation" rests substantially on **manufacturer marketing.**

### Tier 1 — In-vitro / ex-vivo (cell & reconstructed-skin models)

- **Ruth N & Mammone T (Estée Lauder Companies), 2018 — "Anti-aging effects of retinoid hydroxypinacolone retinoate on skin models."** Conference abstract, dual-published in *J Am Acad Dermatol* 79(3 Suppl) and *J Invest Dermatol* 138(5 Suppl). [9][10][11]
  - **Methods:** RARE-luciferase reporter assay (to measure retinoid-receptor gene transcription); cytotoxicity testing; organotypic/reconstructed human skin tissue models (EpiDerm-FT/EFT-300-type) with procollagen histology. Comparators: retinol (Rol), retinaldehyde (Ral), retinyl palmitate (RP), and ATRA (tretinoin) as the positive control.
  - **Results (all in-vitro / reconstructed-skin, not human skin):** HPR produced **greater RARE gene transcription than retinol, retinal, and retinyl palmitate at equal concentrations** — but **did NOT reach ATRA's transcription level.** HPR was less cytotoxic even at ~10× higher concentration. In *reconstructed-skin tissue models* HPR **significantly increased procollagen vs untreated control, and was reported comparable to ATRA**, with the highest HPR dose out-performing ATRA on histological collagen staining; HPR did not induce more inflammation than ATRA or vehicle. **(Wording confirmed against the published abstract during verification — see §13.)** Note this "comparable-to-ATRA" result is *tissue-model procollagen staining*, not a human clinical endpoint, and must never be quoted as "as effective as tretinoin."
  - **Limitations (important):** This is a **conference abstract, not a full peer-reviewed paper**; **authors are Estée Lauder employees** (clear commercial interest); in-vitro/ex-vivo only; the "direct binding" framing is asserted, not proven by binding-affinity data. This single abstract is the foundation cited (directly or indirectly) by most "HPR works" claims online.

- **Supramolecular nanoparticle / nanoemulsion stability-penetration work** (e.g. retinol propionate + HPR supramolecular nanoparticles, and HPR nanoemulsion studies): report **high stability, low irritation, good permeability** for engineered HPR delivery systems. These are **formulation/physical-chemistry studies**, not efficacy proof, and again often pair HPR with other retinoids. [12][13]

### Tier 2 — Animal

- **No meaningful, HPR-specific animal anti-aging literature** identified in the public peer-reviewed record. (The retinoid *class* has extensive animal data, but that is not HPR.) Treat as **a gap**, not as support.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL

- **Not applicable.** HPR is a topical cosmetic ester; there is no oral HPR use or oral evidence, and none should be borrowed.

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL

All identified human studies are **topical**, but note the pattern: open-label, sponsor-employed authors, multi-active formulas, mostly **no vehicle/placebo and no HPR-monotherapy arm.**

**(a) Lower-tier / open-label, multi-active, brand-funded (the bulk of the evidence):**

- **Shen et al., 2026, *J Cosmet Dermatol* (Proya Cosmetics)** — "An innovative serum with retinol, HPR, peptides, and silybin improves mild photoaged facial skin in middle-aged Chinese women." [14][15]
  - **Design:** Open-label, single-arm, **no placebo/vehicle control.** n = 31 completers, 8 weeks.
  - **Active stack:** retinol **+** HPR **+** peptides (palmitoyl tripeptide-5; a urea-peptide) **+** silybin — proprietary percentages.
  - **Results:** significant wrinkle-volume reduction across zones; ~+23.5% elasticity (R2); ~+56% stratum-corneum hydration; ~−57% pore volume; brighter complexion; good tolerability.
  - **Limitations:** **HPR never isolated;** no comparator/placebo; funded by Proya, **all authors are Proya employees**; mild-photoaging single population; mechanistic causality not proven. Cannot attribute any benefit specifically to HPR.

- **Kruger et al., 2024/2025, *J Cosmet Dermatol* — HPR-peptide serum vs fractional CO2 laser in facial aging.** [16][17]
  - **Design:** Comparative (active-vs-procedure), **not vehicle-controlled.** n = 71 women aged 40–65 (42 topical serum, mean age 55.0; 29 laser, mean age 56.2). Topical group used an HPR + peptide serum twice daily for 16 weeks vs a single fractional-CO2 session.
  - **Results:** the topical HPR-peptide serum achieved **parity or statistically better improvement** than a single laser session on marionette lines, global fine lines/wrinkles, crow's-feet wrinkles, nasolabial folds, texture, smoothness, hyperpigmentation, lift, and photodamage (p<0.05 on several).
  - **Limitations:** **HPR + peptides combined** (not HPR alone); comparator is a *single* laser pass (a low bar — clinical CO2 protocols are usually multi-session); no vehicle arm; cosmetic-industry study. **Authors are affiliated with The Estée Lauder Companies R&D** — i.e. the *same company* behind the foundational Ruth/Mammone in-vitro abstract (§4 Tier 1). The "no conflicts of interest" declaration in the paper should be read against that affiliation. Shows the *finished serum* is respectable, not that HPR is the driver.

- **Wang et al., 2023, *J Cosmet Dermatol* — synergistic effect of retinyl propionate + HPR on skin aging.** [18] In-vitro + an 8-week study (~42 Chinese volunteers) of an optimized retinyl propionate **+** HPR combination; reported efficacy with **no adverse reactions.** Again **combination, not HPR alone**, and industry-oriented.

**(b) Higher-tier (RCT / vehicle-controlled, HPR isolated):**

- **None identified.** This is the central evidence gap. There is **no published double-blind, vehicle-controlled RCT of HPR by itself**, and no independent head-to-head of HPR vs retinol vs tretinoin that isolates HPR. (Contrast: retinol and tretinoin both have this.)

**(c) Manufacturer in-house claims (lowest tier — treat as marketing):**

- Grant Industries reports **~10× better oxidative stability** for HPR-in-DMI vs retinol under thermal stress; that **0.5% HPR caused significantly *less* irritation than 0.5% retinol at 24h**; and a "dramatic reduction of lines and wrinkles" with **0.2% HPR after 14 days twice-daily eye-area use.** [7][8] These are **unpublished, in-house, uncontrolled** datapoints. The stability and tolerability claims are plausible and broadly consistent with HPR being a stable lipophilic ester; the efficacy claim is **not independently verified.**

**Explicit "proven for topical human use vs borrowed" verdict:**
- **Proven for topical human HPR use (independently, HPR alone, vehicle-controlled):** *essentially nothing.*
- **Reasonably supported (finished multi-active formulas containing HPR improve photoaging signs, well tolerated):** moderate, but confounded — cannot credit HPR specifically.
- **Borrowed / inferred (retinoid-class collagen & turnover benefit):** strong for the *class*, **assumed** for HPR via mechanism + the Estée Lauder skin-model abstract.
- **Manufacturer-only (potency = retinoic-acid-level, 10× stability, less irritation than retinol):** marketing-tier; directionally believable on stability/tolerability, unproven on potency.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- HPR is **lipophilic** (an oil-soluble ester), so it integrates with stratum-corneum lipids and penetrates reasonably well. The ester structure is thought to **slow penetration / give gradual release**, which is the proposed basis for lower irritation. [5][19]
- The **DMI (dimethyl isosorbide) solvent** in the Granactive Retinoid blend is itself a recognized **penetration enhancer**, aiding delivery. [3]
- **Open question:** if HPR must be hydrolyzed to retinoic acid intracellularly (the conventional ester expectation), then effective bioavailability depends on cutaneous esterase activity — which is not quantified for HPR in the public literature. If it genuinely acts intact (manufacturer claim), penetration of the intact ester is what matters. Either way, **delivery is considered "good for a retinoid," but precise % bioavailability is not established.**

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** Commonly used at **2–10% of the Granactive Retinoid blend = ~0.2–1.0% actual HPR.** Popular consumer benchmarks: "2% Granactive Retinoid" (0.2% HPR, beginner) and "5%" (0.5% HPR). There is **no established dose-response curve from controlled trials** — concentrations are set by precedent/marketing, not RCT.
- **pH:** Like most retinoids, **most stable around pH ~5–7** (HPR is **not** an acid-functional active needing low pH, unlike vitamin C/AHAs). [20]
- **Stability:** **More stable than retinol** — a genuine formulation advantage. The ester is far less prone to oxidative degradation; the DMI matrix protects it (manufacturer cites ~10× oxidative stability vs retinol). Still a polyene retinoid, so **antioxidants (tocopherol/BHT), opaque/airless packaging, and inert atmosphere during manufacture are still best practice.** [7][20]
- **Anhydrous-friendly:** the raw blend is anhydrous (DMI base); slots cleanly into oil-phase/anhydrous serums and emulsions.
- **Pairs well with:** niacinamide, peptides, ceramides, hyaluronic acid, panthenol, antioxidants — buffering/soothing co-actives. Often **stacked with retinol** (most "HPR" products are really retinol+HPR blends) for a "buffered renewal" story. Tolerates **AM or PM** better than retinol in principle, but PM use + daily SPF remains the responsible default for any retinoid.
- **Conflicts:** Standard retinoid caution — avoid stacking same-routine with strong AHAs/BHAs, benzoyl peroxide, or high-strength L-ascorbic acid to limit irritation/instability; alternate instead.
- **Sensory:** Oil-soluble, elegant in serums; DMI gives a light, fast-spreading feel. Generally **less "retinization" (flaking/stinging)** reported than retinol.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation:** **Low — its main selling point.** Reported (manufacturer + clinical-formula data) as gentler than retinol with little visible peeling/stinging. Real, but partly because the *delivered retinoic-acid-equivalent dose may simply be lower/slower* — "gentler" can mean "milder," not "equally potent but kinder."
- **Sun sensitivity:** As a retinoid, **use PM and pair with daily SPF** (cross-reference [`sunscreen-photoprotection.md`](sunscreen-photoprotection.md)). HPR is not strongly photo-degrading like retinol but the routine standard stands.
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** **Avoid as a precaution.** HPR is an ester of retinoic acid; topical retinoids are class-avoided in pregnancy out of caution (systemic retinoids are teratogens, topical absorption is low but the precautionary standard is to abstain). EWG flags HPR with a **data-gap rating** for developmental/reproductive toxicity; no formal pregnancy safety clearance exists. **Do not market HPR as "pregnancy-safe."** Use "consult your doctor" language. (For a pregnancy-conscious renewal option, see [`bakuchiol.md`](bakuchiol.md) — though that too lacks formal clearance.) [21][22]
- **Sensitization / allergy:** Low reported sensitization; standard retinoid caution. DMI is well tolerated.
- **Sourcing / sustainability:** Fully **synthetic**, lab-made; no wild-harvest sustainability issue (unlike crude botanical actives). Supply is concentrated among a handful of (largely Asian) fine-chemical manufacturers plus the branded Grant Industries blend.

## 8. Regulatory & claims

- **Cosmetic vs drug:** HPR is sold and regulated as a **cosmetic ingredient** (the *free acid* tretinoin is the drug). It is **not** an OTC-monograph drug active. So HPR products live in cosmetic-claim territory — which is also a **constraint:** we may describe *appearance* benefits, not disease treatment or structure/function changes.
- **Safe cosmetic language:** "helps visibly smooth the look of fine lines," "supports skin's natural renewal," "a gentle, stable retinoid for visibly improved tone and texture," "next-generation retinoid alternative to retinol — gentler on skin."
- **Risky / needs substantiation or avoid:**
  - "as strong as / equivalent to prescription retinoic acid / tretinoin" — **overreach;** also flirts with implying a drug. Avoid.
  - "boosts collagen by X%," "increases cell turnover," "repairs/reverses aging," "treats wrinkles/acne" — **structure/function & drug-like;** avoid or substantiate.
  - "binds retinoic-acid receptors directly without conversion" — **mechanism is manufacturer-derived and unconfirmed;** don't state as fact in consumer copy.
  - "pregnancy-safe" — **do not claim** (see §7).
  - Citing **blend %** ("5% retinoid") to imply potency — misleading vs the real ~0.5% HPR; if we quote a number, be transparent about active vs blend.
- See the claims guardrail in [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md) and [`../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier:** **Moderate–high** for the active. Pure HPR is a costly fine chemical; the Granactive Retinoid / PromaCare-HPR blends are priced at a premium vs commodity retinol but are widely available to formulators (Grant Industries; plus generic HPR/DMI blends from suppliers such as Uniproma, ZF Biotec, and others). [3][4][6][23]
- **Hidden costs:** lower than retinol's — **better stability eases packaging/antioxidant burden** (a real cost advantage), though good practice (opaque/airless, antioxidants) still applies.
- **Supply:** synthetic and scalable; no botanical supply-chain risk. Verify supplier purity/COA.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **The biggest hype:** that HPR is **"retinoic-acid-level potency with no irritation"** or "as effective as tretinoin without a prescription." The *only* data approaching "ATRA-comparable" is a single **industry conference abstract on skin models** (procollagen staining), and even there HPR **under-performed ATRA on receptor gene transcription.** There is **no human RCT** backing equivalence. This is the central overreach to refuse.
- **The percentage illusion:** "high-strength" HPR products quoting the **blend %** (2–10%) make HPR look more concentrated than retinol when the **actual HPR dose is ~0.2–1%.**
- **"Direct receptor binding / no conversion needed":** repeated as fact across blogs and supplier pages but **traces to the manufacturer**; not independently confirmed and pharmacologically debatable for an ester.
- **"Pregnancy-safe retinol alternative":** unsupported and reckless to claim.
- **Where the hype is *fair*:** HPR is **genuinely more stable than retinol** and **genuinely well-tolerated** in formula — those are its real, defensible advantages.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL — a stable, gentle retinoid for a tolerability-led renewal story, NOT a hero and NOT a "stronger-than-retinol" claim.**

- **One-line reasoning:** HPR is a legitimate, very stable, well-tolerated cosmetic retinoid whose *real* selling points are stability and gentleness — but its efficacy evidence is thin, industry-funded, and never isolates HPR, so it cannot carry a flagship "proven anti-aging" claim the way retinol/retinaldehyde can.
- **How it should be used:** as a **supporting renewal active**, ideally in a **buffered blend with encapsulated retinol or retinaldehyde** (which is how the market actually uses it and how the better studies were run) — letting us tell a "gentle, stable, next-generation retinoid" story for sensitive-skin/retinoid-curious customers. If the brand wants a single evidence-backed renewal **hero**, that role belongs to **encapsulated retinol or retinaldehyde** (see [`retinoids.md`](retinoids.md)); HPR plays the **gentleness** position, with **bakuchiol** as the non-retinoid sensitive/pregnancy-conscious alternative ([`bakuchiol.md`](bakuchiol.md)).
- **Conditions to clear before adopting:** (1) commit to **transparent dosing** (state actual HPR %, not blend %); (2) **no equivalence-to-tretinoin or "pregnancy-safe" claims**; (3) treat as supporting, pending any independent vehicle-controlled HPR data.
- **Longevity-relevance note:** HPR's relevance to skin *longevity* is the standard retinoid mechanism — supporting ongoing keratinocyte turnover and dermal procollagen, i.e. helping the skin keep renewing its own matrix over time. It does **not** address senescence, mitochondrial, glycation, or microbiome hallmarks; it is a "keep-renewing" tool, not a deep-longevity actuator. Best framed as **gentle, sustainable daily renewal** rather than aggressive intervention — which fits a slow-renewal, barrier-respecting longevity thesis well.

## 13. Evidence verification (adversarial)

_Independent adversarial re-check performed 2026-06-19. Method: each cited study was located in the public record (PubMed / PMC / Wiley / JID / INCIDecoder / Grant Industries) and read against the dossier's characterization, checking specifically for (a) human-vs-borrowed evidence, (b) whether studies are real and say what's claimed, (c) industry funding and sample size._

### Overall evidence grade: **C → leaning D** (weak / indirect; essentially unproven for HPR *in isolation* in humans)

**Why C and not B:** there is genuine, *verified* signal that finished formulas containing HPR improve photoaging signs in humans, plus a real in-vitro mechanistic basis (RARE transcription + reconstructed-skin procollagen). **Why it slides toward D:** none of that isolates HPR. Every human study uses HPR inside a multi-active stack with retinol and/or peptides, every one is open-label or non-vehicle-controlled, and every one is funded by — and authored by employees of — the cosmetics company selling the product. There is **no independent, randomized, vehicle-controlled trial of HPR alone**. The molecule's signature claim ("retinoic-acid-level potency, gently") rests on a single industry conference *abstract* on cell/tissue models. By the dossier's own A–D scale, HPR is a **C** for "retinoid-class member that plausibly works," and a **D** for "HPR specifically proven topically in humans."

### Verification results — claim by claim

**The citations are real and accurately represented.** This is the dossier's strongest feature. Verified against source:
- **Ruth & Mammone 2018 (JID #1310 / JAAD suppl)** — real; Estée Lauder authored; conference abstract, not a full paper. The dossier's specifics are *correct*: HPR > retinol/retinal/retinyl palmitate on RARE transcription but **below ATRA**; procollagen in reconstructed skin "comparable to ATRA" with the top HPR dose out-staining ATRA; lower cytotoxicity at ~10×; no excess inflammation. ✔ accurate.
- **Shen et al. 2026 (J Cosmet Dermatol, Proya)** — real. Verified: open-label, single-arm, **no vehicle control**, n=31 completers, 8 weeks; retinol + HPR + peptides + silybin; ~+23.5% R2 elasticity, ~+56% hydration, ~−57% pore volume; **all 10 authors are Proya employees**, Proya-funded; HPR never isolated against vehicle in humans. The paper *does* include an in-vitro arm (TGF-β/Smad, retinol+HPR vs retinol alone) — that mechanistic synergy is cell-model, not clinical. ✔ accurate.
- **Kruger et al. 2024/2025 (J Cosmet Dermatol)** — real. Verified: comparative, **not vehicle-controlled**; n=71 (42 serum / 29 laser), ages 40–65, 16 weeks; HPR+peptide serum vs a *single* fractional-CO2 pass; topical reached parity-or-better on several endpoints. **New caveat added to §4:** the authors are **Estée Lauder R&D** — the *same company* as the foundational abstract, despite a "no conflicts" declaration. ✔ accurate, now with sponsor flagged.
- **Wang et al. 2023 (J Cosmet Dermatol)** — real. Verified: in-vitro (HFF-1 fibroblasts) + patch test (n=33) + clinical (n=42 women); retinyl propionate **+** HPR combination, not HPR alone. ✔ accurate.
- **Identity / label math** — verified: Granactive Retinoid = 10% HPR + 90% dimethyl isosorbide; "2% Granactive Retinoid = 0.2% HPR." ✔ accurate and well-flagged.
- **Manufacturer claims** (≈10× stability, 0.5% HPR < 0.5% retinol irritation at 24h, 0.2% eye-area 14-day wrinkle claim) — confirmed to *exist* as Grant Industries material; correctly tagged marketing-tier and uncontrolled. ✔.

**No evidence was found to be borrowed from oral or animal data and passed off as topical.** The dossier explicitly marks Tier 2 (animal) and Tier 3 (oral) as "no data / not applicable" rather than inflating them. ✔ This is handled correctly.

**The "direct receptor binding, no conversion needed" mechanism remains UNPROVEN.** Cross-checked independently: secondary sources (INCIDecoder, suppliers, derm blogs) repeat "binds directly without conversion" as fact, but the *only* experimental anchor is the RARE reporter assay — which measures **downstream gene transcription**, not binding affinity, and cannot distinguish "intact ester binds the receptor" from "ester is hydrolyzed in-situ, then the freed acid binds." The dossier's §2 skepticism is therefore **correct and, if anything, more justified than the surrounding literature admits.** ✔

### Main caveats (the things that cap the grade)

1. **Single-company concentration risk.** Two of the load-bearing studies (the foundational 2018 in-vitro abstract *and* the Kruger clinical comparison) come from **Estée Lauder**; the rest come from other cosmetics firms (Proya, etc.) publishing on their own products. The "independent corroboration" the dossier's reputation needs does not exist.
2. **HPR is never isolated in any human study.** Retinol and/or peptides ride along in every clinical formula, so no human result can be attributed to HPR. This is the single most important limitation and is correctly stated.
3. **No vehicle/placebo control in any human study.** Open-label or active-comparator only — high susceptibility to expectancy/regression effects.
4. **The flagship in-vitro evidence is a conference abstract,** not a full peer-reviewed paper with complete methods/peer scrutiny.
5. **"Comparable to ATRA" is a tissue-model procollagen result,** not a human clinical endpoint, and HPR *under-performed* ATRA on the receptor-transcription assay in the same study. It must never be lifted into "as effective as tretinoin."
6. **No dose-response from controlled trials** — usage concentrations are precedent/marketing-set.

### Claims that should be softened (and were, in this pass)

- **§3 hallmarks table, Collagen/ECM row** — was "clinical formulas improve wrinkles/elasticity" (reads as HPR doing it). **Softened** to specify procollagen is shown *in reconstructed-skin models only* and that multi-active formulas (HPR not isolated) report the clinical improvements; strength downgraded to "Moderate-to-weak."
- **§4 Tier 1 results** — added explicit "all in-vitro / reconstructed-skin, not human" framing and an inline warning that "comparable-to-ATRA" is tissue-model procollagen staining, never to be quoted as tretinoin-equivalent.
- **§4 Kruger entry** — added the Estée Lauder sponsor disclosure and the note that it shares a parent company with the foundational abstract.

### Claims that were already defensible (no change needed)

- The headline honesty statement (§4), the "percentage illusion," the refusal of "pregnancy-safe," the refusal of "tretinoin-equivalent," and the §11 verdict ("SUPPORTING/CONDITIONAL, not a hero, not stronger-than-retinol") are all well-calibrated and survive adversarial review. The dossier is unusually honest about its own thin evidence base; the corrections above are tightening, not reversal.

### One-line verdict

**Grade C (leaning D): the dossier's citations are real and faithfully represented, and it does not borrow oral/animal data — but HPR's topical-in-human evidence is thin, uncontrolled, multi-active, and single-industry-funded, so HPR cannot carry any standalone efficacy or "stronger/equal-to-retinol/tretinoin" claim. Use only as a tolerability-positioned *supporting* retinoid, with transparent active-% dosing.**
