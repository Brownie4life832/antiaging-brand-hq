# Ingredient: Glyceryl Glucoside (Glycoin) (INCI: Glyceryl Glucoside)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Humectants & hydration actives (glycoside osmolyte / "extremolyte"; aquaporin-3 modulator)

> **One-line honest summary:** Glyceryl glucoside (GG) is a glycerol-glucose osmolyte from the desert "resurrection plant" with a genuinely interesting, *mechanistically plausible and partly human-validated* trick — it upregulates **aquaporin-3 (AQP3)**, the skin's water/glycerol channel — backed by **one real peer-reviewed human topical study** (Schrader/Beiersdorf 2012) plus solid in-vitro work. Beyond that one study, the glossier anti-aging claims (firmness +24%, elasticity +41%, brightening −38%) come almost entirely from **supplier-funded, non-peer-reviewed in-vivo studies** for the commercial grade *Glycoin natural*. It is a **credible, well-tolerated hydration active with a strong story**, not a proven anti-aging hero. Best framed as **supporting**: a smarter, slower-release humectant with a real (if modest) mechanism.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Glyceryl glucoside (GG); also glucosylglycerol, glucosylglycerin, α-D-glucosylglycerol.
- **Trade names:** **Glycoin® natural** (Bitop AG — the dominant cosmetic grade); **Gluco-glycerol** (Beiersdorf/Eucerin's term for the same molecule in *AQUAporin ACTIVE*). Multiple Chinese/biotech suppliers now offer it.
- **INCI:** **Glyceryl Glucoside** (commercial grades list **Glyceryl Glucoside (and) Aqua/Water** as it ships as an aqueous solution).
- **CAS:** 22160-26-5 (and the related descriptor 100402-60-6 appears for stereo/positional variants). [9]
- **Chemistry:** A glycoside of glucose + glycerol — a glucose ring (glucopyranosyl) joined through a glycosidic bond to a glycerol unit. The biologically relevant natural isomer is **2-O-(α-D-glucopyranosyl)-sn-glycerol** (a.k.a. α-D-glucosylglycerol). Molecular formula **C₉H₁₈O₈**, MW ≈ **254.2 g/mol**. ChEBI: 2-O-(α-D-glucopyranosyl)glycerol (CHEBI:82766). [9]
- **Physical/chemistry notes:** Small, highly water-soluble, non-volatile, chemically stable polyol-type osmolyte. Mild/neutral, no strong pH effect (contrast with spermidine). Behaves as a **humectant**, but unlike free glycerol it carries a sugar moiety that (a) slows transepidermal loss/penetration and (b) is the basis for its claimed *signaling* (AQP3) and *prebiotic* actions.
- **Note on isomers:** "Glyceryl glucoside" as an INCI term covers a small family of positional/anomeric isomers; the **α-D / 2-O isomer** is the natural extremolyte and the one in the published science and in Glycoin. Generic/synthetic "glyceryl glucoside" raws may be isomer mixtures — verify the isomer on the COA, because the AQP3 story rests on the natural α-isomer. [9]

**Source/origin (the narrative hook):** GG is the protective osmolyte (an "extremolyte") that lets the South African desert **resurrection plant *Myrothamnus flabellifolia*** survive near-total desiccation and rehydrate when water returns; it is also produced by **cyanobacteria (blue-green algae)** under osmotic/UV stress. Commercial cosmetic GG is **not** wild-harvested from the plant — Glycoin and most grades are made by **biotechnological/enzymatic synthesis** (e.g., sucrose-phosphorylase-catalysed glucosylation of glycerol from sucrose), giving a defined, sustainable, single-isomer product. [2][9][13]

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it works at the skin/cell level)

GG sits at the intersection of three mechanisms — one well-evidenced (humectancy), one genuinely interesting and partly human-validated (AQP3), one mostly supplier-claimed (cell-energy/"revitalizing").

1. **Humectant / osmolyte (the boring, real part).** Like glycerol, GG is a polyol that binds and holds water in the stratum corneum, supporting hydration and a smoother surface. As a compatible osmolyte, it stabilizes proteins/membranes against desiccation stress (its native job in the resurrection plant). This is the floor of its activity and is not in doubt.

2. **Aquaporin-3 (AQP3) upregulation (the differentiator).** AQP3 is an *aquaglyceroporin* — the main water- and glycerol-transport channel in epidermal keratinocytes — and is central to epidermal hydration, barrier function, keratinocyte proliferation/migration, and wound repair. AQP3 expression **declines/dysregulates with age and in barrier-impaired skin**. GG has been shown to **increase AQP3 mRNA and protein** in keratinocytes and, importantly, **in living human skin after topical use**, improving water/glycerol handling from the inside rather than just sitting on top. This "we don't just add water, we restore the skin's own water-channel machinery" mechanism is GG's whole reason to exist over cheap glycerol. [1][3][4]

3. **Cell "revitalization" / stress protection (mostly supplier-claimed).** Bitop's materials attribute to Glycoin: increased **ATP synthesis**, stimulation of **pro-collagen I**, **SOD1** (antioxidant enzyme) and growth factors (FGF7/KGF, TGF-β1), heat-shock/desiccation stress protection, and radical scavenging — framed as "stimulating sluggish aged cells." These are mechanistically plausible for an osmolyte but rest on **supplier-funded, non-peer-reviewed** data; treat as marketing-tier until independently shown. [5][6][7]

4. **Prebiotic / microbiome support.** As a glycoside sugar, GG is claimed to act as a selective nutrient that supports commensal skin flora — a "prebiotic" effect. Plausible in principle (sugars feed microbes) but the *skin* microbiome data are supplier-level, not independent clinical proof. [6][8]

**Honest mechanistic read:** Mechanism #1 is certain. Mechanism #2 is the real story and has the best evidence (including one human topical study) — but the *magnitude* of benefit it adds over a good humectant is modest and not established against an active comparator. Mechanisms #3–#4 are plausible narrative, not proven.

**Is it better than plain glycerol?** This is the central skeptic's question. Published work specifically tested **GG**, not glycerol, for AQP3 upregulation; AQP3 reviews list *both* glycerol and GG among AQP3 modulators, so GG's edge over high-quality glycerol on AQP3 is **assumed, not cleanly demonstrated head-to-head**. GG's defensible advantages are: the sugar moiety slows release (longer-lasting feel, less tacky than equivalent glycerol), the desiccation-tolerance/osmolyte story, and the prebiotic angle. [3][10]

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** GG is unusual among "extremolyte/longevity" actives because it has **one genuinely peer-reviewed human topical study** that supports its headline mechanism (AQP3 ↑, TEWL ↓ in real skin). That puts it **ahead** of ingredients whose only human data are oral or borrowed. **However**, that study is from the ingredient's commercial backer's research group (Beiersdorf), it measured a *mechanistic/hydration* endpoint (not wrinkles), and **all the sexier anti-aging numbers** (firmness, elasticity, brightening, "8h/24h hydration," skin thickness) trace to **Bitop's own non-peer-reviewed in-vivo studies** behind Glycoin. Independent, peer-reviewed efficacy beyond hydration/AQP3 is essentially absent.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell models)

- **Schrader et al., 2012, *Skin Pharmacology and Physiology* (in-vitro arm) — DOI 10.1159/000338190; PubMed 22584263.** In cultured **human keratinocytes**, GG at **3% w/v** significantly increased **AQP3 mRNA (real-time RT-PCR) and protein (FACS)**. This is the mechanistic anchor. *Limitation: cell culture; authors affiliated with Beiersdorf (Eucerin maker).* [1]
- **Del Vecchio et al., 2024, *Cellular Physiology & Biochemistry* — "Dysregulation of Aquaporin-3 and Glyceryl Glucoside Restoring Action in Hidradenitis Suppurativa in Vitro Models."** HaCaT keratinocytes (wild-type vs NCSTN−/− and PSENEN−/− knockouts modeling hidradenitis suppurativa). KO cells had **suppressed AQP3** and **hyperproliferation**; **Glycoin (GG 4% v/v, 24 h)** significantly **restored AQP3 mRNA/protein** (e.g., NCSTN−/− mRNA 0.88→1.66, p=0.0043), **reduced hyperproliferation** (NCSTN−/− 0.67-fold at 72 h, p=0.0001), and **enhanced migration** (scratch assay, p=0.0002). Proteomics (5,763 proteins) showed GG shifted integrin/EGFR/FGFR/GPER1 pathways. *Limitation: in-vitro disease model (HS, not aging); authors explicitly state it "only partially mimics" real pathology and call for in-vivo validation; uses the commercial Glycoin product.* [11]
- **AQP3 biology context (supporting, not GG-specific):** AQP3 is the dominant epidermal aquaglyceroporin governing water/glycerol transport, proliferation, migration and barrier repair (Hara-Chikuma & Verkman; *J Invest Dermatol* roles-of-AQP3 reviews). AQP3 is **modulated by AGEs/TLR signaling** (direction is condition-dependent — see [12]) and altered in aged and diseased skin — establishing *why* an AQP3-restoring active is a plausible hydration target. *Caveat: the "AGEs downregulate AQP3" shorthand overstates ref [12], which actually found AGEs raised AQP3 under basal conditions and lowered it only under specific/chronic conditions.* [3][12]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)

- **No dedicated animal efficacy studies for topical GG on skin aging were located.** (AQP3-knockout mouse work establishes AQP3's role in hydration/barrier/proliferation, but does not test GG.) *This is a genuine gap — there is essentially no rodent skin-aging dataset for GG; the field skipped from cells to human hydration testing.* [3] [unverified — no GG-specific animal skin study found]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL (not the skin story)

- **Oral glucosylglycerol (= GG) exists as a sports-nutrition / glycemic ingredient**, studied for **slowing carbohydrate absorption / smoother blood-glucose response and endurance fuelling** (e.g., commercial "PromOat/GG" and glucosylglycerol supplements). This is a *different use case* and tells us GG is **orally well tolerated and metabolically benign**, but it carries **no read-across to topical skin benefit**. *Do not import oral data into skin claims.* [unverified — specific oral RCTs not retrieved in this pass; treat as context, not skin evidence]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters — one real study + supplier trials)

**(a) Peer-reviewed:**
- **Schrader A., Siefken W., Kueper T., et al. (Beiersdorf), 2012, *Skin Pharmacology and Physiology* 25(4):192–199 — "Effects of glyceryl glucoside on AQP3 expression, barrier function and hydration of human skin."** The pivotal study, three-part:
  - *Ex vivo penetration:* topical GG **5%** applied to intact human skin; **suction-blister biopsies** confirmed GG **penetrated the epidermis**.
  - *In-vivo molecular endpoint:* topical GG (forearm, ~7 days) **significantly increased AQP3 mRNA** vs vehicle control — measured on **suction-blister-derived epidermis** harvested from the treated (living) skin (i.e. an in-vivo-treated, ex-vivo-assayed readout, not an in-situ biopsy of intact skin).
  - *Functional endpoint:* in a **vehicle-controlled volunteer study (n≈23, twice daily, ~3 weeks)**, **significantly reduced TEWL** (improved barrier) and improved hydration vs vehicle/untreated.
  - *Verdict:* This is the **strongest single piece of evidence** — real human skin, mechanistic + functional readouts, vehicle-controlled. *Limitations: small (TEWL arm ~23 subjects); short duration (~3 weeks); endpoints are hydration/barrier/AQP3, NOT wrinkles/firmness; the human AQP3 mRNA readout is from suction-blister epidermis, not intact in-situ skin; conducted by the manufacturer (Beiersdorf) whose Eucerin line is built on this ingredient — a clear conflict of interest, though it is genuinely peer-reviewed.* [1]

**(b) Manufacturer / non-peer-reviewed in-vivo (the source of the "anti-aging" numbers — treat with skepticism):**
- **Schagen S. et al., 2017, EURO COSMETICS 1/2-2017 — "New data confirm skin revitalizing and stress protection by Glycoin® natural."** Trade-magazine article (Bitop-affiliated). Reports a **placebo-controlled, randomized in-vivo study, n=30 women, 2% Glycoin twice daily on the face for 8 weeks**: firmness **+24%**, roughness **−3%**, wrinkle volume **−7%**, biological elasticity **+30%**, net elasticity **+34%**, raw elasticity **+41%** (all "significant"). *Limitations: not a peer-reviewed journal; supplier-authored/funded; selective reporting likely; full methods/stats not independently reviewable.* [4]
- **Bitop technical/marketing claims (Glycoin® natural):** brightening / pigmentation **−38%**, moisture **+23–24.7%**, "**8h moisturizing after one application**," skin density/thickness **+9.9%**, growth-factor (FGF7, TGF-β1) stimulation, ATP/pro-collagen-I/SOD1 stimulation, blue-light protection, prebiotic effect; recommended use **0.5–2%**. Other promotional figures (elasticity "up to 93%," smoothness "up to 61%") appear in trade press. *Limitations: supplier datasheet / promotional; "up to" framing; no independent replication; not peer-reviewed.* [5][6][7]
- **Beiersdorf/Eucerin (AQUAporin ACTIVE):** brand claims the product **increases aquaporins by 215% after 24 h**, with immediate, long-lasting hydration; consumer/clinical testing (43 volunteers over 2–4 weeks; 160-volunteer use test, 4 weeks). *Limitations: brand marketing built on ref [1]. The underlying "+215%" magnitude appears to trace to the* in-vitro keratinocyte *arm of the peer-reviewed work (AQP3 mRNA increase in cultured cells); Eucerin re-packages it as a* finished-product, on-skin, 24h *result — which is the marketing leap, not a peer-reviewed finished-product outcome. Do not present "+215% aquaporins" as a proven on-skin product claim.* [1][14]

**Bottom line by tier:** **Proven (human topical, peer-reviewed):** GG penetrates the epidermis, raises AQP3, lowers TEWL, and improves hydration — i.e., it is a *real, mechanistically-distinct hydration/barrier active*. **Not independently proven:** firmness, elasticity, anti-wrinkle, brightening, density, antioxidant, prebiotic, blue-light, "revitalization" — these rest on **supplier studies** and in-vitro work. The honest claim ceiling is **hydration + barrier (+ AQP3 mechanism)**; everything else is narrative until we generate our own finished-formula data.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **In favor (best-in-class for this question):** Unlike most "extremolyte/longevity" actives, GG has **direct human penetration data** — suction-blister biopsies in Schrader 2012 confirmed topical GG (5%) reaches the **viable epidermis**, where it then upregulates AQP3. That is a real, documented advantage over peptides, spermidine, growth factors, etc., whose skin penetration is assumed. [1]
- **Why it penetrates reasonably:** small (MW ~254), but it is hydrophilic/polar — so penetration is "documented but not deep." It acts primarily in the **epidermis** (where AQP3 and the barrier live), which is exactly where a hydration/barrier active needs to work; dermal/collagen claims would require dermal delivery that is **not** demonstrated.
- **Caveat:** The penetration study used 5% in a specific vehicle; penetration is always formulation-dependent. Our finished formula's vehicle, occlusion and pH will determine real uptake.
- **Practical read:** Among hydration actives, GG has an unusually **evidence-backed** epidermal-delivery + mechanism story. The weak link is not "does it get in?" (it does, for epidermal action) but "how *much* extra benefit does the AQP3 effect add over a good humectant base?" — which is unquantified against an active comparator.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** Supplier recommendation **0.5–2%** (Glycoin natural, which is an aqueous solution — so 2% *product* is less GG actives). Published human work used **5% GG** (penetration/AQP3) and **3% w/v** (in vitro); the 8-week facial in-vivo study used **2% Glycoin**. Practical sweet spot: **1–3% of the GG raw**, with 2% as a defensible, study-anchored level. Don't imply a validated dose-response beyond hydration.
- **pH:** Tolerates a **broad pH range**; no strong pH effect of its own (neutral-ish glycoside). Compatible with low-pH (vitamin C, AHA) and high-pH systems alike — a real formulation advantage. Confirm against final-formula stability.
- **Stability:** Excellent — chemically stable, non-volatile, heat-stable (it is literally a desiccation/stress-survival osmolyte). Ships as an aqueous solution; fully water-soluble. Long shelf life expected. Standard preservation of the water phase required.
- **Pairings (conceptual):**
  - **Glycerol + hyaluronic acid** — layered humectancy; GG adds the AQP3/osmolyte angle and a longer-lasting, less-tacky feel. Natural "next-gen humectant" trio.
  - **Niacinamide** — barrier + AQP3-adjacent hydration story; both well-tolerated; coherent "barrier-repair" narrative central to the brand thesis.
  - **Ceramides / barrier lipids** — pairs the "channel" (AQP3/water transport) with the "wall" (lipids); strong fit for the brand's barrier-repair hero concept.
  - **Ectoin / other extremolytes** — a credible "extremolyte" hydration-and-stress-protection cluster (Bitop also sells ectoin; coherent narrative).
  - **Antioxidants / vitamin C** — pH-flexibility makes co-formulation easy.
- **Conflicts:** No notable incompatibilities reported ("no incompatibility with other actives or excipients" per supplier). As a sugar, watch for potential microbial-load/preservation considerations in the water phase and any (minor) Maillard-type discoloration with reactive amines over long-term storage — run stability/challenge testing.
- **Sensory:** Humectant slip without the heavy tack of high-dose glycerol; pleasant, lightweight. Good for "all-day hydration" positioning. No color/odor issues at use levels.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation/sensitization:** No specific irritation or sensitization signal at cosmetic use levels; GG is an endogenous-type osmolyte (found in plants/microbes, structurally a benign sugar-glycerol). Widely used in marketed products (Eucerin, Medik8, etc.) without notable adverse-event reports. *No dedicated published HRIPT/patch-test data for GG specifically was located — generate our own for the finished formula.* [10] [unverified — no GG-specific CIR report]
- **Sun sensitivity:** None expected; if anything the plant-biology narrative is *photoprotective*, though "blue-light protection" is a supplier claim, not proven. No photosensitization flag.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** No specific topical pregnancy data, but as a mild sugar-glycerol humectant it has no mechanistic red flags (no retinoid/hormonal/penetrant-drug concern). Conservative copy nonetheless ("consult your doctor"). [unverified — no pregnancy-specific study]
- **Eye/mucosa:** Mild; humectant-class.
- **Sourcing/allergen:** Biotech/enzymatically synthesized grades (Glycoin) avoid plant-harvest and are low-allergen; confirm the substrate (sucrose/glycerol source) and that the grade is **plant-/vegan-suitable and ECOCERT/COSMOS-grade** if that matters to the brand. No gluten/nut concerns inherent. Wild-harvest of *Myrothamnus* would raise sustainability issues — **commercial GG is synthesized, not wild-harvested**, which is the responsible (and marketable) path. [2][6]
- **General read:** **Low-risk, well-tolerated.** This is one of the cleaner safety profiles in the hydration-active category.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic status:** Listed INCI **Glyceryl Glucoside**; cosmetic functions = **humectant / skin-conditioning**. Established, in-market ingredient (Eucerin AQUAporin line, many others). No special restriction known; confirm per target market. [10][14]
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** "Hydrates," "supports the moisture barrier," "helps skin retain water," "softens and smooths the appearance of skin" are **safe cosmetic claims**. Trouble starts when AQP3 mechanism language drifts into structure/function/drug territory or into unsubstantiated anti-aging.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language (well-supported):** "intense, long-lasting hydration," "helps reinforce the skin's moisture barrier" (TEWL data supports), "inspired by the desert resurrection plant's survival science," "an osmolyte that helps skin hold onto water," "supports the skin's own water-channel network (aquaporins)" — *the aquaporin wording is defensible because of ref [1], but keep it appearance/support-framed.*
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation or our own data:** "boosts collagen," "increases firmness/elasticity by X%," "reduces wrinkles by X%," "brightens / fades pigmentation," "protects against blue light," "antioxidant/anti-aging at the cellular level," "prebiotic," "increases aquaporins by 215%/30%." **All of these trace to supplier or brand marketing, not independent peer-reviewed proof** — do not put them in our copy without our own finished-formula testing or an explicit supplier-data citation that legal accepts.
- **Substantiation reality:** We can substantiate **hydration and barrier/TEWL** relatively easily (instrumental testing is standard and the mechanism is published). The **AQP3 mechanism** can be referenced as ingredient science (cite ref [1]) but should not be over-claimed as a finished-product outcome. Everything beyond hydration/barrier needs our own study. ## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Forms/suppliers:** Dominant cosmetic grade is **Bitop AG's Glycoin® natural** (aqueous, biotech-produced, ECOCERT/COSMOS-positioned, the single-isomer α-D natural extremolyte). **Beiersdorf** uses its own "Gluco-glycerol" internally. A growing field of **Chinese/biotech suppliers** (Seebio, Harworld, Uniproma, etc.) offer glyceryl glucoside — **verify isomer purity (α-D / 2-O), actives %, and that AQP3-relevant data apply** to their grade, not just Glycoin's.
- **Cost tier:** **Mid to mid-high.** More expensive than commodity glycerol (the whole point), cheaper than premium peptides/growth factors. As an aqueous solution, factor the **% actives** into true cost-in-use; the branded Glycoin grade carries a premium over generic GG.
- **Supply/standardization risk:** Main risk is **isomer/grade variability** in generic suppliers — the science is on the natural α-isomer. Require COA with isomer ID and actives content. Sustainable, synthetic supply is a *plus* for brand storytelling (no plant over-harvest).

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **"Restores your skin's aquaporins / water channels."** *Partly true* — and unusually well-supported (ref [1] shows AQP3 mRNA ↑ and TEWL ↓ in real human skin). This is GG's legitimate edge. But the specific **"+215%" / "+30% aquaporins"** figures, *as on-skin finished-product claims,* are **brand/supplier framing**: the "+215%" magnitude appears to originate in the *in-vitro keratinocyte* arm of the peer-reviewed work and is then re-packaged by Eucerin as a 24h on-skin product result. The *finished-product* hydration benefit over a good humectant base is modest and not quantified head-to-head.
- **The firmness/elasticity/wrinkle numbers (+24% firmness, +41% elasticity, etc.).** These come from a **supplier trade-magazine in-vivo study (n=30)**, not peer review. Plausible-but-unverified; do not present as established fact.
- **"Brightening −38% / blue-light protection / prebiotic / antioxidant / collagen boost."** **Supplier marketing.** No independent peer-reviewed support located. The brightening and blue-light claims in particular are weakly grounded.
- **"Better than glycerol."** GG's *advantage* over glycerol on AQP3 is **assumed, not demonstrated head-to-head** — reviews list glycerol itself as an AQP3 modulator. GG's defensible wins are slower release/feel, the osmolyte/desiccation story, pH-flexibility, and the documented human AQP3/penetration data. Don't claim glycerol "doesn't work" by comparison.
- **The resurrection-plant story.** Genuinely true and a *great* narrative — *Myrothamnus* really does use GG to survive desiccation — but "survives the desert" is plant biology, **not** a human skin anti-aging outcome. Use the story for emotion, not as efficacy proof.
- **Conflict-of-interest pattern.** The strongest human study (ref [1]) is **Beiersdorf's** (it sells the ingredient); the anti-aging numbers are **Bitop's** (it sells the ingredient). Independent, disinterested efficacy data are thin. That doesn't make it bad — but it sets the honesty bar.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING — a credible, well-tolerated, mechanism-backed hydration active with a premium story; not a proven anti-aging hero.**

**One-line:** Glyceryl glucoside is one of the **better-evidenced "smart humectants"** available — it has a real, peer-reviewed *human topical* mechanism (AQP3 upregulation, lower TEWL, documented epidermal penetration) plus an irresistible resurrection-plant narrative and a clean safety/formulation profile — so it earns a spot as a **supporting hydration/barrier ingredient**, but its anti-aging (firmness/elasticity/brightening) claims rest on **supplier data** and should not be sold as proven.

**Where it fits:** Excellent **supporting** active in the **barrier-repair cream** that anchors the brand thesis — it pairs naturally with ceramides (the "wall") and niacinamide, adding the "water-channel/osmolyte" layer (the "plumbing") and a differentiated, science-forward, sustainably-sourced story (extremolyte from the resurrection plant). It is **not** a standalone hero: the proven benefit is hydration/barrier, which is table-stakes, and the unique mechanism's *added* magnitude is modest/unquantified.

**Longevity-relevance note:** GG's tie to skin *longevity* is real but specific: **AQP3 declines/dysregulates with age and barrier impairment**, and GG is among the few hydration actives shown (in human skin) to *restore* that machinery rather than merely add surface water — a genuine "support the skin's own systems" angle that fits a skin-longevity brand. The extremolyte/desiccation-tolerance biology (cyanobacteria, resurrection plant) is a strong *educational/differentiation* asset. The honest gap is that the **anti-aging endpoints beyond hydration are unproven independently** — if we want to claim firmness/elasticity, the path is **our own finished-formula instrumental + clinical testing**, not the existing literature.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

> _Independent adversarial review, 2026-06-19. Each headline claim checked for: (a) HUMAN + TOPICAL vs borrowed in-vitro/animal/oral evidence; (b) whether cited studies are real and say what's claimed; (c) industry funding / sample size. Primary sources (refs 1, 4, 11, 12, 15) were re-fetched/re-searched and verified._

### Overall grade: **B− (moderate, for HYDRATION + BARRIER only; everything beyond that is C/D)**

GG is genuinely *unusual* among "extremolyte/longevity" actives in having **one real, peer-reviewed, human, topical, vehicle-controlled study** (Schrader/Beiersdorf 2012) that supports its headline mechanism (AQP3 ↑, TEWL ↓). That earns it a **B for the hydration/barrier claim** — but it is held below A because: the single human study is **small (TEWL arm ~23 subjects), short (~3 weeks), manufacturer-conducted (Beiersdorf COI), and limited to hydration/barrier/AQP3 endpoints (no wrinkle/firmness/elasticity)**; there is **no independent replication**; and the human AQP3 readout was assayed on **suction-blister epidermis**, not intact in-situ skin. Every claim beyond hydration/barrier is **C–D** (supplier in-vivo or in-vitro only).

### What verified cleanly (claims are defensible as written/edited)
- **Ref 1 (Schrader 2012, *Skin Pharmacol Physiol* 25(4):192–199, PMID 22584263):** REAL. Abstract confirms: in-vitro keratinocytes (3% w/v) ↑AQP3 mRNA + protein; topical GG 5% penetrated epidermis (suction blister); GG "significantly increased AQP3 mRNA levels in the skin and reduced transepidermal water loss vs vehicle-controlled areas." This is **human + topical** — the dossier's central claim holds. (Edited to add the n≈23 / suction-blister nuance.)
- **Ref 4 (Schagen 2017, EURO COSMETICS):** REAL **trade-magazine** (Schagen, Overhagen, Bilstein), **not peer-reviewed**, supplier-affiliated (Bitop). The numbers cited (firmness +24%, roughness −3%, wrinkle vol −7%, elasticity +30/34/41%) match the source. Dossier's skepticism is correct.
- **Ref 11 (Del Vecchio 2024, *Cell Physiol Biochem*, PMID 39166549):** REAL. In-vitro HS disease model; GG restores AQP3, reduces hyperproliferation, enhances migration; authors themselves note it "only partially mimics" in-vivo pathology. Correctly tiered as in-vitro, not anti-aging.
- **Ref 15 (2025 *Cosmetics* review):** REAL and supports the honest "GG is one of several AQP3 modulators, not uniquely superior" point.

### Errors / overstatements found and corrected in this dossier
1. **Ref 15 author was wrong.** Dossier said "Mehta H. et al." — the actual **sole author is Agnieszka Gunia-Krzyżak** (Jagiellonian University). **Fixed.**
2. **Ref 12 (AGEs ↓ AQP3) was overstated.** Dossier asserted flatly "AGEs *reduce* AQP3." The real paper (Luo et al. 2023, *Int J Mol Sci* 24(2):1376) found AGEs **increased** AQP3 under basal conditions and decreased it only under specific/chronic (e.g. HDAC-inhibited, diabetes-relevant) conditions — i.e. **bidirectional/condition-dependent, not a clean downregulation.** **Softened in the table, Tier-1 text, and reference note.**
3. **"+215% aquaporins" framing sharpened.** The dossier called it purely a "brand figure, not from the peer-reviewed paper." More precisely, the **+215% magnitude appears to originate in the in-vitro keratinocyte arm of the peer-reviewed work**, then is re-packaged by Eucerin as a **finished-product, on-skin, 24h** result — which is the marketing leap. Either way it is **not a proven on-skin product outcome.** **Clarified.**
4. **Pivotal-study human endpoint framed too cleanly.** Added that the human AQP3 mRNA result is from **suction-blister-derived epidermis** (in-vivo-treated, ex-vivo-assayed), and supplied the verified **n≈23 / ~3-week** detail for the TEWL arm.

### Main caveats (carry into any copy/formulation decision)
- **Conflict-of-interest concentration:** the only peer-reviewed human study is **Beiersdorf's** (sells the ingredient via Eucerin); all the "anti-aging" numbers are **Bitop's** (sells Glycoin). Independent, disinterested human efficacy data are **absent**.
- **Borrowed-tier evidence to NOT import:** oral glucosylglycerol data (sports-nutrition/glycemic) carry **no read-across** to skin; AQP3-knockout mouse work establishes AQP3 biology but **does not test GG**; the resurrection-plant survival story is **plant biology, not a human skin outcome**.
- **No head-to-head vs glycerol.** GG's edge over good glycerol on AQP3 is **assumed, not demonstrated** (ref 15 lists both as modulators).

### Claims to SOFTEN or NOT use in brand copy (D-tier — unproven topically / supplier-only)
- ❌ Firmness +24% / elasticity +30–41% / wrinkle volume −7% (supplier trade-mag, n=30, not peer-reviewed)
- ❌ Brightening / pigmentation −38% (supplier datasheet only)
- ❌ Blue-light protection, prebiotic/microbiome, antioxidant/SOD1, ATP/"cell revitalization," pro-collagen I, skin density +9.9% (supplier or in-vitro only)
- ❌ "Increases aquaporins by 215% / 30%" as an on-skin finished-product claim
- ✅ **Defensible (B-tier, with ref 1):** "intense, long-lasting hydration," "helps reinforce the skin's moisture barrier" (TEWL data), "supports the skin's own water-channel network (aquaporins)" — kept appearance/support-framed — plus the resurrection-plant story as **narrative, not efficacy proof.**
