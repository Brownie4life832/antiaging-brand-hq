# Ingredient: Copper Peptides — GHK-Cu (INCI: Copper Tripeptide-1)

**Researched:** 2026-06-18 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product

## What it is
GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring tripeptide (glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine) bound to copper. It declines in the body with age and is one of the most-studied skincare peptides, with a genuine wound-healing and tissue-remodeling pedigree. Marketed for collagen support, firmness, and skin "regeneration." Among the "longevity" actives in this set it has some of the **oldest and deepest** science (decades of work, including by Pickart).

## What it does (claimed → evidenced)
| Benefit | Evidence strength | Source(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Increases collagen / skin density (human topical trial) | moderate (small RCTs, e.g., ~21 women, ~28% avg collagen increase) | EurekAlert / clinical summary [1]; review [3] |
| Improves firmness / reduces lines & wrinkles, increases skin thickness (eye cream, photodamage) | moderate (small placebo-controlled trials, ~41 women) | [3][4] |
| Outperformed vitamin C / retinoic acid on collagen in a comparison | weak–moderate (small, older study) | [3] |
| Anti-inflammatory, wound-healing, antioxidant, broad gene modulation | moderate (strong mechanistic + wound literature) | [3] |
| Independent wrinkle improvement on laser-resurfaced skin | weak / null (no significant independent effect in that specific context) | Liebert/Arch Facial Plast Surg [2] |

## How it fits Kintsugi
Strong fit. GHK-Cu sits at the intersection of our "repair / renew / mend" thesis and the longevity/regenerative-aesthetics conversation, but — unlike exosomes, growth factors, and OS-01 — it is **openly available, affordable, well-characterized, and backed by decades of (admittedly small) human and mechanistic data.** It is a credible, defensible **supporting hero** that lets us tell a regenerative-peptide story without the evidence/regulatory exposure of the flashier actives.

## Formulation notes
- Distinctive **blue color** (copper) — affects product aesthetics; can be a feature or a constraint.
- **Compatibility caveats:** classic guidance is to separate GHK-Cu from direct/high-dose **vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid)** and strong exfoliating acids in the same step (potential redox/efficacy interactions); copper can also theoretically affect oxidation of some antioxidants. Layer at different times or formulate carefully.
- Works around mildly acidic-to-neutral pH; water-soluble; low use levels. Pairs well with HA, niacinamide, ceramides, gentle peptides.

## Concerns & cautions
- Generally well tolerated; possible irritation/sensitivity in some users at higher levels.
- Theoretical "too much copper" concerns are not supported as a practical issue at cosmetic use levels, but copper-sensitive individuals should patch test.
- No major pregnancy or photosensitivity flags identified; conservative copy advised.
- Sustainability/sourcing straightforward (synthetic peptide + copper); fewer ethical/provenance issues than biologically-derived actives.

## Claims caution
- ✅ Safe cosmetic language: "supports the appearance of firmer, denser-looking skin," "helps visibly improve firmness and fine lines," "supports skin's renewal," "comforting/soothing."
- ⚠️ Risky / drug-like: "boosts collagen by 28%," "regenerates skin," "heals wounds," "remodels tissue." The collagen-% figures come from small studies and as marketing claims would need substantiation/legal review; wound-healing language is drug territory. Prefer "supports" over quantified "boosts." See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## Cost / sourcing
Low-to-mid cost tier; widely available as Copper Tripeptide-1 from many cosmetic peptide suppliers. Easy, stable supply — a practical advantage over the rarer/proprietary longevity actives.

## Verdict for Kintsugi
✅ — **Strong supporting active, and the most defensible "regenerative peptide" in this set.** Best evidence-to-risk ratio of the longevity-adjacent actives: real (if small-scale) human data, decades of mechanism, open sourcing, clean regulatory status. Use as a credibility anchor in a repair/renewal product; could co-headline a "longevity peptide" hero alongside a senotherapeutic peptide.
