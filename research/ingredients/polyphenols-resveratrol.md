# Ingredient: Resveratrol & related polyphenol antioxidants (INCI: Resveratrol; related: Ferulic Acid, Tetrahydrocurcumin, Epigallocatechin Gallate/green tea, etc.)

**Researched:** 2026-06-18 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product

## What it is
Resveratrol is a plant polyphenol (from grapes, Japanese knotweed, etc.) best known in the longevity world as a **sirtuin (SIRT1) activator** — the same biology behind the "red wine / caloric-restriction-mimetic" longevity story. Topically it is an antioxidant that scavenges free radicals and modulates SIRT1/AMPK and NF-κB pathways. We group it here with the broader family of polyphenol antioxidants used for environmental defense.

## What it does (claimed → evidenced)
| Benefit | Evidence strength | Source(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Antioxidant / free-radical scavenging; raises intrinsic antioxidant capacity | moderate (mechanistic + formulation studies) | Critical review [3]; MDPI review [5] |
| Improves fine lines, firmness, elasticity, hyperpigmentation, radiance (12-wk, in a blend) | moderate (clinical study of 1% resveratrol + baicalin + vitamin E blend) | Farris et al. / JDD [2] |
| SIRT1 "longevity pathway" activation in skin | weak for topical (mechanistically plausible; human-skin longevity outcomes not demonstrated) | [3][5] |
| Soothing / supports several inflammatory skin conditions | weak–moderate (mostly review-level) | [3] |

## How it fits Kintsugi
Good fit and brand-resonant in two ways: (1) it's a credible antioxidant for "environmental defense / photoaging" — the evidence-based core of anti-aging; and (2) the **SIRT1 longevity-pathway** narrative dovetails with our skin-longevity positioning, giving a science-forward story with a real (if oral-derived) longevity pedigree. Honesty caveat: most of resveratrol's *longevity* fame comes from oral/animal research, and topical longevity outcomes in human skin are not established. Best used as a **supporting antioxidant** with a tasteful longevity framing.

## Formulation notes
- **Stability is the central challenge:** poor aqueous solubility; degrades under UV, heat, and pH swings. Needs smart formulation (encapsulation/nanocarriers/dendrimers, opaque/airless packaging, antioxidant pairings).
- Synergizes well with **ferulic acid + vitamin E** (and vitamin C) — classic antioxidant network that also improves stability.
- Effective levels in studied blends ~1%. Keep away from conditions that accelerate oxidation.

## Concerns & cautions
- Generally well tolerated; low irritation; suitable for sensitive skin in most formulations.
- Sourcing: plant-derived (often from Japanese knotweed); standardize purity (trans-resveratrol content).
- No significant photosensitivity flag (it's antioxidant), but it is not a sunscreen — pair with SPF for any photoaging claim.
- Pregnancy: no specific topical flag identified; conservative copy advised.

## Claims caution
- ✅ Safe cosmetic language: "antioxidant defense," "helps protect the look of skin from environmental stress," "helps improve the appearance of fine lines and radiance," "supports skin's resilience."
- ⚠️ Risky / drug-like: "activates longevity genes," "extends skin cell lifespan," "reverses aging." SIRT1/longevity-pathway language is fine as *education/mechanism* but must not become an efficacy/disease claim. Avoid implying systemic longevity benefit from a topical. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## Cost / sourcing
Low-to-mid cost tier for the raw material; stabilized/encapsulated trade-name versions cost more. Widely available; well-understood supply chain.

## Verdict for Kintsugi
✅ — **Solid supporting antioxidant with a usable longevity (SIRT1) story.** Evidence is decent for antioxidant/photoaging-appearance benefits (especially in well-formulated blends) and the longevity-pathway angle is on-brand if framed as mechanism, not miracle. Not a standalone hero, but a credible, low-risk pillar of a "skin longevity / environmental defense" product. Pair with ferulic acid + vitamin E.
