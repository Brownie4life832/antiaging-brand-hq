# Ingredient: Resveratrol (INCI: Resveratrol)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** NAD+ / sirtuin / AMPK / cellular-energy actives (polyphenol stilbene)
**Slug:** polyphenols-resveratrol

> **Read this first (the one-paragraph honest version).** Resveratrol is a genuinely interesting antioxidant polyphenol with a deep mechanistic literature (SIRT1, AMPK, Nrf2, NF-κB, anti-glycation) and a powerful *longevity* brand story — but that story is overwhelmingly built on **oral and animal** research, much of which has been **disappointing or contested in humans**. For *topical human skin*, the evidence is real but **thin and mostly from manufacturer-funded studies of multi-ingredient blends or low-dose monotherapy**. The most rigorous standalone topical RCT (2025) found only a **modest, statistically marginal wrinkle benefit and that only when combined with oral dosing**. Resveratrol is also **chemically fragile** (photo-isomerizes, poorly soluble, poorly penetrating) and, in a few in-vitro models, behaves as a **UVA photosensitizer**. Verdict preview: a credible **supporting** antioxidant with an on-brand longevity narrative, **not** a proven hero — and the cleaner play may be a stabilized derivative (resveratryl triacetate) or a sister stilbene (pterostilbene). Detail below.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Resveratrol (trans-resveratrol is the bioactive isomer).
- **INCI:** `Resveratrol`.
- **Chemistry:** 3,5,4′-trihydroxy-*trans*-stilbene. A stilbenoid polyphenol / phytoalexin (plants make it under stress). MW ~228 g/mol. CAS 501-36-0.
- **Natural sources:** grape skins (*Vitis vinifera*), red wine, peanuts, berries; the dominant *commercial* source is **Japanese knotweed (*Polygonum / Reynoutria cuspidatum*)** root extract, and increasingly **fermentation-derived** (yeast-biosynthesized) trans-resveratrol (e.g. Evolva/Lallemand "Veri-te").
- **Key forms / derivatives used in skincare:**
  - **trans-Resveratrol** — the active, but unstable form.
  - **Resveratryl Triacetate (RTA)** (INCI: `Resveratryl Triacetate`) — acetylated, far more stable pro-form; hydrolyzes to resveratrol in skin. Approved/used as a functional whitening active in Korea. *This is often the smarter formulation choice than free resveratrol.*
  - **Resveratryl Triglycolate (RTG)** — another stabilized ester analog.
  - **Oxyresveratrol** — hydroxylated analog; notably more potent on tyrosinase and anti-glycation in vitro.
  - **Pterostilbene** — dimethylated resveratrol analog (from blueberries); more lipophilic, more stable, much higher oral bioavailability; a strong "sister molecule" to consider.
  - **Piceatannol, piceid (polydatin)** — related stilbene glycosides.
- **Adjacent polyphenol antioxidants** (previously grouped in this dossier; each warrants its own file if pursued): ferulic acid, epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG / green tea), tetrahydrocurcumin. They are *not* the same molecule and should not be cited interchangeably.

## 2. Class & Mechanism (how it works at the skin/cell level)

Resveratrol is the canonical **caloric-restriction mimetic** of the longevity field. In skin it acts through several overlapping pathways (note: most are demonstrated in cell/animal models, not in living human skin):

1. **Direct antioxidant / free-radical scavenging.** Quenches ROS generated by UV and pollution; the phenolic hydroxyls donate electrons. This is its most format-agnostic, plausible topical action.
2. **Nrf2 activation.** Upregulates the cell's *endogenous* antioxidant machinery (e.g. heme oxygenase-1/HO-1, glutathione enzymes) — i.e. it can boost intrinsic defense, not just neutralize radicals directly.
3. **SIRT1 (sirtuin) activation.** The famous "longevity gene" axis. SIRT1 is an NAD⁺-dependent deacetylase; activating it promotes mitochondrial function, autophagy, genomic-stress resistance, and deacetylation/repression of NF-κB. *Caveat: whether resveratrol activates SIRT1 directly or indirectly (via AMPK / a fluorophore-substrate artifact in the original assays) has been scientifically contested for over a decade.*
4. **AMPK activation.** Cellular energy-sensor; drives autophagy and mitochondrial biogenesis. Some evidence places AMPK *upstream* of the SIRT1 effects.
5. **NF-κB suppression → anti-inflammatory ("inflammaging").** Damps pro-inflammatory cytokine cascades.
6. **MMP-1 down-regulation.** Inhibits the matrix metalloproteinase that degrades collagen after UV/IL-1β exposure → collagen-sparing in theory.
7. **Anti-glycation.** Reduces formation of advanced glycation end-products (AGEs, e.g. CML) and the senescence/stiffening they cause in the dermal matrix (in-vitro 3D-skin and fibroblast data — see §4).
8. **Anti-senescence.** Lowers senescence markers (p16^INK4a, p21) and SASP cytokines in stressed fibroblasts/keratinocytes — *context-dependent* (at high concentrations resveratrol can itself induce senescence/apoptosis; see §4 honesty note).
9. **Tyrosinase / melanogenesis inhibition.** Suppresses tyrosinase activity, expression, and MITF signaling → pigment-lightening mechanism.

**Class fit (NAD+/sirtuin/AMPK/cellular-energy actives):** Resveratrol is arguably the *flagship* small molecule of this class for brand-story purposes — it is the molecule that put "sirtuins" on the map. That is exactly why claims discipline matters: the narrative outruns the topical human data.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core)

> Read each tier's "format" label carefully. The biggest honesty trap with resveratrol is **borrowing oral/animal/in-vitro findings to imply topical benefit.**

### TIER 1 — In vitro (cell culture, reconstructed skin) — *abundant, mechanistically rich, but lowest translational weight*

- **Antioxidant / Nrf2 / AMPK-FOXO3 anti-senescence in keratinocytes.** Resveratrol activated the AMPK–FOXO3 cascade and prevented oxidative-stress-induced senescence and proliferative dysfunction in primary human keratinocytes. *Source [9].*
- **UVB photoprotection in keratinocytes (HaCaT).** Resveratrol upregulated HSP27, raised the Bcl-2/Bax ratio, inhibited caspase-3, and reduced UVB-induced ROS — i.e. protected keratinocytes from UVB photoaging. *Source [10].* A separate study showed resveratrol couples apoptosis with autophagy in UVB-irradiated HaCaT cells. *Source [11].*
- **⚠️ UVA photosensitization (the inconvenient finding).** In several reports resveratrol *sensitized* human keratinocytes to **UVA**-induced apoptosis via mitochondrial oxidative stress and pore opening — i.e. under UVA it can act as a **pro-oxidant photosensitizer**, not a protector. *Source [12].* This is a genuine caution for a daytime "antioxidant defense" positioning and argues for night use / robust SPF pairing.
- **MMP-1 / collagen.** Resveratrol suppresses IL-1β- and UVB-induced MMP-1 in dermal fibroblasts and, via SIRT1, restrains excess TGF-β1/collagen disorganization (wound-healing / anti-scarring models). *Sources [2], [8].*
- **Anti-glycation + anti-senescence in 3D human skin (EpiDermFT).** Markiewicz et al. (2022): methylglyoxal-induced glycation in full-thickness skin models was reversed by topical 0.5% resveratrol and analogs — AGE autofluorescence ↓~50–60%, CML ↓~85% (oxyresveratrol), p16^INK4a ↓~75%, dermal fibroblast density ↑~2.5×, collagen restored. **Oxyresveratrol ≥ resveratrol** on most endpoints. *Source [6].* (Still in-vitro, but among the more skin-relevant models. **Industry-affiliated: authors employed by Estée Lauder and HexisLab — interpret accordingly.**)
- **Tyrosinase / melanogenesis.** Resveratrol inhibits tyrosinase catalytic activity, expression, and MITF signaling; melanin suppressed up to 200 µM without cytotoxicity. But it is a relatively *weak* standalone depigmenter — reviews conclude it works best as a **co-adjuvant**, with **oxyresveratrol** (tyrosinase IC₅₀ ~2.27 µg/mL) and the ester analogs being stronger. *Sources [5], [13].*
- **⚠️ Hormesis / pro-senescence at high dose.** Resveratrol can itself induce DNA-damage-mediated **premature senescence** and down-regulate SIRT1/SIRT2 at higher concentrations — a classic biphasic/hormetic compound. *Source [14].* "More is better" is false here.

### TIER 2 — Animal (mouse/pig)

- **UV photocarcinogenesis / photoprotection.** Topical resveratrol reduced UVB-induced skin tumorigenesis, edema, and oxidative markers in mouse models; mechanistic reviews of UVR-induced skin carcinogenesis describe survivin, NF-κB, and mTOR modulation. *Sources [10], (Aziz et al. 2018 review).*
- **Pigmentation.** Topical 1% resveratrol reduced UV-induced pigmentation in animal models. *Source [5].*
- **Wound healing / collagen organization.** Resveratrol produced thinner, more organized collagen via SIRT1 in murine wound models (vs disordered collagen with SIRT1 deletion). *Source [8].*
- *Translational caveat:* rodent skin penetration and biology differ markedly from human; these support plausibility, not efficacy claims.

### TIER 3 — Human ORAL (the source of the "longevity" fame — NOT topical)

- **Pharmacokinetics / bioavailability.** Oral resveratrol is **notoriously poorly bioavailable**: rapidly conjugated to resveratrol-3-O-sulfate, resveratrol-3-O-glucuronide, resveratrol-4′-O-glucuronide by gut/liver first-pass, leaving very low free-parent plasma levels. *Sources [15], [16].* Delivery tech (LipiSperse, micronization) raises exposure modestly. *Source [16].*
- **Systemic outcome trials are mixed/disappointing.** RCTs in NAFLD, dyslipidemia, metabolic and exercise contexts show inconsistent results — some metabolic/antioxidant-marker improvements, many null. *Sources [15] and refs therein.* The grand "resveratrol extends human lifespan" promise is **not** established.
- **Relevance to skin:** the 2025 skin RCT (below) confirms oral dosing raises serum resveratrol conjugates and *contributed* to the wrinkle effect — but this is an **oral/ingestible (nutricosmetic) result**, not proof that a topical cream alone works.

### TIER 4 — Human TOPICAL (what actually matters for a cream)

**4a. Randomized controlled (highest topical tier — but sparse and industry-funded):**

- **Rao et al., 2025 — *Frontiers in Aging* — the key standalone trans-resveratrol RCT.** Double-blind, placebo-controlled, 4-arm (oral±, topical±) factorial; n=134 enrolled / 122 completed; healthy women ≥40; **1.5% trans-resveratrol cream** (30 mg/dose) and/or **75 mg oral** twice daily for **8 weeks**.
  - **Wrinkles:** all arms improved; only the **oral+topical (A/A)** arm beat placebo significantly (≈11.9% vs 6.3% reduction, p<0.05). **Topical-alone did not significantly beat placebo on the primary wrinkle endpoint.**
  - **Pigmentation:** topical arms (P/A and A/A) improved significantly vs placebo (p<0.001) — the most convincing *topical-attributable* signal.
  - **Sebum:** topical arms *increased* U-zone sebum significantly (88–109%) — neutral-to-negative for oily/acne-prone skin.
  - **No significant difference** in hydration, elasticity, pore size, or skin temperature.
  - **Funding:** Evolva AG / Lallemand (resveratrol manufacturer); several authors employed by the sponsor. **Interpret accordingly.**
  - *Source [1].* **Honest read:** the best-designed standalone topical trial shows a *modest* benefit that largely required the oral component; topical monotherapy's strongest result was on pigmentation.

**4b. Lower-tier human topical (open-label / blends / vendor studies):**

- **Farris et al., 2014 — *JDD* — resveratrol+baicalin+vitamin E night serum (SkinCeuticals "Resveratrol B E", 1% resveratrol / 0.5% baicalin / 1% vitamin E).** Open-label, ~12 weeks: statistically significant improvement vs baseline in fine lines/wrinkles, firmness, elasticity, laxity, hyperpigmentation, radiance, roughness; periorbital **dermal thickness +18.9%** by ultrasound; demonstrated percutaneous absorption and gene-expression changes (HO-1, VEGFA, COL3A1). **Limitations:** no placebo/vehicle control, manufacturer-developed product, **blend** (cannot isolate resveratrol's contribution from baicalin + vitamin E + the antioxidant-network synergy). *Source [2].*
- **Lephart 2021 review** cites topical resveratrol studies in postmenopausal women reporting 63–83% improvement over baseline in wrinkles/firmness/elasticity/hydration at 12 weeks — but again **uncontrolled / phytoestrogen-cosmetic** context. *Source [4].*
- **Pigment / acne signals.** Reviews note a topical resveratrol gel reduced microcomedone area ~66.7% in acne and topical formulations reduced melanin / improved elasticity in small studies. *Source [3].*
- **Skin-lightening analogs (better human data than resveratrol itself).** Resveratryl triacetate (RTA) and resveratryl triglycolate (RTG) have human clinical support as **functional whitening** actives; reviews conclude **resveratrol alone is too weak to whiten as monotherapy** and is best as a co-adjuvant, while the **stabilized esters** perform better. *Sources [5], [13].*

**Bottom line by tier:** Mechanism (in-vitro) = rich. Animal = supportive. Oral human = the famous-but-shaky longevity literature, **not transferable to topical claims**. **Topical human = one modest manufacturer-funded RCT (best result on pigmentation; wrinkle benefit needed oral co-dosing) plus uncontrolled blend studies.** Treat topical anti-wrinkle efficacy as *plausible and emerging, not proven.*

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- Resveratrol penetrates the **stratum corneum** well but **largely stays there**; relatively little reaches viable epidermis/dermis. Tape-stripping/Raman studies show high SC concentration near the surface and a lower constant level in upper viable epidermis. *Source [3].*
- Ex-vivo human-skin data vary widely with vehicle: one study reported ~62% of applied resveratrol permeating after 24 h with substantial SC + epidermis/dermis retention — but this is **formulation-dependent**, not intrinsic. *Source [17].*
- **Penetration enhancers help:** ethosomes, niosomes, solid-lipid nanoparticles, nanoemulsions, liposomes, and oils (e.g. pomegranate-seed oil increased trans-resveratrol penetration up to ~3.1×). *Sources [3], [17], [18].*
- **Implication:** free resveratrol's benefit may be **surface/SC-weighted** (antioxidant film, photoprotective layer) unless deliberately formulated for depth. Lipophilic analogs (pterostilbene) or stabilized esters (RTA) penetrate/behave better.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** studied topical levels are ~**0.5–1.5%** (1% in the Farris blend; 1.5% in the Rao RCT). Reviews cite up to higher % for non-cosmetic (wound/psoriasis) uses. **>1–2% rarely buys more and worsens stability/color.** Avoid "hero dose" inflation — the molecule is hormetic.
- **pH:** trans-resveratrol is most stable roughly **pH 5–8**; **pH > 8 triggers near-immediate trans→cis isomerization**; very low pH (≈2) generates a photo-degradation product. Keep formulas mildly acidic-to-neutral. *Source [19].*
- **Stability — the central problem:**
  - Poor aqueous solubility (lipophilic-ish polyphenol).
  - **Photo-isomerization:** UV converts active *trans* to less-active *cis*; ~70% trans can be lost under UV without protection. *Source [19].*
  - Oxidizes; discolors (yellowing).
  - **Mitigations:** opaque/airless packaging; antioxidant network (vitamin C + E + **ferulic acid**); UV-filter co-formulation (ethylhexyl methoxycrylene cut UV loss from ~70%→~10%); encapsulation (lipid microparticles, niosomes, cyclodextrin complexes) cut photodegradation substantially; or **use a stabilized derivative (RTA) or pterostilbene** to sidestep the problem. *Source [19].*
- **Pairings (synergy):** **ferulic acid + vitamin E + vitamin C** (classic antioxidant network — improves both efficacy and stability); baicalin (per the SkinCeuticals blend). Reasonable alongside niacinamide and peptides.
- **Conflicts / cautions:** high-pH systems; strong direct-acid actives if they push pH extremes; unprotected daytime use given the UVA-photosensitizer in-vitro signal. Not a substitute for SPF.
- **Sensory:** can impart yellow/tan tint and a faint herbaceous note at higher loads; formulate for elegance.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation/tolerability:** generally **well tolerated**; the Rao RCT reported only mild, infrequent adverse events across all arms; reviews report no significant irritation in pilot topical use. *Sources [1], [3].*
- **Sensitization:** low sensitization potential; a sulfated derivative tested **below OECD sensitization thresholds** and even mitigated allergen-induced sensitization. *Source [20].*
- **Sun sensitivity / photosafety:** **nuanced.** Resveratrol is *not* a registered photosensitizer in product use, **but** multiple in-vitro studies show it can sensitize keratinocytes to **UVA**-induced apoptosis (pro-oxidant under UVA). Prudent posture: position as **PM/night** active and/or always pair with broad-spectrum SPF; don't market a standalone daytime "sun protection" benefit. *Source [12].*
- **Estrogenic / phytoestrogen concern:** resveratrol is a **weak** mixed estrogen-receptor agonist/antagonist — binding affinity **>1000× weaker than 17β-estradiol**; reviews conclude topical safety concern at cosmetic concentrations (1–3%) is **minimal**. *Source [4].* Still, for a skincare brand it is wise to **avoid making hormonal/"estrogen-deficient skin" claims** and to keep conservative copy.
- **Pregnancy/lactation:** no specific *topical* contraindication identified, but data are limited and resveratrol's ER activity + general "avoid unstudied actives in pregnancy" caution argue for **conservative, non-specific copy** (and "consult your doctor" framing). Do not affirmatively market to pregnant users. *[Unverified for definitive topical-pregnancy safety — no dedicated study found.]*
- **Acne-prone caution:** topical resveratrol **raised sebum** in the 2025 RCT — flag for oily/acne-prone audiences. *Source [1].*
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** plant extraction (Japanese knotweed — note knotweed is itself an invasive species, so harvesting can be framed positively) or **fermentation-derived** (often the more consistent, vegan, traceable, and sustainability-friendly route). Specify **trans-isomer purity** in supplier specs.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic vs. drug line:** as a topical antioxidant for *appearance*, resveratrol sits comfortably on the **cosmetic** side. The moment copy implies it **activates longevity genes**, **extends cell lifespan**, **boosts collagen by X%**, **treats/prevents** photoaging or skin cancer, or alters skin **structure/function**, it crosses toward **drug** claims (US FDA) and would need substantiation/legal review.
- **✅ Lower-risk cosmetic language:** "antioxidant defense," "helps protect the look of skin against environmental/oxidative stress," "helps improve the appearance of fine lines, tone, and radiance," "supports skin's natural resilience," "visibly evens tone." Education about the SIRT1/sirtuin *mechanism* is acceptable as **science storytelling**, clearly framed as how the molecule behaves in research — not as a promised user outcome.
- **⚠️ Risky / avoid without substantiation + legal:** "activates your longevity genes," "turns on sirtuins to reverse aging," "extends skin-cell lifespan," "boosts collagen," "anti-glycation that reverses sugar damage," "sun protection / photoprotection," "estrogen for menopausal skin," any disease/cancer-prevention implication.
- **The longevity-story trap (brand-specific):** because resveratrol's fame is *systemic/oral*, marketing a *topical* with the full longevity halo risks an implied **structure/function** claim and is the single biggest claims-discipline hazard for this ingredient. Keep mechanism = education, benefit = appearance.
- **Substantiation reality:** the human topical evidence (one modest, sponsor-funded RCT + uncontrolled blends) is **thin support** for strong efficacy claims. Lean on appearance-level, hedged language; reserve numeric/efficacy claims for in-house consumer-perception or instrumental testing on the *final* formula.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier:** **low-to-mid** for standard trans-resveratrol (knotweed extract). **Mid-to-higher** for: high-purity fermentation-derived trans-resveratrol (e.g. Veri-te), stabilized esters (**resveratryl triacetate**), oxyresveratrol, pterostilbene, and encapsulated/trade-name delivery systems.
- **Supply:** widely available, mature supply chain; vegan fermentation routes increasingly common. Specify **% trans-resveratrol** and stabilization/encapsulation in the brief.
- **Practical recommendation:** for a premium, claims-disciplined product, budget for either (a) a **stabilized derivative (RTA / encapsulated trans-resveratrol)** or (b) **pterostilbene**, paired in a **ferulic acid + vitamin E (+C)** antioxidant network, in opaque airless packaging.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (marketing overreach to avoid)

- **"Longevity molecule that switches on your sirtuins."** Overreach for topical. The SIRT1-direct-activation mechanism is **scientifically contested**, and the longevity fame is from **oral/animal** work — much of it null in humans.
- **"Resveratrol = drinking red wine for your skin."** Cute but false-equivalence; wine concentrations are trivial and oral ≠ topical.
- **"Reverses sun/sugar damage."** In-vitro/3D-model anti-glycation and photo-protection data don't license a "reverses damage" promise on human skin.
- **"Powerful skin brightener."** Resveratrol *alone* is a **weak** depigmenter; the credible whitening data are for the **analogs (RTA/RTG)** or oxyresveratrol.
- **"Antioxidant sun shield."** Ignores the **UVA-photosensitizer** in-vitro signal; never substitute for SPF.
- **"More % = more anti-aging."** False — hormetic molecule; high doses can be **pro-senescent** in vitro.
- **Honest hero claim available:** "a science-storied antioxidant that helps defend skin's appearance against daily oxidative stress, with emerging evidence for tone and fine-line *appearance*." That's defensible.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ SUPPORTING (conditional on smart formulation) — not a standalone hero.**

One-line reasoning: Resveratrol is a credible, well-tolerated antioxidant with an unmatched *longevity narrative* for a NAD+/sirtuin-class story, but topical human efficacy is **thin (one modest sponsor-funded RCT + uncontrolled blends)** and the molecule is **fragile and biphasic** — so it earns a supporting role inside an antioxidant network (ferulic acid + vitamin E ± C), ideally as a **stabilized derivative (resveratryl triacetate) or its sister stilbene pterostilbene**, with disciplined "appearance/defense" claims.

- **Longevity-relevance note:** This is the brand's **best storytelling ingredient** for the "skin longevity / sirtuin / cellular-energy" theme *because* of its fame — which is exactly why it must be handled with the most claims discipline. Use it to *teach* the sirtuin/AMPK longevity mechanism (education) while promising only **appearance-level** benefits (compliance). If a true hero in this class is wanted, pair resveratrol's story with a partner that has stronger topical human data, or treat the **whole antioxidant network** as the hero rather than resveratrol alone.
- **Next steps if pursued:** (1) decide free resveratrol vs RTA vs pterostilbene; (2) lock antioxidant-network + opaque/airless packaging; (3) PM-positioning + SPF pairing; (4) reserve efficacy claims for in-house testing on the final formula. ## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Independent skeptical pass over the headline efficacy claims, checking (a) whether each claim's evidence is genuinely *human + topical* or quietly borrowed from in-vitro/animal/oral data, (b) whether the cited studies are real and say what is claimed, and (c) whether key studies are industry-funded or underpowered.

### Overall evidence grade: **C** (weak / indirect for topical human efficacy)

Resveratrol is a mechanistically rich antioxidant whose *topical human* anti-aging case rests on a single modest, manufacturer-funded RCT (whose only significant topical-attributable signal was pigmentation, **not** wrinkles) plus uncontrolled blend/vendor studies. Everything stronger (lifespan/longevity, sirtuin "activation", anti-glycation reversal, collagen boosting, photoprotection) is in-vitro, animal, or oral — and oral resveratrol's own systemic literature is largely null/disappointing in humans. This is a **C**: real but weak/indirect topically, with one genuine bright spot (topical pigmentation, p<0.001) that on its own would merit a B. It is **not** a D — the dossier is unusually honest, does not pretend topical anti-wrinkle efficacy is proven, and the headline numbers check out.

### What was independently verified (and held up)

- **Rao et al. 2025 RCT (Source [1]) — confirmed accurate to the number.** 134 enrolled / 122 completed; wrinkle change P/P −6.3%, A/A −11.9% with only A/A (oral+topical) significant vs placebo (p<0.05); **topical-alone (P/A) did NOT significantly beat placebo on the primary wrinkle endpoint** — correctly stated. Pigmentation: P/A and A/A significant vs placebo (p<0.001) — correct. Sebum: P/A +88.7%, A/A +108.7% (dossier "88–109%") — exact. No significant difference in hydration, elasticity, pore size — correct. **Funded by Evolva AG; authors employed by Evolva/Lallemand** — correctly disclosed. This is the linchpin claim and it is faithfully reported.
- **Markiewicz et al. 2022 (Source [6]) — confirmed.** In-vitro 3D (EpiDermFT) / fibroblasts, NOT living human skin (correctly labeled). AGE ↓~50–60%, CML ↓~85% (oxyresveratrol), p16 ↓~75%, fibroblast density ↑~2.5× (oxyresveratrol), oxyresveratrol ≥ resveratrol — all match. **Industry-affiliated (Estée Lauder + HexisLab employees)** — this was omitted from the original dossier and has now been added inline.
- **UVA-photosensitizer finding (Source [12]) — confirmed real** (PMID 22673012) and accurately characterized as an in-vitro pro-oxidant signal warranting caution. Note a contradicting in-vitro report exists (resveratrol *protecting* HaCaT from UVA via Keap1 downregulation), so the dossier is right to treat this as nuanced/contested rather than settled.

### Main caveats

1. **The longevity halo is borrowed.** Resveratrol's fame is oral/animal and largely unconfirmed in human systemic trials. None of it transfers to a topical cream. The dossier already says this loudly (§3, §10, §11) — keep it that way.
2. **One sponsor-funded topical RCT carries the whole topical case**, and its topical-alone arm failed on the primary (wrinkle) endpoint. The only clean topical-attributable win was **pigmentation**.
3. **Two of the most-cited supporting studies are industry-affiliated** (Rao = Evolva/Lallemand; Markiewicz = Estée Lauder/HexisLab). No independent, vehicle-controlled topical RCT of resveratrol monotherapy was found.
4. **"Anti-glycation / anti-senescence / collagen restoration" numbers are in-vitro 3D-model figures**, not human-skin outcomes — fine as mechanism, must never be quoted as user results.
5. **Hormetic / biphasic** (Source [14], confirmed plausible): high doses can be pro-senescent in vitro — undercuts any "more %" messaging.

### Claims softened in this pass (edits made to the dossier)

- **Hallmarks table — "Oxidative stress / environmental defense":** human-topical tier downgraded from *moderate* → **weak** (the human-topical evidence is entirely indirect/blend-level; no human-topical study measured resveratrol-monotherapy oxidative-defense endpoints).
- **Hallmarks table — "Photoaging (UV)":** corrected from "blend RCT (moderate)" → **weak**, noting the supporting blend study was *open-label (not an RCT)* and the one true topical RCT showed a **null topical-alone wrinkle result**. (The prior cell mislabeled an uncontrolled study as an RCT and overstated the tier.)
- **Markiewicz [6] entry:** added explicit **Estée Lauder / HexisLab industry-employment** disclosure (previously omitted).

### Claims that remain defensible as written

- §4a Rao RCT reporting (numbers exact, including the honest "topical needed oral co-dosing" read).
- §4 / §10 framing of oral, animal, and in-vitro data as non-transferable to topical claims.
- Pigmentation as the strongest topical-attributable signal (verified p<0.001, both topical arms).
- UVA-photosensitizer caution; hormesis caution; "weak standalone depigmenter / esters are stronger" conclusion.
- §8 claims-discipline guardrails and §10 hype-vs-evidence list — these are conservative and well-aligned with the evidence.

**Net:** the dossier was already adversarially honest; verification upgraded its accuracy at the margins (two tier downgrades + one missing COI disclosure) rather than uncovering fabrication or overreach. Grade **C** reflects the *ingredient's* thin topical-human evidence, not a defect in the write-up.
