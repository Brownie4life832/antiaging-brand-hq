# Ingredient: Legacy UVB organic filters (INCI: Ethylhexyl Methoxycinnamate; Homosalate; Ethylhexyl Salicylate; Octocrylene; Phenylbenzimidazole Sulfonic Acid; Isoamyl p-Methoxycinnamate; Diethylhexyl Butamido Triazone; Polysilicone-15; Disodium Phenyl Dibenzimidazole Tetrasulfonate)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⚠️ conditional — formulation building-blocks for a possible SPF, not heroes; one member (octinoxate) flagged for retirement · **Class:** Sunscreen / photoprotection filters & adjuncts

> Exhaustive, citation-backed literature review of a **family of organic UVB (and UVB-leaning) filters**, treated together because they share a niche: they cover the burning end of the spectrum (≈290–320 nm), most contribute little or no UVA, and several are mainly used as **SPF-boosters, oil-phase solubilizers, or photostabilizers** rather than as the centrepiece of a sunscreen. Evidence is graded by tier (in-vitro → animal → human oral → human topical) and the dossier is deliberately blunt about where the evidence is borrowed across formats. These are **UV filters / OTC-drug actives**, not cosmetic "treatment" ingredients — that framing drives the whole analysis. Cosmetic-claims guardrails per [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md) and [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md). See the parent category file [`sunscreen-photoprotection.md`](sunscreen-photoprotection.md), the UVA workhorse [`avobenzone.md`](avobenzone.md), the modern alternatives in [`next-gen-uv-filters.md`](next-gen-uv-filters.md), and the mineral filters [`zinc-oxide.md`](zinc-oxide.md) / [`titanium-dioxide.md`](titanium-dioxide.md).

## 1. Identity

A heterogeneous group of nine organic filters, related by **function (UVB-region absorption / SPF-boosting / photostabilizing)** rather than by chemistry. Five chemical sub-families are represented: **cinnamates, salicylates, a cinnamate-like camphor-free acrylate (octocrylene), a benzimidazole-sulfonate pair, a triazine, and a silicone-bound benzalmalonate polymer.**

> The split is the single most important strategic fact in this file: **the five "old guard" US-legal filters (octinoxate, homosalate, octisalate, octocrylene, ensulizole) carry the most baggage**, while the four genuinely better, more photostable members (amiloxate, iscotrizinol, polysilicone-15, Neo Heliopan AP) are **EU/Asia-only and US-illegal**. So a US sunscreen is stuck choosing among the more compromised filters; an EU/UK/Asia sunscreen has the elegant ones available. The US monograph has not added a new filter since the 1990s. (FDA OTC Monograph M020; SpecialChem/INCIDecoder regional limits.)

## 2. Class & mechanism

All nine are **UV photon absorbers**, not biological actors on skin cells. The mechanism is photophysical, not pharmacological:

1. **UVB absorption.** Each is a conjugated aromatic chromophore (cinnamate, salicylate, benzimidazole, triazine, benzalmalonate) that **absorbs a UV photon** — mostly in the UVB (290–320 nm) where sunburn and direct CPD-type DNA damage occur — promoting the molecule to an excited electronic state. (Neo Heliopan AP is the exception: it absorbs **UVA2**, 320–350 nm.) Photons absorbed by the film are photons that never reach living keratinocytes, fibroblasts, melanocytes or dermal collagen/elastin/DNA.
2. **Energy dissipation.** The excited molecule returns to ground state, releasing the energy mainly as **heat** (and minor fluorescence/photochemistry). How *cleanly* it does this is the divider between a "good" and a "fragile" filter.
3. **The two failure modes that split this family:**
   - **Cinnamates (octinoxate, amiloxate) are photolabile.** Octinoxate undergoes **trans→cis (E/Z) photoisomerization** under UV; the *cis* isomer absorbs less, so SPF drifts down with exposure. In aggregates/films it goes further — irreversible breakdown to p-methoxybenzaldehyde and cyclodimers with reduced UV absorbance and some cytotoxicity (Baker 2022; Stein 2017). Crucially, **octinoxate is photochemically incompatible with avobenzone**: together they undergo accelerated photolysis and generate long-lived radicals (Sayre 2005), so the two best-known cheap organic filters should not share a formula.
   - **The photostable members (octocrylene, iscotrizinol, polysilicone-15, Neo Heliopan AP) relax cleanly and survive UV.** Octocrylene's special value is that it acts as a **triplet-state quencher**, soaking up the excited-state energy of avobenzone and **rescuing avobenzone's photostability** — which is why octocrylene appears in so many avobenzone sunscreens despite being a mediocre filter on its own.
4. **Salicylates are weak, gentle "SPF-boosters."** Homosalate and octisalate are poor absorbers (each contributes only a few SPF units even at max %), but they are **excellent oil-phase solvents** that **dissolve crystalline filters (notably avobenzone and oxybenzone)** and improve film uniformity. Their value in a formula is as much **solubilizer/sensory** as it is **filtration**.
5. **No biological "active" role.** None of these signal fibroblasts, build collagen, brighten, hydrate, or repair the barrier. Any anti-aging benefit is entirely **indirect**: photons not absorbed are photons not aging the skin.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

Value = **prevention of UV-driven damage** (mostly UVB; Neo Heliopan AP adds UVA2), upstream of photoaging — but only as part of a finished, broad-spectrum sunscreen, and only via filtration. None acts on skin cells directly.

| Hallmark | Targeted? | Strength (for TOPICAL human use, as part of a sunscreen) |
|---|---|---|
| Photoaging (UV-driven wrinkling, elastosis, sagging) | ✅ Yes — the entire reason to exist | Strong *category* evidence; the Nambour RCT sunscreen was **octinoxate-based** (see §4) |
| Collagen / ECM loss | ✅ Indirect | Strong mechanistic (UV → MMP-1/dermal MMPs); preserved by blocking the photons |
| Pigmentation / uneven tone | ⚠️ Partial / indirect | UVB drives lentigines & tanning; this family is **UVB-heavy and weak on UVA/visible**, the parts most tied to melasma/persistent pigment. Real but incomplete |
| Oxidative stress | ⚠️ Double-edged | Filtering UVB lowers ROS load **in skin**; but *photolabile* octinoxate (esp. with avobenzone) can **add** radicals via photodegradation. Photostable members are neutral-to-good |
| Inflammaging | ➖ Indirect | UVB is a driver of sunburn/erythema/inflammation; filtering helps, no direct anti-inflammatory action |
| Barrier / hydration / TEWL | ❌ No | These filters do nothing for the barrier (other formula components might) |
| Glycation / AGEs | ❌ No | Not a mechanism |
| Cellular senescence | ➖ Indirect only | UVB induces keratinocyte senescence; preventing it plausibly lowers photo-senescence, but no filter-specific human data |
| Mitochondrial decline | ❌ No (and a flag) | Conversely, several (octinoxate, octocrylene, octisalate) show **mitochondrial/oxidative stress signals in vitro** at sub-cytotoxic doses; no human-skin benefit |
| Microbiome | ❌ No | No data |

**Bottom line:** this family's honest territory is **"UVB protection / SPF that helps prevent sunburn and photoaging"** — and only as the *UVB pillar* of a broad-spectrum SPF that gets its UVA coverage elsewhere (avobenzone, Tinosorb, zinc oxide). They are **shields, not treatments**, and most are **partial shields** (UVB-only).

## 4. Evidence review by tier

> The hierarchy matters. There are **no studies of any of these filters as a leave-on "anti-aging active"** because none is one. The relevant questions are: (a) does it absorb UV and stay stable enough to matter? (in-vitro/photophysics), and (b) does a **sunscreen containing these filters** prevent photoaging in humans? (clinical tier). As with avobenzone, the most important fact is that **the single best photoaging-prevention RCT used a sunscreen whose UVB filter was octinoxate** — so for once part of the human evidence is *not* borrowed across formats.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro / photophysics (mechanism + the stability split)

Strong and consistent; it is where this family's character is set.

- **Octinoxate photoisomerization / photodegradation** — the trans→cis isomerization and SPF drift, plus irreversible breakdown in films/aggregates to p-methoxybenzaldehyde and cyclodimers with reduced UV absorbance and cellular toxicity. Baker LA et al., *Photochemical Degradation of the UV Filter Octyl Methoxy Cinnamate Probed via Laser-Interfaced Mass Spectrometry*, Molecules 2022;27(24):8796 (PMID 36557928); MacManus-Spencer / Stein on photolysis & cytotoxic photoproducts. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36557928/ · https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26035445/
- **Octinoxate + avobenzone incompatibility** — Sayre RM, Dowdy JC, et al., *Unexpected Photolysis of the Sunscreen Octinoxate in the Presence of the Sunscreen Avobenzone*, Photochem Photobiol 2005;81(2):452-6 (PMID 15560736): the pair undergoes mutual photolysis (over the expected E/Z isomerization) and produces **long-lived ESR-detectable free radicals** persisting after irradiation ends. Mechanistic root of the "don't combine octinoxate + avobenzone" formulation rule. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15560736/
- **Octocrylene as avobenzone photostabilizer (triplet quencher)** — octocrylene is itself photostable and quenches avobenzone's excited triplet state, markedly improving avobenzone retention under UV. This is octocrylene's main reason to exist in modern formulas (it is a weak filter on its own). Reviewed in ScienceDirect *Octocrylene — an overview*. https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/medicine-and-dentistry/octocrylene
- **Octocrylene → benzophenone degradation** — Downs CA, et al., *Benzophenone Accumulates over Time from the Degradation of Octocrylene in Commercial Sunscreen Products*, Chem Res Toxicol 2021;34(4):1046-54: across 17 commercial sunscreens, an FDA accelerated-aging protocol (6 wk ≈ 1 yr) produced **+38.7% to +199.4% benzophenone** (a known mutagen/carcinogen/endocrine disruptor). The headline study behind octocrylene's reputational damage; note it triggered formal published rebuttals (see §10). https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.chemrestox.0c00461
- **Salicylates as solubilizers, not strong filters** — homosalate/octisalate each add only ~2–3 SPF units even at max %, with no UVA; their formulation value is dissolving crystalline filters (avobenzone) and film uniformity. CIR safety assessments; SPFList technical summaries. https://spflist.com/conventional-sunscreens/octisalate
- **Photostability of the EU-only members** — iscotrizinol (Uvasorb HEB) is among the most photostable organic filters (manufacturer/INCIDecoder: loses only ~10% efficacy after prolonged irradiation); polysilicone-15 (Parsol SLX) embeds the chromophore in a silicone backbone for outstanding photostability and **negligible skin penetration** (very high MW); Neo Heliopan AP (water-soluble, UVA2) is photostable with very low penetration. DSM/Symrise technical data; INCIDecoder. https://incidecoder.com/ingredients/diethylhexyl-butamido-triazone · https://incidecoder.com/ingredients/polysilicone-15
- **Sub-cytotoxic cellular stress signals** — Subcytotoxic avobenzone + ethylhexyl salicylate (octisalate) modulated microRNAs and stress-responsive PI3K/AKT & MAPK signaling in differentiated SH-SY5Y neuronal cells (2025, PMC12897653); octinoxate shows mitochondrial/oxidative-stress signals in vitro. These are **mechanistic flags at concentrations and cell types not representative of skin-surface use** — hypothesis-generating, not evidence of topical harm. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12897653/
- **Limitations:** all photophysics/cell work; tells us how to keep these filters working and where the chemistry is fragile, not whether they confer cosmetic benefit. The biologically relevant readout is the clinical tier.

### Tier 2 — Animal

- Standard nude/hairless-mouse and pig-skin photoprotection and phototoxicity models underlie filter approval (reductions in UV-induced erythema and epidermal thickening with octinoxate/octocrylene systems). De-risked and supported the human work; not independently decisive for cosmetic claims.
- **Endocrine in-vivo (the historically load-bearing animal data):** Schlumpf M, et al., *In vitro and in vivo estrogenicity of UV screens*, Environ Health Perspect 2001;109(3):239-44 (PMID 11333184) — in the immature-rat **uterotrophic assay**, octinoxate (OMC) increased uterine weight **only at very high dietary doses (ED50 ≈ 935 mg/kg/day)**; homosalate was estrogenic in MCF-7 cells; 4-MBC (not in this family) was the most potent. **Context that matters:** the OMC effective dose is enormous relative to any human topical exposure, and later weight-of-evidence work (ToxCast, §7) found octinoxate essentially inactive after data curation. The Schlumpf paper is real and important historically but has been **over-cited as proof of human risk**. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11333184/
- **Aquatic/coral toxicity** (octinoxate especially) is animal/cell data covered in §7 — a sourcing/ESG issue, not an efficacy point.

### Tier 3 — Human, ORAL

- **Not applicable / not relevant.** These are topical UV filters; there is no oral use. The FDA maximal-use *absorption* studies (§5/§7) detect them in plasma after **topical** application — that is a safety/regulatory flag against systemic exposure, not an oral-longevity benefit. Any oral framing would be category error.

### Tier 4 — Human, TOPICAL

#### 4a. RCTs / controlled clinical trials (load-bearing)

There is **no RCT of any of these filters as an isolated active.** The high-quality human evidence is for **broad-spectrum sunscreens that use them** preventing photoaging:

- **Hughes MCB et al. (2013), the Nambour Skin Aging RCT — the landmark.** 903 adults <55 y randomized to **daily** vs. discretionary application of an **SPF 15+ broad-spectrum sunscreen** over 4.5 years; daily use → **~24% less skin aging** by validated microtopography. **The trial sunscreen (Auscreen) combined octinoxate with avobenzone** — i.e., **octinoxate was the UVB filter in the single best photoaging-prevention RCT in the literature.** *Ann Intern Med* 158(11):781-90 (PMID 23732711). https://www.acpjournals.org/doi/10.7326/0003-4819-158-11-201306040-00002
  - **Limitation for *this* file:** the trial proves *daily broad-spectrum sunscreen containing octinoxate (+ avobenzone)* prevents photoaging; it does **not** isolate octinoxate's contribution from avobenzone, SPF, or behavior. Category-level proof with octinoxate as a named component — much stronger than "borrowed across formats," but not a single-ingredient claim. (Ironically, that same octinoxate+avobenzone pairing is the one §4 Tier 1 flags as photochemically fragile.)
- **Green AC et al. (1999), the Nambour skin-cancer RCT** — the parent trial of the same cohort/sunscreen showed daily use reduced **squamous cell carcinoma** incidence; supports that the filter system (octinoxate-based) delivers real biological protection. (Summarized with Hughes 2013.)
- **Vehicle-controlled solar-elastosis & photoaging-regression trials** — daily broad-spectrum SPF over 12–24 months reduces solar elastosis and improves existing photoaging; the consumer SPFs used in these typically contain octinoxate/octisalate/homosalate/octocrylene. Supportive, lower internal validity than Nambour. (StatPearls *Sunscreens and Photoprotection*, NBK537164.) https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK537164/

#### 4b. Lower-tier human topical (reviews, photopatch series, biomonitoring, safety surveillance)

- **Systemic absorption RCTs (Matta 2019/2020, JAMA)** — under maximal use (2 mg/cm², 75% BSA, 4×/day × 4 d), **octinoxate, octisalate, octocrylene and homosalate are all systemically absorbed above the FDA 0.5 ng/mL data-trigger.** Representative day-1 geometric-mean Cmax: **homosalate up to ~23 ng/mL**, octocrylene ~6–8 ng/mL, octinoxate ~5–8 ng/mL, octisalate ~5–6 ng/mL (values vary by vehicle). Matta MK et al., *JAMA* 2020;323(3):256-67. https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2759002
  - **What this means (and doesn't):** crossing 0.5 ng/mL means the FDA wants **further data**, NOT that harm was shown — the authors explicitly said do **not** stop using sunscreen. It is the reason these filters are **not classified GRASE** in the US (see §8). Polysilicone-15, Neo Heliopan AP and ensulizole are designed for **minimal penetration** (high MW or water-soluble/charged), so they are the absorption "winners" — but they're EU-only or UVB-limited.
- **Photoallergy / contact allergy series** — **Octinoxate and octocrylene are the two most clinically relevant photoallergens in this family.** Octocrylene is a notable cause of **photoallergic contact dermatitis**, especially in adults sensitized via ketoprofen (cross-reactivity) and in children; octinoxate is repeatedly named among the commonest UV-filter photoallergens. Salicylates (homo-/octisalate) and ensulizole are comparatively low-allergenicity. Diagnosis needs **photopatch testing**. (European multicentre photopatch studies; endocrine/photoallergy review PMC12317922.) https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12317922/
- **Endocrine biomonitoring (human)** — the 2014–2024 review (PMC12317922) found the **strongest human endocrine signals for oxybenzone/BP-3 and BP-2** (NOT in this family): reduced adolescent testosterone/T3, altered pubertal timing. For *this* family the human signals are sparse and weaker: one study associated **octocrylene with PCOS in overweight Chinese women**; in-vitro salicylate "acrosome-reaction" effects; little for octinoxate in humans despite the rat data. **Read honestly: the scariest endocrine filter (oxybenzone) is not on this list; the members here are lower human concern.** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12317922/

**Honest synthesis of Tier 4:** As *filters*, this family is backed by the strongest evidence in all of anti-aging at the **category** level — daily broad-spectrum sunscreen prevents photoaging, and the flagship RCT used octinoxate. As *molecules judged on their own*, there is no isolated efficacy trial (nor should there be). The genuine clinical *liabilities* are (1) octinoxate's photoinstability/avobenzone incompatibility and ESG overhang, (2) octocrylene's photoallergy and benzophenone-degradation question, and (3) systemic absorption across the US-legal members — not lack of benefit.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

For a UV filter, the *goal* is the opposite of an active: you want it to **stay in/on the stratum-corneum film**, not enter circulation.

- **Penetrators (a flag):** the oil-soluble, low-MW US-legal members — **octinoxate, octisalate, homosalate, octocrylene** — penetrate the stratum corneum and are **detectable in plasma and urine** under maximal use (Matta 2019/2020, §4b). Homosalate shows the highest plasma Cmax of the four (~23 ng/mL). This is a **safety/regulatory** topic, not an efficacy one.
- **Low-penetration members (an advantage):** **Polysilicone-15** (very high MW silicone polymer) and **Neo Heliopan AP** / **ensulizole** (water-soluble, charged/sulfonated) are designed to **stay on the surface** and show negligible systemic absorption — exactly what you want from a filter. Iscotrizinol is large and lipophilic with low penetration. The catch: three of these four are **EU/Asia-only**, and ensulizole is UVB-only.
- **Practical reading:** if "low systemic absorption" is a brand value, the filter choice points **toward polysilicone-15 / Neo Heliopan AP / mineral filters and away from octinoxate/homosalate/octisalate/octocrylene** — but only the EU/Asia market gives you the good options.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentrations (max, region-dependent):** octinoxate US ≤7.5% / EU ≤10%; homosalate US ≤15% / EU ≤7.34% (face-only); octisalate ≤5%; octocrylene ≤10%; ensulizole US ≤4% / EU ≤8%; amiloxate ≤10% (EU/AUS); iscotrizinol ≤10% (EU/AUS); polysilicone-15 ≤10% (EU/AUS); Neo Heliopan AP ≤10% (EU/AUS).
- **pH:**
  - Oil-soluble esters (octinoxate, homosalate, octisalate, octocrylene, amiloxate, iscotrizinol) are pH-tolerant in the oil phase; the dominant stability variable is **light/co-formulation**, not pH.
  - **Ensulizole and Neo Heliopan AP are acids/salts requiring neutralization** — ensulizole (the free acid) must be neutralized (e.g., with TEA, aminomethyl propanol, or NaOH) to dissolve in the water phase, typically targeting pH ~7–8. This is a real formulation constraint and limits pairing with low-pH actives.
- **Stability — the central split:**
  - **Photolabile:** octinoxate (and amiloxate) isomerize/degrade under UV; **never combine octinoxate with avobenzone** (mutual photolysis + radicals, Sayre 2005). If octinoxate is used, pair it with **photostable** co-filters and antioxidants, or skip it.
  - **Photostable + stabilizing:** **octocrylene** is the family's photostabilizer (rescues avobenzone). **Iscotrizinol** and **polysilicone-15** also help stabilize partner filters. Neo Heliopan AP is robust.
  - **Solubilizers:** **octisalate and homosalate dissolve crystalline avobenzone/oxybenzone** and improve film-forming — their biggest formulation value.
- **Pairings (good):** octocrylene + avobenzone (classic US broad-spectrum pair); salicylates as avobenzone solvents; water-soluble UVB (ensulizole) or UVA2 (Neo Heliopan AP) to balance oil-heavy systems and cut greasiness; iscotrizinol/polysilicone-15 to raise SPF photostably (EU). Antioxidants (vitamin E, ferulic) mop up any photodegradation radicals.
- **Pairings (avoid):** **octinoxate + avobenzone** (photolysis); octinoxate as the UVA partner (it isn't one — it's UVB); relying on salicylates for meaningful SPF (they're weak).
- **Sensory:** oil-soluble esters (octinoxate, octisalate, homosalate, octocrylene) contribute an **emollient-to-oily, sometimes heavy/tacky** feel; octocrylene in particular can feel sticky at high %. **Water-soluble ensulizole and Neo Heliopan AP enable lighter, clearer, gel/serum-type sunscreens** — a genuine sensory advantage. Polysilicone-15 (silicone backbone) gives a silky slip.

## 7. Safety

Filter-by-filter, because the family is not uniform:

- **Octinoxate** — generally well tolerated on skin; the concerns are (1) **photoallergy** (among commonest UV-filter photoallergens), (2) **systemic absorption** above the FDA trigger, (3) historical **estrogenic/thyroid signals** that are real in high-dose rodent and in-vitro work (Schlumpf 2001; thyroid-disruption studies) but **weak-to-absent at human-relevant exposures** (ToxCast found it inactive after curation), and (4) the dominant issue — **environmental/coral toxicity** (see below). The net: octinoxate is the **most problematic member** chiefly for **ESG + photoinstability**, less for proven human harm.
- **Homosalate** — low skin irritation/allergenicity; the live issue is **endocrine**: the EU SCCS judged the prior 10% use unsafe and **restricted homosalate to ≤7.34% in face products only** out of an abundance of caution over equivocal endocrine data and a thin margin of safety (SCCS/1638/21). Highest plasma absorption of the group (Matta). ToxCast: estrogen/androgen/thyroid hits but only ~150–500× above plasma (low concern). https://health.ec.europa.eu/publications/scientific-advice-safety-homosalate-cas-no-118-56-9-ec-no-204-260-8-uv-filter-cosmetic-products_en
- **Octisalate** — among the **gentlest** filters; CIR concluded safe as used; weak estrogen signal only at ~700–2,900× plasma (very low concern). Mild stinging on broken skin (salicylate). Generally a low-worry ingredient.
- **Octocrylene** — two real concerns: (1) **photoallergic contact dermatitis** (notable, incl. ketoprofen cross-reactivity and pediatric cases) — the family's leading allergen alongside octinoxate; and (2) **degradation to benzophenone** over shelf life (Downs 2021), a mutagen/carcinogen/endocrine disruptor. The EU SCCS, weighing this, still concluded **octocrylene safe up to 10%** (SCCS/1627/21), and Downs 2021 drew formal rebuttals (§10) — so the benzophenone issue is **contested, not settled**. ToxCast: weak androgen hit at ~580–3,100× plasma. https://health.ec.europa.eu/document/download/2928185b-f8b7-474e-937a-052a90a90970_en
- **Ensulizole** — water-soluble, minimal penetration, generally well tolerated; the theoretical flag is **photochemical generation of ROS / potential to promote oxidative DNA damage in vitro** (it is a known model photosensitizer in some assays), so it is best paired with antioxidants and not used as a sole filter. Low allergenicity.
- **Amiloxate** — limited safety dataset (newer, EU/AUS); structurally a cinnamate, so plausibly shares octinoxate-type photolability and (untested) endocrine questions; some advocacy sources flag "suspected endocrine disruptor" on a precautionary, not evidentiary, basis.
- **Iscotrizinol, Polysilicone-15, Neo Heliopan AP** — the **best safety profiles** of the nine: large/photostable/poorly absorbed, low irritation and sensitization, no meaningful endocrine signal at use levels. Their only "flaw" is **US illegality**.
- **Pregnancy/lactation:** Given measurable systemic absorption of the oil-soluble US members (octinoxate especially, with its endocrine baggage), **mineral (zinc/titanium) filters remain the conservative pregnancy-messaging choice**; if organics are used, the **low-absorption members (polysilicone-15, Neo Heliopan AP) or octisalate** are the more reassuring picks. Attach "consult your doctor" boilerplate (not medical advice — see CLAUDE.md).
- **Sourcing / sustainability (the decisive ESG flag — concentrated in octinoxate):** **Octinoxate is one of the two filters banned by Hawai‘i (Act 104, with oxybenzone; effective 2021) and by Key West, Palau, Bonaire, Aruba, and other jurisdictions** for coral-reef harm. It is associated with **coral bleaching by promoting viral infections** in field/aquarium work (Danovaro et al. 2008) and is implicated in larval DNA damage and endocrine disruption in marine organisms (Downs et al.). Octocrylene is also on watchlists (its benzophenone byproduct is environmentally persistent and toxic to oyster larvae). **This makes a "reef-conscious / reef-safe" positioning incompatible with octinoxate (and questionable with octocrylene)** — a hard strategic constraint, not a nicety. Danovaro: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18414624/ · Act 104: https://data.capitol.hawaii.gov/sessions/sessionlaws/Years/SLH2018/SLH2018_Act104.pdf · reef review: https://enveurope.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s12302-021-00515-w

## 8. Regulatory & claims (US FDA framing per CLAUDE.md)

- **These are OTC DRUG actives in the US, not cosmetic ingredients.** Any product using them for SPF is a **drug**, subject to the sunscreen monograph: finished-product **SPF + broad-spectrum testing**, **Drug Facts** labeling, GMP, the works — the costly path flagged in [`sunscreen-photoprotection.md`](sunscreen-photoprotection.md).
- **US GRASE status (critical):** In the FDA's **2019 Proposed Rule** and **2021 Proposed Order (OTC000008)**, octinoxate, homosalate, octisalate, octocrylene and ensulizole — like nearly all organic filters — are **NOT currently classified GRASE** (Category III-equivalent: "need more data," chiefly the absorption findings). **This is not a finding of unsafety**; they remain **legally marketable** while data are developed. Only **zinc oxide and titanium dioxide** are proposed GRASE. FDA: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2021/09/27/2021-20780/amending-over-the-counter-monograph-m020-sunscreen-drug-products-for-over-the-counter-human-use-over
- **Amiloxate, iscotrizinol, polysilicone-15, Neo Heliopan AP are NOT in the US monograph at all** — they cannot be used in a US OTC sunscreen, full stop, until/unless FDA approves new filters (none added since the 1990s; a bemotrizinol proposed order was only published in late 2025).
- **EU/UK/Australia/Asia:** all nine are **approved cosmetic UV filters** (Annex VI), with two notable EU restrictions: **homosalate ≤7.34% (face products only)** and **octinoxate ≤10%** but under environmental scrutiny. Octocrylene confirmed safe ≤10% by SCCS.
- **What can be claimed (with monograph compliance & finished-product testing):** the SPF value, **"broad spectrum"** (only if the product genuinely is — these UVB filters need a UVA partner to qualify), "helps prevent sunburn," and the monograph skin-cancer/early-aging language **only if** Broad Spectrum SPF 15+.
- ✅ **Safer language:** "broad-spectrum protection," "helps protect against UVB / sunburn," "daily SPF helps protect the appearance of skin over time" (with substantiation).
- ⚠️ **Risky / non-compliant:** any SPF claim **without** finished-product testing & monograph compliance; "sunblock," "all-day," "waterproof," "100% protection"; **"reef-safe" on any octinoxate (or arguably octocrylene) product** (fails it); calling any of these an "anti-aging active/treatment" (they're filters); implying UVA/broad-spectrum from a UVB-only filter set. **Substantiation reality:** the Nambour RCT was on *another* product — our SPF and any "prevents aging" framing need **our** finished-product testing.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier: low (commodity).** Octinoxate, homosalate, octisalate and octocrylene are mature, high-volume commodity filters (BASF/DSM-Firmenich/Symrise + many generics), among the **cheapest UV filters available** and freely second-sourceable — a big reason they dominate legacy/mass sunscreens. Ensulizole is also inexpensive.
- **Mid-tier:** the EU-only performance filters — **iscotrizinol, polysilicone-15, Neo Heliopan AP, amiloxate** — cost more than the commodities but less than the premium broad-spectrum triazines (bemotrizinol/Tinosorb).
- **The real cost is the *system + regulation*, not the molecule:** for any sunscreen, **OTC-drug regulatory + SPF/broad-spectrum/photostability testing** dwarfs raw-material cost, and a US formula is constrained to the commodity (more compromised) filters.
- **Supply considerations:** stable global supply for the commodities; the binding constraints are non-price — **octinoxate's reef bans/ESG overhang**, **homosalate's EU face-only cap**, **octocrylene's benzophenone reputational question**, and **US illegality of the four best members**.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **"Chemical sunscreen = anti-aging active":** **Overreach.** None of these treats anything; they filter UV (mostly UVB). The anti-aging benefit is real but **preventive and indirect**, riding on the *finished broad-spectrum sunscreen*, not the molecule.
- **"Octinoxate is a dangerous estrogen disruptor" (EWG-adjacent scare):** **Overstated.** The estrogenic data are high-dose rodent/in-vitro (Schlumpf ED50 ~935 mg/kg/day); ToxCast found octinoxate effectively inactive after curation; human biomonitoring signals are sparse. The *defensible* knock on octinoxate is **reef toxicity + photoinstability**, not human endocrine harm.
- **"Octinoxate + avobenzone = great cheap broad-spectrum":** **False / a formulation trap.** The two undergo mutual photolysis and generate radicals (Sayre 2005). The historically common pairing is chemically poor; the Nambour sunscreen worked despite, not because of, this combination.
- **"Octocrylene turns into a carcinogen in the bottle":** **Contested, not settled.** Downs 2021 is real (benzophenone rises on aging), but it drew formal published rebuttals over methodology and real-world relevance, and the EU SCCS still cleared octocrylene at 10%. Treat as a genuine *uncertainty* and a reason to prefer fresh stock / alternatives, not a proven hazard.
- **"Salicylates give strong sun protection":** **No.** Homosalate/octisalate are weak filters (a few SPF units); their job is **solubilizing avobenzone and boosting/feel**, not headline SPF.
- **"Reef-safe" + octinoxate (or octocrylene):** **Contradiction.** Octinoxate is explicitly reef-banned; do not pair either with reef-safe marketing.
- **"Newer EU filters are just marketing":** **Wrong.** Iscotrizinol, polysilicone-15 and Neo Heliopan AP are genuinely more photostable and less absorbed than the US commodities — the problem is purely that they're **US-illegal**.
- **"Not GRASE = unsafe":** **Misleading.** Not-GRASE = **needs more data**, not "proven harmful." Be honest in both directions.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **CONDITIONAL — these are formulation building-blocks for a possible SPF SKU, never marquee "actives," and they split sharply on desirability.** Treat the family in three buckets:

- **Useful, defensible commodities:** **octisalate** (gentle avobenzone solubilizer), **octocrylene** (the practical US avobenzone photostabilizer — with eyes open on photoallergy and the contested benzophenone question), and **ensulizole** (water-soluble UVB for lighter textures). These are reasonable supporting components of a US broad-spectrum sunscreen.
- **Genuinely better but US-illegal:** **iscotrizinol, polysilicone-15, Neo Heliopan AP** (and **amiloxate**) — more photostable, less absorbed, more elegant. If the brand's first sunscreen is **EU/UK/Asia-market**, prefer these (plus Tinosorb — see [`next-gen-uv-filters.md`](next-gen-uv-filters.md)) over the US commodities.
- **Avoid / retire:** **octinoxate** — the photoinstability (esp. with avobenzone), the reef bans, and the dated endocrine cloud make it a strategic liability for a premium, science-forward, plausibly "clean/reef-conscious" brand. There is no formulation reason to choose it when better UVB options exist. **Homosalate** is a secondary caution given the EU face-only cap and high systemic absorption — usable, but not a selling point.

If the brand wants a **clean / mineral / pregnancy-first / reef-conscious** sunscreen story, **lead with coated mineral filters (zinc/titanium) and skip this family (especially octinoxate)**. If it wants an **elegant chemical/hybrid SPF**, use **octisalate + octocrylene + avobenzone (US)** or the **photostable EU filters (outside the US)** — never octinoxate-with-avobenzone.

**Longevity-relevance note:** like avobenzone, this family maps onto the **single most evidence-based anti-aging lever there is — daily broad-spectrum photoprotection** — and via octinoxate's *named* role in the Nambour RCT, the skin-longevity throughline is unusually well grounded. But these are **shields, not repair actives** (and mostly *partial*, UVB-only shields). They belong in the brand's **photoprotection/prevention pillar** (and routine/education), not among the "treatment" heroes (retinoids, vitamin C, peptides). Treat as **infrastructure for a possible SPF SKU**, decided alongside the broader sunscreen go/no-go in [`sunscreen-photoprotection.md`](sunscreen-photoprotection.md).

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Independent adversarial re-check of every headline efficacy and safety claim against the primary literature (PubMed/journal/regulatory sources fetched directly). The question asked of each claim: is the evidence **human + topical**, or quietly borrowed from in-vitro / animal / oral data? Are the cited studies real and do they say what the dossier says? Are key studies industry-funded, small, or contested?

### Overall evidence grade: **B–** (for these filters' *actual* claim — UVB/SPF protection that helps prevent sunburn and photoaging, as components inside a tested broad-spectrum sunscreen)

Rationale:
- The dossier does **not** overclaim. It repeatedly frames the family as **filters/shields, not treatment actives**, stresses that most are **UVB-only (partial) shields**, and states there is **no single-ingredient RCT** (nor should there be). That honesty clears C.
- It falls short of avobenzone's **B** because (a) the strongest human evidence (Nambour) is **category-level and used octinoxate *with* avobenzone**, so it cannot isolate any one filter — and the very pairing it validates is the one the mechanism section flags as photochemically poor; and (b) several members carry **unresolved safety/ESG questions** (octinoxate reef bans, octocrylene→benzophenone, homosalate EU cap) that avobenzone does not. The minus reflects that mixed, partly-contested safety picture, not a writing flaw.

### What checked out (real, accurately characterized)
- **US monograph caps** (octinoxate 7.5%, homosalate 15%, octisalate 5%, octocrylene 10%, ensulizole 4%) — verified against FDA M020. The earlier search hit claiming octocrylene "15%" was **wrong** and was corrected to 10%.
- **US illegality of amiloxate, iscotrizinol, polysilicone-15, Neo Heliopan AP** — confirmed; these are EU/AUS/Asia Annex VI filters absent from the US monograph.
- **Nambour RCT** (Hughes 2013, PMID 23732711) — real; trial sunscreen combined octinoxate + avobenzone; ~24% less aging with daily use. Limitation (category- not molecule-level) stated.
- **Sayre 2005** octinoxate+avobenzone photolysis/radicals (PMID 15560736) — real and correctly characterized.
- **Downs 2021** benzophenone accumulation (Chem Res Toxicol; +38.7–199.4%) — real; the dossier correctly flags it as **contested** (formal rebuttals exist) rather than settled.
- **Schlumpf 2001** (PMID 11333184) — real; octinoxate uterotrophic ED50 ≈ 935 mg/kg/day (very high) and homosalate MCF-7 estrogenicity accurate; correctly contextualized as high-dose, over-cited.
- **ToxCast/Tox21** (Suh 2023, PMC10613966) — verified: octinoxate effectively inactive after curation; homosalate/octisalate/octocrylene endocrine hits only ~150–3,100× above plasma → low concern. Correctly used to *temper* the endocrine scare.
- **SCCS homosalate ≤7.34% face-only** (SCCS/1638/21) and **octocrylene ≤10%** (SCCS/1627/21) — verified against EC sources.
- **Hawai‘i Act 104** (octinoxate + oxybenzone, effective 2021) and **Danovaro 2008** coral bleaching (correct PMID 18414624 / PMC2291018) — verified; correctly presented as ESG/sourcing, not efficacy.
- **Matta 2019/2020** plasma absorption above 0.5 ng/mL trigger for the oil-soluble US members — verified; correctly framed as safety/regulatory, not harm and not efficacy.

### Main caveats
1. **Flagship human evidence is category- and pairing-level, not ingredient-level.** Nambour validates *broad-spectrum sunscreen* (octinoxate + avobenzone), never any filter alone. Internal validity for "octinoxate specifically" is low; for "sunscreens like this" high.
2. **Octocrylene→benzophenone is genuinely disputed.** Downs 2021 is real but methodologically challenged (published comments) and the EU SCCS still cleared octocrylene at 10%. The dossier treats it as uncertainty, which is the defensible reading.
3. **In-vitro "stress-signal" papers (SH-SY5Y, mitochondrial) are non-skin models at sub-cytotoxic doses** — hypothesis-generating only; flagged as such, not smuggled in as topical harm.
4. **Some λmax / SPF-contribution / photostability figures come from supplier/INCIDecoder/SPFList technical profiles, not peer-reviewed primaries** — directionally robust (well corroborated across sources) but exact numbers should be treated as illustrative.
5. **Octinoxate endocrine framing is deliberately two-sided** — real high-dose/in-vitro signals vs. weak human relevance (ToxCast). The dossier resists both the EWG scare and an industry all-clear; the binding knock on octinoxate is reef toxicity + photoinstability, which is well supported.

### Net
An honest, well-tiered family dossier with no fabricated citations and no oral/in-vitro data passed off as human-topical proof. The one factual error found in source material (octocrylene "15%") was caught and corrected to the true US cap of 10%. Grade **B–** reflects the genuine ceiling for partial (UVB-only) UV filters judged on human-topical evidence, plus the mixed/contested safety-and-ESG profile of the US-legal members — not a weakness in the write-up.
