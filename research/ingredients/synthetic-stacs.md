# Ingredient: Synthetic STACs — SRT1720, SRT2104, SRT3025 (INCI: none established — see §1)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⬜ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ☑ rejected (topical) / ⬜ in a product
**Slug:** synthetic-stacs · **Class:** NAD+ / sirtuin / AMPK / cellular-energy actives (specifically: **synthetic Sirtuin-Activating Compounds (STACs)** — designed small-molecule allosteric activators of **SIRT1**)

> **One-line honest summary:** SRT1720, SRT2104 and SRT3025 are the famous *synthetic* sirtuin-activating compounds — the "1000× more potent than resveratrol" SIRT1 activators discovered by **Sirtris Pharmaceuticals** and developed by **GlaxoSmithKline** as anti-aging / metabolic *drugs*. They sit on a legitimately central skin-longevity node (the NAD+ / SIRT1 axis governs collagen turnover, DNA repair, senescence, mitochondrial energy and inflammaging). But they are **investigational pharmaceuticals, not cosmetic ingredients**: they have **no cosmetic INCI and no cosmetic-grade supply**; the program is **largely dead** (Sirtris was shut down by GSK in 2013; **SRT3025 was halted for QT prolongation**, **SRT2379 for preclinical toxicity**, and **SRT2104 produced neutral results in ~5 of 8 clinical trials** and is "no longer in development"); their very mechanism was the subject of a **major "are these even real SIRT1 activators?" controversy** (Pacholec 2010 fluorophore-artifact finding vs Hubbard/Sinclair 2013 rebuttal); and there is **zero human topical skin evidence** — the skin story is in-vitro fibroblast/keratinocyte work plus borrowed sirtuin biology. For a credible skincare brand this is a **skip as a topical active**. Like AICAR, its value to us is as the *mechanistic north star* (SIRT1/NAD+) that **safe, cosmetic-legal actives** (niacinamide/NAD-axis, ferments, peptides, resveratrol) gesture toward — not as something to put in a jar.

---

## 0. The honest punchline (read this first)

- **The target (SIRT1 / the NAD+–sirtuin axis) is real and matters in skin.** SIRT1 declines with age, suppresses MMP-1/-3/-9 (collagen-degrading enzymes), deacetylates FOXO3a and p53 to blunt UV oxidative damage and apoptosis, drives autophagy and SOD2 antioxidant defense, and damps NF-κB inflammaging. That biology is well reviewed and not in dispute. [13][14]
- **These specific molecules are *drugs*, and the drug program failed.** The STAC story is one of the most expensive disappointments in longevity pharma: GSK bought Sirtris for **~$720M in 2008** [20], shelved the resveratrol lead (SRT501) by 2010 after renal toxicity/deaths in a myeloma trial [20], and **shut Sirtris down in 2013** [21]. Of the synthetic STACs: **SRT3025** was discontinued after **QT-interval prolongation** in its single human PK study [10]; **SRT2379** was terminated for **preclinical toxicities** [10]; **SRT2104** is **no longer in development** after ~5/8 clinical trials returned neutral/insignificant results [10]. SRT1720 never even entered humans.
- **Even the mechanism was contested.** Pacholec et al. (2010) showed the apparent "SIRT1 activation" by SRT1720/SRT2183/SRT1460 (and resveratrol) was an **artifact of a fluorophore-tagged peptide substrate**, and that these compounds are **promiscuous** (hit many unrelated targets) [8]. Sinclair's group (Hubbard 2013) later argued for a **genuine allosteric mechanism** requiring **SIRT1 Glu230** and specific hydrophobic substrate motifs [9]. The field partly reconciled — but a hero cosmetic claim built on a mechanism that needed a *Science* paper to rescue it is not a strong foundation.
- **There IS real skin-cell and rodent data — and zero human topical data.** SRT1720 reduces UVA-induced ROS/death and senescence in human dermal fibroblasts and improved markers in aged-mouse skin (in-vitro + systemic mouse) [4][16]; SRT1720 improved diabetic wound angiogenesis/healing in mice [17]; SRT2104 (oral) improved psoriasis *histology* in a 40-patient RCT — but the **histologic improvement did not consistently track the clinical PASI score** [5][6]. **No peer-reviewed human topical/cosmetic study of any SRT-STAC exists.**
- **Net for the brand:** **Skip as a topical active.** No INCI, no cosmetic supply, dead/abandoned drug program, real safety signals (QT, preclinical tox, mortality-at-high-dose dispute), contested mechanism, and zero human topical evidence. Keep it as the **SIRT1/NAD+ mechanism reference** and deliver the longevity narrative through cosmetic-legal actives instead — anchored on **niacinamide** (a proven NAD+ precursor; see `nad-niacinamide-longevity.md`).

Do **not** assume they are cosmetic-legal anywhere. [11][12]

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it's *meant* to work at the skin/cell level)

These are **designed allosteric activators of SIRT1**, the NAD⁺-dependent class-III deacetylase that is the headline "longevity enzyme."

1. **SIRT1 allosteric activation (the intended core mechanism).** STACs are proposed to bind an N-terminal SIRT1 allosteric site and **lower the Km for the acetylated substrate**, increasing deacetylase rate — *as if* mimicking calorie restriction. In the original assay SRT1720 had **EC1.5 ≈ 2.9 µM, ~4.5-fold max activation**, vs resveratrol's EC1.5 ≈ 46 µM. [1][7][9]
2. **The contested step (carry this everywhere).** Pacholec 2010 [8] showed the apparent activation depended on a **fluorophore (TAMRA/AMC)-tagged peptide substrate** and vanished with native peptide/full-length substrates; the compounds also hit **dozens of unrelated targets** (receptors, kinases, transporters) → "promiscuous, not direct activators." Hubbard/Sinclair 2013 [9] rebutted: with substrates bearing specific **hydrophobic +1 motifs** (as in real SIRT1 substrates like **PGC-1α, FOXO3a**), activation is real and requires **SIRT1 Glu230**; mutating Glu230 abolished STAC metabolic effects in cells. **So: the mechanism is *probably* real for the right substrates, but it took years of dispute to establish — a fragile basis for marketing.**
3. **Downstream SIRT1 effects relevant to skin (if activation is achieved):**
   - **↓ MMP-1/-3/-9** → less collagen/ECM breakdown (SIRT1 represses collagenases). [13][14]
   - **FOXO3a deacetylation → ↑ SOD2/catalase** → antioxidant defense; **p53 deacetylation** → less UVB-driven apoptosis/senescence. [13][14]
   - **Autophagy / mitophagy induction** (LC3-II, Beclin-1) → clearance of damaged organelles; reverses senescence markers in fibroblasts. [4][16]
   - **NF-κB (p65) deacetylation → ↓ inflammaging** (TNF-α, IL-6, SASP cytokines). [4][16]
   - **PGC-1α deacetylation → mitochondrial biogenesis / bioenergetics** (the "exercise-mimetic" axis; also why SRT2104 shows exercise-mimetic effects). [3][9]
4. **NAD⁺ dependence (the catch).** Sirtuins *consume* NAD⁺. A STAC can only do anything if **cellular NAD⁺ is adequate**, and NAD⁺ itself declines with age — which is exactly why the cosmetic-legal version of this story usually centres on **NAD⁺ precursors (niacinamide/NR/NMN)** rather than enzyme activators. [13]

**Skin-relevant translation (honest):** SIRT1 is a *legitimately* central skin-aging hub, and these are the canonical pharmacological tools to push it. **The problem is not the target — it's the molecules.** "SIRT1 is good for skin" does not make "put a discontinued, QT-prolonging, promiscuous research drug with no INCI on your face" a sound or legal product decision. The same hub is reachable by safer, cosmetic-legal actives.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** the deep, real evidence for SRT-STACs is as **research SIRT1 probes** and as **systemic (oral) investigational drugs** in metabolism, inflammation, cardiology and oncology. Their **best human data are oral pharma trials that mostly returned neutral results, plus two programs halted for safety.** For **human topical skin**, the literature is **effectively empty**. A "topical SRT1720/SRT2104 serum" today would rest on **borrowed SIRT1 biology + in-vitro fibroblast/keratinocyte papers + rodent skin** — and would be selling a *discontinued investigational drug with a contested mechanism and active safety signals*. Be brutal about this.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell models) — *this is where almost all skin-relevant STAC data live*

- **Milne J.C., Westphal C.H., Sinclair D.A., et al. (2007), *Nature* 450:712–716 (DOI 10.1038/nature06261).** The foundational discovery paper: HTS-derived SIRT1 activators **structurally unrelated to and far more potent than resveratrol** (SRT1720 ~1000× more potent in the assay); improved insulin sensitivity / mitochondrial function in diabetic/obese rodents. *Limitation: the SIRT1-activation assay used the fluorophore-tagged substrate later challenged by Pacholec; metabolic endpoints are not skin.* [1]
- **Pacholec M., et al. (2010), *J Biol Chem* 285(11):8340–8351 (DOI 10.1074/jbc.M109.088682).** The bombshell: **SRT1720, SRT2183, SRT1460 and resveratrol are NOT direct SIRT1 activators** — apparent activation was an **artifact of the fluorophore-conjugated peptide substrate** (confirmed by NMR/SPR/ITC showing the compounds bind the tagged peptide); the compounds are **highly promiscuous** (multiple off-targets); **SRT1720 did not lower glucose or improve mitochondrial capacity in high-fat-diet mice**, and **100 mg/kg SRT1720 increased mortality** in their hands. *Limitation: itself disputed (substrate-choice issue); but it permanently reframed the field's confidence.* [8]
- **Hubbard B.P., Gomes A.P., Dai H., … Sinclair D.A. (2013), *Science* 339(6124):1216–1219 (DOI 10.1126/science.1231097).** The rebuttal/reconciliation: a **common allosteric mechanism** real for SIRT1 substrates with hydrophobic +1 residues (PGC-1α, FOXO3a), requiring **SIRT1 Glu230**; STAC metabolic effects were **lost in cells expressing activation-defective SIRT1**. *Limitation: establishes plausibility, not topical skin efficacy; the need for this paper is itself a caution.* [9]
- **SRT1720 in UVA-irradiated human dermal fibroblasts.** SRT1720 (**1 µM, 1 h**) **decreased cell death and ROS** in UVA-irradiated cells and **reversed UVA suppression of PGC-1α, NRF2 and TFAM** — i.e. mitochondrial/antioxidant rescue via SIRT1. *Limitation: in-vitro fibroblasts; tool-compound; no topical/finished-product arm.* [4][16]
- **Sung J.Y., Kim S.G., Kim J.-R., Choi H.C. (2021), *Exp Gerontol* 150:111394 (DOI 10.1016/j.exger.2021.111394; PubMed 33965557).** In **senescent human dermal fibroblasts**, SRT1720 **inhibited inflammatory-cytokine induction and senescence** by **deacetylating acetyl-NF-κB**, raising SIRT1 and **autophagy proteins (LC3-II, Beclin-1)**; effects also seen in **aged mice** given SRT1720 (↓acetyl-NF-κB, ↓SASP, ↓senescence markers). *Limitation: in-vitro HDF + systemic aged-mouse; not topical; route/dose not detailed in abstract.* [16]
- **Potthast A.B., et al. — sirtuins in COX-deficient human skin fibroblasts.** SRT1720 (with paeonol) **modulated SIRT1/3/4 levels and altered respiratory-chain function** in human skin fibroblasts from mitochondrial-disease patients. *Limitation: disease-cell model; mechanism demonstration, not anti-aging efficacy.* [22]
- **NED416 / Aquatide (context — *other* SIRT1 activators with skin data).** NED416 (a different SIRT1 activator, "more potent than resveratrol") promoted SIRT1 in fibroblasts/keratinocytes, accelerated wound closure and collagen formation in vitro and in vivo (Rac1/Cdc42); Aquatide reduced UVB senescence via autophagy. *Cited only to show the **pathway** is reachable by cosmetic-adjacent actives — not SRT-STACs.* [14]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo) — *systemic/oral, essentially never topical skin*

- **Minor R.K., et al. (2011), *Scientific Reports* 1:70 (DOI 10.1038/srep00070).** **SRT1720 improved survival and healthspan of obese mice** (high-fat diet), with multiple metabolic-tissue benefits. *Limitation: oral, metabolic; obese model; not skin. Also part of the contested SRT1720 efficacy/toxicity debate.* [15]
- **Mercken E.M., Mitchell S.J., … de Cabo R. (2014), *Aging Cell* 13(5):787–796 (DOI 10.1111/acel.12220).** **SRT2104 extended mean lifespan ~9.7% (max/10th-percentile ~4.9%) in male mice on a *standard* diet**, preserving bone/muscle mass, motor coordination, insulin sensitivity, ↑mitochondrial content, ↓inflammation. *Limitation: oral; male mice; modest effect; whole-organism, not skin — but the cleanest "STAC extends lifespan" datapoint.* [3]
- **Miranda M.X., … Matter C.M. (2015), *Eur Heart J* 36(1):51–59 (DOI 10.1093/eurheartj/ehu095; "Sirt1 activator SRT3025 provides atheroprotection in Apoe⁻/⁻ mice").** Oral **SRT3025 ↓LDL/total cholesterol and atherosclerosis** via ↓hepatic PCSK9 / ↑LDLR. *Limitation: oral; cardiovascular; the same compound later failed human safety (QT). Not skin.* [18]
- **SRT1720 diabetic-wound healing (mice).** SIRT1 activation (incl. SRT1720) **improved angiogenesis and wound healing** in STZ-diabetic mice by protecting endothelial cells from oxidative stress. *Limitation: rodent diabetic wound model; systemic/local injection, not a cosmetic topical; closest "skin" in-vivo signal.* [17]
- **Bleomycin lung-injury caution.** SRT1720 **aggravated** bleomycin-induced lung injury in mice in one model — a reminder SIRT1 activation is **context-dependent, not uniformly protective**. [19]

### Tier 3 — Human (systemic/oral) — *the real human data are oral pharma trials; mostly neutral, two programs halted for safety*

- **Libri V., … (2012), *PLoS One* 7(12):e51395 (DOI 10.1371/journal.pone.0051395; PubMed 23284689).** **First-in-human SRT2104 Phase I:** randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, **elderly volunteers**, oral **0.5 or 2.0 g/day ×28 days**. **Safe/tolerable**; biological effects "consistent with SIRT1 activation"; but PK **highly variable**. *Limitation: small, safety/PK-focused, no clinical efficacy endpoint; not skin.* [2]
- **Hoffmann E., et al. (2013), *Br J Clin Pharmacol* — SRT2104 PK.** Oral **bioavailability ≈ 14%**, improved with food; **highly variable exposure** (AUC %CV 51–89% in later trials). *Decisive practical point: even as an oral drug, delivery was a problem — and these molecules are large, lipophilic, drug-like.* [10]
- **SRT2104 Type-2-diabetes trials.** Phase I (10 subjects, 2 g/day ×7 d) and **Phase II (86 subjects, 2 g/day ×28 d): "no consistent effects on insulin sensitivity."** [10]
- **Krueger J.G., Suárez-Fariñas M., et al. (2015), *PLoS One* 10(11):e0142081 (DOI 10.1371/journal.pone.0142081; PubMed 26556603).** **The most skin-relevant human trial:** Phase II RCT of **oral** SRT2104 in **moderate-to-severe psoriasis**, **40 patients (4:1 drug:placebo), 250–1000 mg/day ×84 days**. **~35% achieved "good-to-excellent" histological improvement (p<0.0001)** with modulation of **IL-17/TNF-α** signalling and keratinocyte-differentiation genes — **BUT histologic improvement "was not consistently in agreement with PASI"** (the clinical severity score), exposure was highly variable, and AEs occurred in 69% (including placebo). *Limitation: ORAL not topical; disease (psoriasis) not cosmetic aging; histology-vs-clinical discordance; small. This is the closest STAC-in-human-skin datapoint and it is **equivocal**.* [5][6]
- **Ulcerative colitis Phase II (17 subjects): "SRT2104 did not improve UC."** [10]
- **Overall human verdict (Bonkowski/Sinclair-adjacent reviews):** across SRT2104's program, **~5 of 8 clinical trials had neutral or statistically insignificant results**, and the compound is **"no longer in development."** [10]
- **SRT3025 — single human PK study HALTED for QT prolongation** (NCT01340911): a corrected-QT-interval prolongation signal raised proarrhythmia risk and **terminated** development. [10]
- **SRT2379 — terminated for preclinical toxicities** (NCT01416376). [10]
- **SRT1720 — never entered human trials** (tool compound). [10][15]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters for a skincare brand)

- **None identified.** After multiple targeted searches, **no peer-reviewed human topical/clinical skin study of SRT1720, SRT2104, SRT3025 (or any SRT-STAC) was found** — no RCT, no lower-tier clinical, no published cosmetic finished-product efficacy study, no transdermal/penetration study on human skin. None has a cosmetic INCI and none appears in mainstream cosmetic actives portfolios. Topical "SIRT1 / longevity skincare" discussion centres on **resveratrol, ferments, peptides (Aquatide, NED416), and NAD+ precursors** — *not* SRT-STACs. [11][12][14]
- **Implication:** any topical SRT-STAC claim would be **100% borrowed** from (a) systemic/animal SIRT1 pharmacology, (b) in-vitro fibroblast/keratinocyte papers [4][16], and (c) an **oral, equivocal** psoriasis trial [5]. There is **no human topical efficacy or safety dataset** to stand on.

**Bottom line by tier:** Mechanistically central and the canonical synthetic SIRT1 activators (in-vitro/animal/human-oral). Genuine in-vitro human-skin-cell findings (SRT1720: ↓UVA-ROS/senescence, ↑autophagy, NF-κB deacetylation) and rodent skin (wound healing). **Best human evidence overall = oral pharma trials that mostly failed, plus two safety halts (QT; preclinical tox).** **Human topical skin evidence = zero.** And the mechanism itself survived only after a published controversy.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Against (significant):** these are **large, lipophilic, drug-like heterocycles (MW ~470–610)** with poor aqueous solubility — well above the rough "≤500 Da, balanced logP" heuristics for efficient stratum-corneum passage, and engineered as **oral** drugs, not topicals.
- **Oral delivery was *already* a problem.** Human **bioavailability ≈ 14%** with **highly variable exposure** even when swallowed and absorbed across gut [10] — a poor omen for passive transdermal delivery to viable epidermis/dermis at meaningful, reproducible doses.
- **No human (or ex-vivo human skin) penetration/PK data** exist for any SRT-STAC quantifying delivery to viable skin or intracellular SIRT1 engagement after topical application.
- **NAD⁺ dependence adds a second hurdle.** Even if delivered, a SIRT1 *activator* only works where **NAD⁺ is sufficient** — and skin NAD⁺ falls with age/UV. The cosmetic-legal way to address this is **precursor** strategies (niacinamide), not enzyme activators.
- **Practical read:** topical penetration of these STACs is **likely poor and entirely unproven**; meaningful skin activity would require an unproven delivery system — not a problem worth solving for discontinued drugs with no INCI and live safety signals (§7).

## 6. Formulation

> Provided for completeness only — the verdict is **skip** (§11). There is **no validated cosmetic formulation pathway** and no cosmetic-grade raw material.

- **Effective concentration:** **No established topical efficacy concentration.** In-vitro SIRT1-activating/skin doses are typically **~1 µM (SRT1720 in HDFs)** up to low-µM; cell-free EC50 ~0.16 µM for SRT1720. These say nothing about a topical dose-response. [4][12][16]
- **pH / solubility:** poorly water-soluble, drug-like; DMSO-dependent in the lab. No published cosmetic stability or solubilization work.
- **Stability:** uncharacterised in cosmetic systems (no published data).
- **Pairings:** not applicable as a recommended active. *If* exploring the **SIRT1/NAD+ theme** with **legal actives** (not SRT-STACs), the coherent stack is **NAD⁺ precursors (niacinamide)** + **antioxidant network (resveratrol/ferulic/vitamin C/E)** + **barrier lipids** — see this folder's `nad-niacinamide-longevity.md`, `polyphenols-resveratrol.md`, `resveratrol-analogs.md`, `sirtuin-ferments-peptides.md`.
- **Sensory:** unknown in cosmetic context.

## 7. Safety

This is where SRT-STACs fail hardest for a leave-on consumer cosmetic.

- **Documented human safety signals (the headline concern).** **SRT3025 prolonged the QT interval** in its only human PK study → **proarrhythmia risk → development halted** [10]. **SRT2379 was terminated for preclinical toxicities** [10]. The sibling lead **SRT501 (resveratrol formulation) was halted after 5 patients died of renal failure** in a myeloma trial (causation contested — GSK/Sirtris argued the underlying myeloma plus dehydration from GI side effects, not direct SRT501 nephrotoxicity, were likely drivers; the renal-toxicity signal was real enough to suspend the trial) [20]. This is a chemical *family* with a real toxicology track record — the opposite of a reassuring leave-on profile.
- **Disputed SRT1720 toxicity.** Pacholec reported **100 mg/kg SRT1720 was not tolerated and increased mortality in mice** [8]; defenders counter that higher doses were tolerated for longer in other studies [15] — i.e. the safety picture is **unresolved**, which is itself disqualifying for a cosmetic.
- **Context-dependent / off-target activity.** Pacholec showed these compounds are **promiscuous** (many unrelated targets) [8]; SRT1720 **aggravated** bleomycin lung injury in one model [19]. SIRT1 activation is **not uniformly protective** — a poor prior for uncontrolled chronic topical exposure.
- **Tolerability when it *did* dose:** oral SRT2104 was broadly tolerated in elderly volunteers and psoriasis patients (AEs mostly mild/moderate; 69% AE rate incl. placebo) [2][5] — but **oral tolerability in monitored trials does not transfer to chronic leave-on topical use**, and tells us nothing about skin irritation/sensitisation.
- **Irritation / sensitisation / photo-safety:** **No human topical patch-test/HRIPT/photo-safety data found.** Unknown.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** no topical data; given the investigational-drug, QT/proarrhythmia and preclinical-tox profile of the family, default to **avoid**.
- **Sourcing:** research-grade biochemical only (Selleck/Sigma/Cayman/Abcam/MCE/SCB/MedKoo). **No cosmetic-grade supply chain, no cosmetic COA/spec.**

**Safety read:** even setting aside legality, the combination of **(a real family toxicology record: QT, preclinical tox, renal deaths in the sibling lead) + (disputed SRT1720 mortality) + (promiscuous off-targets) + (no human skin safety data) + (no cosmetic-grade supply)** makes SRT-STACs unsuitable for a consumer cosmetic.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic ingredient status:** **No established cosmetic INCI; not recognised cosmetic ingredients.** They are **investigational drugs / research chemicals**. Putting a clinically-developed, pharmacologically active SIRT1-activator drug in a cosmetic would very likely render the product an **unapproved drug** (US FDA structure/function framing) and/or an **unauthorised ingredient** (EU/UK and most markets restrict pharmacologically active/medicinal substances in cosmetics). **Do not assume cosmetic-legal anywhere.** [11][12]
- **The drug/cosmetic line (decisive):** the *entire* value proposition — "activates your skin's longevity enzyme SIRT1 / mimics calorie restriction / extends cellular lifespan / reverses senescence" — is **structure/function (drug) language**. There is **no honest cosmetic claim available** because there is **no human topical evidence** to substantiate even a modest appearance claim, and the molecules are drug-like with a clinical/safety history.
- **"Clinically studied" trap:** technically true (SRT2104 reached Phase II) — but the trials were **oral**, for **disease**, and **mostly neutral/halted**. Using "clinically studied" in cosmetic copy would be misleading. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** *None recommended for SRT-STACs.* If the brand wants the **sirtuin / cellular-energy / longevity narrative**, attach it to **cosmetic-legal SIRT1/NAD+-adjacent actives** with their own substantiation — **niacinamide** ("supports skin's cellular energy," well-evidenced), ferments/peptides (narrative/secondary), resveratrol (antioxidant). See glossary and adjacent dossiers.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **"1000× more potent than resveratrol → a super anti-aging active."** The "1000×" figure is from the **2007 in-vitro assay later shown to be substrate/fluorophore-artifact-prone** [1][8]; potency in a contested assay ≠ topical skin benefit.
- **"SIRT1 is the longevity gene, so a topical SIRT1 activator is anti-aging."** SIRT1 *is* a legitimate longevity hub, but (a) STAC activation is documented **systemically/in-vitro**, not in human skin; (b) it depends on **NAD⁺** (which declines with age); and (c) SIRT1 activation is **context-dependent**, not uniformly protective [19]. Mechanism ≠ safe topical outcome.
- **"Clinically proven / clinically studied molecule."** SRT2104 reached Phase II — but trials were **oral**, for **disease**, and **~5/8 were neutral**, with the diabetes insulin-sensitivity endpoint negative and the psoriasis histology **discordant from clinical PASI** [5][10]. "Clinically studied" here is a **caution**, not an endorsement.
- **"Backed by a $720M Big-Pharma program."** That program **shelved its resveratrol lead after deaths, halted SRT3025 for QT, terminated SRT2379 for tox, found SRT2104 mostly neutral, and was shut down in 2013** [10][20][21]. The pedigree is a **cautionary tale**, not a credential.
- **"The mechanism is established."** It required a **2013 *Science* paper to rescue it from a 2010 *JBC* artifact finding** [8][9]; even the reconciled mechanism is substrate-specific and was demonstrated in cells/biochemistry, not human skin.
- **The real skin findings are in-vitro/rodent.** SRT1720's HDF/UVA/senescence and mouse wound-healing data are genuine and interesting [4][16][17] — but they are **mechanism support, not finished-product proof**, and SRT1720 never even reached humans.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

❌ **SKIP as a topical active — strong *target story* (SIRT1/NAD+), unusable *molecules*.**
**One-line:** SRT1720/SRT2104/SRT3025 are the canonical synthetic sirtuin-activating compounds sitting directly on the SIRT1/NAD+ longevity hub — but they are **discontinued/abandoned investigational drugs with no cosmetic INCI, no cosmetic-grade supply, poor/unproven skin penetration, a contested activation mechanism, documented family safety signals (QT prolongation, preclinical toxicity, renal deaths in the sibling lead), and zero human topical evidence.** For a credibility-first skincare brand they are a **clear skip for use in product**; their value is purely **educational/directional** — they mark the *target* (SIRT1 / sirtuins / NAD+ / cellular energy) we should pursue through **safe, cosmetic-legal actives** instead.

**Why not "conditional":** unlike Ca-AKG, spermidine, or the sirtuin ferments/peptides (benign-but-unproven and at least formulatable), SRT-STACs carry **active disqualifiers**: (1) **no cosmetic INCI / likely not cosmetic-legal**; (2) **no cosmetic supply chain**; (3) **real, documented safety signals** across the chemical family (QT, preclinical tox, mortality dispute); (4) **a publicly contested mechanism**; (5) **a dead drug program**; (6) **zero human topical data**. Any one bench-worthy; together, a firm skip. (This mirrors the AICAR verdict — see [`aicar-acadesine.md`](aicar-acadesine.md).)

**Longevity-relevance note:** for a skin-*longevity* brand, the SRT-STACs are genuinely useful as a **map, not a destination**. They demonstrate *why* the **SIRT1 / NAD+ / cellular-energy axis** belongs in our worldview (ECM protection, autophagy, senescence/SASP control, mitochondrial bioenergetics, inflammaging) — and they let us tell that story credibly *if* we deliver it through **cosmetic-appropriate actives**: anchor on **niacinamide** (proven, and an NAD⁺ precursor; `nad-niacinamide-longevity.md`), with **resveratrol/analogs** (`polyphenols-resveratrol.md`, `resveratrol-analogs.md`), **sirtuin ferments/peptides** (`sirtuin-ferments-peptides.md`), and **spermidine/autophagy** (`spermidine.md`) as narrative/secondary players — plus the **#1 evidence-based sirtuin-friendly behaviour: UV protection** (UV depletes skin SIRT1). Watch the broader **topical-geroprotector** field for the legitimate version of this idea — the SRT-STACs themselves are not it.

Log in [`../../ideas/not-considering.md`](../../ideas/not-considering.md) (topical use) — reason: no cosmetic INCI/legal status, dead/abandoned drug program, documented family safety signals (QT/preclinical tox/renal deaths), contested mechanism, no human topical evidence, no cosmetic-grade supply.
