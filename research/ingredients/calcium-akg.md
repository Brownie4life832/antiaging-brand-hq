# Ingredient: Calcium Alpha-Ketoglutarate (Ca-AKG) (INCI: see §1 — not a settled cosmetic INCI)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Autophagy & mitochondrial-targeted actives (TCA-cycle metabolite; geroprotector / epigenetic & collagen cofactor)

> **One-line honest summary:** Alpha-ketoglutarate (AKG) is one of the most genuinely interesting "longevity metabolites" — it is a *direct enzymatic cofactor* for the collagen-building hydroxylases AND for the TET/Jumonji demethylases that drive the epigenetic clock, which is a real mechanistic story for a skin-*longevity* brand. But its **proven** strength is **oral/animal** (worm + mouse lifespan, one uncontrolled human epigenetic-age study). For **human TOPICAL skin** the evidence is thin: essentially **one small uncontrolled cosmetic study** plus a 2007 in-vitro + hairless-mouse paper. The form sold to longevity consumers is the **calcium salt for swallowing**; whether that same salt does anything meaningful applied to skin is largely unproven. Treat it as a **science-forward supporting/narrative active**, not an evidence-backed topical hero.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Alpha-ketoglutarate (AKG); 2-oxoglutarate; oxoglutaric acid. The supplement/longevity form is the **calcium salt, "Ca-AKG" / "CaAKG"** (chosen because the free acid is hygroscopic and unstable; the Ca salt is a shelf-stable powder).
- **INCI status (important nuance):** There is **no single clean, universally-recognised cosmetic INCI for "calcium alpha-ketoglutarate."** What appears on cosmetic labels is variable and should be verified per supplier/market:
  - Free acid is listed in cosmetic databases as **Oxoglutaric Acid** (a.k.a. 2-oxoglutaric acid / α-ketoglutaric acid).
  - Salt forms appear as **Calcium Ketoglutarate**, **Sodium Oxoglutarate / Sodium Ketoglutarate**, etc.
  - Some products deliver it via a **fermented carrier** (e.g., rice-ferment filtrate; see Yang 2022 [4]) and label the carrier, not "AKG."
  - **Action item before formulating: confirm the exact INCI the chosen supplier's grade carries and that it is cosmetic-permitted in target markets.** Do not assume "Calcium Alpha-Ketoglutarate" is itself a recognised INCI. [unverified — no single authoritative cosmetic INCI monograph confirmed]
- **CAS:** AKG free acid **328-50-7**; calcium alpha-ketoglutarate commonly cited as **71686-01-6** (also seen as the monohydrate). Verify on the supplier COA.
- **Molecular formula / weight:** Free acid C₅H₆O₅, MW ~146.1 g/mol (a *very* small molecule — relevant to penetration prospects). Calcium salt commonly C₅H₄CaO₅ (·xH₂O), MW ~184 g/mol anhydrous.
- **Chemical class:** A 5-carbon **dicarboxylic keto-acid** and a central **tricarboxylic-acid (Krebs) cycle intermediate** sitting at the crossroads of carbon and nitrogen metabolism (it is the carbon skeleton that accepts/donates the amino group in glutamate ⇄ AKG transamination).
- **Physical/chemistry notes:** Free acid is white, water-soluble, **acidic and hygroscopic/unstable**; the **calcium salt is the practical, stable, near-neutral-handling form**. Sodium salt is more water-soluble.
- **Key forms/derivatives:**
  - **Calcium AKG (Ca-AKG)** — the dominant *oral longevity* form (e.g., Rejuvant®, and most "Ca-AKG" supplements). Built for swallowing, not skin.
  - **Free α-ketoglutaric acid** — the form used in the 2007 topical/in-vitro skin study [1] and the Yang cream [4].
  - **Arginine-AKG (AAKG)** — a sports/NO supplement form (different evidence base; do not conflate).
  - **Hydrophobic esters (e.g., "octyl-AKG" / hydrophobically-modified AKG, HEαKG)** — cosmetic derivatives engineered for skin penetration; cytoplasmic esterases cleave them back to AKG inside the cell. In-vitro + small in-vivo cosmetic data only [5]. This is the most skin-rational derivative.

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it works at the skin/cell level)

AKG is best understood as a **metabolic + epigenetic cofactor** and a **caloric-restriction-mimetic / geroprotector**. Unlike many actives, several of its mechanisms are *directly* skin-relevant because the enzymes involved live in fibroblasts and keratinocytes.

Mechanistic pillars:

1. **Obligate cofactor for collagen hydroxylases (the strongest skin mechanism).** AKG is the co-substrate for **prolyl-4-hydroxylase** and lysyl hydroxylase — the Fe²⁺/AKG-dependent dioxygenases that hydroxylate proline/lysine on procollagen chains. Hydroxyproline is *required* for stable collagen triple-helix formation. More AKG availability can therefore support procollagen maturation. AKG also raised **prolidase** activity (a rate-limiting enzyme that recycles proline for collagen synthesis) in human fibroblasts; prolidase inhibition blocked the effect. [1]
2. **Cofactor for TET DNA demethylases and Jumonji-C (JmjC) histone demethylases — the "epigenetic clock" mechanism.** The same Fe²⁺/AKG-dependent dioxygenase family includes **TET** enzymes (active DNA demethylation, 5mC→5hmC) and **JmjC histone demethylases**. AKG availability gates these reactions; this is the mechanistic basis for the claim that AKG can favourably shift epigenetic age. [6]
3. **Anti-inflammaging via IL-10 / cytokine modulation.** In aged mice, dietary AKG decreased systemic pro-inflammatory cytokines and induced **IL-10** from T cells. [2][3]
4. **Mitochondrial / nutrient-sensing effects (the original longevity mechanism).** In *C. elegans*, AKG **inhibits ATP synthase (complex V)** and **downregulates TOR**, mimicking dietary restriction and extending lifespan. [7]
5. **Antioxidant / nitrogen scavenging.** As a keto-acid, AKG can non-enzymatically scavenge reactive oxygen/nitrogen species and ammonia.
6. **Endogenous decline with age.** Circulating/tissue AKG falls with age; the geroprotection hypothesis is that restoring it re-enables these dioxygenase-dependent programs.

**Skin-relevant translation (honest):** Pillars 1 and 2 are *mechanistically excellent* for a collagen/epigenetic-aging story AND the enzymes genuinely operate in skin cells — this is a real advantage over hand-wavy actives. BUT "the cofactor exists in skin" ≠ "a topical dose raises intracellular AKG enough to push these reactions in living human skin." That last step is the unproven link.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** AKG's deep, prestigious evidence is for **lifespan/healthspan via ORAL/dietary AKG in worms and mice**, plus **one uncontrolled human ORAL epigenetic-age study**. For **human TOPICAL skin**, the entire literature is roughly: **one 2007 paper (human fibroblasts in vitro + hairless-mouse topical)** and **one small uncontrolled 2022 cosmetic cream study**, plus supplier/derivative data. A "Ca-AKG serum" today is sold largely on **borrowed oral/animal longevity prestige**, not on topical human proof. Be brutal about this in copy.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell models)

- **Son et al., 2007, *Biological & Pharmaceutical Bulletin* 30(8):1395–1399 (DOI 10.1248/bpb.30.1395).** α-Ketoglutarate **10 µM** on cultured **human dermal fibroblasts** → **+25.6 ± 4.6% procollagen** vs control. Mechanism: ↑**prolidase** activity; the prolidase inhibitor Cbz-Pro **blocked** the procollagen increase, confirming the pathway. *Limitation: a single cell type, supraphysiologic-defined culture dose, procollagen (not mature deposited collagen), no human topical arm in this part.* [1]
- **Hydrophobic-AKG ester (octyl-AKG / HEαKG) — cosmetic supplier in-vitro data (Cosmetics & Toiletries; corresponds to Barbachan/Sederma-type active).** A hydrophobically-modified AKG (esterase-cleaved to AKG intracellularly) **increased collagen production in senescent / older-donor fibroblasts in vitro (reported up to ~200% in old-donor cells)**. *Limitation: trade-press / supplier data, derivative not plain Ca-AKG, primary peer-reviewed methodology not fully retrieved — treat the 200% figure as supplier-reported.* [5] [partially unverified]
- **Epigenetic-cofactor biology (mechanistic, not skin-specific).** Multiple peer-reviewed sources establish AKG as the obligate co-substrate of **TET DNA demethylases** and **JmjC histone demethylases**, and of the **Fe²⁺/AKG collagen hydroxylases**. This is settled enzymology, but demonstrated in generic cell systems, not as a topical-skin outcome. [6]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)

- **Son et al., 2007 — hairless-mouse topical arm.** **1% α-ketoglutarate** in 70% propylene glycol / 30% ethanol, applied topically to **UVB-irradiated hairless mice for 12 weeks**, **reduced wrinkle formation** vs vehicle, attributed to increased collagen via the prolidase/hydroxylase pathway. *Limitation: hairless mouse (not human), small, vehicle is a penetration-enhancing solvent system, wrinkle scoring; the only "topical skin in vivo" data and it's rodent.* [1]
- **Asadi Shahmirzadi et al., 2020, *Cell Metabolism* 32(3):447–456 (DOI 10.1016/j.cmet.2020.08.004).** Landmark mouse study. **Ca-AKG added to the diet of C57BL/6 mice starting at ~18 months (540 days).** Results: **median lifespan and survival increased ~16.6% / 19.7% in the first female cohort; ~12% average lifespan extension across cohorts; >40% reduction in frailty (healthspan) measures** — i.e., **compression of morbidity**. Mechanism linked to **↓systemic inflammatory cytokines and ↑IL-10**. Effect was **sexually dimorphic (stronger in females).** *Limitation for us: ORAL/dietary, mouse, late-life dosing; says nothing about skin or topical use; Ponce de Leon Health (commercialiser of Rejuvant) was involved — note commercial interest.* [2][3]
- **AKG / C. elegans foundational work — Chin et al., 2014, *Nature* 510:397–401 (DOI 10.1038/nature13264).** AKG **extended adult *C. elegans* lifespan ~50%** by binding and **inhibiting ATP synthase subunit β** and **downregulating TOR** (a dietary-restriction-mimetic mechanism). *Limitation: worm; systemic exposure; mechanistic prestige but not skin.* [7]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL (where most human data live — and it's about biological age, not skin)

- **Demidenko et al., 2021, *Aging (Albany NY)* 13:24485–24499 (DOI 10.18632/aging.203736) — the "Rejuvant 8-year" study.** **Retrospective, NON-placebo-controlled, observational** analysis of **n = 42** Rejuvant® users (14 F / 28 M, mean age ~63) taking **1000 mg Ca-AKG + vitamins** (males +Vit A, females +Vit D), 2 tablets/day, average **~7 months (4–10 mo)**. **DNA-methylation ("TruAge") biological age decreased on average ~8 years** (males −8.44 y, females −6.98 y; p ≈ 6.5×10⁻¹²); only 2 of 42 increased. *Limitations (the authors' own list): no placebo control (placebo/regression effects cannot be excluded), small n, single proprietary/undisclosed epigenetic clock, no clinical aging markers, no durability data, commercial conflict (B.K. Kennedy consults for Ponce de Leon Health; data are from their customers). The headline "8 years younger" is the single most over-cited AKG number and rests on weak design.* [8]
- **ABLE RCT (protocol: Mkrtchyan/Demidenko et al., 2023, *GeroScience*, DOI 10.1007/s11357-023-00813-6).** The proper test: **double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT, n = 120**, 40–60-year-olds with elevated epigenetic age, **1 g sustained-release Ca-AKG vs placebo, 6 months** + 3-month follow-up; primary outcome = change in DNAm age; secondary outcomes include arterial stiffness, strength, and **skin autofluorescence (an AGE proxy)**. **Status as of 2026-06: enrollment completed (recruitment-evaluation paper Aug 2025); primary results NOT yet published.** This is the study that will make-or-break the human oral claim — do not pre-empt it. [9]
- **Tolerability (oral context).** AKG is broadly well tolerated orally; reviews note mild GI effects (bloating, nausea) at higher doses, and AKG-based regimens up to multi-gram/day for extended periods reported no significant safety signal. AKG is generally regarded as safe in dietary amounts (commonly described as GRAS-status for dietary use — confirm formal status). [10][11]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters for a skincare brand — and it's very thin)

- **Yang et al., 2022, *Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology* 21(7):2887–2896 (DOI 10.1111/jocd.14635).** The one human topical cosmetic study. An **anti-aging cream containing α-ketoglutarate** (produced via **yeast fermentation of rice**) was tested on human volunteers. Reported instrument-measured results: wrinkles ↓**23.64% immediately** after application; after 7 days outer-corner wrinkles ↓15.23% and nasolabial ↓25.68%; after **56 days** crow's-feet ↓25.42%, skin firmness ↑41.40%, skin gloss ↑28.67%. Mechanistically tied to ↑**filaggrin, involucrin, serine-palmitoyltransferase** mRNA and keratinocyte proliferation (barrier/hydration). *Limitations (significant): appears to be a manufacturer/efficacy study with no clearly reported placebo or randomized control arm, small sample, finished-cream (not isolated AKG — cannot attribute effects to AKG alone vs. the rice ferment and base), "immediate 23.64% wrinkle reduction" is a hallmark of transient film/occlusion smoothing rather than biology, single study, not independently replicated.* [4]
- **Hydrophobic-AKG ester — cosmetic in-vivo (supplier-reported).** For the octyl/HEαKG derivative, a 28-day in-vivo cosmetic test reported a **~20% reduction in a viscoelastic coefficient (improved elasticity) vs unchanged placebo**, with reported wrinkle/elasticity improvement. *Limitation: supplier/trade-press data, derivative not Ca-AKG, methodology not peer-reviewed-retrieved.* [5] [partially unverified]

**Bottom line by tier:** Proven for AKG specifically → lifespan/healthspan (worm, mouse, ORAL), epigenetic-age signal in one uncontrolled human ORAL study. **Mechanistically excellent and skin-relevant** (collagen hydroxylase + TET/JmjC cofactor). **NOT robustly proven for topical Ca-AKG in human skin** — that rests on one 2007 in-vitro/rodent paper, one small uncontrolled 2022 cream study, and supplier derivative data.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **In favor:** Very small (~146 g/mol free acid) and water-soluble → on size alone, good penetration prospects relative to peptides/proteins/growth factors. The 2007 mouse study used a propylene-glycol/ethanol vehicle (penetration-enhancing) and saw an effect [1].
- **Against / unknown:** AKG is a **charged dicarboxylate** at skin pH (two carboxyls, pKa low) → hydrophilic, ionised molecules cross the lipophilic stratum corneum **poorly** without a vehicle or delivery system. The **calcium salt** (the longevity-supplement form) is even less skin-optimised — it's built for GI dissolution, and the **calcium counter-ion contributes nothing topically** and could interact with anionic thickeners.
- **The derivative tell:** The fact that cosmetic developers engineered **hydrophobic esters (octyl-AKG/HEαKG)** specifically to get AKG *into* cells [5] is itself evidence that **plain/charged AKG penetration is considered inadequate**. If we want real skin delivery, an ester or encapsulation is the rational route — not raw Ca-AKG.
- **No human transdermal data:** There are **no published human penetration/PK data** quantifying how much topical AKG reaches viable epidermis/dermis or raises intracellular AKG enough to drive the hydroxylase/demethylase reactions.
- **Practical read:** Penetration of *plain* AKG is plausible-but-unproven; the *calcium salt* is the least favorable topical form; **a hydrophobic derivative or encapsulation is the evidence-rational choice** if topical activity is the goal.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** **No established human topical efficacy concentration.** Reference points: 10 µM in fibroblast culture [1]; **1%** in the mouse topical study [1]; cosmetic suppliers cite broad ranges (~**0.5–5%**, with 0.5–2% "daily" and 3–5% "targeted") but these are usage suggestions, **not validated dose-response in humans** [12]. Formulate conservatively; do not imply a proven dose-response.
- **pH:** **Free α-ketoglutaric acid is acidic** — added neat it will drop formula pH and needs neutralisation/buffering toward skin-friendly **~5–6**. The **calcium/sodium salts are closer to neutral** and easier to handle. Match the acid/salt choice to the rest of the formula.
- **Stability:** The **free acid is hygroscopic and not very stable**; the **calcium salt is the shelf-stable powder** (this is *why* the longevity industry uses Ca-AKG). As a reactive keto-acid it can in principle participate in browning/condensation reactions with amines/reducing sugars over time — run stability + color/odor monitoring. Aqueous solutions of the keto-acid may degrade; consider anhydrous/encapsulated systems or the ester derivative for robustness.
- **Pairings (conceptual, science-forward):**
  - **Vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid):** mechanistically synergistic — ascorbate is the *other* required cofactor (the reductant that keeps Fe²⁺ active) for the **same** prolyl-hydroxylase reaction AKG feeds. "AKG + vitamin C = the two cofactors of collagen hydroxylation" is an honest, mechanism-true pairing story (still needs our own substantiation for a quantified claim).
  - **Niacinamide / NAD-axis actives:** metabolic-renewal narrative; barrier support.
  - **Spermidine / other geroprotectors:** coherent "longevity-metabolite" stack for a skin-longevity brand (both are oral-data-rich, topical-data-poor — pair the *story*, not over-claimed proof).
  - **Ceramides / barrier lipids:** supports the barrier-repair thesis and the filaggrin/involucrin/SPT mechanism [4].
- **Conflicts:** Calcium salt + strongly anionic systems (carbomer, some emulsifiers) can cause incompatibility/precipitation — calcium is a known carbomer-thickening killer. If using the acid, watch pH-sensitive partners. Vitamin C co-formulation needs pH and oxidation management.
- **Sensory:** At cosmetic levels, low color/odor expected from purified salt; ferment-carrier versions [4] may carry slight scent/color.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation/sensitization:** No specific topical irritation or sensitization signal at cosmetic levels; AKG is an endogenous metabolite. **Caveat:** the *free acid is acidic* — an unbuffered high-% acid formula could sting/irritate; neutralise or use the salt. **No published human topical patch-test/HRIPT data found — generate our own.**
- **Sun sensitivity:** No photosensitivity flag; the 2007 data are *protective* against UVB-induced wrinkling in mice [1]. No human topical photo-safety data either way.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** No topical pregnancy data. For oral use, data are limited; default to conservative copy ("if pregnant or nursing, consult your doctor"). Do not make reassuring safety claims beyond evidence.
- **Oral tolerability (supportive context only):** Generally well tolerated; mild GI effects at higher oral doses; long-term/multi-gram oral regimens reported without significant safety signals; commonly described as safe in dietary amounts. [10][11] This does **not** transfer automatically to a different (topical) route, but it argues the molecule itself is benign.
- **Counter-ion note:** The **calcium** load is trivial and irrelevant topically (a non-issue for skin safety; relevant only to formulation compatibility).
- **Sourcing/allergen:** Synthetic and fermentation-derived grades exist; ferment-carrier versions (rice ferment [4]) should disclose the source for allergen-aware customers.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic ingredient status:** Confirm the exact permitted **INCI** per supplier/market (likely **Oxoglutaric Acid** / **Calcium Ketoglutarate** / **Sodium Oxoglutarate**, or a ferment carrier) — see §1. Do **not** assume "Calcium Alpha-Ketoglutarate" is itself a recognised cosmetic INCI. Verify cosmetic-permitted status before formulating. [unverified]
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** The whole value of AKG is "longevity science" — which is exactly the trap. "Reverses biological age," "epigenetic age reversal," "extends cell/skin lifespan," "activates longevity pathways," "boosts collagen by X%," "anti-aging at the cellular level" all read as **structure/function (drug) claims** and/or unsubstantiated efficacy claims. The "**8 years younger**" figure [8] is from an **uncontrolled ORAL** study and must **never** be ported to a topical product.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "a cellular-energy metabolite naturally found in the body," "inspired by longevity / epigenetic-aging science," "a cofactor your skin's collagen-building enzymes use," "helps support the look of firmer, smoother skin," "supports the appearance of a healthy moisture barrier" (and even firmness/barrier wording should be hedged — only one small uncontrolled human topical study).
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation or legal review:** "reverses biological age," "reduces your epigenetic age," "8 years younger," "activates autophagy / TOR / longevity pathways in your skin," "increases collagen 200%/41%," "anti-aging." None are supported for **topical** human use.
- **Substantiation reality:** To make even modest topical performance claims we'd want **our own finished-formula human testing** — ideally with a *derivatised/encapsulated* AKG for real delivery. The existing literature will not substantiate a conventional Ca-AKG serum claim. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Forms/suppliers:** Widely available as **bulk Ca-AKG powder** riding the oral-longevity boom (e.g., Shenzhen Hygieia Biotech, Nutri Avenue, various Chinese manufacturers; fine-chemical free acid from standard suppliers). Cosmetic-grade clarity varies — most supply is **nutraceutical-grade**, so confirm a cosmetic-suitable spec. The **hydrophobic ester** and ferment-carrier versions come from specialty cosmetic-actives houses at higher cost.
- **Cost tier:** **Low–mid** for raw Ca-AKG (commoditised by the supplement market — a genuine cost advantage). **Mid–high** for delivery-engineered cosmetic derivatives (octyl-AKG/HEαKG, encapsulated, ferment actives), which is also where the *(thin)* topical data sit.
- **Supply/standardization risk:** Most material is sold for ingestion; require a **COA** confirming identity (CAS), purity, counter-ion, and cosmetic suitability. Beware conflating Ca-AKG with **arginine-AKG (AAKG)** — different ingredient, different (sports) evidence base.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **The route switch (the big one).** The marketing energy comes from **mouse lifespan + the "8-years-younger" human epigenetic study** — both **ORAL**. Selling a **topical** serum on that prestige is the central sleight of hand. Oral longevity ≠ topical skin benefit.
- **"8 years younger."** From an **n=42, uncontrolled, no-placebo, single-proprietary-clock, commercially-conflicted** study [8]. The authors themselves flagged every one of these limits. It is hypothesis-generating, not proof — and it's about swallowing 1 g/day, not applying it.
- **"Epigenetic age reversal in your skin."** AKG *is* a TET/JmjC cofactor [6] — true biochemistry — but there is **no human topical study** showing a serum shifts skin epigenetic age. Mechanism ≠ outcome.
- **The collagen percentages.** "+200% collagen" is **supplier in-vitro old-donor-cell** data for a *derivative* [5]; "+41% firmness" is from **one uncontrolled finished-cream study** [4]; the clean human-fibroblast figure is the modest **+25.6% procollagen at 10 µM** [1]. Don't headline the big numbers.
- **"Immediate 23.64% wrinkle reduction"** [4] is almost certainly **transient film/occlusion smoothing**, not collagen biology — a classic cosmetic-instrument artifact.
- **The form mismatch.** The **calcium salt** is engineered for the gut. The (scant) topical efficacy data used the **free acid** [1] or a **hydrophobic ester** [5] — not Ca-AKG. A "Ca-AKG serum" may be using the *least* topically-rational form precisely because it's the recognisable longevity buzzword.
- **The honest pending test.** The proper human oral RCT (**ABLE** [9]) hasn't reported. Even the *oral* human story is not yet confirmed by a controlled trial.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL — outstanding narrative + mechanism, weak topical-efficacy proof (today).**
**One-line:** Ca-AKG is a top-tier *skin-longevity story* ingredient — a real cofactor for both collagen-building hydroxylases and the epigenetic-clock demethylases, with genuine mouse-lifespan and human epigenetic-age pedigree — but that pedigree is **oral/animal**, and topical human proof is one small uncontrolled cream study plus a 2007 rodent paper. Use it as a **science-forward supporting active with strictly honest, mechanism-framed copy**, and if we want it to *perform* (not just narrate), formulate a **derivatised/encapsulated AKG + vitamin C** and run **our own human testing** — do not deploy raw Ca-AKG and import the "8-years-younger" oral claims.

**Why "conditional":** It earns a place *if* (a) we use a delivery-rational form (ester/encapsulated, or at least the acid in a penetrating vehicle — not just Ca salt for name recognition), and (b) we discipline the copy to mechanism + appearance language. As a raw Ca-AKG dusting sold on borrowed longevity hype, it's a **skip**.

**Longevity-relevance note:** For a skin-*longevity* brand, AKG is arguably *more* on-thesis than almost anything: it sits at the intersection of **mitochondrial metabolism, epigenetic aging, and collagen synthesis** — three legitimate hallmarks — and it pairs naturally with vitamin C (shared collagen-cofactor mechanism) and with spermidine/NAD actives as a coherent "longevity-metabolite" stack. The differentiation value (education, narrative, founder-credibility) is high **even where the topical efficacy data are not**, which is exactly the line the brand must walk honestly. The catalyst to watch is the **ABLE RCT readout** [9] — a positive controlled result would meaningfully upgrade the (oral) story.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified 2026-06-19** by independent re-check of every load-bearing citation against the primary literature (PubMed/journal/Nature/Cell/Aging-US originals). Verdict on each headline claim — is the evidence (a) HUMAN + TOPICAL or borrowed from in-vitro/animal/oral; (b) a real study that says what's claimed; (c) industry-funded/tiny?

### Overall grade: **C → C/D for the topical hero use that copy would imply**

- **Grade C (weak/indirect) is the honest ceiling for topical Ca-AKG.** The molecule's strong, prestigious evidence is real but **oral/animal** (worm, mouse, one uncontrolled human oral study). The *human topical* base is **one small uncontrolled finished-cream study [4] + one 2007 in-vitro/hairless-mouse paper [1] + supplier derivative data [5]** — which is exactly **D (unproven topically / hype-adjacent)** if used to sell a Ca-AKG serum on "longevity" prestige. The dossier already says this plainly; verification confirms it. It is **not** an A or B ingredient for topical efficacy.

### Main caveats (what could mislead a reader or copywriter)

1. **The route switch is the core risk.** Essentially all the impressive evidence is **oral/dietary** (mouse lifespan, worm lifespan, the "8-years-younger" human study). None of it tests **topical** skin. Any topical product borrows this prestige; the dossier names this correctly as "the central sleight of hand."
2. **The human topical tier is genuinely one weak study [4].** Uncontrolled, finished-cream (rice ferment + base, not isolated AKG), instrument-measured, single, unreplicated. The "immediate 23.64% wrinkle reduction" is almost certainly transient film/occlusion smoothing, not biology.
3. **Form mismatch.** Ca-AKG (the salable "longevity" buzzword) is the **gut-optimised salt**; the only topical efficacy signals used the **free acid [1]** or a **hydrophobic ester [5]** — i.e., a raw Ca-AKG serum may use the least topically-rational form.
4. **Two of the three strongest human/animal anchors carry a commercial conflict** (Ponce de Leon Health, commercialiser of Rejuvant, in both [2] and [8]). [8] additionally has no placebo and a proprietary/undisclosed epigenetic clock.
5. **The biggest derivative numbers ("+200% collagen") are supplier/trade-press [5]** and could not be independently confirmed in peer-reviewed literature.

### Claims to soften / never make (topical product)

- ❌ "8 years younger," "reverses/reduces biological (epigenetic) age," "epigenetic age reversal in your skin" — oral, uncontrolled, conflicted; **never port to topical**.
- ❌ "+200% / +41% collagen," "boosts collagen by X%" — supplier in-vitro derivative [5] and one uncontrolled cream [4]; the only clean human-cell figure is the modest **+25.6% procollagen in vitro [1]**.
- ❌ "activates autophagy / longevity / TOR pathways in your skin" — worm/mouse systemic mechanism, no human-skin demonstration.
- ⚠️ Even "firms / supports the moisture barrier" should stay **appearance-framed and hedged** — it rests on the single uncontrolled cream [4].
- ✅ Defensible framing: "a cellular-energy metabolite the body makes," "a cofactor your skin's collagen-building enzymes use," "inspired by longevity / epigenetic-aging science," appearance-of-firmness/smoothness language — all with our own finished-formula substantiation before any quantified claim.

### Verification conclusion

The dossier is **accurate and unusually well-calibrated** — every primary citation is real and faithfully summarised, including the disclosed conflicts and the "results-pending" status of the ABLE RCT. No fabricated studies or overstated primary findings were found. Its self-described tiering (mechanism = strong/skin-relevant; topical human efficacy = thin) is correct. The honest grade for **topical** Ca-AKG is **C** (weak/indirect), sliding to **D** if marketed as an evidence-backed topical hero on borrowed oral-longevity prestige. Suitable role: **science-forward supporting/narrative active**, ideally as a delivery-engineered (ester/encapsulated) form + vitamin C, with brand-run human testing before any performance claim.
