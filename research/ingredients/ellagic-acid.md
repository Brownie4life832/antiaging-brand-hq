# Ingredient: Ellagic Acid (INCI: Ellagic Acid)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Autophagy & mitochondrial-targeted actives (polyphenol; tyrosinase-inhibiting antioxidant; precursor of the mitophagy metabolite urolithin A)

> **One-line honest summary:** Ellagic acid is a genuinely multi-functional plant polyphenol with the *best human topical evidence of any "longevity-adjacent" active in our list for one specific job — evening pigmentation/dark spots* (it is an officially **approved whitening quasi-drug active in Japan since 1997**, and has multiple human topical trials). Its antioxidant, MMP-sparing, anti-glycation and mitochondrial/autophagy biology is real but the "longevity/mitochondrial" story is mostly **in-vitro, animal, or borrowed from its gut-metabolite urolithin A** (which the *skin* cannot make from a topical dose). The honest framing: **a proven topical brightener and antioxidant; a speculative topical "mitochondrial/autophagy" active.** Its Achilles heel is **terrible aqueous solubility (~9.7 µg/mL) and poor skin penetration** — efficacy is delivery-system-dependent.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Ellagic acid (EA)
- **INCI:** **Ellagic Acid** (cosmetic INCI function listed as *skin conditioning* / antioxidant). [16][17]
- **CAS:** **476-66-4** (anhydrous free acid). A dihydrate form also appears in commerce. [16]
- **EINECS/EC:** 207-508-3 [16]
- **Molecular formula / weight:** C₁₄H₆O₈ · **302.19 g/mol**. [1][12]
- **IUPAC / structure:** 2,3,7,8-tetrahydroxy-chromeno[5,4,3-cde]chromene-5,10-dione. It is the **dilactone of hexahydroxydiphenic acid** — effectively a **dimer of gallic acid** — a planar, four-fused-ring polyphenol bearing **four phenolic –OH groups and two lactone groups**. Amphiphilic: lipophilic biphenyl core + hydrophilic hydroxyls. [1][12]
- **Appearance:** light-yellow to brownish crystalline powder; very high melting point (~350 °C), thermostable. [1][12]
- **Where it comes from:** EA exists in plants mostly as **ellagitannins** (e.g., punicalagin, geraniin) that hydrolyze to release free EA. Richest dietary sources: **pomegranate** (700 mg/kg arils; up to ~38,700 mg/kg in mesocarp), raspberries (1,500–3,300 mg/kg), boysenberries, cloudberries, blackberries, strawberries, **walnuts (~590 mg/kg), pecans**, oak/wood, and Kakadu plum. [1][12]
- **Key forms/derivatives used in cosmetics:**
  - **Synthetic / purified ellagic acid** (highest purity grades, e.g., "Ellagic Acid 90/98%," "SpecWhite").
  - **Pomegranate (Punica granatum) extracts standardized to EA** (e.g., 40% EA) — the dominant "natural" route; content varies, verify on COA. [16]
  - **Delivery-enhanced forms** (the practically important category): EA in **liposomes, niosomes, microemulsions/nanoemulsions, cyclodextrin complexes, nanoparticles, nanostructured lipid carriers** — created specifically to overcome EA's solubility/penetration problem (see §5–6). [1]
  - **Urolithin A** — *not* ellagic acid, but its gut-bacterial metabolite; a separate INCI and a separate (also-thin) topical story. Relevant because the headline *mitochondrial/mitophagy* longevity biology belongs to **urolithin A, not EA**. [3][14]

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it works at the skin/cell level)

EA is a **broad-acting polyphenol** with several mechanistically distinct actions. For our taxonomy it is filed under **autophagy & mitochondrial-targeted actives**, which is *defensible but needs an asterisk* — the mitochondrial/mitophagy headline largely belongs to its metabolite urolithin A, and the autophagy data in skin are specifically in *melanocytes* (depigmentation), not a general "skin autophagy" effect.

Mechanistic pillars:

1. **Tyrosinase inhibition via copper chelation (best-evidenced skin action).** EA binds/chelates the **copper at the active site of tyrosinase**, the rate-limiting enzyme of melanogenesis, inhibiting it in a dose-dependent, non-competitive manner → less melanin. This is the basis of its approved whitening use. [4][16][17]
2. **Anti-melanogenic autophagy in melanocytes.** In B16F10 melanocytes, EA **induces autophagy** (↑LC3-II, p62/SQSTM1 changes, ↓ATG4B, PI3K/AKT/mTOR inhibition, Beclin-1/Bcl-2 shift) which degrades melanosomes/melanogenic machinery; blocking autophagy (3-MA, LC3 siRNA) **abolishes** the depigmenting effect. In keratinocytes it suppresses **UVA-activated α-MSH/CREB/MITF** signaling **via Nrf2 activation**. [4]
3. **Antioxidant / Nrf2 activation.** EA is a direct ROS scavenger and activates the **Keap1/Nrf2/ARE** pathway → ↑SOD, catalase, glutathione peroxidase, GSH. This is its general "environmental defense" mechanism. [1][3][14]
4. **MMP suppression → collagen sparing (photoaging).** EA blocks UV-induced up-regulation of **matrix metalloproteinases (MMP-1 etc.)** in fibroblasts/keratinocytes and reduces ICAM-1 and inflammatory cytokines, thereby limiting collagen degradation. [5][6]
5. **Anti-glycation.** EA inhibits formation of **advanced glycation end-products (AGEs)** — it traps reactive dicarbonyls (methylglyoxal), reduces CML/CEL adducts, and limits glycation-driven protein cross-linking/β-sheet formation. (Shown chiefly in chemical/diabetes models, not skin collagen in vivo.) [7][8]
6. **Mitochondrial protection (mostly non-skin) + the urolithin A bridge.** EA preserves mitochondrial membrane potential, reduces mitochondrial ROS, supports complex activity, and regulates SIRT1/SIRT3 in liver/heart/brain injury models. Its gut metabolite **urolithin A** is the one that drives **PINK1/Parkin mitophagy and PGC-1α mitochondrial biogenesis** — the canonical "mitochondrial longevity" actions. **Skin tissue is essentially absent from this literature, and topical skin cannot perform the gut-bacterial conversion of EA→urolithin A.** [3][14]

**Skin-relevant honest translation:** #1–2 (pigment) are demonstrated *in human skin*; #3–4 (antioxidant/MMP) are well-shown in cells/animals and plausible in human skin; #5 (glycation) is mechanistically attractive but not proven for human skin collagen; #6 (mitochondria/mitophagy) is the weakest link for *topical EA* and rests on a metabolite the skin won't make from a cream.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** EA's human evidence is **lopsided toward pigmentation**. For *brightening/dark spots*, EA has genuine human topical data (multiple trials + a 1997 Japanese quasi-drug approval) — better than most ingredients in our taxonomy. For *wrinkles/firmness/"mitochondrial longevity"*, the human evidence is **animal-topical, human-oral, or in-vitro** — and almost always EA was tested **inside a combination product or via a pomegranate extract**, so clean attribution to EA alone is rare.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (cell & enzyme models)

- **Tyrosinase / melanogenesis (foundational).** EA inhibits mushroom and cellular tyrosinase by **chelating active-site copper**, dose-dependently, non-competitively; reduces melanin in melanocyte cultures. [4][16]
- **Anti-melanogenic autophagy — Lin/Kim-type mechanism, 2021, *Biochemical Pharmacology* (S0006-2952(21)00050-2; PubMed 33545118).** In B16F10 melanocytes EA induced autophagy (↑LC3-II, p62/SQSTM1, ↓ATG4B, PI3K/AKT/mTOR inhibition, Beclin-1/Bcl-2 dysregulation, acidic-vesicle formation) and **autophagy inhibition (3-MA / LC3 siRNA) abolished the depigmenting effect**; in keratinocytes EA suppressed UVA-driven α-MSH/CREB/MITF via **Nrf2**. *Limitation: melanoma cell line; mechanism for pigment, not "anti-aging" broadly.* [4]
- **MMP / collagen in fibroblasts — Bae et al., 2010, *Experimental Dermatology* 19:e182–e190 (in-vitro arm).** EA **1–10 µmol/L** dose-dependently raised collagen and **completely inhibited UV-B-up-regulated MMP secretion** in human dermal fibroblasts; attenuated UV-B toxicity in HaCaT keratinocytes. [5][6]
- **Antioxidant / Nrf2.** EA scavenges ROS and activates Keap1/Nrf2/ARE (↑SOD, catalase, GPx, GSH) across many cell systems. [1][3][14]
- **Anti-glycation — Prevention of Protein Glycation by Natural Compounds (2015) and related.** EA inhibits AGE formation (↓CML/CEL on lysozyme), prevents glycation-induced β-sheet formation, traps methylglyoxal. *Chemical/protein models, not skin.* [7][8]
- **Mitochondrial protection (non-skin) — "Protective Effect of Ellagic Acid and Its Metabolites Against Organ Injuries: A Mitochondrial Perspective," 2025, *PMC11979624*.** EA preserves ΔΨm, ↓mito-ROS/MDA, supports complex II, regulates SIRT1/SIRT3; **urolithin A** adds **PINK1/Parkin mitophagy and PGC-1α biogenesis**. *Models: liver, kidney, heart, brain — **no skin**; the mitophagy actions are the metabolite's.* [3][14]
- **Antimicrobial (skin-adjacent).** EA MIC vs *Cutibacterium (Propionibacterium) acnes* ≈125 µg/mL; synergy with tetracycline cut biofilm 80–91%; antifungal vs *C. albicans*/*T. rubrum*. *In vitro.* [1]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)

- **Bae et al., 2010 (mouse arm), *Experimental Dermatology* 19:e182–e190.** **Topical EA (10 µM)** applied to **SKH-1 hairless mice** chronically UV-B-irradiated for ~8 weeks **reduced wrinkle formation**, dermal roughening/thickening, and cutaneous accumulation of inflammatory cytokines + ICAM-1. *Limitation: mouse, UV-B model; topical but not human; small molecule applied to thin mouse skin.* This is the most-cited "EA prevents wrinkles" study. [5][6]
- **Synergy study — EA + dihydromyricetin, *J. Funct. Foods* (S1011-1344(17)31099-0).** Co-treatment protected against UV-B photoaging, reportedly via TGF-β1 and Wnt signaling. *Animal/cell; combination.* [referenced via [5] context]
- **Wound-healing / disease models.** Various EA-loaded systems (Zn-EA MOF hydrogels for diabetic wounds; EA-Ag nanoparticles for infected wounds in rats; EA gels for contact dermatitis comparable to triamcinolone) show benefit. *Animal; combination/delivery-system; not aging skin.* [1]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL (substantial for pigmentation/UV; not topical)

- **Kasai et al., 2006, *J. Nutr. Sci. Vitaminol.* 52:383–388.** **Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled**, 3 arms (high 200 mg/d EA, low 100 mg/d EA, placebo), **n≈13/group**, women 20s–40s, **4 weeks oral** EA-rich pomegranate extract; after 1.5-MED UV to the arm, EA groups showed **less UV-induced pigmentation** (luminance, melanin, erythema). *Oral; pomegranate extract (EA + punicalagin), not isolated EA; small.* [17]
- **Pomegranate RCT — 2025, *PMC11733350*.** Randomized, placebo-controlled, **n=80 (40/group)**, **60 days oral**, 300 mg/d standardized pomegranate extract (EA + punicic acid + punicalagin): crow's-feet wrinkles −48%, tactile roughness −43.3%, radiance +42.8% (all P<0.001). *Oral; multi-constituent extract — cannot attribute to EA alone; single-primary-outcome powering.* [15]
- EA's broader oral bioavailability is **very poor** (water solubility ~9.7 µg/mL; plasma half-life <1 h; BCS Class IV), and much of the systemic benefit is actually mediated by **gut-derived urolithins** — a metabotype that varies between people. [1]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters — and here EA is unusually strong *for pigmentation*)

- **Japanese quasi-drug approval (regulatory-grade human evidence) — Lion Corporation, 1997.** EA is an **officially approved whitening (skin-lightening) quasi-drug active** by Japan's MHLW; in the approval/clinical record a **0.5% EA** formulation produced a **69.2% improvement rate on senile (sun-induced) pigment spots after 1–3 months.** EA sits in **Category A** (efficacy on human pigment spots described in scientific journals). *This is the strongest single human-topical data point for EA.* [9][10]
- **Dahl et al., 2013, *J. Drugs Dermatol.* 12(1):52–58.** **Double-blind, randomized, 12 weeks, n=54** multi-ethnic subjects: a product with **0.5% EA + 0.1% salicylic acid** vs **4% hydroquinone**, twice daily. EA product gave **comparable depigmentation to 4% hydroquinone** with better aesthetics/tolerance. *Combination (EA+SA), benchmark non-inferiority; EA not isolated; sponsor-linked.* [11]
- **Ertam et al., 2008, *J. Dermatol.* 35:570–574.** Randomized, prospective, open-label, **n=30** melasma patients in 3 arms (1% arbutin / **1% synthetic EA** / EA-containing plant extract). In the **synthetic-EA arm, 8 of 9** patients had significant melanin reduction by Mexameter (P=0.038). *Small, open-label (no blinding), per-arm n≈9–10.* [18]
- **JAAD clinical evaluations, 2014, *J. Am. Acad. Dermatol.* (S0190-9622(14)00662-8 and companion 00661-6).** Two 12-week studies of a cosmetic formula (**hydroxyphenoxy propionic acid + EA + yeast extract + salicylic acid**): a **skin-lightening-benefit study in n=114** multiethnic subjects (28% African-American, 16% Asian, 31% white, 25% Hispanic) with moderate hyperpigmentation, and a **separate comparison study (n=80, 40/group)** in which the formula reached **12-week parity with the prescription standard (4% hydroquinone + 0.025% tretinoin)** on evening tone/spot size (with the formula better tolerated). *Four-active combination — EA's individual contribution unisolable; sponsor study; abstract/poster-level (full text gated); the n=114 figure is the benefit study, the parity result is the n=80 study.* [19]
- **Microemulsion split-face — *Punica granatum* extract microemulsion, 2014/2015, *PMC4293390*.** **Split-face, n=11**, 12 weeks, O/W microemulsion (Tween 80/propylene glycol): active side **significantly ↓ melanin and erythema** vs placebo (significant at weeks 6–12, P<0.05). *Very small; pomegranate extract; the EA-attributed effect is inferred (copper chelation).* [20]
- **Heliocare 360 Pigment Solution pilot — 2025, *PMC12883900*.** Pilot observational (no control), **n=20** Caucasian women with melasma/photoaging hyperpigmentation, **SPF50+ fluid with iron oxide + Polypodium leucotomos + EA + niacinamide + vitamin E**, 12 wk + 12 wk follow-up: **MASI −44%** (6.9→3.9, P<0.0001), hyperreflective cells 47%→26% on LC-OCT. *Uncontrolled; photoprotection itself is therapeutic; 5-active formula — EA effect not isolable.* [13]

**Bottom line by tier:**
- **Pigmentation/even tone — PROVEN for human topical use** (regulatory approval + multiple clinical trials), though almost always **in combinations or as pomegranate extract**, rarely EA-alone.
- **Wrinkles/firmness — NOT proven for human topical EA** (best data is mouse-topical + human-*oral* pomegranate extracts).
- **"Mitochondrial/autophagy longevity" — NOT demonstrated for topical EA in human skin**; it leans on urolithin A biology the skin can't generate from a cream.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **The core problem:** EA is **almost insoluble in water (~9.7 µg/mL; ~33 µg/mL in pH 7.4 buffer)**, sparingly soluble in alcohol, and is a **BCS Class IV** molecule (**low solubility + low permeability**). Its planar, hydrogen-bonding, four-OH structure self-associates and resists both dissolution and stratum-corneum transit. [1]
- **Penetration is poor as free EA.** Reviews explicitly flag **"low aqueous solubility, instability, and poor skin permeability"** as the main limits on clinical efficacy. [1][16]
- **Delivery systems are not optional — they're the active ingredient story:**
  - **Cyclodextrins (β-CD, HP-β-CD):** 2.2–2.6× solubility boost; **nanosponges** raised solubility to 607–721 µg/mL (vs ~162 free). [1]
  - **Niosomes + penetration enhancers:** Span 60/Tween 60/cholesterol niosomes; **DMSO** drove epidermal deposition (30–90 µm), **NMP** enabled **dermal** penetration (up to ~120 µm). [1]
  - **Microemulsions/nanoemulsions:** O/W droplets ~24 nm gave antioxidant activity comparable to vitamin C and **superior to free EA**, and delivered measurable clinical depigmentation (PMC4293390). [1]
  - **Liposomes (incl. HA-modified, 2025, *Sci. Rep.* s41598-025-06948-0):** improved skin permeation and in-vitro/animal anti-aging activity vs free EA. *Cell/animal-level evidence.* [2]
  - **NLCs, zein/chitosan nanoparticles, supramolecular "sandwich" with gallic acid/catechin (180× dispersibility):** all aimed at the same solubility/penetration bottleneck. [1]
- **Practical read:** A bare "ellagic acid" line in a simple emulsion is likely **under-delivering**. Meaningful topical performance has been seen with **solubilized/encapsulated** EA or via **pomegranate-extract microemulsions** — so *delivery technology, not just %, governs efficacy.*

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration (human topical, pigmentation):** Real-world clinical/approved levels cluster at **~0.5% EA** (Japan quasi-drug; Dahl 2013) up to **1%** (Ertam 2008). Higher % is usually pointless without solubilization (it won't dissolve). Treat **0.5% solubilized EA** as the evidence-anchored target for brightening; do **not** imply a validated dose-response for wrinkles.
- **pH:** As a polyphenolic acid with phenolic OHs, EA is more soluble/ionized at higher pH (~33 µg/mL at pH 7.4) but polyphenols **oxidize and discolor faster as pH rises**. A mildly acidic finished pH (~4.5–5.5) favors color/stability; solubility then depends on the carrier (cyclodextrin/microemulsion), not pH alone.
- **Stability:** EA itself is **thermostable** (mp ~350 °C) but **oxidation-prone in solution** — it is "reactive for oxygen," which is why it can **darken/discolor a formula** over time. Protect with antioxidants, chelators, low headspace oxygen, opaque/airless packaging; run color and stability testing. Its solubility limit also creates **recrystallization/precipitation** risk on storage. [1][16]
- **Pairings (rational):**
  - **Niacinamide** (also approved-tier brightener; complementary anti-pigment mechanism; the two co-star in the Heliocare formula and many cosmetic blends). [13]
  - **Vitamin C / other Nrf2-antioxidants** — antioxidant-network rationale (EA can even help protect the formula from oxidation). [16]
  - **Salicylic acid / hydroxyphenoxy propionic acid** — the clinically tested brightening partners (exfoliation + tyrosinase). [11]
  - **Sunscreen** — pigmentation efficacy is photoprotection-dependent; the strongest pilot embedded EA in SPF50+. [13]
  - **Solubilizers (HP-β-CD, microemulsion surfactants, lecithin/liposomes)** — effectively mandatory.
- **Conflicts/cautions:** As a metal chelator/polyphenol, EA can interact with **metal ions and certain cationic systems**; watch for discoloration with iron/copper traces (use a chelator). Avoid betting on it in a clear/anhydrous-oil system where it simply won't dissolve.
- **Sensory:** Pure EA is a yellow-brown powder and **can impart a yellow/tan tint** and possible color drift — a real formulation/aesthetic consideration at higher loads or in white creams.

## 7. Safety

- **General dermal safety:** Patent/toxicology testing of EA-based actives reports **no significant acute toxicity, skin irritation, skin sensitization, or mutagenicity**, supporting "high safety" at cosmetic use levels; reviews call topical tolerability **excellent**, and it is **not a common contact allergen**. [1]
- **Sensitization:** **One** published case — occupational **allergic contact dermatitis** in a 33-year-old botanist after ~3 months handling an EA-rich plant (*Melianthus comosus*); patch-test positive to plant extract and purified EA; resolved with topical steroids. A single isolated case from intense botanical exposure, not pure-compound cosmetic use — low concern but worth noting. [1]
- **Pro-oxidant caveat:** Like most polyphenols/antioxidants, EA *could* turn pro-oxidant in the presence of redox-active metal ions (Fe/Cu) — a formulation-hygiene point (chelate, control metals), not a known in-use hazard. [16]
- **Sun:** No photosensitization signal; mechanistically EA is **photoprotective/antioxidant**. (It does not replace SPF.) [5][13]
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** No topical pregnancy-specific safety data; EA is a common **food** polyphenol (low systemic absorption topically). Default to conservative copy ("if pregnant/nursing, consult your doctor"). For **oral** EA, note CYP interaction potential (CYP1A2/2C9/2C19) and possible additive hypoglycemia with antidiabetics — relevant to supplement framing, not a topical cream. [1]
- **Sourcing/allergen:** Pomegranate- or nut-derived EA (walnut/pecan are rich sources) — for **synthetic/fermentation-or-pomegranate** grades disclose botanical origin for nut-allergy-aware customers and for "natural" claims.
- **No formal CIR (Cosmetic Ingredient Review) monograph for ellagic acid was located** — generate/commission our own finished-formula HRIPT and irritation data rather than citing a CIR safe-as-used conclusion. [unverified — CIR assessment not found]

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic status (US/EU):** INCI **Ellagic Acid** (CAS 476-66-4); cosmetic function **skin-conditioning / antioxidant**. Sold as a cosmetic raw material by multiple suppliers. No special EU restriction identified (confirm current Annexes per market). [16][17]
- **Japan (notable):** EA is a **government-approved whitening quasi-drug active** (Lion Corp., 1997) — in Japan a *quasi-drug* whitening claim is permissible at the approved level/condition. This **does not** transfer to US/EU, where "whitening/lightening" leans drug-like. [9][10]
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** Anti-pigment and anti-wrinkle claims carry weight. "Lightens/treats melasma/dark spots," "fades age spots," "boosts collagen," "anti-aging at the cellular level," "activates mitophagy/autophagy" all risk **structure/function (drug) claims**. The biggest trap here is **importing EA's oral/animal/urolithin longevity science into topical claims.**
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "helps **visibly even** skin tone and reduce the look of dark spots," "antioxidant defense against environmental/UV-induced dullness," "helps protect the look of skin from daily oxidative stress," "supports a brighter, more radiant complexion." (Brightening framing is its best-supported, lowest-risk lane.)
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation or legal review:** "whitens skin," "treats/cures melasma," "inhibits tyrosinase" (mechanism-as-drug-claim), "increases collagen by X%," "erases wrinkles," "activates mitophagy/autophagy in your skin," "longevity at the mitochondrial level." None are supported for **topical** human use of EA alone.
- **Substantiation reality:** For even modest topical brightening claims, lean on our **own finished-formula human testing** plus the EA brightening literature; do **not** market the mitochondrial/longevity angle as proven. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Forms/suppliers:** High-purity EA (90–98%) and **pomegranate extracts standardized to EA (~40%)** from cosmetic-ingredient suppliers (e.g., Sabinsa, plus various Indian/Chinese manufacturers and specialty houses); also food/nutra-grade riding the pomegranate/urolithin supplement boom. [16]
- **Cost tier:** **Low–mid** as a raw powder (it's an abundant plant polyphenol). **But the *effective* cost is higher** because usable topical EA generally requires a **solubilization/delivery system** (cyclodextrin, microemulsion, liposome) — that's where cost and formulation effort concentrate.
- **Standardization risk:** With pomegranate extracts, **EA content and the EA:punicalagin ratio vary** — require a COA quantifying EA. Synthetic/high-purity grades give cleaner dosing and color control.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **The format/metabolite switch (the big one).** Marketing borrows the **urolithin A / Mitopure mitophagy-longevity** story and the **oral pomegranate** UV/wrinkle data, then sells a **topical EA** serum as if those transfer. **Skin can't convert topical EA into urolithin A** (that needs gut bacteria), and the wrinkle data are mouse-topical or human-oral.
- **"Activates autophagy/mitophagy in your skin."** The only real EA-skin autophagy data are in **melanocytes for depigmentation** — not a general skin-renewal or mitochondrial effect.
- **"Boosts collagen / erases wrinkles."** Human topical EA has **no** wrinkle RCT; the MMP/collagen data are in vitro + hairless mouse. The human pomegranate wrinkle result (−48% crow's feet) was **oral** and a **multi-constituent extract**.
- **"Clinically proven brightener."** This part is **largely true** — but the clinical wins were **combinations** (EA+salicylic/hydroxyphenoxy/niacinamide) or **pomegranate extracts**, rarely EA alone, and Japan's approval is the firmest leg. Don't over-imply that *your* EA-alone serum will replicate 4%-hydroquinone-level results.
- **"X% ellagic acid."** A high % on the label is misleading if it isn't **solubilized** — undissolved EA largely sits on the surface. Delivery > concentration.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **CONDITIONAL → SUPPORTING (a strong *brightening/antioxidant* supporting actor; a *speculative* longevity actor).**
**One-line:** Ellagic acid is one of the **few "longevity-adjacent" actives in our taxonomy with real human topical evidence — but only for evening tone/dark spots** (Japan-approved whitening active; multiple clinical trials), so use it as a credible **brightening + antioxidant supporting ingredient delivered in a proper solubilization system**, and keep the mitochondrial/autophagy "longevity" framing as honest *narrative*, never a topical claim.

**Why conditional, not a flat yes:** (1) its proven human benefit (pigment) is a **brightening** story, which only matters if tone/dark-spots is in our routine's job-to-be-done; (2) the celebrated **mitochondrial/autophagy longevity** science is **urolithin A's**, non-skin, and non-transferable to a topical cream — so EA can't carry our core "skin-longevity/repair" thesis on evidence; (3) efficacy is **delivery-system-dependent** — a naive formulation will under-deliver; (4) **discoloration/solubility** are real formulation costs.

**Longevity-relevance note:** EA is a useful *bridge* ingredient for a skin-longevity brand because it connects to a buzzy, legitimately-researched longevity pathway (ellagitannins → urolithin A → mitophagy/PGC-1α). That makes it excellent **education/differentiation content** ("the pomegranate–urolithin longevity axis") — but the brand must be scrupulous that the *topical proof* is for **brightening and antioxidant defense**, and that the *mitochondrial* story is **systemic/oral biology**, not what a serum does. If we ever want a true topical mitophagy active, **urolithin A** (delivered directly) is the more defensible — though still thin — choice, not EA.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

_Verified 2026-06-19 by independent re-check of every load-bearing citation against PubMed / publisher / PMC sources. This dossier was already written self-critically; the audit largely **confirms** it. Grade reflects the split nature of the evidence._

### Overall grade: **B− (split verdict)**
- **Pigmentation / brightening: B (verging on B+ for "directionally proven").** Multiple **human topical** datapoints — a Japanese MHLW quasi-drug approval (1997, 0.5% EA, 69.2% improvement on senile spots), a double-blind RCT (Dahl 2013, n=54, non-inferior to 4% hydroquinone), an open-label RCT (Ertam 2008, n=30), plus combination-formula clinical work (JAAD 2014) and a split-face microemulsion (n=11). It is **not A**, because *every* human topical study used EA **in a combination** (with salicylic acid, arbutin, hydroxyphenoxy propionic acid, niacinamide, sunscreen) or as a **pomegranate extract** — there is **no modern, blinded, EA-alone-vs-vehicle topical RCT** with isolated EA. So "EA brightens" is well-supported as a *directional* claim but **not cleanly isolated to the molecule**.
- **Wrinkles / firmness / collagen: C.** Best evidence is **in-vitro** (human fibroblasts, Bae 2010) + **hairless-mouse topical** (Bae 2010) + **human ORAL** multi-constituent pomegranate extract (2025 RCT, −48% crow's feet). **No human topical wrinkle RCT for EA exists.** The dossier states this correctly.
- **Mitochondrial / autophagy "longevity": D for topical.** Real biology, but it lives in **liver/kidney/heart/brain** models and in the **gut metabolite urolithin A** (PINK1/Parkin mitophagy, PGC-1α) — which skin cannot generate from a topical dose. The only EA-skin autophagy data are in **melanocytes, for depigmentation**. The dossier already grades this "weak/speculative" and bars it from claims — correct.

### Funding / size caveats (fair-disclosure)
- **Industry / sponsor linkage is the norm here.** Dahl 2013, the JAAD 2014 pair, the microemulsion, the Heliocare pilot, and both pomegranate RCTs are **manufacturer- or supplier-sponsored cosmetic/nutraceutical studies**, not independent academic trials. None is fraudulent, but expect publication/result bias.
- **Small / weak designs.** Ertam (open-label, ~9/arm), microemulsion (n=11 split-face), Heliocare (n=20, **uncontrolled**), Kasai (n≈13/arm). The largest clean topical RCT is Dahl (n=54) — and it tested a **combination**, not EA alone.
- **The 1997 Japanese "69.2%" figure** is from an **open (uncontrolled) approval-era study**, regulatory-grade but not a modern placebo RCT.

### Claims to soften / never make (already largely handled in §8/§10; re-affirmed)
- ❌ "Activates mitophagy/autophagy in your skin," "mitochondrial longevity," "cellular-level anti-aging" — **D-tier for topical; do not use.** The urolithin-A biology does **not** transfer to a topical cream.
- ❌ "Boosts collagen / erases wrinkles" — **no human topical evidence;** mouse + oral only.
- ⚠️ "Clinically proven brightener" — **defensible only as a soft/visible claim and only for a finished formula we test;** EA-alone topical efficacy is **inferred, never isolated**. Do not imply an EA-alone serum matches 4%-hydroquinone results.
- ✅ Best-supported, lowest-risk lane stands: **"helps visibly even tone / reduce the look of dark spots" + "antioxidant environmental defense."**
