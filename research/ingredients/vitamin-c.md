# Ingredient: Vitamin C — L-Ascorbic Acid + derivatives (INCI: Ascorbic Acid; Tetrahexyldecyl Ascorbate; Sodium Ascorbyl Phosphate; 3-O-Ethyl Ascorbic Acid)

**Researched:** 2026-06-18 · **Status:** ✅ shortlisted (HERO candidate)

## What it is
Vitamin C is a potent antioxidant and an essential **cofactor for collagen synthesis**. Topically it helps neutralize free radicals from UV/pollution and supports brighter, more even-looking tone. **L-ascorbic acid (LAA)** is the pure, best-studied (but least stable) form. Derivatives — **tetrahexyldecyl ascorbate (THD), sodium ascorbyl phosphate (SAP), 3-O-ethyl ascorbic acid (EAC)** — trade some potency/evidence for better stability and gentleness.

## What it does (claimed → evidenced)
| Benefit | Evidence strength | Source(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Antioxidant / environmental (photo) defense | **Strong** mechanistically; supports photoprotection alongside SPF (not a replacement) | [1](https://acofp.org/news-and-publications/journal/article-detail/vol-16-no-3-fall-2024/vitamin-c-topical-retinoids-sunscreen-in-clinical-practice-essentials-for-family-physicians) |

## How it fits the brand
**Strong fit.** Vitamin C is the daytime "environmental defense + radiance" counterpart to a nighttime retinoid — defending the skin's repair work and supporting collagen ("renewal" framing). The "brightness / luminosity in gold" angle is on-brand. A **stable derivative (THD)** suits a premium, low-irritation, elegant-texture product better than a finicky low-pH LAA serum.

## Formulation notes
- **Effective concentration (LAA):** ~10–20% (below ~8% under-effective; above ~20% more irritation, diminishing returns). Classic combo: **15% LAA + vitamin E + ferulic acid** (stabilizes and boosts photoprotection).
- **pH (LAA):** must be **<3.5** for skin penetration and collagen-gene effect — this drives irritation and stability headaches.
- **THD ascorbate:** oil-soluble, works at near-neutral pH, much more stable, gentler; commonly ~5–15%.
- **SAP / EAC:** water-soluble, more stable than LAA, gentler; EAC works across a wider pH range.
- **Stability:** LAA oxidizes readily (turns yellow/brown = degraded). Needs low pH, antioxidants, airless/opaque packaging, sometimes anhydrous formats. Derivatives are far more stable.
- **Pairs well with:** Vitamin E + ferulic acid (synergy), SPF (AM). Niacinamide and vitamin C **can** be used together in modern formulas (the old "they cancel out" claim is largely overstated).
- **Conflicts:** Avoid layering high-strength LAA with strong retinoids/AHAs in the same step (irritation); typical routine is **vitamin C AM, retinoid PM.**

## Concerns & cautions
- **Irritation:** Low-pH LAA can sting/irritate sensitive skin; derivatives are gentler.
- **Sun sensitivity:** Does **not** increase photosensitivity; actually complements SPF. Still: not a sunscreen.
- **Pregnancy:** Considered low-risk / generally regarded as safe topically; still include "consult your doctor" boilerplate.
- **Stability/sourcing:** Oxidation is the main quality risk — a degraded product is a brand-credibility risk for an evidence-led brand.

## Claims caution
- ✅ **Safe cosmetic language:** "antioxidant defense against environmental stressors," "helps brighten and even the look of skin tone," "for visible radiance," "supports skin's natural luminosity."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like:** "boosts collagen by X%," "prevents sun damage" / "replaces sunscreen," "treats hyperpigmentation/melasma" (disease/structure-function). "Supports collagen" is softer but still flag for substantiation if quantified.

## Cost / sourcing
- LAA raw material is **cheap**, but stable, elegant formulation/packaging is the real cost. **THD ascorbate is significantly more expensive** (premium positioning fits). Widely available (DSM-Firmenich, Corum/THD suppliers, Spec-Chem).

✅ **HERO (AM)** — best-in-class antioxidant + radiance story that pairs with retinoid PM; use a **stable derivative (THD)** for a premium, gentle, on-brand serum. Log in [`../../ideas/considering.md`](../../ideas/considering.md).
