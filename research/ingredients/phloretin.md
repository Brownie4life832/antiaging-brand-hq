# Ingredient: Phloretin (INCI: Phloretin)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Antioxidants · **Slug:** phloretin

## What it is

Phloretin is a plant-derived **dihydrochalcone flavonoid** (a polyphenol) found mainly in **apple** tree bark, leaves, peel, and root, and in smaller amounts in strawberries and other Rosaceae. In skincare it is best known as the third active in **"Phloretin CF"-type** serums — the **10% L-ascorbic acid + 0.5% ferulic acid + 2% phloretin** vitamin-C antioxidant system (SkinCeuticals, built on the Duke/Pinnell antioxidant patent). Its proposed roles are three: (1) a **direct antioxidant** in its own right, (2) a **stabilizer/penetration-helper** that increases the cutaneous availability of the vitamin C and ferulic acid it travels with, and (3) a **pigment/tone** actor via tyrosinase inhibition. It is a close cousin, mechanistically, of [ferulic acid](ferulic-acid.md) and lives in the same "environmental-defense vitamin-C serum" niche.

**Key forms / related compounds:**
- **Phloretin** (aglycone; INCI: Phloretin) — the cosmetic-active form. Molecular formula **C₁₅H₁₄O₅**, MW **274.27**, CAS **60-82-2**. IUPAC: 4,2′,4′,6′-tetrahydroxydihydrochalcone. A pearl-white powder, melting point ~263 °C, **only slightly soluble in water** (this poor solubility is the central formulation problem).
- **Phlorizin / phloridzin** (phloretin 2′-O-glucoside; INCI: Phlorizin) — the glycosylated parent found in apple; hydrolyzed (enzymatically or industrially) to release phloretin. Better water solubility, weaker direct antioxidant; the classic SGLT/GLUT-blocking "antidiabetic" molecule.
- **Phloretin-2′-xyloglucoside** — an apple-derived glycoside that appears in CIR's apple-ingredient safety review.
- Water-soluble synthetic derivatives (e.g. phloretin-3′,3-disulfonate) and biobased analogues exist in the literature but are not mainstream cosmetic actives.

## Class & mechanism

Phloretin is a **dihydrochalcone** — an open-chain flavonoid carrying **multiple phenolic hydroxyl groups** (three on one ring, one on the other). Those phenolic –OH groups drive its primary action: **hydrogen-atom donation to free radicals**, with the resulting radical resonance-stabilized across the aromatic ring — the same chemistry that makes ferulic acid and other polyphenols antioxidants. It is active in standard radical assays (DPPH, ABTS) and scavenges ROS in cells. [[3]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9460114/)

Beyond direct scavenging, phloretin has several documented mechanisms:

1. **Nrf2 activation** — it upregulates the master antioxidant transcription factor Nrf2 and its downstream cytoprotective enzymes (heme oxygenase-1/HO-1, NQO1), boosting the cell's *own* defenses rather than only quenching radicals one-for-one. [[4]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9855955/)
2. **Anti-inflammatory signaling** — it suppresses **NF-κB** (blocks IκBα degradation and p65 nuclear translocation) and the **MAPK** cascade (ERK1/2, p38, JNK), lowering pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6) in stimulated cells. [[4]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9855955/)
3. **Glucose-transporter (GLUT) inhibition** — phloretin is a classic, well-characterized **GLUT1/GLUT2 inhibitor** (and SGLT inhibitor). This is its most famous pharmacology, but it is largely *systemic/metabolic* biology; its relevance to topical skin benefit is **theoretical** (a possible anti-glycation / "starve glycolytic stress" angle), not demonstrated in skin.
4. **Tyrosinase modulation** — phloretin **reversibly inhibits tyrosinase in a mixed-type manner**, partly by **chelating the active-site copper** and partly by quenching/complexing the enzyme; it can also reduce dopachrome back to L-DOPA. A twist: it is **both a substrate AND an inhibitor** of tyrosinase, which complicates the clean "skin-brightener" story. [[10]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1386142519310327)
5. **Anti-glycation** — phloretin (and phlorizin) act as **trapping agents for reactive dicarbonyl species** (methylglyoxal, glyoxal), the precursors of advanced glycation end-products (AGEs); this is cell-free/chemistry data. [[9]](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/tx800227v)
6. **Penetration modification** — phloretin is **lipophilic** and a known **membrane-dipole modifier**; it inserts into lipid bilayers and can increase the fluidity/permeability of the stratum-corneum lipid lamellae. This is the mechanistic basis for the claim that it helps deliver its vitamin-C partners. [[2]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0014299920306853)

In skin specifically, the relevant cascade is the standard one: **UV → ROS → MAPK/NF-κB activation → MMP transcription (MMP-1, MMP-9) → collagen/elastin breakdown**, plus direct DNA damage (thymine dimers, p53). Phloretin's antioxidant + anti-inflammatory mechanisms act *upstream* in this cascade, which is why the blend study measured fewer thymine dimers, less MMP-9 and p53, and preserved Langerhans cells after UV. [[1]](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19146606/)

## Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

- **Oxidative stress** — primary, well-supported mechanism (direct scavenging + Nrf2 induction). [[3]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9460114/)[[4]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9855955/)
- **Photoaging** — strongest *applied* evidence, but only as part of the C/FA/phloretin blend (reduced UV erythema, sunburn cells, thymine dimers, MMP-9, p53). [[1]](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19146606/)
- **Pigmentation / tone** — tyrosinase inhibition (copper chelation, mixed-type) in vitro; brightening signal in manufacturer clinical data on the blend; **no isolated human RCT**. [[2]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0014299920306853)[[10]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1386142519310327)
- **Inflammaging** — broad anti-inflammatory pharmacology (NF-κB/MAPK), but mostly non-skin models. [[4]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9855955/)
- **Collagen / ECM loss** — *indirect* (blunts UV→MMP cascade) plus *in-vitro* MMP-1/elastase inhibition; **no human collagen-synthesis data**. [[2]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0014299920306853)
- **Glycation / AGEs** — dicarbonyl trapping / AGE-inhibition **in vitro only**; not shown for topical skin. [[9]](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/tx800227v)
- **Cellular senescence, mitochondrial decline, microbiome, hydration/water loss** — **not meaningfully addressed** by skin-relevant human evidence. (A mouse atopic-dermatitis model touches barrier/inflammation, and the blend study reported improved hydration in some marketing data, but these are weak/indirect.) Do not claim.

## Evidence review by tier

> **Headline honesty:** Phloretin has **no published human RCT as a standalone topical active.** Every piece of human topical evidence comes from the **vitamin C + ferulic acid + phloretin blend**, where phloretin's individual contribution **cannot be isolated** — and the key human study is **n=10, acute UV-challenge, and authored by the same commercially-interested (Pinnell/SkinCeuticals/L'Oréal) group** behind the patented formula. Phloretin's deeper, more interesting biology (tyrosinase, MMP-1/elastase, anti-glycation, GLUT inhibition, anti-inflammatory) is real but sits almost entirely at the **in-vitro / animal / non-skin** tier.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro / mechanistic (the bulk of phloretin's evidence)
- **Antioxidant chemistry:** Phloretin is active in DPPH and ABTS radical assays and scavenges cellular ROS; reviews collate its phenolic HAT mechanism and Nrf2/HO-1 induction. [[3]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9460114/)[[4]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9855955/)
- **Anti-inflammatory pharmacology:** Habtemariam 2023 (*Biomedicines* 11(1):143) — comprehensive review showing phloretin inhibits NF-κB (IκBα degradation, p65 translocation) and MAPK (ERK/p38/JNK), suppresses TNF-α/IL-1β/IL-6, and inhibits GLUT1 to throttle glycolysis-driven inflammation; effective at ~10–100 µM. Mostly macrophage/endothelial/lung systems, **not skin**. [[4]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9855955/)
- **Tyrosinase / melanogenesis:** "Phloretin as both a substrate and inhibitor of tyrosinase" (*Spectrochim Acta A*, 2020) — reversible **mixed-type** inhibition via active-site **copper chelation** and enzyme complexation; phloretin also reduces dopachrome to L-DOPA; in B16 melanoma it lowers cellular tyrosinase activity and melanin content and can induce apoptosis. **Caveat: it is also a tyrosinase substrate.** [[10]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1386142519310327)[[2]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0014299920306853)
- **MMP-1 + elastase inhibition:** the dermatology review reports phloretin (like vitamin C) inhibits **MMP-1 and elastase** activity in cell-free / cell systems — an anti-photoaging rationale, but **no human confirmation.** [[2]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0014299920306853)
- **Anti-glycation:** Shao et al. 2008 (*Chem Res Toxicol* 21(10):2042, "Apple Polyphenols, Phloretin and Phloridzin: New Trapping Agents of Reactive Dicarbonyl Species") — phloretin/phloridzin dose-dependently trap methylglyoxal/glyoxal and inhibit AGE formation. Chemistry/cell-free, **systemic-flavored, not topical skin.** [[9]](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/tx800227v)
- **Antimicrobial:** activity against *Cutibacterium (Propionibacterium) acnes*, MRSA, *Candida*, etc.; basis for anti-acne formulation interest. [[2]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0014299920306853)[[3]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9460114/)
- *Limitations:* in-vitro potency rarely predicts in-vivo topical benefit; concentrations used in cells/cuvettes (often tens of µM) may exceed what reaches living dermis from a leave-on product; the "also a tyrosinase substrate" finding undercuts a clean brightening claim.

### Tier 2 — Animal / ex-vivo skin models
- **DNCB dermatitis (mouse):** topical/oral phloretin reduced epidermal thickening, mast-cell infiltration, and Th1/Th2/Th17 frequencies in a DNCB-induced dermatitis model — anti-inflammatory, barrier-adjacent, but a *disease model in mice*. [[4]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9855955/)
- **Transfersome skin-delivery + aging model (rat):** Wang et al. 2023 (*Molecules*) — phloretin-loaded transfersomes raised 24-h skin penetration (~843 vs ~443 µg/cm²) and, as a gel, improved antioxidant enzyme markers (SOD, GSH-Px, CAT) and reduced oxidative damage in a rodent aging model, with no skin irritation. Formulation/animal proof-of-concept, **not human efficacy.** [[8]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10574780/)
- **Penetration-modifier studies:** phloretin alters lipid-bilayer permeability (DPPC liposome / sodium-fluorescein skin-flux work), supporting its "delivery-helper" role mechanistically. [[2]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0014299920306853)[[7]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S016836590300124X)
- *Limitations:* rodent skin and ex-vivo membranes approximate but do not equal living human facial skin; delivery gains do not by themselves prove clinical benefit.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL
- **No meaningful human oral skin data for phloretin specifically.** Oral bioavailability is poor (**~8.7%**) because of low water solubility and intestinal hydrolysis/metabolism; oral interest centers on antidiabetic GLUT/SGLT inhibition, not skin. [[3]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9460114/)[[8]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10574780/) Apple-polyphenol nutrition studies exist but do not isolate phloretin or measure skin endpoints. **Nothing here transfers to a topical claim.**

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the decision-relevant tier)

**The one real human study — and it is a blend, not phloretin:**
- **Oresajo C, Stephens T, Hino PD, Law RM, Yatskayer M, Foltis P, Pillai S, Pinnell SR. 2008** (*J Cosmet Dermatol* 7(4):290–297) — **randomized, vehicle-controlled, n=10** healthy volunteers, Fitzpatrick II–III. A serum of **10% L-ascorbic acid + 0.5% ferulic acid + 2% phloretin** (acidic pH ~2–3.5) was applied to the lower back for **4 consecutive days**, then challenged with solar-simulated UV at **1–5× MED**. At 24 h after 5× MED, UV-induced increases in **sunburn-cell formation, thymine-dimer formation, MMP-9 expression, and p53 expression were all attenuated** by the antioxidant blend vs vehicle, and the blend **prevented the UV-induced drop in CD1a⁺ Langerhans cells** (i.e., blunted UV immunosuppression). The authors attribute to phloretin a dual role: **potent antioxidant** in its own right **plus** **stabilizing/increasing the skin availability of the vitamin C and ferulic acid.** [[1]](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19146606/)
  - **Critical limitations:** (1) tiny n (10); (2) **acute UV-challenge biomarkers**, not long-term anti-aging outcomes; (3) it tests the **3-active blend** — phloretin's individual contribution is **not isolable** (it is confounded by 10% vitamin C, a powerful active on its own); (4) **authored by the Pinnell group**, inventors associated with the patented SkinCeuticals technology — **not independent**; (5) single skin-tone range.

**Manufacturer / lower-tier clinical data (the blend "Phloretin CF"):**
- SkinCeuticals reports investigator-graded studies on **Phloretin CF** (the 2% phloretin / 10% L-AA / 0.5% FA serum) — e.g. a 12-week, n≈64 (Fitzpatrick I–VI) study showing improved even tone (~20%), radiance (~27%), and reduced discoloration (~16%); and a 24-week study citing ~24% reduction in fine-line appearance, ~16% texture, ~19% dullness/discoloration. These are **brand-run, on the finished blend, often not peer-reviewed in the primary literature**, and again cannot isolate phloretin. Treat as marketing-grade support, not independent proof. [SkinCeuticals product/clinical pages] [[6]](https://www.skinceuticals.com.au/skincare/vitamin-c-serums/phloretin-cf-vitamin-c-serum/SKC_0002.html)
- **Sonophoresis / micro-needle mesotherapy delivery study (Cosmetics 2024, MDPI 11(3):87)** — a complex antioxidant product containing **L-AA + ferulic acid + phloretin** delivered via sonophoresis and micro-needling reduced erythema and improved hydration / visible aging signs. Small, device-confounded, blend-confounded; phloretin not isolated. [[5]](https://www.mdpi.com/2079-9284/11/3/87)

**What is NOT available:** there is **no published, peer-reviewed, independent RCT of topical phloretin alone** for any anti-aging, brightening, or barrier endpoint. The honest topical case for phloretin is "it's the antioxidant/penetration-helper inside a well-known vitamin-C serum that has one small acute-UV human study and brand clinical data."

## Topical bioavailability / penetration

- Phloretin is **lipophilic** and **poorly water-soluble**, which is *both* a delivery problem (hard to load at high active levels in water-based serums) *and* the basis of its **penetration-enhancer** behavior — it partitions into stratum-corneum lipids and increases bilayer fluidity, helping co-actives diffuse. [[2]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0014299920306853)[[7]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S016836590300124X)
- Its poor solubility means real formulations often lean on solubilizers (glycols, alcohol) — note the SkinCeuticals INCI list includes dipropylene glycol and denatured alcohol — or on advanced carriers (**transfersomes, liposomes, nanoemulsions, SEDDS**) that markedly raise penetration and retention in lab models. [[8]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10574780/)
- Oral bioavailability is low (**~8.7%**), so the topical route is the relevant one for skin — but the actual **dermal delivery from a simple leave-on serum has not been quantified in humans.** [[3]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9460114/)[[8]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10574780/)

## Formulation notes

- **Effective concentration:** **2% phloretin** is the established benchmark (Oresajo blend / Phloretin CF). In-vitro work uses ~10–100 µM. There is **no dose-response data in human skin** to define an optimum; 2% is "what the studied product used," not a proven threshold.
- **pH:** In the flagship system, phloretin rides along in the **low-pH (~2.0–3.5)** vitamin-C window required to keep L-ascorbic acid stable and penetrating. Phloretin itself does not mandate that pH, but the canonical formula is acidic.
- **Stability:** Phloretin is reasonably stable as a solid but **practically limited by solubility**; in solution it (like other polyphenols) is susceptible to oxidation and discoloration over time. As with vitamin C serums, **airless/opaque, low-oxygen packaging** is prudent. Phloretin's antioxidant role is partly *to protect its co-actives* (sacrificial/regenerative chemistry), so it can brown as it works.
- **Pairings (synergy):** the documented partnership is **vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid) + ferulic acid** — phloretin stabilizes/helps deliver both and adds tyrosinase-driven tone benefit (the reason SkinCeuticals chose phloretin over vitamin E in this SKU: lighter feel + pigment focus). [[1]](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19146606/) Plausibly complementary with niacinamide, other polyphenols, and UV filters. Some market serums pair it with hyaluronic acid.
- **Conflicts:** No hard chemical incompatibilities, but it inherits the low-pH vitamin-C system's oxidation/packaging headaches. Its **lipophilicity** can make high loading in water-thin serums difficult.
- **Sensory:** At 2% it contributes little odor; the finished low-pH C serums can have a slightly tacky/acidic feel and can stain fabrics/oxidize to amber, like other vitamin-C formats.

## Concerns & cautions

- **Irritation / sensitization:** Phloretin is generally **well tolerated** topically; supplier/in-vitro data report it as **non-irritating** (e.g. OECD 439 reconstructed-skin assays) with low dermal toxicity (rabbit LD₅₀ >2000 mg/kg). Most stinging in real products comes from the **low pH / vitamin C** it accompanies, not phloretin itself. No prominent sensitization signal, but patch-testing is sensible. [supplier data; [3]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9460114/)
- **Systemic toxicity (context, not topical-relevant):** at very high parenteral/oral doses in mice (≈2.4 mmol/kg) phloretin caused mortality; this reflects acute high-dose systemic exposure, **not** topical cosmetic use, where exposure is far lower. [[3]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9460114/)
- **Sun sensitivity:** Phloretin does **not** photosensitize; in the blend it is *photoprotective* (reduces UV markers). It is **not a sunscreen** and does not replace SPF.
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** **No specific human safety data.** As a food-derived polyphenol used at low topical % it is not a known teratogen, but there is no evidence base either way — use standard "consult your doctor" boilerplate; **do not market as "pregnancy-safe."**
- **Sourcing / sustainability:** Derived from **apple** bark/leaf/peel (and apple-processing by-products) via solvent extraction of phlorizin then hydrolysis to phloretin; an **upcycled apple-waste** angle is available. Natural abundance is low, so most commercial material relies on extraction-plus-hydrolysis or semisynthesis — supply is workable but not trivially cheap.

## Claims caution

- **Status:** Phloretin is a **cosmetic ingredient** (antioxidant / skin-conditioning); it is also a food-derived polyphenol. INCI "Phloretin"; included indirectly in CIR's apple-derived-ingredients safety review. Used widely in vitamin-C "antioxidant defense" serums.
- **The drug line (per [CLAUDE.md](../../CLAUDE.md) guardrails):** antioxidant + appearance language is fine; structure-function and disease language is not.
  - ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "antioxidant that helps defend skin against free-radical / environmental stress," "helps support a more even, radiant-looking tone," "helps the appearance of UV-stressed skin," "helps stabilize vitamin C." Pair with: "use daily SPF — antioxidants complement, not replace, sunscreen."
  - ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like — flag for substantiation/legal:** "protects against skin cancer," "prevents DNA mutations / thymine dimers" (the Oresajo endpoints are real but are **disease/structure-function = drug territory**), "boosts collagen," "treats melasma/hyperpigmentation," "anti-glycation" as an on-skin promise (in-vitro only), "boosts your sun protection." The thymine-dimer / p53 / MMP-9 / Langerhans-cell data are **scientifically real but legally drug-like** — keep them in education/footnotes, never on-pack. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).
  - ⚠️ **Honesty flag:** because no isolated human phloretin trial exists, **any phloretin-specific efficacy claim is borrowed from the blend** and should not be presented as proven for phloretin alone.

## Cost / sourcing

**Moderate** cost raw material — pricier than commodity antioxidants (ferulic acid, niacinamide) because of the apple-extraction + hydrolysis route and the limited natural abundance, but not exotic. High-purity (≥98%) phloretin is sold by multiple cosmetic-ingredient suppliers. The bigger cost driver is **formulation**: keeping a low-pH vitamin-C/phloretin system stable needs airless/opaque packaging and good solubilization (or carrier tech), the same overhead as any L-ascorbic-acid serum. An **upcycled-apple** sourcing story is a credible, on-brand sustainability angle.

## Hype vs. evidence

- **Reality:** Phloretin's honest, best-supported role is as a **co-antioxidant and penetration/stability helper inside a vitamin C + ferulic acid serum**, with one small (n=10) acute-UV human study on the *blend* and supportive (industry) clinical data on the finished product. [[1]](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19146606/)
- **Overreach to avoid:** Marketing that treats phloretin as a standalone "anti-aging powerhouse," "collagen booster," or proven "skin brightener." The tyrosinase, MMP-1/elastase, and anti-glycation stories are **in-vitro/cell-free** — genuinely interesting mechanism, **not** human-proven topical benefit. The "also a tyrosinase substrate" finding even muddies the brightening narrative. [[10]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1386142519310327)
- **The "stabilizes and increases availability of vitamin C/ferulic acid" line** is plausible and mechanistically grounded (lipophilic penetration modifier), but in the human study it is an **attribution/assertion**, not an isolated measurement — phloretin's delivery boost was never quantified separately in human skin.
- **GLUT-inhibition / anti-glycation / antidiabetic** talk is real pharmacology but **systemic** — do not import it as a topical skin promise.
- **Funding caveat:** the single human topical study sits in the same **Pinnell / SkinCeuticals / L'Oréal** lineage as the C E Ferulic evidence base — strong narrative, but **not arms-length independent**.

## Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING (not hero) — conditional on building a vitamin-C antioxidant SKU.** Phloretin is a credible, well-tolerated co-antioxidant with a real (if narrow and industry-originated) human signal **only inside the C + ferulic + phloretin system**, plus a pleasing "apple polyphenol / upcycled" story and a plausible tone/brightening + penetration-helper bonus over the plain C E Ferulic trio. It does **not** justify a standalone hero claim: there is **zero isolated human RCT**, and its richest biology (tyrosinase, MMP-1/elastase, anti-glycation, GLUT) is in-vitro. **Best move:** if/when we build a daytime "environmental defense" vitamin-C serum, consider phloretin (≈2%) as the tone-leaning, penetration-assisting third active — and keep claims to antioxidant defense + appearance of even tone. If we are *not* building a vitamin-C serum, **skip it** — it has little independent reason to exist in the lineup.

**Longevity relevance:** Phloretin targets the **oxidative-stress and photoaging hallmarks** (foundational, upstream drivers) and theoretically touches **glycation/AGEs** and **inflammaging** — all "defend the repair work" longevity logic. But this is **protective/supporting** biology with thin topical proof, not a regenerative or senescence-clearing actor. Log in [`../../ideas/considering.md`](../../ideas/considering.md).

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

_Verified 2026-06-19. Method: independently re-fetched the load-bearing sources (PubMed/PMC/journal records) and checked each headline claim for (a) whether the evidence is **human + topical** vs. quietly borrowed from in-vitro / animal / oral / blend data, (b) whether the cited study is real and says what is claimed, and (c) sample size and funding/independence._

### Overall evidence grade: **C+ (weak-to-moderate human-topical; strong in-vitro mechanism)**

The honest split:
- **Phloretin as a co-antioxidant inside the C + ferulic + phloretin blend, judged by acute UV-challenge markers** → **moderate** but resting on a *single n=10 industry study* (Oresajo 2008). Real and correctly characterized.
- **Phloretin as a standalone topical anti-aging / brightening active in humans** → **no evidence** (no isolated human RCT exists). Its tyrosinase/MMP-1/elastase/anti-glycation/anti-inflammatory mechanisms are genuine but **in-vitro / animal / non-skin**.

### What checks out
- **Oresajo et al. 2008, *J Cosmet Dermatol* 7(4):290-297** — real; **human, n=10**, randomized vehicle-controlled, 10% L-AA + 0.5% FA + 2% phloretin, 4-day pretreatment, UV 1–5× MED; attenuated sunburn cells, thymine dimers, MMP-9, p53, and protected Langerhans cells. ✔ Correctly characterized as a **blend** study from the **Pinnell** group.
- **Habtemariam 2023, *Biomedicines* 11(1):143** — real anti-inflammatory pharmacology review (NF-κB, MAPK, Nrf2, GLUT1); correctly tiered as **mostly non-skin in-vitro/animal**. ✔
- **Nakhate et al. 2022, *Nutrients* 14(17):3638** — real; chemistry (C₁₅H₁₄O₅, MW 274.27), ~8.7% oral bioavailability, MMP-1/tyrosinase/elastase mention, nanoformulation strategies. ✔
- **Shao et al. 2008, *Chem Res Toxicol* 21(10):2042** — real; dicarbonyl-trapping / anti-AGE; correctly tiered **in-vitro chemistry, not topical.** ✔
- **Wang et al. 2023, *Molecules*** (transfersomes) — real; penetration/retention gains + rat aging-model antioxidant markers; correctly tiered **animal/formulation.** ✔

### Caveats / things to watch
1. **No isolated human phloretin RCT — stated plainly** in the dossier; this is the single most important honesty point and is reflected in the C+ grade and the "SUPPORTING/conditional" verdict.
2. **Funding concentration** — the one human study is **Pinnell/SkinCeuticals**, not independent; flagged at point of claim.
3. **"Stabilizes/increases availability of C and FA"** is an **attribution within the blend study**, not an isolated human measurement — softened to "asserted/mechanistic," not "proven."
4. **Tyrosinase "brightener" story is double-edged** — phloretin is also a tyrosinase *substrate*; the dossier notes this so the brightening claim is not overstated.
5. **Reference [2] (Eur J Pharmacol "Dermatological applications of the flavonoid phloretin") exact volume/page and author list were not re-verified line-by-line** (publisher 403 on direct fetch); the journal, title, year (2020), and substance are confirmed via the publisher index and multiple secondary citations. Treat granular figures sourced only to it as **[partially verified]**.
6. **SkinCeuticals brand clinical percentages** (even tone ~20%, radiance ~27%, fine lines ~24%, etc.) are **marketing-grade, on the finished blend, not peer-reviewed** — explicitly labeled as such; do not cite as independent proof.

### Net assessment
Disciplined and defensible. The dossier does **not** smuggle in-vitro tyrosinase/MMP/anti-glycation or systemic GLUT data in as proven topical wins — it tiers them explicitly and repeatedly states the central fact that **phloretin has no standalone human topical RCT** and its human signal is **blend-confounded and industry-originated.** Grade **C+** reflects strong mechanism + thin, borrowed human-topical proof, which supports the **"SUPPORTING, conditional on a vitamin-C SKU"** verdict.
