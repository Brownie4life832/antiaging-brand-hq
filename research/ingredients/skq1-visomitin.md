# Ingredient: SkQ1 / Visomitin (INCI: Plastoquinonyldecyltriphenylphosphonium — no harmonised INCI in skincare use)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⬜ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ✅ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Autophagy & mitochondrial-targeted actives (mitochondria-targeted antioxidant; cardiolipin-peroxidation inhibitor) · **Slug:** `skq1-visomitin`

> **TL;DR for a busy reader.** SkQ1 is the most *intellectually* exciting molecule in the mitochondrial-longevity space and the *weakest* in human topical-skin evidence. It is a "Skulachev ion" — a plastoquinone antioxidant bolted to a positively-charged triphenylphosphonium tail that drives it into mitochondria, where it is calculated to accumulate up to ~10⁸-fold (a theoretical figure from membrane-potential/partition modelling, not a measured skin value) and quenches the reactive oxygen species (mtROS) and cardiolipin peroxidation implicated in mitochondrial aging. The animal data are genuinely striking (lifespan extension in mice; reversal of alopecia, graying, and impaired wound healing) **but every one of those skin/hair results used SYSTEMIC (oral/drinking-water) dosing, not a topical cream.** The *only* human efficacy data are for an **eye drop** (Visomitin, 250 nM) for dry-eye — and the two pivotal US Phase 3 trials (VISTA-1/VISTA-2) **MISSED their co-primary endpoints.** There is **zero peer-reviewed human topical-skin RCT.** A commercial cosmetic line (MitoVitan, by the same developer, Mitotech) makes anti-wrinkle claims that rest on mechanism + animal data + manufacturer assertion, not published clinical proof. It also has a real **bell-shaped antioxidant→pro-oxidant dose curve** (antioxidant ~25 nM, pro-oxidant ~800 nM), so concentration is a genuine safety variable. **Promising biology, unproven on human skin, IP-locked to one company, and easy to over-claim.** See Verdict.

## 1. Identity

- **Common name(s):** SkQ1; "Skulachev ion Q1"; **Visomitin** (the ophthalmic drug brand); MitoVitan / MitoVitan Active / Exomitin (the cosmetic brands). [1][6][13]
- **Chemical name:** **10-(6′-plastoquinonyl)decyltriphenylphosphonium** (bromide salt). A plastoquinone (the antioxidant moiety, borrowed from plant chloroplast electron transport) joined by a **10-carbon decyl linker** to a **triphenylphosphonium (TPP⁺) lipophilic cation**. CAS for the bromide commonly cited as 1042103-87-8. [1][9]
- **INCI / regulatory identity:** There is **no clean, harmonised cosmetic INCI** for SkQ1 in wide use; it is a patented proprietary active, not a commodity raw material. This matters for both formulation and labelling.
- **Key forms / family ("SkQ" / Skulachev ions):** [1]
  - **SkQ1** — the lead; plastoquinone + TPP⁺, 10-carbon linker.
  - **SkQR1** — plastoquinone + **rhodamine-19** cation (fluorescent; used to visualise uptake; also bioactive).
  - **SkQ3** — methylplastoquinone variant (less active than SkQ1).
  - **SkQ5** — 5-carbon linker (weaker membrane penetration).
  - **MitoQ** — the better-known *cousin*: same TPP⁺ targeting strategy but uses **ubiquinone** (CoQ10-type quinone) instead of plastoquinone. Important comparator (see §4, §10).
- **Developer / ownership:** Vladimir P. **Skulachev**, Moscow State University; commercialised by **Mitotech S.A.** (Luxembourg). Patented in RU, US, CN, JP, EU. [1][9]

## 2. Class & mechanism

SkQ1 is the archetypal **mitochondria-targeted antioxidant**. Its design is the elegant part:

1. **Targeting (the TPP⁺ cation).** Lipophilic cations cross membranes and are driven by membrane potential into the negatively-charged mitochondrial matrix. Given typical membrane potentials and SkQ1's lipid:water partition (~13,000:1), the calculated concentration of SkQ1 in the inner leaflet of the inner mitochondrial membrane is on the order of **10⁸× higher than extracellular** — so a vanishingly small applied dose becomes a high *local* mitochondrial dose. This is the principle Liberman & Skulachev established with penetrating ions back in 1969. [9]
2. **Antioxidant chemistry (the plastoquinone head).** Inside the membrane, the reduced plastoquinol form **chain-breaks lipid peroxidation** — specifically it protects **cardiolipin** (the signature inner-membrane phospholipid) from peroxidation, a key early event in mitochondrial aging and apoptosis. It is **"rechargeable":** the respiratory chain re-reduces the oxidised quinone, so one molecule works catalytically rather than being consumed once. [1][6][12]
3. **mtROS scavenging → downstream signalling.** By lowering mitochondrial ROS, SkQ1 dampens ROS-driven programs: NF-κB inflammation, TNF/apoptotic signalling, endothelial barrier breakdown (VE-cadherin), and senescence-associated pathways. In animal wound models this translates into faster inflammatory-phase resolution, more myofibroblasts, and better angiogenesis. [3][4][5]
4. **Mitophagy / autophagy link (the "class" rationale, but thinner).** In **human LHON-patient skin fibroblasts**, SkQ1 preserved mitochondrial function and modulated **PINK1/PRKN-mediated mitophagy** (interacting with PINK1, supporting SOD2). This is the closest thing to a true autophagy mechanism in a *skin* cell — but it is a disease-model fibroblast study, not normal aging skin, and the relationship was correlational/network-pharmacology-derived. [11]
- ⚠️ **Pro-oxidant flip side.** Like all redox quinones, SkQ1 is **biphasic**: antioxidant at low nM, **pro-oxidant at high nM**. Reported window: antioxidant ~**25 nM**, pro-oxidant ~**800 nM** (a ~32-fold window — notably *wider* than MitoQ's <2-fold, which is a genuine design advantage, but the ceiling is real). [12]

**Honest summary:** the mechanism maps beautifully onto *mitochondrial decline, oxidative stress and inflammaging* — the trendiest longevity hallmarks — but the **skin-outcome evidence is animal/systemic, not human/topical.**

## 4. Evidence review by tier — the core of this dossier

> Read this as a ladder. SkQ1 is unusual: it has **real, peer-reviewed human clinical trials** — but for the **eye, not the skin**, and even those **failed their primary endpoints in Phase 3.** The skin/hair benefits everyone cites are **animal, and delivered systemically (oral/drinking water), never as a cream.** The top rung (human topical-skin RCT) is **empty.**

### Tier 1 — In-vitro / biochemical
- **Antonenko, Skulachev et al. (2008). "Mitochondria-targeted plastoquinone derivatives… 1. Cationic plastoquinone derivatives: synthesis and in vitro studies."** *Biochemistry (Moscow)* 73(12):1273–1287. Foundational synthesis + membrane/mitochondrial-uptake characterisation; established the antioxidant chemistry and ~10⁸ accumulation rationale. [9]
- **Bell-shaped dose-response.** Cationic quinones are strong **pro-oxidants at µM** and antioxidants at sub-µM; SkQ1's anti-/pro-oxidant concentrations differ ~32-fold (≈25 vs ≈800 nM), the widest "safe window" among SkQ/MitoQ family. [12]
- **Anti-inflammatory in human cells:** inhibits **leukotriene synthesis in human neutrophils** (Front Pharmacol 2022) and mast-cell degranulation (Biochemistry Moscow 2017). [pattern of anti-inflammatory in-vitro work] [10]
- **Human LHON skin fibroblasts (Xu et al. 2024, *Biomedicines* 12(9):2020).** SkQ1 reduced ROS, stabilised mitochondrial membrane potential, and modulated **PINK1/PRKN mitophagy** to preserve mitochondrial function. Closest "skin cell" mechanistic study — but disease-derived fibroblasts, in vitro. [11]

### Tier 2 — Animal / ex-vivo (the bulk of the "anti-aging" story — note the route)
- **Skulachev et al. (2008/2009). "SkQ1 prolongs lifespan and prevents development of traits of senescence" (paper #5 in the series)** *Biochemistry (Moscow)* 73(12):1329–1342; and **"An attempt to prevent senescence: a mitochondrial approach,"** *Biochim Biophys Acta* 2009. Subnanomolar SkQ1 prolonged lifespan in fungus, crustacean, insect and **mouse**, and inhibited age-related **balding, graying (canities), cataract, retinopathy, osteoporosis, thymic involution.** **Route: oral/drinking water.** [1][7]
- **Anisimov et al. (2011). "Effects of SkQ1 on lifespan of rodents."** *Aging* 3(11):1110–1119. Outbred SHR mice, inbred strains, dwarf hamsters, mole-voles; **1–250 nmol/kg/day in drinking water/food.** Median lifespan ~doubled in conventional vivaria. ⚠️ **Crucial honest caveat:** under **specific-pathogen-free (SPF) conditions, simply eliminating pathogens reproduced much of SkQ1's apparent benefit**, and SkQ1 "fails to further increase lifespan of animals who die of cancer rather than infection." Effects varied by **sex, strain, and housing.** So part of the headline "doubled lifespan" is an **infection-control artifact**, not pure anti-aging. [2]
- **Shabalina et al. (2017). "Improved health-span and lifespan in mtDNA mutator mice treated with SkQ1."** *Aging* 9(2):315–339. n=38 untreated vs 17 treated; **~0.7–1.0 µmol/kg/day in drinking water** from ~100 days. Median lifespan +16% (290→335 d); **markedly reduced alopecia** and kyphosis. The clearest "skin/fur" benefit — but a **synthetic accelerated-aging model**, small n, and **systemic dosing.** [8]
- **Demyanenko et al. (2015). "SkQ1 improves impaired dermal wound healing in old mice."** *Aging* 7(7):475–485. C57BL/CBA mice; young (6 mo, n=12), old (24 mo, n=12), old+SkQ1 (n=10); **100 nmol/kg/day SYSTEMIC over ~8 months** (NOT applied to the wound). SkQ1 sped inflammatory-phase resolution, granulation tissue, vascularisation, epithelialisation; ↑ myofibroblasts (ECM/growth factors), prevented VE-cadherin breakdown. [3]
- **Demyanenko et al. (2017). "SkQ1 Improves Dermal Wound Healing in Genetically Diabetic Mice."** *Oxid Med Cell Longev* 2017:6408278. db/db mice (n=12 treated, 9 control, 5 non-diabetic); **250 nmol/kg/day PER OS (oral) for 12 weeks.** Faster wound closure to non-diabetic levels; ↑ myofibroblasts (α-SMA), ↓ neutrophils, ↑ macrophages, ↑ vessel density, stronger epithelialisation. **Again oral, not topical.** [4]
- **Corneal (eye-surface, a topical-tissue analog).** SkQ1 protected **corneal epithelium from UV and mechanical injury** and aided ocular-surface wound healing (BMC Ophthalmol 2018; Wei et al. *Ophthalmol Ther* 2018/2019, in vitro human conjunctival/corneal-limbal cells — no cytotoxicity to conjunctival cells ≤250 nM and to corneal-limbal cells ≤2500 nM; ~25–50 nM stimulated proliferation/closure). The nearest thing to "topical epithelial protection" data — but it is **eye, not skin.** [5][14]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL / SYSTEMIC
- **None for skin.** There is no oral human SkQ1 trial reporting skin endpoints. (Contrast idebenone/CoQ10, which at least have oral human drug data.) The systemic anti-aging case rests entirely on **rodents.**

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL
**(a) Eye drops (the real human clinical data — and it is mixed-to-negative at Phase 3):**
- **Brzheskiy et al. (2015). Visomitin dry-eye RCT.** *Adv Ther* 32(12):1263–1279. **Visomitin = 0.155 µg/mL (250 nM) SkQ1**, TID, 6-week treatment + 6-week observation; multicentre, randomised, double-masked, placebo-controlled; **n=240 (120/group)**, Russia/Ukraine. Improved tear-film stability, reduced corneal staining and symptoms; "well tolerated" (34 AEs in SkQ1 vs 21 placebo; 1 unrelated SAE). This is the dataset behind Russia's 2012 approval. [6]
- **Petrov et al. (2016). SkQ1 ophthalmic Phase 2 (US, Controlled Adverse Environment).** *Adv Ther.* Safety + efficacy signals in dry-eye. [15]
- **VISTA-1 & VISTA-2 (US Phase 3, Mitotech/Essex Bio-Technology, ~2019–2022).** Multicentre, double-masked, randomised, placebo-controlled. ⚠️ **Both studies MISSED their co-primary endpoints.** VISTA-2 reported statistically significant *secondary*/pre-specified endpoints (central corneal fluorescein staining, BCVA at day 28; p<0.05); VISTA-1 showed early discomfort reduction. The honest read: **the strongest human program for SkQ1 anywhere failed its primary efficacy bar.** Visomitin is **not FDA-approved.** [16][17]

**(b) Topical SKIN (the format we actually care about):**
- **No peer-reviewed human topical-skin RCT exists.** Searches of PubMed/journals return **no controlled clinical trial of SkQ1 applied to skin for wrinkles, photoaging, or barrier.**
- **MitoVitan / MitoVitan Active (Mitotech cosmetic line)** market SkQ1 serums claiming prevention/reduction of wrinkles and "interruption of aging at the cellular level." These are **manufacturer marketing claims** built on the mechanism + rodent data + IP, **not** on published human skin trials. Treat the anti-wrinkle claims as **unsubstantiated for our purposes.** [13]

**Bottom line by tier:** strong, elegant in-vitro chemistry → genuinely impressive animal lifespan/wound/fur data **delivered systemically** → **no oral human skin data** → real but **partly failed** human *eye-drop* trials → **a complete absence of human topical-skin efficacy evidence.** Almost everything attractive about SkQ1 for skincare is **borrowed from a different format (oral) or a different tissue (eye).**

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **The targeting works *intracellularly*; getting *across the stratum corneum* is a separate, unstudied problem.** SkQ1's superpower (10⁸ mitochondrial accumulation via membrane potential) operates *once it is inside a cell*. Whether a meaningful, controlled dose crosses intact human stratum corneum from a cream — and reaches viable epidermal/dermal cells at the **antioxidant (not pro-oxidant) concentration** — is **not established in any published study I could find.** [9]
- **It is a charged (cationic), fairly large amphiphilic molecule.** Permanent positive charge generally hampers passive percutaneous absorption; the developer's cosmetic uses very low concentrations (consistent with the nM activity window), which conveniently also limits how much penetration even matters. No quantitative human skin-permeation data published.
- **Implication:** for skin we have *neither* a penetration dataset *nor* an efficacy dataset — a double blind spot for an evidence-led brand.

## 6. Formulation

- **Active concentration:** biologically the antioxidant window is **~25 nM**, pro-oxidant from **~800 nM** — i.e. it is dosed in **nanomolar/trace** amounts, not the 0.5–2% range typical of cosmetic actives. Visomitin eye drops are **250 nM (0.155 µg/mL)**; cosmetic serums use similarly trace levels. **More is not better and can flip pro-oxidant** — concentration control is a *safety* issue, not just efficacy. [6][12]
- **pH / stability:** redox-active quinone; oxidation-sensitive. Like idebenone/CoQ10 it benefits from **light/air protection and airless packaging**; specifics for cosmetic SkQ1 are proprietary (Mitotech), not openly published.
- **Pairings/conflicts:** as a lipophilic, mitochondrially-targeted antioxidant it conceptually complements aqueous antioxidants (vitamin C derivatives) and barrier lipids; no published compatibility/conflict data. Avoid pairing logic that pushes total redox load toward the pro-oxidant ceiling.
- **Sensory:** delivered at trace levels in serums; the finished MitoVitan products lean on conventional humectants (e.g. Aquaxyl) for feel, i.e. the *sensory* story is the base, not SkQ1.

## 7. Safety

- **Eye (best-characterised):** Visomitin well tolerated across ~1,000+ ophthalmic trial subjects; AE rate modestly higher than placebo but no serious treatment-related signal; no skin/dermal red flags from the ocular program. [6][16]
- **In-vitro skin/epithelial cytotoxicity:** no apparent cytotoxicity to human conjunctival/corneal epithelial cells ≤250 nM; corneal epithelium tolerated 0–400 nM in vitro. [5][14] No dedicated **dermal** irritation/sensitisation dataset published.
- **Pro-oxidant risk is the distinctive concern.** Above its window SkQ1 *generates* oxidative stress — so an over-concentrated or unstable formula could plausibly do net harm. The wide (~32×) window is reassuring but not a blank cheque. [12]
- **Sun sensitivity:** not a sunscreen; no evidence of photosensitisation, but also no skin photoprotection efficacy data.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** no cosmetic safety data; default caution.
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** fully **synthetic** (plastoquinone moiety inspired by plant biochemistry but the molecule is manufactured). No animal/wildcraft sourcing tension. The constraint is **IP/supply**, not ethics (see §9).

## 8. Regulatory & claims

- **Cosmetic vs. drug line is unusually sharp here** because the *same molecule is an approved drug* (Visomitin eye drops, Russia, for dry-eye/early cataract) and a *failed* US drug candidate. Using its drug heritage to sell a cream ("clinically proven," "FDA-trialed") is a **claims trap** — the trials are for eyes and the pivotal ones *failed*. [1][16][17]
- ✅ **Defensible cosmetic language (if we ever used it):** "antioxidant," "helps defend the look of skin against oxidative/environmental stress," "supports the look of skin." Mechanism education must stay appearance-based.
- ⚠️ **Risky / would need substantiation or legal review (and we lack the data):** "interrupts aging at the cellular level," "anti-wrinkle / reduces wrinkles," "mitochondrial rejuvenation," "clinically proven," "Nobel-level science," "protects/repairs mitochondria in your skin," any lifespan/longevity-outcome implication. These map to the manufacturer's claims and are **not** supported by human skin trials — squarely the kind of structure/function + efficacy overreach CLAUDE.md warns against.
- See [`../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md) guardrails and [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Effectively single-source / IP-locked.** SkQ1 and its cosmetic use are **patented and controlled by Mitotech/Skulachev group** across RU/US/CN/JP/EU; it is **not** an open commodity active you can buy generically at cosmetic grade with a clean INCI and COA the way you can niacinamide or ergothioneine. [1][9][13]
- **Practical consequence for the brand:** licensing/supply dependence on one company, regulatory-identity ambiguity (no standard INCI), and no second source — all serious supply-chain and formulation-control risks for a brand whose thesis is "buy commodity bases, invest in the brand."
- **Cost:** dosed at trace nM levels so *material cost per unit* is small, but **access/licensing cost and formulation/stability overhead are high**, and you'd be building marketing on an asset you don't control.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

| Marketing claim (incl. MitoVitan / general SkQ1 buzz) | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Doubles lifespan / Nobel-grade anti-aging." | Lifespan doubling was in **mice**, **partly an infection-control artifact** (SPF housing reproduced much of it), sex/strain/housing-dependent, and **fails when animals die of cancer.** [2] |
| "Reverses graying, balding, repairs aging skin." | True-ish in **rodents on oral/drinking-water dosing**, in artificial accelerated-aging models — **never demonstrated from a topical cream on human skin.** [7][8] |
| "1000× more powerful than CoQ10." | Refers to **mitochondrial targeting/accumulation** in vitro, not a clinical skin-efficacy comparison. [9] |
| "Clinically proven." | The clinical trials are **eye drops for dry-eye**, and the pivotal **US Phase 3 (VISTA-1/2) MISSED primary endpoints**; not FDA-approved. No skin RCT exists. [6][16][17] |
| "Interrupts aging at the cellular level (in your skin)." | Mechanistic extrapolation; the one human-*skin*-cell study is **disease-model fibroblasts in a dish**, not aging skin in people. [11] |
| "Pure, safe antioxidant." | **Biphasic**: pro-oxidant above ~800 nM. Concentration is a safety variable. [12] |

## 11. Verdict for the brand

❌ **SKIP (as a hero or named active) — watch-item only.**
**One line:** The most scientifically romantic molecule in the mitochondrial-longevity field and the least defensible on human skin — striking *rodent/systemic* data, real but partly-*failed* human *eye-drop* trials, **no human topical-skin RCT**, a pro-oxidant ceiling, and single-company IP lock-in make it a credibility and supply risk for an evidence-led premium brand.

- **Why not even "conditional":** unlike idebenone (also thin, but an open commodity), SkQ1 is **IP-locked, has no clean INCI, no skin penetration data, and no human skin efficacy data** — three independent dealbreakers for a brand built on substantiation and on owning its own narrative. We'd be renting another company's unproven (for skin) story.
- **Longevity relevance (genuine, hence "watch"):** mechanistically it is a near-perfect fit for the **mitochondrial-decline / oxidative-stress / inflammaging** hallmarks and for a "skin-longevity" worldview — *if* a credible human topical dataset ever appears (or if Mitotech licenses it with real skin RCTs), revisit. The mitophagy/PINK1 angle [11] is the most "on-class" finding to track for the Autophagy & mitochondrial-targeted actives category.
- **Better-evidenced neighbours for the same story:** for a mitochondrial/antioxidant longevity narrative with more (still imperfect) human topical support, prefer **ergothioneine**, **vitamin C (THD)**, or **CoQ10/idebenone** as supporting actors — see [`ergothioneine.md`](ergothioneine.md), [`vitamin-c.md`](vitamin-c.md), [`coenzyme-q10.md`](coenzyme-q10.md), [`idebenone.md`](idebenone.md). For the *autophagy* hallmark specifically, **spermidine** [`spermidine.md`](spermidine.md) and **topical rapamycin** [`topical-rapamycin.md`](topical-rapamycin.md) have more direct human-skin work.
- **Action:** log in [`../../ideas/not-considering.md`](../../ideas/not-considering.md) with reason "no human topical-skin evidence + IP-locked single-source + pro-oxidant ceiling; revisit only if a credible topical RCT emerges."

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Method: re-checked the headline efficacy claims against the cited primary literature via web search/fetch (PubMed, journal pages, ARVO/IOVS, manufacturer releases), asking for each: is the evidence *human and topical-to-skin*, are the studies real and faithfully represented, and are they small or industry-funded?

### Overall evidence grade (for TOPICAL SKIN use): **D** — essentially unproven on human skin / hype-adjacent if marketed as a skin active.

Grade rationale: the top rung (human topical-**skin** RCT) is empty. There is genuine human *clinical* evidence, but it is for an **eye drop** and the pivotal Phase 3 program **failed its co-primary endpoints**; all skin/hair benefits are **animal and delivered systemically (oral/drinking water)**, never from a cream. By the grading scale (A = strong human-topical RCT; B = moderate human-topical; C = weak/indirect; D = essentially unproven topically), SkQ1-for-skin is a clear **D**. (For the *eye-drop* indication the human evidence would grade higher — roughly C/B given the mixed Phase 3 — but that is the wrong tissue and format for this brand.) Note this **D is for the evidence, not the biology**: the mechanism is real and the molecule is legitimate; the gap is the missing human-skin format. The dossier's own TL;DR, tier ladder (§4), and Verdict (§11) already reach this conclusion. This verification confirms it rather than overturning it.

### Citation accuracy: claims check out as written.
Every headline citation I checked is real and faithfully represented — this dossier does **not** quietly borrow in-vitro/animal/eye data and dress it up as human-topical-skin evidence; it labels the tier and route each time. Specifically confirmed:
- **VISTA-1/VISTA-2 (US Phase 3)** — both **missed co-primary endpoints**; VISTA-2 hit a pre-specified *secondary* endpoint (central corneal fluorescein staining, p<0.05) in a Schirmer-defined subpopulation; not FDA-approved. Sample sizes (not previously in the dossier): VISTA-1 n≈451 (1:1:1, low/high/placebo); VISTA-2 n≈610 (1:1). Faithful. [16][17]
- **Brzheskiy 2015** — real *Adv Ther* multicentre, randomised, double-masked, placebo-controlled dry-eye RCT, n=240, 6-wk treatment + 6-wk follow-up; positive on tear-film/staining/symptoms; basis of Russia's 2012 approval. Faithful. **Eye, not skin.** [6]
- **Anisimov 2011** — the "doubled lifespan" caveat is accurate and arguably *understated*: switching from a conventional to a low-pathogen vivarium **doubled female-mouse lifespan on its own**, matching SkQ1's effect — i.e. a large part of the headline benefit is infection-control, not anti-aging; effects were sex/strain/housing-dependent. Oral/drinking-water dosing. Faithful. [2]
- **Demyanenko 2015 / 2017** — real wound-healing papers in 24-mo old mice (100 nmol/kg/day) and db/db diabetic mice (250 nmol/kg/day); both **systemic (oral)**, NOT applied to the wound, exactly as stated. Faithful. [3][4]
- **Xu 2024 (LHON skin fibroblasts)** — real; the PINK1/SOD2 mitophagy link was derived by **network pharmacology + molecular docking** (computational/correlational) in **disease-derived fibroblasts in a dish** — the dossier's hedging ("correlational/network-pharmacology-derived," "not normal aging skin") is correct and important. Faithful. [11]
- **Antioxidant/pro-oxidant window (~25 nM vs ~800 nM, ~32× vs MitoQ <2×)** and **Wei 2018 corneal** data confirmed; the dossier's earlier "no cytotoxicity ≤250 nM" was conservative — corneal-limbal cells tolerated ≤2500 nM (now corrected in §4). [5][12][14]

### Main caveats (what an adversarial reader should hold onto)
1. **Tissue & route mismatch is total.** Zero human topical-skin efficacy data; zero human topical-skin **penetration** data. Every "anti-aging skin/hair" result is rodent + systemic. This is the single decisive fact.
2. **The best human program FAILED its primary bar.** The "clinically proven" halo derives from eye drops whose pivotal Phase 3 missed co-primary endpoints. Any skin marketing leaning on the clinical heritage is a claims trap.
3. **Pervasive single-group / industry origin (NOT previously flagged for the science).** Almost the entire SkQ1 efficacy corpus — chemistry, lifespan, wound healing, *and* the eye trials — originates from the **Skulachev group / Mitotech**, the patent holder and commercial developer. This is a structural conflict-of-interest across the *whole* evidence base, not just the MitoVitan marketing. Independent replication is thin. Treat the rodent "anti-aging" literature as developer-generated until independently reproduced.
4. **Small n in the key skin-relevant animal studies.** The clearest "fur/skin" benefit (Shabalina 2017, mtDNA-mutator mice) is n=17 treated vs 38 control in a *synthetic accelerated-aging* model; wound studies are n≈10–12/arm. Underpowered and model-dependent.
5. **Biphasic safety ceiling is real** (pro-oxidant >~800 nM); the wide window helps but concentration is a genuine safety variable, especially absent skin-penetration/dose data.
6. **No clean cosmetic INCI + IP lock-in** compound the evidence problem with a supply/identity problem.

### Claims that were softened / should stay soft
- **"~10⁸-fold accumulation"** (TL;DR) — softened to flag it as a **calculated/theoretical** membrane-potential figure, not a measured skin value.
- **Corneal cytotoxicity threshold** (§4) — corrected to reflect Wei 2018 (≤250 nM conjunctival, ≤2500 nM corneal-limbal).
- **Already appropriately soft in the source dossier (keep as-is):** "interrupts aging at the cellular level," "anti-wrinkle," "mitochondrial rejuvenation," "clinically proven," "1000× more powerful than CoQ10," and all lifespan/longevity-outcome language are correctly tagged as manufacturer marketing / mechanistic extrapolation / wrong-tissue in §10 and §8. No further softening required.
- **One remaining watch-item:** if this molecule is ever revisited, do **not** cite the rodent lifespan/wound or eye-drop trials as support for a *skin* claim — even informally — because of caveats 1–3 above.

**Net:** the dossier is accurate, well-sourced, and already self-skeptical; its SKIP verdict is sound. The only substantive thing this pass adds is the **structural industry/single-group conflict across the entire science base** (caveat 3) and explicit small-n flags — both reinforce, not weaken, the SKIP. Grade **D for topical-skin evidence** stands.
