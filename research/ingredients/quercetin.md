# Ingredient: Quercetin (INCI: Quercetin)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering (lean toward supporting / conditional — see Verdict)
**Class:** NAD+ / sirtuin / AMPK / cellular-energy actives (flavonoid polyphenol; senolytic/senostatic)

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Quercetin (a flavonol, sub-class of flavonoids).
- **INCI:** **Quercetin**. Related INCI names exist for derivatives and plant sources (e.g. *Sophora Japonica* extract, a common botanical source).
- **Chemistry:** 3,3',4',5,7-pentahydroxyflavone. A yellow crystalline polyphenol. Five hydroxyl groups drive both its antioxidant power and its formulation headaches (poor water solubility, oxidation-prone, light/pH sensitive).
- **Key forms / derivatives:**
  - **Aglycone quercetin** — the free, unbound form (most studied, least soluble/stable).
  - **Glycosides** — sugar-bound forms found in plants and used in formulation: **quercetin-3-O-glucoside (isoquercetin/isoquercitrin)**, **rutin (quercetin-3-O-rutinoside)**, **quercitrin (quercetin-3-O-rhamnoside)**. Generally more water-soluble and more stable than the aglycone; absorption/efficacy differs by glycone.
  - **Esterified / lipophilic derivatives** — e.g. **quercetin caprylate** (a more skin-compatible, proteasome-activating ester used in some cosmetic work).
  - **Phytosome / phospholipid complexes** — e.g. **Quercevita** (1% cream), built to overcome poor penetration.
  - **Nanocarriers** — solid lipid nanoparticles (SLN), nanostructured lipid carriers (NLC), chitosan nanoparticles, nanoemulsions, liposomes/transfersomes; all developed specifically to make topical quercetin usable.

---

## 2. Class & mechanism (skin/cell level)

Quercetin is a broad-acting flavonoid that hits several aging-relevant pathways. Most mechanistic data is **in-vitro or animal**; the human picture is thinner (see Section 4).

- **Direct antioxidant / radical scavenger.** The polyphenol scaffold quenches reactive oxygen species (ROS) and chelates metal ions. In skin models it preserves endogenous **glutathione (GSH)** after UV insult — one of the better-supported topical effects [3][4].
- **Anti-inflammatory (NF-κB / MAPK / JAK-STAT).** In human keratinocytes, quercetin suppresses **UV-induced NF-κB activation**, lowering IL-1β, IL-6, IL-8 and TNF-α [5][6]. In ex-vivo human skin it directly inhibits **JAK2 and PKCδ**, blocking downstream ERK/JNK/Akt/STAT3 signaling and **AP-1** [7].
- **MMP inhibition / collagen protection.** By suppressing **MMP-1** and **COX-2**, quercetin reduces UV-driven collagen breakdown in human skin tissue and fibroblasts [6][7] — i.e. it acts more as a *collagen-degradation brake* than a proven collagen-builder.
- **Senolytic + senostatic (the "longevity" mechanism).** Quercetin was one of the first molecules shown to clear senescent cells. As a **senostatic** it dampens the senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP); as a **senolytic** it (especially **paired with dasatinib, "D+Q"**) selectively eliminates senescent fibroblasts [1][8][9]. It also stimulates **AMPK** in stressed fibroblasts, reducing stress-induced senescence [8].
- **Proteasome activation.** Quercetin and quercetin caprylate can activate the proteasome (clearing damaged proteins), proposed as a fibroblast-rejuvenation route [11][12].
- **Pigmentation — bidirectional and unsettled.** In cell-free assays quercetin is a competitive **tyrosinase inhibitor**, but in living human melanocytes/melanoma cells it has been shown to **increase** tyrosinase synthesis and melanin (up to ~7-fold melanin, ~60-fold tyrosinase activity in one study) [13][14]. Direction depends on model and concentration — do **not** position this as a brightening active.

## 4. Evidence review by tier (core of the dossier)

> **Bottom line up front:** Quercetin's longevity reputation rests overwhelmingly on **in-vitro, animal, and human *oral*/systemic** senolytic work — almost none of which used the skin as the target organ or the topical route. **Topical human evidence is limited to one small open/instrumental cosmetic study, one phytosome soothing study, and one negative irritation study.** The senolytic story is real science but is largely *borrowed from another format* when applied to a topical cream.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro (mechanistic; abundant)
- **Domaszewska-Szostek et al., 2021, *Int. J. Mol. Sci.* (review).** Synthesizes flavonoid/quercetin data: keratinocyte NF-κB suppression (↓IL-1β/IL-6/IL-8/TNF-α); PKCδ/JAK2 interaction blocking COX-2 and MMP-1; AMPK-mediated reduction of stress-induced fibroblast senescence; senolytic activity with D+Q [1].
- **Takaya & Kishi (Keio Univ.), 2024, *Biogerontology* — in-vitro arm.** D+Q selectively eliminated senescent human dermal fibroblasts across replicative, irradiation- and doxorubicin-induced senescence models [9]. (The in-vivo arm of the same paper is a **mouse–human chimera** — aged human skin grafted onto nude mice with **oral** D+Q for 30 days — not a topical human study.)
- **Nagata et al., 2004, *Pigment Cell Research*.** Quercetin *increased* melanin (~7×) and tyrosinase activity (~62×) in human melanoma cells and normal human melanocytes — the key counter-evidence to "quercetin brightens" [13].
- **Multiple keratinocyte/fibroblast UVA/UVB studies** confirm ROS scavenging and cytokine suppression; quality varies, results consistent directionally.
- **Limitations:** cell models don't predict topical clinical effect; many use concentrations unachievable in skin given penetration limits; pro-oxidant behavior appears at higher doses.

### Tier 2 — Animal (mostly topical, photoprotection)
- **Casagrande et al., 2006, *J. Photochem. Photobiol. B*, 84(1):21–27.** Topical quercetin formulation on **hairless mice**: prevented UVB-induced GSH depletion and reduced proteinase secretion/activity; no irritation from the vehicle/quercetin formulation [3].
- **Vicentini et al., 2008, *Eur. J. Pharm. Biopharm.* (quercetin w/o microemulsion).** In-vitro + in-vivo skin penetration plus efficacy against UVB damage — microemulsion improved delivery and protection [4].
- **Nan et al., 2018, *Front. Pharmacol.* (quercetin-loaded chitosan nanoparticles).** HaCaT cells + **C57BL/6 mice**: improved penetration vs free quercetin; enhanced NF-κB/COX-2 inhibition; reduced PGE2 and skin edema [10].
- **⚠️ Andersen/Bjerring group, 2024, *Phytomedicine Plus*.** **ORAL** quercetin (and fisetin) **increased** UVR-induced photocarcinogenesis in hairless mice — shorter time to tumor onset, accelerated growth; effect *not* explained by skin accumulation [15]. An important honesty flag: oral senolytic flavonoids are not automatically "skin-protective," and may be the opposite under UV.
- **Limitations:** rodent skin ≠ human skin; most studies short-term; the photocarcinogenesis signal is oral, not topical, but tempers any "quercetin = sun protection" leap.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL / systemic
- **D+Q (dasatinib + quercetin) clinical senolytic trials** exist for diabetic kidney disease, IPF, osteoporosis, etc. — these establish that *systemic* quercetin (with dasatinib) can reduce senescent-cell burden in humans, **but none used skin appearance as the endpoint.** Relevance to a face cream is mechanistic/indirect only.
- **Oral safety:** adverse effects rare and mild across human intervention studies; doses typically 500–1000 mg/day; high doses can cause GI upset, headache, tingling (Andres et al., 2018, *Mol. Nutr. Food Res.*) [16].
- **Limitations:** no oral quercetin RCT shows skin-elasticity/wrinkle benefit; quercetin's oral bioavailability is notoriously poor; drug-interaction potential exists.

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the decisive tier — and it's thin)
- **Nebus et al., 2011, *J. Am. Acad. Dermatol.* — "oak quercetin" SPF 15 cream.** **n=32 women, 45–60**, twice daily, 60 days, dermatologist + instrumental assessment. Reported within-group improvement (P<.05) in wrinkling, elasticity, smoothness, radiance, moisturization; >90% perceived improvement at 60 days [11]. **Read with heavy caveats:** small; **no placebo/vehicle control reported** (so this is a before/after change, not a controlled effect vs. quercetin); contained **SPF 15** (sunscreen alone improves photoaged-skin parameters), confounding the quercetin attribution; **manufacturer-associated** (authors affiliated with Johnson & Johnson and Korres, the formulators — verified); not a true blinded RCT. The headline efficacy of the dossier rests on this one confounded study.
- **Maramaldi et al., 2016, *Clin. Cosmet. Investig. Dermatol.* — quercetin phytosome (Quercevita 1% cream).** **Single-blind, n=30 healthy volunteers.** Soothing / anti-itch and anti-erythema effects vs nicotinate- and histamine-induced reactions [2]. **Limitations:** single-blind, small, endpoints are soothing/anti-irritant — **not anti-aging**; industry-linked.
- **Katsarou et al., 2000, *Contact Dermatitis*, 42(2):85–89 — NEGATIVE.** **n=15**, topical quercetin after SLS-induced irritant dermatitis did **not** speed recovery of barrier function (TEWL) or erythema vs vehicle [17]. Directly undercuts "quercetin repairs the barrier" marketing.
- **What is proven for topical human use:** modest soothing/anti-erythema (one small study); cosmetic-appearance improvement in one small uncontrolled/SPF-confounded study. **What is borrowed (not proven topically):** senolytic "clears zombie cells," collagen "rebuilding," barrier repair, and systemic-longevity claims.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Poor as the free aglycone.** Quercetin is highly lipophilic *and* poorly water-soluble, oxidation-prone, and a large polar molecule — a bad combination for stratum-corneum passage. Native quercetin penetrates skin poorly and degrades in formulation.
- **Delivery is the whole ballgame.** Microemulsions [4], phospholipid/phytosome complexes [2], chitosan nanoparticles [10], SLN/NLC, liposomes/transfersomes, and nanocrystal gels all measurably improve penetration and stability. A bare "1% quercetin" in a basic emulsion likely under-delivers and oxidizes.
- **Implication for the brand:** any quercetin claim should be tied to a *specific stabilized/encapsulated form*, not raw powder. Penetration data is mostly from animal/ex-vivo skin and Franz-cell models, not in-vivo human depth profiling.

---

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration (topical):** Human cosmetic studies used **~1%** (phytosome cream [2]; oak-quercetin cream [11]). No established dose-response in human skin; higher is not clearly better and raises pro-oxidant/color concerns.
- **pH:** Stability favors mildly acidic; quercetin oxidizes faster at alkaline pH and under light/heat/oxygen. Antioxidant flavonoids generally hold up best ~pH 3–6.
- **Stability:** **The major formulation challenge.** Prone to oxidation and color shift (yellow→brown), light- and air-sensitive. Requires encapsulation and/or chelators/co-antioxidants, opaque airless packaging, and inert processing. Antilipoperoxidative activity has been used as a functional stability marker across formulations (Scalia/Mezzena-type work) [12].
- **Color/sensory:** Intrinsically **yellow** — can tint a cream and stain. Encapsulated/derivatized forms mitigate this. Generally neutral odor.
- **Pairings:** Logical with other antioxidants (vitamin C/E — flavonoids can help regenerate tocopherol), niacinamide, ferulic acid, soothing actives. Encapsulation pairs it with lipid systems.
- **Conflicts:** Avoid high-pH systems and unprotected exposure to light/oxygen; caution combining with other strong pro-oxidant-capable actives at high load. No well-documented hard incompatibility, but it is **chemically fussy**.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation/tolerability (topical):** Generally well tolerated; cosmetic studies at ~1% reported good tolerability and even soothing/anti-itch effects [2][3]. The SLS study found it *neither* helped *nor* notably harmed barrier recovery [17].
- **Sensitization:** No strong signal of topical quercetin as a common sensitizer in the cosmetic literature reviewed; robust HRIPT data specific to quercetin is limited [unverified for a formal CIR safety conclusion — see note].
- **Pro-oxidant / pigmentation caution:** At higher concentrations quercetin can flip pro-oxidant, and it has **increased melanin in human melanocytes** in vitro [13][14] — a reason to be cautious about high-dose use and any "brightening" positioning, particularly for melasma-prone skin.
- **Sun behavior:** Topically it is studied as photo-*protective* (antioxidant) [3][4][10]. **However**, *oral* quercetin/fisetin **increased** UV photocarcinogenesis in mice [15] — so "quercetin protects you from the sun" is not a safe claim and SPF should never be implied.
- **Pregnancy/lactation:** No topical-specific safety data; standard conservative cosmetic guidance ("consult a physician") applies. Oral high-dose supplementation is not advised in pregnancy due to lack of data.
- **CIR / regulatory safety status:** No specific CIR final report confirmed in this research pass — **[unverified]**; verify before relying on it in a dossier or label defense.
- **Sustainability/sourcing:** Plant-derived (commonly from *Sophora japonica* buds, onion skins, oak); generally considered a sustainable, plant-based, often byproduct-derived ingredient.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic vs. drug line:** Quercetin is used as a cosmetic ingredient. Anti-aging *appearance* claims are cosmetic; mechanism claims drift toward drug territory fast.
- **✅ Likely defensible (cosmetic) language:** "antioxidant," "helps defend against environmental/oxidative stress," "soothing / helps calm the look of redness" (supported by the phytosome study [2], keep it appearance-based), "helps improve the appearance of fine lines / elasticity / smoothness" *if* tied to the specific formula and tested — note the oak-quercetin support is weak/SPF-confounded.
- **⚠️ Risky / needs substantiation or legal review:**
  - "clears senescent cells / zombie cells," "senolytic," "rejuvenates at the cellular level," "longevity" — these are **drug-like / unsubstantiated for a topical** and rest on oral/in-vitro data. Flag for legal.
  - "boosts collagen," "rebuilds collagen" — evidence is *protection from degradation*, not synthesis; reframe as "helps protect" at most, with substantiation.
  - "repairs the skin barrier," "reduces water loss/TEWL" — the one controlled human test was **negative** [17]; do not claim.
  - "sun protection," "protects from UV damage" — implies SPF; not allowed without OTC sunscreen status; oral data even points the wrong way [15].
  - "brightens / evens tone / fades dark spots" — **avoid**; human-cell data shows quercetin can *increase* melanin [13].
- **No medical/therapeutic claims** (atopic dermatitis, psoriasis, acne treatment) — those are drug claims even though preclinical data exists.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier:** **Low–moderate.** Bulk cosmetic-grade quercetin (often *Sophora japonica*-derived) is inexpensive as a raw powder. The cost lever is **the delivery system** — phytosome/encapsulated/nanocarrier grades and branded forms (e.g. Quercevita, stabilized SLN/NLC dispersions) cost meaningfully more and are what the evidence actually used.
- **Suppliers:** Widely available from cosmetic-actives and nutraceutical suppliers; commodity raw material with multiple sources, plus branded stabilized/complexed grades.
- **Supply considerations:** Plant-derived and abundant; supply risk low. Main practical risk is **batch purity, particle size, and oxidation state** — specify a stabilized/standardized grade.

---

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **The hype:** Quercetin rides the "senolytic / longevity / zombie-cell-clearing" wave (largely from David Sinclair-era D+Q research and supplement marketing). Brands imply a topical cream delivers cellular rejuvenation and collagen regrowth.
- **The reality:**
  - Senolytic human data is **systemic, with dasatinib, and in non-skin organs** — not a topical face cream clearing senescent skin cells.
  - The headline skin-appearance study [11] is **small, uncontrolled, and contained SPF 15**.
  - The best *clean* topical human result is **soothing/anti-redness** [2], not anti-aging.
  - A controlled barrier study was **negative** [17].
  - Pigmentation effects can go the *wrong* way [13].
  - Oral quercetin **worsened** UV carcinogenesis in mice [15].
- **Honest read:** A legitimate, well-studied **antioxidant/anti-inflammatory** flavonoid with a **promising but largely unproven-for-topical** senolytic story and a real **stability/penetration** problem. Strong "story ingredient," weak "hero claim ingredient."

---

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ Conditional / supporting — not a hero.**

One-line: Use quercetin as a **supporting antioxidant/soothing/environmental-defense active in a stabilized, encapsulated form** — it earns a place for its real anti-inflammatory/antioxidant profile and an on-trend longevity narrative, but the topical anti-aging and senolytic evidence is too thin (and partly confounded/negative) to anchor a hero barrier-repair claim, and it carries stability, pigmentation, and claims-discipline risks.

**Longevity-relevance note:** Quercetin is genuinely a cornerstone *longevity-biology* molecule (senolytic/senostatic, AMPK, SASP) — which makes it a natural fit for a skin-longevity *story* — but that biology is proven *systemically/in vitro*, not in a topical cream. If the brand wants the longevity narrative, quercetin can support it *honestly* only as "antioxidant defense / supports skin's resilience," with the senolytic science cited as *background science*, never as a product claim. Pairs conceptually with the barrier-repair thesis (ceramides/niacinamide as the proven core; quercetin as antioxidant support).

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

_Verification pass: 2026-06-19. Independent adversarial re-check of every headline efficacy claim against the primary sources (PubMed/PMC/publisher pages)._

### Overall evidence grade: **C** (weak / indirect for the topical anti-aging use case)

For **topical, human, anti-aging efficacy** — the only thing that matters for a face cream — the evidence is **C**. There is **no controlled human topical RCT** showing a wrinkle/elasticity/collagen benefit. The single supportive cosmetic-appearance study is small, uncontrolled, SPF-15-confounded, and manufacturer-run. The strongest *clean* human topical result is **soothing/anti-erythema** (a single single-blind, industry-linked study) — that narrow claim would rate **B–**. Everything carrying the "longevity / senolytic" weight is **D** as a *topical product claim* (it is real science, but borrowed from in-vitro and human *oral/systemic* data in non-skin organs). The dossier's own Section 4–11 framing already lands at roughly this grade; this pass confirms it rather than overturning it.

### What I verified (all citations are real and say what is claimed, with one error)

- **Nebus 2011 [11]** — real (JAAD 2011). n=32 women 45–60, 60 days, SPF 15, instrumental + dermatologist scoring all confirmed. **Author affiliations confirmed as Johnson & Johnson + Korres** (the formulators) — the "manufacturer-associated" flag is correct. The "no vehicle control" and "SPF confound" caveats are correct and decisive.
- **Maramaldi 2016 [2]** — real. Single-blind, n=30, soothing/anti-itch/anti-erythema (NOT anti-aging). **Authors affiliated with Indena S.p.A.** (maker of the Quercevita phytosome) — industry link confirmed.
- **Nagata 2004 [13]** — real. ~7× melanin and **61.8×** tyrosinase in human melanoma/melanocytes confirmed; the "quercetin can *raise* melanin" counter-evidence is genuine.
- **Katsarou 2000 [17]** — real, n=15. Precisely, it was a **null** result (topical quercetin did **not increase** recovery of barrier function/TEWL/erythema after SLS) — i.e. no benefit, not active harm. The dossier's "negative" framing plus its "neither helped nor harmed" phrasing elsewhere is accurate.
- **Andersen/Bjerring 2024 [15]** — real (Phytomedicine Plus 2024). **ORAL** quercetin + fisetin shortened time to UV tumour onset in hairless mice (n=125); rutin had no effect. The "oral, not topical; tempers any sun-protection leap" framing is correct.
- **Casagrande 2006 [3]**, **Shin 2019 [7]** (ex-vivo human skin + JB6 cells), **Domaszewska-Szostek 2021 [1]** (review) — all real and accurately characterised; mechanistic data is in-vitro/animal/ex-vivo as the dossier states.

### Errors / fixes made to the dossier in this pass
- **Citation error corrected:** Ref 9 was attributed to "**Lim CJ et al.**" — the actual authors are **Takaya K & Kishi K** (Keio University). PMID 38619669, journal *Biogerontology* 2024, title, and findings are all correct; only the author names were wrong. Fixed in Section 4 (Tier 1) and Section 12. Also clarified that this study's in-vivo arm is a **mouse–human chimera with ORAL D+Q**, not a topical human study.
- **Softened** the Section 3 table's "topical human RCT-adjacent study" → "small *uncontrolled, SPF-confounded* topical human cosmetic study" (calling Nebus "RCT-adjacent" overstated it).
- **Softened** the Nebus Tier-4 entry to flag inline that it is a before/after within-group change (not a controlled effect) and that the dossier's headline efficacy rests on this one confounded study.

### Main caveats (the load-bearing ones)
1. **Borrowed-format evidence.** The entire senolytic/longevity reputation is in-vitro + human *oral/systemic* (D+Q in kidney/lung/bone), never a topical cream clearing senescent *skin* cells. Treat as background science only.
2. **The one supportive human-topical efficacy study is triple-compromised** (uncontrolled, SPF-15, manufacturer-run). It cannot anchor any anti-aging product claim.
3. **A controlled human barrier test was null** [17] — actively undercuts barrier/TEWL claims.
4. **Pigmentation can go the wrong way** in human cells [13] — do not position as brightening.
5. **Oral analogue worsened UV carcinogenesis in mice** [15] — never imply sun protection.
6. **Industry funding on both human studies** (J&J/Korres for [11]; Indena for [2]) — the two studies a brand would most want to cite are the two with the clearest commercial interest.

### Claims that should be softened or avoided (verdict)
- ❌ **"Senolytic / clears zombie cells / cellular rejuvenation / longevity"** as a *product* claim — D-grade for topical; keep strictly as background science (dossier already says this — endorsed).
- ❌ **"Boosts/rebuilds collagen"** — evidence is degradation-braking (MMP-1↓), not synthesis. "Helps protect against collagen breakdown" at most.
- ❌ **"Repairs barrier / reduces TEWL"** — contradicted by the one controlled human test.
