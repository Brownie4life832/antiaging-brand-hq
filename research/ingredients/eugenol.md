# Ingredient: Eugenol (INCI: Eugenol) — spermidine co-active

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⬜ considering · ⬜ shortlisted · ☑ rejected as standalone / conditional micro-additive (see verdict) · ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Autophagy & mitochondrial-targeted actives · **Slug:** `eugenol`

> **One-line honesty flag up front:** Eugenol is the **clove-oil phenol** best known to cosmetic chemistry as a **regulated fragrance allergen** (one of the EU's labeled fragrance allergens; IFRA-restricted; RIFM-classified weak sensitizer). Its "longevity" credential is that, *in cell culture*, it **co-induces autophagy alongside spermidine** — and one **dietary (oral) mouse study** showed anti-photoaging effects. **There is no human topical efficacy study for skin aging, the autophagy data is in-vitro only, and eugenol's main real-world cosmetic role is as a sensitizing fragrance ingredient with tight legal concentration caps.** The "spermidine co-active" framing is mechanistically interesting but commercially awkward: the molecule you'd be adding is one a clean-beauty consumer is actively told to avoid.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Eugenol (4-allyl-2-methoxyphenol; "clove oil"/"oil of cloves" principal constituent).
- **INCI:** **Eugenol.** Listed in EU CosIng; a recognized cosmetic fragrance ingredient. [10][11]
- **Chemistry:** 2-methoxy-4-(2-propenyl)phenol; C₁₀H₁₂O₂; **CAS 97-53-0**. A **phenylpropanoid** — a phenol bearing a methoxy and an allyl side chain. Clear-to-pale-yellow oily liquid, spicy clove odor. [10][11]
- **Natural origin:** The dominant volatile of **clove** (*Syzygium aromaticum*) bud/leaf oil (often 70–90%+ of clove oil), and present in cinnamon leaf/bark, bay/laurel leaf, basil (sweet/holy), nutmeg, ylang-ylang. Commercial eugenol is isolated from clove oil or **manufactured synthetically**; cosmetic/fragrance grades are typically high purity (≥98–99%). [10][11][16]
- **Related forms / derivatives:** **Isoeugenol** (a separate, *stronger* sensitizer and separately regulated allergen), **eugenyl acetate**, **methyleugenol** (a genotoxic carcinogen — **restricted/effectively banned** in EU cosmetics; do not confuse with eugenol), **bis-eugenol**. [17][18]
- **"Spermidine co-active" context:** In the longevity-ingredient literature, eugenol appears specifically as the **clove-derived partner to wheat-germ spermidine** in autophagy-stimulating supplement work (Truzzi et al.). It is *not* an established skincare active in its own right — the co-active label comes from that nutraceutical research line, not from a topical skin program. [1][2]

---

## 2. Class & mechanism

Eugenol's proposed "longevity" mechanism is **autophagy co-induction**, layered on top of well-established **antioxidant and anti-inflammatory** chemistry.

- **Autophagy (the headline, co-active mechanism).** In cell culture, eugenol (0.2 mM) **increased LC3-II accumulation** (the canonical autophagosome marker) and, combined with spermidine (0.3 mM), drove autophagic flux (LC3-II turnover, p62/SQSTM1 turnover, upregulation of multiple ATG proteins). The leading mechanistic model for the *spermidine* half is **inhibition of the acetyltransferase EP300/p300**, a master brake on autophagy; eugenol's own molecular trigger is **less well defined** and is best described as **co-/potentiating** rather than independently characterized. [1][2][6]
- **Antioxidant — but dual (this matters).** The phenolic –OH donates a hydrogen atom to quench free radicals; eugenol is a competent DPPH/ABTS scavenger (reported DPPH IC₅₀ ≈ 11.7 µg/mL). **Crucially it is concentration-dependently *pro-oxidant*** — antioxidant at low doses, ROS-generating/cytotoxic above a threshold (in one cell model the flip occurred around ~60 µg/mL). [3][7][14]
- **Anti-inflammatory.** Suppresses NF-κB-driven and UV-driven cytokines (IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α, IFN-γ, MCP-1) in keratinocyte/fibroblast and mouse-skin models. [3]
- **Senomorphic signal (non-skin).** A 2026 vascular study found eugenol attenuated angiotensin-II-induced **vascular smooth-muscle cell senescence and SASP** via MFG-E8 downregulation — a *senomorphic* (SASP-quieting) action, but in **vascular, not skin**, cells. [8]
- **Penetration enhancer.** As a small lipophilic terpenoid-class phenol, eugenol **fluidizes stratum-corneum lipids** and is used as a *transdermal penetration enhancer* (≈3-fold flux enhancement for some drugs at 1%, comparable to DMSO in some reports). This is double-edged: good delivery, but enhanced penetration also raises irritation/sensitization exposure. [4][9]

**Plain-language:** Eugenol is the spicy phenol from cloves. At low doses it mops up free radicals and calms inflammation; in a dish it helps switch on "cellular self-cleaning" (autophagy), especially next to spermidine. But it's a Jekyll-and-Hyde molecule — push the dose and it turns pro-oxidant — and it's a known fragrance allergen that the skin can become sensitized to.

## 4. Evidence review by tier (the core)

> **Read this before believing any marketing.** The single most important fact: **the most impressive anti-aging dataset (Tong 2024) is DIETARY/ORAL in mice, and the autophagy "longevity" data is IN-VITRO in non-skin cell lines. There is NO human topical skin study for eugenol.** Everything that would justify putting it in a cream is either borrowed from oral dosing or from a dish.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro (cell studies)

**Autophagy co-active line (the reason it's in this ingredient class):**
- **Truzzi, Tibaldi, Vitali et al. (2022), *Molecules* 27(11):3425.** "Wheat Germ Spermidine and Clove Eugenol in Combination Stimulate Autophagy In Vitro…" Tested **spermidine 0.3 mM, eugenol 0.2 mM**, alone and combined, across **SH-SY5Y (neuro), HUVEC (endothelial), HBEpiC (bronchial epithelial), Caco-2, L929, U937** — **no skin cells.** Both compounds and the combination **significantly increased LC3-II** without cytotoxicity at those doses; in Caco-2, autophagy proteins were upregulated across all flux stages. Framed for **antiviral/immune support (COVID-era)**, not skin. DOI: 10.3390/molecules27113425. **In-vitro only; not skin; sample = cell lines.** [1]
- **Truzzi, Whittaker, et al. (2023), *Int. J. Mol. Sci.* 24(4):4131.** "Spermidine-Eugenol Supplement Preserved Inflammation-Challenged Intestinal Cells by Stimulating Autophagy." Caco-2 / NCM460 intestinal cells + 3D co-cultures; the SPD+EUG combination/supplement raised **LC3-II⁺ cells to ~80% vs ~40% control**, restored occludin, and lowered midkine/ROS under LPS challenge. **Eugenol's independent contribution was not isolated** (tested as combo/supplement). DOI: 10.3390/ijms24044131. **In-vitro; intestinal, not skin.** [2]

**Skin-relevant in-vitro (from the photoaging paper, see Tier 2):**
- **Tong, Geng, Kang, Li, Huang (2024), *Antioxidants* 13(2):168** — in-vitro arm: **HaCaT keratinocytes + Hs68 dermal fibroblasts**, eugenol **50 µmol/L**, UVB 5 mJ/cm². Eugenol **suppressed UVB-induced ROS, restored SOD, restored collagen content, suppressed MMP-1.** DOI: 10.3390/antiox13020168; PMID 38397766. [3]

**Antioxidant / pro-oxidant chemistry:**
- **Gülçin (2011), structure–activity** and multiple assays: eugenol is a hydrogen-atom-donating phenolic antioxidant (DPPH IC₅₀ ≈ 11.7 µg/mL; ~86% DPPH inhibition at 25 µg/mL). [7][14]
- **Atsumi et al. (2005), *Arch Oral Biol* / related cytotoxicity work:** eugenol's **antioxidant action flips to pro-oxidant and cytotoxic above a threshold** (≈62 µg/mL in a submandibular cell model), with ROS↑, GSH↓, ATP↓, mitochondrial-membrane-potential↓ at higher doses. DOI: 10.1016/S0887-2333(05)00072-X (PMID 12126794 for the related eugenol/isoeugenol prooxidant paper). **This dual nature is a real formulation/safety caveat.** [13][14]

**Verdict on Tier 1:** The autophagy co-active story is **real but thin and off-target for skin** — the cell lines are neuro/endothelial/epithelial/intestinal, not dermal, and eugenol is rarely tested alone for the autophagy endpoint. The skin-cell data (Tong 2024) is supportive (ROS↓, collagen↑, MMP-1↓) but is a single group's in-vitro arm. In-vitro is also the weakest tier for predicting cream performance, and eugenol's pro-oxidant cliff makes "more is better" actively wrong.

### Tier 2 — Animal — *exists for skin, but ORAL, and microbiome-mediated*

- **Tong et al. (2024), *Antioxidants* 13(2):168 — in-vivo arm.** Female **SKH-1 hairless mice, n=21 (7/group)**, chronic UVB (3×/week up to 3 MED), **14 weeks**. **Administration = DIETARY (0.05% w/w eugenol in AIN93G chow, ~50 mg/kg/day; authors equate to a ~0.24 g/day human dose). NOT topical.** Results: **↓ average/maximum wrinkle depth and total wrinkle volume; restored dermal collagen (Masson; ↑COL1A1/COL1A2/COL3A1); ↑ hyaluronan (HAS1-3); ↓ MMP-1/-3/-9/-13; ↓ IL-1β/IL-6/TNF-α/IFN-γ/MCP-1; ↓ MDA, ↑ SOD; restored barrier proteins (filaggrin, ZO-1, keratin 1/10) and hydration; ↓ p21/p53.** RNA-seq: 418 DEGs reversed; ECM–receptor and cytokine–cytokine-receptor pathways normalized. Also **reversed UVB-induced gut dysbiosis** (gut–skin axis invoked as part of the mechanism). **Single-sex (female); mouse↔human skin differences acknowledged; no human data.** Funding: Shandong Provincial NSF; authors declare no COI. DOI: 10.3390/antiox13020168. [3]
  - **The decisive caveat the paper itself reports:** even with chronic *oral* dosing, **eugenol skin accumulation was only ~0.06 ± 0.02 µmol/kg** — i.e., very little eugenol actually reached the skin, and the authors hypothesize **metabolite- and microbiome-mediated** (systemic), not direct-skin, action. This *undercuts* the case for a topical eugenol product: the benefit in this study did not depend on eugenol sitting in the skin.
- I found **no peer-reviewed animal study applying eugenol topically for skin aging.** (Treat any "proven in animals for topical anti-aging" claim as **[unverified]**.)

**Verdict on Tier 2:** Genuinely encouraging *as a story about the molecule's biology* — multi-omics, multiple hallmarks, the right direction on collagen/MMP/barrier/wrinkles. **But it is oral, single-study, single-sex, in mice, and explicitly NOT mediated by eugenol reaching the skin.** It is evidence for an *ingestible*, not for a serum.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL

- **No human oral trial of eugenol (or the spermidine-eugenol supplement) with a skin-aging endpoint was located.** The spermidine-eugenol combo's human rationale is **immune/cellular** (the Truzzi line), and even that is supplement-design/in-vitro, not a completed human skin RCT. [1][2]
- For context, **spermidine** (the partner molecule) has the stronger human longevity literature (oral; lifespan/autophagy "anti-aging vitamin" framing) and a small oral hair-anagen trial — **but that is spermidine, not eugenol, and not topical skin.** Do not let spermidine's reputation launder eugenol's. [5][12]
- **Eugenol oral pharmacokinetics (human):** rapidly absorbed and **near-completely metabolized** via glucuronide/sulfate conjugation, >55% excreted in urine within 24 h, plasma t½ ≈ 14 h, **<0.1% excreted unchanged** — i.e., as an oral agent it's a transient, heavily first-pass-metabolized molecule. [15]

**Verdict on Tier 3:** Empty for skin. There is no human oral skin-aging evidence for eugenol.

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the only tier that matters for a skincare SKU)

- **None.** I found **no human topical clinical study of eugenol for skin aging, wrinkles, barrier, or pigmentation.** The human topical literature on eugenol is essentially **dermatology/allergy testing** — patch tests and ROAT studies characterizing it as a **contact allergen** (see §7), the opposite of an efficacy file. [19][20][21]
- The only human-use data that exists is **safety/sensitization**, and it is unfavorable: eugenol is a **labeled fragrance allergen** with documented positive patch tests. [19][20][21][22]

**Verdict on Tier 4:** This is the dispositive gap. **For the format you would actually sell (a topical), eugenol has zero human efficacy evidence and a real human *adverse*-event (sensitization) file.**

**Explicit borrowed-vs-proven map:**
- *Proven for topical human use:* **nothing** (efficacy). The only human-topical facts are *negative* (sensitization).
- *Shown in mice ORALLY (do NOT claim for a cream):* wrinkle/collagen/barrier/microbiome benefits.
- *Shown in vitro (cells, mostly non-skin; do NOT claim as consumer outcomes):* autophagy co-induction, ROS scavenging, MMP-1↓, collagen↑.
- *Borrowed from spermidine (a different molecule):* "autophagy anti-aging vitamin," hair, lifespan.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Penetration is not the problem — it's almost too good.** Eugenol is a small (MW 164), lipophilic phenol and a **recognized stratum-corneum penetration enhancer** (≈3× flux for co-applied drugs at 1%; mechanism = SC lipid fluidization). So a topical eugenol *would* enter skin and could ferry other actives in. [4][9]
- **But:** (a) it's **volatile and odor-active**, so it evaporates/disperses and reads as fragrance; (b) good penetration of a *sensitizer* means **more allergen reaching Langerhans cells** — penetration enhancement and sensitization risk rise together; (c) the one efficacy study (Tong 2024) explicitly found benefit **without** eugenol accumulating in skin (oral, ~0.06 µmol/kg), so there's no validated topical dose–response to target. [3][4]
- **No topical skin-aging Franz-cell-to-efficacy study exists for eugenol.** Delivered-dose-to-fibroblast for an *anti-aging* outcome is **[unverified]**.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration (topical, anti-aging):** **Unknown / unestablished** — no topical efficacy study to anchor it. The in-vitro skin work used **50 µmol/L (~0.0008%) in media** (not a topical dose), and the mouse benefit came from **oral** dosing. Any topical % would be a guess, **capped by allergen regulation, not by efficacy data.**
- **Regulatory concentration ceiling (the binding constraint):** **IFRA (Amendment 49/51) restricts eugenol** by product category; for leave-on skin, category limits cited are roughly **~1.7% (body, Cat 5A) and ~0.64% (face, Cat 5B)**, ~2.5% for fine fragrance (Cat 4). These are **safety ceilings for fragrance use, not efficacy doses.** RIFM weight-of-evidence **NESIL = 5900 µg/cm²** (weak sensitizer). [22][23][24]
- **EU mandatory allergen labeling:** must be declared on-pack as **"Eugenol"** when >**0.001% in leave-on** / >0.01% in rinse-off (Reg. (EC) 1223/2009 Annex III; the 2023/1545 amendment keeps eugenol on the labeled-allergen list; compliance for the expanded list by **31 July 2026** / sell-through to 2028). **Any cosmetically/biologically meaningful level is far above the label threshold** — so it will appear in the allergen line. [25][26]
- **pH:** As a phenol it's most stable nearer neutral-to-mildly-acidic; the phenolic –OH can ionize/oxidize at higher pH. **No published optimal cosmetic-pH window for an anti-aging eugenol product — confirm with supplier TDS.** **[unverified]**
- **Stability:** **Oxidation-prone** (phenol + allyl group); air/light exposure forms colored oxidation/dimerization products and can *increase* sensitizing potency over time (a documented pattern for oxidizable fragrance terpenoids). Needs antioxidant protection (it partly protects itself but also self-oxidizes), opaque/airless packaging, low headspace. Volatile → can be lost during hot processing and contributes scent. [13][17]
- **Pairings:**
  - *Spermidine* — the on-thesis pairing (the whole "co-active" rationale); mechanistically complementary for autophagy, but the combo's skin benefit is unproven topically. [1][2]
  - *Antioxidant systems (vit E/ferulic)* — could buffer eugenol's pro-oxidant/oxidation tendency.
- **Conflicts / cautions:** Its **penetration-enhancing** property means it can amplify the irritancy/penetration of other actives (acids, retinoids) — a formulation hazard, not a feature, in a leave-on. Avoid stacking with other fragrance allergens that compound the total sensitizer load (fragrance-mix synergy). [4][22]
- **Sensory:** Strong, warm, spicy-clove odor and a **slight oral-numbing/warming** character (eugenol is the classic dental "clove" analgesic). Hard to make sensory-neutral; reads unmistakably as fragrance.

## 7. Safety

- **Skin sensitization — the central safety issue.** Eugenol is a **clinically established contact allergen / weak skin sensitizer** and a member of **Fragrance Mix I** used in baseline patch-test screening. It behaves as a **prohapten/prehapten** (becomes allergenic after oxidation/metabolic activation). RIFM weight-of-evidence: **sensitizer, WoE NESIL 5900 µg/cm².** [19][22][24]
- **Patch-test / clinical data:** In a dilution-series study, **~62% of eugenol-allergic subjects (21/34) were patch-test positive**; a ROAT study explored elicitation thresholds across dilutions. Positive-patch patients are advised to **avoid eugenol-containing perfumed products.** (Nuance: one ROAT study struggled to *elicit* dermatitis at low use concentrations in already-sensitized subjects — induction risk and elicitation threshold differ — but the *induction*/labeling concern stands.) [19][20][21]
- **Pro-oxidant / cytotoxic at higher doses** (§2, §4): antioxidant low, pro-oxidant/ROS-generating and cytotoxic above ~60 µg/mL in cell models — argues against high-load formulation. [13][14]
- **Sun:** RIFM assessed eugenol as **not phototoxic/photoallergenic** at relevant levels — it is *not* a photosensitizer (distinct from its baseline sensitization). [22]
- **Pregnancy/lactation:** **Culinary clove = fine; concentrated clove oil/eugenol is cautioned in pregnancy** (theoretical uterine-stimulant and bleeding/anticoagulant concerns; limited data). For a leave-on cosmetic at trace fragrance levels the systemic exposure is low, but **default to cautious "consult your doctor" language** and avoid marketing it as a pregnancy-safe active. **[partly unverified]** [27][28]
- **Oral safety / GRAS:** **FDA GRAS** as a food flavoring (typically <0.5% food use); EPA exempted from tolerance as a biopesticide inert/active in some uses. **GRAS pertains to ingestion, not a topical-cosmetic sensitization determination.** [27][29]
- **Methyleugenol confusion (important):** the related **methyleugenol** is a **genotoxic rodent carcinogen, restricted/effectively banned in EU cosmetics** — eugenol is *not* methyleugenol, but trace methyleugenol can co-occur in natural clove material; **specify low-methyleugenol grade.** [17][18]
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** Clove-derived (Indonesia/Madagascar/Comoros supply, agricultural price volatility) or synthetic; high-purity grades available. No major sustainability red flag beyond ordinary essential-oil supply variability; **synthetic/redistilled grade gives better purity (lower methyleugenol/isoeugenol).** [16][29]

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic vs drug line:** Eugenol in a topical product is a **cosmetic fragrance ingredient.** Claiming it **treats/prevents disease or alters skin structure/function** ("induces autophagy in your skin," "boosts collagen," "reverses photoaging") would push toward **drug** territory (US FDA) and is **unsupported by any human topical data** — a double problem (claims discipline *and* no substantiation).
- **The defining regulatory facts:**
  1. **Mandatory allergen declaration** as "Eugenol" above 0.001% leave-on (EU; UK mirrors). Any meaningful dose is disclosed on-pack as an allergen — at odds with a "clean/sensitive-skin" positioning. [25][26]
  2. **IFRA concentration caps** (leave-on face ~0.64%, body ~1.7%) bound usage for safety, **below** any demonstrated efficacy window (which doesn't exist anyway). [22][23][24]
- **Substantiation problem specific to eugenol:** efficacy claims would rest on **oral-mouse + in-vitro non-skin** data — **no human, no topical.** Borrowing the Tong 2024 *oral* mouse anti-photoaging results or spermidine's reputation to sell a *cream* would be **misleading** and a clear claims-discipline violation.
- **Safer cosmetic phrasing (✅, if used at all):** treat eugenol as what it is — a **fragrance/aroma component** ("naturally derived clove aroma") — *not* as a hero active. Do **not** make autophagy/anti-aging efficacy claims.
- **Avoid / flag for legal (⚠️):** "activates autophagy," "cellular renewal," "boosts collagen," "anti-photoaging," "spermidine co-active that rejuvenates skin," "longevity active." All unsupported for topical human use.
- See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md) claim cheat-sheet.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Form to buy:** High-purity (≥98–99%) eugenol, ex-clove-oil or synthetic, from fragrance/flavor and chemical suppliers; **specify low-methyleugenol, low-isoeugenol grade** and a current allergen/COA. [16][29]
- **Cost tier:** **Low.** Bulk eugenol is a commodity aroma chemical — roughly **~$25–60/kg** (food/fragrance vs pharma grade), regionally variable. Cost is **not** a barrier; the barriers are **regulatory caps and sensitization**, not price. [16]
- **Strategic note:** Because it's cheap, ubiquitous, and primarily known as an *allergen*, eugenol carries **no premium-ingredient halo** and offers **no defensible story** for a science-forward brand — unlike spermidine, urolithin A, or a branded autophagy active.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **Hype:** "Clove-derived autophagy activator," "spermidine co-active for skin longevity," "natural anti-aging phenol." This **conflates** (a) in-vitro autophagy in non-skin cells, (b) *oral*-mouse anti-photoaging, and (c) spermidine's separate reputation — none of which is topical human skin evidence.
- **Reality check #1:** The autophagy data is **in-vitro and largely tested as a combo with spermidine**, in neuro/endothelial/epithelial/intestinal cells — **not skin.** [1][2]
- **Reality check #2:** The flagship anti-photoaging study is **dietary (oral) in mice**, and the paper itself shows **almost no eugenol reaches the skin** (~0.06 µmol/kg), attributing benefit to systemic/microbiome effects — i.e., it argues *against* a topical product, not for one. [3]
- **Reality check #3:** **No human topical efficacy study exists.** The human-topical literature is about eugenol being an **allergen.** [19][20][21]
- **Reality check #4:** Eugenol is **dual antioxidant/pro-oxidant** — at higher doses it generates ROS and is cytotoxic, the opposite of an anti-aging effect. [13][14]
- **What's genuinely real:** It's a competent antioxidant/anti-inflammatory phenol with a coherent (if non-skin) autophagy signal and a strong *oral* mechanistic mouse story — and it's a useful **penetration enhancer**. Those are real; they just don't add up to a topical skin-aging hero, and the sensitizer status is a hard liability.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**❌ SKIP as a standalone/hero anti-aging active · ⚠️ CONDITIONAL only as a deliberate, declared fragrance/penetration micro-additive.**

One line: *Mechanistically intriguing as a spermidine autophagy co-active, but for a topical skincare product eugenol has **zero human topical efficacy evidence**, its best anti-aging data is **oral mouse**, and its dominant real-world cosmetic identity is a **regulated, labeled fragrance allergen** — so it can carry no honest anti-aging claim and actively conflicts with a sensitive-skin/barrier-repair, clean-label positioning.*

- **Why skip as a hero:** (1) No human topical data. (2) The impressive evidence is the wrong format (oral) and explicitly not skin-delivered. (3) It's a sensitizer with mandatory allergen labeling and IFRA caps — wrong for a barrier-repair brand whose thesis is *not irritating the skin*. (4) No premium/defensible story; cheap commodity aroma chemical.
- **The only defensible roles (conditional):**
  - As a **declared fragrance/aroma component** at trace levels (with the on-pack "Eugenol" allergen line accepted), *if* the brand ever wants a warm-spice scent — but a barrier/sensitive-skin brand should probably **avoid even this**.
  - As a **penetration enhancer / co-solvent** in a delivery system — but safer, non-allergen enhancers exist; not worth the sensitizer trade-off.
- **Promote consideration only if** (a) **independent human topical** efficacy data emerges, **and** (b) the brand's positioning tolerates a labeled fragrance allergen — both unlikely.
- **Longevity relevance:** **Conceptually on-theme** (autophagy is a core hallmark of aging, and the spermidine-co-active framing is fashionable), **but practically misaligned** — the longevity action is demonstrated *systemically/orally*, not topically, and the topical liability (sensitization) is exactly what a skin-longevity/barrier brand should minimize. The better way to play "autophagy" topically is **spermidine itself** (and to watch branded autophagy actives), not eugenol.

Log under [`../../ideas/not-considering.md`](../../ideas/not-considering.md) as **rejected as a topical anti-aging active** (reason: no human topical efficacy; regulated fragrance allergen; best data is oral). Keep a pointer from any "autophagy/spermidine" research thread.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

_Verification pass: 2026-06-19. Method: each headline study in §4 checked for (a) existence, (b) result-vs-source match, (c) HUMAN+TOPICAL vs borrowed (in-vitro/animal/oral), (d) funding/size/independence. **Second, independent re-verification 2026-06-19** via direct source fetch of the PMC full texts ([1] PMC9182079, [2] PMC9964041, [3] PMC10886361) and the RIFM/Elsevier safety record — not a desk re-read. All three core studies and the allergen/RIFM facts confirmed at source; nothing required softening._

### Overall evidence grade for TOPICAL skin efficacy: **D (essentially unproven for topical; mechanism-only)**

Rationale: The tier that matters for a cream — **human topical** — is **empty of efficacy** and, uniquely among the brand's longevity actives, the human-topical record that *does* exist is **adverse** (contact sensitization). The best efficacy data ([3]) is **oral mouse, single study, single-sex, sponsor-clean but unreplicated, and self-reportedly not skin-delivered**. The autophagy rationale ([1][2]) is **in-vitro, non-skin, combo-tested**. This sits below urolithin A's "C" (which at least has a human topical preprint): eugenol has **no human topical efficacy data at all.** Grade D is appropriate — not "F/hype-only," because the oral-mouse multi-omics and in-vitro autophagy work are real and directionally coherent, but they cannot support topical claims.

### What verified out cleanly
- **Truzzi 2022 [1]** — REAL (confirmed at PMC9182079). SPD 0.3 mM + EUG 0.2 mM ↑LC3-II across SH-SY5Y/HUVEC/HBEpiC/Caco-2/L929/U937; antiviral/immune (COVID-era) framing. **No human/dermal skin cells** — note L929 is a *murine connective-tissue fibroblast* line, not a skin/dermal-fibroblast model, so the "no skin cells" tiering holds. Correctly tiered in-vitro/non-skin.
- **Truzzi 2023 [2]** — REAL. Intestinal cells; LC3-II⁺ ~80% vs ~40%; eugenol not isolated. Correctly tiered.
- **Tong 2024 [3]** — REAL. **Dietary/oral** SKH-1 mice (n=21, 7/group), 0.05% w/w; collagen/MMP/barrier/microbiome/wrinkle results match; **skin accumulation ~0.06 µmol/kg** confirmed in source. Correctly flagged as oral, not topical, and not skin-delivered.
- **Eugenol = Fragrance Mix I allergen / IFRA-restricted / RIFM weak sensitizer (NESIL 5900 µg/cm²) [19][22][23]** — REAL and consistent across RIFM, IFRA, EU sources.
- **EU labeling 0.001% leave-on; 2023/1545 retains eugenol [25][26]** — REAL.
- **Dual antioxidant/pro-oxidant [13][14]** — REAL; concentration-dependent flip well documented.
- **Oral PK (t½ ≈14 h; heavy glucuronide/sulfate; <0.1% unchanged) [15]** — REAL.

### Caveats / soft spots in the file
1. **Eugenol's *independent* autophagy mechanism is under-characterized.** The EP300 mechanism [6] is established for *spermidine*; eugenol is best described as co-/potentiating. The file says this — hold the line; don't upgrade eugenol to a defined autophagy inducer.
2. **[3] funding/COI:** declared none; single academic group; **unreplicated.** Do not present as settled.
3. **Pregnancy [28]** rests partly on consumer-grade sourcing — marked **[partly unverified]**; the uterine/anticoagulant concern is for *concentrated* clove, not trace cosmetic fragrance. Keep cautious, not alarmist.
4. **IFRA category numbers [23]** were obtained via secondary summaries; the *exact* current-amendment % per category should be confirmed against the official IFRA Standard sheet before any formulation. **[verify before formulating]**
5. **Isoeugenol vs eugenol vs methyleugenol** must never be conflated — isoeugenol is a stronger sensitizer; methyleugenol is a restricted genotoxic carcinogen. The file separates them; preserve that.

### Claims that must stay boxed
- ❌ "Activates autophagy in skin," "spermidine co-active that renews skin," "anti-photoaging," "boosts collagen" → **no human topical support**; oral-mouse and in-vitro only.
- ❌ Importing spermidine's or Tong 2024's *oral* results onto a topical label → claims-discipline violation.
- ⚠️ If used at all, position eugenol as a **fragrance/aroma component** (declared allergen), not a hero active.

### Bottom line
The dossier is **honest and correctly skeptical**: it refuses to let oral-mouse or in-vitro data sell a cream and foregrounds the sensitizer/allergen liability. **Independent source-fetch verification (2026-06-19) found no overstated claims to soften** — the file's framing already matches what the sources say (route of administration, non-skin cell lines, ~0.06 µmol/kg skin accumulation, funding/COI, RIFM/IFRA/EU allergen status all confirmed at source). The grade-D-vs-urolithin-A-C comparison is also accurate (urolithin-a.md is independently graded C on the strength of one human topical preprint; eugenol has zero human topical efficacy data). Topical-efficacy grade: **D**. Recommendation stands: **skip as a topical anti-aging active**; at most a deliberately declared fragrance/penetration micro-additive a barrier-repair brand should probably still avoid.
