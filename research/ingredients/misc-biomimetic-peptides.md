# Ingredient: Misc / Long-Tail Biomimetic Peptides (basket)

**Slug:** misc-biomimetic-peptides · **Class:** Peptides — signal · carrier · enzyme-inhibitor · neuro-inhibitor
**Researched:** 2026-06-19 (exhaustive) · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product

> **How to read this dossier.** This is not one ingredient — it is a **basket of ~11 low-frequency ("long-tail") branded peptides** that appear on the master taxonomy as a catch-all so nothing falls through the cracks. Each is profiled individually, but the verdict is collective. The single most important honesty point up front: **for almost every peptide in this basket, the entire efficacy case is supplier-generated, in-vitro, and unpublished in independent peer-reviewed journals.** The one genuine exception lineage is **Pentapeptide-4 / KTTKS** (palmitoylated form Pal-KTTKS, "Matrixyl"), which has a real independent human RCT (Robinson 2005, n=93) — and even there the proven molecule is the *palmitoylated* version, not bare Pentapeptide-4. Treat this whole group as **mechanistically interesting story-telling actives, not proof-backed heroes.** Two members (Trifluoroacetyl **Tripeptide-2** / Progeline and **Acetyl Cyclohexapeptide-34**) already have, or belong in, dedicated handling — see notes.

## 2. Class & mechanism

By standard cosmetic-peptide taxonomy (signal / carrier / neurotransmitter-inhibiting / enzyme-inhibiting), the basket spans **three of the four classes** (no true neurotransmitter-inhibitor here — those live in the `argireline-neuropeptides` / `neuro-inhibitor-peptides` dossiers):

- **Signal (matrikine) peptides** — fragments of ECM proteins that "trick" fibroblasts into sensing matrix turnover and up-regulating collagen/elastin/HA/fibronectin. Members: **Pentapeptide-4 (KTTKS)** [pro-collagen I fragment], **Tripeptide-29 (Gly-Pro-Hyp)** [collagen-mimetic], **Tripeptide-38 / Pal-Tripeptide-38** [Matrixyl synthe'6 — multi-ECM], **Acetyl Tetrapeptide-17** [collagen/"dermal filling"], **Elaidoyl Tripeptide-37 (Lipospondin)** [activates latent TGF-β1, a master pro-fibrotic/collagen signal].
- **Signal — stress/chronobiology peptides** — a sub-family that modulates cellular *defense programs* rather than ECM directly. Members: **Acetyl Tetrapeptide-22 (Thermostressine)** [induces HSP70/Hsp72 chaperones → protein-damage/stress tolerance], **Tetrapeptide-26 (Chronogen)** and **Tripeptide-32 (Chronolux)** [up-regulate circadian clock genes CLOCK/BMAL1/PER1 in keratinocytes → daytime UV-defense + nighttime DNA-repair framing].
- **Enzyme-inhibitor peptides** — block matrix-degrading proteases. Members: **Elaidoyl Tripeptide-37** [elaidic-acid moiety inhibits MMP-1/-2/-3/-9], **Tripeptide-2 (Progeline)** [elastase inhibitor / elafin mimic — see its own dossier].
- **Carrier / "targeting" peptides** — designed to *deliver or home* actives, not to act on collagen themselves. Members: **Heptapeptide-15 Palmitate** [marketed as a fibroblast-receptor-seeking delivery peptide], and **Acetyl Cyclohexapeptide-34** [adipocyte-targeting lipopeptide in X50 Silhouette].
- **Poorly-characterized / supplier-defined** — **Hexapeptide-48 HCl**: marketed as a receptor-acting "anti-wrinkle"/skin-conditioning peptide, but with essentially **no public sequence or mechanism** and no independent literature. This is the weakest-defined member.

**Honest mechanistic read:** The matrikine logic (signal peptides) and the protease-inhibition logic (enzyme-inhibitors) are *biologically real and well-precedented as concepts* — the matrikine field is anchored by the KTTKS literature. The chronobiology and HSP70 angles are clever and mechanistically plausible *in cells*, but their translation to visible topical benefit in living human skin is essentially unproven outside supplier data. The carrier peptides' "homing" claims are marketing constructs with no public penetration/biodistribution data.

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **Reading guide.** A benefit is only "proven for topical human use" if it lives in **Tier 4 (human topical), ideally RCT.** As you'll see, that tier is **nearly empty** for this basket — the lone robust resident is **Pal-Pentapeptide-4**. Everything else is mechanism (in-vitro / biochemical), supplier-internal, or borrowed.

### Tier 1 — In vitro / biochemical (where almost all of this basket lives)

- **Elaidoyl Tripeptide-37 (Lipospondin) — Cauchard JH, Berton A, Godeau G, Hornebeck W, Bellon G (2004), *Biochemical Pharmacology* 67(11):2013–22.** [1] The best *primary, peer-reviewed* paper for any enzyme-inhibitor in this basket. Elaidyl-KFK **activated latent TGF-β1** (solid-phase assay with immobilized LAP-TGF-β; up to ~10% of added latent TGF-β recovered as active form) **and inhibited MMP activity** via the elaidic-acid moiety, using human dermal fibroblast cultures and skin sections. *Limitation: in-vitro / ex-vivo only — **no human in-vivo testing**; a "promising model compound," in the authors' own words.*
- **Pentapeptide-4 (KTTKS) — foundational matrikine biology.** KTTKS is the C-terminal fragment of the pro-collagen I C-propeptide; in fibroblast culture it up-regulates collagen I, III and fibronectin. *This is the mechanistic bedrock the entire signal-peptide category leans on.* (Reviewed across the peptide literature; see [4][5].) *Limitation: the bare peptide is ionic and does not cross the stratum corneum without help (§5) — hence palmitoylation.*
- **Tripeptide-29 (Gly-Pro-Hyp).** Collagen-derived tripeptide reported to stimulate fibroblast collagen synthesis and dermal–epidermal-junction support in cell/absorption studies; supplier cites Gly-Pro-Hyp recognition/photoaging studies. [9] *Limitation: mechanistic / absorption studies, not finished-product human efficacy; much of the Gly-Pro-Hyp literature is for **oral** collagen peptides, not topical.*
- **Acetyl Tetrapeptide-22 (Thermostressine).** Screened from a 331,776-tetrapeptide combinatorial library as an Hsp72 promoter inducer; **in-vitro** it ↑HSP70 in human keratinocytes and protected cells against UVB, heat, cold and dehydration. [6][10] *Limitation: in-vitro keratinocyte data; supplier-driven; no independent human topical RCT located.*
- **Tetrapeptide-26 (Chronogen) & Tripeptide-32 (Chronolux).** Up-regulate circadian clock genes (CLOCK, BMAL1, PER1) in keratinocytes → framed as daytime UV-defense + nighttime DNA-repair. [4][11][12] *Limitation: gene-expression rationale (SPLQ motif tied to tau/circadian biology); no published, independent human clinical efficacy located.*
- **Acetyl Tetrapeptide-17.** Marketed as activating collagen synthesis / "dermal filling, repair and renewal." [7] *Limitation: supplier mechanism; no independent peer-reviewed human data located.*
- **Hexapeptide-48 HCl.** Marketed receptor-acting "anti-wrinkle"/skin-conditioning peptide. [8] *Limitation: **essentially no public sequence, mechanism, or study** — the least-substantiated member.*
- **Heptapeptide-15 Palmitate.** Marketed as a fibroblast-receptor-"seeking" delivery/targeting peptide for encapsulated actives. [13] *Limitation: no published sequence, no penetration/biodistribution data, no efficacy study — a marketing construct.*
- **Acetyl Cyclohexapeptide-34.** Adipocyte-targeting lipopeptide in the X50 Silhouette body complex. [14] *Limitation: body-contouring rationale; not a facial anti-aging active; no independent facial-skin data.*

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)
- **None located** specific to these peptides as topical skin actives. (The matrikine/TGF-β/MMP biology is supported in broader animal wound/fibrosis literature, but no member here has a clean, attributable in-vivo animal skin-aging study in the public record.)

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL
- **Not applicable** as a topical-claim source. Note one trap: **Tripeptide-29 / Gly-Pro-Hyp** shares its tripeptide identity with the dominant **collagen-peptide oral supplement** literature (oral hydrolyzed collagen does show skin-hydration/elasticity benefits in RCTs and meta-analyses). That oral evidence **does not transfer** to a topical Tripeptide-29 claim — flag any copy that conflates them.

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters — and it's nearly empty)

- **Pal-Pentapeptide-4 (Matrixyl) — Robinson LR et al. (2005), *International Journal of Cosmetic Science* 27(3):155–160. DOI: 10.1111/j.1467-2494.2005.00261.x.** [2] **The one strong, independent-ish human RCT in this entire basket.** 12-week, **double-blind, placebo (vehicle)-controlled, split-face, randomized**, **n = 93** Caucasian women (35–55). Moisturizer + **3 ppm Pal-KTTKS** vs the same moisturizer alone: significant reduction in wrinkles/fine lines by quantitative image analysis and expert grading, well tolerated, no irritation. *Critical caveats: (a) the active is the **palmitoylated** Pal-Pentapeptide-4, **not the bare Pentapeptide-4** named in this basket; (b) effect sizes are modest and the formula contained other moisturizing actives; (c) industry-affiliated (P&G).* A companion paper (Lupo/Cole context, *JAAD* 2004) reported a moisturizer with palmitoyl pentapeptide improved appearance of aging skin. [3]
- **Palmitoyl Tripeptide-38 (Matrixyl synthe'6).** Supplier (Sederma) reports human-panel improvements in wrinkle depth/volume (e.g., crow's feet, nasolabial) over ~2 months. *Limitation: **supplier-internal**, not independently published RCTs; again, the studied molecule is the **palmitoylated** Tripeptide-38, not bare Tripeptide-38.*
- **All other basket members (Tetrapeptide-26, Tripeptide-32, Tripeptide-29, Acetyl Tetrapeptide-17, Acetyl Tetrapeptide-22, Hexapeptide-48, Heptapeptide-15 Palmitate, Elaidoyl Tripeptide-37, Acetyl Cyclohexapeptide-34):** **no independent, peer-reviewed, placebo-controlled human topical RCT located** as of 2026-06-19. Any human efficacy claims rest on **supplier marketing dossiers** (mostly small, in-house, often single-arm or vehicle-only).

**Context from the broader field:** Recent systematic reviews/meta-analyses of topical + oral peptides (e.g., 2024–2025 meta-analyses pooling ~16–19 RCTs, ~1,300 subjects) find peptides *as a class* can modestly improve hydration and wrinkle depth, **but repeatedly flag** non-standardized endpoints, small samples, short durations, lack of histology, and **the dominance of industry funding** — and note that **skin permeability is a "dismissed necessity"** for anti-wrinkle peptide performance. [15] None of those pooled RCTs rehabilitate the *specific* long-tail peptides in this basket; the meta-analytic signal is carried by a few well-studied peptides (KTTKS lineage, copper-GHK, argireline), not the obscure ones here.

**Bottom line by tier:** Proven for topical human use → **Pal-Pentapeptide-4 only** (modest, industry RCT). Mechanistically credible but human-topical-unproven → the signal/enzyme-inhibitor peptides (Lipospondin, Tripeptide-29/-38, Acetyl Tetrapeptide-17/-22, circadian peptides). Essentially unsubstantiated → **Hexapeptide-48 HCl, Heptapeptide-15 Palmitate**. Out-of-scope (body) → **Acetyl Cyclohexapeptide-34**.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **The central problem:** Most of these peptides are **hydrophilic and charged**, and the stratum corneum is a lipophilic barrier. The classic "500-Dalton rule" and the well-documented poor permeation of **bare KTTKS** are the textbook case: Pentapeptide-4 (KTTKS) is too polar to cross intact skin meaningfully **without help** — which is exactly why the **palmitoyl** version was created (the C16 lipid tail boosts lipophilicity and SC partitioning). [2][15]
- **Lipidation is the workaround** used by the efficacious members: **Pal-**Pentapeptide-4, **Pal-**Tripeptide-38, **Elaidoyl** Tripeptide-37 (elaidic acid), **Heptapeptide-15 Palmitate**, **Acetyl Cyclohexapeptide-34** are all fatty-acid-conjugated to improve penetration. Acetylation (Acetyl Tetrapeptide-17/-22) is a milder modification mainly for stability/charge, with weaker penetration benefit.
- **Even with lipidation, depth is limited.** CIR-reviewed in-vitro data on a related palmitoyl peptide (palmitoyl carnosine) showed it **reached epidermis and dermis but did not pass beyond the dermis** (none in receptor fluid) — i.e., it can reach fibroblast territory but is not systemically absorbed. [16] That's the optimistic read for the lipidated members.
- **No public penetration data** exist for several members (Hexapeptide-48 HCl, Tetrapeptide-26, Tripeptide-32, Acetyl Tetrapeptide-17/-22 in finished formulas) — penetration is *assumed*, not demonstrated.
- **Practical read:** For the lipidated signal/enzyme-inhibitor peptides, penetration to viable epidermis/upper dermis is **plausible**; for the bare and the poorly-characterized peptides it is **plausible-to-doubtful and unverified**. Delivery system (liposomes, encapsulation) and the lipid tail matter more than the headline INCI name.

## 6. Formulation

- **Typical use levels:** These are **active at parts-per-million to low-percent of the *trade dilution***. Trade ingredients are pre-diluted (often 50–1000 ppm peptide in a glycerin/water/butylene-glycol carrier), used at ~1–5% of formula. Example anchors: Pal-KTTKS efficacy shown at **3 ppm** finished [2]; Tripeptide-29 cited at 0.1–1.0% of the trade material [9]. **Do not imply a validated dose-response** for the unstudied members.
- **pH:** Most peptides are happiest **mildly acidic to neutral (~5–6.5)**. Avoid strongly acidic (L-ascorbic acid, high-% AHA) or strongly alkaline environments that can hydrolyze peptide bonds or alter charge. The HCl salt form (Hexapeptide-48 HCl) is supplied to ease handling/solubility.
- **Stability:** Peptide bonds are vulnerable to hydrolysis, oxidation, and microbial/enzymatic attack. Use airless/opaque packaging, robust preservation, and **avoid co-formulating raw peptides with proteolytic-enzyme actives or high heat**. Lipidated peptides are somewhat more robust. Run finished-formula stability + challenge testing — public stability data are thin.
- **Pairings (conceptual, brand-thesis-aligned):** Niacinamide (barrier/B3), hyaluronic acid (hydration vehicle commonly co-formulated), ceramides (barrier thesis), copper-GHK and the better-studied matrikines (peptide stacking is conventional). Circadian peptides (Chronogen/Chronolux) and HSP peptide (Thermostressine) fit a **"day-defense / night-repair"** narrative pairing with antioxidants (day) and repair actives (night).
- **Conflicts:** Strong acids/bases; high concentrations of direct acids in the same phase; possible incompatibility of charged peptides with anionic/cationic systems. Test peptide blends together, not assume additivity.
- **Sensory:** At in-use levels, generally odorless/colorless and texture-neutral; trade carriers (glycerin/glycol) add slight slip. No notable sensory penalty.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation / sensitization:** Cosmetic peptides as a class have a **favorable** irritation/sensitization profile. The **CIR (Cosmetic Ingredient Review)** safety assessments of **palmitoyl oligopeptides / pentapeptides / tripeptide-1 & hexapeptide-12** concluded they are **safe in present practices of use and concentration**; the Pal-KTTKS RCT (n=93) reported no irritation, and an HRIPT on 53 volunteers found a palmitoyl peptide non-irritating/non-sensitizing. [16][17] Low use levels (ppm) further limit risk.
- **The data-gap caveat:** CIR repeatedly notes **published acute/repeat-dose and reproductive/developmental toxicity data are largely absent** for many of these peptides — safety is inferred from low exposure, peptide chemistry (digested to amino acids), and clean clinical-use records, **not** from comprehensive tox dossiers. The **long-tail members (Hexapeptide-48, Tetrapeptide-26, Acetyl Tetrapeptide-17/-22, Heptapeptide-15 Palmitate)** have especially thin public safety data — generate our own patch/HRIPT on the finished formula. [16]
- **Sun sensitivity:** No photosensitization flag; several members are *framed* as photoprotective (circadian, HSP70, KTTKS in photoaged skin). None require sun avoidance, but none replace SPF.
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** No specific reproductive-toxicity data located for these peptides (a CIR-noted gap). [16] Peptides are generally considered low-risk topically (large/charged, minimal systemic absorption — palmitoyl carnosine didn't pass beyond dermis [16]), but with **no positive safety data**, default to conservative copy ("if pregnant or nursing, consult your doctor").
- **Sourcing/allergen:** Synthetic (solid-phase peptide synthesis) — no animal/plant allergen by default; confirm carrier excipients (some trade dilutions use wheat/soy hydrolysates in *blends* — not these specific peptides, but verify the COA of any blended trade material).
- **Acetyl Cyclohexapeptide-34 specific:** an adipocyte-active lipopeptide intended for body/contouring use; no facial-skin safety rationale established — another reason to keep it out of a face product.

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic ingredient status:** All listed as INCI names with CosIng entries and "skin conditioning" (or "skin protecting") functions — **cosmetic ingredients, not approved drugs.** Confirm legality/registration per target market and obtain trade-material COAs and the supplier's own claim-substantiation file before relying on any benefit.
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** This basket is a **minefield** for structure/function (drug) claims because the supplier marketing leans hard on biology: "activates collagen," "boosts collagen by X%," "repairs DNA," "induces heat-shock proteins," "reduces progerin / reverses cellular aging," "stimulates TGF-β." Each of these can read as a **drug claim** and/or requires substantiation we do not possess for the specific peptide/format.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "biomimetic peptide that supports the look of firmer, smoother skin," "helps improve the appearance of fine lines," "inspired by the skin's own repair signals," "supports a visibly more resilient, even-toned complexion," "part of a day-defense / night-renewal routine" (framed as concept, not measured outcome).
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation or legal review:** any **% efficacy** number, "boosts/builds collagen," "repairs DNA," "resets your skin's clock," "reverses aging at the cellular level," "induces heat-shock proteins in your skin," "activates TGF-β." For all but Pal-Pentapeptide-4, even modest performance claims should be **backed by our own finished-formula human testing**, not the literature.
- **Source-honesty rule for copy:** Because Tripeptide-29 (Gly-Pro-Hyp) shares an identity with **oral** collagen-peptide science, never let copy import oral-supplement RCT results into a topical claim. Likewise, never present supplier in-house data as if it were independent peer-reviewed evidence. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Forms/suppliers:** Premium trade ingredients from **Sederma/IFF** (KTTKS/Matrixyl, Pal-Tripeptide-38/synthe'6, Progeline, X50 Silhouette), **Lipotec/Lubrizol** (Chronogen, Chronolux, Thermostressine), and a long tail of **Chinese fine-chemical / peptide houses** (Hexapeptide-48 HCl, Tripeptide-29, generic palmitoyl peptides) at lower cost. Research-grade powders are cheap; **cosmetic trade dilutions and branded complexes carry a large premium** (you pay for the dossier, the brand, and the carrier system).
- **Cost tier:** **Mid-to-high** at the branded-trade level (especially Sederma marquee actives); **low** for generic long-tail peptides from commodity suppliers (but then you inherit thinner data and QA risk).
- **Supply / QA risk:** Long-tail peptides from commodity suppliers vary in purity and identity — require **COA with sequence/identity confirmation, purity (HPLC), and peptide content** (trade dilutions are mostly carrier). For branded complexes, the **supplier's claim-substantiation pack is part of the value** — get it.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **"Studied, clinically proven peptide" — usually means supplier data.** For everything except Pal-Pentapeptide-4, "clinically tested" almost always = a small, in-house, supplier-run panel, not an independent peer-reviewed RCT. The marketing borrows the *credibility* of the matrikine field (built by KTTKS) and applies it to peptides that haven't earned it.
- **The bare-vs-palmitoylated bait-and-switch.** The basket names "Pentapeptide-4" and "Tripeptide-38," but the human/efficacy data are for the **palmitoylated** forms. Bare KTTKS barely penetrates skin [2][15]. A "Pentapeptide-4 serum" implying Matrixyl results is overreach unless it's actually Pal-Pentapeptide-4 at a meaningful level.
- **Oral-to-topical leap (Tripeptide-29/Gly-Pro-Hyp).** The strong RCT evidence for Gly-Pro-Hyp is for **ingested** collagen peptides; topical Tripeptide-29 has no comparable proof.
- **"Resets your circadian clock" / "repairs DNA at night" (Chronogen/Chronolux).** Elegant gene-expression story in keratinocyte culture; **no independent human visible-outcome proof**.
- **"Induces heat-shock proteins to protect your skin" (Thermostressine).** Real in-vitro HSP70 induction; **unproven** that a topical dose does this meaningfully in living human skin.
- **"Targeting/homing peptide finds your fibroblasts" (Heptapeptide-15 Palmitate).** A delivery-marketing construct with **no public penetration or efficacy data**.
- **Hexapeptide-48 HCl** is the clearest example of a peptide sold on **vibes** — an impressive-sounding INCI with essentially no public mechanism, sequence, or study.
- **Acetyl Cyclohexapeptide-34** is sometimes seen in *facial* "elasticity" products despite being an **adipocyte/body-contouring** peptide — a mismatch worth flagging.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **CONDITIONAL / SUPPORTING — strong storytelling, weak independent proof (cherry-pick, don't adopt the basket).**

**One-line:** As a *group* these are long-tail, supplier-substantiated peptides whose anti-aging case is mostly mechanism-and-marketing; the brand should **not** treat the basket as proven actives, but **may** selectively use one or two members as **science-narrative supporting ingredients** — with strictly hedged, mechanism-framed copy and our own finished-formula testing before any performance claim.

**Member-by-member recommendation:**
- **Pal-Pentapeptide-4 (KTTKS/Matrixyl) — SUPPORTING (the only "conditional-hero" candidate here).** Has the one real independent human RCT (Robinson 2005, n=93). The most defensible peptide in the basket. *(Note: it overlaps the dedicated matrikine/firming-peptide dossiers — coordinate there.)*
- **Palmitoyl Tripeptide-38 (Matrixyl synthe'6) — SUPPORTING/CONDITIONAL.** Credible multi-ECM matrikine with supplier human data; reasonable second peptide if we want a Sederma-grade firming story.
- **Elaidoyl Tripeptide-37 (Lipospondin) — CONDITIONAL.** Best *primary peer-reviewed mechanism* (TGF-β activation + MMP inhibition, Cauchard 2004) but **no human data** — good narrative active, not a claim-backer.
- **Tetrapeptide-26 / Tripeptide-32 / Acetyl Tetrapeptide-22 (circadian + HSP) — CONDITIONAL, narrative only.** Differentiated "chrono / stress-defense" story for a longevity brand, but human-topical-unproven; use only with concept-level copy.
- **Tripeptide-29, Acetyl Tetrapeptide-17 — CONDITIONAL, low priority.** Generic collagen-mimetic stories with thin independent data and (for Tripeptide-29) an oral-vs-topical confound.
- **Hexapeptide-48 HCl, Heptapeptide-15 Palmitate — SKIP.** Essentially unsubstantiated; no public mechanism or evidence to stand behind.
- **Acetyl Cyclohexapeptide-34 — SKIP (out of scope).** Body/adipocyte-contouring peptide, not a facial skin-longevity active.
- **Trifluoroacetyl Tripeptide-2 (Progeline) — see its own dossier** (`progeline-tfa-tripeptide-2.md`); same conditional verdict.

**Longevity-relevance note:** For a *skin-longevity* brand, the genuinely on-thesis sub-group is the **stress-defense / chronobiology peptides** (HSP70 induction, circadian DNA-repair framing) plus the **senescence-targeting Progeline** — these map to inflammaging, photoaging, and senescence hallmarks in a way that's narratively distinctive. But their evidence is *cell-level*, not human-topical, so they belong in the **education/differentiation** column, not the proof column. The brand's "evidence over hype" guardrail argues for a **lean peptide stack built around the few proven matrikines (KTTKS lineage, copper-GHK), with at most one or two long-tail peptides as story/support** — not a kitchen-sink "12-peptide complex," which is exactly the SKU-bloat-and-overclaim trap this dossier warns against.

Log in [`../../ideas/considering.md`](../../ideas/considering.md) (Pal-Pentapeptide-4, Pal-Tripeptide-38, Lipospondin, circadian/HSP peptides as supporting/narrative) and [`../../ideas/not-considering.md`](../../ideas/not-considering.md) (Hexapeptide-48 HCl, Heptapeptide-15 Palmitate, Acetyl Cyclohexapeptide-34 — with reasons).
