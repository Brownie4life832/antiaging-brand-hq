# Ingredient: Rosemary + actives (Carnosic acid / Rosmarinic acid) (INCI: Rosmarinus Officinalis (Rosemary) Leaf Extract; Salvia Rosmarinus Leaf Extract)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Botanicals & plant bioactives (phenolic-diterpene + polyphenol antioxidant; Nrf2/ARE activator)

> **One-line honest summary:** Rosemary is a genuinely strong, broad-spectrum *antioxidant* botanical — its lead actives (carnosic acid, carnosol, rosmarinic acid) have solid **in-vitro and animal** anti-photoaging, anti-MMP, Nrf2-activating and anti-glycation mechanisms. But the **human** clinical wins (↑MED, ↓lipid peroxidation, ↓wrinkles, ↓glycation, ↑elasticity) all come from **ORAL** supplements (Nutroxsun, BioR), and the most exciting recent topical-regeneration finding (TRPA1) is **mouse-only**. There is essentially **no human topical RCT** of a defined rosemary serum. Treat it as a credible **supporting antioxidant / formula-stabilizer**, not a proven topical hero. It also carries a real **allergic-contact-dermatitis / fragrance-allergen** caution that must be managed.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Rosemary (rosemary leaf extract). Botanical: *Rosmarinus officinalis* L., now formally reclassified as ***Salvia rosmarinus*** Spenn. (Lamiaceae / mint family).
- **INCI names (the labels you'll actually see/use):**
  - **Rosmarinus Officinalis (Rosemary) Leaf Extract** (the dominant water/glycol/ethanol leaf extract)
  - **Salvia Rosmarinus Leaf Extract** (newer botanically-correct INCI; same plant)
  - **Rosmarinus Officinalis (Rosemary) Leaf Oil** (the *essential oil* — a different, more allergenic material; do not conflate with the polyphenol extract)
  - **Rosmarinus Officinalis (Rosemary) Extract**, **…Flower/Leaf/Stem Extract**, **…Leaf Powder**, **…Leaf Water**, **…Callus Culture Extract**
  - Isolated actives sometimes appear as **Carnosic Acid**, **Carnosol**, **Rosmarinic Acid** (rarely as standalone INCI in finished cosmetics; usually delivered *via* the standardized extract).
- **Food-additive identity (relevant to safety/supply):** Rosemary extract is authorised in the EU as the food antioxidant **E 392**. [13]
- **The three "marker" actives (this is the heart of the ingredient):**
  - **Carnosic acid (CA)** — CAS 3650-09-7; C₂₀H₂₈O₄; ~332.4 g/mol. A **phenolic abietane diterpene** restricted largely to rosemary/sage; **lipophilic** (fat-soluble), catechol-bearing → very strong antioxidant; the primary "anti-aging" diterpene. [11]
  - **Carnosol** — CAS 5957-80-2; C₂₀H₂₆O₄; ~330.4 g/mol. The major **oxidation product** of carnosic acid (i.e., CA degrading *into* carnosol is a stability story, see §6); lipophilic antioxidant with anti-inflammatory activity. [11]
  - **Rosmarinic acid (RA)** — CAS 20283-92-5 (also 537-15-5 forms cited); C₁₈H₁₆O₈; ~360.3 g/mol. An ester of caffeic acid + dihydroxyphenyllactic acid; **water-soluble** polyphenol; strong radical scavenger, anti-glycation, anti-inflammatory. Found across the Lamiaceae (also lemon balm, sage, basil). [11][18]
- **Why the pair matters:** rosemary spans **both compartments** — lipid-soluble diterpenes (CA/carnosol) protect membranes/lipids; water-soluble RA protects the aqueous phase. That dual solubility is the mechanistic basis for the "rosemary + grapefruit/citrus" photoprotection combos. [3][4]
- **Forms on the market:** CO₂ or solvent extracts standardized to **5–20% carnosic acid** (cosmetic/antioxidant grade), RA-standardized aqueous extracts, oil-dispersed antioxidant grades (e.g., 5% CA in sunflower oil), powders, leaf water, and (separately) the **essential oil** (camphor/1,8-cineole-rich — different risk profile). [19]

## 2. Class & mechanism (how it works at the skin/cell level)

Rosemary is best understood as a **multi-active antioxidant botanical** whose skin-relevant actions are driven mainly by **carnosic acid, carnosol, and rosmarinic acid** acting through several converging mechanisms:

1. **Direct ROS quenching / radical scavenging.** Carnosic acid is a **ROS quencher** (its catechol moiety donates H to neutralize free radicals); carnosol works more by **interrupting lipid peroxidation** directly. Pure CA is reported to be a *more potent* lipid antioxidant than α-tocopherol in bulk-oil models. RA scavenges aqueous-phase radicals. [11]
2. **Nrf2/ARE pathway activation ("indirect" / catalytic antioxidant defense).** CA and carnosol are **electrophilic catechol/quinone-type Nrf2 activators**: they modify Keap1, freeing Nrf2 to enter the nucleus and upregulate endogenous antioxidant/detox genes (heme oxygenase-1, NQO1, glutathione enzymes). This is the same "switch on the cell's own defenses" mechanism prized in skin photoprotection. [2][5][8]
3. **Anti-MMP / ECM protection.** In UV-exposed human fibroblasts and keratinocytes, CA **suppresses UV-induced MMP-1/-3/-9** by blunting ROS → EGFR → ERK → AP-1 signaling — i.e., it interrupts the cascade that breaks down dermal collagen in photoaging. Nutroxsun (rosemary+grapefruit) inhibits MMP-1,-2,-3,-8,-12 in vitro. [3][6]
4. **Anti-inflammatory.** CA/carnosol reduce IL-1β, TNF-α, NF-κB signaling; RA notably suppresses IL-8. Relevant to "inflammaging," erythema, and acne-type inflammation. [7][14]
5. **Anti-glycation / AGE modulation.** RA inhibits formation of advanced glycation end-products (e.g., pentosidine) and interferes with the AGE–RAGE crosslinking that stiffens collagen/elastin — the mechanistic basis for the oral "anti-glycation" rosemary clinicals. [10][12][18]
6. **Genoprotection.** Rosemary diterpenes (+citrus flavanones) reduced UVB-induced DNA double-strand breaks and γ-H2AX activation in keratinocytes in vitro. [15]
7. **Antimicrobial / quorum-sensing.** CA/carnosol inhibit *S. aureus* virulence (quorum sensing) and rosemary suppresses *Cutibacterium (Propionibacterium) acnes*-driven inflammation — relevant to blemish-prone positioning and to natural preservation boosting. [11][14]
8. **(New, mouse-only) TRPA1-driven regeneration.** Topical rosemary/CA activates the **TRPA1** sensory-neuron channel → IL-23 → γδ-T-cell IL-17/IL-22 → enhanced wound closure and *reduced* fibrosis/scarring in mice. A novel "pro-regenerative" mechanism, but unproven in humans. [1]

**Skin-relevant translation (honest):** Mechanisms 1–6 are well-established *biology* and are demonstrated in **cells and (for oral) humans**; whether a **topical** rosemary serum delivers enough CA/RA into living human dermis to reproduce them is **largely unproven** for finished cosmetics (see §4–5).

## 4. EVIDENCE REVIEW BY TIER (the core of this dossier)

> **The single most important honesty point:** Rosemary's strongest *human* anti-aging data are for **ORAL supplements** — chiefly **Nutroxsun** (rosemary+grapefruit polyphenols) and **BioR/skinbetter** (rosemary extract). The strongest *topical/skin* data are **in-vitro (human cell)** and **animal (mouse/rat)**. The headline 2025 regeneration story (TRPA1) is **mouse-only**. A rigorous **human topical RCT of a defined rosemary cosmetic** does **not** appear to exist in the literature. So most topical anti-aging claims rest on borrowed (oral) or lower-tier (cell/animal) evidence.

### Tier 1 — In vitro (human cells & skin models) — *the deepest, most directly relevant mechanistic tier*

- **Park M., Han J., Lee C.S. et al. (2013), *Experimental Dermatology* 22(5):336–341 (DOI 10.1111/exd.12138).** Carnosic acid **prevented UV-induced MMP-1/-3/-9** in human dermal fibroblasts (Hs68, primary HDFs) and normal human epidermal keratinocytes by inhibiting ROS→EGFR→ERK→AP-1 signaling. *The cornerstone topical-mechanism paper — but cell culture, direct dosing, not transdermal/human.* [3]
- **Nutroxsun in-vitro arm — Navarro P., Castillo J., Jones J. et al. (2025), *IJMS* 26(9):4001 (DOI 10.3390/ijms26094001).** In HaCaT keratinocytes + normal human dermal fibroblasts: dose-dependent inhibition of **MMP-1/-2/-3/-8/-12 (IC50 <20 µg/mL)**, up to **~100% ROS reduction** at high UVB dose, ↓IL-1α/IL-6/IL-8, ↑procollagen I and elastin, **MMP-1 secretion −57.2%**. *Robust mechanism; but a branded blend, cell models, and 4/5 authors are Monteloeder (the supplier).* [6]
- **Sánchez-Marzo N., Pérez-Sánchez A., Barrajón-Catalán E. et al. (2020), *Antioxidants (Basel)* 9(3):255 (DOI 10.3390/antiox9030255).** Rosemary diterpenes + citrus flavanones in HaCaT keratinocytes reduced **UVB-induced DNA double-strand breaks by 64.9%** and **γ-H2AX by 70.8%** at 200 µg/mL (genoprotection). *In-vitro cell model; combination product.* [15]
- **Nrf2/ARE photoprotection (review-documented).** Rosemary extract / carnosic acid activate the **Nrf2/ARE** system in keratinocytes and inhibit UVB-induced TNF-α and MMP-1; combos with tomato/carotenoids **synergistically** activate ARE/Nrf2 and suppress IL-6. *Summarized in the Nrf2-photoprotection review; underlying data are cell-based.* [2][5][8]
- **Carnosic acid vs carnosol mechanism — Birtić/Munné-Bosch et al. (2017), *Plant Physiology* 175:1381 (and related, DOI 10.1104/pp.17.01007 / PMID 28916593).** Establishes CA as a ROS quencher vs carnosol acting in the lipid-oxidation process — the chemistry behind the antioxidant claims. [11]
- **Rosmarinic acid protects collagen in human fibroblasts — Bakar/Stojiljković-type studies (2020), *IJMS* (PMC7281983) & (2021) *IJMS* 22(21):11451 (PMC8584053).** RA gave partial/total protection against **paraben-** and **benzophenone-3-induced** declines in type I/III collagen and modulation of MMP-1/-2 in human skin fibroblasts. *Defensive/“protect against an insult” model, not pro-aging-reversal; cell culture.* [17]
- **Anti-glycation (RA).** RA inhibits AGE formation (e.g., pentosidine) and ribose-induced glycation of collagen/elastin fibers in vitro. *Mechanistic basis for the oral anti-glycation clinicals.* [18]

### Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)

- **TRPA1 regeneration — Rapp E., Pang J. … Leung T.H. (2025), *JCI Insight* (DOI 10.1172/jci.insight.196267).** **Topical** ethanol rosemary extract and **carnosic acid (5 mg/mL in Cetaphil)** on **C57BL/6J mice**: ear-punch closure **~90% vs ~35%** control at 4 weeks, faster dorsal re-epithelialization, **less fibrosis** (picrosirius red), and regeneration of follicles/sebaceous glands/fat. Mechanism: CA→**TRPA1** on sensory neurons→IL-23→γδ-T-cell IL-17/IL-22; TRPA1-knockout abolished the benefit. *Striking, mechanistically clean — but **mouse only**; authors explicitly note human skin differs and human trials are still needed.* [1]
- **Rosemary hexane-extract lipid nanocapsule gel, UVB rats — (2022), *Drug Delivery* / related, PMC9338973.** **Topical** nanoencapsulated rosemary (CA-rich) on UVB-irradiated rats restored antioxidant status, improved epidermal/dermal histology, and reduced inflammatory + wrinkling markers; nanocapsules 56–66 nm improved skin permeation. *Animal; a delivery-enhanced form — shows plain CA needs help penetrating (see §5).* [9]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL (where most of the *human* anti-aging data actually live — and it's not topical)

- **Nutroxsun pivotal RCT — Nobile V., Michelotti A., Cestone E. et al. (2016), *Food & Nutrition Research* 60:31871 (DOI 10.3402/fnr.v60.31871; PMID 27374032).** Randomized, **double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group**, **n=90 women** (phototypes I–III), 2 months, **oral** 100 or 250 mg/day rosemary+grapefruit. Results vs placebo: **MED +29.8% (100 mg)/+26.9% (250 mg)**, basal lipid-peroxidation (MDA) **−18.7%/−32.6%**, **wrinkle depth −14.8%/−13.9%**, net elasticity (R5) **+9.0%/+7.4%**; benefits emerged by 2 weeks. *Strong design for an oral nutraceutical — but **oral**, female-only Caucasian, sponsor-funded (Monteloeder/Nutrafur), subjects avoided UV.* [4]
- **Nutroxsun acute crossover — Navarro et al. (2025), *IJMS* (same paper as [6]).** Monocentric, **placebo-controlled crossover**, **n=20** (phototypes I–III), **oral** 100 vs 250 mg dosed before 1-MED UVB: erythema at 24 h **+41–45% (active) vs +62.7% (placebo)** (p≈0.006–0.02). *Small (n=20), acute single-exposure, sponsor-affiliated authors, no PK data.* [6]
- **BioR / skinbetter anti-glycation RCT — Draelos Z.D., Gueniche A., Yatskayer M., Nelson D.B. (2025), *J. Clin. Aesthet. Dermatol.* 18(3):28–33.** Single-center, **double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled**, **n=104 (100 completed)** peri-/post-menopausal women 40–65, **12 weeks**, **oral** rosemary-extract supplement (300 mg/cap + biotin + zinc). Results vs placebo: global skin quality **+17%** (wk 12), dullness **−28%**, roughness **−30%**, erythema **−14%**, pore size **−2%**; **no** pigmentation benefit. The **histological glycation/AGE-marker** reduction and the elasticity/retraction figures (~+12%) are **not in this paper** — Draelos states biopsy data are "reported separately"; they appear in the **companion glycation analysis [12]** (and an earlier pilot). *Best "anti-glycation" human signal — but **oral**, the capsule also contains **biotin + zinc** cofactors (can't isolate rosemary's contribution), **L'Oréal-funded** (authors are L'Oréal/skinbetter employees & consultants), mostly white women, placebo effect noted.* [10][12]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that matters most for a topical skincare brand — and it's essentially empty)

- **No published human topical RCT of a defined rosemary/carnosic-acid cosmetic** was found. The only human "topical" exposures in the literature are **patch tests / contact-dermatitis case reports** (a *safety* tier, see §7), and human **volunteers in combination photoprotection work were dosed ORALLY**, not topically. [4][6][20]
- The 2025 TRPA1 paper used **topical** rosemary/CA **but in mice**, with authors explicitly stating human topical trials remain to be done. [1]
- **Bottom line:** any "topical rosemary serum reduces wrinkles / repairs skin" claim today is **extrapolated** from oral human data, human-cell data, and mouse data — not demonstrated for a finished topical product in humans.

**Bottom line by tier:** Proven for rosemary actives → antioxidant/anti-MMP/Nrf2/genoprotection (human cells), photoprotection + anti-glycation + elasticity/wrinkle (human **ORAL** RCTs), regeneration/anti-scarring (**mouse**). **NOT proven** → finished **topical** rosemary cosmetic in a human RCT.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Carnosic acid / carnosol (lipophilic):** Mid-size (~330 g/mol), fat-soluble with a catechol head — partition into the stratum corneum lipids is *plausible*, and lipophilicity favours residence in the lipid-rich barrier. **But there are essentially no human transdermal PK data** quantifying how much CA reaches viable epidermis/dermis from a finished cosmetic. The fact that researchers reach for **lipid nanocapsules / nanoencapsulation** to get efficacy in animal skin [9] is itself a signal that **plain CA penetration/stability is a limiting factor**.
- **Rosmarinic acid (hydrophilic):** Water-soluble polyphenol with multiple OH groups → like most polyphenols, **poor passive penetration** of the lipophilic stratum corneum; tends to stay superficial without a delivery system. Good for surface/antioxidant duty; uncertain for dermal targets.
- **Oral vs topical mismatch:** The human efficacy is **oral**, i.e., the actives are absorbed, metabolized, and distributed systemically to skin — a fundamentally different route than topical. Topical delivery of the *same* benefit is **not** established.
- **Stability caveat compounds the problem (see §6):** CA degrades to carnosol and further on exposure to air/light/heat/protic solvents, so even the dose you *apply* may not be the dose that's *active* by the time of use.
- **Practical read:** As a **surface + barrier-lipid antioxidant and formula stabilizer**, rosemary is credible. As a **dermal anti-aging actor via topical delivery**, penetration is *plausible but unproven*; expect to need encapsulation/lipophilic vehicles and our own testing to substantiate dermal claims.

## 6. Formulation

- **Effective concentration:** No validated *topical* human efficacy concentration. Cosmetic antioxidant grades are standardized to **5–20% carnosic acid** and typically used at **~0.1–1%** of the standardized extract (often <0.5% for the diterpene-rich grades; antioxidant/preservative-booster use is even lower). CIR notes leaf extract used up to **10%** in some body/hand products. [13][19] Formulate conservatively; do not imply a proven dose-response.
- **pH:** RA and the phenolic diterpenes are happiest **mildly acidic to neutral (~pH 4–6)**; CA is *more* resistant to degradation at low pH. Keep finished formulas in the skin-friendly **~4.5–6** range. [11]
- **Stability (the #1 formulation challenge):** **Carnosic acid auto-oxidizes** in air/light/heat — converting to carnosol and further degradants — and degrades **faster in protic (water/ethanol) solvents** and with trace metal ions (Fe). [11] Mitigations: prefer **anhydrous or low-water systems** or robust encapsulation; **add a chelator** (e.g., EDTA/phytate) to bind catalytic metals; **co-formulate with tocopherol** (synergistic antioxidant protection); protect from light (opaque/airless packaging); avoid high-temperature processing; verify residual CA on stability pulls (it can drift even at low temperature). RA is somewhat more robust but still oxidizable.
- **Pairings (rational):**
  - **Vitamin E (tocopherol)** — classic antioxidant synergy + helps stabilize CA. [11]
  - **Vitamin C / niacinamide / other antioxidants** — network antioxidant story (lipid + aqueous coverage).
  - **Citrus/grapefruit flavonoids, carotenoids, tomato extract** — documented photoprotection/Nrf2 synergy (the Nutroxsun and tomato-combo logic). [4][6][8]
  - **Sunscreen / SPF systems** — rosemary's evidence frames it as *adjunct to*, never *replacement for*, sunscreen. [4]
  - **Chelators** — functionally essential for CA stability.
- **Conflicts / cautions:** Strong oxidizers and pro-oxidant metal contamination accelerate CA loss; high-water, high-heat, light-exposed systems are worst-case. The **essential oil** form (camphor/cineole) is a different, more sensitizing material — don't substitute it for the polyphenol extract when chasing antioxidant claims.
- **Sensory:** Standardized extracts can carry a faint herbal/green color and characteristic rosemary odor at higher use levels; oil-dispersed grades add to the oil phase cleanly. Manage scent so it reads intentional, not "kitchen herb."
- **Bonus functional value:** Rosemary extract is a legitimate **natural antioxidant for the oil phase** — it can *protect the formula itself* (retard rancidity of plant oils) and act as a preservative-system booster, which is a real, defensible formulation benefit independent of any skin claim. [13][19]

## 7. Safety

- **Overall cosmetic verdict (authoritative):** The **CIR Expert Panel (Fiume et al., 2018, *Int. J. Toxicol.*)** reviewed ~10 *Rosmarinus officinalis*-derived ingredients and concluded they are **"safe as used … when formulated to be non-sensitizing."** That conditional clause is the whole ballgame. [20][21]
- **Sensitization / allergic contact dermatitis (the headline caution):** Rosemary is a documented **contact allergen**. Multiple case reports describe allergic contact dermatitis from rosemary leaf extract (e.g., in a cleansing gel) and from rosemary as a plant; **carnosol** has been implicated as a sensitizing constituent, and rosemary can **cross-react with other Lamiaceae** (thyme, mint, sage). [20][22] Action items: keep use levels modest, patch-test finished formulas (HRIPT), and treat "non-sensitizing formulation" as a hard requirement.
- **Irritation:** Low at cosmetic concentrations for the leaf extract; the **essential oil** (camphor, 1,8-cineole) is the more irritating/allergenic form and should be assessed separately and used at fragrance-level restraint.
- **Fragrance-allergen / "clean-fragrance" angle:** Even the extract carries aromatic constituents; for fragrance-sensitive positioning, disclose and patch-test.
- **Sun:** **Not** a known photosensitizer — mechanistically the opposite (Nrf2/antioxidant photoprotection). No phototoxicity flag for the leaf extract. (Note: *grapefruit/citrus* partners can carry furocoumarin/phototoxicity concerns — that's a citrus issue, not rosemary.) [3][4]
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** The caution is about **oral/medicinal high doses** — rosemary is a traditional **emmenagogue** and high oral doses raised reproductive-tox concerns in rats; medicinal oral use is generally **not recommended in pregnancy**. For **topical cosmetic** use at normal low concentrations, systemic absorption is minimal and reproductive/developmental toxicity is **not** considered a concern by CIR; still, advise conservative copy ("if pregnant or nursing, consult your doctor"). The **essential oil** warrants more caution than the extract. [20][23]
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** Plant-derived, generally GRAS-adjacent (food antioxidant E 392); main considerations are **standardization variability** (CA/RA content swings with cultivar, harvest, extraction) and pesticide/heavy-metal residues — require a COA. No major sustainability red flags; rosemary is widely cultivated.
- **General tolerability context (oral):** The oral RCTs ([4][6][10]) reported good tolerability; BioR noted only mild transient GI events. (Supportive context, not a topical safety substitute.)

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic ingredient status:** Well-established cosmetic INCI (Rosmarinus Officinalis / Salvia Rosmarinus Leaf Extract); functions: **antioxidant, skin-conditioning, fragrance, (in some grades) preservative-booster**. Also an authorised **food antioxidant (EU E 392)**. CIR: safe as used **when non-sensitizing**. [13][20]
- **The drug/cosmetic line (US FDA frame):** Importing the **oral** human longevity/photoprotection data (↑MED, ↓wrinkles, ↓glycation, "repairs," "reverses glycation") into **topical** structure/function claims is the central legal trap. "Repairs," "boosts collagen," "anti-aging," "prevents photoaging," "increases MED/SPF" are **drug-leaning / require substantiation** — and we don't have human topical substantiation. "Increases sun protection / MED" is especially risky (sounds like an SPF/drug claim) and was an **oral** finding regardless.
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language (topical):** "antioxidant-rich botanical," "helps defend the look of skin against environmental/oxidative stress," "supports a more even, radiant-looking complexion," "soothing botanical extract," "rich in rosemary polyphenols (carnosic & rosmarinic acid)." Keep antioxidant claims about *appearance* and *environmental defense*, not disease/structure.
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation or legal review:** "reverses glycation/wrinkles," "boosts collagen," "repairs sun damage," "increases your skin's SPF/MED," "anti-aging at the cellular level," "regenerates / heals wounds without scars" (the last is *mouse* data). None are supported for a **topical** human cosmetic.
- **Substantiation reality:** To make even modest topical performance claims, we'd want **our own finished-formula human testing**; the published literature substantiates an **oral** supplement and **cell/animal** mechanisms, not a topical serum. See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Forms/suppliers:** Broadly available cosmetic-grade extracts standardized to **5%, 10%, 15%, or 20% carnosic acid** (e.g., New Directions Aromatics, Nutra Green, bulk naturals/oil-dispersed grades), plus RA-standardized aqueous extracts and leaf powders/waters; fine-chemical CA/RA/carnosol from lab suppliers (Sigma, etc.). MOQs commonly **1–25 kg**, R&D quantities available. [19]
- **Cost tier:** **Low-to-mid.** Standard leaf extract is inexpensive (it's a commodity food antioxidant); **higher-CA standardized grades** and any **encapsulated/nanoformulated** versions cost more. Excellent cost-to-story ratio.
- **Supply/standardization risk:** CA/RA content varies by cultivar, season, and extraction; **demand a COA quantifying carnosic + rosmarinic acid** and specify the grade. For a stable formula, source a grade with documented oxidative stability or pair with a delivery/stabilization system.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence (call out the overreach)

- **The route switch (the big one).** The compelling human numbers — ↑MED ~27–30%, ↓wrinkle depth ~14%, ↓lipid peroxidation ~33%, reduced skin AGEs, ↑elasticity ~12% — are **ORAL supplement** results ([4][10]). Marketing a *topical* rosemary serum as if those transfer is the central distortion. They may *not* transfer.
- **"Clinically proven to fight wrinkles/glycation."** True for an **ingested** rosemary supplement; **not** demonstrated for any topical cosmetic in a human RCT.
- **"Heals and regenerates skin / reduces scarring."** That's the **2025 TRPA1 mouse** study [1] — exciting and mechanistically elegant, but the authors themselves say human topical trials haven't been done.
- **"Natural sun protection / boosts your SPF."** Photoprotection signal is real but **oral**, **adjunctive**, and modest — never a sunscreen replacement, and "boosts SPF" edges into drug-claim territory.
- **"Brightens / fades dark spots."** The best human (oral) RCT showed **no** pigmentation benefit [10]; topical anti-melanogenic claims for rosemary are weak.
- **Stability silence.** Vendors tout carnosic acid's antioxidant power while glossing over that **CA is notoriously unstable** (oxidizes to carnosol in air/light/heat) [11] — the applied dose may not equal the active dose.
- **Sensitization glossed over.** The "gentle natural botanical" framing collides with documented **allergic contact dermatitis** and CIR's explicit "**when non-sensitizing**" condition [20][22].

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING (conditional) — a credible antioxidant / formula-protecting botanical and a good *story* ingredient, not an evidence-backed topical hero (today).**

**One-line:** Rosemary's actives (carnosic & rosmarinic acid) have genuinely strong **antioxidant, anti-MMP, Nrf2-activating, anti-glycation** mechanisms and even real **human anti-aging outcomes — but those human wins are ORAL**, the topical evidence is cell/animal, and there's a real **contact-allergen** caution plus a **carnosic-acid stability** problem. Use it as a science-forward **supporting antioxidant / lipid-phase stabilizer** with honest, appearance-focused copy — not as a proof-backed dermal hero.

**Where it fits the repo thesis:** It does **not** carry the barrier-repair core on its own (barrier evidence is thin), but it pairs naturally with the antioxidant/"environmental defense" layer of a lean routine, and it doubles as a **functional formula antioxidant** (protecting our plant-oil/base from oxidation) — a real, defensible reason to include it even before any skin claim.

**Longevity-relevance note:** Rosemary has an attractive **longevity narrative** — carnosic acid is a bona-fide **Nrf2 activator** (the master "turn on your own antioxidant defenses" switch central to geroprotection), it's discussed among **terpenoid geroprotectors**, and it hits **glycation** (a recognized aging hallmark) with actual human (oral) biopsy data [10][12][16]. That makes it a strong **education/differentiation** ingredient for a skin-*longevity* brand. The honest gap is purely **format/route** (oral→topical) and **penetration/stability**. If we want real topical claims, the path is **our own finished-formula human testing** and likely a **stabilized/encapsulated** carnosic-acid form — not the existing literature.

**Conditions to graduate from "supporting" to "hero/claimed":** (1) source a stabilized/encapsulated CA grade with COA-verified CA+RA; (2) formulate non-sensitizing + HRIPT-tested; (3) run our own topical human study before making performance claims.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Overall grade: C (weak/indirect for *topical* use).** Verified 2026-06-19 against primary sources.

This is the honest grade *for a topical skincare context*, which is what matters for this brand. It is **not** a knock on the ingredient's science — the underlying biology is real and some of the human data are genuinely strong. The grade is capped at C because **every strong human result is ORAL**, and there is **no human topical RCT** of a finished rosemary cosmetic. If this dossier were grading rosemary as an *oral* nutraceutical, it would earn a B (multiple double-blind, placebo-controlled human RCTs). As a *topical* hero it is C; as a *topical formula-protecting antioxidant / supporting actor* it is defensible.

**What I checked and what held up:** I verified the highest-stakes citations against primary sources (PMC/publisher/PubMed). All major studies are **real and accurately characterized** — the dossier did not invent or misquote its evidence, and it pre-emptively flags its own weaknesses (the oral→topical route switch, mouse-only regeneration, sponsor funding). That is rare and good.

**Main caveats (the load-bearing ones):**
1. **Route switch is the whole story.** ↑MED ~27–30%, ↓wrinkle ~14%, ↓lipid-peroxidation ~33%, ↑elasticity ~12%, ↓skin-AGEs — **all ORAL** ([4][10][12]). None has been reproduced for a topical product in humans. Tier 4 (human topical) is empty by the dossier's own admission, and that is correct.
2. **Sponsorship is pervasive in the human data.** The two best topical-adjacent human stories are supplier/brand-funded: Nutroxsun [4][6] (Monteloeder, the supplier) and BioRewind [10][12] (L'Oréal/skinbetter). Independent corroboration of the human numbers is thin.
3. **Confounded supplement.** The Draelos/BioRewind capsule contains **biotin + zinc** alongside rosemary, so the human outcomes cannot be attributed to rosemary alone — a point the dossier makes and that I confirmed at source.
4. **Best regeneration story is mouse-only.** The 2025 TRPA1 paper [1] is mechanistically elegant and verified, but C57BL/6J mice; the authors themselves caution human skin differs.
5. **Two real ingredient-level problems** (well covered): carnosic-acid **instability** (oxidizes to carnosol) and a documented **contact-allergen** profile (CIR's "when non-sensitizing" is a hard condition).

**Claims softened in this pass:**
- Re-attributed the **glycation/AGE biopsy** and **elasticity ~12%** findings away from [10] (which doesn't contain them) to the **companion paper [12]**, and surfaced the **biotin+zinc cofactor** confound and **L'Oréal funding** explicitly in §4.
- Corrected the §3 glycation row to flag the cofactor-containing oral supplement.
- Fixed the [22] citation metadata (journal/year).

**Claims that were already appropriately hedged (no change needed):** the §10 hype section, the per-tier "bottom line," the §11 verdict ("SUPPORTING, not a topical hero"), the §8 claims-discipline flags, and the penetration/stability discussion in §5–6. The dossier consistently labels oral data as oral and mouse data as mouse.

**Net:** the dossier is **trustworthy and unusually self-critical**; no fabricated or materially misread sources were found. The only defensible-claims edit was the [10]/[12] attribution. The headline grade for **topical** use is **C** — credible supporting antioxidant and formula stabilizer, not a proven topical anti-aging hero. To move toward a B/A would require an independent **human topical RCT of a defined, stabilized rosemary formula** (the dossier's own "graduate to hero" conditions).
