# Ingredient: Acetyl Zingerone (INCI: Acetyl Zingerone)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Class:** Antioxidants · **Status:** ☑ shortlisted (conditional — strong supporting, not hero) · ⬜ considering / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product

> **One-line read:** A clever, photostable, ginger-derived synthetic antioxidant with genuinely interesting multi-mechanism in-vitro data and *one* independent human topical RCT — but the evidence base is small, young, and heavily authored/funded by its single manufacturer (Sytheon). Promising **supporting** antioxidant, not a proven hero.

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common / trade name:** Acetyl Zingerone (AZ). Sole commercial source is **Synoxyl® AZ** by **Sytheon Ltd.** (Parsippany/Boonton, NJ).
- **INCI:** Acetyl Zingerone.
- **Chemical name:** 3-[(4-hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)methyl]pentane-2,4-dione (a.k.a. 2,4-pentanedione, 3-[(4-hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)methyl]). [1][2]
- **CAS:** 30881-23-3. **REACH/EC#:** 820-605-0. **Purity:** ≥99%. [1][9]
- **Origin / how it's made:** A **lab-synthesized hybrid molecule**, not a plant extract. Designed to combine the structure of **zingerone** (a phenolic ketone from ginger, *Zingiber officinale*) with the 1,3-dicarbonyl ("di-keto") moiety of **curcumin**. Synthesized by condensing **vanillin** with **acetylacetone**, then catalytically hydrogenating the intermediate. [1][2]
- **Why the hybrid design matters:** It keeps the antioxidant phenol ring of zingerone/curcumin but adds metal-chelating di-carbonyl chemistry — while being far more **photostable** than curcumin (which degrades rapidly in sunlight) and than vitamin E. [1]
- **Key analog/derivative:** **MBPD** (a methoxy-benzylidene pentanedione analog studied alongside AZ in the CPD/DNA work) — experimental, not a cosmetic ingredient yet. [6]
- **Note on naming:** "Acetyl zingerone" is **not** simply acetylated zingerone; the name is a trade convention. It is a distinct synthetic molecule.

## 2. Class & mechanism

A multifunctional **antioxidant + chelator** (CosIng functions: antioxidant, chelating, skin protecting). [2] Proposed mechanisms (mostly in-vitro/biochemical):

1. **Direct free-radical scavenging** — the phenol donates electrons to neutralize hydroxyl (•OH), peroxyl (ROO•) and peroxynitrite (ONOO⁻) radicals. In biochemical assays AZ outperformed α-tocopherol by reported factors of ~17.7× (•OH), ~39.6× (ROO•) and ~126× (ONOO⁻). [1] *(Manufacturer assays — see COI caveat.)*
2. **Singlet-oxygen (¹O₂) physical quenching** — returns ¹O₂ to ground state *without consuming itself* (non-sacrificial), reported ~2.7× more effective than α-tocopherol. This is the basis for its "vitamin C stabilizer" positioning. [1][5]
3. **Selective metal chelation** — the di-keto tautomer chelates Fe²⁺/Cu²⁺, shutting down Fenton-reaction •OH generation. [1]
4. **Photostability** — retains chemical integrity under UV where α-tocopherol fully degrades, so it keeps working in sun-exposed (daytime) products. [1]
5. **"After-sun" DNA protection** — scavenges ONOO⁻ and quenches excited-state carbonyls that drive **delayed/"dark" CPDs** (DNA dimers that keep forming in melanocytes for hours *after* UV stops), plus upregulates **nucleotide-excision-repair (NER)** genes (Xpa, Xpc). [6][7]
6. **Gene-expression / matrisome modulation** — in reconstituted epidermis, shifts transcription toward collagen/proteoglycan synthesis and away from MMPs, senescence, and IL-17A inflammatory programs. [3][8]

## 4. Evidence review by tier (the core)

> **Overarching caveat — read first.** The AZ literature is **young (≈2019–2024), small, and dominated by its manufacturer.** Sytheon (CEO **Ratan K. Chaudhuri**, with collaborators T. Meyer and W. Swindell) funded and/or authored the foundational chemistry, the genomics, the THDC-stabilization work, and the flagship 2023 review. This is a textbook conflict-of-interest pattern: the molecule is **patent-protected and single-source**, so essentially all primary data trace back to the patent holder. The two genuinely **independent academic** signals are (a) the UC-Davis / Sivamani-group human RCTs published in *Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology*, and (b) the **Moffitt Cancer Center / Sanjay Premi + Douglas Brash (Yale)** dark-CPD melanocyte work (Brash discovered dark CPDs) — though even these were partly Sytheon-funded. Treat efficacy magnitudes as **directional, not gospel.**

### Tier A — In-vitro (cell & biochemical) — *moderate volume, manufacturer-heavy*
- **Antioxidant chemistry (Meyer/Swindell/Chaudhuri 2023 review compiling Sytheon assays).** AZ neutralizes •OH, ROO•, ONOO⁻; physically quenches ¹O₂; chelates Fe²⁺/Cu²⁺; outperforms α-tocopherol and parent zingerone; remains photostable under UV. [1]
- **Keratinocytes (HaCaT).** AZ pre-treatment (31.2–250 µg/mL) cut ROS dose-dependently after blue light / particulate-matter exposure; 25–50 µg/mL cut UVA-induced ROS by 35–46%; lipid peroxidation reduced 62–88%. [1]
- **Melanocytes — delayed ("dark") CPDs.** AZ added *after* UVA blocked delayed CPD formation (~82% inhibition at 25 µg/mL); mechanism via ONOO⁻ scavenging + NER upregulation. **Chaudhuri, Meyer, Premi & Brash 2020, *Int. J. Cosmet. Sci.*** [7] Extended in **Srivastava et al. 2023, *Antioxidants*** (Moffitt + Sytheon): across mouse/human melanocytes, fibroblasts, keratinocytes, AZ gave ~30% incident + complete delayed-CPD blockade; analog MBPD reached ~80% total CPD inhibition. Non-toxic at mM levels. [6]
- **MMP inhibition.** AZ inhibited MMP-1, MMP-3, MMP-12 enzyme activity in vitro (greater for MMP-3/-12 than parent zingerone). [3][8]
- **THDC (vitamin C ester) stabilization.** **Swindell, Randhawa, Quijas, Bojanowski & Chaudhuri 2021, *Int. J. Mol. Sci.*** THDC alone degraded ~completely within ~6 min under ¹O₂; with AZ, held to ~25% loss at 10 min. AZ alone ↑ collagen I 15–31% in fibroblasts; AZ also suppressed an unexpected pro-inflammatory type-I-interferon response that THDC triggered alone. **Sytheon-funded.** [5]

### Tier B — Gene-expression (reconstituted human epidermis / fibroblasts) — *manufacturer*
- **Swindell et al. 2020, *Journal of Investigative Dermatology* (peer-reviewed, full paper + abstract #851).** Microarray on reconstituted epidermis: AZ ↑ collagen genes (COL11A2, COL6A3, COL4A1), proteoglycans (VCAN, OGN, PODN), ECM regulators (TIMP1), glycoproteins (SPARC, FBLN2); ↑ Notch pathway (NOTCH1, MAML3); ↓ ECM-disassembly genes (MMP3, CTSV); opposed fibroblast-senescence and IL-17A-driven inflammatory signatures. [3][8] *Mechanistically rich but transcriptomic — gene shifts ≠ proven clinical outcomes.*

### Tier C — Human ORAL
- **None relevant.** AZ is a topical cosmetic actives molecule; no oral/supplement human data. (Parent compound *zingerone* has separate oral pharmacology literature, but that does **not** transfer to topical AZ. [10])

### Tier D — Human TOPICAL
**RCTs (the strongest evidence, and notably independent):**
- **Dhaliwal S, Rybak I, Pourang A, Burney W, Haas K, Sandhu S, Crawford R, Sivamani RK. 2021. *J. Cosmet. Dermatol.* 20:166–173. (UC Davis Dermatology — independent academic group.)** Randomized, double-blind, **vehicle-controlled**; **n=31**, mean age ~44; **1% AZ** lotion vs vehicle, twice daily, **8 weeks**. Results in AZ group: wrinkle severity −25.7% (p=0.019), total wrinkle volume −30.1% (p=0.003), pigment intensity −25.6% (p=0.021), redness intensity −20.7% (p=0.035). Well tolerated, no irritation. [4]
  - **Limitations:** small n; single site; 8 weeks (short for "anti-aging"); image-analysis surrogate endpoints, not biopsy-confirmed collagen; vehicle-controlled (not vs an active comparator like retinol/vitamin C), so it shows AZ beats *nothing*, not that it beats incumbents.
- **Afzal S, … Sivamani RK. 2024. *J. Cosmet. Dermatol.* (Integrative Skin Science & Research / UC Davis group.)** Prospective, randomized, double-blind **comparative** RCT; **n=44**, ages 30–65; **THDC 5% + AZ 1%** vs **THDC 5% alone**, 8 weeks. Both improved photoaging/pigmentation; supports the AZ-stabilizes-and-augments-vitamin-C combination. [11] *Limitation: tests the **combo**, so it does not cleanly isolate AZ's independent contribution; modest n.*

**Lower-tier human (open-label, no control):**
- **Min M, … 2024. *J. Cosmet. Dermatol.* Open-label** application of a THDC + AZ serum improved photoaging/uneven pigmentation. [12] *Open-label, uncontrolled → weak; supportive only.*

**Other manufacturer-cited human signals (within the 2023 review, primary write-ups less accessible):** a 7-day **UV-defense** study (0.5% AZ) reporting ~33–39% erythema reduction; and a microbiome study reporting ↑ *S. epidermidis* with no dysbiosis. [1] *Treat as manufacturer data pending independent replication.*

### What is actually PROVEN for human topical use vs borrowed
- **Proven (independent human RCT):** 1% topical AZ visibly **reduces wrinkle appearance, dyspigmentation and redness** over 8 weeks vs vehicle (one small RCT). [4]
- **Plausible but in-vitro/transcriptomic only (not yet proven clinically in humans on AZ alone):** collagen/matrisome synthesis, MMP inhibition, senescence opposition, dark-CPD/DNA protection, microbiome friendliness, "stabilizes vitamin C in the bottle."
- **Borrowed / combination-confounded:** the THDC-pairing clinical benefits belong to the **pair**, not to AZ in isolation. [11][12]
- **Not demonstrated at all:** barrier repair, TEWL/hydration, mitochondrial effects, any oral benefit.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Oil-soluble, lipophilic** (soluble in oils, esters, alcohols, glycols; only slightly water-soluble), which favors stratum-corneum partitioning. Active in cell and ex-vivo skin models, and effective in human RCTs at 0.5–1% — consistent with adequate delivery from a well-designed vehicle. [9][13]
- **No dedicated human percutaneous-penetration / Franz-cell pharmacokinetic study located** for AZ specifically. Delivery is inferred from formulation behavior and clinical effect, not directly measured. **[partial inference]**

## 6. Formulation notes

- **Use level:** **0.5–1%.** Clinical efficacy demonstrated at 1%; **>1% not advised** by supplier. [13]
- **pH:** keep **acidic, below ~6.5** (phenolic stability); supplier overall window cited as ~4–8 for activity, with <6.5 preferred. [9][13]
- **Solubility / incorporation:** pre-disperse by heating to ~65–70 °C in a solubilizer (e.g., C12–15 alkyl benzoate, dimethyl isosorbide, glycols) or add to the heated oil phase; may need high-shear if added post-emulsion. Max solubility ~25% in dimethyl isosorbide/DMSO. [13]
- **Stability:** heat-stable through processing; **photostable** (a key differentiator) — opaque/airless packaging still recommended; ~4-year shelf life sealed at 10–30 °C. [13]
- **Color/sensory:** white/off-white powder; solutions are pale yellow but shouldn't tint finished product; **mild vanilla scent.** [13]
- **Pairings (synergy):** strong rationale with **vitamin C esters (THDC)** — AZ quenches ¹O₂ to slow C degradation and the combo has human data. [5][11] Sensible alongside **niacinamide, peptides, sunscreens** (daytime antioxidant booster); complements barrier actives (ceramides) rather than competing.
- **Conflicts / cautions:** incompatible with strong **hydroxides** (NaOH/KOH/aluminum hydroxide) and high-pH systems; **cannot be used in toners** (per supplier); **iron/copper ions can discolor** — add a chelator (0.1–0.3% EDTA/EDDS). No documented negative interaction with vitamin C, retinol, niacinamide, or UV filters. [13]

## 7. Safety

- **General profile:** Well tolerated in the human RCT (no irritation reported at 1%). [4] EWG Skin Deep lists it with **low** hazard across cancer, allergy/immunotoxicity, and developmental/reproductive categories; not on restricted/unacceptable lists. [14]
- **Irritation / sensitization:** No notable irritation or sensitization signal in available studies; it is a synthetic phenolic ketone (not the common fragrance/botanical-extract allergen profile), but it is **relatively new**, so long-term/large-population sensitization data are thin. **[limited long-term data]**
- **Sun sensitivity:** None expected — opposite, it's pitched as photostable and photoprotective-adjunct. It is **not** a sunscreen (minimal UV absorbance) and must **not** be claimed to replace SPF. [1]
- **Pregnancy/lactation:** No specific human safety data; EWG flags no reproductive concern, but **absence of evidence ≠ evidence of safety** — standard "consult physician" hedge for a novel cosmetic active. **[unverified for pregnancy]**
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** Fully synthetic from vanillin + acetylacetone (no botanical-harvest pressure on ginger); high purity (≥99%). Single-supplier dependency (Sytheon/distributors) is a **supply-chain risk**, not an environmental one. [1][9]

## 8. Regulatory & claims

- **Status:** A **cosmetic ingredient** (antioxidant/chelating/skin-protecting), REACH-registered; **not a drug, not a sunscreen filter.** [2][9]
- **✅ Defensible cosmetic copy:** "antioxidant that helps defend skin against environmental/free-radical stress," "helps improve the visible appearance of wrinkles, uneven tone and redness," "photostable antioxidant," "helps stabilize vitamin C." Tie wrinkle/tone/redness language to the appearance-level RCT endpoints. [4]
- **⚠️ Risky / needs substantiation or avoid (per CLAUDE.md & glossary):** "boosts collagen," "repairs DNA," "prevents skin cancer / melanoma," "reverses aging," any structure/function or disease-prevention claim. The DNA/CPD and melanoma framing is **mechanistic/in-vitro and manufacturer-driven** — using it in consumer copy would invite a drug-claim challenge and is not supported by human outcome data. [1][6][7]
- **⚠️ Do not** imply it replaces sunscreen.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Supplier:** Sytheon (Synoxyl® AZ); distributed via Hallstar, DKSH, Glenn Corp/Azelis. **Single originator → limited price competition.** [9][13]
- **Cost tier:** **Premium specialty active.** Exact per-kg pricing not public (quote-based), but as a patented, single-source, ≥99% synthetic active it sits well above commodity antioxidants (e.g., tocopherol, niacinamide). Used at only 0.5–1%, so **cost-in-formula is manageable** despite high raw-material price. **[price-per-kg unverified — request quote]**

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **The hype:** "Next-generation antioxidant," "1000× vitamin E," "24/7 prevention & repair," "stops DNA damage / cancer-relevant," "matrisome-restoring." Widely repeated by trade press and brands (NIOD, Paula's Choice, Revision, Image, etc.). [2]
- **The honest read:**
  - The **antioxidant chemistry is genuinely strong and the photostability is a real, differentiating advantage** over vitamin C/E and curcumin.
  - But the headline multiples ("39×," "126×," "1000×") are **biochemical-assay ratios from the manufacturer**, not clinical potency — they should never be implied as in-skin performance.
  - **Exactly one** independent, vehicle-controlled human RCT supports the visible-aging claims (n=31, 8 weeks). That is real but thin; no active-comparator trial vs retinoids/vitamin C exists.
  - The most marketing-attractive stories (DNA repair, dark-CPD, anti-melanoma, anti-senescence, matrisome rebuilding, microbiome) are **in-vitro / transcriptomic / manufacturer-funded** and should be treated as mechanism, not benefit.
  - "Stabilizes your vitamin C" is fair as a **formulation** rationale; framing it as a consumer skin benefit overreaches.

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**⚠️ CONDITIONAL — strong SUPPORTING actor, not a hero.** AZ is a credible, well-formulating, photostable antioxidant that pairs naturally with the barrier-repair thesis (it protects/complements rather than competes) and earns a place in a daytime/antioxidant step — *if* we accept that its deeper claims rest on a small, manufacturer-dominated evidence base. Use it to **augment a vitamin C** or as a defensible daytime antioxidant, with conservative "supports/helps visibly" copy; **do not** build a hero SKU or DNA/anti-aging-cure narrative on it.

- **Longevity relevance:** Mechanistically on-thesis for skin-longevity (oxidative stress, photoaging, ECM/MMP, senescence-signature, DNA-damage hallmarks), but longevity benefits remain **preclinical/transcriptomic**; the only human-proven outcome is appearance-level photoaging improvement.
