# Ingredient: Branded anti-glycation actives (NAHP/Glycostop, Collrepair DG, Aldenine, Preventhelia, Amadorine, Antiglyskin, Glistin, Ameliox, Aglycal) (INCI: various — see §1)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Class:** Anti-glycation & related · **Status:** ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product

> Brutally honest one-liner: this is a **basket of supplier-branded "anti-glycation" complexes**, not a single molecule — they share a genuinely real and underexploited mechanism (skin glycation is a true aging hallmark) but almost all of their efficacy data is **manufacturer-generated, in-vitro / ex-vivo, in MGO-spiked artificial-glycation models, with penetration to the dermis essentially never demonstrated.** The two with the **most credible human/ex-vivo data** are **NAHP (Beiersdorf)** and **Collrepair DG (BASF)**; the rest range from thin to marketing-grade. The "reverses 20 years of glycation" type claims are **the single biggest hype red flag in this entire category.** Strategic value is as a **differentiated narrative platform** built mostly on real-but-cheap generic actives (carnosine, NAHP, niacinamide), buying a branded blend only where the supplier story is defensible.

---

## 1. Identity

These are **proprietary trade-name actives** sold by ingredient houses, each a different chemistry. Treat them as **separate ingredients that happen to share a marketing lane** ("anti-glycation"). Composition and owner:

| Trade name | Owner / supplier | INCI / composition | Core claimed action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NAHP / "Glycostop"** | **Beiersdorf** (proprietary; used in Eucerin/NIVEA "Hyaluron-Filler" sugar-aging lines) | **N-Acetyl-L-Hydroxyproline** (a small, hydroxyproline derivative) | **Glycation *prevention*** — sacrificial carbonyl/sugar scavenger; blocks AGE formation [1][6] |
| **Collrepair™ DG (BC10036)** | **BASF** | **Aqua, Caprylyl Glycol, Hexylene Glycol, Niacin, Salvia Miltiorrhiza (red sage / danshen) Flower/Leaf/Root Extract, Xanthan Gum** (a niacin + botanical blend — *not* a peptide; verified vs. BASF/Azelis TDS) [2][7] | **AGE *breaking* / "deglycation"** + collagen support [2][7] |
| **Aldenine®** | **Lipotec / Lubrizol** | Aqua, **Hydrolyzed Wheat Protein, Hydrolyzed Soy Protein**, Xanthan Gum, **Tripeptide-1 (GHK)** | RCS (reactive carbonyl) scavenging + Collagen III synthesis [3] |
| **Preventhelia®** | **Lipotec / Lubrizol** | **Diaminopropionoyl Tripeptide-33** (+ solution carrier) | Quenches 4-HNE (a reactive carbonyl from lipid peroxidation); UV/DNA photoprotection [4][8] |
| **Amadorine®** | (legacy; Solabia/various) | proprietary blend (marketed vs. **Amadori products**, the *early*-stage glycation intermediates) | Blocks the Amadori→AGE step [9, unverified composition] |
| **Antiglyskin®** | **Croda Beauty / Sederma (ex-Vincience)** | proprietary peptide/aminoacid blend (composition not publicly confirmed) | Anti-glycation / AGE-formation inhibition [10, unverified] |
| **Glistin®** | **Exsymol / Biosil Technologies** | proprietary (silanol-/peptide-family active) | Anti-glycation positioning [11, unverified composition] |
| **Ameliox™** | **Mibelle Biochemistry** | **Lecithin, Carnosine, Tocopherol, Silybum Marianum Fruit Extract, Glycerin, Alcohol/Phenoxyethanol, Aqua** (liposomal) | Carnosine anti-glycation + silymarin/vit-E antioxidant [12] |
| **Aglycal® (LS 8777)** | **Laboratoires Sérobiologiques / Cognis (→ BASF)** | **Mannitol, Cyclodextrin, Glycogen, Arctostaphylos Uva Ursi Leaf Extract** | Anti-glycation + antioxidant ("dual aging" defense) [13] |

**Key forms / overlaps to be aware of:**
- **Ameliox is essentially liposomal carnosine** + milk thistle + vit-E — so its evidence collapses into our **[`carnosine.md`](carnosine.md)** and **[`celyscence-milk-thistle.md`](celyscence-milk-thistle.md)** / silymarin dossiers. It is a *delivery/blend* play, not a new molecule.
- **Aldenine is a GHK (Tripeptide-1) delivery + plant-protein blend** — its peptide story overlaps **[`copper-peptides-ghk-cu.md`](copper-peptides-ghk-cu.md)** (GHK, the metal-free form).
- **Preventhelia is really a photoprotection/anti-carbonylation peptide** that gets shelved under "anti-glycation" by association; its primary data is UV/DNA, not classic AGE inhibition.
- **NAHP and Collrepair DG are the two purpose-built, publication-backed anti-glycation actives** in the basket and deserve most of the attention.

## 2. Class & mechanism

All of these sit on the **glycation cascade**, which has three intervention points. The branded actives cluster differently — and *which* point they hit determines how plausible the claim is:

1. **Sugar / dicarbonyl scavenging (PREVENT formation) — the most defensible.**
   Reducing sugars (glucose, fructose) and **reactive dicarbonyls / reactive carbonyl species (RCS)** — methylglyoxal (MGO), glyoxal, 3-deoxyglucosone, and the lipid-peroxidation carbonyl **4-HNE** — react non-enzymatically with lysine/arginine residues on long-lived dermal proteins. A **sacrificial nucleophile** that mops up sugars/RCS *before* they cross-link proteins prevents new AGEs. **NAHP** (sugar/carbonyl neutralization) [1][6], **Aldenine** (RCS/HNE scavenging via GHK) [3], **Preventhelia** (4-HNE quenching) [4][8], and **carnosine in Ameliox** [12] all claim this mode. Mechanistically sound; this is the lane with real chemistry behind it.

2. **AGE-crosslink *breaking* / "deglycation" (REVERSE existing damage) — the most over-claimed.**
   Once collagen/elastin are cross-linked by mature AGEs (pentosidine, **glucosepane**, CML, MG-H1), the matrix stiffens, yellows and resists turnover. **Collrepair DG** is marketed as *breaking* established crosslinks both intra- and extracellularly [2][7]. This is the **alagebrium (ALT-711) dream** — and alagebrium, the most-studied true "AGE breaker," **never made it through clinical development** [5][14]. Glucosepane (the dominant human dermal crosslink) has **no validated breaker** to date. So "deglycation in a cream" claims warrant the highest skepticism.

3. **Glyoxalase / enzymatic detoxification (UP-REGULATE the body's own defense).**
   The **glyoxalase-1 (GLO1)** system detoxifies MGO endogenously and declines with age. Some actives (and the fish collagen-tripeptide comparator) claim to *raise GLO1 activity* [15]. Biologically elegant but, for the branded basket, largely theoretical / in-vitro.

**Net framing:** the category's *prevention* claims are credible chemistry; its *reversal/deglycation* claims are the frontier of cardiovascular research that **failed to clear clinical trials in a far better-funded setting** — do not let cosmetic copy imply the reversal problem is solved.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Mechanistic rationale | Strength of *topical human* evidence for this basket |
|---|---|---|
| **Glycation / AGEs** | The whole point — scavenge sugars/RCS, (claimed) break crosslinks | Moderate for *prevention* (NAHP ex-vivo; collagen-tripeptide in-vivo); **weak/unproven for *reversal*** |
| **Collagen / ECM loss** | Protect collagen from stiffening; Collrepair/Aldenine claim collagen support | Weak-indirect (mostly in-vitro fibroblast data) |
| **Oxidative stress** | RCS/4-HNE and lipid-peroxidation overlap with ROS; Ameliox/Aglycal add antioxidants | Moderate (but generic to any antioxidant) |
| **Photoaging** | UV drives RCS/AGEs; Preventhelia targets UVA/DNA | Weak (Preventhelia data in-vitro) |
| **Pigmentation / tone (sallowness/yellowing)** | AGEs yellow the dermis; reducing them claims to restore radiance | Weak (claim-grade; Collrepair "17 yrs sallowness" is supplier data) |
| **Mitochondrial decline** | Tangential (RCS damage mitochondria) | Negligible / theoretical |
| **Inflammaging** | AGE–RAGE axis is pro-inflammatory | In-vitro / mechanistic only |
| **Cellular senescence** | Glycative stress can drive senescence | In-vitro only |
| **Hydration / barrier / microbiome** | Not primary mechanisms (Aglycal adds humectant mannitol) | Negligible (incidental) |

**Distinctive lane = glycation + the visible "sallowness/yellowing" story.** Few mainstream actives address glycation head-on, so this is a genuine **white-space narrative** — see [`carnosine.md`](carnosine.md) §3 for the same strategic point.

## 4. Evidence review by tier (the core)

> **Read this first.** Across this entire basket there is **no independent (non-manufacturer), double-blind, placebo-controlled topical RCT** isolating any one branded active. The strongest data is **in-vitro / ex-vivo in artificial MGO-glycation models**, generated by the supplier. The **best human-skin evidence for the *mechanism* (topical glycation reduction)** comes from **non-branded comparators** — a fish collagen-tripeptide pilot [15] and a Korean tryptamine-derivative trial [16] — not from the marquee branded blends. The independent reviews are blunt that **topical anti-glycation is real in principle but clinically immature** [5][17][20].

### Tier 1 — In-vitro / chemistry (the bulk of the data; supplier-dominated)

- **NAHP — Knoblich et al. (2024), *Int J Cosmet Sci* 46(2)** [6] (Beiersdorf). Screened **~1,700 compounds**; N-acetyl-L-hydroxyproline **dose-dependently inhibited AGE formation in protein solutions (up to ~68%)** and in cell-based assays under glycation stress. Mechanism: neutralizes sugars/carbonyls pre-protein-binding. **Best-characterized prevention chemistry in the basket; manufacturer-authored.**
- **Collrepair DG — BASF technical dossier / Leoty-Okombi et al. (conference)** [2][7]. In-vitro: claims to cleave AGE crosslinks in fibroblasts and in dermal matrix; ex-vivo reductions in AGE markers and "restoration of fiber flexibility." **No independent replication located; trade-literature, not a peer-reviewed RCT.**
- **Aldenine — Lipotec in-vitro (Univ. of Milan collaboration)** [3]. GHK/Tripeptide-1 scavenges RCS (HNE) in a SOD-glycation model; plant proteins claimed to **boost Collagen III "up to 300% in a week" in human fibroblasts.** The 300% figure is a **monoculture gene/protein readout, not skin** — classic in-vitro inflation.
- **Preventhelia — Lipotec in-vitro** [4][8]. Diaminopropionoyl Tripeptide-33 quenches 4-HNE; reduces UVA-induced DNA breaks (comet assay) in keratinocytes/melanocytes; raises Neutral-Red viability post-UVA. **All cellular; an anti-carbonylation/photoprotection peptide more than a classic AGE inhibitor.**
- **Ameliox — Mibelle in-vitro** [12]. Liposomal carnosine + silymarin + tocopherol shows anti-glycation + antioxidant activity in vitro. Inherits the **carnosine** anti-glycation chemistry (well-replicated; see [`carnosine.md`](carnosine.md) §4 Tier 1) and milk-thistle/silymarin anti-glycation data (e.g., Choi et al., milk thistle flower phenolics on human explants) [18].
- **Aglycal, Antiglyskin, Glistin, Amadorine** — supplier claims of anti-glycation/antioxidant activity; **no peer-reviewed primary studies located** for the branded forms. Composition of Antiglyskin/Glistin/Amadorine not publicly confirmed → mark **[unverified]**.
- **Independent mechanism base:** carnosine anti-glycation chemistry is robust and well-replicated [Ghodsi & Kheirouri 2018, see carnosine.md]; resveratrol-derivative anti-glycation in 3D human-skin models (Boo et al., 2022, *Clin Cosmet Investig Dermatol*) [19]; blueberry/Ericaceae and milk-thistle phenolics in explants [17][18]. These validate the *category chemistry* — but for **generic, cheaper actives**, not the branded blends.

**Verdict on Tier 1:** the *chemistry of preventing* glycation is real and reproducible across many actives. The *chemistry of breaking* established crosslinks (Collrepair's pitch) is the weak, largely unreplicated, commercially-motivated part.

### Tier 2 — Animal

- Sparse for the branded actives specifically. The broader AGE field has animal data: **alagebrium (ALT-711)** reduced collagen crosslinking and improved arterial/skin elasticity in aged/diabetic rodents [5][14]; aminoguanidine reduced AGE accumulation in diabetic animals [17]. **Neither is in cosmetic use** — alagebrium's program halted (funding), aminoguanidine dropped for safety/efficacy balance [5][17]. These are the cautionary precedents, not support for the cosmetic blends.

**Verdict on Tier 2:** the best-funded AGE-breaker programs in animals **did not translate to an approved product** — a sober backdrop for any "breaks AGEs" cosmetic claim.

### Tier 3 — Human, ORAL

- Not applicable to the branded *topical* actives. The adjacent oral evidence (carnosine/HA/MSM nutraceutical RCT; fish collagen-peptide oral studies) is covered in [`carnosine.md`](carnosine.md) §4 Tier 3 and is **not transferable to a topical claim.**

### Tier 4 — Human, TOPICAL (the tier that matters — and the thinnest for the branded actives)

**For the branded blends specifically:** efficacy rests on **supplier in-vivo panels** that are **open-label, uncontrolled, small, and unpublished in peer-reviewed journals** (Collrepair "20 years"; Ameliox "−24%/−27% wrinkles"; Aglycal panels). These are **claim-substantiation studies, not independent RCTs.**

- **Collrepair DG (BASF)** [2][7] — supplier in-vivo claim: in **60–69-year-olds**, removes "**~20 years of accumulated AGEs**," "**~17 years of sallowness/yellowing**," and improves elasticity. **No peer-reviewed publication, no public protocol, no control arm disclosed, manufacturer-run.** The "20 years reversed" framing is biologically implausible for a leave-on cream against glucosepane crosslinks (no validated breaker exists) — treat as **marketing.**
- **Ameliox (Mibelle)** [12] — in-vivo: **forehead wrinkle depth −24%, crow's-feet −27%**, plus smoothness/firmness/hydration. **No published sample size, duration, control, or independent replication.** Use level 1–5%.
- **Aglycal, Aldenine, Preventhelia, Antiglyskin, Glistin, Amadorine** — supplier marketing references in-vivo "improvement" but **no independent, controlled human topical trial located.** Mark efficacy **[unverified]** for human topical use.

**The most credible human-skin data in the whole category is from NON-branded comparators** (use to support the *mechanism*, not any one brand):

- **Topical collagen tripeptide (EverCTP™, NOT a brand on our list) — Lee et al. (2022), *Int J Mol Sci* 23(3):1101** [15]. **Prospective single-arm pilot, n=22 Asian women (mean 47 y), 4 weeks, BID.** 4% Gly-Pro-Hyp tripeptide. **Skin AGE autofluorescence fell 2.26→2.16 AU (p<0.001)**; periorbital roughness 20.77→19.24 µm (p<0.001); density +; R2 elasticity 0.81→0.83 (p<0.001). In-vitro: ↓pentosidine/MGO/AGEs, **↑glyoxalase-1**, ↓MMP-1/-3/-9, ↑Col-1. **Limitations:** small, short, **no control arm**, single ethnicity, **sponsor = NEWTREE (manufacturer).** Still the cleanest published demonstration that a topical can *measurably lower skin AGE autofluorescence in vivo.*
- **N-dichloroacetyl tryptamine (NdCAT, a research compound — NOT a brand on our list) — Shin et al. (2025), *J Cosmet Dermatol*** [16]. In-vitro + ex-vivo + **clinical n=21 Asian women (mean 44.9 y), all in their 40s, 4 weeks, 10 ppm topical.** The eye-catching **−55.5%p (2 wk) / −66.9%p (4 wk)** reduction is **corneocyte fluorescence-intensity from only 6 randomly selected participants**, not the full-cohort skin-autofluorescence (SAF) endpoint — so the headline number rests on n=6, not n=21 (the full-cohort SAF also fell, but by less). Elasticity & hydration ↑ (p<0.001). **Limitations:** tiny, short, single demographic (40s, one ethnicity), **open-label, no placebo arm**, headline figure from a 6-person sub-sample, and the compound is an Incospharm (industry) candidate developed under a Korean government commercialization grant — i.e., a pre-commercial research active, not an independent third-party result.

**What is actually proven for human TOPICAL use:**
- **Proven (modest, mechanism-level):** a topical *can* reduce skin **AGE autofluorescence** and modestly improve elasticity/roughness over 4 weeks — but the cleanest evidence is for **non-branded collagen tripeptide** [15] and a **research compound** [16], both small/uncontrolled/sponsor-linked.
- **Proven for the branded basket specifically:** essentially **nothing at independent-RCT level.** NAHP has the strongest *mechanistic* (ex-vivo) package [6]; Collrepair has the boldest *unsubstantiated* in-vivo claim [2].
- **NOT established:** *reversal* of established AGE crosslinks ("deglycation") in human dermis by any cosmetic; the specific % claims (20 years, −27% wrinkles, 300% Collagen III) as consumer benefits; dermal delivery of the active to where collagen glycates.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

This is the category's **shared Achilles' heel** and applies to *every* item on the list:

- The therapeutic target — **glycated collagen/elastin — lives in the dermis**, below the stratum corneum. A leave-on cream must deliver an *intact, sufficient* dose of a polar, often peptidic active to the dermis. **None of the branded actives publishes human penetration/dermal-delivery data** confirming this happens.
- The published positive ex-vivo/in-vivo studies (NAHP [6]; collagen tripeptide [15]) **measure outcome markers (AGE autofluorescence, MMPs) without measuring how much active reached the dermis** — so we cannot attribute the effect to dermal action vs. surface/epidermal effects or formulation artifacts. This is the **same penetration gap flagged for carnosine** ([`carnosine.md`](carnosine.md) §5).
- **Peptidic / polar actives** (Aldenine's GHK/plant proteins, Preventhelia's tripeptide-33) face stratum-corneum partitioning limits and peptidase degradation; Collrepair's botanical (red sage) tanshinones and niacin face their own polarity/partitioning hurdles for reaching the dermis.
- **Encapsulation is the partial answer used in the better products:** **Ameliox is explicitly liposomal** [12]; **Aglycal includes cyclodextrin** [13]. Encapsulated/liposomal grades materially strengthen the delivery story (and claim defensibility) over plain aqueous blends.

**Implication:** any anti-glycation positioning we adopt should (a) prefer **encapsulated** forms, and (b) lean on **prevention** language (acting on freshly forming AGEs over time) rather than **reversal** (which needs deep, sustained dermal delivery that is unproven).

## 6. Formulation

- **Use levels (supplier-recommended):** highly active-specific — e.g. **Ameliox 1–5%** [12]; peptide solutions (Aldenine, Preventhelia, Collrepair) typically **1–5–10%** of the trade material (which is itself a dilute solution — actual active is far lower); NAHP used at low % in finished products. **There is no established in-vivo dose-response for any of them.** Follow each supplier's TDS and substantiate at the finished-product level.
- **pH:** most are water-phase, near-neutral-tolerant (peptide/aminoacid actives prefer ~5–7). Carnosine-based Ameliox favors ~6–7.5 (see carnosine.md §6). Confirm per TDS.
- **Stability:** peptide and protein-hydrolysate actives (Aldenine, Antiglyskin) need protection from heat/oxidation and microbial load (Aldenine carries hydrolyzed wheat/soy protein → preservation discipline). Collrepair's red-sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) phenolics/tanshinones are oxidation- and light-sensitive botanicals → protect from heat/light. Liposomal Ameliox needs gentle processing (don't high-shear/high-heat the liposomes).
- **Pairings (sensible):**
  - **Antioxidant/anti-glycation stacking** — niacinamide, carnosine, ergothioneine, vitamin E, blueberry/Ericaceae extracts, resveratrol, milk thistle/silymarin. A coherent "anti-glycation + antioxidant defense" complex.
  - **Humectants/barrier** — glycerin, HA, ceramides for the hydration/comfort layer.
  - **Retinoids** — logical co-positioning (both "anti-aging"), but watch irritation and pH; layer thoughtfully.
- **Conflicts:** **GHK-containing Aldenine** is incompatible with strong chelators/high-acid systems (same caution as GHK-Cu — see [`copper-peptides-ghk-cu.md`](copper-peptides-ghk-cu.md)); **hydrolyzed wheat/gluten proteins** (Aldenine, some Antiglyskin grades) carry an **allergen flag** (see §7). Liposomes dislike high electrolyte/high-alcohol systems.
- **Sensory:** mostly water-soluble, low-odor, formulation-neutral at use levels; liposomal/protein blends can add slight tack or cloudiness.

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation/sensitization:** generally **well tolerated** at cosmetic use levels; these are mostly small peptides, aminoacid derivatives, or antioxidants. Standard patch-test advice.
- **⚠️ Wheat/gluten allergen flag (specific & important):** **Aldenine** (and some anti-glycation blends) contain **Hydrolyzed Wheat Protein**. Hydrolyzed wheat proteins in cosmetics have a documented history of **IgE-mediated contact urticaria and even anaphylaxis** (the well-known "Yuka/Glupearl-19S" soap cases in Japan) [21]. This must be disclosed and is a **meaningful contraindication for wheat-allergic users.** Prefer wheat-free anti-glycation actives if we want a clean allergen profile.
- **Sun sensitivity:** no photosensitization signal; several actives are antioxidant/photoprotection-adjacent (Preventhelia, Ameliox) — do **not** over-claim "sun protection."
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** no specific red flags for these cosmetic actives; defer to "consult your physician" copy rather than asserting safety.
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** synthetic/fermentation/plant-derived; no major provenance issues. Confirm vegan status (Aldenine's plant proteins are plant-derived; Ameliox lecithin is typically soy — confirm). Collrepair DG is a niacin + botanical (red sage) blend, so it has no peptide counter-ion concern; standard botanical-extract provenance applies.

## 8. Regulatory & claims

- **Cosmetic, not drug** — all are recognized cosmetic ingredients in the EU/US **provided claims stay appearance-based.** Anti-glycation itself is a **structure/function gray zone**: "prevents glycation," "breaks AGEs," and "reverses X years of aging" describe *biological alteration of the skin's proteins* and are the **most drug-adjacent, most substantiation-hungry claims in this category.**
- ✅ **Safer cosmetic language:** "helps defend the look of skin against glycation (sugar-related dullness, stiffness and yellowing)," "antioxidant support," "helps protect the appearance of skin's firmness and radiance from everyday sugar and environmental stress," "for a fresher, more even-toned complexion."
- ⚠️ **Risky / likely-needs-legal-and-substantiation — flag every one of these:**
  - **"Reverses 20 years of glycation," "breaks AGEs," "deglycates skin," "reverses sallowness by 17 years"** (Collrepair-style) — **reversal of dermal crosslinks is unproven for cosmetics; the best-funded AGE-breaker never cleared trials.** These are the highest-risk claims here.
  - **Specific efficacy %** ("−27% wrinkles," "68% less AGEs," "300% Collagen III") quoted as **consumer benefits** — these are **in-vitro / supplier-panel / ex-vivo MGO-model numbers**; using them as on-skin claims needs robust substantiation/legal review.
  - **"Anti-glycation = anti-aging," "treats/prevents aging," "boosts collagen"** — drug-adjacent and/or unproven for human skin.
  - **"Clinically proven"** for any branded blend — the supporting data is open-label/uncontrolled/manufacturer-run; not defensible as independent clinical proof.
- See claims guardrail in [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md) and [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Cost tier:** **moderate-to-high.** These are **proprietary branded actives** sold at a premium over generic anti-glycation chemistry. Per *delivered active*, you pay a brand markup vs. buying **carnosine, NAHP-equivalent chemistry, niacinamide, or blueberry/silymarin extract** directly.
- **Strategic cost note (important):** much of the category's *real* mechanism is achievable with **cheap, generic actives** (carnosine, niacinamide, antioxidants). The branded blends mainly buy you (a) a **delivery system** (Ameliox liposomes, Aglycal cyclodextrin) and (b) a **claim-substantiation story/dossier** you don't have to fund yourself. Decide whether that dossier is worth the markup — for Collrepair the dossier is bold but weak; for NAHP it's the most credible.
- **Supply:** all are available through their owners (BASF, Lubrizol/Lipotec, Croda/Sederma, Mibelle, Exsymol) with standard cosmetic MOQs. NAHP is Beiersdorf-proprietary (harder to source as the exact "Glycostop" — but N-acetyl-hydroxyproline chemistry is obtainable generically).

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

| Marketing claim seen in the wild | Reality |
|---|---|
| **"Reverses 20 years of skin glycation in 4 months" (Collrepair DG)** | **Supplier in-vivo panel, no peer-reviewed publication, no control arm.** Reversing mature dermal crosslinks (glucosepane) is the exact problem the best-funded AGE-breaker (alagebrium) **failed to solve clinically** [5][14]. Biologically implausible for a cream; the #1 hype flag here. |
| **"Boosts Collagen III up to 300% in a week" (Aldenine)** | **In-vitro fibroblast monoculture readout** [3], not human skin. Standard in-vitro inflation; not a skin claim. |
| **"−24%/−27% wrinkle depth" (Ameliox)** | **Manufacturer in-vivo, no published n/duration/control** [12]. Carnosine + silymarin + vit-E is a reasonable antioxidant blend, but the % is not independently verified. |
| **"68% AGE inhibition" (NAHP)** | True *in a protein-solution / cell assay* [6] — the most credible mechanism number here — but it is an **in-vitro prevention** figure, **not** an in-vivo consumer benefit. |
| **"Anti-glycation = the new anti-aging hero ingredient"** | Glycation is a **real hallmark** and an underexploited *narrative* lane — but **topical anti-glycation is clinically immature**; independent reviews say current topicals are "few and not very effective" [5][17][20]. Genuine angle, oversold maturity. |
| **"Clinically proven to break AGEs / deglycate"** | No independent RCT for any branded active; "deglycation" in cosmetics is unproven. The credible human data (AGE-autofluorescence drop) is for **prevention** with **non-branded** actives [15][16]. |
| **"Penetrates to repair glycated collagen in the dermis"** | **Dermal delivery never demonstrated** for these actives (§5). Outcome markers measured without penetration data. |

**Bottom line:** the **mechanism and the hallmark are real and differentiated**, the actives are **mostly safe and easy to formulate**, but the **branded efficacy claims wildly outrun the independent evidence**, and the boldest ("reversal/deglycation") sit on a foundation that **failed in better-funded pharma trials.** Not snake oil — but heavily over-marketed, and the value is more **narrative + prevention** than **proven repair.**

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL — own the *anti-glycation story*, but build it mostly on cheap generic actives + (at most) one credible branded blend; never on the "reversal" claims.**

- **One-line reasoning:** Glycation is a genuine, under-owned aging hallmark and a strong narrative lane, but the **branded "anti-glycation" actives are a premium-priced, manufacturer-evidenced, penetration-unproven basket** whose boldest claims (deglycation/"20 years reversed") are unsubstantiated — so it earns a *supporting/narrative* role, not a hero, and only via prevention-framed copy.
- **If we use a branded active, ranking by evidence credibility:** **(1) NAHP / N-acetyl-hydroxyproline** (best mechanism dossier, Beiersdorf-published, *prevention*) → **(2) Ameliox** (liposomal carnosine + antioxidants; collapses into our carnosine/silymarin science, decent delivery) → **(3) Aglycal** (dual anti-glycation/antioxidant, encapsulated) → **(skip or de-prioritize) Collrepair DG** (boldest, weakest claim), **Aldenine** (wheat-allergen + in-vitro 300% inflation), **Antiglyskin / Glistin / Amadorine** (composition/evidence [unverified]).
- **Cheaper equivalent path:** much of the mechanism is deliverable with **carnosine + niacinamide + blueberry/Ericaceae + silymarin + ergothioneine** — consider an in-house "anti-glycation defense" complex and reserve a branded buy only where the supplier dossier is worth the markup (i.e., NAHP).
- **Hard guardrails:** prevention language only; no "reverse/break/deglycate"; no supplier % as consumer claims; flag the **wheat allergen** if Aldenine-type actives are used; prefer **encapsulated** forms.
- **Longevity-relevance note:** strong *conceptual* fit — glycation/AGEs is a bona fide longevity hallmark linked to crosslinking, stiffness and inflammaging — so this is excellent **scaffolding for a skin-longevity worldview**, provided we keep the in-vitro/supplier science clearly separated from what's proven on human skin. Pairs naturally with the brand's other longevity sub-themes (carnosine, spermidine, NAD/niacinamide, antioxidant network).

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19, by independent source-checking of the cited literature (fetched/searched Wiley, PMC, MDPI, ResearchGate, BASF/Azelis, Mibelle, ci.guide, ClinicalTrials.gov, Beiersdorf, COSSMA).

### Overall evidence grade: **C (weak / indirect for the branded basket)** — with one **D** sub-grade

For the **branded actives as marketed for human topical anti-aging**, the grade is **C, trending D on the reversal claims:**

- **No item in the basket has independent (non-manufacturer), placebo-controlled, human-topical RCT evidence.** This is verified, not asserted — every supplier dataset checked (NAHP, Collrepair, Aldenine, Preventhelia, Ameliox, Aglycal) is in-vitro / ex-vivo or an unpublished/uncontrolled supplier in-vivo panel.
- The **strongest mechanism dossier (NAHP [6])** is real and accurately described, but is **in-vitro + ex-vivo only with no human in-vivo AGE measurement** → at best a **B for mechanism, C for human topical benefit.**
- The **AGE-*reversal* / "deglycation" claims (Collrepair) earn a D** — biologically implausible for a leave-on cosmetic against mature dermal crosslinks (glucosepane has no validated breaker), and the best-funded true AGE-breaker (alagebrium) had its Phase 2 program (NCT00516646, "BENEFICIAL") **terminated early for funding**, confirming the precedent the dossier cites.
- The cleanest *human-topical* signal in the whole space (a measurable drop in skin AGE autofluorescence) comes from **non-branded comparators** [15][16] that are themselves small, short, uncontrolled and sponsor-linked → **C+ for the mechanism, not transferable to any brand.**

### Main caveats (read before using any of this in copy)

1. **The whole basket is manufacturer-evidenced.** Treat every efficacy % as a supplier/in-vitro number until a finished-product, controlled study exists. "Clinically proven" is **not** defensible for any branded blend.
2. **Human + topical evidence is the thinnest tier and is borrowed.** The only published human-topical AGE-reduction signals are non-branded ([15][16]), small (n=21–22), short (4 wk), uncontrolled, and sponsor/grant-linked. Do not present them as proof for NAHP/Collrepair/etc.
3. **Reversal/"deglycation" is essentially unproven (grade D).** No validated breaker exists for the dominant human dermal crosslink (glucosepane); the lead pharma AGE-breaker's program was halted. Collrepair's "20 years reversed" is the category's biggest red flag.
4. **Dermal delivery is never demonstrated** for any item — outcome markers are measured without confirming the active reaches glycated dermal collagen.
5. **Identity error corrected:** the original draft listed Collrepair DG as a peptidomimetic (*Tetradecyl Aminobutyroylvalylaminobutyric Urea Trifluoroacetate*). The **actual BASF Collrepair DG (BC10036) INCI is Aqua, Caprylyl Glycol, Hexylene Glycol, Niacin, Salvia Miltiorrhiza (red sage) Flower/Leaf/Root Extract, Xanthan Gum** — a niacin + botanical blend. Fixed in §1 and downstream (§5–§7).

- **§4 / §12:** Shin [16] −66.9%p reframed as a **6-person corneocyte sub-analysis**, not the full-cohort SAF endpoint; flagged open-label + industry/grant funding.
- **§1, §5, §6, §7:** **Collrepair DG composition corrected** from peptidomimetic to the niacin + red-sage blend; all downstream "peptide"/counter-ion references updated.
