# Ingredient: Cytokines in conditioned-media complexes — IL-6, IL-8, G-CSF, NGF, HB-EGF (the "inflammatory/neurotrophic" sub-fraction of secretomes)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ⬜ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ☑ rejected (lean SKIP as a marketed actor) / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Growth factors, cytokines, exosomes, conditioned media & regenerative · **Slug:** cytokine-complexes

> **One-line bottom line:** This is **not a buyable raw material** — it is the **interleukin/colony-stimulating/neurotrophin sub-fraction that rides along inside conditioned-media (CM) / secretome products** (fibroblast TNS, ADSC-CM, umbilical-cord-blood-MSC CM, etc.). Four of the five named factors — **IL-6, IL-8, G-CSF, NGF** — are, in the *primary aging literature*, **markers and effectors of exactly the things a longevity brand is trying to fight**: the **senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP)** and **inflammaging** (IL-6/IL-8 are the canonical SASP duo [1][2][3]; chronically elevated IL-6 is a hallmark of inflammaging and is *associated with worse aging outcomes* [4][5]; NGF drives sensory-nerve sprouting/sensitization and itch [6][7]). Only **HB-EGF** is a clean, EGFR-axis repair/re-epithelialization signal [8][9]. So a brand that markets "cytokine complex" as anti-aging is, scientifically, **selling the SASP back to the customer** — a genuine honesty trap. On top of that, the whole sub-fraction inherits every CM weakness: large fragile proteins **far above the ~500-Da skin-penetration ceiling**, no standardization, no independent RCT *on this fraction*, and the **FDA's active 2024–2025 enforcement** against regenerative/secretome claims [10]. **Lean SKIP** as a *named, marketed* active. (You cannot fully avoid these molecules if you use any CM/secretome — but you should never *headline* them, and they argue against the CM/secretome category for a longevity-positioned brand.)

---

## 1. Identity

**What the brand actually means by "cytokine complex":** a shorthand for a **named bundle of signaling proteins that are present inside conditioned media / secretome ingredients** — specifically the *non-classical-growth-factor* signals: interleukins (**IL-6, IL-8**), a colony-stimulating factor (**G-CSF**), and a neurotrophin (**NGF**), plus one bona-fide EGF-family growth factor (**HB-EGF**). They are not sold as a stand-alone INCI; they are **constituents** of larger CM/secretome materials (see `conditioned-media-secretomes.md`, `exosomes.md`, `growth-factors.md`).

**INCI / label reality:** there is **no INCI called "cytokine complex."** On a finished label these factors are *invisible* — they are subsumed under:
- **"Human Fibroblast Conditioned Media"** (the SkinMedica TNS / NouriCel-MD family — supplier copy claims "380+ growth factors and cytokines," and ingredient references explicitly list **IL-6 and IL-8** among them) [11][12];
- **"Human/Adipose/Umbilical-cord Conditioned Media"** or "…Cell Conditioned Media" (ADSC-CM, UCB-MSC-CM) [13];
- generic **"Cytokines"** or branded secretome trade names.

Because they ride inside an undefined biological broth, **the exact identity, concentration, and ratio of IL-6/IL-8/G-CSF/NGF/HB-EGF in any given product is unknown and lot-variable** — a central problem (see §4, §6).

**The five factors — identity, size, native source, canonical biology:**

| Factor | Full name | Approx. MW (monomer) | Native skin source | Canonical role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **IL-6** | Interleukin-6 | ~21–26 kDa (glycosylated) [14] | Fibroblasts, keratinocytes, immune cells; **secreted by senescent cells (SASP)** | Pleiotropic pro-inflammatory cytokine; acute-phase/immune signaling; **core SASP + inflammaging marker** [1][3][4] |
| **IL-8 (CXCL8)** | Interleukin-8 | ~8–10 kDa [15] | Keratinocytes, fibroblasts; **SASP** | Neutrophil chemokine; **core SASP factor**; drives/maintains senescence-inflammation loop [1][2] |
| **G-CSF (CSF3)** | Granulocyte colony-stimulating factor | ~19 kDa [16] | Various stromal/immune cells | Granulopoiesis; in wounds, supports angiogenesis / scarless-regeneration signaling [17][18] |
| **NGF (β-NGF)** | Nerve growth factor (beta) | ~13 kDa monomer (~26 kDa dimer) | **Basal keratinocytes**, fibroblasts, mast/Merkel cells [6] | Neurotrophin; sensory/autonomic nerve growth & sensitization; keratinocyte proliferation modulation; **itch/inflammation mediator** [6][7] |
| **HB-EGF** | Heparin-binding EGF-like growth factor | ~16–23 kDa (processed) [16] | Keratinocytes, macrophages | EGFR/ErbB ligand; **the predominant driver of keratinocyte *migration* and re-epithelialization in wounds** [8][9] |

> **Critical identity note:** the marketing instinct is to lump these as "regenerative growth factors." Biologically that's only true for **HB-EGF** (an EGF-family growth factor) and arguably **G-CSF** (a regenerative colony-stimulating factor in wounds). **IL-6, IL-8 are interleukins/chemokines** whose dominant identity in the *aging* literature is **pro-inflammatory SASP**, and **NGF is a neurotrophin** whose dominant skin identity is **nerve sprouting / itch / sensitization**, not "rejuvenation." Conflating the five hides the problem (see §10).

## 2. Class & mechanism

These factors sit in the **conditioned-media / secretome / regenerative** class, but mechanistically they split into **two incompatible stories**:

**(a) The "repair" story the marketing wants to tell.** In an *acute wound*, a coordinated cytokine cascade orchestrates healing: **HB-EGF** binds **EGFR/ErbB** and drives keratinocyte **migration** to close the wound (it accelerates migration more than proliferation) [8][9]; **G-CSF** supports **angiogenesis, anti-apoptosis, and even scarless regeneration / hair-follicle neogenesis** in animal wound models [17][18]; **IL-6** and **IL-8** transiently recruit immune cells and prime fibroblasts; **NGF** re-innervates the healing field. Topically resupplying this milieu is meant to "re-instruct" tired skin toward a repair phenotype. This is real biology — **in a wound.**

**(b) The "aging" story the literature actually tells.** In *intact, chronologically/photo-aged skin* the very same molecules are the machinery of decline:
- **IL-6 + IL-8 are the two most-cited components of the SASP** — the suite of factors secreted by senescent cells (Coppé/Campisi 2008; reviewed for skin) [1][2][3]. They form a **self-reinforcing senescence-inflammation loop** (IL-1α → IL-6/IL-8 network; IL-6/IL-8 reinforce senescence in neighbours) [2][19]. Senescent fibroblasts secreting this milieu **suppress collagen synthesis and upregulate MMPs**, i.e. they *cause* ECM loss [20][21].
- **IL-6 is a hallmark of *inflammaging*** (chronic, low-grade, age-associated inflammation; Franceschi 2000) — serum IL-6/IL-8/TNF-α rise with age, and **higher IL-6 is associated with *unsuccessful* aging and mortality** [4][5][21].
- **NGF** in skin drives **sensory-nerve sprouting and sensitization** and is **elevated in itchy/inflammatory dermatoses** (atopic dermatitis, psoriasis), correlating with itch severity [6][7] — the opposite of "calm."
- **G-CSF / HB-EGF** are the least problematic, but their pro-proliferative/EGFR/angiogenic actions carry their own theoretical cautions (see §7).

**The mechanistic verdict:** of the five, **only HB-EGF (and conditionally G-CSF)** map cleanly onto a *desirable* skin-aging mechanism. **IL-6, IL-8, and NGF are, in the aging-skin context, more naturally read as part of the *problem* than the solution.** A "cytokine complex" anti-aging claim therefore rests on borrowing **acute-wound** logic and quietly ignoring the **chronic-aging** logic of the same molecules.

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Plausibility (for THIS sub-fraction) | Honest read |
|---|---|---|
| **Inflammaging / cell-cell communication** | **Adverse, not protective** | IL-6/IL-8 are *defining inflammaging/SASP markers* [1][3][4]; topically *adding* them is mechanistically backwards. Any "modulates inflammation" claim (as cosmetic copy frames IL-6/IL-8 [12]) is the *opposite* of their dominant aging role. |
| **Cellular senescence** | **Adverse, not protective** | IL-6/IL-8 *propagate* senescence (paracrine bystander effect) [2][19]. This sub-fraction is closer to a *pro-senescent* signature than a senolytic one. |
| **Collagen / ECM loss** | Mixed-to-adverse | HB-EGF/G-CSF could support repair; but IL-6/IL-8-rich SASP *suppresses collagen and raises MMPs* [20][21]. Net effect of the *fraction* is ambiguous at best. |
| **Barrier decline / re-epithelialization** | Low-moderate (HB-EGF only) | HB-EGF genuinely drives keratinocyte migration/re-epithelialization [8][9] — but that's wound biology, not leave-on barrier repair of intact skin. |
| **Photoaging (lines/texture/tone)** | Weak, borrowed | Only via the parent CM/GF category's modest signal [22]; nothing attributable to this fraction. |
| **Pigmentation / tone** | Weak/uncertain | NGF has been *patented* for pigmentation modulation [6]; no robust cosmetic efficacy. |
| **Oxidative stress** | None | Not their mechanism. |
| **Glycation / AGEs** | None | Not a mechanism. |
| **Mitochondrial decline** | None | Not a mechanism. |
| **Hydration / water-loss** | None (intrinsic) | Any "feel" comes from the CM carrier base, not these proteins. |
| **Microbiome** | None | Not a mechanism. |
| **Sensory comfort** | **Adverse** | NGF promotes nerve sensitization/itch [6][7] — a reason for caution in sensitive/reactive skin. |

**Honest summary:** the *only* hallmark this fraction plausibly helps is **re-epithelialization/repair via HB-EGF (± G-CSF)** — and that is wound logic. For **senescence, inflammaging, and ECM**, the IL-6/IL-8/NGF members point the **wrong way**. This is the rare ingredient where deeper reading makes the case *worse*, not better.

## 4. Evidence review by tier — the core of this dossier

> **Reading guide (critical).** There is **no product, supplier, or trial that sells or studies "IL-6 + IL-8 + G-CSF + NGF + HB-EGF" as a defined topical complex.** These factors are **detected analytes inside conditioned media** [11][12][13]. So there are **two evidence questions**, and they pull apart:
> 1. *What do these individual cytokines do to skin?* — answerable from primary literature (below), and the answer is **mixed-to-unfavorable for aging**.
> 2. *Does a CM/secretome product that contains them rejuvenate skin?* — answerable only by **borrowing** the parent CM/GF clinical data, which is **modest, heterogeneous, and mostly manufacturer-run** [22], and which **cannot be attributed to this fraction**.
> The dossier keeps these separate and refuses to launder wound/in-vitro data into a leave-on anti-aging claim for the fraction.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro (cell culture)

**(a) For the "repair" members — supportive but wound-framed:**
- **Shirakata Y, Kimura R, Nanba D, et al. (2005)**, *J Cell Sci* 118(11):2363–2370 — in human/mouse keratinocyte scrape-wound assays (and HB-EGF-knockout mice, Tier 2), **HB-EGF is the predominant EGFR ligand driving keratinocyte *migration* and re-epithelialization** (accelerates migration more than proliferation) [8]. Real, well-characterized — but it is **wound re-epithelialization**, not anti-wrinkle biology. [8][9]
- **G-CSF** protects endothelial cells, enhances angiogenesis and attenuates apoptosis in injury models; recent work shows **G-CSF promotes scarless regeneration and hair-follicle neogenesis** in mouse wounds [17][18]. Again: **wound/regeneration**, not intact-skin aging.

**(b) For the "SASP" members — the damaging story:**
- **Coppé JP, Patil CK, Rodier F, … Campisi J (2008)**, *PLoS Biol* 6(12):2853–2868 (PMID 19053174) — foundational SASP paper: **IL-6 and IL-8 are barely detectable in pre-senescent cells but strongly secreted by senescent fibroblasts**, and the SASP (IL-6/IL-8-dependent in part) drives **EMT/invasiveness** in neighbouring cells [1]. This is the canonical "these molecules are aging signals" reference.
- **Ortiz-Montero P, Londoño-Vallejo A, Vernot JP (2017)**, *Cell Commun Signal* (PMC5418812) — **senescence-associated IL-6 and IL-8 induce a self- and cross-reinforced senescence/inflammatory milieu** strengthening tumorigenic capabilities [2]. Direct evidence that *exogenous-style* IL-6/IL-8 **propagate** senescence.
- **Skin SASP reviews** (Wang & Dreesen; Frontiers 2024/2025; dermal-fibroblast-senescence reviews) confirm **IL-6/IL-8** as central skin-aging SASP effectors that **suppress collagen and elevate MMPs** [3][20][21].

**Limitation across Tier 1:** cultured cells have **no stratum corneum**, so receptor access is guaranteed in a dish and *not* through intact skin (see §5). And the SASP work means the "good in a dish" framing is doubly misleading — for IL-6/IL-8 the dish shows them doing *harm*.

### Tier 2 — Animal
- **HB-EGF:** keratinocyte-specific HB-EGF-knockout mice show **markedly impaired wound closure**; topical HB-EGF accelerates burn re-epithelialization [8][9]. Robust **for wounds.**
- **G-CSF:** local G-CSF reduces scarring and boosts hair-follicle regeneration in mouse wounds (~5× follicle increase reported) [18]; accelerates wound repair in hemorrhagic-shock rats via angiogenesis/anti-apoptosis [17].
- **NGF:** overexpression in skin **increases sensory innervation** [6]; NGF is elevated in murine itch/atopy models [7].
- **No animal model shows a leave-on "cytokine complex" reversing intrinsic/photo-aging of intact skin.** Do **not** borrow wound/regeneration data as anti-wrinkle evidence.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL — N/A (do not borrow)
All five are proteins → digested if ingested. No oral evidence is relevant to a topical. (Systemic recombinant G-CSF [filgrastim] and NGF have *clinical* uses — neutropenia, neuropathy trials — but those are injected drugs, irrelevant to cosmetics and *not* importable as cosmetic support.)

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL — the decision-relevant tier

**(a) On the IL-6/IL-8/G-CSF/NGF/HB-EGF *fraction* specifically — NONE.** No published trial isolates or tests this cytokine sub-fraction topically. Cosmetic copy that lists IL-6/IL-8 as "inflammation-modulating" actives [12] is **mechanistically inverted marketing**, not trial evidence.

**(b) Borrowed from the parent conditioned-media / growth-factor category (the only proxy):**
- **Quinlan DJ, Ghanem AM, Hassan H (2023)**, *J Cosmet Dermatol* 22(7):2023–2039 (PMID 37222303) — systematic review, **33 studies (9 RCTs + 24 uncontrolled series), 1,180 participants, 23 GF/cytokine preparations.** Investigator-assessed improvement vs baseline was **modest**: texture median **<50%**, fine lines **<35%**, overall appearance **<20%**; **participant scores > investigator scores** (placebo/expectation tell); **3 controlled RCTs found NO significant difference between treatments**; durability >6 mo unknown [22]. This is the **best available proxy** and it is small and heterogeneous — and it is about *whole GF/cytokine preparations*, not this fraction.
- **SkinMedica TNS (Human Fibroblast Conditioned Media / NouriCel-MD)** — the flagship CM product that demonstrably *contains* IL-6/IL-8 [11][12]:
  - Early **open-label, n=14**, 60 days, twice-daily TNS Recovery Complex → histologic new collagen / epidermal thickening (no control) [11].
  - **Double-blind randomized, n=60**, 6 months → significant fine-line/wrinkle grading improvement at 3 months [11].
  - **Naughton GK, Jiang LI, Makino ET, et al. (2022/2023)** TNS Advanced+ (fibroblast CM + botanicals), **24-wk randomized double-blind vehicle-controlled crossover, ~66–68 subjects** → active beat vehicle on lines/photodamage/pigment/sagging; **funded by Allergan Aesthetics (AbbVie); author COIs** [23].
  - **Postmenopausal open-label, n=15**, 12 wks [11].
- **ADSC-CM / UCB-MSC-CM:** split-face and microneedle-assisted RCTs (e.g., **n=30 ADSC-secretome**, single-blind split-face; microneedling + ADSC-secretome double-blind RCTs) show wrinkle/texture gains — but secretomes, with microneedling (barrier-breach) confounds, and no isolation of the cytokine fraction [24][25][13].

**(c) Penetration-bypassing procedural studies (NOT leave-on cosmetic):** microneedling/laser + secretome show benefit but **deliberately breach the barrier** — they evidence a *procedure*, not a leave-on cream containing these cytokines [24][25].

### Tier-4 honesty summary
- **Proven for the IL-6/IL-8/G-CSF/NGF/HB-EGF fraction itself:** *nothing.*
- **What the primary literature *does* prove about the members:** IL-6/IL-8 are **SASP/inflammaging drivers** [1][2][3][4]; NGF drives **nerve sensitization/itch** [6][7]; HB-EGF/G-CSF are **wound-repair signals** [8][17][18]. None of that is a leave-on anti-aging proof; for IL-6/IL-8/NGF it is closer to a **counter-indication**.
- **Best proxy (CM/GF category):** a **small, modest, heterogeneous, largely manufacturer-funded** human-topical signal for texture/fine-lines [22][23] — un-attributable to this fraction and confounded by carrier bases, botanicals, and (in some studies) microneedling.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration — a hard ceiling, with a perverse twist

- **The ~500-Da rule** (Bos & Meinardi 2000): passive penetration of intact stratum corneum drops sharply above ~500 Daltons [26]. **Every one of these factors is far over the ceiling:** IL-8 ~8–10 kDa (~16–20×), NGF ~13 kDa (~26×), HB-EGF ~16–23 kDa (~32–46×), G-CSF ~19 kDa (~38×), IL-6 ~21–26 kDa (~42–52×) [14][15][16]. By this rule, meaningful **passive** delivery of intact, bioactive protein to viable epidermis/dermis from a leave-on cream is **implausible.**
- **The perverse twist:** for **HB-EGF/G-CSF** poor penetration is the usual "shame, it can't get in" problem. But for **IL-6/IL-8/NGF**, *poor penetration is arguably protective* — you would not *want* SASP cytokines and an itch-promoting neurotrophin reaching viable cells. The penetration ceiling is the main reason a CM/secretome product is unlikely to actively *worsen* skin via this fraction; it is also the reason it is unlikely to *help* via HB-EGF.
- **Where any signal could occur:** EGFR (HB-EGF's receptor) and cytokine receptors sit in the **upper viable epidermis**; some surface/upper-epidermal and **follicular** uptake is possible, more so in thin/compromised/aged skin — but there is **no skin-permeation or clinical-PK data on this cytokine fraction.**
- **Stability compounds the problem:** these are large, fragile, heat/oxidation/protease-sensitive proteins with very short half-lives outside controlled conditions; un-encapsulated, much of the labelled cytokine content likely degrades before use (the same instability that plagues recombinant GFs — see `growth-factors.md`).

**Brutal read:** the fraction is mostly **barrier-excluded either way**. That makes the *upside* (HB-EGF repair) speculative and the *downside* (IL-6/IL-8/NGF) probably limited under normal use — but it also means any "cytokine complex actively rejuvenates" claim is **biologically unsupported at the skin-delivery level**, and any *procedural* (microneedled) use removes the protective barrier exclusion precisely for the molecules you'd least want delivered.

## 6. Formulation

- **You cannot formulate "the fraction" directly** — there is no raw material. In practice you'd be **buying a CM/secretome ingredient** (fibroblast CM, ADSC-CM, etc.) that *contains* these cytokines at **undefined, lot-variable levels** [11][13]. Dosing is by the *CM material* (typically a few percent of finished formula), not by cytokine content.
- **Standardization — the killer issue:** CM cytokine profiles vary by donor cell, passage number, culture conditions, harvest timing, and purification. **IL-6/IL-8/G-CSF/NGF/HB-EGF concentrations are not specified or guaranteed** on any cosmetic CoA. You literally do not know how much SASP cytokine you are adding.
- **pH / stability:** CM proteins are happiest near-neutral (~pH 5–7); low-pH actives, oxidants, and heat degrade them. Cool-process, airless/refrigerated packaging, and protein-friendly bases are required — and even then, potency is unverifiable for these specific factors.
- **Pairings (if a CM is used at all):** barrier-friendly **HA, niacinamide, panthenol, ceramides, gentle peptides**. **Avoid** co-formulating with low-pH vitamin C / AHAs/BHAs in the same layer (protein denaturation).
- **Sensory:** the CM base is typically light; the cytokines themselves are sensorially silent.
- **Formulation honesty flag:** because IL-6/IL-8 are *inflammaging markers*, a longevity brand should treat their presence in a CM as a **liability to minimize/disclose-internally**, not a feature to amplify. There is no formulation move that turns a SASP cytokine into an anti-aging benefit.

## 7. Safety

- **General tolerability:** finished CM/secretome cosmetics are usually well tolerated, with most reactions tracing to other components [22]. No specific safety signal is published for "the cytokine fraction" — largely because barrier exclusion limits exposure (§5).
- **NGF — a genuine, fraction-specific caution:** NGF promotes **sensory-nerve sprouting and sensitization**, is **elevated in atopic dermatitis and psoriasis**, and **correlates with itch severity** [6][7]. Topically supplying NGF is theoretically **pro-pruritic / pro-sensitization** — a poor fit for sensitive, reactive, or barrier-compromised skin, and an awkward ingredient to defend to a dermatologist.
- **IL-6 / IL-8 — inflammaging/senescence concern:** these are pro-inflammatory and senescence-propagating in skin [2][3][20]. While barrier exclusion likely limits real-world impact, **deliberately adding SASP cytokines** is hard to reconcile with a "calm, longevity" thesis, and **microneedle-assisted delivery** (which bypasses the barrier) is where any pro-inflammatory/pro-senescent risk would be greatest.
- **HB-EGF / G-CSF — proliferation/angiogenesis cautions:** EGFR-axis stimulation (HB-EGF) and pro-angiogenic signaling (G-CSF) carry the **same theoretical proliferation/neoangiogenesis concerns as other growth factors** — avoid on/near active lesions, personal skin-cancer history, and the eye area; maintain **daily SPF**.
- **Sun:** no direct photosensitization from the proteins, but the proliferative/angiogenic/inflammatory biology makes **daily broad-spectrum SPF a non-negotiable companion** for any GF/cytokine product.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** **no specific data**; precautionary avoidance reasonable for a multi-signal cytokine/neurotrophin blend.
- **Sourcing/biosafety:** human/animal-cell-derived CM carries **donor-screening, sterility, endotoxin, and provenance** burdens, plus the regulatory gray-zone of cell-derived biologics (§8).

## 8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

- **Cosmetic vs. drug line (US/FDA):** CM/secretome cosmetics are sold as **cosmetics** (no premarket approval; FDA polices safety/labeling/**claims**). The line is the claim: improving *appearance* = cosmetic; affecting *structure/function* (heal, regenerate, "modulate inflammation," "reduce senescence") = **drug**.
  - ✅ Cosmetic-safe (if a CM is used at all): "helps improve the look of texture and firmness," "visibly smooths the appearance of fine lines."
  - ⚠️ Drug-territory / avoid: "**modulates inflammation**," "**reduces senescence/SASP**," "**regenerates/repairs/heals**," "**boosts collagen**," anything implying wound healing or anti-inflammatory pharmacology — *especially* dangerous here because the named molecules (IL-6/IL-8/NGF) are **pro**-inflammatory in the aging context, so an "anti-inflammatory cytokine" claim is both a drug claim *and* scientifically false.
- **The active 2024–2025 enforcement zone:** FDA has issued **warning letters treating exosome/secretome/cell-derived "regenerative" products as unapproved drugs/biologics** — e.g., **Evolutionary Biologics (EXO RNA, Dec 2024)**, **Supreme Rejuvenation (umbilical-cord MSC exosomes, May 2025)**, and amniotic-derived "CharaExo" — all framed as unapproved new drugs / unlicensed biologics under the FD&C Act and PHS Act §351 [10]. A "cytokine complex" headline that leans on regenerative biology sits squarely in this enforcement lane.
- **Substantiation gap:** no published trial on this fraction; any efficacy claim must rest on finished-product consumer/instrumental testing of the *whole formula*, never on borrowed wound/in-vitro/SASP data — and the SASP literature actively *undermines* an anti-inflammatory/anti-aging cytokine narrative.
- See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md) claim cheat-sheet and [`../science/`] claims tracking.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Not separately sourceable.** You source a **CM/secretome ingredient** (e.g., human fibroblast CM / NouriCel-MD family; ADSC-CM; UCB-MSC-CM) — see `conditioned-media-secretomes.md`. The cytokine fraction comes "free" inside it, uncharacterized.
- **Cost tier: HIGH** (premium biologic; cell-culture-derived, cold-chain, screening/sterility/endotoxin testing).
- **Diligence required:** donor screening & viral/sterility/endotoxin certificates; lot-to-lot consistency (knowing it will *not* be guaranteed for individual cytokines); regulatory positioning vs. the exosome/secretome enforcement zone; and an explicit internal decision on whether IL-6/IL-8/NGF content is acceptable for a longevity brand.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

- **"Cytokine complex rejuvenates / modulates inflammation."** The single biggest distortion. IL-6 and IL-8 are **the canonical SASP/inflammaging cytokines** [1][3][4]; framing them as anti-aging "inflammation modulators" [12] is **scientifically inverted**. There is **no trial** showing this fraction reduces inflammation or aging in skin — and a large primary literature in which these molecules are **markers and paracrine effectors of senescence/inflammaging** (they propagate senescence to neighbouring cells in vitro and rise with age [1][2][4][19]), i.e. the opposite of the marketed role. (Note: this is mechanistic/associative evidence; no study has topically *added* these cytokines to intact human skin and measured accelerated aging — the point is that the marketed benefit is unsupported and the dominant biology runs the wrong way, not that a leave-on cream is proven to age skin.)
- **"Growth factors and cytokines work synergistically."** True in an **acute wound**; the leap to *intact aging skin via a leave-on cosmetic* is unproven, and for the IL-6/IL-8/NGF members the "synergy" is with **senescence and itch**, not rejuvenation.
- **"NGF revitalizes skin's nerves/youthful signaling."** NGF's dominant, well-documented skin role is **nerve sprouting, sensitization, and itch** in inflammatory dermatoses [6][7] — a caution, not a selling point.
- **"Clinically proven."** Only the **whole CM/GF category** has (modest, manufacturer-skewed) clinical data [22][23]; **the cytokine fraction has none**, and the category data cannot be attributed to it.
- **"Penetrates and works."** All five are **far above the 500-Da ceiling** [26]; no skin-PK exists. Encapsulation/microneedling can change delivery — but microneedling delivering SASP cytokines past the barrier is a *risk*, not a feature.
- **Where reality lands:** there is a real, modest cosmetic signal for *well-formulated CM/GF serums as a whole* — but **nothing** that supports marketing IL-6/IL-8/G-CSF/NGF/HB-EGF as a beneficial "cytokine complex," and the most-defensible reading is that the **inflammatory/neurotrophic members are off-thesis for a longevity brand.**

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**❌ — Lean SKIP as a named/marketed active. Never a hero; never a "longevity" or "anti-inflammatory" story.**

**One-line reasoning:** "Cytokine complex (IL-6, IL-8, G-CSF, NGF, HB-EGF)" is not a real raw material but a **rebranding of the inflammatory/neurotrophic sub-fraction of conditioned media** — and four of the five named molecules are, in the primary aging literature, **drivers or markers of the SASP, inflammaging, and itch** that a skin-longevity brand exists to oppose [1][2][3][4][6][7]. Only HB-EGF (± G-CSF) is a clean repair signal, and even that is **wound biology**, barrier-excluded from intact skin [8][26]. There is **no independent RCT on the fraction**, the parent CM/GF evidence is modest and un-attributable [22][23], standardization is impossible (lot-variable, uncharacterized cytokine content), and the category sits in the **FDA's active 2024–2025 enforcement zone** [10]. Marketing this as anti-aging is, almost literally, **selling the SASP back to the customer.**

**Longevity-relevance note (important and inverted):** For a *skin-longevity* positioning this fraction is **anti-thesis**. The on-thesis longevity move is to **reduce IL-6/IL-8/SASP and inflammaging** (senolytics/senomorphics, anti-inflammatory actives, barrier-repair lipids, antioxidants), **not to topically supply them**. Citing a "cytokine complex" as longevity-aligned would be scientifically backwards in the same way (worse, even) that IGF-1 mimetics are for the bio-placenta dossier — and should never be done. If the brand ever uses a CM/secretome at all (a separate decision in `conditioned-media-secretomes.md`), the presence of IL-6/IL-8/NGF should be treated as a **liability to minimize and never headline**, with cosmetic-appearance claims only, mandatory SPF, and an explicit avoid in sensitive/reactive skin (NGF/itch).

**Bottom line:** **Skip it as a marketed actor.** Prefer better-evidenced, cleaner-story, on-thesis renewal options — well-substantiated **copper peptides / GHK-Cu**, established **signal peptides**, **retinoids**, **niacinamide**, **barrier-repair lipids**, and genuine **senomorphic/anti-inflammatory** actives — none of which require defending why you put inflammaging cytokines in an anti-aging product.

→ Log in [`../../ideas/not-considering.md`](../../ideas/not-considering.md) as **skip (rejected)**, reason: *not a real raw material — it's the IL-6/IL-8/G-CSF/NGF/HB-EGF sub-fraction inside conditioned media; four of five are SASP/inflammaging/itch drivers (anti-thesis for a longevity brand), only HB-EGF/G-CSF are repair signals and those are wound-only and barrier-excluded; no RCT on the fraction, no standardization, FDA secretome-enforcement risk, and an "anti-inflammatory cytokine" claim is both a drug claim and scientifically false.*

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

*Verified 2026-06-19, and independently re-checked 2026-06-19, against the primary literature (PubMed/PMC abstracts & full texts where reachable, INCI registries, FDA warning-letter pages, and the parent CM/GF clinical record). Goal: catch the specific failure mode of this ingredient — laundering acute-wound and in-vitro data into a leave-on anti-aging claim while ignoring that IL-6/IL-8/NGF are, in the aging literature, part of the problem.*

*Second-pass adversarial re-check (2026-06-19): I re-pulled the five load-bearing citations from source. **All confirmed real and accurately characterized**, with no citation laundering found: (a) **[1] Coppé/Campisi 2008** PMID 19053174 — confirmed PLoS Biol, IL-6/IL-8 as the SASP factors whose secretory effects are "largely caused by IL-6 and IL-8"; (b) **[8] Shirakata 2005** confirmed J Cell Sci 118(11):2363–2370, keratinocyte-specific HB-EGF-KO mice show impaired wound closure — correctly tagged as **wound** biology; (c) **[22] Quinlan 2023** PMID 37222303 — abstract numbers match the dossier exactly (33 studies = 9 RCTs + 24 case series, 1180 participants, 23 GF preparations; investigator-assessed texture median <50%, fine lines <35%, overall <20%; participant > investigator; "three comparative RCTs showed no statistically significant differences between treatments"); (d) **[23] Naughton TNS Advanced+** PMID 36374431 / PMC9823186 — confirmed in *Dermatology and Therapy* 2023;13(1):169–186, with AbbVie-employee authors (own Allergan/SkinMedica) plus a Histogen founder — COI flag accurate; (e) **[10] FDA letters** — Supreme Rejuvenation (ref 700749, 05/05/2025) and Evolutionary Biologics (ref 681586, 12/30/2024) both confirmed live on FDA.gov as unapproved-drug/unlicensed-biologic actions. **One overstatement softened** during this pass: §10's "strong primary literature showing these molecules **drive** both [inflammation and aging]" was changed to make explicit that the evidence is mechanistic/associative (paracrine senescence propagation in vitro + age-associated rises), not a topical-addition experiment on human skin — the defensible point is that the marketed benefit is unproven and the dominant biology runs the wrong way, not that a leave-on cream is proven to age skin. No other claim required editing; the EVIDENCE/HUMAN/TOPICAL discipline in §4 (Tier 4 = "nothing on the fraction") is sound and is the dossier's strongest feature.*

### Overall evidence grade: **D (very weak — and partly *counter-indicated*) for the marketed anti-aging claim**

For the decision-relevant question — *does a leave-on "cytokine complex" (IL-6/IL-8/G-CSF/NGF/HB-EGF) rejuvenate intact aging skin?* — the honest grade is **D**, *below* the parent CM/GF category, for two compounding reasons: (1) **there is no trial on the fraction at all** (only borrowed, modest, manufacturer-skewed CM/GF data [22][23]); and (2) **the primary literature on the named members points the wrong way** — IL-6/IL-8 are canonical SASP/inflammaging effectors [1][2][3][4] and NGF is an itch/sensitization mediator [6][7]. The mechanism is coherent only for **HB-EGF (± G-CSF) in wounds** [8][17][18], which is the wrong context. Penetration is barrier-excluded for all five (16–52× over the 500-Da rule) [26].

### What I verified (citations REAL and accurately characterized)

NGF is a documented keratinocyte/fibroblast secretion [6]. **No supplier sells the five as a defined complex** — confirmed; the ingredient is a literature/marketing construct, correctly flagged in-text.
- **[1] Coppé/Campisi 2008** (PMID 19053174) — confirmed foundational SASP paper; IL-6/IL-8 are core senescent-cell secretions; accurately used to show these cytokines are *aging signals*, not anti-aging actives.

- **[8] Shirakata 2005** (*J Cell Sci* 118:2363) — confirmed: HB-EGF is the predominant EGFR ligand for keratinocyte **migration**/re-epithelialization; KO mice show impaired wound closure. Correctly flagged as **wound** biology, not anti-wrinkle.

- **[26] Bos & Meinardi 2000** 500-Da rule — confirmed; all five factors (8–26 kDa) are 16–52× over the ceiling.

### Main caveats (honest reader's warnings)
1. **The ingredient barely exists as sold.** It is the inflammatory/neurotrophic sub-fraction of conditioned media, not a raw material — every efficacy question collapses into the CM/secretome dossiers.
2. **The defining failure mode is inverted marketing.** Calling IL-6/IL-8 "inflammation-modulating" anti-aging actives contradicts the SASP/inflammaging literature [1][3][4]. This is the rare case where the more you read, the *worse* the ingredient looks.
3. **NGF is a specific liability** (itch/sensitization) [6][7] — under-acknowledged in cosmetic copy.
4. **No standardization** — cytokine content in CM is lot-variable and uncharacterized; you cannot dose or guarantee it.
5. **Barrier exclusion cuts both ways** — limits both the HB-EGF upside and (helpfully) the IL-6/IL-8/NGF downside; microneedle delivery removes the protective exclusion for the worst molecules.
6. **Regulatory exposure** — the FDA secretome enforcement zone [10] makes regenerative/anti-inflammatory headlines risky.

### Net
A "cytokine complex (IL-6, IL-8, G-CSF, NGF, HB-EGF)" anti-aging active is **scientifically backwards for a longevity brand**: four of five named molecules are SASP/inflammaging/itch effectors, the fifth (HB-EGF, ± G-CSF) is barrier-excluded wound biology, there is no trial on the fraction, no standardization, and live FDA enforcement risk. The **D grade** and **SKIP (rejected)** verdict — cosmetic-claims-only and never-headlined *if* a CM is ever used for other reasons — are well-supported. Confidence: **high.**
