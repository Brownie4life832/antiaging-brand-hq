# Ingredient: Hydroxypinacolone Retinoate (INCI: Hydroxypinacolone Retinoate)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ☑ considering (shortlist candidate as a gentler retinoid actor)
**Class:** Retinoids & retinoid alternatives — an *ester of all-trans retinoic acid (tretinoin)*

> **One-line honest summary:** HPR is a stable, low-irritation retinoid ester with a genuinely interesting mechanism (it binds retinoid receptors directly) and solid *in-vitro* and *ex-vivo* data, but the human topical evidence is thin, almost entirely industry-funded, mostly tested *inside multi-active formulas* (so HPR's solo contribution is unproven), and has **no independent head-to-head RCT against retinol or tretinoin** at equivalent doses. Promising, not proven.

## 2. Class & mechanism

HPR is a **retinoid** — specifically a cosmetic-grade *ester of all-trans retinoic acid (ATRA / tretinoin)*. The classical retinoid potency ladder is: retinyl esters → retinol → retinaldehyde → **retinoic acid (ATRA)**, where each step requires enzymatic conversion in skin and only ATRA is receptor-active.

HPR's distinguishing claim is mechanistic: because it is an *ester of ATRA itself*, vendor/industry research reports it can **bind retinoic acid receptors (RARs) directly and drive gene transcription without first being metabolised to retinol → retinaldehyde → ATRA**. [1][9][13] This is the core of the "retinoid activity without the conversion penalty / irritation" story.

At the cell level, an active retinoid signal does the canonical things: binds RAR/RXR nuclear receptors → those bind **Retinoic Acid Response Elements (RAREs)** in DNA → up-regulates genes for **keratinocyte turnover, procollagen synthesis, and dermal remodelling**, and down-regulates **matrix metalloproteinases (MMP-1, MMP-3)** that break down collagen. [4][15]

**Honest caveat on the mechanism:** The "binds receptors directly, no conversion needed" framing is repeated everywhere but traces back to a small number of *industry (Grant Industries / Estée Lauder)* RARE-reporter assays, not independent confirmation. Even those assays show HPR drives **less** transcription than ATRA at equal concentration. [1][9] So "direct binding" is plausible and supported by reporter data, but its *magnitude* in real skin is modest and not independently replicated.

## 4. Evidence review by tier ★ (core of this dossier)

> **Read this first.** The HPR literature is small, recent, and **dominated by ingredient-manufacturer and brand-owned research** (Grant Industries, Estée Lauder/Clinique-adjacent, Proya, contract labs). There is, as of June 2026, **no independent, double-blind, randomized controlled trial of HPR as a *standalone* topical active vs. a proper comparator.** Almost every "HPR works on people" claim comes from a *multi-ingredient* product where HPR cannot be isolated. Hold this in mind through everything below.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro (cell/reporter assays)

- **Ruth & Mammone — "Anti-aging effects of retinoid hydroxypinacolone retinoate on skin models"** (abstract, *J Invest Dermatol* 2018; 138(5):S222 / also *JAAD* 2018 abstract). [1][4][15] The foundational mechanistic work. Using a **RARE reporter assay**, HPR drove **greater gene transcription than retinol, retinaldehyde, and retinyl palmitate** at equal concentration, and was **less cytotoxic** even at 10× higher concentration — **but did NOT reach ATRA's transcription level.** An **IL-1α ELISA** showed HPR did not raise inflammatory signal above ATRA or vehicle. *Attribution check:* both authors are **The Estée Lauder Companies R&D** (Tom Mammone is EL's VP of Skin Physiology & Pharmacology) — so this foundational assay is *Estée Lauder's*, not Grant Industries'. *Limitations:* conference-abstract depth only (not a full peer-reviewed paper), industry-run (MatTek skin models), no dose-response in humans.
- **Wang et al. 2023, *J Cosmet Dermatol* 22(7), DOI 10.1111/jocd.15662** — HPR + retinyl propionate (RP) synergy in **HFF-1 human foreskin fibroblasts**. [5] Identified an optimal RP:HPR ratio; co-treatment raised anti-aging and retinoid-receptor gene expression more than either alone. *Limitations:* pure cell-culture, optimizing a *combination*, isolates neither component's standalone human effect.
- **Senescence/SASP signals** (the *in-vitro* arm of Fang et al. 2025, below): HPR-containing mix suppressed P16/P21 mRNA and SASP cytokines in cultured cells. [6] Combination, in-vitro only.

**Verdict for Tier 1:** Consistent, internally plausible, *favorable* — HPR behaves like a real but sub-ATRA retinoid with low cytotoxicity. All of it is mechanistic and largely industry-run.

### Tier 2 — Animal

- **No meaningful animal-model literature specific to topical HPR was found.** [unverified — absence of evidence] Retinoid teratogenicity data exists for the *retinoid class* (oral), not for topical HPR specifically.

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL

- **Not applicable.** HPR is a topical cosmetic ester; there is no oral HPR literature, and none should exist. (Oral retinoid teratogenicity is a *class* concern carried over by precaution — see Safety.)

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL

This is what actually matters for a face cream. Sorted from strongest design to weakest. **Note: every human study below tests HPR *inside a multi-active formula*, not alone.**

**(a) Comparative / controlled-ish:**

- **Kruger, Bambino, Schmalenberg, Santhanam, Orentreich D, Orentreich C, Logerfo, Saliou. 2025. "Efficacy of Topical Hydroxypinacolone Retinoate-Peptide Product Versus Fractional CO₂ Laser in Facial Aging." *J Cosmet Dermatol* 24(1):e16621. PMID 39394831; PMCID PMC11743323.** [10][11][12]
  - **Design:** Two-cell comparative (parallel groups), 16 weeks. **n=71** women aged 40–65 with moderate photodamage (29 laser cell, mean age 56.2; 42 topical cell, mean age 55.0). Subjects were **randomized** to a cell; clinical grading was done by a **blinded grader**. (Subjects were necessarily *not* blinded — laser-vs-cream cannot be masked.) Topical = serum containing **0.1% HPR + 9.5% peptides** (acetyl hexapeptide-8, palmitoyl hexapeptide-12, palmitoyl tripeptide-1, palmitoyl tetrapeptide-7), **twice daily**, vs. a **single fractional CO₂ laser** session.
  - **Results:** Topical serum cell achieved **statistically greater (p<0.05)** improvement than laser in Marionette lines, global fine lines, global wrinkles, crow's-feet wrinkles, nasolabial folds, texture, tactile smoothness, global hyperpigmentation, lift, and photodamage; **parity** on several others. Tolerability similar between arms.
  - **Limitations (substantial):** **Authored by The Estée Lauder Companies R&D + Orentreich Medical Group** (the paper declares "no specific funding," but the conflict-of-interest is structural — it is the manufacturer testing its own product). Subject-level blinding was impossible (open to subjects), so expectation/placebo effects on appearance endpoints cannot be excluded; the final **cell sizes were unequal (29 vs 42)** despite randomization; the comparator is a *single laser pass* (a low bar — multi-session laser is standard), not retinol/tretinoin; and crucially it is a **HPR + peptide** product, so **HPR's individual contribution cannot be isolated** (the peptides are present at ~95× the HPR level). The "beats laser" headline is real in this dataset but is a marketing-friendly comparison, not proof HPR > retinol.

**(b) Open-label / single-arm (no control):**

- **Shen et al. "An Innovative Serum With Retinol, Hydroxypinacolone Retinoate, Peptides, and Silybin Improves Mild Photoaged Facial Skin in Middle-Aged Chinese Women." *J Cosmet Dermatol* 25(1):e70627; DOI 10.1111/jocd.70627 (online Dec 2025). PMCID PMC12741608.** [2]
  - **Design:** Mechanistic (transcriptomics + *ex-vivo*) **plus open-label, single-arm** clinical. **n=31**, 8 weeks, no control, no blinding.
  - **Clinical results:** Wrinkle volume/area down (p<0.001), elasticity R2 +23.5%, SC hydration +56.0%, TEWL down, pore volume −57.2%, ITA° +18.7% (all p<0.001). Tolerability "excellent" (only mild transient dryness/redness).
  - **Mechanism (the more credible part):** retinol+HPR combo up-regulated COL4A1 (+61%), COL4A2 (+27%), FN1 (+43%), TGF-β/SMAD components; down-regulated MMP1/MMP3; silybin further boosted p-Smad and elastin/collagen-XVII *ex-vivo*.
  - **Limitations:** **Funded by Proya Cosmetics; all authors are Proya employees.** Open-label single-arm (no placebo, high expectation bias for "appearance" endpoints). **Proprietary, undisclosed HPR/retinol %** — cannot replicate. **Combination product** — retinol, two peptides, and silybin all confound HPR's contribution. Single ethnicity, mild photoaging only.

- **Fang, Qiao, Zhang, Chen, Bian. 2025. "A Functional Skincare Formulation Mixed With Retinyl Propionate, Hydroxypinacolone Retinoate, and Vitamin C on Antiaging and Whitening … Han Women in Shanghai." *J Cosmet Dermatol*, DOI 10.1111/jocd.16747. PMID 40029737; PMCID PMC11875225.** [6]
  - **Design:** Single-center, **open-label, self-controlled**, 28 days; **n=120** Han women 25–40 in 4 groups of 30; in-vitro arm included.
  - **Results:** Elasticity R2 ↑ / F4 ↓ (p<0.001 by day 28), crow's-feet and fine-grain area improved (p<0.01), nasolabial folds improved by day 28 (p<0.05), L*/ITA° ↑ (p<0.001). In-vitro: COL-I/COL-IV and elastin ↑, P16/P21 and SASP ↓.
  - **Limitations:** Open-label, no placebo, short (28 d), single ethnicity, **triple-active formula (RP + HPR + Vit C)** so HPR is again not isolated. Vendor-style efficacy study.

**(c) Manufacturer in-house claims (lowest tier, treat as marketing):**

- Grant Industries / Granactive Retinoid in-house data: "0.5% HPR under 24h occlusive patch caused significantly less irritation than 0.5% retinol," and "0.2% HPR around the eyes twice daily for 14 days → dramatic reduction of lines/wrinkles." [9][13] **Unpublished, unblinded vendor data — not citable as proof.**

### What is actually PROVEN for topical human use vs. borrowed

| Claim | Status for HPR specifically |
|---|---|
| "Has retinoid receptor activity" | **Proven (in-vitro), industry-run.** Real but sub-ATRA. |
| "Increases procollagen / reduces MMPs in skin tissue" | **Proven *ex-vivo*** for HPR-containing mixes; **strongly suggestive** for the molecule. |
| "Lower irritation than retinol" | **Supported** by vendor patch data + low-IL-1α in-vitro + good tolerability in combo trials. **No independent equal-dose RCT.** |
| "Improves wrinkles/tone/texture on real faces" | **Demonstrated only inside multi-active formulas**, mostly open-label and/or industry-funded. **HPR's solo human contribution is unproven.** |
| "As effective as / better than tretinoin (or retinol)" | **NOT proven.** No independent head-to-head equal-dose human RCT exists. In-vitro it is *weaker* than ATRA. The "beats CO₂ laser" study used a single laser pass and a combination serum. |
| "Senescence / SASP reduction (longevity angle)" | **In-vitro only, combination — must not be claimed in copy.** |

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Lipophilic ester** → reasonable affinity for the stratum corneum lipid matrix; supplied in dimethyl isosorbide (a strong penetration enhancer) in Granactive Retinoid. [9][13]
- **Encapsulation markedly improves skin retention:** HPR **nanoliposomes** raised skin retention to ~**59%** vs. free formulations; **nanoemulsions** (20–200 nm droplets) reached skin-retention of ~17–29 µg/mL, far above free HPR, while improving stability and lowering irritation. [3][16]
- **Low systemic absorption (a safety positive):** in 24h Franz-cell diffusion studies, **HPR was not detected in the receptor compartment** — i.e. it stays in the skin layers with minimal transdermal passage. [3] This supports a low systemic-exposure argument (relevant to the pregnancy discussion, though precaution still applies).
- *Caveat:* most penetration data come from **encapsulated** HPR studies (often industry/academic formulation papers); plain HPR-in-oil penetration is less characterized.

## 6. Formulation

| Parameter | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Effective concentration** | Marketed products span **0.05%–5% Granactive Retinoid** (= 0.005%–0.5% actual HPR). [9] Common "active" feel sits around **0.1–0.5% actual HPR** (i.e. 1–5% Granactive Retinoid). There is **no established dose-response curve for efficacy in humans** — concentrations are vendor-guided, not clinically titrated. |
| **pH** | Tolerates a broad range; not acid-dependent like vitamin C. More stable in **lower-water, higher-oil/silicone** systems. [11] No strict pH window required. |
| **Stability** | **Strong selling point.** More resistant to light/air/heat oxidation than retinol (vendors claim "~30× more stable" — marketing figure, treat skeptically). [11][12] Still **light-sensitive**; store/ship cold, use opaque airless packaging. Adding ~0.2% glucosylrutin reportedly cuts degradation further. [11] |
| **Pairings** | Plays well with **peptides, niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, ceramides**; combo data exist with **retinol, retinyl propionate, vitamin C, silybin**. [2][5][6] Layering with another mild retinoid is a known formulation pattern. |
| **Conflicts** | Standard retinoid caution: introduce slowly alongside exfoliating acids (AHA/BHA); high-strength vitamin C in the *same* low-pH phase can complicate stability (though a co-formulated Vit C product worked fine in Fang 2025). Avoid stacking with prescription tretinoin (redundant + irritation). |
| **Sensory** | Oil-soluble, light yellow; behaves like an oil-phase active. Generally **elegant, low-tack**, and — its headline benefit — **does not impart the sting/flaking of retinol/tretinoin** at comparable use. |

## 7. Safety

- **Irritation:** The defining advantage. Lower irritation than retinol at equal % in vendor patch testing; **does not elevate IL-1α** vs vehicle in-vitro; "excellent"/mild-transient tolerability across the human combo trials. [1][2][9][10] Net: **a low-irritation retinoid**, suitable for sensitive/retinoid-intolerant users. (Caveat: "low-irritation" is partly *because* its activity is sub-ATRA.)
- **Sun sensitivity:** As a retinoid, evening use + daily SPF is the prudent default. HPR is itself light-sensitive (formulation/stability reason) and standard retinoid photo-precaution applies. Less photo-destabilizing in-product than retinol. [11]
- **Pregnancy / breastfeeding:** **Avoid as a class precaution.** Oral retinoids are proven teratogens; topical retinoids (incl. HPR) are conventionally avoided in pregnancy despite HPR's very low measured systemic absorption. [3] There is **no HPR-specific safety-in-pregnancy data** — so the honest position is "insufficient data; follow the standard retinoid-avoidance precaution; defer to a clinician." **No medical advice in copy.**
- **Sensitization / allergy:** No notable sensitization signal in the literature found; standard cosmetic patch-test advice applies. The dimethyl isosorbide carrier is well tolerated.
- **Sourcing / sustainability:** Fully **synthetic** (lab-made retinoic-acid ester) — no animal/biodiversity sourcing pressure; consistent, traceable supply. No specific sustainability red flags found. Cold-chain storage is the main logistics note.

## 8. Regulatory & claims

- **Cosmetic vs. drug line (US/FDA):** HPR is used as a **cosmetic skin-conditioning agent**. The moment copy claims it **"treats," "prevents," "boosts collagen by X%," "repairs," or alters the skin's structure/function**, it risks being treated as an **unapproved drug claim** (per CLAUDE.md guardrails and the glossary cheat-sheet). The compliant register is **appearance language**: *"helps improve the appearance of fine lines," "visibly smooths," "supports a more even-looking tone."*
- **EU / SCCS context (strategically important):** New EU rules (Reg. 2024/996) cap **Retinol, Retinyl Acetate, Retinyl Palmitate** at 0.05% RE (body lotion) / 0.3% RE (other) with mandatory "Contains Vitamin A" labelling, enforced from **1 Nov 2025** (existing stock to 1 May 2027). [17] **HPR/Granactive Retinoid is explicitly being adopted by brands as a retinol *alternative* that sits outside those named-substance caps** — a real regulatory tailwind. [17] *Caveat:* HPR is not formally exempt forever; "retinol-equivalent"/vitamin-A scrutiny could broaden, so monitor SCCS opinions.
- **What we CAN say:** "a next-generation retinoid," "gentle/low-irritation retinoid," "helps improve the look of lines, texture and tone," "supports skin renewal (appearance)."
- **What we must NOT say without substantiation/legal review:** "as effective as prescription tretinoin/Retin-A," "reverses aging," "boosts collagen by X%," "anti-senescence," "repairs DNA," any disease/structure-function claim. The "as good as tretinoin/beats laser" lines are **not independently substantiated** and would be high-risk.

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Primary source:** **Grant Industries** (Granactive Retinoid = 10% HPR in dimethyl isosorbide) is the reference material; multiple Chinese manufacturers supply raw HPR (Uniproma/PromaCare-HPR, Coachchem, ANECO, Lyphar, etc.). [8][9][13]
- **Cost tier:** **Mid-to-high** for a cosmetic active. Pure HPR is far cheaper *per gram of active* than its in-formula impression suggests because **products use it at fractions of a percent** (0.1–0.5% actual HPR). Exact per-kg pricing is quote-only/not publicly listed [unverified]; budget it as more expensive than retinol but trivial as a share of a premium cream's COGS at typical use levels.
- **Supply:** Stable, synthetic, multi-supplier — **no single-source or scarcity risk**. Cold storage + opaque/airless packaging are the real cost/ops considerations, not the molecule price.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

| Marketing claim seen in the wild | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Works like tretinoin / Retin-A without irritation" | **Overreach.** In-vitro HPR is *weaker* than ATRA; **no independent equal-dose human RCT** supports equivalence. |
| "Beats CO₂ laser" | One **manufacturer (Estée Lauder) study** — randomized with a blinded grader, but subjects unblinded — vs. a **single** laser pass, using a **HPR + peptide** serum (HPR cannot be isolated). Real data, oversold headline. [10] |
| "Anti-senescence / longevity retinoid" | **In-vitro, combination only.** Not a defensible human claim. [6] |
| "30× more stable than retinol" | Vendor figure; directionally true (HPR *is* more stable) but the exact multiple is marketing. [11] |
| "No conversion needed, so more effective" | "No conversion" is mechanistically supported; "therefore more effective in skin" does **not** follow — its receptor activity is sub-ATRA. [1] |
| "Pregnancy-safe retinoid" | **Not established.** Low systemic absorption is reassuring but HPR-specific pregnancy data are absent; default to class avoidance. [3] |

**The single biggest evidence gap:** No independent, double-blind RCT of **HPR alone** vs. retinol vs. tretinoin at matched doses. Until that exists, HPR is "credible gentle retinoid," not "proven retinol-beater."

## 11. Verdict for the brand

**Conditional → strong-supporting (not yet hero).**

One-line: *HPR is the best-positioned "gentle retinoid" actor for a low-irritation, sensitive-skin-friendly anti-aging line — stable, well-tolerated, regulatory-tailwind in the EU — but its human evidence is industry-funded and combination-bound, so it earns a **supporting/co-star** role, not a solo hero claim.*

- **Where it fits the repo's thesis:** The brand's settled logic centers on a **premium barrier-repair cream + lean routine**. HPR is a natural **renewal/retinoid co-star** that pairs cleanly with the barrier story (ceramides, peptides, niacinamide) **precisely because it's low-irritation** — it lets the brand offer "retinoid benefits without the barrier-wrecking sting," which is on-thesis. It is *not* the barrier-repair hero itself.
- **If used as the headline retinoid:** lean on **"gentle/next-gen retinoid," stability, and the EU-compliant-alternative angle** — all defensible — and **avoid** tretinoin-equivalence and anti-senescence claims.
- **Longevity-relevance note:** The senescence/SASP and TGF-β/collagen mechanistic data give HPR a *narratively attractive* "skin-longevity" hook, but that data is **in-vitro and combination-based**. It can inform internal R&D framing; it **cannot** carry consumer longevity claims without substantiation/legal review.
- **Recommended next step:** Shortlist HPR as the renewal actor; if budget allows, the brand's own **small split-face HPR-vs-retinol tolerability/efficacy test** would generate the *independent* substantiation the category currently lacks — a genuine credibility differentiator.

### Reviewer's evidence-quality flag (for internal use)
The HPR human evidence base is **small, recent (2023–2025), heavily industry-funded (Estée Lauder, Proya, Grant Industries), and combination-bound.** Treat any "as good as tretinoin," "beats laser," or "anti-senescence" framing as **unsubstantiated for HPR alone** and route to legal/substantiation review before use in copy. The mechanistic and tolerability story is genuinely good; the *standalone human efficacy* story is not yet proven.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

_Verified 2026-06-19 by independent re-check of the primary sources (PMC, PubMed, JID/JAAD abstract listings, Wiley). Each headline efficacy claim was tested against three questions: (a) is the evidence human + topical, or quietly borrowed from in-vitro/animal/oral data? (b) are the cited studies real and do they say what is claimed? (c) are they industry-funded or very small?_

### Overall evidence grade: **C (weak / indirect for standalone topical HPR)**

HPR is **not** grade A or B: there is no independent, blinded, randomized controlled trial of HPR *as a standalone topical active* against a real comparator (retinol or tretinoin) at a matched dose. Every human-topical study locates HPR *inside a multi-active formula* (with peptides, retinol, retinyl propionate, vitamin C, and/or silybin), so HPR's individual human contribution is **inferred, never measured.** What pushes it up to **C rather than D** is that (i) the *mechanism* (direct RARE-reporter activity, sub-ATRA but real; pro-collagen comparable to ATRA *ex-vivo*) is genuinely demonstrated, and (ii) the *low-irritation* property is consistently supported. The molecule is real and plausible; the **human efficacy claims are the weak link.** This is a "credible gentle retinoid," not a proven one — the dossier's own "promising, not proven" framing is correct.

### What the independent re-check confirmed (the dossier is largely accurate)

- **All five load-bearing citations are real and say what the dossier claims** (no fabricated or misquoted studies found):
  - **#1 Ruth & Mammone 2018 (JID abstract):** Real. HPR > retinol/retinaldehyde/retinyl palmitate in RARE transcription but **did NOT reach ATRA's level**; less cytotoxic at 10×; pro-collagen "comparable to ATRA" in skin models. Correctly reported. *(Affiliation corrected below.)*
  - **#2 Shen 2025/2026 (Proya):** Real. **Confirmed: funded by Proya, all authors are Proya employees, n=31, open-label single-arm 8 wk, HPR/retinol % proprietary/undisclosed, combination product.** Authors themselves concede "a randomized controlled trial against a retinol-only product or a placebo would strengthen the evidence." Dossier accurate.
  - **#6 Fang 2025:** Real. n=120, RP+HPR+Vit C triple-active, open-label self-controlled + in-vitro; suppressed p16/p21 and SASP in vitro. One author group is a biotech company (Zhan Qiu Biotechnology); "no conflict declared" but it is still an industry-adjacent efficacy study. Dossier accurate.
  - **#10 Kruger 2025 (Estée Lauder):** Real. **Combination HPR+peptide serum (0.1% HPR + 9.5% peptides) vs. a single CO₂ laser pass.** Dossier accurate on the "combination, single laser pass, EL-affiliated, can't isolate HPR" criticisms.
- The dossier's **central skeptical thesis is sound and, if anything, was already appropriately harsh.** Its hype-vs-evidence table, "what is proven vs borrowed" table, and the explicit "no standalone HPR RCT" callout are all defensible.

### Caveats / corrections applied to the dossier (claims that were *overstated* or wrong and have been edited)

1. **"The Kruger study was NOT randomized" — was FALSE; corrected.** The paper explicitly states subjects were "**randomly placed into cell 1 or cell 2**" and graded by "**a blinded, expert, trained grader**." It *was* randomized with blinded grading; it simply could not blind *subjects* (laser vs. cream is unmaskable). The dossier originally called it "not randomized... non-randomized group sizes," which overstated the weakness in the wrong direction. Edited to: randomized, blinded grader, subject-unblinded, unequal final cell sizes. (The valid criticisms — single laser pass, combination product, manufacturer-run — stand.)
2. **Kruger funding statement nuance — corrected.** The paper declares **"no specific funding,"** while the authors are Estée Lauder R&D + Orentreich Medical Group. The honest framing is a *structural* conflict (manufacturer testing own product), not a named funding grant. Edited.
3. **Foundational-assay attribution — corrected.** Section 1/2 implied the core RARE-reporter work was "tied to Grant Industries' Granactive program." The assay (#1) is actually **Estée Lauder's** (Mammone is EL's VP of Skin Physiology). Grant Industries supplies the material and makes *separate* in-house claims (#9/#13). Both are industry, but the attribution was imprecise; edited.
4. **Kruger HPR concentration IS disclosed (0.1%).** The dossier elsewhere implies doses are uniformly proprietary; Kruger discloses 0.1% HPR + 9.5% peptides. Noted in-line. (This does *not* rescue the isolation problem — peptides are ~95× the HPR level, so HPR's contribution is still unknowable.)

### Minor citation-hygiene flags (not load-bearing, left as-is or lightly fixed)

- **#2 Shen** was dated "(publ. Dec 2024)" — the online publication is **Dec 2025** (Wiley DOI **10.1111/jocd.70627**, which the dossier had omitted). Fixed.
- **#1 Ruth & Mammone** page is **S223**, not S222 as listed (trivial pagination; left, flagged here).
- **Molecular weight "~414" vendor figures** — the dossier already flags these as likely errors; the C₂₆H₃₈O₃ / 398.6 figure is internally consistent and was not independently re-derived here. No change.

### Claims that should be softened (and were) — quick list for copy/legal

These remain the high-risk lines; all are now hedged in the dossier and **must not** appear in consumer copy without substantiation:
- ❌ "as effective as / better than tretinoin or retinol" — **no equal-dose human RCT exists; in-vitro HPR is weaker than ATRA.**
- ❌ "beats CO₂ laser" — one manufacturer study, combination serum, single laser pass; oversold.
- ❌ "anti-senescence / longevity retinoid" — **in-vitro + combination only.**
- ❌ "boosts collagen by X% / repairs / reverses aging" — structure-function (drug) claims; the collagen data are *ex-vivo* on combinations.
- ❌ "pregnancy-safe retinoid" — **no HPR-specific pregnancy data;** default to class avoidance.
- ⚠️ "30× more stable than retinol" — vendor figure; directionally true, exact multiple is marketing.
- ✅ Defensible: "gentle / next-generation / low-irritation retinoid," "helps improve the appearance of lines, texture and tone," and the **EU retinol-cap-alternative** regulatory angle.

**Bottom line:** The dossier is honest, well-sourced, and its skepticism is justified — grade **C**. The only substantive factual error found (Kruger "not randomized") has been corrected; everything else was citation hygiene. The standalone human efficacy of HPR remains essentially unproven, and the brand should treat HPR as a *supporting* gentle-retinoid actor with appearance-only claims, exactly as Section 11 recommends.
