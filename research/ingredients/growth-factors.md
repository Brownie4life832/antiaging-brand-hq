# Ingredient: Growth Factors (EGF and others) (INCI: e.g., sh-Oligopeptide-1 (EGF), sh-Polypeptide-X; "recombinant human/[plant] EGF," various "conditioned media")

**Researched:** 2026-06-18 · **Status:** ⬜ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ☑ rejected (lean) / ⬜ in a product

## What it is
Growth factors are signaling proteins that tell skin cells to proliferate, migrate, and produce matrix (collagen/elastin). The most common in skincare is **EGF (epidermal growth factor)**; others include FGF, TGF-β, IGF, VEGF and PDGF. Sourced as recombinant human proteins, plant/barley-expressed versions, or as undefined "conditioned media" / secretome (overlaps with the exosome category). Marketed as "the new retinol" for repair and renewal.

## What it does (claimed → evidenced)
| Benefit | Evidence strength | Source(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Facial rejuvenation — wrinkles, texture, firmness (topical preparations) | weak–moderate (systematic review of mostly small, often industry-linked trials) | Quinlan et al. 2023 systematic review [3] |
| Promotes cell turnover / collagen synthesis / barrier repair (mechanistic) | moderate in-vitro; weaker in intact human skin | Eskens 2021 review [2]; Woman&Home derm summary [4] |
| Penetrates intact skin to reach living layers | weak — EGF is >500 Da and penetrates the stratum corneum poorly | [2][1] |
| Wound healing (medical, e.g., rhPDGF / Regranex) | strong — but that's an FDA-approved **drug**, not a cosmetic | [1] |

## How it fits the brand
Conceptually aligned with "repair / renew," premium-priced, and already flagged in our glossary as "premium, evidence varies — vet carefully." But the practical case is weak: poor penetration of a large protein, modest and largely industry-linked human data, a theoretical (unproven for topicals) safety question, and sourcing that blurs into the exosome/secretome gray zone. Hard to defend as a hero for a brand built on honesty.

## Formulation notes
Large, fragile proteins: stability is a real problem (heat, pH, time degrade activity), and penetration through intact stratum corneum is limited (>500 Da rule). Often paired with delivery aids (liposomes, microneedling) — the latter again moves toward a procedure. Keep away from harsh actives/oxidants. Expect cold/short shelf-life considerations and lot-to-lot potency variability.

## Concerns & cautions
- **Theoretical safety concern:** EGFR is implicated in cell proliferation, so triggering it raises a theoretical worry about promoting growth of pre-malignant/malignant cells. Importantly, **no cases of skin cancer from topical EGF serums have been reported**, and poor penetration likely limits exposure — but the concern persists in the literature and warrants conservative use (avoid on active lesions/skin cancer history; caution near eyes). [2]
- **Regulatory:** Only **rhPDGF-BB (Regranex)** is FDA-approved among growth factors — and as a wound-healing **drug**. Cosmetic EGF products sit outside drug approval but their efficacy claims are scrutinized; "conditioned media"/secretome sourcing overlaps the exosome regulatory gray area.
- Sourcing/ethics: human-cell-derived and conditioned-media products raise the same provenance/contamination questions as exosomes.

## Claims caution
- ✅ Safe cosmetic language: "helps improve the look of firmness and texture," "supports the appearance of renewed skin," "smooths the look of fine lines."
- ⚠️ Risky / drug-like: "stimulates collagen production," "regenerates skin," "repairs damage," "heals" — structure/function claims. Also avoid any wound-healing implication (that's the drug lane). See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## Cost / sourcing
High cost tier; recombinant proteins and conditioned-media are expensive and variable. Plant-expressed EGF is cheaper but with even thinner human efficacy data. Diligence on potency assays, stability data, and source documentation is essential.

## Verdict for the brand
❌/⚠️ — **Lean skip.** The differentiation (premium "growth factor" cachet) is not worth the combination of poor penetration, modest/industry-linked evidence, a lingering theoretical safety question, and sourcing that drags us toward the exosome gray zone. If a longevity "renewal" hero is wanted, a senotherapeutic peptide or copper peptide is a stronger, cleaner story. Could revisit a well-characterized, well-substantiated single recombinant factor as a *supporting* actor later. Log in [`../../ideas/not-considering.md`](../../ideas/not-considering.md) with reason: weak topical evidence + penetration limits + theoretical EGFR safety flag + sourcing gray area.
