# Ingredient: PQQ — Pyrroloquinoline Quinone (INCI: Pyrroloquinoline Quinone Disodium Salt / topical form: Pyrroloquinoline Quinone Allyl Ester)

**Researched:** 2026-06-19 · **Status:** ✅ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product
**Class:** Antioxidant (redox-active *o*-quinone; mitochondrial-biogenesis modulator) · **Slug:** `pqq`

> **TL;DR for a busy reader.** PQQ is a small, water-soluble, redox-cycling *o*-quinone that became a supplement star for one striking piece of biology: in cells (and PQQ-deprived rodents) it drives **mitochondrial biogenesis** via the **PGC-1α / SIRT1 / CREB** axis — exactly the "fix the power plants" story a longevity brand wants. The catch for *skincare* is the evidence ladder. The mitochondrial-biogenesis data are **cell/animal**; the human data are mostly **oral** and **industry-run** (a Mitsubishi/Kyowa dry-skin trial, Nakano 2015, and a 2025 combo RCT where PQQ is bundled with ergothioneine + NR so its individual effect can't be isolated). The one real human **topical** efficacy trial (Draelos 2023, *J Clin Aesthet Dermatol*) used a **stabilized allyl-ester derivative** — not parent PQQ — was **open-label with no vehicle control**, **sponsored by skinbetter science**, and tested PQQ **inside a 19-antioxidant cocktail**, so you cannot attribute the results to PQQ specifically. The "24% wrinkle reduction" stat circulating online is from a **patent**, not a peer-reviewed trial. Parent PQQ is **intrinsically irritating and unstable** topically (the whole reason the allyl ester exists), it can act as a **pro-oxidant** via redox cycling at higher doses, and at very high oral intakes there is a **kidney-damage signal** in animals. Honest read: *mechanistically gorgeous, on-trend for a longevity narrative, but the human-topical efficacy evidence is thin, confounded, and tied to one derivative + one sponsor.* Supporting actor at most. See Verdict (§11).

---

## 1. Identity

- **Common name:** Pyrroloquinoline quinone (PQQ). Historic name **methoxatin**. Marketed in supplements as **BioPQQ™**, **PureQQ®**, "PQQ disodium salt."
- **Parent molecule:** 4,5-dihydro-4,5-dioxo-1H-pyrrolo[2,3-f]quinoline-2,7,9-tricarboxylic acid. **Formula C₁₄H₆N₂O₈, MW ≈ 330.2 g/mol. CAS 72909-34-3.** A tricarboxylic, tricyclic *o*-quinone — i.e., it carries **three carboxylic-acid groups** (highly polar, anionic at skin pH). [1][7]
- **Disodium salt (the commodity form):** **C₁₄H₄N₂Na₂O₈, MW ≈ 374.2, CAS 122628-50-6.** Reddish-brown powder, water-soluble (~3 g/L at 25 °C), mp >300 °C. This is the GRAS/novel-food supplement form. [1][7]
- **Topical derivative (the one with the only human topical trial):** **Pyrroloquinoline Quinone Allyl Ester** (a.k.a. allyl-PQQ, "TAP," monoallyl PQQ ester). **INCI: Pyrroloquinoline Quinone Allyl Ester. CAS 175429-96-6.** The allyl ester was created specifically because **parent PQQ is too irritating and unstable to use topically** — esterifying it improves stability and reduces irritation. ⚠️ This is a different molecule from the supplement form, and **most "PQQ skincare" evidence is really allyl-PQQ evidence.** [2][8][9]
- **Other salts/forms in the literature:** lithium, calcium, choline, glycine-betaine, tetra-alkali salts (solubility/stability engineering). Reduced form is **PQQH₂ (pyrroloquinolinol)** — the antioxidant-active state. [7]
- **Where it occurs naturally:** soil bacteria (it's a bacterial redox cofactor — the "third redox cofactor" after NAD and flavin), kiwifruit, fermented soy (natto), parsley, green tea, and **human breast milk**. Not synthesized by mammalian cells but ubiquitous in diet → sometimes loosely called a "vitamin-like" compound (it is **not** an established vitamin). [1][4]

## 2. Class & mechanism

PQQ is a **redox-active *o*-quinone**. Its two headline mechanisms:

1. **Mitochondrial biogenesis (the signature mechanism).** The foundational paper — **Chowanadisai, Bauerly, Tchaparian, Wong, Cortopassi, Rucker (2010), *J Biol Chem* 285(1):142–152 (PMID 19861415)** — showed PQQ (in mouse Hepa1-6 hepatocytes, ~10–30 µM, 24–48 h) increases mitochondrial DNA content, MitoTracker staining, citrate synthase and cytochrome-c-oxidase activity, and cellular oxygen consumption. Mechanism: PQQ phosphorylates **CREB at Ser133**, activates the **PGC-1α** promoter, raises PGC-1α mRNA/protein, and drives downstream **NRF-1/NRF-2, Tfam/TFB1M/TFB2M**. Knockdown of CREB *or* PGC-1α abolishes the effect. A later study (**Saihara et al., *Biochemistry* 2017, 56(50):6615–6625**) adds a **SIRT1/PGC-1α** deacetylation arm. This is the "more, healthier mitochondria" story. [3][10]
2. **Direct antioxidant / redox cycling.** The reduced form PQQH₂ scavenges reactive oxygen species (ROS) — notably superoxide and peroxyl radicals — and PQQ is unusually stable to *cyclic* redox turnover (it can do thousands of oxidation/reduction cycles, unlike many one-shot antioxidants). It also activates **Nrf2/HO-1** antioxidant-response signaling. [4][6]

**Skin-level extrapolation (what's plausible vs. shown):** in cultured **human dermal fibroblasts** PQQ activates **SIRT1, SIRT6 and Nrf2/HO-1** and blunts UVA-induced senescence (see §4). The *narrative* — "recharge aging skin's failing mitochondria, calm oxidative stress, slow fibroblast senescence" — is mechanistically coherent. But almost all of it is **cell-level**; the jump to "this rejuvenates human facial skin applied topically" is largely inference.

⚠️ **The pro-oxidant caveat (matters for an evidence-led brand).** PQQ is a *redox cycler*: PQQH₂ can hand electrons to molecular O₂, regenerating superoxide → H₂O₂. At low doses this is part of beneficial signaling; at **higher concentrations / in sensitive or tumoral cells it behaves as a pro-oxidant**, depleting glutathione and inducing apoptosis (e.g., U937 leukemia cells). PQQ is therefore **not an unconditionally "clean" antioxidant** — dose and context decide. This is the same double-edged-quinone problem flagged for idebenone. [5][6]

## 3. Hallmarks of skin aging targeted

| Hallmark | Plausibility | Best evidence tier available |
|---|---|---|
| **Mitochondrial decline** | Core, signature mechanism | **Cell + animal strong** (biogenesis); **no human-skin biopsy proof** |
| **Oxidative stress / inflammaging** | Direct antioxidant + Nrf2/HO-1 | Cell strong; human topical only inside a 19-AOX cocktail |
| **Cellular senescence** | Blunts UVA-induced fibroblast senescence | **In-vitro** (HDFs); animal (Bmi-1 mouse) |
| **Collagen/ECM loss (photoaging)** | Down-regulates MMP1/MMP3/MMP9 | In-vitro + ex-vivo gene expression; histology only n=4 |
| **Hydration / water loss (TEWL/barrier)** | Reduced TEWL | **Human ORAL** (Nakano 2015, industry) — *not topical* |
| **Pigmentation / tone / dullness** | "Brightening," even tone | Human topical, but **cocktail + open-label** (Draelos 2023) |
| **Glycation / AGEs** | Antioxidant-adjacent | Minimal direct skin data |
| **Microbiome** | None | None |

**Honest summary:** the mechanism maps onto the *most fashionable* longevity hallmarks (mitochondria, senescence, oxidative stress). The **human topical** proof for any of them is weak and confounded.

## 4. Evidence review by tier — the core of this dossier

> Read this as a ladder. PQQ's biology is genuinely strong at the **bottom** rungs (cell/animal mitochondrial biogenesis). The **top** rung — independent, vehicle-controlled, human topical RCT of PQQ *alone* — is **empty**. Every human "skincare" result has at least one of: oral format, ingredient-cocktail confounding, no vehicle control, or industry sponsorship.

### Tier 1 — In-vitro / biochemical (strongest, but cells only)
- **Mitochondrial biogenesis — Chowanadisai et al. 2010, *J Biol Chem* 285(1):142–152 (PMID 19861415).** Mouse Hepa1-6 cells, 10–30 µM PQQ → ↑mtDNA, ↑O₂ consumption, ↑citrate synthase / COX, via CREB→PGC-1α. The cornerstone paper. **Limitation:** hepatocytes, not skin cells; supraphysiologic in-dish doses. [3]
- **SIRT1/PGC-1α arm — Saihara et al. 2017, *Biochemistry* 56(50):6615–6625.** Confirms PQQ deacetylates/activates PGC-1α via SIRT1. [10]
- **UVA fibroblast senescence — Zhang C, Wen C, Lin J, Shen G. 2015, *Mol Med Rep* 12(3):4382–4388 (PMID 26126510).** Human dermal fibroblasts, UVA 9 J/cm², PQQ **50/100/200 ng/mL** added 24 h pre-UVA. PQQ 50 ng/mL cut SA-β-gal-positive (senescent) cells from **53% → 29%**, reduced **MMP1/MMP3**, and acted via **SIRT1, SIRT6, Nrf2/HO-1**. The single most skin-relevant mechanistic paper. **Limitation:** strictly in-vitro; one cell type; no collagen-synthesis or barrier endpoint. [11]
- **Pro-oxidant / redox biology — He et al. (Antioxidant and pro-oxidant properties of PQQ) and U937 apoptosis work.** Documents glutathione depletion / ROS generation / apoptosis at higher doses. The honest counterweight to "potent antioxidant." [5][6]

### Tier 2 — Animal
- **Bmi-1-deficient mouse skin-aging rescue — Li J et al. 2022, *BioMed Research International* (PMC8849985).** Bmi-1-knockout mice show accelerated skin aging (thinner skin, fewer follicles, less collagen, ↑ROS, ↑DNA damage). **Dietary PQQ 4 mg/kg for 5 weeks substantially reversed** these signs (↓ROS, ↑SOD2, ↓DNA-damage markers). **Limitation:** genetic-deficiency model + *oral* PQQ — relevant to "PQQ counters oxidative skin aging," **not** to topical delivery. [12]
- **Dry-skin mouse model — Nakano et al. 2015 (companion to the human arm below):** oral PQQ improved barrier function / reduced TEWL in barrier-impaired mice. [13]
- Broad rodent literature: PQQ-deprived diets reduce mitochondrial content; reproductive/growth deficits on PQQ-free diets — the original rationale that PQQ is conditionally important. [1][3]

### Tier 3 — Human ORAL (different format — context only; do NOT borrow for a cream)
- **Dry skin — Nakano M, Kamimura A, Watanabe F, et al. 2015, *J Nutr Sci Vitaminol* 61(3):241–246.** Healthy women with subjective dry skin; **oral PQQ disodium 20 mg/day × 8 weeks**; endpoint included forearm **TEWL** (significantly inhibited the rise in TEWL) plus subjective improvement. ⚠️ Authors affiliated with **Mitsubishi Gas Chemical, Kyowa Hakko Bio, TTC Co.** (the PQQ producers); abstract does not clearly state n or a placebo arm in the available record. Industry-run, small. [13]
- **Combination RCT — "Skin-Condition-Improving Effects of a Formula Containing Ergothioneine, PQQ, and NR in Naturally Aging Asian Women," *Current Developments in Nutrition* 2025.** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; **62 Chinese women, 36–60, 56 days**, oral. Reported ↑moisture, ↑brightness, ↓spots, ↑elasticity, ↓pore size, plus vitality/memory. ⚠️ **PQQ is bundled with ergothioneine + nicotinamide riboside (NR) — its individual contribution cannot be isolated.** Oral, not topical. [14]
- **General oral context:** numerous oral PQQ trials for cognition, fatigue, mitochondrial markers (e.g., Nakano cognition studies; sports/recovery trials) — establish systemic bioactivity and tolerability, **say nothing about topical skin.** [4]

### Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the rung that should matter most — and it is weak)
**(a) The one real efficacy trial — but it tests a *derivative* in a *cocktail*, open-label, sponsor-funded:**
- **Draelos ZD, et al. 2023. "Evaluation of a New, Advanced Antioxidant Containing Topical Allyl Pyrroloquinoline Quinone…" *J Clin Aesthet Dermatol* 16(4):53–59 (PMC10110291).** Two parts: (i) **ex-vivo** human skin gene-expression panel and (ii) **multicenter, open-label clinical trial**, 44 enrolled / **39 completed** facial-photodamage assessment, **4 biopsies**, ages 40–65, FST I–VI, 12 weeks.
  - **Clinical (n≈39–40), Week 12, all p<0.0001:** dullness **−42%**, texture **−35%**, tone **−25%**, erythema **−27%**; significant gains by Week 4.
  - **Histology (n=4):** solar elastosis **−33% at Wk 6, −60% at Wk 12** (p≤0.01).
  - **Ex-vivo gene expression vs. saline control:** allyl-PQQ (TAP) significantly ↑ DNMT3A/3B, COX10, TP53, **SIRT1**, SQSTM1, HSPD1, **TXNRD1**; ↓ **MMP1, MMP9**. A leading vitamin-C cream produced *non-significant* changes in the same markers in this assay.
  - ⚠️ **Limitations that gut the headline:** **no vehicle/placebo control, open-label** (so moisturizer/expectation effects unaccounted for); the product is a **19-antioxidant blend ("TAP + WEL")**, so **results cannot be attributed to PQQ/allyl-PQQ specifically**; allyl-PQQ **concentration is not disclosed**; n=4 biopsies; **funded by skinbetter science, LLC**, with author financial ties. This is *the* human topical citation — and it is a confounded, uncontrolled, sponsor-run study of a derivative-in-a-blend. [2]

**(b) The "24% wrinkle reduction" stat — it's from a PATENT, not a trial:**
- **US 10,799,441 B2 / US 2014/0178316 A1, "Skin treatments containing pyrroloquinoline quinone (PQQ) esters."** Reports an internal study: 0% (control), **0.1%** and **0.5% PQQ ester**, BID, with fine-line/wrinkle reductions of **9.5% (control) vs 24% (0.1%) vs 20% (0.5%)**. ⚠️ **This is patent data — not peer-reviewed, no published methods/sample size, inventor-controlled.** The widely-quoted "24% reduction in wrinkles" almost always traces back here. Treat as marketing-grade, not evidence-grade. [9][15]

**Bottom line by tier:** **cell + animal = strong** (mitochondrial biogenesis, anti-senescence, antioxidant) → **human oral = modest and industry-run** (dry-skin TEWL; combo RCT confounded) → **human topical = one open-label, vehicle-uncontrolled, sponsor-funded trial of a *derivative inside a 19-antioxidant cocktail*, plus a patent.** There is **no independent, vehicle-controlled, human topical RCT of PQQ (or allyl-PQQ) as a standalone active.** Anti-aging *topical* efficacy for PQQ specifically is **not** established.

## 5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

- **Parent PQQ is a poor topical candidate.** It's small (MW 330) but carries **three carboxylic-acid groups** → highly **polar and anionic** at skin pH, which fights passage through the lipophilic stratum corneum. Combined with **instability and intrinsic irritancy**, raw PQQ disodium is not a practical leave-on active. [7][8]
- **The allyl ester exists to solve this.** Esterifying converts PQQ to a more lipophilic, **more stable, less-irritating** form (allyl-PQQ / "TAP") better suited to topical formulation and, plausibly, better skin uptake — though **no published quantitative human penetration/permeation data** for allyl-PQQ were located; "better delivery" is a formulation rationale, not a measured datum. [2][8][9]
- **Implication:** if the brand ever uses PQQ topically, it should almost certainly be the **allyl ester**, and we should not assume parent-PQQ supplement data transfer to the skin.

## 6. Formulation

- **Use the allyl ester, not parent PQQ,** for any leave-on. Parent PQQ disodium = irritation + instability (and a strong reddish-brown color that tints formulas). [8][9]
- **Concentration:** the only public numbers are the patent's **0.1–0.5% PQQ ester** (with 0.1% ≈ 0.5%, suggesting a low plateau); the Draelos product's allyl-PQQ level is undisclosed. No validated effective topical concentration exists. Treat **~0.05–0.5%** as a plausible exploratory range pending supplier guidance. [9]
- **pH / stability:** PQQ is a tricarboxylic acid; the salt is water-soluble and the molecule is redox-active and **oxidation-/light-sensitive**. Expect a need for **antioxidant-buffered, light-protected, airless** packaging. Allyl ester improves but does not eliminate stability concern. Precise pH-stability windows for the allyl ester are not well published — request a supplier stability dossier. [7][8]
- **Color/sensory:** intrinsically **red/brown-pigmented** — can tint a formula; relevant for a clean-looking premium cream.
- **Pairings (rational, not proven synergy):** logically complements the brand's **NAD⁺/mitochondrial-longevity narrative** (niacinamide/NR) and other antioxidants (vitamin C, ergothioneine — note the existing dossiers [`nad-niacinamide-longevity.md`](nad-niacinamide-longevity.md), [`ergothioneine.md`](ergothioneine.md), [`vitamin-c.md`](vitamin-c.md), [`coenzyme-q10.md`](coenzyme-q10.md), [`idebenone.md`](idebenone.md)). The Draelos blend pairs it with 18 other antioxidants — which is also why its data can't isolate PQQ.
- **Conflicts/cautions:** as a redox cycler, avoid contexts that push it toward **pro-oxidant** behavior (high transition-metal load, very high concentration). Don't co-pitch as both "the" antioxidant and stack it carelessly with other strong oxidant-sensitive actives without stability testing.

## 7. Safety

- **Oral safety is well-characterized and reassuring at supplement doses.** Human studies used **20–60 mg/day** with no adverse effects on standard labs; PQQ disodium is **FDA GRAS** (GRN 590, 641, 709) and **EFSA-accepted as a novel food** (BioPQQ™, max ~20 mg/day), produced by fermentation (*Hyphomicrobium denitrificans*), ≥99% purity. Rat 13-week NOAEL **100 mg/kg/day**; no genotoxicity. [16][17][18]
- ⚠️ **High-dose kidney signal.** At excessive intakes, animal data show **renal toxicity risk** — i.e., a real ceiling, not "infinitely safe." Relevant if a brand ever floated very-high-dose oral adjuncts (we are not). [16]
- **Topical irritation / sensitization.** **Parent PQQ is intrinsically irritating to skin** — explicitly the reason the allyl ester was developed. The allyl ester is reported "highly tolerable" with mild/transient AEs in the open-label Draelos study, but there is **no independent sensitization/patch-test dataset** and no CIR safety assessment specific to Pyrroloquinoline Quinone Allyl Ester that was located. Treat topical safety as **plausible but under-characterized**; do HRIPT/patch testing on any finished formula. [8][2]
- **Pro-oxidant risk:** see §2/§4 — context-dependent; another reason to keep concentration modest and stability tight.
- **Sun sensitivity:** no evidence it increases photosensitivity; it is *not* a sunscreen and shouldn't be implied as one.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** PQQ occurs naturally in breast milk and diet; however **no topical cosmetic safety data in pregnancy** — use standard "consult your doctor" boilerplate.
- **Sourcing/sustainability:** commercial PQQ is made by **bacterial fermentation** (not animal/plant extraction) → favorable provenance story; main supply-chain risk is oxidative degradation and purity, plus a relatively **concentrated supplier base** (largely Japanese originators + Chinese fermenters).

## 8. Regulatory & claims

- **Cosmetic vs. drug line.** Topically, **Pyrroloquinoline Quinone Allyl Ester** functions as a cosmetic **antioxidant** ingredient. PQQ disodium's **GRAS/novel-food** status is a *food/supplement* clearance — **do not let it imply topical or therapeutic approval.** "Mitochondrial," "energy," "biogenesis," "anti-senescence," "regenerates" language drifts toward **drug/structure-function** territory and is **not** supported by human topical data — high substantiation/legal risk.
- ✅ **Defensible cosmetic language:** "antioxidant," "helps defend the look of skin against environmental/oxidative stress," "helps improve the *appearance* of dullness, texture and uneven tone," "for visibly smoother, more radiant-looking skin."
- ⚠️ **Risky / needs substantiation or legal review (and our data don't support):** "boosts/recharges your skin's mitochondria," "powers cellular energy," "24% fewer wrinkles" (patent-only), "reverses photoaging," "senolytic/anti-senescence," "clinically proven anti-aging" (the only topical trial is open-label, uncontrolled, cocktail-confounded, sponsor-run). Pulling the mitochondrial-biogenesis cell data into consumer copy is a classic **format/level-transfer overreach** (cell → face) and a **claims liability**.
- See [`../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md) guardrails and [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## 9. Sourcing & cost

- **Tier: premium specialty raw material.** Parent PQQ disodium (BioPQQ/PureQQ) is a comparatively **expensive fermentation product** (far pricier than commodity antioxidants like niacinamide; broadly in the CoQ10-and-up range). The **allyl ester is more specialized/proprietary** and likely costlier and harder to second-source.
- **Supply concentration:** originators are largely Japanese (Mitsubishi Gas Chemical / Kyowa Hakko lineage) with Chinese fermentation suppliers; the cosmetic allyl-ester supply chain is narrower. Vet supplier stability/purity dossiers and IP (note the PQQ-ester patents, §4b). [9][15]
- **Net:** real raw-material cost + formulation/stability/packaging overhead, for an active whose *topical* efficacy is unproven — a poor cost-to-evidence ratio for a hero claim, acceptable for a small supporting/story role.

## 10. Hype vs. evidence

| Marketing claim | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Recharges/boosts your skin's mitochondria." | True *mechanism in cells/rodents* (PGC-1α/SIRT1 biogenesis); **never demonstrated in human skin topically.** Cell-to-face transfer. [3][10] |
| "Clinically proven to reduce wrinkles 24%." | From a **patent**, not a peer-reviewed trial (0.1% PQQ ester). No published methods/n. [9][15] |
| "Clinically proven antioxidant skincare." | The lone human topical trial (Draelos 2023) is **open-label, no vehicle control, sponsor-funded, PQQ inside a 19-antioxidant blend** → can't credit PQQ. [2] |
| "Potent pure antioxidant." | It's a **redox cycler** that can turn **pro-oxidant** (GSH depletion, apoptosis) at higher doses/sensitive cells. [5][6] |
| "Anti-senescence / senolytic." | In-vitro fibroblast anti-senescence (Zhang 2015) is real but **cell-level**; "senolytic" is unsupported and a drug-like claim. [11] |
| "Proven to improve skin from within." | Oral data are **industry-run** (Nakano dry-skin TEWL) or **confounded by ergothioneine+NR** (2025 combo RCT). [13][14] |

## 11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ **CONDITIONAL — supporting / narrative actor at best; not a hero, not a standalone claim driver.**

**One line:** PQQ has one of the most *seductive longevity mechanisms* of any antioxidant (PGC-1α/SIRT1 mitochondrial biogenesis + fibroblast anti-senescence) — but every strand of *human topical* evidence is either a derivative (allyl ester), inside a cocktail, oral, uncontrolled, or sponsor/patent-sourced, so it can't credibly carry a hero anti-aging claim for an evidence-led premium brand.

- **If used:** only as the **allyl ester**, at a modest concentration in a **stabilized, light-protected, airless** format, as a **supporting** antioxidant — and **only with appearance-based cosmetic claims**, never "mitochondrial/energy/senescence/24% wrinkle" language. Patch/HRIPT test the finished formula.
- **Where it earns its place:** as **education and narrative** within the brand's *longevity-mechanism* storytelling (the NAD⁺/mitochondria thread alongside niacinamide/NR and ergothioneine), **told honestly** — "an antioxidant studied for skin-cell mitochondrial support," not "recharges your mitochondria." The brand's actual efficacy promise should keep running through better-substantiated actives (ceramide barrier complex; vitamin C; retinoids).
- **Longevity relevance (high *as a story*, low *as proven skin outcome*):** PQQ sits squarely on the mitochondrial-decline / cellular-senescence / oxidative-stress hallmarks and pairs naturally with the brand's NAD⁺/longevity angle — but the mechanism-to-topical-outcome gap means longevity copy would currently outrun the data.
- **Recommendation:** **keep on the "considering — conditional/watch" list** as a supporting/longevity-narrative ingredient (allyl ester only), not a hero. Log in [`../../ideas/considering.md`](../../ideas/considering.md). Revisit if an independent, vehicle-controlled human topical RCT of standalone allyl-PQQ appears.

## Evidence verification (adversarial)

**Verified:** 2026-06-19. Independent adversarial re-check of every headline efficacy claim and key citation against primary sources (PubMed, J Biol Chem, J Clin Aesthet Dermatol/PMC, Mol Med Rep, J Nutr Sci Vitaminol, EFSA, FDA GRAS, USPTO).

### Overall evidence grade for TOPICAL anti-aging efficacy: **D** (mechanistically rich, human-topically unproven)

- **D** for the *topical anti-aging efficacy* claim: there is **no independent, vehicle-controlled, human topical RCT of PQQ or allyl-PQQ as a standalone active.** The single human topical trial (Draelos 2023) is **open-label, vehicle-uncontrolled, sponsor-funded, and tests PQQ inside a 19-antioxidant blend**, so its real numbers (dullness −42% etc.) **cannot be attributed to PQQ.** The famous "24% wrinkle" figure is **patent**, not trial, data.
- The **mechanism** grade is genuinely high (**A-/B+** at the cell/animal level): PGC-1α/SIRT1 mitochondrial biogenesis (Chowanadisai 2010, Saihara 2017) and UVA-fibroblast anti-senescence (Zhang 2015) are solid primary findings. The grade collapses on **translation to human topical skin outcomes.**

### Citation audit — all real, accurately represented
- Chowanadisai 2010 (PMID 19861415, *J Biol Chem*): **confirmed** — CREB→PGC-1α biogenesis, Hepa1-6, the field's cornerstone.
- Saihara 2017 (*Biochemistry*, ACS): **confirmed** — SIRT1/PGC-1α arm.
- Zhang 2015 (PMID 26126510, *Mol Med Rep*): **confirmed** — HDFs, 50–200 ng/mL, SA-β-gal 53%→29%, ↓MMP1/MMP3, SIRT1/SIRT6/Nrf2/HO-1, in-vitro only.
- Draelos 2023 (PMC10110291, *JCAD*): **confirmed** — open-label, 44 enrolled/39 completed, 4 biopsies, 12 wk, Wk-12 dullness −42%/texture −35%/tone −25%/erythema −27%, solar elastosis −60%, **skinbetter science** funded, **allyl-PQQ in a multi-antioxidant blend, no vehicle control, concentration undisclosed.**
- Nakano 2015 (*J Nutr Sci Vitaminol* 61(3):241–246): **confirmed** — oral 20 mg/d × 8 wk, forearm TEWL endpoint, industry authorship; n/placebo not clearly stated in available abstract (flagged).
- Combo RCT 2025 (*Curr Dev Nutr*): **confirmed** — n=62, 56 d, oral, ergothioneine+PQQ+NR (PQQ not isolable).
- Li 2022 (PMC8849985): **confirmed** — Bmi-1-KO mice, dietary PQQ 4 mg/kg × 5 wk rescue.
- Pro-oxidant biology: **confirmed** — redox cycling, GSH depletion, U937 apoptosis; context/dose-dependent.
- Safety: EFSA 2017 novel food + FDA GRAS (590/641/709) + rat NOAEL 100 mg/kg/day + high-dose renal signal — **confirmed.**
- Identity: parent CAS 72909-34-3 / MW 330; disodium CAS 122628-50-6 / MW 374; **allyl ester INCI Pyrroloquinoline Quinone Allyl Ester, CAS 175429-96-6** — **confirmed** across multiple sources.

### Main caveats a marketer must not forget
1. **Most "PQQ skincare" data is allyl-ester data** — a different molecule from the supplement form.
2. **The one human topical trial can't credit PQQ** (blend + open-label + sponsor).
3. **"24% wrinkle reduction" is a patent stat**, not a peer-reviewed result.
4. **Mitochondrial-biogenesis evidence is cell/animal** — do not project it onto a human face.
5. **PQQ can act as a pro-oxidant**; parent PQQ is irritating/unstable topically.
6. **Oral human data are industry-run and/or cocktail-confounded** — and oral ≠ topical.

### Verdict held
"Mechanistically gorgeous, on-trend for longevity narrative, human-topical efficacy thin and confounded → **conditional supporting / story actor (allyl ester only), not a hero.**" Survives scrutiny.
