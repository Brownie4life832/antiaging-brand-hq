# Ingredient: Azelaic Acid (INCI: Azelaic Acid)

**Researched:** 2026-06-18 · **Status:** ⬜ considering (SUPPORTING — with a regulatory caveat)

## What it is
Azelaic acid (AzA) is a dicarboxylic acid (naturally found in grains like barley/wheat) with antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and **tyrosinase-inhibiting (tone-evening)** properties. It is unusual in being well-tolerated yet effective on **redness, uneven tone, and post-inflammatory dark marks** — making it a quiet multitasker for an even, calm, radiant complexion. Higher concentrations (15–20%) are prescription/drug strengths for rosacea and acne; lower concentrations appear in cosmetics.

## What it does (claimed → evidenced)
| Benefit | Evidence strength | Source(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Evens tone / reduces hyperpigmentation & melasma (antimelanogenic) | **Strong (at 15–20%, medical use)** — 20% comparable to hydroquinone in melasma | [1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12472904/), [2](https://www.dermatologyadvisor.com/news/azelaic-acid-effective-treatment-rosacea-acne-vulgaris-melasma/) |
| Reduces redness / calms (rosacea) | **Strong (at 15%, drug indication)** — 15% gel comparable to metronidazole | [1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12472904/) |
| Antioxidant / supports skin clarity & resilience | **Moderate** | [1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12472904/) |
| Improves appearance of skin texture/clarity at cosmetic (<10%) doses | **Moderate** — most robust data is at 15–20% drug strengths; lower-% cosmetic evidence thinner | [1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12472904/) |
| Direct wrinkle reduction | **Weak** — not its strength; value is tone/redness/clarity | [1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12472904/) |

## How it fits Kintsugi
**Good supporting fit for the "even, calm, radiant" dimension of renewal** — especially for tone, redness, and dark marks, which are core "visible aging" concerns. It's gentle and pairs with niacinamide and vitamin C for a tone-evening story. **Key caveat:** its strongest evidence is at **15–20%, which is drug-strength (prescription Rx in many markets)**; cosmetic products use lower % where evidence is weaker and high-% claims become drug claims. So it's a **supporting tone/clarity active at cosmetic strength**, not a hero, unless we pursue a regulated drug path.

## Formulation notes
- **Effective concentration:** Medical efficacy proven at **15% (gel/foam) and 20% (cream)**; cosmetic leave-ons typically use **~5–10%** (and "azelaic acid derivatives" like potassium azeloyl diglycinate, which are more soluble/gentle but less proven).
- **pH:** Effective around mildly acidic pH; formulation can be challenging — pure AzA has **poor solubility and can feel gritty**, which is why advanced delivery (liposomes, derivatives) is common [1].
- **Stability:** Reasonably stable; solubility/texture is the main formulation hurdle.
- **Pairs well with:** Niacinamide, vitamin C (tone synergy), hyaluronic acid, gentle hydrators. Generally tolerant alongside other actives.
- **Conflicts:** Few; introduce gradually with other exfoliating/retinoid actives to limit irritation.
- **Sensory:** Can feel gritty/heavy at high % unless well-formulated (derivatives/encapsulation help).

## Concerns & cautions
- **Irritation:** Usually mild — transient tingling, itching, or dryness at higher %.
- **Sun sensitivity:** Does not notably increase photosensitivity; SPF still standard.
- **Pregnancy:** Often cited as one of the more pregnancy-friendly tone actives; still use "consult your doctor" language and avoid guarantees.
- **Sourcing:** Synthetic/derived; widely available. Derivatives (potassium azeloyl diglycinate) trade proof for elegance.

## Claims caution
- ✅ **Safe cosmetic language (at cosmetic strengths):** "helps visibly even skin tone," "reduces the look of dark marks and uneven texture," "for a calmer, clearer, more radiant-looking complexion."
- ⚠️ **Risky / drug-like:** "treats rosacea / melasma / acne," any 15–20% efficacy claims in an OTC cosmetic (these are **drug indications** — using them would make the product a drug). Don't borrow the prescription evidence for cosmetic-strength marketing.
- See [`../../brand/glossary.md`](../../brand/glossary.md).

## Cost / sourcing
- **Low–moderate** raw cost; the spend is in **solubilization/delivery** to make it elegant (gritty texture is the enemy). Derivatives cost more. Widely available; low supply risk.

## Verdict for Kintsugi
⚠️ **SUPPORTING (conditional)** — a genuinely useful gentle tone/redness/clarity active, but its best evidence sits at drug strengths; include at cosmetic strength for the "even, calm radiance" angle with disciplined claims, or skip if it can't be made elegant.
