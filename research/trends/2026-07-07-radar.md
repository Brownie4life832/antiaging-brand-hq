## Ingredients & Science

- **Topical rapamycin / mTOR-inhibition steps forward as a named ingredient story (via Rapalogix, above).** The underlying science: a small exploratory GeroScience RCT (Chung et al., topical rapamycin ~10 µM) reported **reduced senescence markers (p16), improved dermal collagen and clinical appearance** over 6–8 months, with no systemic absorption — the most-cited human evidence that mTOR inhibition can act at the skin level. **On-thesis read:** rapamycin/rapalogs are pharma molecules (immunosuppressant lineage) — **not a commodity cosmetic active** and almost certainly a drug pathway if marketed for a physiological effect. This is a *watch-and-understand* item, not a formulate-tomorrow one: it's the science Kintsugi should be able to speak to credibly, and a sharp contrast for evidence-over-hype positioning (the honest line: promising senescence data, but long-duration, small-n, and regulatorily a drug — not an OTC barrier cream ingredient). **Claims flag:** any "senescence," "mTOR," "cellular aging" framing is drug territory. [GeroScience — topical rapamycin reduces senescence markers (PMC6925069)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6925069/)

- **Academic framing shift, "anti-aging → skin activation / longevity dermatology," now appearing in the peer-reviewed literature — not just trade press.** Two 2026 papers name the shift: *"Transitioning from Anti-aging to Skin Activation: Limiting Cellular Fatigue and Senescence for Skin Longevity"* (PubMed) and *"Translating Geroscience Into Clinical Longevity Dermatology"* (Haykal, *J Cosmetic Dermatology*). Both frame skin aging around senescence, mitochondrial dysfunction, ECM/DEJ remodeling and barrier restoration — i.e. the same hallmarks-of-aging vocabulary the marketing tier is adopting. **Why it matters:** the "longevity" language Kintsugi's category runs on now has genuine clinical-literature scaffolding to cite — useful for a credible, hedged science page — but the endpoints are still mechanistic/biomarker (structure/function), so they inform *understanding*, not *cosmetic claims*. [PubMed — Skin Activation (41779763)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41779763/) · [Wiley JOCD — Geroscience into Longevity Dermatology (Haykal 2026)](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jocd.70616)

- **Topical senolytics / anti-SASP, CoQ10/NAD+ mitochondrial actives, GHK-Cu copper peptides, PDRN/exosomes** — all carried forward from prior radars as directional; **no named new human RCT in-window** beyond the rapamycin evidence above. Exosomes remain **not FDA-cleared for cosmetic use** in the US. No change.

## Regulatory

- **No fresh in-window FDA enforcement action.** The current through-line, sharpened by 2026 MoCRA coverage: the **two most common warning-letter triggers are (1) unauthorized drug claims and (2) missing MoCRA adverse-event contact info** on-label — every label now needs a domestic address or electronic way for consumers to report adverse events. MoCRA has shifted the **burden of proof onto the manufacturer**: every claim must be substantiated, every ingredient listed correctly, every safety warning prominent. **Action for Kintsugi (unchanged, reinforced):** design the first label with (a) appearance/comfort claims only — keep "repairs the barrier" / "reduce inflammation" / any "senescence/longevity-mechanism" language off-pack — and (b) MoCRA adverse-event contact built in from day one. [Foley & Lardner — how MoCRA is reshaping FDA oversight 2026](https://www.foley.com/insights/publications/2026/03/how-mocra-is-reshaping-fda-oversight-of-cosmetics-in-2026/) · [FDA Inspections — cosmetic labeling violations 2025–2026](https://fdainspections.com/cosmetic-labeling-violations-fda-inspections-2025-2026/)

- **EU clock, still running (flagged 07-03→07-06):** 56 new fragrance allergens mandatory-label from **Jul 31**, updated INCI Glossary mandatory from **Jul 30** (now ~3 weeks out), PFAS REACH cutoff **Oct 10, 2026**. Design the first label EU-ready. [COSlaw — allergen FAQ](https://coslaw.eu/fragrance-allergens-july-eu-deadline-faq/)
