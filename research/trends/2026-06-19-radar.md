## Ingredients

- **SILAB launches LONGEVICELL® — myrtle-leaf active targeting sirtuins, NAD+ and AMPK.** INCI: *Hydrolyzed Myrtus Communis Leaf Extract*; activates sirtuins 1/3/6/7, claims improved mitochondrial activity and reduced glycation/senescence; recommended 1–4%, results from 14 days. A claim-friendlier, botanical route into the same longevity-pathway story OneSkin tells with peptides. **Claims flag:** sirtuin/NAD+/"cell longevity" language edges toward structure/function — keep to appearance/resilience framing. [Cosmetics & Toiletries](https://www.cosmeticsandtoiletries.com/cosmetic-ingredients/actives/news/22933022/silab-silabs-longevicell-targets-sirtuins-coactivators-for-antiaging-longevity-efficacy) · [SILAB](https://www.silab.fr/en/products/skincare/31/longevicell)
- **Pterostilbene clinical readout.** Double-blind, split-face study (n=31, 28 days) of a 0.1% pterostilbene emulsion vs. control reported improved elasticity/firmness and reduced forehead, undereye and crow's-feet wrinkles. Small/short, but adds to the resveratrol-analog antioxidant story. [ScienceDaily background](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2025/09/250914205851.htm)
- **Plant-derived exosomes & PDRN move into clean beauty.** Suppliers are pushing plant-based takes on two of the buzziest regenerative actives. **Reminder:** human/animal exosome therapeutic claims remain a regulatory third rail (see landscape #5) — "plant-derived" reframes sourcing but does not license therapeutic claims. [TrendHunter](https://www.trendhunter.com/amp/trends/Longevity-driven-skincare)

## Trends

- **Inflammation / "inflammaging" care is being named a defining 2026 skin-longevity trend** — formulating to calm chronic low-grade inflammation as the mechanism behind visible aging. Pairs cleanly with a barrier-first, peptide-supported story. [Cosmetics Business](https://cosmeticsbusiness.com/inflammation-care-is-a-key-skin-longevity-trend)
- **"Climate-adaptive beauty"** moving from niche to mainstream — formulas that respond to skin temperature / environment. Worth a watch as a possible product-narrative angle. [Qogita](https://www.qogita.com/blog/tiktok-beauty-trends-2026/)
- **TikTok is now the primary discovery/search engine for beauty** (~65% of Gen Z, ~55% of Millennials research there pre-purchase). Reinforces that a new entrant's content/search strategy on TikTok is table-stakes, not optional. [Qogita](https://www.qogita.com/blog/tiktok-beauty-trends-2026/)
- **"Skin longevity" continues displacing "anti-aging" as the premium frame** across trade and consumer press — consistent with landscape #1; no reversal signal. [Who What Wear](https://www.whowhatwear.com/beauty/skin/2026-skincare-trends)

## Regulatory

- **FDA is expanding monitoring of social media, e-commerce and digital marketing for cosmetic drug claims.** Warning letters increasingly cite Instagram/TikTok posts and Amazon listings as evidence. Our owned + influencer content is in scope, not just the label. [FDA Inspections analysis](https://fdainspections.com/cosmetic-labeling-violations-fda-inspections-2025-2026/)
- **The live line:** "anti-aging" referring to *appearance* is allowed; "reverse aging" / "repair DNA" / cellular-rejuvenation claims read as **drug claims**. Direct guardrail for how we phrase any longevity/senescence messaging. [Glaser Weil](https://www.glaserweil.com/insights/leadership/fda-publishes-new-warning-letters-to-cosmetics-manufacturers-echoing-last-y)
- **MoCRA enforcement:** missing mandatory adverse-event contact info is among the most-cited 2026 violations — a cheap compliance item to get right from day one. [ArentFox Schiff](https://www.afslaw.com/perspectives/alerts/mocra-marches-fda-issues-draft-guidance-mandatory-cosmetic-recalls)
