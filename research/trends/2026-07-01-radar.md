## ⚡ Flagged

- **FDA approved bemotrizinol (BEMT) — the first new sunscreen active ingredient in the US in ~20 years.** Final administrative order (OTC000039) issued **June 9, 2026**, adding BEMT to the OTC sunscreen monograph; it takes effect **August 9, 2026**, and BEMT-containing sunscreens could reach US shelves as early as Aug 2026. BEMT is a broad-spectrum (UVA+UVB), highly photostable filter with low skin absorption, GRASE for ages 6mo+ at up to 6%; it's the first ingredient through the FDA's new streamlined review pathway, bringing the US to 17 approved filters. **Why it matters for Kintsugi:** photoprotection is the single best-evidenced "skin-longevity" intervention there is, and the US filter list has been a well-known laggard vs. the EU/Asia. If the brand ever entertains an SPF SKU (or a day cream with SPF), the formulation landscape just shifted — a modern filter that competitors will race to adopt, and a genuine "prevention" story that stays on the *appearance/protection* side of the claims line. Watch which premium brands announce BEMT reformulations first. [FDA](https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/fda-expands-sunscreen-options-first-time-20-years) · [Dermatology Times](https://www.dermatologytimes.com/view/fda-accepts-addition-of-bemotrizinol-as-first-new-sunscreen-ingredient-in-20-years) · [PolitiFact (2026-06-25 explainer)](https://www.politifact.com/article/2026/jun/25/BEMT-bemotrizinol-new-sunscreen-ingredient-FDA/)

## Ingredients & Science

- **EpiSnow (Mibelle Biochemistry) — snow-algae biotech active positioned on "molecular longevity."** A preservative-free, water-soluble active derived from extremophile snow algae, claimed to act via **epigenetic modulation** across "seven key pathways of skin ageing" and to "lower the skin's biological age." Debuted at in-cosmetics Global 2026 and now circulating in ingredient-trend roundups. **Claims flag:** "lowers biological age" and "epigenetic modulation" are about the most drug-adjacent phrasings in the category — supplier marketing language, not something we could echo without serious substantiation/legal review. Useful as a signal of where premium actives are heading. [Premium Beauty News](https://www.premiumbeautynews.com/en/ingredient-trends-to-watch-key,27522) · [CosmeticsDesign-Europe](https://www.cosmeticsdesign-europe.com/Article/2026/03/23/9-new-ingredient-innovations-debut-at-incosmetics-global-2026/)
- **Vicia faba (fava bean) protein active — "recharges drained skin cells."** A new clinically-positioned active built from Vicia faba proteins, marketed around cellular-energy "recharge," collagen biosynthesis and anti-melanogenesis. Same molecular-longevity framing as EpiSnow; part of the 2026 shift toward ingredients that name a specific aging hallmark rather than "anti-aging" broadly. **Claims flag:** "collagen biosynthesis" = structure/function language; keep any reference to appearance/firmness. [Premium Beauty News](https://www.premiumbeautynews.com/en/ingredient-trends-to-watch-key,27522)
- **Topical senolytics/senomorphics — the science under the "senescence" marketing keeps maturing (watch-only).** Recent literature includes a topical ABT-263 study reducing senescence markers (p16/p21) and improving wound healing in *aged mouse* skin, and prior exploratory human trials of topical rapamycin reducing skin senescence markers. This is the mechanistic backdrop for "zombie cell"/senolytic claims now appearing in premium longevity marketing (cf. OneSkin's OS-01). **Reality check + claims flag:** most direct senolytic evidence is preclinical or small/exploratory; "senolytic," "clears senescent cells," and "reverses aging" are firmly drug-claim territory. Track in [`research/science/`](../science/). [ScienceDaily (2026-05)](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260519003215.htm) · [ABT-263 topical study (PMC)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC11810067/) · [OS-01 pilot trial (PMC)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC11975214/)

## Trends

- **"Climate-adaptive beauty" is being called mainstream, not niche, for 2026** — formulas positioned to respond to skin temperature or the surrounding environment. Worth watching as a *framing* trend (a fresh angle on "barrier support under stress") rather than a specific ingredient; adjacent to our barrier-resilience thesis without requiring us to make reactive-tech claims. [Qogita](https://www.qogita.com/blog/tiktok-beauty-trends-2026/)
- **Spicule serums going viral** — serums built on **spicules** (tiny silica micro-structures from marine sponges) that create micro-channels for deeper penetration; users report tingling + texture/renewal effects. A physically-active (mechanical) delivery format; interesting as a delivery-tech talking point but with an irritation/sensitization profile to respect. [Qogita](https://www.qogita.com/blog/tiktok-beauty-trends-2026/)
- **Snail mucin resurgence + "cloud skin" finish** continue as viral staples — mucin sold on barrier-support/hydration (on-thesis for the lean barrier routine); "cloud skin" is a soft-focus makeup/finish look, mostly aesthetic. Lower strategic weight; logged for completeness. [Qogita](https://www.qogita.com/blog/tiktok-beauty-trends-2026/)

## Regulatory

- **Bemotrizinol approval (see Flagged)** is the marquee regulatory event — first monograph sunscreen active in ~20 years, effective 2026-08-09. This also validates that the FDA's new streamlined OTC-actives pathway (from MoCRA-era reform) can actually clear an ingredient — meaningful for future US filter/active expansion. [FDA](https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/fda-expands-sunscreen-options-first-time-20-years)
- **MoCRA enforcement is still building, not yet aimed at marketing claims.** 2026 legal roundups note FDA is exercising its **mandatory cosmetic recall** authority (draft Q&A guidance issued late 2025/early 2026) and has sent GMP warning letters to facilities that make *both* drug and cosmetic products. No fresh in-window *claims* enforcement action surfaced. The cheap day-one compliance wins are unchanged: facility/product **registration**, an **adverse-event contact** on-pack, and clean GMP. [Foley & Lardner](https://www.foley.com/insights/publications/2026/03/how-mocra-is-reshaping-fda-oversight-of-cosmetics-in-2026/) · [FDA — MoCRA](https://www.fda.gov/cosmetics/cosmetics-laws-regulations/modernization-cosmetics-regulation-act-2022-mocra)
