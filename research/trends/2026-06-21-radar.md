## ⚡ Flagged

- **Trend forecasters now say *aggressive "anti-aging" messaging itself is fading* — while barrier health, longevity and lean multifunctional routines are the rising frames.** Beauty Independent's 2026 "what's rising / what's losing sizzle" panel names the fading list explicitly: "freeze, peel and purge" harsh-correction messaging, single-ingredient/"one molecule" serums, viral TikTok hacks (skin cycling, slugging), and **fear-based "clean" positioning**. Rising: longevity & cellular repair, biotech actives (exosomes/PDRN/peptides), **barrier + microbiome support**, and **minimalist multifunctional routines**. **Why it matters for Kintsugi:** This is the most direct external validation yet of two of our settled bets — the **premium barrier-repair cream** and the **lean-routine-beats-SKU-sprawl** thesis are exactly the rising lanes, while the *language* of aggressive anti-aging is now a liability. Two takeaways: (1) it strengthens the case to *avoid* "anti-aging/correction" framing in name + voice and lean into resilience/longevity language; (2) the "fear-based clean positioning is losing traction" note is a caution against an easy-but-fading wedge. [Beauty Independent](https://www.beautyindependent.com/top-skincare-trends-2026-those-losing-sizzle/)

## Ingredients & Science

- **Bioactive collagen peptides RCT (oral) — randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, n=77, 5,000 mg/day for 12 weeks.** Reported significant improvements in dermal density, hydration and transepidermal water loss (TEWL) vs. placebo. A reasonably-powered, real-endpoint readout (TEWL = a literal barrier-function measure) that strengthens the *oral* collagen-peptide evidence base — relevant only if we ever entertain an ingestible adjacency (cf. AB's "The Skin"); the active is *oral*, not topical. **Claims flag:** "boosts collagen / collagen synthesis" remains structure/function language — keep any reference to appearance/hydration/firmness framing. [PMC (study)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12661388/)
- **New oral-collagen barrier-function trial recruiting (NCT07529249, "Evaluation of Oral Collagen Peptides on Skin Barrier Function in Women").** Recruiting as of May 2026; primary completion ~Aug 2026. Watch-only — signals continued clinical investment specifically in the *barrier-function* endpoint that underpins our category. [ClinicalTrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07529249)

## Regulatory

- **Quiet day — no fresh enforcement action in the strict window.** Of note for the record: searches surfaced the long-standing withdrawal of **FDA Import Alert 66-38** (the 30-year block on imported anti-aging cosmetics) — but that withdrawal dates to **late 2017**, so it is *not* news; logged only to avoid re-surfacing it as "new" in a future edition. The live guardrails remain those in the 06-19/06-20 radars: appearance-only "anti-aging" language is allowed; "repair/rejuvenate/boost collagen/biological age" read as drug claims; MoCRA registration + adverse-event contact info are the cheap day-one compliance wins. [Davis+Gilbert (2017 analysis)](https://www.dglaw.com/fda-withdraws-anti-aging-import-alert-and-continues-oversight-over-cosmetics-claims/)
