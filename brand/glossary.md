## Ingredient & science terms *(populate as research lands)*

> Keep these accurate — copy will pull from here. Sources tracked in [`../research/ingredients/`](../research/ingredients/).

| Term | Plain-language meaning | Notes / claim caution |
|---|---|---|
| Retinoid / Retinol | Vitamin A derivative; supports cell turnover & visible firmness | Strong evidence; irritation & sun sensitivity; claim care |
| Peptides | Short amino-acid chains; signal skin repair processes | "Supports" not "boosts X%" |
| Niacinamide (B3) | Barrier support, tone, oil balance | Well-tolerated, good evidence |
| Hyaluronic acid | Humectant; holds water in skin | Hydration, plumping (temporary) |
| Ceramides | Lipids that make up the skin barrier | "Barrier repair" — central to the product logic |
| Vitamin C (L-ascorbic, etc.) | Antioxidant; brightness, environmental defense | Stability/formulation matters |
| Growth factors | Signaling proteins for repair/renewal | Premium, evidence varies — vet carefully |
| Skin barrier | Outer layer that holds moisture in, irritants out | The barrier-repair thesis lives here |
| SPF / photoaging | Sun-driven aging | #1 evidence-based "anti-aging" step |

## Claim-language cheat sheet

- ✅ Safer cosmetic language: "helps improve the appearance of," "supports," "visibly," "nourishes," "hydrates."
- ⚠️ Risky / drug-like: "treats," "cures," "prevents," "boosts collagen by X%," "erases wrinkles." Flag for substantiation/legal.
