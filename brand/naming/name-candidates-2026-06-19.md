# Brand name candidates — 100, the Placek way

> **Status:** v1 (2026-06-19). A working pool to react to — **nothing here is chosen, and positioning is still OPEN.** These were generated by running David Placek / Lexicon's actual method (see the [`brand-naming` skill](../../.claude/skills/brand-naming/SKILL.md) and the [creative brief](creative-brief.md)) — **3,128 raw candidates** across two rounds, 21 "treasure-hunt" teams (insider + disguised + database, à la his three-teams trick), screened to a shortlist, then **adversarially web-verified** for existing-brand collisions and bad meanings. The 100 below are the survivors.
>
> They're organized by the five **positioning lanes** (A–E from [positioning.md](../positioning.md)) so you can pick a *name and a direction together*. The pool deliberately skews to **B (pro-aging)** and **E (barrier/resilience)** — your two strongest white-space lanes — and is thin on **A ("fight aging")**, the lane you're trying to *differentiate away from*.

## How to go through these (read first — it's Placek's whole point)

1. **Don't evaluate — speculate.** For each name you like, don't ask "do I like it?" Ask **"what could this name *do* for us?"** Suspend judgment; that's how the bold ones survive.
2. **Discomfort is a buy signal.** "If your team is comfortable with the name, you don't have the name yet." Notice the ones that make you a little uneasy *and* won't leave your head. **Look for polarization** — the name we argue about has energy.
3. **Two drills to pressure-test a finalist:**
   - Tell a friend *"a new competitor just launched, they're called ___"* — their reaction tells you what the name *does*, not their taste.
   - The signal you want: *"I don't know much about them, but they're **not like the other guys**."*
4. **The `.com` does not gate this.** Get the right name first; a prefix/suffix, `.co`, or `.ai` is fine, or buy the exact `.com` later. Don't kill a great name over a domain.
5. **"Clear?" is a *web-screen, not a legal clearance.*** ✓ = no obvious collision found; ⚠ = a real flag worth a proper trademark/availability check before you fall in love. Every finalist still needs a formal knockout search.

## ⭐ Start here — 15 to fight for

The boldest, cleanest, most Placek-shaped of the set (each is in the tables below too). These are the ones to *speculate* on first:

- **Goldseam** *(B/E)* — kintsugi's golden-repair idea in fresh, ownable English; "the mend made beautiful." The whole worldview in one word, no Japanese borrow.
- **Latebloom** *(B)* — the purest age-positive thesis: beauty that arrives *with* age.
- **Carapace** *(E/B)* — the shell that shields *and grows with* the animal; barrier + quiet strength + time.
- **Slowmend** *(E/B)* — repair on the skin's own time; a direct rebuke to the category's urgency.
- **Keptwell** *(B/E)* — "skin kept, not fixed; well-kept, not fought." (⚠ homophone of a small "Well Kept" beauty brand — check.)
- **Anneal** *(C/E)* — to heat-treat *out* stress and *into* toughness; resilience as science + craft.
- **Krasis** *(C/E)* — Greek for a balanced blend in right proportion; names your *multi-active* system as harmony, not a pile of actives.
- **Tueri** *(E/B)* — Latin "to watch over, guard"; quiet guardianship as a verb.
- **Velamen** *(E)* — Latin for a protective veil/covering; oblique, premium, on-brief for the barrier hero.
- **Senza** *(B)* — Italian "without" — literally *without* fear/urgency/age-anxiety. A statement-starter.
- **Tregua** *(B)* — Italian/Spanish "a truce"; "a truce with time" reframes aging beautifully.
- **Solveig** *(B/E)* — Norse "sun-strength"; luminous resilience, warmth with backbone.
- **Reficio** *(A/E)* — Latin "I restore"; a confident, corrective verb-name *without* the fear language.
- **Otium** *(D/B)* — Latin for unhurried repose; skincare as leisure, not warfare.
- **Indusium** *(C/E)* — the membrane that shields a fern's spores; an intelligent, premium image of protecting the vulnerable.
