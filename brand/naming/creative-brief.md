# Naming — Creative Brief (the "Identify" phase)

> **Status:** v1 (2026-06-19). This is the **input** to the brand-naming exercise, built the David Placek / Lexicon way: *forget the word, start with behavior and experience.* It stays **positioning-neutral** — the brand name, story, visual direction, and core positioning (A–E) are all still **OPEN**. We generate names *across* the live directions; we do not assume one.

## The Diamond (Placek's core exercise)

Placek's one exercise for any namer: draw a diamond (two triangles). Fill the four points. Spend most of your time on the bottom-left — *what do we have to say to win* — because that's where the name comes from.

```
                 ▲  WIN — how we define winning
                / \
   what we    /   \   what we
   HAVE  ◄───     ───►  NEED
   to win   \   /    to win
             \ /
              ▼  what we have to SAY to win
```

### ▲ WIN — how we define winning
Own a **meaning-led, resilience-first** position in skin-longevity: become the premium brand people choose because it *stands for something other than fear*. Concretely — a name + identity that earns **asymmetric advantage** (a head start before launch) and **cumulative advantage** (compounds every time it's seen). Winning is *not* "a clever word"; it's the right name that makes a 35–55 buyer think **"these aren't like the other guys."**

### ◄ What we HAVE to win (assets already in hand)
- A real **product white-space**: a premium **barrier-repair cream** hero (ceramide + cholesterol + fatty-acid + niacinamide) where the category is thin.
- A documented **competitive gap**: the field is near-universally **fear-framed**, clinical-white, opaque, and single-molecule-fragile (66-brand teardown).
- **Claims discipline** and a real **evidence base** (20 ingredient analyses) → credibility we can substantiate.
- Founder operating capability (Underdog Games) and DTC economics already reasoned through.

### ► What we NEED to win (gaps to close)
- A **distinctive, ownable name** that isn't a category cliché or a competitor echo.
- The **emotional / worldview layer** the whole category lacks.
- A chosen **positioning** (A–E) the name can support without locking too early.
- **Trademark + domain/handle** clearance (note Placek: the exact `.com` is *not* decisive — a prefix/suffix or `.ai`/`.co` is fine; get the right name first).

### ▼ What we have to SAY to win  ← spend time here
The category screams *fight / erase / reverse / defeat*. We say the opposite, in the marketplace's language but with a different contract:
- **"Skin you keep — not flaws you fight."** (resilience over reversal)
- **"The barrier is the point."** (repair & wholeness, not correction)
- **"Proven, not promised."** (transparency taken upmarket)
- **"Time on your side."** (age as continuity, not enemy)

The name's job is to *start that story*, not *state a fact*. (Swiffer, not "ReadyMop." Azure, not "CloudPro." Sonos, not an entertainment word.)

## Behavior & experience (what the name must evoke)

Placek: a name is bidirectional — how we behave toward the market, and how the market behaves toward us.

| | |
|---|---|
| **How we behave** | Calm, confident, a generous teacher; honest about proof; warm-intelligent — never fearful, urgent, or clinical-cold. |
| **How the market behaves toward us** | Leans in (curiosity, *relief* that it isn't fear-based), trusts the claims, forms a *predisposition to consider* — "not like the other guys." |
| **The felt experience** | Quiet strength · restoration & repair · being protected/shielded · luminous health (not flashy "glow") · ageless composure · time as an ally. Skin that is **whole, resilient, and well — kept rather than fixed.** |

## The creative framework (the "window" the names travel through)

Every candidate is generated and judged against this — *not* a list of objectives, a window to open the search:

1. **Surprisingly familiar** — easy for the lazy brain to process, with something unexpected that makes it stop. Originality *in this category*.
2. **Starts a story, doesn't state a fact** — evocative over descriptive.
3. **Sound symbolism matched to the feeling** — for quiet strength + resilience, balance a *noticeable signal* (a V/S/Z/B or a crisp plosive) against a *smooth, calm flow* (soft L/M/N, long round vowels). Avoid harsh or sterile-clinical phonetics.
4. **Headroom** — works across a full range (cream → serum → SPF → future apex), not just one SKU; and across markets.
5. **Tone-neutral enough to support the chosen positioning (A–E)** — doesn't hard-lock "fight aging" *or* "pro-aging" before that decision is made.
6. **Distinctive & clearable** — no collision/rhyme with the 66-brand set; not a tired root used literally.

## Constraints

**Avoid colliding with** (existing players): Augustinus Bader, OneSkin, Timeline, La Mer, La Prairie, SkinCeuticals, SkinMedica, Drunk Elephant, The Ordinary, Paula's Choice, Medik8, Dr. Barbara Sturm, Tatcha, CeraVe, BIOEFFECT, Bonjout, Ourself, Noble Panacea, Sulwhasoo, Omorovicza, Caudalie, RéVive, Natura Bissé, 111SKIN, et al.

**Avoid as the whole name** (clichés): `-ceuticals · -medica · -derm · skin- · age/ageless · youth · glow · timeless · eternal · lumière/luminous (literal) · radiance · rejuven- · revive/renew (bare) · fountain · phoenix · elixir · essence · luxe`. Use such roots only if transformed into something genuinely surprising.
