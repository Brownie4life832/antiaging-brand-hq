What to Make — Product Line Recommendation

Working recommendation v0.2 — 2026-06-18 (name / story / positioning OPEN; revised post-adversarial-review; see "Adversarial review — resolved" near the end) Head of Product synthesis of three strategist proposals (Lean MVP · Architecture-Led Flagship · Business-Model-Optimized), reconciled against the strategic brief, the white-space map, and the ingredient verdicts. The brand name, story/metaphor, visual direction, and core positioning remain open decisions; this document fixes only what the evidence and competitive map support regardless of those choices.

The decision in one line

Build a barrier-repair ritual with a clear product architecture as the strategic spine, but launch it lean (Phase 1 = a 3-SKU trio, white-label where the formula was never the moat) and sequence every SKU as a funnel job (front door → LTV anchor → AOV lift → PR apex). What the research supports regardless of positioning is three things: (a) the product architecture (a premium barrier-repair cream hero in a real category gap, plus a lean routine), (b) a credible-floor ingredient strategy (proven actives bought not built; one ownable, transparently-dosed signature complex), and (c) execution quality (radical honesty, premium refillable packaging, and CX). The molecules are the credible floor, bought not built. We invest in exactly one ownable, transparently-dosed signature complex — working title a ceramide + cholesterol + fatty-acid barrier complex — to make the hero merchandisable, and we do not bet the brand on a single patented molecule. Guardrail (internal): the signature complex is a naming/PR/merchandising device — a transparently-dosed off-the-shelf blend — and is never claimed as patented IP or cited as efficacy proof. Efficacy is carried by the named actives' published literature, not by the complex as a construct. This is exactly the trap the white-space map flags (Tier 3 refuted "build a defensible signature complex" because it collapses into "a well-labeled off-the-shelf blend" that L'Oréal/EL out-fund); we keep it as a wrapper, not a moat.

The brand's name, story/metaphor, visual direction, and core positioning remain OPEN. Those choices will shape SKU naming and the brand narrative, but they do not change the architecture, the ingredient floor, or the execution standard below. Where positioning materially changes a decision, it is flagged as positioning-dependent.

How the three proposals were reconciled: - Spine = Architecture-Led Flagship (Proposal 2). It is the only proposal anchored to the #1 structurally-uncopyable opportunity layer (a coherent, top-to-bottom brand identity and worldview, whatever that worldview turns out to be). The brand/meaning layer in this category is largely empty; the science layer is not. Differentiation lives in the brand layer + execution. - Grafted from Lean MVP (Proposal 1): launch discipline. We do not ship the full 8-SKU flagship at once. Phase 1 is a tight trio, and we buy commodity formulas to put scarce cash into brand/aesthetic/content where the moat actually is. - Grafted from Business-Model-Optimized (Proposal 3): funnel + LTV rigor. Every SKU does a job (CAC, LTV, AOV, PR). Refill subscription is live day one. The line is the trade-up ladder.

Conflicts resolved explicitly (see §6 for detail): 1. Hero price — $88 vs $128 vs $92. → $118 (provisional, gated). Positioned above OneSkin's single-benefit hero (we are a multi-active complete system, not a single peptide), comfortably below ultra-luxury, and the airless-refill model lets us hold the price without discounting. We reject the $88 (reads "CeraVe-adjacent," forfeits the prestige quadrant) and the bare $92. Note: the OneSkin benchmark is [partially unverified] ($94/$100/$120); $118 stays provisional until OneSkin's actual current price and the waitlist soft-launch validate it (hard gate — see §5 and Open Question #1). 2. Build vs. buy the hero — pure white-label vs. custom complex. → Buy the base technology, brand the formula. We use a mature off-the-shelf ceramide+cholesterol+fatty-acid system as the engineering base (lean), but specify the named ceramide + cholesterol + fatty-acid barrier complex ratio and run one modest substantiation study post-launch. This is the synthesis: lean cash-out and an ownable, honest signature — without a multi-year patent program (an avoid-zone). 3. Longevity/renewal peptide at launch vs. later. → Later (Phase 3); at launch the ceramide barrier system carries the efficacy promise and GHK-Cu carries only a hedged "emerging" renewal narrative. All three proposals' caution converges here: OS-01 is patent-locked, alternative senotherapeutic peptides carry sourcing/MOQ/substantiation risk. We do not gate first revenue on it. Critical rebalance (per adversarial review): we do not load the launch renewal/"longevity" story onto GHK-Cu — a moderate/small-sample active (~21-41 women, with one study showing no independent wrinkle effect on laser-resurfaced skin). Doing so would over-merchandise thin evidence and contradict the honesty standard. The strong-evidence ceramide+cholesterol+fatty-acid barrier system carries the hero's efficacy promise; GHK-Cu is labeled "emerging/supporting" in the Proof Library; and "longevity" is never the headline benefit on any launch SKU (it is, at most, a search term — not a claim). 4. Range size — 3 vs 8 SKUs. → Launch 3, roadmap to ~6-7. Resist SKU sprawl before scale.

2. Strategic rationale

What the research supports regardless of positioning

Three things hold no matter which brand name, story, or core positioning the founder chooses: 1. The product architecture. A literal barrier-repair hero plus a lean, complete routine sits in a real, evidence-backed category gap. This is the merchandising and efficacy backbone. 2. A credible-floor ingredient strategy. Proven actives (bought, not built) carry the efficacy; one ownable, transparently-dosed signature complex makes the hero merchandisable without betting on a single patented molecule. 3. Execution quality. Radical claims honesty, premium active-protecting refillable packaging, and strong CX are the durable, copy-resistant differentiators in a category full of opaque incumbents.

The product line is the physical proof of whatever the brand decides to stand for. A literal barrier-repair hero makes the "repair" promise true, not decorative — and it remains the right hero whether the eventual story is corrective, age-positive, science/longevity-led, clean/transparency-led, or barrier-health-led. The science is the floor; the brand layer and execution are the flag.

White-space gaps we occupy (ranked by the map)

These describe the market, not a chosen positioning. Several are positioning-dependent and are flagged as such; see §5 for the positioning options. - #1 Authentic, coherent top-to-bottom brand worldview (the spine — structurally uncopyable once chosen). The brand/meaning layer in this category is largely empty; a name, claim set, ritual, and pack that all embody one coherent worldview are hard for fear-based or lab-cold incumbents to follow. Which worldview is an open decision (see §5). - #2 A distinctive REPAIR-led visual direction — a clear visual exit from clinical-white sameness; low-cost, no IP, instantly distinguishable. The specific aesthetic is an open decision. - #3 Radical claims honesty carried upmarket — the empty "honest + luxurious" quadrant. A published Proof Library ("proven vs. emerging," actives named at concentration) is both the conversion wedge and a structural moat against opaque incumbents. (Positioning-independent — an execution standard.) - #8 Barrier/lipidome as a PREMIUM repair-first signature — the hero's efficacy spine and the merchandising gap (only mass owns barrier as hero; Senté's HSA is boxed into rosacea — the nearest threat). (Positioning-independent — the architecture.) - #7 Safe, no-"ick", FDA-uncontroversial alternative to growth-factor/exosome demand — a conversion play via GHK-Cu, the most defensible regenerative peptide. (Positioning-independent.) - #4 The underserved 35-55 "age well" buyer at honest $$-$$$ — strongest when fused with a coherent worldview, weak on price-position alone. - #6 Evidence-clean inflammaging / anti-inflammatory repair — a soothing co-benefit (niacinamide + ectoin), never a standalone hero. - #9 Refillable / airless / active-protecting packaging — both feature and proof point; the LTV/subscription engine. A CX/LTV differentiator, not a moat (easily copied). (Positioning-independent — an execution standard.) - #10 Genuinely unisex stance — one line, unisex voice can capture the male-skewing longevity crowd that female-coded incumbents cede; no separate men's line. (Positioning-dependent on voice/aesthetic.) - #11 Trade-up ladder converting mass-tier value graduates — content/SEO acquisition engine; the go-to-market, not a standalone position.

Avoid-zones we deliberately stay out of (and why)

  • No growth factors / exosomes / stem-cell / conditioned-media — active FDA enforcement, infection cases, "ick"/sourcing baggage, penetration problems. We convert this demand (#7), never serve it.
  • No new retinol as the signature — dupe-saturated, irritation/returns risk. The retinoid (Phase 2) is a buffered supporting step, never the flag.
  • Vitamin C is not the differentiator — table stakes; it is a supporting AM step (Phase 2), never headlines.
  • No PDRN-led or peel/AHA-first identity — commoditizing / conflicts with gentle barrier-respecting repair.
  • Not betting the brand on a single patented molecule — GHK-Cu and ceramides are open/commodity; the moat is the brand layer + execution. The signature complex is named and transparently dosed — the opposite of an opaque "secret complex." Internal guardrail: the complex is a naming/PR device only — never claimed as patented IP or as efficacy proof; efficacy rests on the named actives' published literature. (Per the white-space map's Tier-3 refutation of "defensible signature complex.")
  • No opaque heritage "secret complex" luxury; no clinical-white/blue lookalike aesthetic; no fear/erase/defy framing as a default; no physician-dispensed positioning; no mass/drugstore price war. (Note: whether the brand leans corrective vs. age-positive is an open positioning decision — see §5 — but a fear/erase tone is an avoid-zone for the honesty execution standard regardless.)
  • "Skin longevity" used for search demand only — if used at all, it is an SEO/search term, never the headline benefit on a launch SKU, and never a bare buzzword substituting for a real benefit claim. (The hero is not labeled the "longevity story carrier"; the ceramide barrier system carries efficacy, GHK-Cu carries a hedged emerging-renewal narrative.)
  • No self-reported survey-stat marketing ("96% agreed," "reverse aging") — the exact convention radical honesty differentiates against.

3. The product line — per SKU

Evidence notes are summarized from research/ingredients/. Claims language is the claims-SAFE cosmetic wording; the risky/drug-like alternatives are explicitly avoided. SKU names are neutral working titles pending the open brand/positioning decisions.

⭐ Barrier Repair Cream (working title) — HERO

  • Product role: Replenishing the skin's own barrier lipids is the literal embodiment of "repair." Positioned "results without the burn" — the direct antidote to the category's #1 complaint (aggressive clinical lines that burn the mature/sensitive skin they target). This role holds across positioning options; only the language around it shifts with the chosen worldview.
  • Hero actives & evidence:
  • Ceramide + cholesterol + fatty-acid barrier complex — physiological ceramide blend (NP/AP/EOP) + cholesterol + free fatty acids, cholesterol-forward ratio. Evidence: strong for barrier restoration / reduced TEWL / hydration (RCTs in compromised skin); the ratio matters more than the dose. The single most on-thesis active we have — and the efficacy spine that carries the hero's results promise.
  • Niacinamide ~4-5%. Evidence: strong for barrier/tone; upregulates ceramide synthesis (the synergy story). NAD+ framing is education/mechanism only: niacinamide is an NAD precursor, but topical niacinamide is not proven to raise skin NAD+, so we may explain the mechanism and never claim a benefit from it ("recharges NAD+," "boosts cellular energy," "activates sirtuins" are off-limits per nad-niacinamide-longevity.md).
  • GHK-Cu copper peptide. Evidence: moderate, from small studies (~21-41 women; one study found no independent wrinkle effect on laser-resurfaced skin). A supporting/emerging renewal active — explicitly labeled as such in the Proof Library — not the hero's credibility anchor and not the carrier of the launch efficacy promise. The merchandising weight must not exceed the evidence weight.
  • Ectoin (environmental-stress resilience) + HA + panthenol (comfort).
  • Claims-SAFE language: "helps restore and strengthen the look and feel of the skin barrier," "replenishes skin's essential lipids," "comforts dry, compromised-looking skin." GHK-Cu firmness language ("supports the appearance of firmer, denser-looking skin") is gated: it ships only after cosmetic-claims counsel signs the claims matrix, must be defensible on the existing small-study literature, and the 28% collagen figure is internal-only — it must never appear in consumer copy. Avoid: "barrier repair" as a disease claim, "rebuilds your skin," "boosts collagen by X%."
  • Format: Airless refillable jar, 50 ml. Price: $118 / $96 refill / $82 refill-sub.
  • Job-to-be-done: The AOV anchor, the highest-frequency replenishment SKU, the refill-subscription LTV engine, and the physical proof of whatever the brand stands for.

Renewal Serum (working title)

  • Product role: The science-forward step-up that gives the line a credible emerging renewal narrative without IP or regulatory exposure. It carries a hedged "emerging/supporting" renewal story, not a proven longevity efficacy promise — the regenerative-peptide narrative is told honestly through GHK-Cu, explicitly labeled emerging in the Proof Library. The licensed senotherapeutic peptide is a Phase 3 upgrade, not a launch dependency.
  • Hero actives & evidence: GHK-Cu (moderate, small-sample human data; the most defensible regenerative peptide, but emerging-not-proven), niacinamide (NAD+ = mechanism/education only, never an efficacy claim), ergothioneine (antioxidant; the "longevity vitamin" framing stays at defense/appearance level, never a "longevity benefit"), multi-MW HA, ectoin.
  • Claims-SAFE language: "supports skin's natural renewal," "helps visibly improve the look of firmness and fine lines," "inspired by skin-renewal science." All firmness/renewal phrasing is gated through the claims matrix and counsel before any copy ships. Avoid: "senolytic," "clears zombie cells," "reverses biological age," "recharges NAD+ / boosts cellular energy," quantified collagen boosts, and "longevity" as a stand-alone benefit.
  • Format: Airless refillable serum, 30 ml (mind the GHK-Cu copper-blue cue and the vitamin-C separation caveat). Price: $88 / $75 sub.
  • Job-to-be-done: Step 2 of the ritual; the differentiation/story SKU; warms the subscriber base for the Phase 3 apex trade-up.

Gentle Cleanser (working title)

  • Product role: "Don't strip what you're repairing." The calm entry point to the ritual and the lowest-friction trade-up gateway.
  • Hero actives & evidence: Mild non-stripping surfactants, glycerin, ceramide trace, panthenol. Evidence: gentle-cleansing fundamentals; zero active/regulatory risk — the fastest, cheapest white-label item.
  • Claims-SAFE language: "gently cleanses without stripping," "leaves skin comfortable and barrier-kind." Avoid: treatment/efficacy overclaims on a rinse-off.
  • Format: Cream-gel tube, 150 ml. Price: $34 / $26 refill.
  • Job-to-be-done: Low-CAC acquisition + subscription starter; the habit/replenishment hook; the trade-up ladder's front door for mass-tier graduates.

Routine Set (bundle, working title) & Discovery Trio (minis, working title)

  • Product role: The full daily ritual made tangible (bundle) and the lowest-friction way to try the brand (minis).
  • Job-to-be-done: The bundle ($208) is the primary first-order offer and the AOV driver, auto-enrolling refill subscription. The mini trio ($32, fully credited) is the CAC-payback accelerator that converts content/SEO traffic into subscribers.

Phase 2 SKUs (summary)

  • AM Antioxidant Defense Serum (working title): THD ascorbate + ergothioneine + E/ferulic. Vitamin C is supporting cast — never the differentiator (avoid-zone respected). "Helps defend against environmental stressors / supports radiance."
  • PM Renewal Treatment (working title): encapsulated/buffered retinoid + ceramide buffer + bakuchiol. A supporting renewal step, not a new signature retinol. 4. The signature hero — Barrier Repair Cream + the ceramide + cholesterol + fatty-acid barrier complex

Why this is the brand-defining product. Ceramides are named in the research as the single most on-thesis molecule for a repair-led line — they are barrier repair. A repair-first barrier cream as the HERO (not a retinol, not a vitamin C, not a single patented molecule) is the exact merchandising white space (#8) no premium 35-55 longevity brand occupies, and it is the lowest-risk possible hero: gentle (lowest irritation/return rate), no sun-sensitivity, pregnancy-safe, and synergistic (niacinamide upregulates ceramide synthesis). It directly answers OneSkin's documented "too light for dry skin" gap with warmth, richness, and a level of brand coherence and honesty OneSkin's lab-cold brand cannot match. This logic holds regardless of the brand's eventual name, story, or core positioning.

How to make it defensible WITHOUT betting on a single patented molecule — the core synthesis decision:

  1. The moat is the brand layer + execution, not the molecule. Per the white-space map, the brand cannot and should not out-IP the biotech tier. Ceramide+cholesterol+fatty-acid systems are mature commodity actives. The moat is a coherent brand worldview + a distinctive visual direction + honesty (Tier 1 gaps), which are structurally uncopyable by fear-based incumbents once chosen and executed well.
  2. Name the formula for PR; never claim it as IP or proof. The ceramide + cholesterol + fatty-acid barrier complex is a transparently-dosed, repair-optimized (cholesterol-forward) lipid matrix fused with GHK-Cu + niacinamide + ectoin. It gives PR/merchandising ownability and a quotable signature — without pretending to a patented single molecule and without the opacity of a "secret complex." Hard internal guardrail: it is a naming/merchandising device only — never described as patented, proprietary IP, or as efficacy evidence in itself. The white-space map explicitly refuted "build a defensible signature complex" (it collapses into a well-labeled off-the-shelf blend the majors out-fund); we accept that and treat the complex strictly as a wrapper around individually-substantiated actives. Efficacy claims rest on the actives' published literature, not on the complex. This threads the needle between Proposal 1's pure white-label (no ownable signature) and an avoid-zone patent program. (The final complex name is positioning-dependent and TBD with the brand name/story.)
  3. Multi-active by design = no single-molecule concentration risk. No one ingredient carries the brand, so if any single active underdelivers or faces a sourcing shock, the cream still works and the line still holds. (This is a deliberate trade-off for this architecture — resilience over an ownable-IP story — not a claim that single-molecule models are inherently weak; the single-molecule-vs-multi-active fork is laid out in ../brand/positioning.md.)
  4. Claims must be defensible at launch on existing literature; the study is reinforcement, not the basis. Every launch claim must stand on the actives' already-published literature on day one — which it largely does for ceramides+cholesterol+fatty-acids and niacinamide (both rated "strong"). We then fund one modest in-house/CRO 12-week barrier-and-firmness study (TEWL, corneometry, image analysis) post-revenue as reinforcement of claims-safe language and to feed the Proof Library — not as the basis for claims that ship before it exists. This is the honesty standard's load-bearing requirement and the exact "transparency-becomes-liability" trap to avoid: making appearance/firmness claims on a study that does not yet exist. The study is therefore ring-fenced, non-optional funding, protected from brand/content spend (see Open Question #3).
  5. The aesthetic and brand layer do the elevating. Barrier/ceramide language risks reading "CeraVe at 3x." The defense is the named signature complex, premium airless-refill sensory, the chosen visual direction, the $118 prestige price, and the brand worldview — never sell it as bare hydration. (Any educational mechanism framing may add a halo but is never the efficacy story or the headline benefit.)

6. Build vs. buy + phased launch sequence

Build vs. buy

  • BUY (off-the-shelf / contract-manufactured, fast, low-risk): the ceramide+cholesterol+fatty-acid base, niacinamide, GHK-Cu, ectoin, HA, panthenol, and (Phase 2) THD ascorbate, ergothioneine, encapsulated retinoid, bakuchiol. All commodity-to-mid actives with reliable supply (Evonik/Croda/DSM-class). Formulate via a CM with airless/refillable capability.
  • BRAND, don't merely buy (the synthesis move): specify the ceramide + cholesterol + fatty-acid barrier complex ratio over a mature base and run one modest post-launch substantiation study. Lean cash-out and an ownable, honest signature — no multi-year patent program. (Final complex name follows the brand name/positioning decision.)
  • LICENSE, not build (Phase 3 only): the senotherapeutic "longevity peptide" — license a defensible, commercially-available senomorphic peptide with its own substantiation file (NOT OS-01; never "senolytic"). Do not fund a proprietary multi-year peptide program (out-funded by L'Oréal/EL/OneSkin; an avoid-zone).
  • BUILD in-house (the actual moat, capital-light): the chosen brand worldview, the brand IP and visual direction, the "proven vs. emerging" claims framework / Proof Library, the content/SEO acquisition engine, and the refill-subscription CX. The reusable airless vessel tooling is the one meaningful capex. (The worldview and visual direction are open decisions — but the in-house build IS the moat regardless of which is chosen.)

Phased launch sequence

  • Phase 0 (Weeks 0-6) — brand + content engine, pre-cash-heavy: Lock the brand identity, name, story, positioning, packaging/visual system, and the "proven vs. emerging" claims framework with cosmetic-claims legal review. (These are the open decisions; Phase 0 is where they get made.) Stand up the Proof Library and an editorial/SEO program ("outgrew drugstore retinol?", "results without the burn") to build a low-CAC waitlist before product. Shortlist 2-3 CMs with ceramide-barrier and GHK-Cu stock bases.
  • Phase 1 (Weeks 6-16) — HERO + the funnel: Ship the Barrier Repair Cream, Renewal Serum, and Gentle Cleanser (final names TBD), sold individually, as the Routine Set bundle, and as the Discovery Trio minis — with refill subscription live day one. Soft-launch the hero solo to the waitlist first to validate AOV, return rate, and CAC before committing inventory depth, then open the full trio + bundle.
  • Phase 2 (+3-6 mo) — complete the AM/PM day (only on early retention signal): Add the AM Antioxidant Defense Serum (AM), PM Renewal Treatment (PM), and Eye Renewal Cream (attach). Lift AOV and deepen the routine.
  • Phase 3 (+9-12 mo) — the flag + LTV extension (only once the licensed peptide and substantiation file are locked): Launch the Longevity Peptide Apex Serum as the PR/SEO halo and highest-CM3 trade-up, and the unisex Body Cream. Expand refill SKUs.
  • Phase 4 (later/harder) — defer indefinitely until scale: A finished broad-spectrum SPF only after the OTC-drug pathway (monograph, SPF testing) is resourced — deliberately last; US filters are limited and the path is costlier.

7. Claims & regulatory guardrails

  • Sunscreen is an OTC DRUG in the US (monograph, SPF testing, costlier path; modern EU filters like Tinosorb are unavailable here). A finished SPF SKU is a deliberate Phase 4 play, not launch. No SPF claims on any launch product.
  • Azelaic acid: its strong evidence is at drug-strength 15-20%; cosmetic-strength cannot borrow those claims. Same discipline applies to retinoid strength — keep Phase 2 retinoid claims cosmetic.
  • Never "senolytic" / "clears zombie cells" / "reverses biological age" / "reduces biological age by X years." Any renewal/longevity narrative is told via mechanism only, never as a headline benefit. Of the peptides, GHK-Cu carries the more-substantiated (but still emerging, small-study) renewal load; any licensed Phase 3 peptide is story-only/mechanism copy. Efficacy claims at launch rest on the strong-evidence ceramide barrier system + niacinamide.
  • No self-reported survey-stat marketing ("96% agreed," "up to 84%," "reverse aging"). Claims are concentration-level and labeled proven vs. emerging.
  • Honor cosmetic-vs-drug language throughout. Prefer "supports / helps the appearance of" over "boosts / treats / rebuilds." "Barrier repair" stays tied to support/appearance, never disease (no "treats eczema"). Cite eczema/clinical data internally for credibility, never in consumer claims.
  • Claims matrix BEFORE any copy (the load-bearing gate). Before a single consumer-facing line is written, produce the actual claims matrix: every proposed phrase mapped to (a) its substantiating source and (b) a proven vs. emerging label, then run the whole matrix through cosmetic-claims counsel. No copy ships until counsel signs the matrix. Specifically:
  • NAD+ / sirtuin / cellular-energy = education/mechanism only, never a benefit or efficacy claim. Topical niacinamide raising skin NAD+ is unproven; "recharges NAD+," "boosts cellular energy," "activates sirtuins" are prohibited. This is the single most likely place marketing drifts into an unsubstantiated structure/function claim — gate it hardest.
  • GHK-Cu firmness/density language must be defensible on the existing small-study literature and must never quote the 28% collagen figure in consumer copy (internal-only).
  • Ceramide "barrier" claims stay tied to appearance/support, never disease; eczema/clinical RCT endpoints are internal-only and must not surface in the Proof Library or any consumer-facing proof.
  • Launch claims are defensible at launch on published literature; the study is reinforcement. Ceramides+cholesterol+fatty-acids and niacinamide are "strong"-rated and carry the launch claims today. The 12-week study reinforces — it is never the basis for a claim that ships before it exists.
  • Substantiation is load-bearing and ring-fenced: radical honesty flips from differentiator to liability if any formula underdelivers — every claim passes the hard legal/regulatory gate above, and the hero gets a real (if modest) study funded as non-optional, protected from brand/content spend.
  • Formulation watch-outs: GHK-Cu copper-blue color and the GHK-Cu / direct vitamin-C separation (formulate or time apart); keep peptides away from strong acids/oxidants.

8. Business model

  • Gross margin — plan on ~62%, treat 70-75% as upside. The market research pegs realistic private/indie GM at ~62% (≈7 pts below public-brand benchmarks, due to scale); 70-75% is the optimistic public-comp ceiling, not the planning case. Re-base the model on 62% and re-run CM3 and the CAC ceiling against it, after a realistic returns reserve (8-12%) and glass/hazmat shipping leakage are subtracted. The licensed Phase 3 peptide compresses that SKU's GM but carries the highest absolute CM3, so it is dollar-accretive.
  • Survival metric = CM3 after CAC, not vanity GM. Target CM3 ~25-35%, early sustainable CAC ~$35-55 — but these are category benchmarks, not a brand-specific model. Before scaling paid acquisition, build a bottom-up unit-economics model: per-SKU COGS from CM quotes, blended AOV at the realistic bundle/mini mix (the $32 fully-credited mini trio and $34 cleanser modeled at their true contribution, not assumed), realistic repeat rate, and refill-LTV contribution. Confirm CAC payback on that model — not the borrowed benchmark — before scaling spend. Pre-commit the refill-LTV compensation plan in case content/SEO CAC misses (Open Question #6).
  • AOV: Center the Routine Set bundle ($208 vs ~$240 a la carte) as the headline first-order offer; eye cream (Phase 2) is the margin-accretive attach. Push order value above the CAC ceiling.
  • Subscription / refill LTV: Live day one. Refills priced ~20% below first-fill, a further ~15% on subscription (mirrors OneSkin's Subscribe & Save but on refill pods that also cut COGS by reusing the vessel). The daily cream + cleanser are the recurring annuity (+3-8 pts CM3); aim for the majority of mature revenue subscription-borne. Refillable packaging is the one intentional capex and the LTV flywheel — and it doubles as a "repair, don't discard" proof point for any positioning that values sustainability.
  • CAC payback / acquisition engine: The pre-built content/SEO Proof Library + the $32 fully-credited mini trio + the $34 cleanser are the low-CAC front doors. The line is the trade-up ladder converting mass-tier value graduates. Retention is the make-or-break metric — if cheap content/SEO doesn't scale, refill LTV must compensate.
  • Channel: DTC-first on Shopify (margin + brand control). Selective Sephora/Ulta discovery later for top-of-funnel, accepting margin erosion, rather than over-relying on rising paid DTC. Engineer airless packaging for shipping from day one (protects actives and reduces glass-breakage margin leak).
  • Pricing transparency is itself a conversion asset given only ~14% equate higher price with quality.
