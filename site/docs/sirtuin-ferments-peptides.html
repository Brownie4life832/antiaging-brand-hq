Ingredient: Yeast/biotech sirtuin-inducing ferments & SIRT-activating peptides (Kluyveromyces biopeptides · SIRT6-activating peptides · Surfactin)

Researched: 2026-06-19 · Status: ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product Slug: sirtuin-ferments-peptides · Class: NAD+ / sirtuin / AMPK / cellular-energy actives

Reading note. This is not one ingredient — it is a category of biotech-derived actives sold under a single marketing story: "switch on your skin's longevity enzymes (sirtuins)." The brief bundles three distinct material types: (a) yeast ferment biopeptides (the Kluyveromyces / Dior SIRT1 work), (b) synthetic SIRT6-activating peptides (a patent-defined heptapeptide), and (c) Surfactin (a Bacillus lipopeptide that is not a sirtuin activator at all — it rides along on the "biotech ferment" halo). They share a biological thesis (NAD+/sirtuin axis → cellular longevity) but have very different, and mostly very thin, human topical evidence. This dossier keeps a hard line between the story and the proof, and is brutally honest where the two diverge.

Cross-reference. The underlying NAD+/sirtuin biochemistry is covered in depth in nad-niacinamide-longevity.md and overlaps with the spermidine (autophagy) file spermidine.md. Read those alongside this one; this file focuses on the ferment/peptide/biosurfactant delivery formats of the same longevity thesis.

0. The honest punchline (read this first)

  • Sirtuins are real and matter in skin. SIRT1, SIRT3 and SIRT6 are NAD+-dependent enzymes that decline with age and govern collagen turnover, DNA repair, mitochondrial energy, antioxidant defense and senescence. The biology is well-supported (multiple peer-reviewed reviews). The question is never "are sirtuins real?" — it is "does this cosmetic ingredient meaningfully change sirtuin activity in living human skin and produce a visible benefit?"
  • The strongest single data point in this whole category is the Kluyveromyces biopeptide work from LVMH/Dior (Moreau/Dumas 2007 [1]): +172% SIRT1 in fibroblasts in vitro, reduced UVB senescence/DNA damage, plus a 33-subject 4-week clinical showing appearance improvements. But it is a single, manufacturer-run, uncontrolled (no placebo arm) clinical from ~19 years ago, never independently replicated. That is a weak foundation for a hero claim.
  • SIRT6-activating peptides (e.g. the heptapeptide Gly-Ala-Gly-Val-Ser-Ala-Glu-NH₂) exist only in patent literature [4][5]. Patents are not peer-reviewed, report in-vitro/ex-vivo data selected by the applicant, and contain no independent human RCT. Treat as mechanism-plausible, efficacy-unproven.
  • Surfactin is the odd one out: a Bacillus lipopeptide biosurfactant with genuine anti-inflammatory (NF-κB) and wound-healing data (mostly in vitro + rodent), but it is not a sirtuin activator, and it is hemolytic / membrane-disrupting — a real safety flag at higher concentrations. Its best honest use is as a mild bio-based surfactant/penetration aid, not a "longevity" active.
  • The best-evidenced "sirtuin activator" for human skin is not in this file's headline at all — it is trans-resveratrol, and even there the recent gold-standard RCT (Rao 2025 [7]) found the topical-only arm did NOT significantly reduce wrinkles; only the combined oral+topical arm did. That is the cleanest illustration of the category's core problem: strong mechanism, soft topical proof.
  • Net for the brand: this is a story-rich, proof-poor category. There is a legitimate, honest "supports skin's cellular energy / longevity pathways" narrative — but the substantiation for any single sirtuin-ferment/peptide ingredient is in-vitro-dominant with one or two small manufacturer clinicals. Supporting/conditional, never hero, and never on its own. If we want a defensible "longevity" claim, anchor it on niacinamide (proven, and biochemically an NAD+ precursor) and let a fermented sirtuin active play a narrative/secondary role.

2. Class & mechanism

The sirtuin thesis (well-supported biology): - Sirtuins (SIRT1–7) are NAD+-dependent deacylases. They consume NAD+, linking them to the cell's energy/redox state. In skin they regulate collagen/ECM, DNA repair, mitochondrial bioenergetics, antioxidant enzymes, autophagy and senescence (Bielach-Bazyluk 2021 systematic review [2]; Su & Hsu, Mitochondrial Sirtuins in Skin 2020 [3]). - SIRT1 (nuclear/cytoplasmic): suppresses MMP-1/-3/-9 (collagen-degrading enzymes), enhances DNA repair (XPA deacetylation), reduces p53-driven apoptosis, drives FOXO3α→SOD2 antioxidant defense. SIRT1 declines with age, peaks in fetal skin, drops sharply after ~40 (review [2]). - SIRT3 (mitochondrial): the major mitochondrial NAD+ deacetylase; deacetylates/activates SOD2, protects against UV-induced senescence via AMPK/autophagy (review [3]). - SIRT6 (nuclear, telomere-associated): chromatin/telomere maintenance, base-excision DNA repair, genome stability; SIRT6 overexpression alleviated UVA photoaging in fibroblasts via NRF2/HO-1 and NF-κB modulation (Zhang et al., J Photochem Photobiol B 2023 [8]). - The intervention logic: an ingredient that (i) raises NAD+ (precursor route — see niacinamide/NMN file) and/or (ii) increases sirtuin expression/activity should push cells toward a "younger" phenotype: more ECM, fewer senescent cells, better repair, more mitochondrial ATP.

Where each ingredient claims to act: - Kluyveromyces biopeptides → ↑SIRT1 expression in fibroblasts/keratinocytes; the proposed downstream effect is reduced senescence (↓SA-β-gal) and DNA protection [1]. - SIRT6 peptide → ↑SIRT6 expression; claimed downstream: DNA-repair, TRF2/telomere maintenance, ↑ECM proteins, barrier optimization [4][5]. - Cordyceps extract → ↑NAD+ synthesis (+20%) and ↑SIRT1/3/6 expression, ↑ATP, ↓ROS, ↑collagen in vitro [6]. - Surfactin → NOT a sirtuin route. Anti-inflammatory via NF-κB / IKK-IκBα / MAPK / TLR4 inhibition in macrophages (Zhang/Gan 2015 [10]); wound-healing via HIF-1α/VEGF and keratinocyte migration (Yang 2020 [9]); modest direct radical scavenging (and notably no DPPH activity for pure surfactin in Dussert 2022 [11]).

The honest mechanistic caveat (carry this everywhere): "increases SIRT1 expression in cultured cells" is not the same as "raises sirtuin activity in living human skin" or "produces a visible anti-aging benefit." As the NAD+ literature states (Boo 2021, cited in [companion file]), it is not established that topical actives meaningfully raise skin NAD+/sirtuin activity in vivo, and no specific molecular target has been confirmed for many of these effects. The chain mechanism → expression → activity → clinical benefit is broken at the "clinical benefit, controlled, independent" link for every ingredient in this file.

4. Evidence review by tier

Format key per claim: in-vitro (cell/biochemical) → animal → human ORAL → human TOPICAL (RCT > controlled > uncontrolled/open). I flag which format each finding belongs to, because the marketing collapses them.

Tier 1 — In-vitro (cell & biochemical) — the bulk of the evidence

  1. Kluyveromyces biopeptides ↑SIRT1. Moreau, Dumas et al. 2007 [1] (LVMH/Dior): normal human dermal fibroblasts treated with yeast Kluyveromyces biopeptides showed +172% SIRT1 expression (immunostaining/Western/cytometry), with reduced UVB-induced senescence (β-galactosidase) and reduced DNA fragmentation (comet assay). Limitation: manufacturer lab; single source; SIRT1 expression, not demonstrated activity.
  2. SIRT6 peptide ↑SIRT6. Patent US20110318284A1 / US8569243 / EP2588074B1 [4][5]: heptapeptide Gly-Ala-Gly-Val-Ser-Ala-Glu-NH₂ "increased SIRT6 expression very significantly" in human fibroblasts and keratinocytes; claimed ↓UVB DNA degradation, ↑TRF2 (telomere protein), ↑ECM proteins, barrier optimization. Limitation: patent, not peer-reviewed; applicant-selected data; no independent replication; no RCT.
  3. Cordyceps sinensis extract — NAD+/sirtuin. Di Lorenzo, Laneri et al. 2024, Int J Mol Sci [6]: at 0.0006% in HaCaT keratinocytes, NAD+ +20%, SIRT1 +33% / SIRT3 +10% / SIRT6 +72%, ATP +68%, ROS −30%, collagen +69% (all p<0.05–0.001). Limitation: one academic-industry group (Univ. Naples + ingredient developer); single concentration; HaCaT (immortalized) line.
  4. SIRT6 overexpression vs UVA photoaging. Zhang et al. 2023, J Photochem Photobiol B [8]: forced SIRT6 overexpression in human dermal fibroblasts reduced UVA-induced photoaging via NRF2/HO-1 ↑ and NF-κB ↓, with ↓MMPs / preserved collagen. Crucial limitation: this is genetic overexpression, not a topical ingredient — proves sirtuin biology, not that any cream achieves it.
  5. Surfactin anti-inflammatory. Zhang/Gan et al. 2015, Inflammation [10]: in LPS-activated macrophages surfactin inhibited NO/iNOS, IL-6, IFN-γ, and blocked IKK/IκBα phosphorylation and NF-κB(p65) translocation; downregulated TLR4. Limitation: macrophage model, not skin; relevance to topical inflammaging is inferential.
  6. Surfactin antioxidant — WEAKER than marketed. Dussert et al. 2022, Front Microbiol [11]: in acellular assays, purified surfactin showed essentially no DPPH radical-scavenging (DPPH activity at 500 mg/L was carried by plipastatin, max ≈22.9%, not surfactin) and only modest ROS scavenging (≈6% O₂•⁻, 21% H₂O₂, 3% HO•). Plipastatin (a different Bacillus lipopeptide) carried most antioxidant activity. This directly contradicts the common "surfactin is a strong antioxidant" claim.
  7. Sirtuin biology in skin cells (context). Reviews [2][3] and supporting primaries: resveratrol activates SIRT1/AMPK-FOXO3 to prevent oxidative senescence in primary keratinocytes; caffeine targets SIRT3→SOD2; SIRT1/3/6 activators in NHEK/HDF increase fibrillin, mitophagy/autophagy, mitochondrial ATP and reduce NF-κB inflammation (mechanistic basis cited across [2][3]).

Tier 2 — Animal (in vivo)

  1. Surfactin A wound healing (mouse). Yang et al. 2020, J Agric Food Chem [9]: in a mouse excisional wound model, surfactin A–treated wounds reached 80.65% closure by day 7 vs 44.30% for vehicle; upregulated HIF-1α and VEGF, accelerated keratinocyte migration (MAPK/NF-κB), modulated cytokines and macrophage polarization, and inhibited scar formation. Strong for surfactin's wound/regenerative angle; not an anti-aging cosmetic endpoint.
  2. Ex-vivo human skin (Kluyveromyces). Within [1], biopeptides also raised SIRT1 in epidermal cells of healthy human skin explants ex vivo — bridging cell culture to tissue, but still not in-life. - Gap: there are no published controlled animal anti-aging (wrinkle/photoaging) studies for the Kluyveromyces biopeptide or SIRT6 peptide specifically as topical agents.

Tier 3 — Human ORAL

  1. Trans-resveratrol oral (sirtuin activator, context). Rao et al. 2025, Front Aging [7] (4-arm RCT, n=134): oral 75 mg trans-resveratrol arm did not reach significance for wrinkles vs placebo on its own. Other oral resveratrol/SIRT1 supplement trials show biomarker (H3K56ac/oxidative stress) shifts but inconsistent skin-appearance benefit (review context [2]). Oral NAD+/sirtuin support is plausible but not a clean win for skin appearance. - Note: the headline ferments/peptides in this file are topical; there is no meaningful human oral dataset for Kluyveromyces biopeptide or the SIRT6 peptide.

Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL (the tier that actually matters for a cream)

This is where the category is weakest. There is no independent, placebo-controlled topical RCT for any of the three headline ingredients.

  1. Kluyveromyces biopeptide — open clinical (NOT an RCT). Moreau/Dumas 2007 [1]: 33 women, age 37–64 (mean 51.6), 1% biopeptide formula once daily for 4 weeks. Reported improvements in fine lines, wrinkles, hydration (significant after first application), pigmentation, radiance, firmness, texture; objective imaging showed −4.2% gray-variance, −30.4% surface imperfections. Limitations: no placebo/control arm, small n, short duration, manufacturer-run, not independently replicated in ~19 years. This is the single most-cited "proof" for the category and it is methodologically weak.
  2. Cordyceps extract — double-blind, placebo-controlled clinical. Di Lorenzo/Laneri 2024 [6]: n=40, 28 days, double-blind, placebo-controlled; reported wrinkles −28.0%, skin "energy" +52.0%, collagen +10% (ultrasound) (p<0.01–0.001). Better design than [1] (it has a placebo arm), but small, short, single industry-linked group, single ingredient concentration, no independent replication. The strongest controlled topical signal in the "sirtuin" family — but for Cordyceps, not the headline Kluyveromyces/SIRT6 peptide.
  3. Sirtuin-targeting regimen (Estée Lauder) — uncontrolled. Bruning, Lain, Ablon et al. 2025, J Clin Aesthet Dermatol [13]: n=72, 12 weeks, single-center, assessor-blinded, monadic (no control arm); "Predicted Age" fell ~4 years, 7 facial parameters improved. Limitations: no control, single grader, no biomarker validation, demographic skew, Estée Lauder–funded (3 employee authors, 7 honoraria); ingredients not disclosed ("patented, internal-study sirtuin actives"). Marketing-grade, not efficacy-grade.
  4. Trans-resveratrol topical RCT — the cautionary benchmark. Rao et al. 2025, Front Aging [7]: rigorous 4-arm, n=134, 8-week RCT (placebo, oral 75 mg, topical 1.5% cream, combined). Only the combined oral+topical arm significantly reduced wrinkles; the topical-only arm did NOT. Topical-only did significantly reduce pigmentation (p<0.001) and altered sebum. Funded by Evolva (the active's developer), with employee-author conflicts. Read this as the honest ceiling: even the best-studied sirtuin activator does not reliably move wrinkles from a topical alone over 8 weeks.

Bottom line on TOPICAL human proof: one uncontrolled open study (Kluyveromyces [1]), one small placebo-controlled study on a different sirtuin active (Cordyceps [6]), one uncontrolled regimen study (Estée Lauder [13]), and the resveratrol RCT [7] showing topical-only fails on wrinkles. No independent RCT exists for the named headline ingredients. Everything stronger is in-vitro.

5. Topical bioavailability

  • Peptides (Kluyveromyces biopeptides, SIRT6 heptapeptide): small peptides can have limited stratum-corneum penetration; charged/polar peptides need delivery help (encapsulation, penetration enhancers, optimized vehicle). The Dior work used a 1% biopeptide formulation — efficacy is formulation-dependent and the active fraction reaching living cells in vivo is not quantified in [1].
  • The NAD+/sirtuin delivery problem (shared with the NMN file): NAD+ itself is large, charged and unstable and penetrates poorly; precursors (niacinamide) penetrate well but it is not established that topical application raises intracellular NAD+/sirtuin activity in living human skin (see nad-niacinamide-longevity.md). A ferment/peptide claiming to "activate sirtuins" inherits this unproven last step.
  • Surfactin: as a biosurfactant it is itself a penetration enhancer and forms transparent gels across a wide concentration range — good for delivery/sensory, but the same membrane activity that aids penetration is what makes it potentially irritating/hemolytic. It can act as a carrier for other actives.
  • Ferments generally: ferment filtrates are complex mixtures (peptides, amino acids, organic acids, polysaccharides, vitamins); "bioavailability" is ill-defined because the active entity is often unknown — a substantiation and reproducibility weakness.

6. Formulation notes

Parameter Guidance
Kluyveromyces biopeptide Studied at ~1% in finished formula [1]. Water-phase; ferments are typically pH ~4.5–6.5; heat-sensitive — add in cool-down. Preservation is critical (nutrient-rich ferments support microbial growth).
SIRT6 peptide Use at supplier-recommended low % (peptides typically 0.5–5% of a supplier solution, i.e. ppm-level actual peptide). Water-phase, cool-down, mildly acidic-to-neutral pH. Avoid strong oxidizers/high heat.
Surfactin (Sodium Surfactin) Patent ranges 0.001–90%; practical/safer cosmetic range ~0.01–2.0%. As surfactant/penetration aid keep LOW. Excellent foaming/emulsifying/solubilizing; stable over wide pH; can replace harsher synthetic surfactants. Watch hemolytic/irritation risk above low %.
Cordyceps extract (context) Effective in vitro at 0.0006%; finished-product % per supplier.
Pairings (rational) Pair the "sirtuin" story with the proven NAD+ precursor niacinamide (honest longevity anchor); with antioxidants (vit C/E, ferulic) to support the redox/NRF2 angle; with barrier lipids/ceramides (the brand's core thesis); with peptides/HA for plump/firm appearance. Resveratrol pairs conceptually but is unstable (isomerizes, poorly water-soluble) — encapsulate.
Conflicts Avoid high-acid (low-pH AHA/BHA) co-formulation that can hydrolyze/destabilize peptides and ferments; avoid strong oxidizers. Surfactin's surfactant activity can disrupt delicate emulsions if over-dosed.
Sensory Ferments/peptides are largely odor-neutral to faintly "fermented"; may need masking. Surfactin gives slip/foam and a clean rinse feel.

7. Safety (irritation, sun, pregnancy, sensitization, sourcing)

  • Kluyveromyces biopeptides / SIRT peptides: generally well-tolerated in the cited studies; no notable irritation reported in the small clinicals. Peptides are low-allergenicity in general but any protein-derived/ferment material carries a sensitization possibility; patch-test and review allergen profile of the specific raw material. Ferment filtrates can vary batch-to-batch.
  • Surfactin — the real safety flag: surfactin is hemolytic and membrane-pore-forming (its mechanism as a biosurfactant). At higher concentrations this implies cytotoxic/irritant potential and barrier disruption; this is precisely the opposite of a barrier-repair goal. Keep concentrations low (≤~2%, often <1%), and treat it as a functional surfactant rather than a leave-on "active." Self-affirmed GRAS-type uses exist in food/cosmetics at low levels, but do dedicated irritation testing.
  • Sun: none of these are documented photosensitizers; SIRT6/SIRT1 actives are positioned as photo-protective (cell data). No UV claims without SPF — see claims section.
  • Pregnancy: no specific contraindication established, but also no safety data specific to pregnancy for these biotech actives. Default to "consult a professional" language; do not market to pregnancy.
  • Sourcing/biotech: fermentation-derived (yeast/Bacillus) — generally sustainable, vegan-compatible (note Kluyveromyces is a dairy yeast — confirm whether the specific raw material is dairy-derived for vegan claims), animal-test-free positioning available. Batch consistency and endotoxin control (for Bacillus-derived materials) are real QC considerations.

8. Regulatory & claims (cosmetic vs drug — per CLAUDE.md)

See ../../brand/glossary.md claim cheat-sheet and the CLAUDE.md claims guardrail. This category is a high claims-risk zone because the mechanism language ("activates longevity enzymes," "repairs DNA," "switches on sirtuins") slides toward structure/function / drug territory.

  • ❌ Avoid / drug-like (would need substantiation or cross into drug): "activates your longevity genes," "repairs DNA," "reverses cellular aging," "boosts SIRT1 by 172%" (that figure is in-vitro, not the finished product on people), "increases collagen by X%," "regenerates skin," "anti-senescence treatment," any "telomere" repair claim.
  • ⚠️ Risky without strong substantiation: "raises NAD+ in your skin," "activates sirtuins" (not demonstrated in vivo for the finished product), "energizes cells."
  • ✅ Safer cosmetic framing: "supports the skin's natural cellular-energy and renewal pathways," "biotech ferment inspired by skin-longevity science," "helps improve the appearance of fine lines and uneven tone," "visibly more rested, radiant skin," "supports a healthy-looking, resilient barrier."
  • Substantiation reality: in-vitro %s and patent claims are not adequate substantiation for finished-product efficacy claims in major markets (FTC/US, EU CPR/efficacy-claims regulation, UK ASA). The only on-product clinical-grade data here are small, mostly manufacturer-run. Any appearance claim must rest on our own finished-product consumer/clinical testing, not borrowed in-vitro/patent data. Flag every "sirtuin/NAD/longevity" line for legal review.

9. Sourcing & cost

  • Cost tier: mid-to-premium. SIRT-activating peptides and branded ferments (Orsirtine, Cordyceps-based actives, branded SIRT6 peptides) are specialty, supplier-gated, and carry IP premiums and minimums. Generic ferment filtrates and Surfactin are cheaper; branded "sirtuin" peptides are the expensive end.
  • Suppliers (illustrative): peptide/ferment specialty houses (e.g., Vincience/Ashland for Orsirtine; Lipotec/Lubrizol and Provital-type peptide ranges; Bacillus-derived Sodium Surfactin from several biosurfactant producers e.g. Kaneka/Agae-type and Asian biosurfactant suppliers; the Cordyceps NAD+ active from the Naples-linked developer). Verify exact INCI, actives %, clinical dossier and IP status per supplier before any claim.
  • Supply considerations: ferment batch variability; Bacillus endotoxin control; dairy-origin disclosure for Kluyveromyces (vegan claims); IP/license terms that may restrict claim language to the supplier's substantiation.

10. Hype vs evidence

Marketing claim Reality
"Switches on your skin's longevity enzymes (sirtuins)" Sirtuins are real and decline with age (true biology). That a topical finished product raises sirtuin activity in living human skin is not demonstrated — only ↑expression in cultured cells / patent data.
"+172% SIRT1" / "SIRT6 +72%" Real numbers — but in-vitro, single cells, single labs (Kluyveromyces [1], Cordyceps [6]). Not finished-product, not on people.
"Repairs DNA / protects telomeres" Based on SIRT6 patent claims and overexpression biology [4][8]. No peer-reviewed human topical evidence. Drug-adjacent claim — avoid.
"Surfactin is a powerful antioxidant" Contradicted: pure surfactin showed no DPPH activity [11]. Its real strengths are surfactancy, anti-inflammatory NF-κB inhibition [10], and wound healing [9].
"Clinically proven to reverse skin age" The "−4 years predicted age" study [13] is uncontrolled, single-grader, sponsor-funded, photo-grading only. Not proof of reversal.
"Fermented = better/more bioavailable" Ferments are complex, often with an unidentified active; "bioavailability" is largely unquantified. Convenient story, weak mechanism specificity.
"Longevity skincare" A genuine, on-trend narrative — but for this category it is mechanism-led, not outcome-proven. The honest longevity anchor is niacinamide (proven) + SPF, with ferments/peptides as narrative support.

The single most honest sentence: the biology of sirtuins in skin aging is solid; the evidence that any specific ferment/peptide cosmetic delivers a sirtuin-mediated visible benefit in controlled, independent human studies is essentially absent. This is a story category with in-vitro scaffolding.

11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL — never hero, never standalone.

  • Why not skip: "skin longevity / cellular energy" is exactly the high-end narrative space this brand can credibly play in, and the underlying sirtuin/NAD+ biology is legitimate and citable. A well-chosen biotech ferment or SIRT peptide can be a premium, modern, science-forward storytelling ingredient and a point of differentiation — fitting "buy commodity bases, invest in the brand" by being a distinctive (if costly) active.
  • Why not hero: the human topical, controlled, independent evidence is missing. Building a flagship efficacy claim on +172% in-vitro SIRT1 or a patent would be exactly the kind of hype this brand's credibility thesis is built to avoid, and a claims-discipline/legal liability.
  • Conditional recommendation: 1. If used, position it as a secondary "longevity-support" active alongside the brand's proven core (ceramides/barrier, niacinamide as the honest NAD+ anchor, peptides, antioxidants, SPF). 2. Prefer an active that comes with at least a placebo-controlled finished-product clinical (Cordyceps-type [6] currently looks better-supported than the headline Kluyveromyces/SIRT6 peptide) — and ideally commission our own consumer/clinical test before any appearance claim. 3. Surfactin: consider only as a mild bio-based surfactant / penetration aid at ≤~1–2%, never as a "longevity active," and respect its hemolytic/irritation profile (counter to a barrier-repair brand if overdosed). 4. Keep all copy in safe cosmetic register ("supports the skin's natural renewal/energy pathways," "inspired by skin-longevity science"); route every sirtuin/NAD/DNA line through legal.
  • Longevity note: this is the most "longevity-on-trend" ingredient family in the lineup and pairs naturally with the broader NAD+/autophagy story (see nad-niacinamide-longevity.md, spermidine.md). Its value to the brand is narrative and differentiation today, with real efficacy still to be proven — invest in the story, but substantiate before you sell the science.

Evidence verification (adversarial)

Verified: 2026-06-19 (independent citation + claim audit). Overall grade: D — unproven topically / story-led. For the named headline ingredients (Kluyveromyces biopeptides, SIRT6-activating peptides, Surfactin), there is no independent, placebo-controlled human topical RCT demonstrating a sirtuin-mediated anti-aging benefit. The biology is real (grade A as biology); the finished-product topical efficacy is not (grade D as proof of cosmetic claim). The dossier's own self-assessment ("story-rich, proof-poor … in-vitro scaffolding") is accurate and is the correct grade.

What the audit confirmed (citations are real and fairly characterized)

Every numbered source [1]–[13] was checked against the primary record. All are real, and the dossier's descriptions match the studies — including the load-bearing skeptical claims: - [1] Kluyveromyces / Dior (Moreau, Dumas 2007, J Drugs Dermatol, PMID 17691205). Real. LVMH/Dior authorship confirmed. The +172% SIRT1 figure is in-vitro; the human data is a 33-subject open (uncontrolled) clinical. Dossier characterization is correct. Manufacturer-run, ~19 years old, never independently replicated — a weak hero foundation. - [6] Cordyceps (Di Lorenzo, Laneri 2024, Int J Mol Sci 25:4282). Real. In-vitro SIRT1/3/6 +33/10/72%, NAD+ +20%, ATP +68% confirmed; clinical is double-blind, placebo-controlled, n=40, 28 days (wrinkles −28%, energy +52%). Better design than [1] but single industry-linked group, tiny, short, one concentration, unreplicated. The "skin energy +52%" is a device readout, correctly flagged. - [7] Resveratrol RCT (Rao 2025, Front Aging). Real. 4-arm, n=134 enrolled (122 completed). Confirmed: only the combined oral+topical arm significantly reduced wrinkles; topical-only did NOT. Evolva/industry-funded. The dossier's use of this as the "honest ceiling" is fair and important. - [11] Surfactin antioxidant (Dussert 2022, Front Microbiol). Real. Confirmed: surfactin essentially no DPPH activity (DPPH was carried by plipastatin, ≈22.9% max); surfactin ROS scavenging ≈6/21/3%. The "surfactin is a strong antioxidant" claim is genuinely contradicted, as stated. - [4][5] SIRT6 peptide patents (US8569243 / US20110318284A1 / EP2588074B1). Real. Peptide Gly-Ala-Gly-Val-Ser-Ala-Glu-NH₂ = SEQ ID NO:5 confirmed. Patent only — not peer-reviewed, applicant-selected in-vitro/ex-vivo data, no RCT. Correctly downgraded to "mechanism-plausible, efficacy-unproven." - [8] SIRT6/UVA photoaging (2023, J Photochem Photobiol B 249:112801). Real, but genetic overexpression, not a topical ingredient — correctly flagged. Citation correction made: lead author is Wang T, not "Zhang X" (Zhang X is corresponding/last author); fixed in source list. - [13] Estée Lauder regimen (Bruning, Lain, Ablon 2025, JCAD 18(12):34–41). Real. n=72, 12 weeks, no control arm, photo-grading only, Estée Lauder–funded, ingredients undisclosed. Marketing-grade, as stated. - [2] Bielach-Bazyluk 2021, Cells (SIRT1 systematic review, 46 studies) and [3] mitochondrial sirtuins review — real, correctly used as biology support only.

Evidence character (the core finding)

  • Human + topical + controlled + independent = essentially zero for the three headline ingredients. The strongest controlled topical signal in the whole "sirtuin" family is for Cordyceps [6] — a different ingredient — and even that is one small, short, industry-linked trial.
  • The bulk of the evidence is in-vitro (cultured cells, single labs) and patent data. Animal data exists only for Surfactin wound healing [9] (mouse) — not an anti-aging endpoint.
  • Industry funding / small-n is pervasive, not incidental: [1] LVMH/Dior; [6] ingredient-developer-linked; [7] Evolva; [13] Estée Lauder. Largest human n in the entire file is 134 ([7], resveratrol — and the relevant arm there failed).
  • Surfactin is mis-bundled into the "sirtuin/longevity" story — it is not a sirtuin activator, and it is hemolytic/membrane-disrupting (a barrier-repair liability if overdosed). The dossier already isolates it correctly.

Main caveats (do not let these get lost)

  1. No claim of in-vivo sirtuin activation in human skin is substantiated. "↑SIRT1 expression in cultured cells" ≠ "raises sirtuin activity in living skin" ≠ "visible benefit." The chain breaks at the controlled-independent-human link for every ingredient.
  2. In-vitro percentages (+172% SIRT1, +72% SIRT6, etc.) are not finished-product claims and must never be presented as on-product/on-people results.
  3. Patent data ([4][5]) is not evidence of efficacy. Treat as IP/positioning, not substantiation.
  4. Even the best-studied sirtuin activator (topical resveratrol) failed on wrinkles alone [7]. This is the realistic ceiling for the category.

Claims to soften / already softened

The dossier was already defensible on its claim language (Section 8 and Section 10 are appropriately conservative; the "honest punchline" pre-empts most overstatement). Audit edits made: (a) tightened the Surfactin/DPPH wording in [11] to match the data (plipastatin, not surfactin, carried DPPH activity); (b) corrected the [8] author attribution. No fabricated or misattributed efficacy claims were found. The one phrasing to keep watching is any internal shorthand that Cordyceps is "better-supported" — true relative to the headline ingredients, but still a single small industry trial; the dossier already hedges this and the hedge should remain.

Net: grade D for topical cosmetic proof of the named ingredients (A for the underlying sirtuin biology). Use only as a secondary, narrative "longevity-support" active, anchored on proven niacinamide + SPF, and substantiate with the brand's own finished-product testing before any appearance or sirtuin/NAD/DNA claim. This matches the dossier's existing "SUPPORTING / CONDITIONAL — never hero, never standalone" verdict.
