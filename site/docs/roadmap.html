Strategy

Launch with a tight hero range rather than a sprawling line — a lean routine beats SKU sprawl, and a focused launch is cheaper to formulate, easier to merchandise, and clearer to communicate. A classic lean anti-aging routine is cleanse → treat (serum) → moisturize → protect (SPF).

The evidence points to a premium barrier-repair cream as the hero: barrier repair is well-supported by the research, and the competitive analysis shows a real gap there (the category is crowded on actives-led serums but thinner on a genuinely premium, claims-disciplined barrier product). Anchoring the range on the hero keeps the line lean and lets every other SKU support it.

The final naming convention depends on the brand name, story, and positioning — all still open. Brainstorm in ../ideas/parking-lot.md; record conventions in ../brand/glossary.md once decided.

Build vs. buy

Decide early — drives everything downstream. The default logic from the research and economics: buy commodity bases, build the brand — use proven off-the-shelf formulations where they're as good as anything custom, and invest differentiation into brand, claims discipline, and the few formulations that justify custom work (the hero).

  • Private label / white label — fastest, cheapest, least differentiated. Sensible for commodity steps (cleanser, SPF).
  • Custom formulation via contract manufacturer — slower/pricier, ownable; reserve for the hero where it earns the premium.
