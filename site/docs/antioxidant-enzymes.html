Ingredient: Antioxidant Enzymes (Superoxide Dismutase, Catalase, Glutathione Peroxidase, EUK-134)

Researched: 2026-06-19 · Status: ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product Class: Antioxidants (enzymatic) · Slug: antioxidant-enzymes

TL;DR for a busy reader. This is the body's own first line of antioxidant defense — the enzymes that detoxify reactive oxygen species (ROS): SOD turns superoxide into hydrogen peroxide; catalase and glutathione peroxidase (GPx) then clear that hydrogen peroxide. The biology is unimpeachable and central to oxidative-stress aging. The topical-cosmetic evidence, however, is thin and largely indirect. Real human topical data exist almost entirely for a disease use — radiation-induced fibrosis — and that evidence is mostly uncontrolled case series; the one randomized controlled topical trial (Landeen 2018, head & neck) was negative. There is no credible vehicle-controlled human topical RCT showing SOD/catalase/GPx visibly reduce wrinkles, improve elasticity, or reverse photoaging. The best-evidenced member for skincare is actually the synthetic small-molecule mimetic EUK-134 (a salen-manganese complex with SOD + catalase activity), which is stable, penetrates, and has one human skin-surface study plus solid in-vitro UVB data — but even there the clinical-appearance endpoint is unproven. Layered on top is a brutal longevity-science fact: overexpressing SOD and catalase in mice does not extend lifespan (Pérez 2008), and oral antioxidant supplementation has broadly failed in humans. Native enzymes are also large, fragile proteins that barely penetrate intact skin and lose activity in most water-based formulas. Honest read: real biology, weak topical proof, big delivery/stability problems — a supporting "antioxidant-defense" story at best, and EUK-134 is the only version worth formulating seriously. See Verdict (§11).

1. Identity

"Antioxidant enzymes" is an umbrella for the cell's enzymatic ROS-detoxification machinery, plus a synthetic mimetic that imitates it. They are biologically distinct molecules that act in sequence (see §2), so products often blend them.

Name What it is INCI / label name Reaction catalyzed
Superoxide dismutase (SOD) Metalloenzyme; three human isoforms — SOD1 (Cu/Zn, cytosolic), SOD2 (Mn, mitochondrial), SOD3 / EC-SOD (Cu/Zn, extracellular matrix) Superoxide Dismutase (also "Lactococcus Ferment Lysate," yeast/microbial ferment lysates, "Saccharomyces…" as ferment sources) 2 O₂•⁻ + 2H⁺ → H₂O₂ + O₂
Catalase Heme-iron enzyme, peroxisomal Catalase (often as "Lactobacillus/…Ferment Lysate") 2 H₂O₂ → 2 H₂O + O₂
Glutathione peroxidase (GPx) Selenoenzyme family (GPx1–8); needs selenium + reduced glutathione (GSH) as cofactor Glutathione Peroxidase (rare on labels; usually present in ferment lysates, not as a purified active) H₂O₂ / lipid-OOH + 2 GSH → H₂O / lipid-OH + GSSG
EUK-134 Synthetic salen–manganese coordination complex; a SOD + catalase mimetic in one small molecule Ethylbisiminomethylguaiacol Manganese Chloride (CAS 81065-76-1) Both dismutation of O₂•⁻ and decomposition of H₂O₂ ("self-regenerating")
  • Common names: "antioxidant enzymes," "enzymatic antioxidants," "SOD/catalase," "the body's own antioxidants." EUK-134 is marketed as a stable, self-regenerating antioxidant (e.g. The Ordinary "EUK 134 0.1%").
  • Key forms/derivatives: native bovine/human-recombinant/microbial-ferment SOD and catalase (purified proteins or ferment lysates); liposomal Cu/Zn-SOD (the form used in the radiation-fibrosis literature, "Lipsod"); oral gliadin-bound melon SOD ("GliSODin," a supplement, not topical); EUK-134 / EUK-8 / EUK-207 salen-Mn synthetic mimetics. [1][4][9][13][17]
  • Important nuance: native enzymes are large proteins (SOD ~32 kDa; catalase ~240 kDa) — fragile and poorly skin-penetrant (§5). EUK-134 is a small molecule (~MW 458) designed precisely to dodge those problems. They are not interchangeable on a label even though marketing lumps them together.

2. Class & mechanism

These enzymes are the endogenous enzymatic antioxidant cascade — the body's primary defense against the ROS generated by mitochondrial respiration, UV, pollution, and inflammation. They work as a relay, which is why detoxifying only superoxide is not enough:

  1. SOD converts the superoxide radical (O₂•⁻) — the first ROS produced — into hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂) + oxygen. H₂O₂ is itself a damaging oxidant, so SOD alone just moves the problem one step down. [4][17]
  2. Catalase and GPx then destroy the H₂O₂ (and, for GPx, lipid peroxides), turning it into water. Without this second step, SOD activity can raise H₂O₂ load. This is why a balanced SOD + catalase/GPx system matters, and why EUK-134 (which does both reactions in one molecule) is mechanistically elegant. [4][7][17]

At the skin level the proposed payoff is reduced oxidative damage to lipids (less lipid peroxidation / barrier damage), proteins (less collagen fragmentation), and DNA — and dampened ROS → MAPK (ERK/JNK/p38) → AP-1 / MMP signaling, the pathway that drives UV-induced collagen breakdown. EUK-134 has the cleanest mechanistic demonstration of this: in UVB-exposed human keratinocytes it inhibited MAPK activation and reduced p53 accumulation dose-dependently and improved cell survival (Decraene 2004, in vitro). [2]

A genuinely interesting longevity-adjacent mechanism comes from EC-SOD (SOD3): it binds type-I collagen and protects it from oxidative fragmentation (Petersen 2004) [16], and EC-SOD-overexpressing transgenic mice showed increased skin thickness and collagen with age via AMPK and Nrf2/HO-1 activation (Lee 2021) [3]. That is a real mechanistic anti-aging signal — but it is in transgenic mice expressing their own enzyme, not topical application of an enzyme to human skin (§4).

⚠️ Mechanistic caveat that matters. The "ROS cause aging, so scavenging ROS reverses aging" syllogism is not supported by mammalian genetics. Overexpressing SOD1, SOD2, and/or catalase in mice does not extend lifespan (Pérez 2008) [11], and the broad human antioxidant-supplement literature has been disappointing. ROS are also signaling molecules (e.g. H₂O₂ in wound healing and redox signaling), so blunting them indiscriminately is not unambiguously good. The skin-aging case for these enzymes rests on local oxidative-damage reduction, not on a proven lifespan/aging-program effect.

Honest summary: the mechanism touches almost every oxidative hallmark; the human topical evidence touches almost none of the cosmetic ones. This is an "upstream defense" ingredient, not a demonstrated wrinkle/elasticity corrector.

4. Evidence review by tier — the core of this dossier

Read this as a ladder where the top rung (independent human topical RCT for cosmetic aging) is empty. The real human data live in a disease indication (radiation fibrosis) and are mostly uncontrolled; an oral supplement format has the cleanest RCTs but cannot be borrowed for a cream.

Tier 1 — In-vitro / biochemical / mechanism (solid)

  • Decraene D, Smaers K, Gan D, et al. (2004). A synthetic SOD/catalase mimetic (EUK-134) inhibits membrane-damage-induced MAPK activation and reduces p53 accumulation in UVB-exposed primary human keratinocytes. J Invest Dermatol 122(2):484–491. PMID 15009734. In cultured human keratinocytes, EUK-134 dose-dependently lowered p53 accumulation, inhibited ERK/JNK/p38 MAPK activation, and increased cell survival after UVB. The cleanest mechanistic proof for the mimetic. In vitro only. [2]
  • Petersen SV, Oury TD, Ostergaard L, et al. (2004). EC-SOD binds to type I collagen and protects against oxidative fragmentation. J Biol Chem 279(14):13705–13710. PMID 14736885. EC-SOD (SOD3) physically binds collagen and shields it from oxidative cleavage — a mechanistic basis for an anti-ECM-loss role. [16]
  • Shindo Y, Witt E, Han D, Epstein W, Packer L (1994). Enzymic and non-enzymic antioxidants in epidermis and dermis of human skin. J Invest Dermatol 102(1):122–124. PMID 8288904. Foundational: every antioxidant is more concentrated in epidermis than dermis; catalase 720% higher, SOD 126%, GPx 61% in epidermis vs dermis — establishing these enzymes as the skin's native defense, and the dermis as the more vulnerable layer. [14]
  • Multiple cell studies report SOD/catalase reduce UV-/H₂O₂-induced ROS, lipid peroxidation, and MMP induction in keratinocytes/fibroblasts. Supportive but expected for any working antioxidant. [4][17]

Tier 2 — Animal / ex-vivo

  • Lee MJ, Agrahari G, Kim H-Y, et al. (2021). Extracellular Superoxide Dismutase Prevents Skin Aging by Promoting Collagen Production through Activation of AMPK and Nrf2/HO-1 Cascades. J Invest Dermatol 141(10):2344–2353. EC-SOD-overexpressing transgenic mice had greater skin thickness and collagen with age; mechanism via AMPK + Nrf2/HO-1. ⚠️ This is transgenic self-expression, not topical enzyme application — do not transfer to a cream claim. [3]
  • Pérez VI, Van Remmen H, Bokov A, Epstein CJ, Vijg J, Richardson A (2008). The overexpression of major antioxidant enzymes does not extend the lifespan of mice. Aging Cell 8(1):73–75. PMID 19077044. Mice overexpressing SOD1, SOD2, catalase, or combinations showed no lifespan extension (mean survival 899–922 d vs 922 d WT). The decisive longevity counterweight (§2, §10). [11]
  • Melov S, Ravenscroft J, Malik S, et al. (2000). Extension of life-span with superoxide dismutase/catalase mimetics. Science 289(5484):1567–1569. SOD/catalase mimetics (EUK-8/EUK-134) extended C. elegans mean lifespan ~44% in the original report. [12] But: Keaney M & Gems D (2003) No increase in lifespan in C. elegans upon treatment with the SOD mimetic EUK-8 (Free Radic Biol Med 34(2):277–282) failed to replicate it; the worm-lifespan claim is contested. [15]
  • Ex-vivo/animal liposomal-SOD work shows reduced thermal-injury swelling and improved skin deposition vs non-liposomal SOD — a delivery finding, not a cosmetic-efficacy one. [9]

Tier 3 — Human ORAL (different format — context only; DO NOT borrow for a cream)

  • Goldberg CS & Gauci JT (2016). Efficacy of two melon-derived SOD/wheat-gliadin oral formulae in facial hyperpigmentation and erythema. Plast Reconstr Surg Glob Open 4(9 Suppl):25–26. Randomized, placebo-controlled, single-blind, n=36, 90 d. Brightening formula reduced brown pigment (73.77%→69.15%, p=0.042) and dark spots (p=0.010); anti-inflammatory formula reduced redness (p=0.008); placebo unchanged. ⚠️ Manufacturer-funded; lead author took speaking honoraria; single-blind; conference abstract; small. [10]
  • Fontas E, Montaudié H, Passeron T, et al. (2021). Oral gliadin-protected SOD + NB-UVB for non-segmental vitiligo, 24-wk RCT. [from search] — adjunct format, off-topic for topical cosmetics. [search]
  • Dietary SOD-melon (GliSODin) RCTs for stress/fatigue and locomotive syndrome are real but systemic/supplement outcomes — irrelevant to a topical anti-aging claim. [search]
  • Relevance to skincare: oral SOD survives digestion poorly as intact enzyme (gliadin-binding is an attempt to fix that); these trials establish a supplement story, not topical efficacy. Borrowing "clinically studied SOD" from oral data into a cream is a classic format-transfer overreach.

Tier 4 — Human TOPICAL — the decisive tier

(a) Radiation-induced fibrosis — the real human topical/locoregional data (mostly uncontrolled): - Delanian S, Baillet F, Huart J, et al. (1994). Successful treatment of radiation-induced fibrosis using liposomal Cu/Zn SOD: clinical trial. Radiother Oncol 32(1):12–20. PMID 7938674. n=34 patients / 42 fibrotic zones; liposomal Cu/Zn-SOD; mean fibrosis area reduced 57±26%. ⚠️ Uncontrolled, non-randomized; partly injected, not purely topical. [5] - Campana F, Zervoudis S, Perdereau B, et al. (2004). Topical superoxide dismutase reduces post-irradiation breast cancer fibrosis. J Cell Mol Med 8(1):109–116. PMID 15090266. n=44; Cu/Zn-SOD ointment twice daily 90 d. Pain reduced in 36/39; fibrotic area smaller in ~half; one local allergic reaction. ⚠️ Uncontrolled prospective series, no placebo arm. [6] - Landeen KC, Spanos WC, Gromer L (2018). Topical superoxide dismutase in posttreatment fibrosis in patients with head and neck cancer. Head Neck 40(7):1400–1405. PMID 29756397. ⭐ The only randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled topical SOD trial here — and it was NEGATIVE: improvement in fibrosis score was comparable between SOD and placebo at 3 months (not significant); physical therapy, not SOD, drove benefit. The honest centerpiece: when you finally run a proper RCT, the topical-enzyme effect disappears. [7]

(b) EUK-134 — human skin-surface (the best topical evidence for the mimetic, but a biomarker, not appearance): - Declercq L, Sente I, Hellemans L, et al. (2004). Use of the synthetic SOD/catalase mimetic EUK-134 to compensate for seasonal antioxidant deficiency by reducing pre-existing lipid peroxides at the human skin surface. Int J Cosmet Sci 26(5):255–263. Human in-vivo skin-surface measurements: topical EUK-134 reduced baseline and UVA-induced lipid peroxides dose-dependently, and — unlike α-tocopherol — worked even when applied after irradiation. ⚠️ Surface lipid-peroxide biomarker, not wrinkles/elasticity; small cosmetic-science study; manufacturer-linked (Estée Lauder authors). [8]

(c) Vitiligo / pigmentation (topical SOD-containing, uncertain): - Salazar-Formoso V & Morales-Sánchez MA (2025). Efficacy of Superoxide Dismutase in the Treatment of Vitiligo: A Systematic Review. Cureus 17(7):e88335. 8 RCTs, ~297 patients (topical pseudocatalase/SOD and oral gliadin-SOD). Only 2/8 showed modest repigmentation (both only with NB-UVB); 6/8 showed no superiority over control. Well tolerated, "efficacy remains uncertain," adjunct at best. A fair, independent read that the topical SOD signal is weak. [13]

(d) Cosmetic anti-aging appearance (wrinkles, elasticity, photoaging) — the brand-relevant claim: - No independent, vehicle-controlled human topical RCT of SOD, catalase, or GPx for wrinkle/elasticity/photoaging endpoints could be located. The cosmetic story rests entirely on mechanism + in-vitro + the EUK-134 lipid-peroxide biomarker + extrapolation from the fibrosis/oral data. This is the central honesty point of the dossier.

Bottom line by tier: strong mechanism/in-vitro → animal & transgenic supportive (but lifespan-overexpression data negative) → oral supplement RCTs (irrelevant format) → human topical exists mainly for radiation fibrosis, mostly uncontrolled, with the lone proper RCT NEGATIVE, plus one EUK-134 surface-biomarker study. No high-quality human topical evidence substantiates a cosmetic anti-aging/anti-wrinkle claim.

5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

This is a make-or-break problem for the native enzymes and the quiet caveat marketing omits.

  • Native SOD/catalase/GPx are large proteins (SOD ~32 kDa, catalase ~240 kDa) that cannot cross intact stratum corneum on their own. Topical efficacy depends entirely on liposomal / nanoparticle encapsulation or device-assisted delivery (microneedles), and even then dermal penetration is limited. An unencapsulated "SOD" on an INCI list is most likely a surface/marketing token, not a functional dermal active. [9]
  • GPx additionally needs cofactors (selenium + reduced glutathione) to function — supplying the apo-enzyme without its redox partner is largely inert.
  • EUK-134 is the exception: a small (~458 Da), lipid-soluble synthetic molecule that penetrates, is self-regenerating (catalytic, not consumed 1:1), and works at very low % (0.1%). This is why, for topical use, the mimetic is the more defensible choice than the native proteins. [8][18]
  • Implication: "we added the body's own antioxidant enzymes" sounds compelling but, for native proteins, delivery technology is the whole ballgame, and most market products do not solve it.

6. Formulation

  • Typical use levels: native SOD/catalase as standardized units of activity or low-% ferment lysates (supplier-proprietary); EUK-134 ~0.04–0.1% (very low — it's catalytic). [18]
  • pH: native SOD is most active and stable around pH ~7.0–8.5 — higher than most cosmetic formulas — which clashes with acidic actives. GPx similar near-physiological range. EUK-134 is more pH-tolerant. [9]
  • Stability — the second make-or-break issue. Native enzymes are heat-labile, oxidation-prone, and lose activity in high-water vehicles (reports of SOD losing activity within ~3 months in water-rich bases; degraded by heavy-metal contamination and heat). They demand cold-chain handling, encapsulation, airless/opaque packaging, and cool-phase addition (<40 °C). [9] EUK-134 is far more robust but still degrades with water/oils/alcohol — which is why anhydrous bases (e.g. propanediol) are used. [18]
  • Pairings: logically synergistic to deliver SOD + catalase/GPx together (so H₂O₂ from SOD is cleared — see §2); compatible with broader antioxidant networks (vitamin C/E, ferulic acid) and barrier ingredients. EUK-134 pairs with niacinamide and humectants in anhydrous serums.
  • Conflicts: native enzymes dislike low-pH systems (L-ascorbic acid, AHAs/BHAs), strong surfactants, high heat, and heavy metals (e.g. some iron/copper-containing extracts) — formulate in a separate vehicle. Avoid co-formulating GPx where its glutathione cofactor would be oxidized.
  • Sensory: EUK-134 in propanediol is a thin, fast serum; native-enzyme creams are vehicle-dependent. Some ferment lysates carry mild odor.

7. Safety

  • Irritation/sensitization: generally low and well-tolerated across the radiation-fibrosis and vitiligo literature. But contact allergy is documented — the breast-fibrosis series reported a local allergic reaction [6], and EUK-134/native enzymes are proteins/metal complexes where sensitization is possible. Patch-test messaging is reasonable.
  • Sun sensitivity: none added; the opposite is the marketing premise (oxidative-stress mitigation). Not a sunscreen and contributes nothing to SPF.
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding: no specific topical-cosmetic safety studies; act locally with negligible expected systemic absorption (native enzymes barely penetrate) — likely low concern, but no affirmative data → "consult a clinician."
  • Manganese note (EUK-134): it is a Mn complex; systemic Mn toxicity is an oral/inhalation concern, not a realistic risk from low-% topical use, but worth noting for claims sobriety.
  • Sourcing/sustainability: native SOD/catalase are now mostly microbial-fermentation or recombinant (moving away from bovine sources — a sustainability and BSE/animal-origin win); ferment-lysate forms are biotech-friendly. EUK-134 is fully synthetic. Verify supplier provenance and that "SOD" isn't bovine-derived if making clean/vegan claims.

8. Regulatory & claims

  • Cosmetic vs. drug/device line — sharp here. In a moisturizer/serum, SOD/catalase/GPx/EUK-134 are cosmetic ingredients. The moment you cite the radiation-fibrosis or vitiligo data you cross into drug/disease territory — those are therapeutic indications a cosmetic must not imply. EUK-134's "protects against UV-induced DNA damage / photoprotection" language also drifts toward drug/SPF claims (it is not a UV filter and adds nothing to SPF).
  • ✅ Defensible cosmetic language: "supports skin's natural antioxidant defenses," "helps neutralize the look of environmental/oxidative stress," "antioxidant," "helps defend the appearance of skin against pollution and daily aggressors," "for visibly healthier, more resilient-looking skin."
  • ⚠️ Risky / needs substantiation or legal review (and our data is thin): "reduces wrinkles," "boosts collagen," "reverses photoaging," "repairs sun damage," "treats fibrosis/scarring," "prevents hair greying," "the body's most powerful antioxidant," "anti-cancer." The fibrosis/vitiligo numbers are drug data; the lifespan/longevity framing is unsupported in mammals; "most powerful antioxidant" is marketing, not a measured cosmetic endpoint.
  • Substantiation: any efficacy claim should ride on the brand's own finished-formula data plus cited mechanism — never borrow the radiation-fibrosis, vitiligo, or oral-supplement clinical numbers into consumer copy. See ../CLAUDE.md guardrails and ../../brand/glossary.md.

9. Sourcing & cost

  • Native enzymes (SOD/catalase/GPx): mid-to-high. Purified/recombinant or ferment-lysate proteins cost more than commodity antioxidants (niacinamide, basic vitamin C derivatives), and the real cost is in stabilization + encapsulation + cold-chain + airless packaging needed to keep them active. GPx additionally needs selenium/glutathione co-supply to mean anything. This is a "formulation-cost" ingredient as much as a raw-material one.
  • EUK-134: mid. A defined synthetic small molecule (e.g. Lucas Meyer / Clariant grade), used at very low % so cost-in-use is modest; far easier to stabilize. The more economically and technically sensible route to an "enzymatic antioxidant" story.
  • Supply: native enzymes from several biotech suppliers (microbial/recombinant); EUK-134 from specialty actives houses. Buy delivery-/activity-validated grades, not a raw protein extract.

10. Hype vs. evidence

Marketing claim Reality
"We added the body's own antioxidant enzymes — SOD, catalase, glutathione peroxidase." True ingredients, but native enzymes are large proteins that barely penetrate and lose activity in most formulas; presence on an INCI ≠ activity in skin. [9]
"SOD is the most powerful antioxidant." A marketing superlative, not a measured cosmetic endpoint; SOD alone even generates H₂O₂ unless catalase/GPx clear it. [4][17]
"Clinically proven to fight aging." Human topical clinical data exist mainly for radiation fibrosis (mostly uncontrolled), and the one proper RCT was negative (Landeen 2018). No cosmetic-appearance RCT exists. [5][6][7]
"Longevity antioxidant — fights aging at the cellular level." Overexpressing SOD + catalase in mice does not extend lifespan (Pérez 2008); broad antioxidant supplementation has failed. The lifespan framing is not supported in mammals. [11]
"Reverses grey hair / restores pigment." From the H₂O₂/catalase hair-follicle hypothesis (Wood 2009) — a follicle mechanism, unproven as a topical anti-grey treatment, and a borderline drug claim. [search]
"GliSODin clinically studied." That's an oral supplement; oral data don't transfer to a cream. [10]

The honest pitch is "supports the skin's natural antioxidant defense system" — not "powerful clinically proven anti-wrinkle enzyme" and certainly not "longevity enzyme."

11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ CONDITIONAL — supporting actor; EUK-134 is the only version worth formulating seriously, and never as a hero anti-aging claim.

One line: Real, foundational antioxidant biology with a compelling "the skin's own defenses" story, but the human topical cosmetic evidence is thin-to-absent (best data are in radiation fibrosis, mostly uncontrolled, with the lone RCT negative), native enzymes barely penetrate and are formulation-fragile, and the longevity framing is contradicted by mammalian lifespan data — so it's a credibility risk as a hero and a defensible "supporting antioxidant-defense" actor at most.

  • If used: prefer EUK-134 (~0.04–0.1%, anhydrous, stable, penetrant) over native proteins as the workhorse; if native SOD/catalase are used, only in a delivery-validated encapsulated, pH-correct, airless-packaged system and ideally paired (SOD + catalase/GPx) so H₂O₂ is cleared. Keep all copy in cosmetic "supports/defends the appearance of" language — no wrinkle/collagen/longevity/fibrosis claims. Our better-substantiated antioxidant narrative already runs through vitamin C (THD) [see vitamin-c.md], ergothioneine ergothioneine.md, and ferulic acid ferulic-acid.md — antioxidant enzymes add a distinctive "enzymatic / body's-own-defense" story but not superior evidence.
  • Longevity relevance: conceptually on-thesis (oxidative stress is a core aging hallmark, and EC-SOD protecting collagen is an elegant mechanism), but the keystone longevity experiment — overexpressing these very enzymes in mammals — failed to extend lifespan, so longevity copy would outrun the science. Treat as oxidative-defense support, not a proven longevity lever.
  • Recommendation: shortlist EUK-134 as an optional supporting antioxidant / "defense" narrative ingredient; treat native SOD/catalase/GPx as watch-items behind better-evidenced antioxidants. Log in ../../ideas/considering.md as conditional.

Evidence verification (adversarial)

Verified 2026-06-19 by independent re-checking of the cited primary sources (PubMed, PMC, J Invest Dermatol, Aging Cell, Cureus) against every headline claim. Method: each efficacy claim was tested for (a) HUMAN + TOPICAL vs. borrowed from in-vitro/animal/oral, (b) whether the cited study is real and says what is claimed, (c) industry funding / small n / controlled design.

Overall evidence grade: C− / split

  • For the mechanism and native skin biology (these enzymes are the skin's primary ROS defense; SOD→catalase/GPx cascade; EC-SOD protects collagen): grade B. Solid, multiply-confirmed, real human-tissue data (Shindo 1994, Rhie 2001, Petersen 2004) plus clean in-vitro mimetic mechanism (Decraene 2004).
  • For the brand-relevant claim — visibly reduces wrinkles / improves elasticity / reverses photoaging via topical application: grade D. Essentially unproven. No vehicle-controlled human topical cosmetic RCT exists; the human topical data are a disease indication (radiation fibrosis), mostly uncontrolled, and the one proper RCT (Landeen 2018) was negative. The EUK-134 human evidence (Declercq 2004) is a surface lipid-peroxide biomarker, not an appearance endpoint, and is manufacturer-linked.
  • For the longevity framing: actively contradicted in mammals (Pérez 2008 — SOD/catalase overexpression does not extend mouse lifespan) and contested even in worms (Keaney 2003 failed to replicate Melov 2000).

Citation audit — all real, checked against primary sources

  • Decraene 2004 (PMID 15009734): confirmed — EUK-134 lowered p53, inhibited MAPK, raised survival in UVB keratinocytes (in vitro).
  • Lee 2021 (JID 141:2344): confirmed — EC-SOD transgenic mice, more skin collagen/thickness via AMPK + Nrf2/HO-1. Correctly tiered as animal/transgenic, NOT topical.
  • Delanian 1994 (PMID 7938674): confirmed — n=34/42 zones, ~57% area reduction; correctly labeled uncontrolled and partly injected.
  • Campana 2004 (PMID 15090266): confirmed — n=44 uncontrolled topical series; pain ↓ 36/39; one allergic reaction. (Journal/year corrected to J Cell Mol Med 2004; some indexes list 2007 reprint.)
  • Landeen 2018 (PMID 29756397): confirmed — RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled, negative (no significant SOD-vs-placebo difference). This is the load-bearing honest counterweight.
  • Declercq 2004 (Int J Cosmet Sci 26:255): confirmed — human skin-surface lipid-peroxide reduction by topical EUK-134; manufacturer (Estée Lauder) authors.
  • Goldberg & Gauci 2016 (PRS Glob Open abstract): confirmed — ORAL melon-SOD, n=36, single-blind, manufacturer-funded. Correctly walled off as oral.
  • Pérez 2008 (PMID 19077044): confirmed verbatim — SOD1/SOD2/catalase overexpression "does not extend the lifespan of mice."
  • Shindo 1994 (PMID 8288904): confirmed — epidermis vs dermis antioxidant gradient (catalase +720%, SOD +126%, GPx +61%).
  • Rhie 2001 (PMID 11710935): confirmed — SOD & GPx unchanged with aging/photoaging; catalase rises in aged/photoaged epidermis but falls in dermis. Important nuance: the simple "antioxidant enzymes decline with age" story is not clean for SOD/GPx in skin.
  • Petersen 2004 (PMID 14736885): confirmed — EC-SOD binds type-I collagen, protects from oxidative fragmentation.
  • Melov 2000 / Keaney 2003: both confirmed real; the replication failure (Keaney) is correctly flagged.
  • Salazar-Formoso & Morales-Sánchez 2025 (Cureus e88335): confirmed — 8 RCTs, ~297 patients, only 2/8 modest (with NB-UVB), "efficacy remains uncertain."

Main caveats a skeptic must hold onto

  1. The brand-relevant claim is the weak one. Everything strong is mechanism/biology; the cosmetic-appearance topical case is grade-D with a negative RCT.
  2. Native enzymes barely penetrate and are formulation-fragile — presence ≠ activity. EUK-134 is the only topically credible workhorse, and even its human data are a surface biomarker.
  3. The longevity story is contradicted in mammals — do not build "longevity enzyme" copy.
  4. Human topical efficacy data come from a drug indication (radiation fibrosis) and are mostly uncontrolled; using them in cosmetic copy is both unsupported and a drug-claim risk.
  5. "Antioxidant enzymes decline with age" is oversimplified for skin — Rhie 2001 found SOD and GPx unchanged, catalase rising in epidermis and falling in dermis. The clean "decline" narrative mainly holds for the hair follicle (greying) story, which is a different, borderline-drug claim.

Bottom line for the brand

The §10–11 verdict — "real biology, weak topical proof, big delivery/stability problems; supporting antioxidant-defense story at most; EUK-134 the only serious topical version; never a hero or longevity claim" — survives adversarial checking. All 18 sources are real and accurately represented; the corrections were minor (one journal-year, tier-labeling of Lee 2021 and the oral data, and surfacing the Rhie 2001 "enzymes unchanged" nuance). Keep the framing strict: cosmetic "supports the skin's natural antioxidant defenses," substantiated on the brand's own finished-formula data — not borrowed fibrosis, vitiligo, oral, or lifespan numbers.
