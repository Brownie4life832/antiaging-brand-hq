Ingredient: Bidens Pilosa Extract / "Bio-Retinol" (INCI: Bidens Pilosa Extract)

Researched: 2026-06-19 · Status: ☑ considering / ⬜ shortlisted / ⬜ rejected / ⬜ in a product Class: Retinoids & retinoid alternatives (plant-derived "retinol-like" / RXR-modulating active) Slug: bidens-pilosa-bio-retinol

One-line honesty banner: A genuinely interesting RXR-pathway botanical with a real (if narrow) primary literature — but the "works like 0.3% retinol, clinically proven" marketing rests almost entirely on one unpublished supplier study and in-vitro/ex-vivo data. The single peer-reviewed human topical trial tested it in combination with another active, not alone. Treat as supporting, not hero, and write copy with discipline.

Sibling file: This dossier covers the plant/ingredient class ("Bidens bio-retinol"). The dominant branded commercial form, Revinage® (Chemyunion), has its own deeper dossier at revinage.md — read both; they share the same evidence base and the same "Barbier 2014 is a fabricated citation" finding. Use this file for the general ingredient story, the Revinage file for supplier/grade/sourcing specifics.

1. Identity

  • Common names: Bidens pilosa (botanical); "Picão Preto" (Brazilian Portuguese), "Black Jack," "beggarticks," "hairy beggarticks," "Spanish needle." Marketed in skincare as "bio-retinol," "plant retinol," "natural retinol alternative."
  • Plant: Bidens pilosa L., Asteraceae (daisy family). A pantropical herbaceous weed native to South America (abundant in Brazil/Amazon), now naturalized worldwide. Long history in folk medicine (anti-inflammatory, anti-diabetic, anti-malarial, anti-hypertensive).
  • INCI: Bidens Pilosa Extract.
  • Key commercial form / branded ingredient: Revinage® (Chemyunion, Brazil) — the dominant supplier and the source of nearly all "bio-retinol" marketing claims. It is not pure Bidens extract: it is a blend, sold under one INCI string:
  • Revinage® / Revinage® MB INCI: Bidens Pilosa Extract (and) Elaeis Guineensis (Palm) Oil (and) Gossypium Herbaceum (Cotton) Seed Oil (and) Linum Usitatissimum (Linseed) Seed Oil.
  • Revinage® WPO (palm-free): Astrocaryum Murumuru Seed Butter (and) Bidens Pilosa Extract (and) Gossypium Herbaceum (Cotton) Seed Oil (and) Linum Usitatissimum (Linseed) Seed Oil.
  • Produced by supercritical CO₂ (apolar) extraction, standardized to phytol (dossier: phytol ~0.139%).
  • Active constituent of interest: phytol — a diterpene alcohol from chlorophyll. The proposed mechanism hinges on phytol → phytanic acid (its oxidized metabolite). Bidens also contains flavonoids, polyacetylenes, phenylpropanoids, and fatty acids (palmitic, oleic, linoleic, linolenic).

2. Class & mechanism

Positioned as a "retinol-like" active that works through the retinoid receptor system but is not vitamin A and has no vitamin-A chemical structure. The mechanistic chain (strongest at the bottom, weakest at the top of the funnel — see caveats):

  1. Foundational pharmacology (well established, generic — not skin-specific): Phytanic acid (the oxidation product of dietary/plant phytol) is a bona fide endogenous RXR (Retinoid X Receptor) agonist. Kitareewan et al. 1996 (Mol Biol Cell) isolated it from serum and showed it activates RXR-dependent transcription at 4–64 µM, displacing 9-cis-retinoic acid with a Kᵢ ~4 µM (≈200× weaker than 9-cis-RA, but real). This is the scientific anchor of the whole "bio-retinol" idea.
  2. Receptor logic for skin: Retinoids act via RAR + RXR heterodimers binding RARE/RXRE response elements, driving keratinocyte differentiation, fibroblast proliferation, and ECM (collagen/elastin/GAG) synthesis; they also antagonize AP-1 (the UV-induced transcription factor that turns on MMPs). The Bidens/phytol pitch: selectively push the RXR arm (and even down-regulate RAR), capturing collagen/ECM and anti-AP-1 benefits while sidestepping RAR-driven irritation ("retinoid dermatitis"). This RAR-vs-RXR irritation rationale is biologically plausible and is the explicit thesis of Ye et al. 2025.
  3. Anti-inflammatory mechanism (independent of receptors): Bidens pilosa inhibits p38 MAPK phosphorylation → suppresses IL-1β-induced COX-2 and PGE₂ in human dermal fibroblasts (Yoshida et al. 2006). Supports the "soothing / non-irritating" positioning.
  4. Antioxidant / redox: Decreases reactive oxygen species (and reciprocally raises nitric oxide) in human dermal endothelial cells (Kohda et al. 2013). Supplier data add SOD/CAT stimulation and reduced lipid peroxidation (MDA).

Bottom line on mechanism: The RXR-agonism mechanism is real and peer-reviewed at the molecular level (phytanic acid → RXR). What is weaker is the leap from "phytanic acid activates RXR in a reporter assay" to "topically applied Bidens extract meaningfully remodels aged human skin."

4. Evidence review by tier (the core of this dossier)

Read this section before writing any copy. The tiers are deliberately separated so we never let in-vitro or supplier data masquerade as proven human topical benefit.

Tier 1 — In-vitro (cell culture)

(a) Kitareewan et al. 1996 — Mol Biol Cell 7(8):1153–66. PMID 8856661. - Foundational, peer-reviewed. Phytanic acid (phytol metabolite) activates RXR at 4–64 µM; Kᵢ ≈ 4 µM. Not a skin study — establishes the receptor pharmacology the whole category borrows from.

(b) Yoshida et al. 2006 — J Dermatol 33(10):676–83. PMID 17040496. - Normal human dermal fibroblasts, IL-1β-induced inflammation. Bidens pilosa inhibited p38/ERK/JNK phosphorylation → ↓COX-2 → ↓PGE₂. Solid mechanistic support for anti-inflammatory/soothing claims. (Exact extract concentrations not reported in abstract.)

(c) Kohda et al. 2013 — J Dermatol Sci 72(1):75–7. PMID 23819986. - Human dermal endothelial cells. Bidens extract decreased ROS and increased NO; framed as explaining its benefit in livedo vasculopathy. Antioxidant signal in human cells, but a short correspondence/letter, not an aging study.

(d) Dieamant et al. 2015 — in-vitro arm — Evid Based Complement Alternat Med 2015:280529. PMID 25883669. (Open access, PMC4391488.) - Human dermal fibroblasts + keratinocytes, supercritical CO₂ extract (BPE-CO₂A; phytol 0.139%, plus palmitic/oleic/linoleic acids) at 0.05/0.10/0.20 mg/mL; phytol tested separately. - Reported increases vs control (extract): collagen +32.5% (0.05 mg/mL), elastin +25.2%, TGF-β1 +53.2%, FGF +188.3%, EGFr 2.6-fold, RXR 3.6–3.7-fold. RAR not significantly changed by extract, retinol, or retinoic acid. Phytol alone reproduced the RXR induction → identifies phytol as the driver. - Comparators in the same assays: retinoic acid (10 µM) collagen +62.9%, elastin +66.7%; retinol (10 µM) collagen +45.6%, elastin +16.7%. So in cells, the extract was generally weaker than retinoic acid and comparable-to-weaker than retinol on collagen — useful counterweight to "as good as retinol" hype.

⚠️ Note on this paper: earlier circulating attributions (e.g. "Süntar et al.") are incorrect; verified author list is Dieamant G, Pereda M del C, Nogueira C, Eberlin S, Facchini G, et al. Several authors are affiliated with the ingredient developer — read as developer-associated science.

Tier 2 — Animal

  • No topical/skin animal efficacy studies for Bidens "bio-retinol" were found. Animal Bidens work exists for oral/systemic uses (anti-diabetic, anti-inflammatory, cognition in aged rats — e.g. PMID 31295527), not transferable to topical skin anti-aging.

Tier 3 — Human, ORAL

  • Oral Bidens pilosa pilot data exist (e.g. blood-glucose/β-cell pilot in men, PMC4381681). Irrelevant to topical cosmetic use — listed only to flag that "human studies exist" claims often quote oral work. Do not borrow.

Tier 4 — Human, TOPICAL (the tier that actually matters)

(a) Ye et al. 2025 — the ONLY peer-reviewed human topical trial — Biomolecules 15(2):296. PMID 40001599 / PMC11853381. (LG Household & Health Care R&D; authors disclose company employment.) - Design: half-face, double-blind, n = 14 healthy Korean women (mean age 34), 4 weeks. Test cream = 0.035% andrographolide + 0.35% Bidens pilosa extract (BPE) ("RXR formula") vs control cream = 0.1% retinol. - Results (vs own week-0 baseline): crow's-feet fine wrinkles −8.89%, under-eye fine wrinkles −5.62%, nasolabial folds −10.49%; elasticity and hydration improved; no irritation vs the retinol side. - Critical limitations: (1) Bidens was NOT tested alone — it was combined with andrographolide, so the human benefit cannot be attributed to Bidens specifically; (2) no vehicle/placebo arm — improvements are within-group vs baseline, and the retinol side is an active comparator, not a negative control; (3) n = 14, 4 weeks — small and short; (4) no statistical superiority over retinol claimed — the paper's honest framing is "comparable efficacy, less irritation," not "better."

(b) Chemyunion / Revinage® supplier dossier — UNPUBLISHED, NOT peer-reviewed. - In-vivo claim: n = 40 women, ages 35–55, 42 days, 2% Revinage vs 0.3% retinol palmitate (one formula per hemi-face/forearm), twice daily. Instrumental endpoints (Cutometer, ultrasound, VISIA, PRIMOS). - Headline numbers (these are the source of all "clinically proven" marketing): elasticity +13%, dermal re-densification +25%, roughness −36%, deep wrinkles −28%, hyperpigmented area −37% (all at 42 days); claimed no statistical difference vs 0.3% retinol palmitate. - Why to discount heavily: (1) manufacturer-run, unpublished, no peer review, no DOI/PMID; (2) the efficacy comparison is active-vs-active (retinol palmitate) with no placebo arm for the wrinkle/elasticity endpoints — "as good as retinol" with no vehicle control is weak; (3) retinyl palmitate is itself a weak retinoid, an easy bar to match; (4) permeation study was n = 2; (5) many flashy figures (SIRT6, DHT −64%, NMF +92.78%, ceramide-3 +55.85%) are single in-vitro/ex-vivo readouts, not in-vivo outcomes.

🔴 Citation hygiene flag: A frequently quoted "Barbier J, et al. (2014). Clinical, Cosmetic and Investigational Dermatology, 7, 175–181" on Bidens as a retinol-like active could not be verified — it does not appear in PubMed for Bidens pilosa and appears to be a fabricated / mis-attributed citation propagated by blogs and AI summaries. Do NOT cite it. Marked [unverified — likely nonexistent].

Net for TOPICAL human use: The only legitimate peer-reviewed human topical evidence is one small (n=14, 4-week) combination-product trial that shows modest, baseline-relative wrinkle improvement with good tolerability — and it can't isolate Bidens. Everything else commonly cited is in-vitro, ex-vivo, oral, supplier-internal, or fabricated. Benefits proven specifically for topical Bidens alone in humans: effectively none at RCT grade. Plausible and supportive: yes. Proven hero: no.

5. Topical bioavailability / penetration

  • Bidens "bio-retinol" is oil-soluble (CO₂ apolar extract; phytol is lipophilic). Designed for the oil phase of emulsions — favorable for stratum-corneum partitioning.
  • Penetration evidence is thin: the only data is the supplier Raman-spectroscopy study (n = 2) tracking phytol to ~1–60 µm depth, peaking ~2–4 h post-application. Directionally supportive, statistically meaningless.
  • Mechanistic dependency: efficacy assumes phytol is oxidized to phytanic acid in situ to act on RXR. Whether enough conversion happens in living human skin at cosmetic use levels is not established in vivo — a real open question, not a settled fact.

6. Formulation

Parameter Guidance (per Chemyunion Revinage® dossier)
Use level 1.0–2.0% w/w (Revinage). Human/cell studies used 0.35% BPE (Ye 2025) up to 2% Revinage.
pH 5.5–7.0 suggested.
Solubility Oil-soluble — incorporate into the oil phase, up to ~85 °C.
Format Cream, lotion, opaque gel, gel-cream, serum, oil, lipstick/balm. Not suitable for hydroalcoholic systems.
Stability Claimed far more photostable/oxidation-stable than retinol (supplier HPLC stress data); 24-month shelf-life claim. Plausible (phytol/phytanic acid lack retinol's labile polyene chain) but supplier-sourced.
Pairings No incompatibilities reported by supplier. Logical companions for a barrier-repair brand: niacinamide, peptides, ceramides, bakuchiol, antioxidants. Ex-vivo data put it head-to-head with bakuchiol (similar collagen-I; Revinage edged it on collagen-IV/elastin/fibronectin in supplier data).
Conflicts None documented. Stacking with strong retinoids/acids is redundant rather than dangerous — the whole point is to replace the irritating retinoid.
Sensory Oil-phase botanical; can carry mild plant color/odor. The blend's fatty-acid carriers (palm/cotton/linseed or murumuru) add emolliency.

7. Safety

  • Irritation: Strong selling point — consistently low/no irritation in available data (Ye 2025 half-face vs retinol; supplier data). The RAR-sparing mechanism is the rationale.
  • Sun sensitivity: Marketed as not photosensitizing (can be used AM, unlike many retinoids). Plausible (no vitamin-A polyene) but not formally tested for phototoxicity in independent trials. Daily SPF is still the #1 anti-aging step regardless.
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding: Genuinely ambiguous — do not parrot "pregnancy-safe."
  • It is not vitamin A and does not generate retinoic acid, so the classic teratogenic-retinoid concern doesn't directly apply — a fair point.
  • BUT the active metabolite is phytanic acid, which accumulates pathologically in Refsum disease (a phytanic-acid storage disorder); patients must restrict dietary phytanic acid. Topical cosmetic exposure is tiny vs dietary load, so risk is likely negligible — but this is reasoning, not data. No pregnancy safety study exists.
  • Stance for the brand: never claim "safe in pregnancy." If asked, say it lacks vitamin-A teratogenicity concerns but has no pregnancy safety data; advise consulting a clinician.
  • Sensitization/allergen: Asteraceae/Compositae family → possible contact-allergen risk for people sensitive to daisies/ragweed/chamomile/arnica. Worth a patch-test note.
  • Sourcing/sustainability: Bidens is an abundant, fast-growing weed → low cultivation pressure, a real sustainability talking point. Caveat: standard Revinage uses palm oil (RSPO and palm-free WPO versions exist — choose WPO/MB for a clean sustainability story).

8. Regulatory & claims (per CLAUDE.md guardrails)

  • Status: Cosmetic ingredient. The moment we say it "works like retinol," we invite scrutiny — and structure/function claims ("boosts collagen," "stimulates RXR," "increases elastin by X%") edge toward drug territory and would need substantiation/legal review.
  • The word "retinol" is a regulatory trap. "Bio-retinol," "plant retinol," "natural retinol" can imply equivalence we cannot prove (only one small combination human trial). Some markets may also view "retinol" comparison claims as misleading.

✅ Defensible cosmetic language: - "A plant-derived alternative to retinol, formulated to be gentle." - "Helps improve the appearance of fine lines and supports a smoother, firmer-looking complexion." - "Visibly evens tone; suitable for AM and PM use." - "Well-tolerated; designed for skin that finds retinoids irritating."

⚠️ Avoid / legal-flag (substantiation required): - "Clinically proven as effective as 0.3% retinol" → rests on one unpublished supplier study, active-vs-active, no placebo. High risk. - "Boosts collagen by 24%," "increases elastin," "activates your skin's retinoid receptors," "anti-aging," "repairs," "reduces wrinkles by 28%" → drug-like / requires data we don't own. - "Pregnancy-safe," "retinol-strength results, zero irritation."

9. Sourcing & cost

  • Primary supplier: Chemyunion (Brazil) — Revinage® (+ MB/RSPO and WPO palm-free variants), ECOCERT/COSMOS-certifiable. Effectively the category gatekeeper; most "bio-retinol" products on the market are Revinage relabeled.
  • Cost tier: Mid → upper-mid for a branded botanical active (more than commodity plant extracts/niacinamide; less than growth factors or exotic peptides). Used at 1–2%, cost-in-formula is moderate. Exact pricing needs a supplier/distributor quote (Knowde lists it).
  • Supply: Raw material (a weed) is not scarce; supply risk is low. Specify WPO (palm-free) for sustainability/marketing cleanliness.

10. Hype vs. evidence

Marketing claim seen in the wild Reality
"Clinically proven, as effective as 0.3% retinol" One unpublished supplier study (n=40, no placebo, vs weak retinyl palmitate). Independent peer-reviewed human data: a single n=14, 4-week combination trial that claims comparable, not superior.
"Boosts type I & IV collagen / elastin in skin" Demonstrated in cell/explant assays, not in living human skin for the isolated ingredient. Cosmetic structure-function overreach.
"Activates retinoid receptors like retinol but without irritation" Mechanistically grounded (phytanic acid → RXR; RAR spared) and biologically plausible — but the in-vivo receptor effect at cosmetic doses is unproven.
"Backed by science / human studies" Often conflates oral Bidens studies or quotes the fabricated "Barbier 2014" citation.
"18.2% wrinkle reduction / 86% looked younger" (brand pages) Brand-specific finished-product marketing tests, not ingredient RCTs. Treat as advertising.
"Pregnancy-safe natural retinol" No pregnancy data; phytanic-acid metabolite warrants caution, not reassurance.

Where the hype is fair: tolerability/gentleness, oil-phase stability vs retinol, sustainability (abundant weed, palm-free option), and a plausible, peer-reviewed molecular mechanism (RXR). It is a legitimate "gentle retinol-alternative" story — just not a proven retinol-equal.

11. Verdict for the brand

⚠️ CONDITIONAL — a SUPPORTING actives, not a hero.

One-line reasoning: Bidens "bio-retinol" has a real, peer-reviewed molecular mechanism (phytanic acid → RXR) and a strong tolerability/gentleness story, but its anti-aging efficacy rests on in-vitro/ex-vivo data and a single small combination human trial — far short of the evidence behind actual retinoids — so it earns a place as a gentle, well-tolerated supporting active in a barrier-friendly "sensitive-skin retinol alternative" lane, not as the brand's proven anti-aging hero.

Longevity-relevance note: The RXR-pathway angle is interesting for a skin-longevity narrative (RXR modulates ECM turnover; supplier data flag SIRT6/senescence), and "longevity = gentle, sustainable, daily-tolerable actives that don't compromise the barrier" fits the brand's barrier-repair thesis well. But the senescence/longevity claims are currently mechanistic and supplier-sourced — usable as story texture, not as substantiated efficacy. If pursued, pair with our genuinely barrier-supporting heroes (ceramides, niacinamide, peptides) and position on gentleness + sustainability, with claims kept to "appearance of."
